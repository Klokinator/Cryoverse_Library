"Gah!" He let out an involuntary yelp as Phoebe drove the wooden sword straight into his gut, knocked him flat on his ass, and sprained his tailbone in the process. "God, Jesus, fuck! I think I hit a rock!"

Phoebe stiffened. "Your Grace! Are you alright?!"

He nodded despite the tears rolling down his face. "Yup! Great! Never better! I told you not to hold back! Not your fault!"

The two of them were sparring behind the palace. For the last two months, ever since he had recovered from his paralysis, he had insisted on training with Phoebe and learning how to use a sword. The uneven ground usually wouldn't make him lose his footing, and he should have a size advantage over her, but she still routinely kicked his ass up and down the streets.

Phoebe used to go easy on him until he insisted that he wouldn't learn unless she put his feet to the fire, and he could heal himself, so it wasn't like she would cause any permanent damage...

But _still_! Goddamn! Phoebe was light on her feet, quick to avoid even his most accurate attacks, and his spherical vision didn't matter if his body couldn't react to her movements.

Phoebe stepped forward and hunched down in front of him. "Do you wish to take a break, Your Grace? Your movements have slowed down noticeably of late."

"Nah, I'm fine. **Heal.**" He dropped the shield in his left hand and rubbed his sore ass for a moment before climbing to his feet. "That sudden stab caught me off-guard. Usually, you swing wide."

"Of course. I've been playing around with you, Your Grace, using a repetitious pattern to catch you off-guard when I eventually vary my strike. In battle, a swordsman must be inventive. He who strikes the first blow wins, after all." The heat pattern on her lips changed slightly, which he had grown to learn indicated a smile. 

Reading faces was somewhat tricky when using infrared, but he could do it as long as he focused. Unfortunately, no matter how he tried, he couldn't follow anyone's eye movements, giving him a slight disadvantage. Luckily, he made up the difference by not moving his eyes either, so it crippled his opponent's ability to read him as well.

...Or at least it _would_, if his opponent wasn't Phoebe. Every time he thought he understood her fighting capability, he was surprised. She wasn't just a fantastic caregiver, but an expert swordswoman, too. He couldn't afford to underestimate her.

He forced a grin. "So, uh, where did I go wrong?"

"Your shield. You always forget it exists. You could have easily parried my stab, had you raised your shield, but you didn't. If you don't protect your vitals, you might die before you can use your magic."

Phoebe's smile faltered at the last sentence. Anytime she brought up his potential death, a feeling of melancholy filled the air. _I can't ever let him die,_ her body language said. It was sweet of her and made him want to train even harder.

He grabbed his shield with telekinesis. "Okay. I've gotta guard myself better. Got it."

"It isn't just that, Your Grace." Phoebe reached out her sword and tapped his knee. "You're a head taller than me. In combat, a size difference can be both positive and negative. I use my size as often as possible, ducking under your wide swings and attacking your legs. Your shield has to protect much more body area than mine does, leaving you exposed more often. If you hopped up to avoid my low attacks, that would leave me wide open for you to parry, and you would win."

He nodded, forcing himself to memorize her words. "I think I can remember all that. It's a lot to recall in the middle of combat, though."

Phoebe sighed and rested her sword-hand on her hip. "You shouldn't have to think of these things consciously, Your Grace. Swordfighting comes down to split-second movements and instincts. Your body will move automatically if you train it right. That's all there is to it."

From behind the shadow of the house, he sensed a male's heat signature appear. Evidently overhearing Phoebe, the man laughed. "Hah! There's a lot more to combat than rote body instinct, though the lady has a fair point." He recognized him as Neil Adams, and nodded as he walked over. Neil continued, "Military tactics are the way to win battles! A sword is useless against a musket."

Phoebe's face tensed up. "Unless the musket misses."

"True, true!" Neil stopped near them and examined him. "My liege, have you learned to fight back yet, or are you still taking a beating from this young lass?"

He tried not to blush. "I have yet to land an unearned blow on her."

Phoebe rolled her eyes and smiled. "His Grace doesn't want to hurt me. He's too soft. He always swings slower than he should, giving me time to dodge."

"A gentleman, true to the core. I can respect that." Neil lifted his brimmed hat for a moment to scratch his head, then put it back. From what he understood, Neil was a Frenchman who fought under Napoleon, but his English accent was confusingly thick, without a trace of the French dialect like Joan used. "In any case, since you two are training, this is the perfect time for me to bring up a concern on my mind. Would you care to listen, my liege?"

Without moving his head, he examined Phoebe. She didn't have to say anything for him to know she was ready to stop the training and hear him out. Good enough for him. "Sure. What's up?"

Neil rubbed his hands together. "Have you seen the quality of our military?"

"Yeah. It isn't that great."

"It's terrible," Neil said bluntly. "The monsters have no discipline. Everyone has a signature fighting style, which is disastrous for cohesiveness. I have yet to see teamwork among the different monster species. Have you heard the rumors wandering through the Labyrinth?"

He shook his head.

Neil continued. "The demon Emperors are feeling anxious. They don't like that we've controlled the Core for three months. Without the warp-gates, they can't raid human planets efficiently to replenish souls. They're stuck using the few teleporters located at the corners of Hell, which are less efficient and can only access one planet at a time."

"Replenish souls? Are you referring to the River Styx?"

The middle-aged man's expression turned grim. "Aye. Demons slaughter humans to convert us into souls for the weaker ones, which allows them to mutate into stronger forms. It appears the river has been thinning recently and they're getting antsy. I would expect an attack on the Labyrinth Core within the next month at the latest."

Phoebe bowed her head. "We cannot placate them, either. The only appeasement they would accept would be allowing them to use the portals, and that would mean..."

"...More dead humans," he said, finishing her sentence. "Unacceptable."

"Precisely." Neil stroked his chin and nodded. "Which is why I'm saying we need to restructure the military. I've spoken to a few close friends of mine, and we'd like to list our concerns in full as soon as possible."

He raised an eyebrow. "How quickly can we pull off something like that? We don't have a formal military right now."

Neil looked away. "During your paralysis, I took the liberty of forming some rudimentary militias. There are four squads of a hundred men and monsters each, but we still have thousands of men and monsters who have yet to touch a weapon. My comrades and I lead each militia. One to guard the Core, one to guard the inner gate, one to patrol the mines and village, and the other to guard your castle."

"Oh. I see. That was a good move. Still, we'll need way more than four hundred soldiers. Alright, fine, let's get on with it, then."

Phoebe sighed again. "And to think, we were finally making progress with your training."

He rubbed his ass. "Yeah. Progress."

Several minutes later, he summoned two slices of cinnamon sugared toast in midair and handed one to Phoebe. The two of them munched their bread as they followed Neil, but he declined his offer for a slice. Walking past the castle, they headed down the cliff-side and over to the forest, where several new buildings and a wall now stood. He had erected them with Wordsmithing last month, and the lone guard tower had a telescope aimed at the portal at all times so they could keep an eye out for arrivals. There was even a flare system in case of emergencies or unexpected intrusions. If the Core were to fall, they might not know about it in time.

_If only cell service could transmit through the portal, he would have solved several problems by now. The space barrier didn't do well with energy transmission. He would have to think about it._

Phoebe polished off her bread and wiped the cinnamon from her lips. "Thank you, Your Grace. It was delicious."

He chuckled. "Will you ever stop calling me 'Your Grace,' I wonder?"

"It's just habit at this point." Phoebe reached up and swiped a stray hair out of her face. "What was that food?"

"Cinnamon bread. I just felt like eating something sweet, you know?" Saying this, he finished the last of his toast.

"You should watch yourself. If you keep eating sweets like that, you'll start looking like a hog."

He decided not to tell her that he could change his body image with Wordsmithing — too much trouble.

As they approached the border wall, Neil waved to a group of three men standing over a dead firepit, holding mugs and chatting about something he couldn't quite make out. The heat of the midday sun was extraordinary, yet they didn't look even moderately uncomfortable.

"I've brought our King!" Neil gestured to him. "Sir Jason Hiro, I'd like to introduce you to my three closest friends, and the commanders of your military. Makoto Ueda, Hans Wagner, and Saul."

He nodded politely at the three men as they turned around and made different gestures. Makoto bowed, Hans raised his metal cup, and Saul merely nodded and clasped his hands together. He didn't have anything in his hands, so he guessed he wasn't much of a drinker.

"Uh, pleased to meet all of you," he said, bowing stiffly. Phoebe curtsied politely.

"Ja, ees our pleasure," the first man, Hans Wagner, said. While he appeared to him only as a red blob, he had grown more adept at telling clothing apart. He looked to be wearing a prim and proper uniform, something with a star pinned over his heart. One thing he had learned was that most people on Tarus II came from diverse backgrounds, and many were born in entirely different time periods. This guy seemed Germanic, based on the thick mustache and how his hair was slicked back carefully to preserve a military look.

"I have waited a long time to meet you, King Hiro," the next man, Saul, said. While he was curious as to why Neil didn't include Saul's last name, there was probably a good reason for that. He wore folds of robes wrapped around his body in various ways, a style he had come to associate with ancient Rome or earlier. He might be from pre-Christ times, even. Based on the thick and long beard and mustache, he either came from a time when men didn't have many ways to shave, or he loved long hair.

"It is big honor to meet you, kind leader," the final man said. Unlike the other two, he could immediately tell what his accent was. Since his name was Makoto Ueda, it was immediately apparent he was Japanese, though he couldn't guess in what period he had been born. His garb was that of a male kimono, and based on the katana at his waist, as well as the chomage hairdo, he was likely born before the start of the Industrial Revolution.

The Crown was helpful at times like these. It filled in the blanks from extraneous and inferred information, making his life much more straightforward.

He bowed once again after they introduced themselves, though he couldn't help but feel slightly awkward. All of these men were, quite literally, a hundred thousand years old, yet they treated him like he was a king. He wasn't even nineteen!

Turning to Neil, he asked, "What was it you wanted to talk about?"

Saul raised his free hand. "We wish to work on a comprehensive reformation of human and monster society. The sooner, the better. The current structure is ugly at best, and a crippling weakness at worst."

"All of society?" He raised an eyebrow. "I don't follow. I thought this was about the military."

Hans replied, "Ja, the military ees in a sorry state, Mein Fuhrer, but zee infrastructure ees-"

"Whoa, whoa, hold up." He raised a hand faster than he expected. "Err, what did you call me?"

Judging from how his face shifted, he looked incredibly confused. "M-Mein Fuhrer?"

Neil nudged him. "It means 'leader' in German, his native tongue."

His stomach roiled. "I know what it means. I-I just... I don't feel comfortable being... being called... _that_."

Hans glanced at Ueda, who shrugged in response, before looking back at him. "Ja, very sorry then. Would you prefer being called Mein Lieber? Perhaps Herr Hiro?"

Now he felt sorry for bringing it up. Maybe he should have kept his mouth shut. "Uh, Mein Lieber is fine, I suppose. It's just... 'Fuhrer' has a connotation with a particular man that makes me uncomfortable."

Hans cocked his head curiously. "I... I do not understand. Who?"

He swallowed hard even thinking his name. "Adolf Hitler. He probably came after your time."

"Oh!" Hans smiled eagerly. "Ja, ja! I knew him, of course. He was Mein Fuhrer."

It took him a moment to compile his thoughts. "Y-you knew- that is, you worked for... Adolf Hitler?"

Hans nodded eagerly, apparently unaware of his feelings toward the man. "Ja. Zee Fuhrer was a man with ambitions for power. I did not get to see zee end of zee war, but it would not surprise me if he won. Sadly, such days are far in zee past."

He felt Phoebe's gaze burning into the side of his head. "Are you okay?"

His face turned pale. He couldn't help his reaction. This Hans guy was a Nazi!

_Oh, god. He should have paid more attention! How could he possibly allow a Nazi to establish himself in a leadership position?!_

"Ahh..." He tried to speak, but the words wouldn't come out. He was the only person here, aside from Hans, who knew about what the Nazis did. Phoebe and Neil were both from the ancient past, as were Saul and Makoto, most likely. He was the only one who realized there was a monster in their midst, one wearing human skin.

Hans began to notice his feelings of anger. He reached up and wiped his forehead. "Mein Lieber... have I done something to offend you?"

"You worked for the Nazis..." He managed to breathe words out. "You... how many people did you kill?"

Hans' cheerful demeanor lessened. "Me? None! I was in zee logistics division!" He trailed off for a moment and tilted his head to his side, staring aimlessly at nothing in particular. "Your reaction does bring a few questions to mind..."

"Did you know?" He asked. "About the death camps?"

Hans's entire face decreased in temperature by a degree or two. "I had heard of them, but never visited. I assumed they were just rumors."

"They were real. People died. Millions."

It took a long minute before Hans finally responded. "I was only a lieutenant. I just did whatever zee Fuhrer ordered. It was zee day when I was ordered to kill an innocent child that I couldn't follow orders anymore. I left my post."

Phoebe raised her hand up to her mouth. "W-what? You murdered a child?"

She wasn't the only one shocked by this sudden admission. Neil, Makoto, and Saul all took a step back, unsure of what to think. Hans quickly continued, "Nein, nein! I did not kill him. Instead, I joined a resistance group and began ferrying people out of zee country. For this, zee Schutzstaffel slaughtered my wife and child."

A clearer picture appeared in his head. "You don't think rescuing a few Jews makes it okay that you killed others, do you? You can't possibly atone for-"

"I did not kill anyone!" Hans suddenly yelled, catching him by surprise. "Please do not look at me with such hate-filled eyes. Things are not so simple when zee leader of zee military rules with an iron fist! Zee Schutzstaffel were always watching! Everyone was an enemy!"

His lips turned dry. He wet them before continuing. "I understand. You were merely following orders. I'm sure that helps you sleep at night."

Hans crossed his arms and straightened up to look at him. "I do not ask forgiveness, Mein Lieber. If you think executing me will make zee world a better place, so be it. Throw me in prison, if you must. I am not zee man I was back then. I was a young fool."

"To say the least."

The two of them stared at each other for a few moments. He, with his unseeing eyes, and he with the gaze only a monster could understand. What lurked in his mind, he wondered?

He didn't know what to do. Hans had saved Jews, or so he said. It had been 100,000 years as well. Should he just ignore his tainted past? If he worked under the Nazis, then could he ever trust him not to be tainted by their sins? If he kept him as a military leader, wouldn't that make him as sick as America during Operation Paperclip? They took on Nazi scientists to create new weapons, allowing the men to get away with war crimes.

But... then again... it _had_ been 100,000 years. He wasn't God. He couldn't judge a man by a past he didn't even know. Maybe he should keep an eye on him but use his potential expertise.

There was no right answer, here. He liked to think of himself as a compassionate and forgiving person, though. Jailing a man for ancient sins wasn't in his character, especially not executing him.

"Alright. Hans, what you did was despicable. Unforgivable. If you ever do anything that gives me even a whiff of your origins, it's over for you, understand? But, for now, I can't sentence you for a crime nobody but you remembers. You have to live with the guilt for the rest of your life, so I can only hope that's punishment enough."

Hans nodded meekly and bowed his head. "You are most gracious, Mein Lieber."

Somehow, he managed to force a grim smile. "By the way, Germany lost the war. We tortured and executed every single person who committed a war crime. None escaped judgment."

The fact that he could tell a little lie here and Hans wouldn't know the difference made him feel slightly better. It wasn't true, after all. Thousands of SS officers and camp leaders got off scot-free and lived long, healthy lives. Hans didn't have to know that, though.

To his surprise, Hans smiled back. "Ja. I had hoped that would be zee case. Perhaps I should have been counted among zee bodies, eh?"

"Yeah. Maybe. What happened, anyway? Why didn't you see the end of the war?"

Hans shrugged. "Unfortunate circumstances. I was fleeing zee Schutzstaffel when I encountered a horrifying monster before zee France border. She was Bahamut, of course. You can guess zee rest."

"Mmm. Yeah."

There was an awkward silence as the other men and Phoebe evaluated Hans. Based on her initial impression, he got the idea that Phoebe didn't like him, but mostly because he didn't. She tended to echo his views most of the time. The other three merely regarded Hans with a new level of curiosity. They would probably be prodding him a _lot_ later when this meeting was over.

Saul cleared his throat. "That was dreadfully uncomfortable to watch. Shall we get back to the main topic, then?"

He waved his hand absentmindedly. "Sure, sure. Uh, reformation, right?"

"Yes. I've spent a lot of time talking with the people and monsters here. Morale is low because most people live in primitive huts. As I was saying before, I'd like to work on improving our infrastructure. If we can construct additional-"

"Oy!" A voice called out from above them, interrupting Saul. He angrily looked up in the air at a guard in the tower. "What is it?! I'm trying to speak!"

"Red flare!" The scout yelled out two words, and immediately Saul's attitude shifted. Everyone except for Phoebe and him jumped into high gear. Neil started barking out orders. "The Core is under attack! Gather the horses! Riders assemble! Get your weapons ready! On the double!"

Phoebe looked at him, fear in her eyes. "Jason! We have to do something! Fly over there and help, hurry!"

He grimaced. _Even if he flew, it would take too long. He needed a faster mode of transportation, and stat._

"I have a better idea. Phoebe, you're coming with me. Grab a sword and shield while you wait. This'll take me a minute at most."

He shot one last look at Hans as he ran to a nearby stable with several horses. With a shake of his head, he cast aside the disturbing thoughts in his mind and turned his attention to the empty patch of grass at his side.

"**Car!**"

A moment later, Phoebe stared at the red metal monster before him. A Ferrari, something he had always dreamed about in his youth, but this one was a little different. It packed a fusion engine and could travel at up to 200 miles per hour. It was a four-seater, too.

He cracked open the passenger door for Phoebe with his telekinesis, while jumping in on the driver's side. "Let's go! We're leaving!"

Phoebe stared quizzically at the vehicle. "How?"

He patted the passenger seat after closing his door. "There's no time to explain! Get in the car!"

She didn't question his command. Ordinarily, a Ferrari would be a tight fit, but the Crown allowed him to custom build this one with lots of leg room. As she took a seat, he gently moved her sword and shield to rest at her feet with his telekinesis, then he pulled her seatbelt over her and shut her door.

"Oh, thank you, Your Grace. Err, what is this... metal box?"

"It's our ticket to that gate in one minute flat. **Cement!**"

Aiming his mind ahead, he paved the entire path several miles forward to the warpgate, then cleared any debris off the road with another word. "**Sweep!**"

A moment later, he pressed the ignition button. _Heh, no keys needed._

The car roared to life like a pack of angry lions, and Phoebe's face flushed. "By the gods! What in the Heavens was that sound?!"

"Horsepower!" He stomped the accelerator, and a moment later realized he forgot his seatbelt. He was thrown backward by the sheer force of acceleration. Phoebe screeched in terror as they went from a standstill to sixty miles an hour in under two seconds, rapidly jumped to a hundred, then a hundred and fifty. The terrified screams from the passenger side of the car only fueled his macho instincts. This was more fun than anything he had done in his life!

It only took them two minutes of roaring engines before they reached the warpgate, and he slammed on the brakes. 

_Screech!_

The moment he hit the brakes, his body flung forward, and he slammed against the horn. Naturally, he had modified it to blare deep and loud like the horn on a cruise-ship, and the sound made Phoebe scream in terror even louder.

The car ground to a stop just a hundred feet from the shrine. Once it halted, the sudden inertia slammed him back against his seat, and it took several moments before the adrenaline stopped racing through his body. He turned to smile at Phoebe, only to see a look of anguish and nausea on her face. 

"...out! L-let me out!" Phoebe pounded on the window and held her mouth, so he opened the door with telekinesis and yanked her seatbelt off. The moment she was free, Phoebe stumbled out of the car, past the pavement, and onto the grass and began vomiting.

His smile faded. 

_What the hell is wrong with me?_

He quickly clambered out of the car and ran around to her side. "Phoebe? Phoebe! Are you okay?"

"Bleugh! Gwuuh!" She coughed out even more vomit, getting some of it on her white hair. Her entire body shook, and she started crying uncontrollably as more vomit bubbled up.

He had never seen her in a state like this. Shit. He quickly leaned down and patted her back. "I'm here, Phoebe. I'm so sorry!" It occurred to him that now was the time to Wordsmith, so he spoke again. "**Settle. Clean. Mints.**"

He didn't do anything to stop her crying, but he could at least settle her stomach and clean up the vomit. She continued spasming for another minute before leaning against him and crying into his shirt. "Oh... oh god! I was scared! Terrified! That th-thing is a monster!"

"It's my fault, Phoebe. I'm sorry..." He patted her head and let her cry for a moment as he looked up at the portal. He couldn't see anything inside of it with his infrared or sound perception, so he had no idea what was going on in there. Quickly, he pulled Phoebe away. "Stay here. I need to see what's going on in the Core. When you feel better, follow after me."

She nodded rapidly, but her motions were almost delirious. "Uh... uh huh... huh..."

He pulled himself away from her, though prying her hands from his shirt without hurting her was a small challenge. After leaving her behind, he jumped up to the portal and noticed a single human waiting for him, anxiety written all over his face. He stared at the Ferrari for several moments before looking at him. 

"Sir!" The man snapped into a salute. "It's chaos in there! We need the regiments at once!"

"What happened?"

"Shadow-walker attack, sir! Dozens of them! We believe the Demon Lords have rallied together to attack us!"

He pushed past him, feeling anxious at stepping into a portal when he couldn't see anything on the other side for once. "Uh, stay here and help that woman. Phoebe is feeling ill because of... reasons. I'll be back."

He stepped toward the portal, his senses at the ready.

_Whatever was on the other side, it had better be ready to die._
