Saul rubbed his arm as they strolled through the superheated biosphere. "Are you sure you don't need a T-REX, commander? What about the little one?"

"I Wordsmithed protection over us. No sweat."

Daisy gave him a thumbs up. "Daddy's magic is the best!" _Her cute smile usually makes most people chuckle, but the miserable glint doesn't leave Saul's eyes,_ Jason thought.

Solomon wrapped his ghostly arm around his nephew as though they were best friends. "My boy, tell us how the crops are yielding!"

"Yes, sir..." Saul flippantly gestured toward a multi-level shelf with a dozen flowers encased in glass. "Uhm, the third generation grows much faster than the previous two, and we've taught it how to shape itself into different forms. Leopold thought it would look more decorative if we had some flowers hanging around..."

Solomon nodded enthusiastically. "Hm, hm, yes, I see. So, these are nanite flowers?"

"Yes."

Saul didn't elaborate. _He doesn't need to since we already know the details,_ Jason thought. _Growing self-replicating nanites will quadruple humanity's output of exosuits since our existing replicators can't keep up with the demand._

Jason nudged Saul with his elbow. "How long does the growing process take?"

He shrugged. "Our current capacity is, uh, maybe five million nanites a day. That's enough for one T-REX at most."

"Can you scale production up to meet demand?"

"We're working on that. The problem is that nanites require organic matter to expedite their growth process. The more, the better, and we don't have enough."

"Oh..." Jason muttered, while tapping his lip. "I see the problem. I guess you can't go chop down a tree outside."

Saul tapped his fingers in the air against invisible buttons only he could see. "If you want accelerated nanite growth, then I'll need a system built for regular deliveries from Tarus II. Plants we grow up here are only enough to sustain the lunar personnel."

Solomon rolled his eyes. "Yes, yes, but enough about the nanites! How are you, dearest nephew? Have you been eating well?" The king's ghostly arm seemed to weigh like an anvil on his nephew's shoulders. Saul slumped visibly.

"I'm fine, uncle. Let's stick to important matters."

"Your health is at the top of my priority list!"

"_Swell._"

Solomon snapped his fingers. "Jason, procure that item for me."

_It takes me a minute to remember what he's referring to,_ Jason thought. "**Summon.**"

A small, ornate box materialized in his hand. Jason opened it to reveal a necklace with gold, jewels, and a big, fat ruby hanging at the end.

Solomon grinned eagerly. "A beautiful necklace suited perfectly to your tastes!"

Saul stared at it blankly through his tinted visor. "Ah... aren't necklaces meant for... for women?"

"Psh, nonsense. After six years, you continue to turn down gold and jewelry? Your tormentors must have shattered your mind beyond recognition."

Saul bowed his head. "I've always been like this."

The king winked at Jason and made a cutting motion across his neck. Jason shrugged and put the jewels back in his infinite storage while Solomon guided his nephew away so the two of them could have a private chat.

Daisy and Jason stayed behind. She turned toward the nanite flowers and poked the glass container. The vibrations caused them to bend and twist. "Wow, daddy. These flowers are pretty."

Jason crinkled his nose, but she didn't notice. "I suppose they look... neat."

_To me, they look hideous. Silvery tendrils of metal bend and twist depending on how the sunlight strikes them. If one watches long enough, they might notice the flowers slowly increasing in size as time goes on. Excess nanites get pruned and sent through a tube above the box into a collection facility,_ Jason thought.

_Hmph. The entire production of this biosphere only creates enough nanites to fill one exosuit's reserves. Worse, we don't merely have to develop new T-REX's, but also to repair damaged ones. When demons attack and damage the suits, a percentage of nanites break apart and fizzle into dust, leaving the wearer exposed. The last thing I want is for our troops to have weak points for the demons to strike,_ Jason thought.

The flower inside the glass bent and twisted around. At one point, it mimicked Daisy's figure and appeared to grow two wings, making her squeal with delight and poke the glass even more. "Eee! Look, daddy, it made me an angel!"

Jason glanced at Solomon and Saul as they chatted about something privately a few dozen feet away. "That's right, sweetheart. You're my little angel."

Daisy pouted. "You're not even looking."

Jason turned back to stare at the flower. "Sure, I am. You're still my angel, though."

That seemed to please her. Daisy returned to poking the glass now and then while Jason waited for Solomon and his nephew to stroll back. Saul sighed heavily. "Alright... give me the necklace."

"Haha, atta boy!" Solomon laughed. "I'll beat your memories back into you if I must!"

Saul held his palm out. Jason summoned the box back and placed it in his hand. "Keep it secret. Keep it safe."

He responded with a quizzical stare. "What?"

"Nothing."

Solomon swaggered back to Jason's side. "Don't lose it now, boy. That's a present from your favorite uncle!"

"And me..." Jason muttered. "I crafted it with Wordsmithing..."

"Pish, Jason. The craftsmanship was all my design. You were merely the tool I used to form it."

"I'm glad you keep me in such high regards," Jason snapped back. Solomon gave him a thumbs-up in return.

Saul tapped more invisible buttons in the air, and a compartment opened on his exosuit. He stuffed the necklace inside and exhaled when his suit closed. "Let's return to the other matters. Commander, have you checked the experimental weapons division?"

"Not yet. I only got here a little while ago."

"Right... well, Hans mentioned a new 'nanite bomb' he was working on, as well as a portable healing nanite cocktail for downed soldiers. It could cut fatalities in half, or better."

"Hans is on the moon, too? Since when?"

"Three months ago. Haven't you been up here multiple times? You didn't hear?"

Jason shook his head. "No. I thought he still worked at the Rust Bucket."

"All our best scientists transferred here. You can't blame the man for following the trail of innovation."

"You're not wrong..." Jason sighed. "Haah... I prefer to know about major personnel changes. Why didn't anyone tell me?"

"You're a busy leader, Jason." Saul bowed his head. "You work hard enough as it is, managing Hero City and the colony. Nobody wanted to bother you over something so trivial. Maybe it's time you made another clone to help out?"

Jason shook his head vigorously. "No. Absolutely not. My first clone worked out poorly enough. Hope won't even talk to me these days. The last thing I need is another one running around and causing trouble."

Solomon rested his arm on Jason's shoulder. "Especially if my suspicions are correct."

"Yeah, well, I hope you're not."

"As am I."

Daisy reached up to grab Jason's hand. "Is something wrong, daddy?"

Jason smiled. "Nah, It's probably nothing, sweetheart."

She grinned widely. "Dope!"

Jason's smile faltered. "Uh... what did you say?"

"Hella dope!"

"Who taught you those words?"

Daisy's grin expanded further. "You!"

Solomon rubbed his face. "She continues to pick up your verbosity."

Jason groaned under his breath.

_In the end, words only have power when people give it to them. If I tell Daisy not to say them, she'll use them anyway, since that's what kids do. It's best I treat words as though they were unimportant,_ Jason thought.

_Heh... words not having power... ironic, coming from a Wordsmith,_ Jason thought.

Jason patted Daisy's shoulder. "Alright, sweetheart. Ready to go check out the weapon facility with daddy?"

She shook her head. "I wanna go home."

"Huh? Why?"

"I'm hungry."

"Oh."

Solomon chuckled. "Let's get her something to eat in the cafeteria. Kids quickly tire of adult talk."

"Aww, Solomon..." Jason smiled sweetly at the king. "I thought you didn't care."

The king swiveled his head away. "Hmph. I simply don't want to hear her whine."

"Suuure."

......................................................

An hour later, Daisy patted her stomach, satisfied with the scrumptious meal served in the cafeteria. Even compared to Yamir's cooking, this place wasn't bad at all. _The French-style garlic bread tingles on my tongue long after I finish it off,_ Jason thought.

The three of them traveled through an elevator deep into the heart of the moon. _Down here, there are dozens of levels of protection in case any experiments go wrong. The top-secret facility, Black Star, is a place I keep as hush-hush as possible, so the demons don't learn its purpose,_ Jason thought. _Solomon's Crown allows me to bypass many limits, but even I am not omnipotent. I have energy restrictions regarding what I can create. Until I find a way to skirt those constraints, making humanity produce what it needs is essential,_ Jason thought.

The floor number didn't tick down. The elevator only went from the first floor to the basement, but the wait time for them to descend into the bowels of the planet took forever. Daisy hopped on one foot to pass the time, and within a few minutes, she lost interest in that too.

"Daddy, how much longer?"

Jason shrugged. "**Visualize.** Probably three more minutes."

"How long is three minutes?"

"Not long."

That satisfied her. She hopped around some more, but not thirty seconds later, she squeezed Jason's pant leg. "How much longer now?"

"Uh..."

Immediately, her annoyance level tripled. "Come ooon! Te-puh-lort us!"

"You mean teleport?"

"Yeah!"

"You just want me to use my magic."

"Mhm!!"

Jason scratched his chin. "Mommy says I shouldn't. Teleporting might result in us getting stuck in a wall."

"Then unstuck us!"

"We'll, uh... die. Instantly."

"Die?"

Jason glanced at Solomon. "How do I explain the concept of death to a little girl?"

A look of brilliance entered Solomon's eye. "Don't worry. I know what to say."

_Immediately, I begin to worry,_ Jason thought.

The king knelt to Daisy's level. "Young one, have you ever slept so soundly that you couldn't wake up?"

Daisy shrugged. "I dunno."

"Well, death is a lot like eternal sleep. People die when they are killed. There's no pain or sadness involved, but you can't ever wake up and see your mother and father again."

Daisy's eyes widened. "I can't?"

"Nope. Death is bad, so you have to do what you can to avoid it."

Solomon rose to his feet. _I feel relieved that his description didn't send waves of panic coursing through my daughter's heart,_ Jason thought. Even so, she wrapped her arms around his leg and squeezed. "I don't wanna die!"

Jason patted Daisy's head. "Alright. Then let's wait patiently for this elevator."

The next two minutes passed quickly. Soon, the door slid open, and they traveled down several winding hallways, past various chambers with several guns and such being tested. One included the modified railgun, which now shot rapid-fire. Jason stopped with Daisy to watch through a foot of bulletproof glass as an automated system unloaded shells at lightspeed that bored through the moon's surface a mile deep, obliterating a test dummy instantly.

Daisy's jaw dropped. "Wow... that's hella dope..."

_I'm glad she likes guns, I guess,_ Jason thought.

They continued onward until finally reaching the primary chamber of the facility. Dozens of glass windows revealed military tests with extensive soundproofing to allow observation. Jason spotted Hans immediately, as he swore at a lower-ranked officer. "No, you bloody imbecile! Zees part goes in _zere_, and zat part goes over _zere_! Must I do all zee work around here?" He nudged a disassembled gun with his foot and grumbled even more."Who taught you to be an engineer?!"

The younger officer gave him a look of pain. "Ahh... you did, sir. I'm from the liberated colonies. I barely understand this stuff..."

"Well, learn faster! Zee last thing vee need is for our veapons to fail because you blocked zee barrels!"

The wiry German turned to storm away, only to spot Daisy and Jason standing behind him. He stopped mid-stride and stumbled awkwardly. "O-oh! Mister Hiro! I was not expecting you!"

Jason smiled awkwardly. "Yeah. Sorry to interrupt... Saul told me you had something new to report."

Hans swept his hands through his combover. His eyes stared at Jason like a deer in the headlights. Just as quickly as confusion passed over his face, he figured out Jason's meaning. "Ah, yes, zee new bombs! Come, come, you will love zees!"

He beckoned, and they followed. Daisy hid behind Jason as they walked. _She doesn't like Hans, since he 'talks and looks funny.' I suppose she has a point, but I hope he isn't offended,_ Jason thought.

They followed Hans down the path until arriving at a rack filled with metallic black orbs, each with a red button on top. Hans grinned hugely and plucked one off the shelf and held it up like Rafiki with Simba. "Behold! Zee nanite bomb! A weapon of chaos, designed to terrorize zee enemy legions!"

Jason raised an eyebrow. "That sounds... lethal."

"Indeed, indeed. Zees bomb will chew through organic matter in seconds, evaporating hundreds of demons at once!"

Jason scratched his head. "That's good, I suppose. I thought I told you to prioritize non-lethal weapons where possible."

"Naturally. However, zees weapon has several modes! Look over here at zee test chamber."

Hans pointed to Jason's right, inside yet another glass-walled facility. Jason followed his finger and watched as he pulled out a pad from a bag at his side and tapped several buttons. A moment later, the floor opened inside the chamber, and two dummies appeared. One had a T-REX wrapped around it, while the other did not.

Hans pressed a few more buttons, and a man entered the room from a door in the rear while holding one of the nanite bombs. He activated the orb, which caused it to glow, threw it at the dummies, then quickly exited before the bomb detonated.

A cloud of black 'gas' exploded from the bomb. It moved and twisted unnaturally, forming into a giant hand. The hand jerked toward the dummy without a T-REX, and lunged at it, wrapping around like a snake coiling its prey. The nanite swarm hissed and chewed at the dummy like a swarm of enraged fire-ants. Within seconds, the false skin eroded and melted away, fizzling into dust on the ground. The swarm intensified, growing denser after eating the dummy, and began to circle the room, looking for an escape.

_Something about the ferocity of the nanites forms a pit in my stomach,_ Jason thought. "H-hey, is it done?"

Hans nodded eagerly. "Oh, yes! Yes! Did you see? It devoured zee enemy combatant in seconds, leaving nothing behind, not even bone! Think of how many demons vee could kill with zees weapon!"

"Yeah. I am thinking about it. That's the problem."

The swarm lunged around, here and there, before finally slowing to a halt and reforming itself into an orb-like shape. It solidified once again, and the room entrance opened. Jason noticed uneasily that the orb was now around twenty percent bigger than it was before.

Solomon leaned on Hans's shoulder. "Impressive. Humans tried developing something like this during the Energy Wars, but the energy costs were too high."

Hans nodded. "Indeed! Developing nanite strands takes gigawatts of energy! Zee most efficient way to grow them is by feeding them organic matter, but even zat takes time. Zee wonderful thing about zees bombs is their versatility! What you saw just now was zee kill mode, but zey also have a mode that only devours spiritual energy, leaving demons unconscious. Vee can capture and interrogate prisoners using zee secondary mode."

Slight relief warmed Jason's blood again. "Ah, so the nanites _do_ have non-lethal options. That's good... I guess... but Hans, these nanites seem extremely dangerous. I've read enough sci-fi to know you don't simply unleash self-replicating nanobots against biological worlds. I'm sorry, but I think you should discontinue these. The other weapons are more than good enough."

Jason turned to look at Hans. His face turned ashen. "W-what? Of course, they're dangerous! Dangerous to our enemies! Have no fear, Jason, for zee nanites ignore humans and anyone wearing an exosuit modulator!"

"I'm not talking about danger to humans, Hans. I'm talking about... about..." Jason flippantly waved his hand at the pile of dust inside the test chamber. "The demons! I couldn't stomach killing anyone in such a horrible way. Besides, if we use biological weaponry against the demons, that might make them even crueler! We need to be better than our enemies. The last thing I want is to stoop to their level."

Solomon sighed. "It's war, Jason. They're demons. You need to get past this idea of singing Kumbaya and-"

Cutting Solomon off, Jason held his hand up. "No. I won't. It's not about getting along and treating demons like they're our best friends. It's about de-escalating our conflict and perhaps someday finding a middle ground. Violence begets violence; you know that. If we escalate with weapons like these nanite bombs, that will be the perfect excuse for them to annihilate us. We haven't a chance of standing up against the demons if they get serious."

Silence followed. Solomon averted his gaze from Jason's. Hans lowered his eyes to stare at the nanite bomb in his hand sullenly. "...If that is how zee commander feels..."

"It is."

The disappointment on Hans's face was immeasurable. He sighed and put the bomb back on the rack. "Alright. Well, if you could do me a favor and tell zee commander, zat would be wonderful. I don't leave zee station much, so I won't be able to let him know. He won't be happy to hear your verdict."

Stunned, Jason gaped at Hans. "Neil told you to make these?"

"Indeed. You did not know?"

"No! When did he tell you to make these?"

Hans scratched his scraggly chin-hair. "Hmm... a few months ago? Saul's work on nanite shape-forming was a big boost to mein productivity."

_A strange feeling of treachery rings at the back of my mind. I told Hope and Neil about the weapons facility around that time, and then Hans came up here afterward? The timing is too convenient,_ Jason thought.

"Did, uh, did Neil ask you to work here?"

Hans nodded. "Yes, it was mein privilege! You know me! I enjoy tinkering and creating new things."

"I suppose you do. Well, you're our best weapons expert. Just, uh... in the future... let me know what projects you're working on."

Hans stroked his chin. "Ees there a problem? Did I do something wrong?"

"No. You're fine. I have to speak to Neil, though."

"Mmm. Neil, Saul, and I are all good friends. Jason, surely you must know his heart is in zee right place. Zee commander only wants what is best for humanity. You understand, right?"

"Yes, but I also know that what's best for humanity could be interpreted and twisted into a selfish desire for conquest and revenge. An eye for an eye makes the world blind."

Solomon rolled his eyes. "You repeat that axiom all the time, but in my experience, killing your enemies before they kill you leads to a longer life."

"Didn't you live during a time of harmony without war?"

Solomon shook his head. "Where demons exist, there can be no peace."

"If you say so." Jason sighed and patted Daisy's head. "Alright Hans, before I go, I'd like to see your other invention. Saul mentioned something about a portable healing solution for our troops?"

The fire reignited in Hans' eyes. "Oh, yes! By injecting nanites into zee bloodstream of downed soldiers, we can repair their bodies within moments, then retrieve them before they die! Come, come, I will show you!"

Jason nodded. "Alright. That invention sounds better than bombs."

Ten minutes later, Daisy, Solomon, and Jason all stepped into the elevator and headed back to the surface. Nobody said anything for over a minute until Daisy tugged at Jason's pant leg. "Hey... daddy?"

Jason knelt to her level and noticed a look of concern on her face. "Yeah? What's up, sweetheart?"

Daisy wrung her hands together for a moment. "Um... the smoke ate the person, right?"

"Yup. Did it scare you?"

"A little... does it hurt to be eaten?"

Jason chuckled wryly. "Yeah, I suppose it does. Demons eat people, after all. That's why they're our enemies."

"Oh..." Daisy scrunched up her face. "S-so... if the demons eat people, they die? And that's bad, right?"

"Mhm. Very bad."

"Will demons eat mommy?"

_The color drains from my face,_ Jason thought. "What? No! Of course not. I'd never let that happen."

"Will they eat me?"

"Ahaha... no. If demons ever go near you or mommy, they'll regret it. Daddy's really strong, you know."

Solomon spoke up beside Jason. "Oh? What will you do if the demons attack your family, Jason? I thought you were a pacifist."

Jason grabbed Daisy, lifted her onto his shoulders, and smiled. "I am. But if demons make the wrong move, I don't know what I'm capable of."

Solomon smiled back. "Interesting."

"What?"

He looked away, and his smile faded. "I thought the same thing once. Jason... I mentioned a terrible mistake in my past. It involved demons and my idealistic fantasies. Much like you, I thought I could reason with them. I was wrong."

Solomon went silent for a moment before bowing his head and closing his eyes. "You're on a path to despair, my friend. I don't advise their destruction due to an unquenchable bloodlust, but because of the same pragmatism you and I share. Once you make a mistake like mine... there is no turning back. It's best to learn from the errors of your ancestors, rather than to repeat them yourself."

_Darkness clouds my vision. Daisy leans against the back of my head and rests her chin on my scalp. The weight of my daughter hangs on my shoulders heavier than ever before,_ Jason thought.

"Maybe you're right, but you might also be wrong. You've given me an idea, though."

Solomon raised his head. "And what's that?"

"I should stop trying to guess the demon's motivations. I'll seek out the leader of the demons and test the waters. Perhaps this 'Ose' woman will listen to my request for a peace treaty."

"Pft." Solomon scoffed. "That's the dumbest thing you've ever said. Why would she agree to such a thing? The demons outnumber us a thousand, possibly even a hundred-thousand to one. A treaty is to our benefit and their detriment."

Jason smiled. "Because, Solomon... we have weapons beyond their imagination. If they choose to attack us, we will fight back. Both sides will lose countless lives. In the worst-case scenario, I can always retreat to the Core with my Wordsmithing if she tries to backstab me."

Solomon held up his hand. "I have to stop you there, Jason. Listen. You see other humans in a different light compared to how demons see their brethren. You think of your fellow humans as equal to yourself, brimming with potential. Demons respect power and nothing else. Perhaps you should talk to the angels in your rings if you want to know what happens when you... when you... make a deal with the _devil_."

Solomon went silent again, as did Jason. The two of them stared into each other's eyes, as he flashed images in Jason's mind. Brief, kaleidoscopic still frames of his life appeared and disappeared.

Half his family murdered.

Jerusalem scorched.

His explosive rage as he launched a campaign against those who betrayed him.

The rebuilding of his city, a thousand times mightier than before.

Solomon's final admonition to his sons on his deathbed.

_Never trust a demon._

Solomon's eyes hardened. "If you play with fire, it will burn you. Ask the Archangels what happened. You won't like the answer."

Slowly, Jason rubbed the rings on his right hand with his left.

"Fine. You've made your point. I'll ask Raphael."

"All I want, Jason, is for you to learn the difference between wisdom and knowledge."

"I will."
