Dreams of clouds and rabbits and the taste of Snicker bars gave way to the sensation of something touching Jason's nose.

Groggily, Jason swiped at it. 

"Fuggin' flies. Go away..."

The thing touched Jason's nose again. Jason swatted at it.

"Fugginnn hell..."

Suddenly an anvil slammed onto his stomach, blasting the wind from his body. Jason jerked up in bed, gasping for breath. The light of a supernova blasted his face thanks to Phoebe opening the goddamn blinds again.

"Wha-?! Kuhhh!"

Jason choked out a gasp and shielded his eyes from the light.

"Wake up, daddy! It's morning! Morning morning morning!!"

The little blonde-haired girl flopped off Jason's belly and rolled to the side as he pulled himself into a sitting position. Jason stared at her in annoyance. _Aw, man, can't you lemme nap?_ Jason thought.

"Noooo! You promised me the bestest birthday ever!" His daughter reached up and poked Jason's cheek. Jason summoned a second wind and breathed a little easier.

The door to Jason's room opened. Phoebe stepped inside. "Daisy! What are you doing? I told you to let daddy sleep. He's tired, honey."

Daisy rolled her eyes. "No, he _isn't_. He can wake up with _magic_."

Jason shrugged. A smile escaped his lips. "Well, she's not wrong. **Awake. Alert.**"

One minute later, Jason slid out of bed, his mind as sharp as if he'd slept like Rip Van Winkle. _Wordsmithing is life-changing in small ways one couldn't imagine._ Jason thought. "**Coffee.**"

A cup of Joe popped into Jason's hand, already the perfect temperature. He summoned his clothes and stretched hard, popping his back in the process. Phoebe headed into the bathroom to get ready for her big event. 

Jason was looking forward to a fun-filled day.

Daisy hopped around on the bed. "Come on! What's taking you so long?"

It was Jason's daughter's sixth birthday today. The terrible twos were a distant memory. Honestly, age four was the worst. That was when she started trying to snatch Solomon's Crown off Jason's dresser. If someone wore his crown, Jason couldn't take it off without their permission, unless they died. If Daisy ever got ahold of it, Jason would never get it back. She wouldn't even share snacks with other kids, let alone a sparkly all-powerful crown of knowledge.

"**Unlock.**" With a single word, Jason popped open the bedside drawer and snatched Solomon's artifact out, then placed it on his head. The soundproof dresser was one of Phoebe's ideas. Great for making sure that creepy guy couldn't listen in while Phoebe and Jason tried their hardest to make another baby.

Solomon materialized beside Jason. "Good sleep?"

"Eh, can't complain."

Daisy grabbed Jason's arm. "Where's uncle Sally? I wanna see hiiim!"

Solomon flinched. "Please don't."

"Aww. But Daisy _loves_ you, Solomon. **Project.**" 

Solomon's ghostly body materialized beside Jason, and Daisy squealed in delight. "Eee! Uncle Sally! Uncle Sally! Do you wanna play hopscotch?!"

"No."

"Chess! Let's play chess!!"

"No."

Solomon glared at Jason. His voice spoke in Jason's mind. _I hate it when you do that._ Solomon thought.

Jason was surprised when he learned a few years ago that Solomon detested children. He meant, the guy had had several kids of his own, but then again, in the Bible, Solomon had suggested cutting a baby apart and giving each half to two women. Jason'd have to be one hell of a sicko to imagine such a thing.

Daisy pounced on Jason's back and wrapped her arms and legs around him. "Piggyback! Piggyback!"

Jason nodded and held her legs up so she didn't fall. Predictably, she started tugging on Solomon's Crown, but it didn't go anywhere. "I wanna wear Sally's crown! Make me a princess, daddy!"

"Technically, you already are," Jason replied.

"Yeah, but I want a crown..."

"I'll tell you what, sweetie. When you're ten years old, I'll let you wear Solomon's Crown."

"What?! Really?"

"Yup."

"Promise?"

"Promise."

That satisfied Daisy. She returned to yapping at Solomon about something while Solomon gave Jason a thousand-yard stare, but Jason's eyes traveled elsewhere.

Phoebe stepped out of the bathroom dressed in a full track-suit. The skintight black fiber clung to every inch of her body, arousing Jason like it always did. She stopped in the doorway and met Jason's gaze. "Enjoying the view?"

"Y-yeah. Uh-huh."

"Sometimes, I wonder if you or Solomon are the bigger pervert."

"Me. Definitely me. You look great."

Phoebe opened the door and gestured outside. "After you. Eyes up here, Jason."

Daisy yanked on Jason's hair like a wild animal. "Mommy's big race! Zoom, zoom!"

_One hour later._

"Laaadiesss and gentlemen! Boys and girls! Kobolds and salamanders! Welcome to the Superstar Arena Megadrome Shooowdooown!!"

The racetrack announcer bellowed into his loudspeaker, sending excitement and cheers through the crowd. Daisy hopped around excitedly as the massive trucks in the arena below all began revving their engines. The roar of a dozen twenty-ton monster trucks was somehow noisier than the loudspeaker above.

A series of dirt hills and miniature lakes awaited the challengers. The circular racetrack extended around the entire border of the arena, which itself was three times the size of a football stadium. In the center of the track, every driver had a team of medical experts on call. While Jason couldn't see her, he knew Phoebe's best friend, Samantha, was watching in case Phoebe got in a wreck. Of course, Jason was also here and could heal Phoebe instantly, but it couldn't hurt to have a backup.

Jason rattled a bucket around. "Daisy. Want some popcorn?"

Hundreds of people around Jason screamed and shouted excitedly, distracting his daughter. She bounced up and down, trying to see over the head of a Harpie in front of them.

Daisy whined, "Where's mommy's truck? I can't seeeee it!"

Using his telekinesis, Jason levitated her a foot in the air. It was something he did often enough that she didn't bat an eye. "She's over there. The third one. It has the lightning bolts on the side."

Daisy flailed her finger toward Phoebe's truck. "I see it! I see it! It's the one with the zappers!"

"I just said that..."

Solomon sat beside Jason. He enjoyed being around people, but Phoebe's races didn't interest him much. "Children take forever to learn the value of listening."

Jason stuffed popcorn in his mouth. "Yeah. You're telling me."

Phoebe and Jason had conceived the idea of races several years ago. The demons suffered heavy losses thanks to Uriel's vicious attack, and realizing they weren't a threat, they retreated to the Labyrinth. Thanks to the unexpected peace, they'd had six straight years without war. It afforded Jason the time to develop all sorts of defenses for their planet, but also recreational events.

Still, Jason tried to be humble. Jason always told people that he might be the Hero, but he'd prefer people didn't treat him like he was someone overly important. He could have built a special booth in the Megadrome for Daisy, Solomon, and himself, but he didn't want his daughter growing up with a royalty complex. He had his giant multi-story house and dozens of servants. That was good enough. And besides, in Hero City, everyone lived like kings. No beggars. No impoverished people. Only a few drunkards and loudmouths. It was all thanks to the power of Wordsmithing.

Jason was surprised everything had worked out so well.

Solomon poked Jason. His finger felt like a tingle of ice on Jason's skin. "Jason. We have to visit the colony today. I know it's Daisy's birthday, but we need to improve its biodiversity dramatically."

"Right, right. It can wait a day, can't it?"

Daisy ignored them. She continued to screech and point at Phoebe's truck, yelling things like, "Go, mommy! Vroom, vroom!"

"I suppose. Why not frame it like a birthday present? You haven't shown Daisy the colony yet, have you? I bet she'd like it."

Jason rolled his eyes. "Oh, sure. _Now_ you're the expert on children."

"Hey. I've had my share of ankle-biters. That's why I can't stand them!"

"You're just a grumpy, frumpy old man."

Solomon didn't protest. In the distance, the engines suddenly roared to life. 

The announcer yelled again. "Alright, everyone! Today, the undefeated twelve-time champion, Phoebe Hiro, is up against her arch-nemesis, the blood-bathed, demon-loving, baby-eating monster! That's right; she's facing off against none other than Ogie, the most despicable salamander to ever hit the dirt!!"

The crowd booed and howled. Daisy joined in. "Boo! Uncle Ogre is a big fat meanie!!"

Jason snickered. The racing events in Hero City reminded him of wrestling from his time. There were tales of triumphs, losses, rivalries, and all sorts of things like that. It was all part of a series of running gags, and everyone was in on it — even a six-year-old girl.

A minotaur at the front line howled violently. "ON YOUR MARRRKS! THREEE! TWOOO! OOONE! GOOOOOO!"

His cry lit a fire under the tires of every truck on the line. The racers accelerated and immediately hit the first bump, sailed ten feet into the air and slammed the ground with enough force to rattle the racers' teeth. Naturally, all of the trucks had internal momentum compensators to assist the drivers, but Phoebe was such a rabid competitor that she turned hers off. She raced for the thrills, and also because she was a bit over-zealous.

_If I had known seven years ago what I know now, I'd never have been able to picture Phoebe's transformation into such a sexy badass._ Jason thought. _She used to be demure and polite, but I suppose looking back, there was always a sparkle of bloodthirst in her eyes._

The first lap went mostly as planned. The lakes of water on the opposite side of the stadium exploded outward when the racers hit them, showering the screaming fans with filthy mud. Jason usually positioned himself far away from the tracks, as did the older members of their community. Jason was in no mood for a mud bath.

The last-place driver suddenly jerked to the right and slammed against the concrete and metal wall. Jason inspected the damage with his Wordsmithing, which revealed a twisted axle. Jason quickly repaired it and sent him a telepathic message. _You're good to go, Sam. Don't fall further behind!_

_Thank you, Your Majesty!_

_I hate it when people call me that._ Jason thought.

Sam reversed and rushed back into the action, but he'd already fallen pretty far behind. 

Another icy touch on Jason's arm made him turn to Solomon. "What?"

The king stared at Jason for a moment. "Have you spoken with Hope lately?"

"Ugh. I'm trying to watch the race."

"Jason..." Solomon trailed off. He looked away. "You always dodge the question."

Jason shrugged and ignored him. It didn't matter if Solomon pestered him. Hope and Jason barely talked anymore. Hell, it'd been three months since Jason last saw his face. Hope had a villa on Maiura, and so did Neil. Hope had his life, and Jason had his. That was all there was to it.

Jason exhaled slowly, releasing something that wasn't quite a sigh. _Where did I go wrong with Hope?_ Jason thought. _You'd think a clone of myself would be the easiest person in the universe to get along with. Instead, he's the complete opposite of me._

_Eh. Perhaps I should drop by the Core later. It's been a while. I haven't seen Kar for at least a month. Couldn't hurt to say hello._ Jason thought.

Phoebe's truck zoomed past, and Daisy cried out. "Mommy! That's mommy! I saw her, daddy!"

"Yes, you did, sweetheart."

Absentmindedly, Jason checked his multi-purpose watch. The race would continue for another thirty minutes. Jason had a lot to do today, though. Daisy's birthday party... her human and monster friends would be over later... then Jason had to visit the colony... Wordsmithing training with Solomon...

_Jeez. I can't believe I'm thinking about something other than Phoebe's race._ Jason thought.

_There's so much to do and so little time in the day._ Jason thought.

_An hour later._

In the back of Jason's mind, a silent dread lurked. It had been there ever since Jason built Hero City. Jason was from a different time, a different world. Earth. A planet inhabited primarily by humans. Jason came from a world where people once looked down on each other for minor differences — the color of their skin, political affiliations, religion, and a thousand other discrepancies. 

But... that wasn't the case here, on Tarus II. Humans and monsters lived side-by-side in all-but-perfect harmony. Why judge a man for looking a little ugly compared to other humans when beautiful and disgusting-looking monsters surrounded you?

It wasn't that differences didn't exist. They did. Humans and monsters living on Tarus II knew they had a shared enemy living in the Labyrinth. In some form, Jason believed demons weren't all that bad. Yes, they devoured the flesh of humans and absorbed souls of the departed to gain power, but they weren't inhuman unthinking freaks. Paimon demonstrated that fact, as did Samantha every day.

But... this dread never escaped Jason. Jason was different. Jason tried not to judge others based on how they looked, but it happened unintentionally. What if Jason passed his biases onto Daisy? What if Jason let a joke slip that came off as insensitive or offended people? Jason wasn't some nobody on the street; Jason was the figurehead of their society.

That dread hit Jason in waves as Daisy stared up at the minotaur standing beside them on the bus. The moment she opened her mouth, Jason knew she was going to say something horribly embarrassing.

"Daddy, why do his horns bend all weird like that? He looks different from the other meenaturrs."

Horns, for minotaurs, were a sensitive subject. They had a whole religion about them and how their length and complexity determined the rightful successor to any given clan. 

This big fellow's horns bent slightly downward, which was very different from Yamir's, the proud cook at Jason's house. Jason didn't know a lot about what different horn-shapes meant, but taking the bus was usually reserved for those without a vehicle. Nobody in Hero City lived poorly, but those who took the bus tended to have fewer possessions and prestige than their peers.

Solomon's Crown instantly filled in the blanks in Jason's knowledge regarding minotaur culture. Based on his horns, this one was probably an outcast. He was one of the few who came to Hero City freely, not someone who already lived here. He was from the Labyrinth.

The minotaur tilted his head down to stare at Daisy. Then, he looked at Jason. He must have noticed the look of panic in Jason's eyes because he merely shrugged. "Human whelps should learn to keep such observations to themselves."

"S-sorry. She didn't mean anything by it. Kids, am I right?"

The half-bull half-man quietly reached up and touched his horns. "My family cast me out for my deformity. I am six hundred years old. This world is the only place I've found acceptance in my entire life."

Jason nodded. "I'm glad to hear that. Sorry for Daisy's, erm, rudeness."

He turned away and stared out the windows at the massive buildings as they drove toward the northern side of the city. "Were it not for my deformity, I might not have found such a wonderful city. I wonder if perhaps it is a curse that eventually became a blessing."

Solomon, standing across from Jason, raised his eyebrows. "Do such things exist?"

The other passengers ignored their conversation. Several of them looked like they woke up only a few minutes ago.

"If they do, one may go to the grave without knowing."

Solomon nodded, satisfied by the minotaur's reply. One common theme Jason had noticed among their species was their eloquence and intelligence, not only among monsters, but in general. Minotaurs appeared brutish and powerful, but physical altercations between them were rarer than a blood-red moon. They preferred to draw, cook, and pursue their hobbies.

Still, their intelligence lacked in some other areas. The whole horn-superstition thing, for one. _Oh well, nobody's perfect._ Jason thought.

The bus slowed to a stop. Jason breathed a sigh of relief and nodded at the minotaur. "See you around."

"You as well, Your Majesty."

Jason always took the bus to try and keep Daisy as down-to-Tarus as possible. The last thing Jason wanted was for her to grow up a snob with communication issues. Jason didn't want her to look down her nose at others. It wasn't who Jason was, or who Phoebe was. It wasn't the lifestyle meant for their child.

But, at the same time, Jason wondered if he was doing exactly the opposite. _Is it normal for kids to make such uncivilized comments?_ Jason thought. _Did she somehow pick up the trait from me?_

"Daddy? What's wrong?" The bus drove away, leaving them behind. 

Jason blinked and stared at her. "Huh? What do you mean?"

"You look sad."

"Oh. It's nothing. I'm thinking about, uh, how boring your birthday party is going to be! I only invited fifty of your friends over. You probably won't have any fun at all."

Her eyes widened. "You did?! Even Kilva and Ga... Garu... Gaaaruu...?"

Monster names were hard for her.

"Yep, even G'arunth. You ready to see them?" Jason gestured ahead to the annex he created a few days ago. It had a whole bunch of carnival rides and other things she'd love.

"Oh, yay!! You're the bestest best daddy ever!!"

_I wish that were true._ Jason thought.
