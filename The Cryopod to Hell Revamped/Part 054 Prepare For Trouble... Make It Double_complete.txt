Jason slowed to a stop in front of the sealed doors and sighed. His head hurt. His body ached. Not because of physical pain, but because of emotional and psychological torment. Maybe that second inner voice was right. Accept what happened and move on.

Pressing his hand against the door, he paused. _Shit. Opening this thing last time nearly killed me. How am I going to get inside? I should have installed a doorbell before I left._ Jason thought.

Using his Wordsmithing, Jason opened his mind up to the universe around him, sending it past the doors to see the inside of the Core. Inside, dozens of humans and monsters milled around, but luckily the area past the doors was not blocked.

Pulling his mind back, Jason blinked for a moment as a sickening realization hit him. _I barely see anyone inside. Usually, there are thousands of people working their feet to the bone... but I barely saw two hundred. How many did Beelzebub kill?! That bastard!_ Jason thought.

"**Teleport.**"

His body instantly appeared on the other side, a faint _whoosh_ of air all that signaled his arrival. Nobody noticed.

Trudging forward, Jason spared a few glances around the place. To his left, a small but deep hole in the wall signaled the use of that restricted railgun prototype he had been working on before he left. Someone must have pulled it out against Beelzebub and his army. He hoped it did not misfire.

Scorch points, black splotches, and severe burns popped out of the usually monotonous scenery here and there. Destroyed equipment, overturned tables, and many other marks of destruction signaled a battle that had been recently fought, yet had not had enough time pass for cleanup to finish.

After fifteen seconds of standing and staring at the devastation, one man noticed him. "Hero, sir? Is that you?!"

"The sooner we identify these people, the sooner we can give them a proper burial. It's undignified for men to rot inside of bags, especially when they died so honorably."

Clipboard Guy averted his eyes and quietly nodded at Jason behind Phoebe's back, then walked away.

"When Hoarhiim destroyed the planet and killed Amelia, he destroyed Blaarjiim's orb as well. Since the two of them are linked, Hoarhiim died."

After he walked away, Phoebe closed the gap between them and pressed herself against Jason, hugging him tightly. "After Beelzebub attacked, I spoke to Neil. I don't know if he was anxious or desperate, but something about him was off."

"People cope with loss differently," Jason replied, rubbing the back of her soft white hair gently.

"It wasn't that, Jason. He was looking for someone to blame. He blamed you, me... even Samantha. She saved us from Beelzebub, but Neil seemed to think she may have even instigated the ambush."

Phoebe pulled away and nodded. "Don't you remember? That succubus girl. The one who-" Phoebe's eyes widened. "Oh... oh my god! Is she okay? Is she alive?! When Hoarhiim healed me, she was lying in a pool of blood!"
