Jason ran down the hallways, darting left and right, all the while thinking to himself, _that Beelzebub guy was a loon._ Jason thought.

Not having the Crown of Solomon was a bad idea at first until Jason realized he could say 'Map' and have a chart of the Labyrinth appear in his mind. The downside was that it only outlined places and corridors he had been to, but at the same time, it did allow him to keep track of his route.

Sadly, the last two months were for nothing. Jason had found several divine artifacts, but they were worthless. Amelia had grown stronger during all that time, while his gains were sparse.

Keeping in contact with Hero City was easy enough. Jason could say 'Call,' and speak to Phoebe, Neil, or anyone else as needed.

Despite suffering some setbacks, Jason was surprised at how smoothly things had gone. He had run into a Demon Lord, Beelzebub, but the guy had been such a pushover that now Jason worried he may be the single person who stood between Amelia planet-level hunger. He had only faced Lords up to now, while Amelia had defeated three Dukes at the same time. 

If Jason could not stop her, he did not see how anyone could.

Slowing down, Jason wiped his forehead, realizing he was feeling quite tired. "**Water.**"

A jug of icy cold water popped into existence before him, and Jason plopped down for a moment to catch his breath. The liquid of the gods tasted incredible when he was running around in the Labyrinth. Something about the ambient temperature there was always just a little too warm to be comfortable. He could cool himself down with Wordsmithing, but that unpleasant heat always came creeping back.

And of course, thinking about the heat got him wondering about other things too. What produced oxygen in the Labyrinth? Jason knew plants existed, but they were all in the upper and outer sections.

"**Pizza,**" Jason said, giving himself a slice of pepperoni deep-dish with stuffed crust. Mmm. Pizza on Earth never tasted so perfect. It was cooked exactly the way he liked.

As Jason chowed on the food, a thought occurred to him. _It's funny how I can use my internal spiritual energy to create food, water, and external sources of power. Then when I rest at night, my spiritual energy completely refills._ Jason thought.

The more Jason thought about them, the more he realized how utterly broken his abilities were. If he kept experimenting, he was sure to find new ways to use his Wordsmithing.

After drinking his fill, Jason jumped to his feet and Wordsmithed again. "**Vaporize.**" The jug of water vanished from existence, leaving no trace behind for anyone to find. Gotta cover his tracks.

Jason had been reflecting on Amelia a lot, lately. He had not stopped thinking about how he could put an end to her inhuman rampage since he left the Core. What if he vaporized her with his Wordsmithing? He could kill her, just like that. He could do the same to all the demons.

But... should he? Was it right for him to pass judgment on others?

Jason's power was abnormal. It was not something that should exist. Godlike, incredible potential should not be in the hands of any random human. So why did he have it? Was he chosen for a reason? Was he somehow superior to everyone else? What about Einstein? Would he not have made better use of Wordsmithing if God had given him the ability?

No. It was random luck that Jason was the one bestowed with Wordsmithing. He might be a Hero, the descendant of mythical warriors, but he did not consider himself legendary. He might be the last hope for humanity, but he felt like a child playing with a gun. His ability was a dangerous thing, something that might backfire and hurt the people he loved.

Jason's footsteps echoed off the walls as he started walking down the corridor again.

_I wonder if Phoebe thinks about me when she sleeps._ Jason's mind wandered. _I know I like to dream about her, though I can't sleep in the wilds of the Labyrinth._ Jason thought.

Indeed, over the past two months, Jason had not slept even one time, instead choosing to use his slowly depleting mana reserves to keep himself awake. He did not feel any aftereffects, so it must have been working, but he could not keep it up forever. Sooner or later he would need to catch a catnap and hope the demons did not-

_What was that?_ Jason thought.

A noise behind him made him stop and listen. _Is that... clicking?_ Jason thought.

Moments passed. Nothing. Neither Jason's sonar nor his infrared vision showed any blips.

God, he was getting jumpy. Maybe he should take a nap after all.

A chill went down Jason's back as he continued walking. There was nobody around, but he felt naked and defenseless. An eye watched him.

One or two minutes passed. The feeling intensified. A sensation like a finger pressed against his back, and Jason immediately jumped forward and spun around. "Who's there?!"

A male voice replied. "Ksss, I was carelesss." 

Jason could not see any part of the man at all. There was no heat, and he did not show up in a way that Jason's sonar could detect. 

"I've found you, flessshbag. Your glowing eyesss are quite remarkable. I look forward to adding them to my collection."

"What... what the hell are you? A ghost?!"

"Sssomething like that. I am Mephisssto, Duke of Missst. A pleasssure to make your acquaintance, keh keh keh." Mephisto coughed like a chain-smoker. Jason could only assume he was laughing. "It ssseemsss you're having trouble ssseeing me. Looksss like thossse eyesss are jussst for ssshow."

Jason's heart pounded in his chest. This... _thing_... was a Duke? It had an entirely different presence from that Beelzebub fellow. The killing intent rippled from it and spilled around, creating a sense of dread in the back of Jason's mind.

While the Duke of Mist hissed and tittered to himself, Jason quickly jerked his hand out. His Lightsaber flew off his belt, landed in his palm, and ignited. His backpack's cord attachment snapped onto the lightsaber, and he swung wildly in front of him, trying to hit whoever the hell this new guy was. His slashes cut into the walls on either side, slicing out paths of molten dirt and stone wherever his weapon struck.

Jason jumped back and stared silently, but he could not tell if his attacks had any effect.

"Oh no, you got me," The Duke of Mist said, a mocking tone in his voice. "Ohh, the pain. It hurtsss, I'm dyiiing." He dragged the last syllable out, cranking his sarcasm meter to max levels.

While Mephisto laughed to himself, Jason sent out a wave of telekinetic energy, yanking rocks from the ceiling down and onto where he thought he was standing.

_Crash! Crash!_

The ceiling collapsed on Mephisto's position, only for the Duke to laugh even more uproariously.

"Keh keh keh! Good effort, flessshbag. Ssstill, perhapsss Beelzebub wasss telling the truth. You appear to be a Hero, after all."

"I can't hurt you? Not even a little bit?!"

"You may attempt to ssstrike me if you pleassse, but..." The Duke paused for dramatic effect. "Allow me to ssshow you how it'sss done."

Suddenly, from the point on Jason's back that he had felt his touch a minute ago, a violent pain erupted, sending him sprawling to the floor, his lightsaber just barely missing his face by inches. Agony jolted up his spine in waves, as if currents of electricity were coursing through his internal organs.

"Gaahg! Hnnngh! Fuck! Shiiit!"

Jason nearly bit off his tongue as his body reflexively curled into the fetal position. He could not make the pain stop at all! 

After a few moments of writhing, Jason blinked back tears and remembered his Wordsmithing. "**N-Neutralize! Heal!**"

The pain stopped. Jason froze and shivered on the ground for half a minute as nausea roiled around in his stomach. Using his telekinesis, he deactivated his lightsaber so that he did not kill himself by accident.

"Sssussspiciousss." Mephisto's voice held a note of caution. "Ssstopping my Pain Inducement without making hand sssignsss... very unusssual."

With a force of will that made his head spin, Jason leaped to his feet and quickly dashed away. _Jesus Christ! Mephisto isn't fucking around! I can't see him, and I can't hurt him either!_ Jason thought.

Scarcely had he made it fifty feet, when Mephisto's voice appeared in front of him, making him screech to a halt. "Foul flessshbag! I wassn't done ssspeaking! Ssshow ressspect to your new massster!"

"Fuck that!" Jason yelled and wondered how the hell he got in front of him. "**Wall!**"

The entire Labyrinth shook as a foot-thick barrier formed between them in less than a second, and Jason quickly rushed back in the direction he had just left. He did not want to head back toward Beelzebub, but maybe if he put some distance between himself and Mephisto, there would be a side tunnel or two that-

"Insssolent human!"

Mephisto's voice appeared in front of him yet again, making Jason scream in fright and stumble backward, landing on his ass. _This Duke can move through walls?! Who the fuck am I dealing with?!_ Jason thought.

Before Jason could get up and run away, an iron fist pounded him in the chest. It knocked the wind out of him and sent him tumbling back head over heels.

Jason's vision spun. The nausea from a few moments ago reappeared in violent form, and he vomited all over, only to realize he was on his back. The bile erupted and landed all over his face, neck, and chest.

"Kuh-clean! **Clean!**" Jason belched the words out, and the vomit disappeared, leaving him feeling minty fresh, yet still horribly sickened.

Jason could not see him. Jason could not sense him. Mephisto was a goddamn ghost, able to move faster than Jason and pass through walls. Compared to Beelzebub's little fire-show, this Duke was unspeakably dangerous. Forget Amelia! If Jason could not beat the Duke of Mist, he was done for!

A familiar sound caught Jason's attention. Clicking. The sound he had heard before. Staggering to his feet, his jaw dropped as a massive wall of skeletons shambled toward him. These were not like the weak little buggers Beelzebub had controlled. Their intricately shaped armor, enormous steel shields, and swords all spoke to a level of power Jason had not seen before.

Even so, skeletons usually would not have been a problem, but with Mephisto lurking around, Jason could not afford to treat them lightly. They would distract him while he circled around and jammed a knife into his back.

"Keh keh keh... do you like my creationsss? I am the massster of Death in the Labyrinth." Mephisto's voice was still a little ways away. He must have punched Jason and stayed in his spot. "Reanimation isss trivial for one asss powerful asss I. The other Dukesss don't underssstand the ssskill and danger involved in reanimation, but sssoon they will."

Jason's breath came in gasps. He needed to escape, but the Duke of Mist moved faster than him. He could not hurt him with ordinary attacks, nor could he slow him down with barriers.

_Think, Jason, think!_ Jason thought.

Jason could not. His head was still spinning from that spinal attack. He was out of breath, exhausted, and facing a Demon Duke; one of the most powerful beings in the Labyrinth.

_Amelia beat him in combat?!_ Jason thought.

Yes, that was right. She had killed that Agares fellow, but Mephisto was one of the other two Dukes that were there. If the Scyther brothers were not lying, then that meant he had managed to escape unscathed. Perhaps Amelia could not hurt him.

FUCK!

How did he get out of this situation?!

An idea appeared. _It's worth a shot!_ Jason thought.

"**Speed! Speed! Power! Power! Perception!**"

If the Duke of Mist was fast, then Jason had to be faster!

Spinning on his heel, Jason dashed away, racing toward the fake wall he had created. Unlike last time when he had been with Amelia and he had hyper-speed-dashed into the Great Doors of the Labyrinth Core, this time, his mind managed to keep up with the speed increase.

Jason pounded through the fake wall without stopping, rushing with all his might, occasionally yelling "**Heal!**" whenever the power threatened to tear his limbs apart.

Minutes passed. Jason continued tearing down the Labyrinth halls at speeds approaching a hundred miles an hour. Left, right, through this path and that, he ignored his heart screaming in pain. Ultimately, he could not maintain his mad dash and slowed to a stop. His body ached as if death was moments away.

Gasping for breath, Jason spoke again, "**Normalize,**" returning his body to its original state.

"**Wa... Water.**"

As the jug of icy water appeared in Jason's hand, he lifted it to take a drink.

A voice whispered in his ear.

"Oopsss, you almossst lossst me."
