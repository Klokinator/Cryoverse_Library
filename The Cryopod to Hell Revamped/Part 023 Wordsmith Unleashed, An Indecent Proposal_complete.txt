"**Summon.**"

A suit of metal armor materialized and wrapped around his body. All at once, over a hundred pounds of weight popped into existence, causing him to slump forward. He caught himself before falling on his face and spoke another word of power. 

"**Strength.**"

The metal's weight seemingly vanished as his body's strength increased to heft the added bulk. The only exposed parts of his body were two horizontal slits for his unseeing eyes to pierce through, as well as seven thin vertical openings in front of his mouth. He was not only protected, but he could also breathe, too.

Metal encased his arms, legs, feet, and hands. His abs, heart, back, neck, and every other part of his body could rest easy knowing that only an incredibly precise attack targeting the tiniest jointed areas could hope to hurt him.

The only problem was how slowly he moved. He guessed that was the price to pay for impenetrable defense. Based on the tests he had run, even if a rhino slammed its horn into him, full-force, he might get the wind knocked out of him at worst.

In addition to his armor, his fusion backpack appeared, automatically attaching to his lightsaber when he yanked it into his hand with telekinesis. _It was go time._

His metal suit ground together as he stepped through the portal. The sound was annoying, but at the same time, the noise vibrated the air all around him, giving him a continuous spherical view of the area.

The _whumph_ of air pressure change, as always, was disorienting. Immediately, the cries of pain throughout the room, the clanging of claw and blade, and the sound of magi chanting spells transformed the room into a fully visible entity, enabling him to see every corner and every individual battle with perfect clarity.

Nobody paid his presence any heed. He couldn't complain. It gave him time to figure out what was happening. Winged creatures, possibly harpies, flew around in the air, drawing their bows and arrows and firing with deadly precision into the crowd below. At the far end of the Core, bright red spots indicated hot-blooded demons, each one forming hand signs and chanting to cast magical spells. A bolt of black energy sailed from one of their hands halfway across the room and struck a snake-shaped monster in the back, making it hiss in agony and fall over, dead. He didn't know what the spell was, but it was moderately powerful. He wouldn't want to get hit by it.

Two monsters jerked their heads toward him, and he recognized the shape of their legs and tails, marking them as kobolds. He had only seen a few, and from what Kar had told him, most were fiercely loyal to demons above other monsters. That made them likely enemies.

The two kobolds dashed at him and raised their swords to strike. His lightsaber blade popped out with a high-pitched wail, causing them to flinch and gaze at it in surprise — the perfect opening.

While they stared in shock at the weapon, he yanked the wolf-like monsters toward him with telekinesis and swung the saber up and down in two quick movements, lopping their sword-arms off, then shoving them back with a telekinetic push. _Just like a Jedi, heh._

He didn't want to kill if he could help it, but anyone who attacked him would regret their life choices. The kobolds screeched in pain and slammed into several other monsters at the bottom, alerting them to his presence. While those monsters looked up in alarm and their eyes jumped to his glowing blade, he decided to take things to the next level.

Under his breath, he muttered, "**Amplify. Amplify. Amplify. Deepen. Shift.**"

His voice tasted menacing, reminding him of an enraged minotaur at the prime of its life. Without even speaking he could tell his vocal cords had shifted adequately. Now, it was time to make his grand entrance.

"**SILENCE! CEASE THE FIGHTING, OR I SHALL ANNIHILATE ALL OF YOU!**"

His voice was so loud and powerful that sound waves smashed into the far wall and tore a few chunks off. Nearby monsters went flying, and even he staggered slightly under the recoil from his own voice. Luckily, he used his telekinesis to steady his body.

The fighting in the room careened to a stop as his voice echoed for miles in every direction. Everyone fell to their knees and grabbed their ears, howling in pain. In fact, the room went so silent despite all the screaming that it wasn't until he felt blood running down his neck that he realized he had blown out his goddamn eardrums. "**Heal.**"

He muttered the word as quietly as possible, but it was hard to tell how far his voice traveled. Once his hearing was back, he noticed everyone scanning the room, looking for the source of the Voice of God. 

After a few moments, several heads turned to face him. Then others. Finally, the entire room looked at him.

It was at this point that he noticed the downside of spherical vision. He could mostly make out each species of monster and demon, but he couldn't tell who was on which side. Anyone could be an ally or an enemy.

"Hurgh! Get off of me!" Kar's voice rang out, and he leaped to his feet, throwing two blobs of smoke through the air. He could barely even make them out since they had no heat in their bodies, and when they hit the wall, they evaporated into mist and then reappeared several feet away.

Faint splotches dotted Kar's body. Despite being stabbed several times, and blood rushing from the wounds, he could still move and fight. What incredible stamina he must possess.

His roar of anger shook several enemies from their stupor. One demon leaped into the air toward him, ran across the ceiling fifty feet above after inverting his gravity, and began casting a spell. He looked to be the same one that had shot the black magic bolt from before. He didn't want to get hit by it.

Oh. A simple solution.

Without moving a finger, he reached up with his telekinesis and yanked the demon from the ceiling, smashing him into the ground. The yelp of fright the demon made on the way down was both amusing and also sickening. "Eeyak?!"

He raised him into the air and smashed him against the ground two more times before letting him go. His body flopped like a ragdoll and landed with a sickening _crunch_. 

Naptime.

A strange sensation washed over him. _He had enjoyed that. He had held the demon's life in his hands and nearly snuffed it out._

_He had never felt this powerful before. It was... a little intoxicating._

Before he could dwell on the morality of his attack, a group of kobolds rushed forward. One of them hurled three knives at him. He didn't bother blocking them. The blades smacked against his armor and _tink_-ed to the ground harmlessly. Not one to be deterred by his impenetrable defenses, that same kobold dashed in front of him and delivered a gut-busting punch straight into his stomach...

And broke every bone in his hand.

For a single moment, a look of shock raced across the kobold's face before every muscle in its arm popped at once. The poor thing howled in pain, and he lopped its arm off with a quick lightsaber swipe. He could always grow it back later if it decided to join his side. If not, the limb was useless anyway.

Shoving it away with his telekinesis, he glanced over at the Demon Lords. All of them chanted in sync and pooled their energy together. "**Analyze.**"

His voice's almost robotic timbre boomed out, and a series of information flooded into his mind as his Wordsmithing broke down the components of their spell. It was the equivalent of a micro-nuke, but made of dark energy, and if they unleashed it, the bomb would probably wipe out every soul in the room. They had realized he was a significant threat, so this must have been their only way to win.

Either way, he couldn't let them cast that spell. At the moment they fired it, his voice boomed again. "**Absorb.**" All the energy from the spell vanished and flooded his body. An odd sensation, like pins and needles, spread through his limbs. It was more uncomfortable than painful, and it dissipated as quickly as it had arrived. It felt as though he had dive-bombed into uncomfortably hot water, then climbed right back out.

Weird.

All of the Demon Lords took a step back in surprise as their ultimate magic vanished into the breeze. He decided to use his dominant voice one more time, though he toned the volume down substantially. "ALL WHO DO NOT SURRENDER WILL FACE THE SAME FATE AS THE DEMONS WHO DARED TO ATTACK ME."

Saying this, he lifted the dozen or so Demon Lords and smashed them into the ground before they could react. A few slams later, and they were out cold. "**Normalize.**" He returned his voice to normal afterward and smirked.

His proclamation and follow-up attack caught the attention of all the enemies in the room. Every remaining monster and demon peeled away and raced out the Great Doors, grabbing their Lords hurriedly on the way.

A minute later, a collective sigh of relief washed through the room. "Turtle!" Kar yelled out, and when he turned to look at him, the already low temperature in his body had dropped another few degrees. The giant lizard-sphinx stumbled forward and fell flat on his face.

Using his telekinesis, he deactivated his lightsaber and lifted his armored form through the air with telekinesis to land next to Kar. He flopped onto his back and gasped painfully. "Huh... hurgh... this is... this is it, for me. The shadow-walkers h-hit too many vital areas... I'm a goner."

He fought the overwhelming urge to laugh. "**Heal.**"

Before Kar could continue giving him a deathbed speech, all of his injuries vanished. The lizard-sphinx blinked in surprise and suddenly sat up. "O-oh. You can do that. I forgot."

"Yeah... sorry, I interrupted your dramatic speech."

Kar cleared his throat and jumped to his feet. "Ahem, ah, well, this is uncomfortable. I had prepared myself to die. My power is nearly on par with a Demon Duke, but there were too many enemies. You saved my life."

"Hey, no sweat. You saved mine too, remember? Just think of it as me repaying a favor."

Glancing around, he quickly healed every monster in the room that he could see, reducing his internal energy substantially. Some of their allies were already dead, and he considered resurrecting them. _He had never tried it before. Would it work? Now was as good a chance as any to find out._

Kar interrupted his thoughts. "Turtle. Thank you for showing up. You've saved our lives, but this was just the beginning. Those demons were not ordered by the Dukes and Emperors to attack us. They arrived of their own volition."

He turned to look at him questioningly. "What do you mean?"

Kar grimaced. "All demons must obey the orders given by the Demon Dukes. However, in the absence of an order, they are free to do as they please, even killing one another. There are only a few strict rules demons must follow, and none of those cover our situation. The Demon Dukes and Emperors didn't order those Lords to attack, but they didn't order them _not_ to attack. Do you understand?"

Slowly, he nodded. "More will likely strike, knowing the first wave weakened us already. And those were just Lords and Monsters. If the Dukes and Emperors decide to attack..."

"We'll lose," Kar replied flatly. "Demon Dukes are not to be trifled with. The lowest-ranked Duke, Mephisto, commands the Labyrinth's undead, and even a few shadow-walkers. He can pass through walls and reap souls from bodies. Little is known of the other Dukes and Emperors since they prefer secrecy, but you won't sleep well knowing they all tower over Mephisto."

He flicked his gaze to the Great Doors. "How did they get inside? We shut the doors more than a month ago."

"Hurgh. Several Demon Lords working together have more than enough power to blow the doors off their hinges, let alone merely push them open. Besides that, it appears Bahamut installed demonic codes into the doors. If one knows the password, they can speak a few words, and the doors will automatically open."

"Oh, fantastic. That's _great_ news," he muttered. 

Kar shook his head. "I disagree, but you're free to your opinion."

From behind him, the sound of people exiting the Tarus II portal echoed inside the chamber. Phoebe, Neil, Hans, Saul, Makoto, and their militia came charging through, swords and bows at the ready, but confusion passed over their faces as they realized the battle was already over.

"Excuse me, Kar. I'll be right back." Instead of levitating to them with his telekinesis, he de-summoned his armor and jogged over.

"Phoebe! Are you alright?" 

As he slowed to a stop, Phoebe looked away. "I'm fine, no thanks to you. Ass."

"What happened?" Neil asked. He continued scanning the room, as several of their monsters pinned down enemy monsters he had healed up. He would have been amused at the incredulous looks on their faces if it weren't for his focus on Phoebe. Right now, if all the enemy monsters and demons died, he wouldn't even care.

"I came in and put an end to the fighting. Nonlethally."

Makoto rested a hand on the katana at his belt. "Impressive, Jason-san. Did you heal everyone with your magic?"

"Yes. Even the enemies."

"Most honorable. You are a kind and compassionate leader."

Makoto heaped some praise on him, and he forced a smile, but honestly, he couldn't care less. Stepping forward, he reached past everyone to touch Phoebe's arm. "Hey, listen. Can we talk?"

She looked like she wanted to pull away, but reluctantly sighed. "Is the situation stable here?"

He had never seen her this grumpy before. It was a little unsettling. "For the most part."

Phoebe jerked her head back and forth to look around for a moment, before gesturing to a quiet spot in the distance behind one of the portals. "Over there."

Nodding, he followed behind her as she took off. 

_He was going to apologize and hope she didn't hate him. He couldn't just say what he did was a prank. It was mean-spirited and not like him at all._

_He hoped she would be willing to listen._

---

Phoebe stared at him icily, keeping her arms crossed and body language closed. Her usual warm smile had turned into a blank facade of half-visible anger. The look on her face made him feel like a backstabber, someone who had intentionally hurt her for his amusement.

"Look, Phoebe, about what happened... I didn't mean... that is, I never intended to hurt you. I didn't think about how you'd feel being trapped in a vehicle you'd never seen before, and-"

She silently held a hand up. "I've thought about what you did to me, Jason. You trapped me in that metal box, tied me down so I couldn't escape, and unleashed a pack of lions on me. Their roars were unmistakable. What were you hoping to accomplish?"

Phoebe's words made him flinch. 

She didn't know what a 'car' was, let alone its function. All she knew was that he had seemingly terrorized her for no reason. He had treated her reactions like a joke... and ended up hurting her needlessly.

_He was awful._

"Phoebe, I'm sorry. I screwed up. That's all there is to it. If you hate me, I understand. I want to try and explain what I was thinking, okay? I thought it would be a hilarious prank until I realized how badly I hurt you. I'll never do anything like that ever again."

Phoebe pursed her lips. "Never?"

"Never."

Her head turned slightly as she looked past him at the wall. It was only now that he noticed her lip quivering. _Was she going to cry again? Fuck. Goddamn, he had really stepped in the shit this time. He might never live this down..._

"Hahaha!" Phoebe suddenly laughed, and then the laugh exploded into an uproar as she doubled over and held her stomach with both hands. "Haahahaha! Oh, my goodness, Your Grace's expression! It's like a puppy caught pooping on the rug for the third time! How could I ever stay mad at you?! Hahaha!"

He was beyond confused. "W-what? You're not angry?!"

"Of course not! Heavens, no! Oh lord, have mercy!" Phoebe nearly fell to the ground laughing, and this time her laughter was an upbeat new emotion he had never seen her display before. Relief overwhelmed him.

Phoebe laughed for a solid twenty seconds, before choking to a stop and wiping her forehead. "Oh, my days! I was so scared at the time, but after you left, I realized what an exhilarating feeling that speed was! I couldn't walk straight until the militia showed up. Hans explained to me what an 'automobile' was but also how he'd never seen one as advanced as yours."

He nodded, unsure of how to respond. "Hans did, huh? Well, that was nice of him. S-saves me the trouble, I suppose..."

Phoebe forced herself to look stern. "Nevertheless! If you ever do something like that to me again, I'll beat you senseless during our next training! Do you understand?"

He quickly stood rigid and saluted. "Y-yes ma'am!"

A moment passed, and Phoebe relaxed visibly. "Praise God in the heavens above. I must say, Jason, that had you properly warned me about that machine, I might have enjoyed it. I would like to ride in it in the future, if you don't mind." She paused before quickly adding, "Not now, of course. Later. We've... dead to bury."

The joking mood sobered up between them as he glanced out at the cooling corpses. "You've got that right."

---

An hour later, he moved the final body to the right side of the room, as per Neil's instructions. Having divided up the allies and enemies, he now knew that over a thousand Central Core monsters died, as well as three thousand monsters and demons on the opposing side. Unfortunately, the fallen from their team were more powerful, higher ranked monsters. A quick estimate told him they took a three to five loss. Well, the numbers were meaningless anyway, since the demons were an unending horde outside the Great Doors.

They were few, and the demons were many.

Thinking similar thoughts, Neil exhaled. "Huuu... not good, my liege. We'll be lucky to survive the next skirmish, let alone last to the end of the month. I hope you have a plan."

Makoto Ueda gracefully walked over to them, hands cupped in front of himself. "Hiro-sama, before we were so rudely interrupted, we were discussing rebuilding human society. Today has shown that we must continue that conversation and get to work at once."

"I concur," Saul said, as he meandered to them with Hans in tow. "If Demon Lords are already our enemies, I don't imagine it will be long before a Baron or a Duke shows up. Had you not healed Kar, we'd have lost one of our best warriors, and the title of King of Monsters would pass to another."

Glancing at him warily, he asked, "If Kar dies, what happens to the power of the Sphinx?"

"I haven't the faintest clue."

Hans gestured toward the Great Doors. "Vee need veapons. Good ones. Using swords and sticks to battle the demons is entirely useless. Vhy can't vee make guns? Zey vould give us a ranged tactical advantage."

His hand brushed against the lightsaber at his belt. _Hans was right. Advanced weapons and armor would give them a fighting chance. It didn't matter how much damage a demon's fists and claws could cause if he gunned them down before they got close. DOOM had taught him that much._

"Good idea, Hans. Maybe I should add some automated defenses too, like turrets and such. The fewer casualties we suffer, the better."

Phoebe walked up to the group with a spark in her eyes. "Demons are highly resilient. I fought alongside Arthur and slew many in ages past. If you want to kill a demon, you need to pierce their hides."

Hans grunted. "Nnn. If that is that case, perhaps bullets vould not vork very vell. Vee might need something more sophisticated than the technology I used during the Great War."

He looked at him pointedly, as if to say, _you're the genius inventor, here. Surely you can think of something._

His lightsaber and armor came to mind. _If we need raw force to withstand demonic attacks, and the Crown of Solomon allows him to envision complex machinery like his lightsaber, then he had barely begun to tap into its latent potential._

"Fine. I have a few ideas." He paused to gaze meaningfully at the five people surrounding him. "If my plans work as well as I hope, then I need a promise from everyone here today. My goal is not to conquer and subjugate, but to unify humans, monsters, and demons alike. We have to try not to kill when possible. You can slaughter demons all day long, but every demon you spare will catch their attention and defrost their hearts. I'd like to release the ones I healed today, too, after we interrogate them."

After he finished speaking, Neil glanced at Makoto. "Have you been talking to the king recently? His words sound like something you would say."

"I met his majesty only today, when you introduced us," Makoto replied, smiling.

Neil adjusted his jacket. "Mmm. I wouldn't go so far as to call your goals naive, my liege, but demons are not the soft-hearted creatures you believe them to be. Perhaps after you combat them again and again, you will understand how they think. They will slaughter all of us in a second the moment they realize they can end our resistance. In fact, I assume that will be their next goal."

Nodding, he replied, "I know. However, I won't budge on this issue. We have to try not to kill demons where possible. I'll make sure to equip our troops with better weaponry and armor, but we have to lead by example. In the era I came from, only those who stood up against injustice and took risks with their lives changed the world for the better."

"That's admirable," Neil said with a nod. "Very well. We'll try your approach, but you'd better hurry. More demons could attack at any moment. The sooner we fortify our position, the better."

"Already three steps ahead of you," he murmured.

Makoto folded his hands together and bowed his head. "It has been long time since I see a leader work hard to spare the lives of his enemies. His majesty, Hiro-sama, is most gracious and kind."

He forced a smile. "Thanks, Makoto...san? I'm more American than Japanese, so I don't know honorifics very well."

He cocked his head questioningly. "Did your mother or father not teach you about your homeland?"

"My mother died when I was very young. My father abandoned me. I don't remember either of them."

Makoto's face paled. "Ah, very sorry to bring up sensitive topic. If you ever need guidance, feel free to seek my help, Hiro-sama."

His smile shone more genuinely. "Thanks. I'll keep that offer in mind."

---

It took him a minute of concentration to picture the mass of gears, circuits, machinery, and metal in his mind. The Crown worked overtime forming mental bridges from neurons to receptors, and finally, when he opened his eyes, he spoke a word. "**Turret!**"

Attached to the wall outside the Great Doors, a round half-orb appeared. Reaching twice the size of his body, with a red iris that stared ahead blankly, the weapon sat at attention, waiting for an enemy to step in range. Bulletproof black glass enveloped the iris, protecting it from attack.

A laser turret. It could fire lasers at the speed of light, cutting through most armor and flesh with little to no resistance. If the demons attacked, they would come from the side doors or the end of the hallway, exposing themselves to turret-fire.

"**Turret! Turret! Turret!**"

He created three more turrets, placing two on each side of the Great Doors. These weapons alone should eviscerate any attack force that approached them. Good enough for now.

There was still a problem, though. The turrets would run out of energy after a minute of continuous fire. Just like his lightsaber, they needed a sustainable power source. He would have to build a fusion reactor somewhere.

Neil stepped through the still-opened Great Doors and stared at the black bubbles on the walls. "What are these spheres?"

"Automated defensive turrets. They have advanced Friend or Foe targeting systems, and can see a mile in absolute darkness. They're always scanning for threats, so if demons show up, they'll take them down."

Neil nodded seriously. "I have no idea what you said, but it sounds good. I thought you were going for a non-lethal approach, though? These spheres seem antithetical to those values of yours."

"They have several settings. I set their attack priorities to 'incapacitate,' though they will switch to a lethal mode if they come under attack. I'll install a broadcast system to warn incoming demons of our defenses, too. If they ignore the warning, that's on them."

Phoebe slipped out of the Core and approached him. "We'd like to close the doors as soon as possible. Are you done out here, Your Grace?"

"Not yet. Soon, Phoebe."

She lingered for a moment and stared at the turrets before walking back inside, a briskness in her step. She seemed slightly afraid of them.

He couldn't blame her, though. They reminded him of the computer from an old movie he used to watch. _2001, a Space Odyssey._ They resembled HAL 9000, with his unmoving red eyeball.

Creepy. He couldn't say he was a fan of evil AI.

He spent the next several minutes installing a speaker and camera system, along with lights in the ceiling to illuminate the corridor. He could see in the darkness, but the others couldn't. A lack of light might make some demons think they were sneaky, so it was best to remedy that, now.

Stepping back inside the Core, he created two new computer terminals and beckoned for two nearby Knolls to come over. They shot questioning gazes at him but shrugged since they still didn't know who he was. _Thanks, Kar._

They stopped before him, and he beckoned at the computer terminals. "I don't care who does it, but I need someone on each of these computers 24/7. This one is set to monitor the cameras outside the Great Doors, and this one is for keeping an eye on energy levels of the turrets, as well as a bunch of subsystems. You'll pick everything pertinent up as you operate the computers."

Both gnolls nodded, their eyes wide. "Issh assh you sshay, hooman. You build weaponsh to protect ush, we thank you. Lose many brothersh, we did."

"I'm sorry to hear that. I hope that, in due time, no one else will have to die."

He left them behind and walked over to an empty section of the room. It took a few minutes for the Crown to help him envision a small fusion reactor, but when he finished, he embedded the circular reactor halfway into the wall.

The rest of the work involved magically creating power lines inside the walls of the Labyrinth to power the turrets, but that took only a few seconds.

Once he finished, he paused to sit down. His mind was woozy. Creating complex mechanical devices drained both his mana and his brainpower. It had been a while since he felt this tired.

"Are you unwell, Your Grace?" Phoebe appeared beside him, and he flinched. He hadn't even noticed her there; that was how distracted he was.

"I... I'm fine — just a little tired. I can't rest yet, though. I need to start making some weapons too."

She nodded and leaned down beside him. "For what it's worth, I wanted you to know that your concern for my well-being was very touching."

It took him a moment to form a coherent thought. God, he was slow as shit right now. "Yeah? Well, it's you. I always think about you. I never want anything to happen to you, Phoebe. You mean more to me than anyone else. Perhaps even myself."

"Oh, don't say such an awful thing like that," Phoebe pleaded. "You have to take care of yourself. Think of how it would make me feel if you were badly hurt. I couldn't bear to see you in such a state again."

A smile spread across his face. "Yeah, but then you could take care of me again, like you did before. We'd have a lot more time together."

She blushed and looked away. "You don't have to get hurt for that to happen. We could do that now."

"Oh? Are you asking me on a date?"

She stood up quickly. "Gods, guide me. You're always ready with a funny quip, Your Grace."

His smile faded. "I wasn't joking."

For once, Phoebe was at a loss for words. She opened and closed her mouth several times before forming a sentence. "Haven't we gone far enough?"

"You've asked that before. I think you're making excuses." He reached over and gently took her hand in his. "I don't know if someone hurt you in the past. I know you cared for King Arthur, but I'm not him. If... if you think I can't match up to him or something, say the word. I'll stop prying."

She didn't pull away. "It isn't that. I... I've lost everyone I ever cared about to the winds of time. Maybe... secretly... I fear developing a deep bond with someone."

"Maybe? You're usually more certain than this. Unlike you, I don't have such fears. When I entered my Cryopod, I knew everyone I cared about would disappear into the past. I felt empty enough that I didn't care. You changed that, Phoebe. You changed me."

Rising from his chair, he tried to hunch down a little to her level, but probably ended up looking goofy. Just this once, he didn't want to tower over her.

"Phoebe, I told you a few months ago after my paralysis that I cared about you. That hasn't changed. If anything, my feelings have only deepened. Every minute I spend with you gives me the energy to live life to the fullest. I'm not only protecting the other people and monsters, but I'm also protecting _you_. Everything I do, I do it for you."

He was stunned by the words coming out of his mouth. He used to think he'd die of embarrassment if he opened up like this to someone else, but Phoebe meant everything to him. He didn't just care about her because she had healed him... he... he loved her.

Yeah. That was exactly it. "Phoebe, I love you. I want you to be mine, whatever that entails."

She stayed quiet for several seconds, the look in her eyes like a deer in the headlights. The expression passed, and her gaze softened.

"I do too, J-Jason. Love you, that is. Maybe I was afraid to admit it to myself, but... if I don't say it now..."

"You'll regret it later?"

"Yes."

"I know the feeling."

Silently, he knelt down on one leg, and muttered under his breath, "**Ring.**"

A shiny silver ring with a tiny red gem inset appeared in his palm. He suddenly felt horribly flustered.

"Uh, look, here's a ring. If you, uh, if you wear it, that means you're... well, you and I are, sort of... you know?"

She grinned hugely. "Yes! I know exactly what you mean!"

"So... will you wear this?"

Phoebe pursed her lips. "What does the ring mean? Say it."

"Uh. It means I'm proposing marriage."

"Oooh!" Phoebe bowed down suddenly and wrapped her arms around him, taking him by surprise. "You're so cute when you're flustered! I will! I'll marry you!"

With her face pressed against his, she kissed him on the cheek, and he grinned stupidly.

_Today was the best day of his life. Nothing could take this moment from him._
