"**ARTHUR!! Stand still for two seconds, you little rat!**"

Diablo pounded his chest and limbs like an angry gorilla. He repeatedly tried to slap the tiny light form dashing around his body, occasionally stabbing and jabbing the Archdemon with spears of light.

At one point, Arthur paused for a moment and stood still, allowing Diablo to swat at him. When one of the Emperor's claws raced at the Hero-King, Arthur jammed a spear into the Archdemon's belly, then formed a chain of light and a second spear, attaching the two weapons.

Just as Diablo's attack was about to hit, Arthur impaled the Archdemon's palm and transmitted away, chaining it to Diablo's gut.

Diablo screamed in anger as he tugged and pulled, trying to free his limb. "**Coward! Pest! I'll crush you flat!!**"

Arthur reappeared a mile away inside a small depression in the ground. He evaluated the damage he was dealing and immediately calculated that his offense was insufficient.

_I require a new weapon._ Arthur thought.

With Dellfingler out of commission, Arthur swept the battlefield with his piercing gaze. A single sword lying near a crater caught his eye. 

_Catherine._ Arthur thought.

Arthur raised his hand and used the power of holy magic to call the weapon to him. Despite the immense distance, the sword rattled and shook for a few moments. Then, it leaped into the air and spun like an enraged scythe. Catherine flew across two miles of plains within a few seconds and struck the flat of Arthur's palm. Arthur wrapped his fingers around the blade, examining it for a moment to determine its power level.

Seconds later, something unexpected happened. Arthur staggers as a new voice appeared in his mind.

_Hero. New Hero? Not the Wordsmith. Someone else? An empty soul. I am Joan of Arc. Who are you...?_ Joan thought.

Arthur tried to shake the fog from his mind. Joan spoke to him. _Fear not, warrior of virtue. Thou lack a soul, so I shall use thy body. Let us smite our enemies on this battlefield with our shared divinity._ Joan thought.

Arthur's eyes shifted silently. His body trembled as his soulless vessel tried futilely to resist Catherine's power.

The Sword From Heaven shimmered with divine light. Eventually, a brilliant light transferred through Arthur's palm, arm, and chest. Once it reached his brain, it took over and engulfed his mind.

Moments later, Arthur jumped to his feet. His stance changed. The King's mind vanished, replaced by Joan's consciousness.

Joan swiveled Arthur's body around and evaluated the battlefield in an instant. "Darkness blankets this land. A servant of evil taller than mountains stands beside his master, the one known as Satan and Devil. Which foe shall I send to an eternal slumber first?"

Across the rising and falling plains, Satan crouched low and stared at Diablo as the enraged Archdemon yanked and tugged at the chain binding his palm to his gut.

_Oh, Diablo. Why did you have to transform? I'll have to use attacks I once deemed too dangerous for my health simply to slow you down._ Satan thought.

Satan quickly took inventory of his souls. _Ten thousand, one hundred and sixty-one. I can refill my energy to its maximum at the cost of one soul each, or expend several souls at once to temporarily increase my cap. However... my most potent magic will require more than a thousand souls worth of energy to use a single time._ Satan thought.

Satan bit his lip. _Damn! I wish I could take Lilia away from here and flee! I don't want to waste valuable souls attacking my ally. There must be a way out of this fight._ Satan thought.

Diablo's tentacles slammed the planet repeatedly like a hurricane of destruction. "**Graaah! How dare you snare my arm! I'll make you pay, Hero! YOU'LL PAAAAY!**"

A light bulb brightened in Satan's mind. _Of course! If Diablo is defeated and the Black Queen gives me no new orders, I'll be free to do as I please! Blaarjiim didn't specify that I had to return to him once I killed the Archdemon, so I won't!_ Satan thought.

Satan paused and blinked. He watched Arthur's bright body begin running across the battlefield again, but for some reason, the Hero-King moved much more slowly. He didn't transmit around as he did before. Given the darkness of night shrouding the planet, his body, glowing with Holy Energy, drew Diablo's attention.

"**Aha! The coward shows himself! I won't let you get close this time!**"

Diablo sent a flurry of tentacles out, biting at the pinprick of light with feverish intensity. However, Joan of Arc tossed her sword out and used her advanced control of Catherine to transform it into a whirling buzzsaw. The blade lunged to and fro hungrily, spinning around the massive appendages and severing them two and three at a time.

Metric tons of flesh slammed into Polaris's soil. Diablo recoiled in pain as ten of his lower limbs vanished. "**Gaaah! You worthless human!**"

Joan ignored him, recalling her sword and diving underground before he could launch a counter-attack. She burrowed furiously, using her powers of earth manipulation to tear through rock and stone at nearly the same speed as she could run.

_Arthur, thy powers are formidable. Thy divine energy appears limitless. It shines as bright as the sun. How can one mortal hold such overwhelming reserves? With our powers combined, we shall slay this monstrosity._ Joan thought.

Joan lifted her head as she rushed forward. _The beast rises above me. Soon, he shall sense my approach. The time to strike is now!_ Joan thought.

She burst from the dirt and activated Arthur's spears of light, an ability she once held. Rapidly, Joan summoned and hurled a dozen of the partisans into the ground, keeping her eye on the hungry maws of Diablo's tentacles as they lunged at her. She finished within seconds and formed more chained spears to attach to the ones buried in the earth.

_A demon tied down and unable to fight shan't pose any more of a threat than a field hare caught in a trap!_ Joan thought.

Diablo lunged his remaining three limbs down to grab the Hero, and she used Arthur's powers of light manipulation to send out the remaining chained spears, lancing through the Archdemon's hands and binding them.

Diablo bellowed in rage. "**Damn you!! Arthur! Your tricks are pissing me off!!**"

Joan dived back underground and burrowed over two hundred feet below the planet's surface, then used Arthur's light manipulation to pull the holy chains with tremendous force, yanking the Archdemon against the earth and onto his belly.

All the while, Satan watched silently.

_Is this... Arthur? Up until now, he never demonstrated this much tactical prowess. I find it hard to believe a single Hero is taking on the Archdemon and... and winning!_ Satan thought.

Diablo's rage subsided as the Third Emperor scowled at his sudden predicament. The five-hundred-foot-tall demon cocked his head left and right, thinking about how to escape the Hero's snares.

Joan emerged from the ground, another spear held at the ready, and reared back to hurl it into the Archdemon's skull.

Suddenly, all of Joan's plans flipped upside-down.

Diablo inhaled, creating a micro-monsoon as hurricane-force winds sucked into his mouth. With one mighty exhale, the demon blasted the surface in front of his face with the full force of his fiery rage. Polaris shook violently, throwing Joan and Satan around like pizza dough in the hands of a practiced chef. The resulting explosion boiled back up from the planet's interior, destroying the chains bound in the dirt and blowing out a massive crater under the Archdemon.

The ejection shot the Emperor several hundred feet into the sky. He landed with a _WHOOMPH_ half a mile away, his forty remaining tentacles absorbing the brunt of the impact.

Joan landed on her back and jumped to her feet immediately. _Mon dieu! This brute is neither slow nor stupid as I first imagined. No matter the era, evil grows without the forces of justice to oppose it._ Joan thought.

Satan, still standing over a mile away, nodded. _Arthur can't stop Diablo, then. Soon, my old friend will begin unleashing his full rage. The madness in his mind has scarcely emerged._ Satan thought.

"Satan!" A female voice made the Devil jump. He turned around to see Lilia running toward him. "I'm here to help!"

Despite his beautiful mate arriving to see him, Satan shook his head. "Dammit, Lilia! I told you! You are no use against Diablo. Leave, save yourself. I'm going to unleash the Genesis Flood."

The Second Emperor slowed to a stop beside him. "What? B-but you said the energy requirement was..."

"Aye, Lilia. It will cost me at least a thousand souls. However, I'm planning to spend more than two thousand. The more souls I burn, the more powerful my magic. You should get to high ground."

Satan started to turn away, but paused. "Weren't you going to fight the Black Queen?"

An odd look appeared in her eyes. "I was, but I lost. The one fighting her now is... the Wordsmith... but something is very different about him. There's an uncanny look in his eyes."

"Ah." Satan turned back to the Archdemon. "Well, I'm going to unleash my magic now. If you must stay by my side, then assume your Succubus form and prepare to fly. You'll need-"

"I can't." Lilia rubbed her back. "I lost to the Black Queen after transforming again. I can barely use any magic, let alone attempt a third transformation."

Satan sighed. "Devils. Fine. After I activate the spell, I'll take you somewhere safe. We can watch the destruction from a distance."

"Satan."

Belial spoke again, but this time, she lowered her voice to a whisper.

"Hm? What's the matter?" Satan asked.

"I... I just want you to know how much I love you. I stayed faithful all this time. I never once looked at another man."

Satan lowered his eyes.

"You're a good woman, Lilia. You deserve better than me. I thought about what you said. You're right. I've always been a murderous psychopath. I got my just desserts when the Black Queen took my soul. After all the souls I devoured, I ended up swallowed by the one who created me. In a way, you could call it poetic justice."

"There is no justice in death," Belial murmured. "If we get out of this together... I want you to come with me and leave all of this behind. Forget the battles and the strife. Stay by my side, and let us enjoy eternity together."

Satan closed his eyes.

"I'll do what I can, toots. I'll do anything for you."

He turned to face Belial. A smile crept onto his face.

"And for what it's worth... I'm sorry I left you alone. You deserved better."

Belial blushed. "I'll forgive you, but only if we make it out of here alive."

"Haha!" Satan laughed. "I ain't gonna let Deebs kill us. Come on! We ain't got a moment to waste."

"Alright."

_Minutes before._

Hoarhiim's figure appeared in Jason's mind as a vaguely human-shaped apparition of light.

"Jason. We share a life bond. My orb is not far. Reach out and place it in your hand. I can help you, but only if you trust me." Hoarhiim said.

Jason tried to lift his head a fraction of an inch, but failed. 

_I... can't. Everything hurts. I don't have any strength left._ Jason thought.

"You do!" Hoarhiim beckoned with both hands. "Come, Jason! Don't let my brother win! Blaarjiim consumed tens of millions of human souls! I know you won't let his evil plans succeed!"

Jason's eyelids drooped. 

_I've already failed. I lost to Satan, and I lost to Blaarjiim. I couldn't convince Amelia to abandon the path of corruption. I'm no Hero. I'm a failure._ Jason thought.

Hoarhiim's soft gaze hardened. "Imbecile! When did the Jason I knew become a weakling who gave up because the going got a little too tough?! Look at Phoebe! She's lying facedown in the dirt, breathing her last, weak gasps of air! After everything Phoebe did for you, will you let her die? Will you let her become the victim of a psychopath?!"

Jason's eyes closed for a moment. _I don't want that... but... I have nothing left. No juice. No batteries. I can't even speak a word to heal her. I'll die soon when Amelia consumes my soul. I deserve-_ Jason thought.

"Shut up!" Hoarhiim grew uncharacteristically angry. "I won't tolerate another word! Use your telekinesis, boy! Just a little pull, that's all it takes! Yank out my orb! Touch it with a single finger! Do that, and earn your title! Become the Hero you were meant to be!"

Something about Hoarhiim's anger stirred an emotion deep in Jason's chest. Sadness. Anguish.

Beelzebub had told Jason that he attacked the Core. Jason had been terrified at the possibility that he killed Phoebe, yet here she was, alive, but dying. Jason had nearly failed her once, but he couldn't do that a second time.

Hoarhiim was right. Jason wouldn't fail her.

Jason wouldn't fail anyone ever again!

Pain surged through Jason's mind like electricity. The sounds of Beelzebub and Blaarjiim fighting echoed through the canyon, lighting up his surroundings. Hoarhiim's orb glowed in his bag like a flare in the darkness.

Reaching out with his mind, Jason panted like a dog as he expended what minuscule amount of energy he had left to tug at Hoarhiim's orb, but it rested deep inside Phoebe's bag, buried under an avalanche of minor trinkets and gadgets.

Releasing his pull, Jason gulped air desperately. _Hoarhiim! I can't budge it! What do I do?!_ Jason thought.

Hoarhiim made a pulling motion with his arms. "Flip the bag on its side toward you. My orb should fall out and roll downhill toward your hand."

Gears clicked into place. _Of course! I should have thought of that!_ Jason thought.

This time, Jason aimed for the whole bag, tugging and pulling and tugging some more. Beelzebub yelped in pain, and Blaarjiim bellowed angrily as the two of them traded more blows.

Suddenly, Jason's heart skipped a beat. Something he was doing must have worked. Phoebe's bag tilted onto its side, and Hoarhiim's orb dropped out, hit the ground without bouncing, and rolled toward his outstretched palm.

The orb came to a stop a few inches away, and Jason used all his remaining strength to stretch toward it.

_Come on, worthless body! Work! WORK!!!!_ Jason thought.

Jason's finger touched the orb.

A tingle shot down his arm. 

Power engulfed his body. The orb sucked into his palm as if he'd suddenly gained control over magnetism.

"Well done, Hero," Hoarhiim said with a smile. "It's time to pay my brother back in full."

"Attacking from the high ground... the mark of a coward! Allow me to meet you on equal terms, and you won't live to see the sunrise!"

Beelzebub jerked his whip forward again, then blasted several heat bullets from his finger, battering the female human around. "Not cowardice, but intelligence. Only a fool gives his enemy a fair shake. All is fair when it comes to war, little insect."

Blaarjiim gnashed his teeth and shot a shadow spike at the demon, but Beelzebub dodged it, letting the dark weapon fly past him harmlessly. The Baron of Flames countered with a concentrated blast of fire, turning the heat up as high as possible as he attempted to cook the dark god inside his shadow-shell.

Unnoticed by them, the Hero made contact with the orb of light. Hoarhiim's energy instantly flooded Jason's body. Jason's wounds regenerated as Hoarhiim yanked the globe into his palm.

Inhaling, the deity of light jumped to his feet and patted his chest.

_I made it. Barely._ Hoarhiim thought.

Hoarhiim trotted over to Phoebe and flipped her onto her back. "**Heal.**"

Life returned to her body, and Phoebe gasped for air. Hoarhiim stared at her while her broken arm fixed itself. "**Examine.**"

The power of Wordsmithing filled Hoarhiim's mind with tens of trillions of pieces of information regarding Phoebe's physical well-being. Hoarhiim concentrated and stared into the woman's brain, then nodded.

_As I suspected. Phoebe suffered a head injury at some point. I wondered why she was having such trouble standing and walking. Beelzebub likely didn't knock her unconscious. She must have passed out due to this skull fracture. Not to mention the blood building up in her parietal lobe._ Hoarhiim thought.

Phoebe's eyes fluttered open. "J-Ja...son? What-? Where-?"

"Not Jason. I am Hoarhiim." The deity of light glanced over at the two dueling combatants who would surely notice him at any moment. "It is dangerous for you to stay. I will send you somewhere safe."

"Wait!" Phoebe squeaked the interruption out, even as she followed Hoarhiim's gaze to the battle between Beelzebub and Blaarjiim. "If you're Hoarhiim, then where's Jason? Aren't you, well, him?"

"I have taken over Jason's body temporarily, in much the same way as my brother has taken over Amelia's. Jason will be quite alright. I apologize for rushing you, but, **Return.**"

Ignoring Phoebe's protests, the deity of light transferred her body to the Labyrinth in the blink of an eye.

With Phoebe gone, Hoarhiim Wordsmiths again, directing his spell at the Second Emperor. "**Heal. Regenerate.**"

The succubus coughed and shook her head as life returned to her soul. "Huh? What's going on?"

"I have healed you. Take your leave. Return to your kind. I must deal with my brother now."

The sound of Beelzebub yelping in pain as one of Blaarjiim's shadow lances struck his arm jarred Belial awake. She jerked her head over to stare at the battle in progress. "He... what? I don't understand."

"There's no time to explain. Leave." Hoarhiim used his telekinesis to hurl the succubus up toward the top of the pit. She yelped in surprise, but righted herself in midair and landed on her hands and feet with catlike grace.

Beelzebub and Amelia looked over, having heard Belial's shriek. Blaarjiim's jaw dropped. "What? The mortal is moving again?"

Blaarjiim's eyes instantly gravitated to the glowing orb held in Jason's hands. "Hoarhiim? You damned, meddling-!"

Beelzebub, unsure of what was going on, glanced warily at the Second Emperor as she pivoted to face him. "The devil-whore is up as well? I thought all three were down for the count!"

Belial curled up her lip. "You thought wrong. And I owe you for what you did to Phoebe."

Hoarhiim held up his left palm. "Belial, leave. I will handle both of-"

"Don't worry. It'll only take me a minute to pound this little man into pulp." Belial smashed her fist into her palm. "_Someone_ didn't learn their lesson the first time around."

Blaarjiim, having already stopped caring about his squabble with the Baron of Flames, pivoted to face his brother. "Hoarhiim! It has been many ages since both of us took physical form at the same time! Let the two demons hash out their differences."

"Agreed," Hoarhiim replied. "I warn you, brother... we share a deep bond. The lives you've stolen increased my power as much as they did yours. I tasted their pain within my soul. Our family ties end here and now."

"Haha, I wouldn't have it any other way," Blaarjiim sneered. "Calling a weak-minded simpleton like you a brother has always been my greatest shame."

The two orb-beings and their vessels slowly walked toward each other until they were only twenty feet apart. 

Meanwhile, Belial jumped at Beelzebub, shrugged off his fire, and delivered a bone-snapping punch to his chest. He slammed into the dirt and grass brutally, breaking many more bones in the process. 

Before Beelzebub could regenerate his body, Belial grabbed him by his leg and lifted him overhead, then hurled him into the ground with enough force to embed him five feet deep. The planet trembled from the impact.

Unsatisfied with the damage she dealt, Belial stretched her arm out and hurled punches into the shallow pit, smashing the suited demon repeatedly until she'd broken every bone in his body ten times over. Belial smacked her palms together and dusted herself off.

"I hope you've learned your lesson this time, regenerating Phoenix-boy."

Sleep took Beelzebub. The darkness of the shallow grave surrounded him, and Belial left.

Hoarhiim stared at his brother stonily, seeing past the soft face of Amelia deep into the corrupted evil of Blaarjiim's mind.

"You are vile. An aberration of nature. An all-consuming plague that annihilates everything it touches. Because of your lust for power, I have been forced to taste the misery of millions. I felt each of their screams as your so-called 'queen' tore them apart one-by-one. The boy does not understand how utterly irredeemable the two of you are... but I do. I know the depths to which you've sunk."

"Spare me your platitudes," Blaarjiim sneered. "Empty words from a vacuous mind. You play the arbiter of justice, the pure and holy crusader of light... but you are as willing to kill as I. The only difference is that you add qualifiers to make yourself feel warm and fuzzy. Murder is only acceptable if the one you must kill is evil. Isn't that right, brother?"

Hoarhiim sighed and opened his free palm. A tower-shield of light appeared in his grasp, one that he could summon with a mere thought. "I target those who commit atrocities. You attack wantonly. That is the difference between us. Ready yourself, Blaarjiim. I will unleash the full power of the Wordsmith upon you."

Blaarjiim formed a club of darkness in his free hand, one made of skulls and bones. "I wonder, Hoarhiim... which of us will win?"

"Destiny has already decided," Hoarhiim murmured.

In the sky overhead, the first traces of sunlight began to emerge.

_Morning is coming._ Hoarhiim thought.
