Part 113: Dragonslayer

While Camael jumps to her feet, Catherine yanks her sword of light from the ground. Valac slithers backward, while the other three Valkyries take up their positions between their sister and me.

"Catherine!" Camael says. "Keep thy distance. Avoid the Reaper's Scythe. It will tear out thy soul if it strikes thee!"

Catherine nods. She summons a shield of light in her left hand and charges at Valac, aiming to knock him off balance by throwing her weight against him.

Unlike Catherine, the other three Valkyries don't wear helmets. A short, pink-haired girl cries out in alarm. "Gah! Hey, Cath! Don't just go off on your own!" She creates a bo staff out of Holy Energy and follows her sister.

Camael's eyes follow the newcomer. _Sophia and Catherine are both too reckless. I need to help them. However, my mana is slowly draining from my wounds. I'll only slow them down in my current state._ She gestures to the other two Valkyries, both of them dark-skinned women of above-average height. "Hyacinth! Valac's minions badly injured me during the battle. Come quickly and heal my wounds!"

Both of the remaining Valkyries turn to me, looks of concern on their face. The one on the left has white hair like most other angels in Heaven, but the one on the right, nearly seven-feet-tall, has green hair like Uzziel.

The tall one nudges her white-haired sister. "Hyacinth. Don't worry. I'll keep the Reaper busy."

Hyacinth nods. "Alright. Once I finish, I'll suppress the demon with my arrows. Make sure Sophia doesn't do anything stupid."

Penelope groans. "That's impossible, but I'll try. We must avenge Uriel and return our mentor's honor."

"Aye."

The dark-skinned giantess stomps toward Valac, and all Hell breaks loose.

Catherine shoves herself against Valac, knocking him off-balance by using her shield as a battering ram. In the next instant, she swipes her sword downward toward his shoulder. A rush of mana fires from her blade, releasing a curved projectile that rushes at the Grim Reaper's chest. Valac turns slightly to the left. Catherine's attack gashes apart the hundreds of disgusting, fetid rags covering his skeleton, but misses his exposed bones.

Not waiting for the Reaper to counter her, Catherine dives under his robes and slashes right, intending to cut his leg off. Valac hops quickly, somehow righting himself with remarkable agility and avoiding her attack in the process.

A flash of movement.

Valac's fist lashes out faster than Catherine can react. He batters her helmet and knocks the daylights out of her. The Valkyrie tumbles several feet backward, but manages to right herself mid-sprawl. She lands in a cat-like pose and runs at him again, readying her sword for another attack.

Valac snarls. "You're resilient and tough; everything I hate in a female. However, your movements are stilted, destined to fail!"

The Reaper pivots on his heel to face the incoming threat. His Scythe rushes forward and deflects Catherine's sword. _Ting!_

He reaches out and tries to grab her. Catherine bats his skeletal hand aside with her shield and kicks at his leg. The unexpected counter trips Valac.

Penelope swings a massive two-handed mace of Holy Magic against Valac's spine. The undead demon slams into the grass, shaking Heaven violently. At the same time, Sophia bounces, twirls, and backflips over her giant sister. She pirouettes off Penelope's head and brings her staff down, smacking it square against what I can only presume is his skull. Valac's body holds no mass as he lies collapsed in the dirt, so it's impossible to tell which parts are his rags and which are his bones.

Catherine pauses. The armored woman glances left and right, seemingly looking for something. Suddenly, she bolts forward. Catherine dives at Sophia and shoves her aside as Valac materializes out of thin air and swings his Scythe toward the pink-haired Valkyrie. He misses by inches, nearly tearing off the back of Catherine's armor.

Camael narrows her eyes. _What was that? The Reaper teleported without hand signs. They knocked him to the ground, but did he disappear before he landed? I've never seen similar spatial magic in all my life._

I glance at the white-haired woman beside me as she furrows her brow and weaves a spell.

"Come, Hyacinth. We haven't much time. Heal faster."

Hyacinth flicks her gaze from my stomach up to my eyes, then back down. "Apologies, eldest sister. My healing spell is nowhere as potent as Uzziel's. I am not as well-versed in the ways of medical magic as she."

"Do thy best," Camael replies, a hint of urgency in her voice. Grunting, she quickly returns her attention to the other three Valkyries. 

Having somehow predicted Valac's reappearance, Catherine shoves herself off the ground and narrowly deflects the tip of Valac's Scythe with her shield as he swings it around to impale her. The Reaper's immense strength flings Catherine to the side, but she manages to land on her feet with only a minor stumble.

Everything I've seen from Catherine indicates that not only is she physically strong and agile, but quick-witted, too. However, she never says a word, and I don't know why. She might be mute.

Valac, now hovering a foot off the ground, pulls back and cackles as the three Valkyries regroup. "If this is the peak of your technique, then squeak and shriek, for your future looks quite bleak!"

Penelope reaches her hand out and rests it on Catherine's shoulders, stopping her sister from charging again. "No. Follow-up. I lead."

Catherine nods and allows her taller sister to take the lead, never showing any doubts regarding Penelope's abilities.

Valac's bundle of rags lies on the ground where the Valkyries toppled him before. However, while they still appear cut-up and torn from all of the earlier attacks he dodged, his new rags are in mint condition, the same as when Camael first attacked him.

Gears slowly click in my mind as I ponder the possibilities.

When I battled Satan several years ago, the First Emperor told me he could regenerate his body by dissipating one of the ten-thousand souls within himself. Given Valac's status as a fellow soul manipulator, I wonder if he might have a similar ability.

...No, that seems unlikely. When Satan regenerated, he rematerialized in the nude. He had to summon a new pair of clothes. While not the most critical detail, Valac reappeared with all of his rags on. Further, there was a short delay before his reappearance, almost as if the Reaper was casting an illusion...

Penelope takes several measured steps toward the Grim Reaper. Soon, they stand only ten feet apart. She holds her two-handed mace in a defensive posture, as if ready to deflect any attack he throws at her. Catherine stays to her right and Sophia to her left.

Valac giggles to himself. "Kekeke, look who's come to fight, a little girl with a twig. You should have brought a shovel for the grave you'll soon dig."

Penelope shifts her feet. A moment later, she tilts forward and swings her mace down, slamming it into the dirt. All of Heaven seems to shake as the ground spikes and explodes in front of her. A hail of soil and stones flies at Valac. The Reaper raises an arm to shield himself, but that proves to be a mistake.

Sophia motions with her hands. A ball of water materializes in midair. She stabs it with her bo staff, and a thin beam of liquid shoots out. Valac flies backward as the compressed water hits him with the force of a firetruck. The Reaper tumbles and rolls into the dirt as Heaven itself tears apart and tries to swallow him whole.

After Penelope's planet-shaker attack subsides and Heaven goes still, Valac rematerializes in midair above his fallen body. The Grim Reaper glances around hesitantly, almost as if waiting for a follow-up strike.

"Okay! My turn!" Sophia shouts. The Valkyrie flaps her wings, dashes toward the Reaper, and begins executing a series of spins, kicks, and flips as she bounces toward him.

"Sophia! No! Don't rush in!" Penelope yells. "Wait for-"

Valac follows the Valkyrie's movements, and at the last second, he lashes his Scythe out, aiming to skewer her in midair. Sophia executes an upside-down triple-spin, counters Valac with her bo staff, and uses the momentum of his attack to flip to his side and swing at his knee.

"Gah! Pesky chicken!" Valac trips awkwardly as Sophia's attack hits. He tries to grab her by the hair, but she twirls her staff and bats his hand aside. With another spin, she dives behind the Reaper and swings her staff in a wide arc. Her weapon slugs Valac's spine like a baseball bat. He folds over awkwardly and tumbles to the side, once again landing as a crumpled set of rags in the dirt.

After several seconds, slightly longer than before, Valac rematerializes in midair. This time, however, he keeps his distance.

"Look at all of you, ganging up on me. I'm just one little demon, so why won't you let me be? While this small one bounces around and giggles with glee, I've realized the lot of you are far too pesky for me!"

Sophia frowns. She takes a step back from Valac. "Hey, why does this weirdo constantly rhyme? It's creepy."

Valac's shrouded face betrays no emotion. He slowly raises his upper left arm and summons his lantern. "Kukuku... All this time, I've been holding myself back. I wanted to test your might after you talked all that smack. I don't like to brag, but my powers are quite prophetic. I guessed as much already, but your strength is simply _pathetic._"

While Valac's two right arms grip his Scythe and the upper left one lifts his lantern high, the lower left one begins weaving magic signs.

Camael's eyes widen. "Sophia! Get back! Catherine, Penelope, watch-"

Screams erupt from Valac's lantern. A hundred translucent faces rush out of it and tear through the air, aiming for each of the Valkyries, as well as myself.

Hyacinth yelps in fright. She stops her healing spell and throws me backward right as one of the ghostly apparitions slams into her back.

_Boom!_

The ghost explodes like a stick of dynamite, sending Hyacinth spinning and twisting. She tumbles across the ground and careens to a stop, while I, too, land on my ass. Her last-second save allows me to avoid one of Valac's kamikaze souls. It harmlessly explodes in midair.

After pulling myself to my feet, I wince and rub the back of my head. Hyacinth lays on her back; black scorch marks etched across her white armor. She swallows shallow breaths and blinks, trying to recover from Valac's surprise attack.

"Sister!" Camael stumbles toward the Valkyrie and pulls her up, helping Hyacinth to her feet. It takes a moment before she can shake the disorientation from her mind. "Can thou standeth?"

"A-aye. Ohh. My mind throbs..."

Camael nods. "Those were souls of the damned. They released mana when they exploded; you must have suffered some feedback. Ah! The others!"

I quickly turn to look for the rest of the Valkyries.

Catherine remains standing. She holds her shield up to protect herself. Her chest shifts forward and back as she breathes heavily. Behind her, Sophia and Penelope lie on the ground, almost catatonic from Valac's surprise attack.

Valac towers over the sole remaining Valkyrie. "Kukuku... your friends thought splashing me with water would bring me strife, and burying me in the dirt would end my life. However, my power is infinite; going beyond time and space. Now bow before your new god, as I reveal your face."

Another soul ripples out of Valac's lantern. This time, when Catherine flinches, waiting for an attack, it doesn't come. Valac leans forward, exposing the skull beneath his robes. He opens his mouth, and the soul enters his body.

Nothing happens for a few seconds.

Then, a bolt of lightning shoots from Valac's robes. It slams into the ground with a thunderous _crash_! Several more follow. One thunderbolt arcs outward and hits Catherine's shield, but it only nudges her back a few inches.

Valac gasps. He leans forward and curls his arms forward. "Haah... haah... yes... YES... **YES!!!** Such power can only come from the netherworld! I feel it in my bones, the creaking of muscles! The rebuilding of my body!"

Valac holds up his lower left hand. Mottled, grey skin begins rushing up his bones, covering his hand in nasty, rotting flesh. The Grim Reaper howls in delight as glowing red eyes appear beneath his robes.

"Ahahaha! Here it comes! Ready yourself, little girl!"

Catherine digs her foot into the ground.

Then, Valac disappears.

In the blink of an eye, he appears behind Catherine. The Valkyrie jerks in surprise and whirls to face him, but his meaty fist swings out and smashes her in the back, sending her flying forward, toppling end over end. Catherine drops her shield and sword as she plows into the dirt, bounces off, and sails into a boulder.

Hardly has the Valkyrie come to a stop before Valac appears in front of her, moving at incredible speeds. He yanks a hand out, grabs one of her arms, and lightly tosses the Valkyrie behind himself.

"Catherine!!" Camael howls in shock. She jumps forward to try and save the Valkyrie, but Valac appears before us, a fist raised.

"Ah-ah-ah. _You stay right there._"

I don't even feel the impact of his fist.

The Reaper hits me so hard that several of my teeth shatter and splinter to pieces. It takes several seconds before my vision is anything more than a mass of spots. At some point, I end up on my back in the dirt, but I don't know when.

Somewhere in the distance, the sound of muffled _clangs_ and _whumps_ unveil Valac's onslaught. The Reaper batters and bashes Catherine savagely, striking her as viciously as possible as he obliterates every last vestige of her defenses.

"This is what you get for defying me, foolish insect! Nobody makes a fool out of the Grim Reaper! Nobody!!"

I slowly raise my head to watch as Valac grabs her by both arms, raises her above his head like a doll, and slams her into the ground.

Catherine never once cries out or screams.

I pull myself together and slowly claw myself to my side. Camael gasps as she tries not to choke on the blood pooling in her mouth. "C... Cath... Cath..."

Valac holds the Valkyrie by her right leg. She hangs limply in his grasp as he titters to himself. "Nyehehe... No more fight left, hm? I suppose you are only a _woman_, after all. Useless like all the rest."

Motioning with his Scythe, Valac reaches its tip over and lightly pulls on Catherine's helmet. The dented and battered iron falls from her head and lands in the torn-up dirt below.

Finally, I get a look at Catherine's face. Her short, curly brown hair contrasts a massive burn wound going from her right eye all the way past her jaw, neck, and further into her armor than that. Her right eye sags noticeably... but the burns don't appear to be from Valac. They're old. Ancient, even. They tell tales of a battle from a different era.

Camael's thoughts come to me in slow, measured bursts. _No... Catherine... Penelope... Sophia... Hyacinth... please, one of you must fight. Don't let... don't let the Reaper win._

As if to answer her request, Sophia leaps to her feet. Blood drips from her nose, but she appears to have recovered from Valac's attack. Penelope rises as well, though the giantess appears less steady on her feet. She swoons back and forth and leans on Sophia's shoulder for support.

"Release my sister, foul demon!" Sophia yells. "I'll only give thee one warning!!"

Hyacinth appears at my side. "Eldest sister. Forgive my weakness. I'll heal thee at once."

Their sudden recovery astonishes me. It hasn't even been a minute since Valac swallowed one of his souls, but they appear to have regained their composure.

Valac turns his head back to gaze at Sophia. "Hmm? Do you want to fight? Foolish women. You don't stand a chance against the King of the Dead."

In the few seconds where Valac's attention leaves Catherine, her eyes bolt open. The battle-scarred woman rapidly motions with her hands and summons her inner mana. Valac turns to look at her just in time for Catherine to open her mouth.

_FWOOMPH!_

A wall of fire bursts out of Catherine's stomach, tears through her lips, and pounces at the Reaper's face. Valac's rags immediately catch fire. Reflexively, he drops his captive and jumps back in surprise. "EEEEEH! What- what is this?! Ahhh! It burns! IT BURNS!!"

The Grim Reaper flails around wildly. He drops his Scythe and screeches like a banshee as he tugs and tears at the rags covering his body. Catherine doesn't let up. The moment he releases her, she pivots in midair, lands on her feet, and continues her infernal onslaught.

Hyacinth begins to heal me, but Camael's gaze narrows.

_Sister. For thou to summon Jörmungandr's flames... thy rage must burn like never before. I am sorry that thou hath awakened such a primal hatred._

Catherine's single functioning eye presses together, and she holds up her hand to narrow the flames racing from her mouth. Valac screeches and falls on his back, futilely holding his hand up to try and stop her inferno. His rotted flesh melts away. His bones crackle and snap as the seconds pass. 

Finally, Catherine stops. She pauses to gasp for breath as the Reaper writhes on the ground. No longer does he speak a word. His mass of bones, now charred black beyond recognition, begin to curl together and snap as they cool.

Soon, Valac's body stops moving.

Several seconds pass.

Hyacinth finishes healing me. I jump to my feet and quickly fly over to Catherine's side, keeping a wary eye on Valac's bones.

"Catherine! Art thou alright? Hath thee any mana left?"

Catherine looks at me. She shakes her head. Slowly, she raises a trembling arm. Magical runes appear in the air as she begins writing letters with her magic.

_No mana. Fires burned. Reaper dead?_

Standing closer to her, I begin to understand the extent of her scars. Catherine's burns cover not only half of her face, but they extend down to her throat as well.

That's why she can't talk. The scar tissue prevents it.

Camael nods. "I think... thou hath defeated the Reaper. Be on thy guard. He may yet rise again and-"

"Sister! Sister, look!" Sophia yells excitedly. I turn to follow her gaze as she points in the distance.

Valac's undead servants, once embroiled in a brutal battle against Gabriel and his army, stand and hover silently, unmoving. Many of them hold their weapons above their heads as if to strike, yet they don't move.

One by one, each of them lowers their weapons. Looks of confusion pass across the faces of the undead and the living. Valac's servants blink their eyes and glance around.

"What's happening?" Camael asks. "Why aren't they fighting?"

Sophia's voice lilts. "B-because! Cath killed Valac! He lost control over them! It's a miracle from the Creator!"

"What?" Penelope's jaw drops. "I can't believe it. Is that true, Camael?"

I shake my head slowly in disbelief. "I... I don't know. Is it? What if Valac comes back to life again? Hyacinth, check his bones for traces of mana."

Hyacinth nods. She flies over to Valac, summons a knife made from Holy Energy, and gingerly pokes at his bones. Like logs that have burned for too long, the pile of black bones hisses and falls apart. They melt into a pile of ash and blow away into the wind.

"I don't sense anything. If he's still alive... I would be surprised."

Catherine nods. A wave of fatigue hits her. The brown-haired woman closes her left eye, stumbles, and falls forward. I quickly reach out and catch her before she hits the ground.

"Aye, sister. Rest, now. Thou hath earned it."

Sighs of relief pass between Sophia, Penelope, and Hyacinth. The three of them come together in a group hug and share huge grins with each other at a job well done.

Camael, however, lowers her gaze to the ground. Scuff marks, torn-apart grass and dirt, and shattered boulders tell the story of Catherine's epic battle. Camael's thoughts turn inward.

_The undead hath ceased their fighting. That means... all we needed to do was defeat the Reaper, and our brothers and sisters would have been spared. Instead, we slew thousands of them. We murdered them. The others haven't realized... but I have._

_Valac. Bastard. Thou hath planned this all along. Even in death, thy touch haunts us. I can only pray the Creator will damn thee eternally to the lake of fire. Burn... fall to ash... forever._

A few minutes later, all five of us fly toward Gabriel. He and Raphael stand facing one another. Raphael yells at his younger sibling. "Nay! Their souls are damned! We must kill them at once!"

Gabriel clenches his fist. "Raphael. Calm thyself. Camael slew the Reaper. The undead are no longer our enemies. Please, have we not lost enough of our brethren today?"

I flap my wings and land behind Raphael, along with the Valkyries behind me. "Raphael. Gabriel. What is the problem?"

The old man turns to me. Anger boils in his eyes. "Camael! Gabriel doesn't understand, but surely thou will. We must finish the job. Our brothers and sisters have been tainted by Valac's touch. We must send them to the Great Beyond at once. 'Tis the only merciful way to treat them, now."

My jaw drops. "What? Why?! Raphael, I... I can't believe thou woulds't utter such filth!"

Cherubs and Seraphs congregate around us. Upon hearing Raphael's words, they begin to whisper among themselves. Rumors spread like wildfire.

Raphael shakes his fist. "They're tainted, Camael! Can't ye see? Even if their faculties have returned-"

"I won't hear another word," Camael replies, cutting Raphael off. "We slew the Reaper. In doing so, we saved the lives of those he brought back. Their minds have returned. They control their bodies once again. There is no need for further bloodshed."

"Camael speaks the truth," Gabriel replies. "Brother, I know thy body took a beating during the battle, but ye needn't harbor such ill will towards our family. 'Tis a miracle we may embrace them once more. Had we focused on killing the Reaper sooner, we might have saved other lives. Why must thou wish death upon them now?"

Raphael grumbles for a moment. He quickly glances around at the thousands of cherubs surrounding us, as well as those floating above. The undead angels keep their distance. They bow their heads in sorrow, unable to process the horrible deeds they've committed. Looks of anguish stretch across the faces of many. Some turn their gazes towards Valac's resting spot with expressions that speak a thousand words of rage toward him.

Raphael squeezes his staff. The old man's bloodied robes, once a beautiful tapestry of wealth which encased his body in lavishness, are now torn apart like those belonging to a beggar. He appears haggard, worn-out, and exhausted.

"Gah... perhaps I am over-reacting. I know not. Thy victory over Valac seemed impossible. He repelled the attack of a million seraphs effortlessly. How dids't thee defeat him?"

Raphael's gaze settles on me. He thinks I killed Valac.

Instead, I step aside and gesture toward Catherine. The exhausted Valkyrie blushes and looks away as she leans against Sophia for support.

"Oh? 'Twas not thee who slew the Reaper, but Michael's apprentice?"

I nod. "Aye. Catherine unleashed Jörmungandr's balefire upon Valac. The Grim Reaper could not withstand the flames of the last dragon."

Raphael clicks his tongue. "Tsk. I see. Catherine, our thanks go out to thee. Thou hath saved countless lives today."

Catherine bows her head and motions with her hand, forming more signs in the air. 

_Protect family. My duty._

"T'was not duty, but valor," Gabriel says. He walks over to Catherine and kneels to hug her. "I thank thee, sister. We all do. Not only dids't thee save Michael's life against the demon hordes, but Camael's as well. Thy deeds mirror the ancient past, when-"

Gabriel halts. He pulls away from his embrace of Catherine, and a look of awkwardness passes over his face. "Ah, that is... I didn't mean to bring up..."

Catherine glances at Sophia, then hastily writes, _Ancient era. No memories. Do not worry._

Gabriel nods. He stares at Catherine for a moment, then traces his fingers across her burns. "Already, thou hath given up so much for thy family. Never will we forget thy bravery."

The Archangel of Power shuffles back to Raphael's side. His gaze turns distant as he internally curses himself for dredging up ancient scars.

Catherine motions in the air. _Michael. Where? Alive?_

Raphael nods. "Aye. He is recovering. I am sure thou wisheth to see thy brother soon. However, first, we must sort out the mess in Heaven."

The old man glances warily at the undead. Unlike those untouched by Valac's magic, the skin of the undead have begun to turn ashen and cracked. Their blackened eyes give them a horrifying appearance, almost as if they have become agents of evil. Even the more open-minded cherubs keep away from those touched by Valac's soul manipulation.

"I am not so certain that allowing these... these _Damned_ to live is the best course of action. Still, that devil's black magic appears to have faded. Maybe this aged one is too tired to think properly."

"Aye," Camael says, nodding. "Thou hath earned a rest, brother. Ah! Satan. What happened to him? Dids't thee defeat him?"

Raphael smiles, but the expression appears hollow. "In a manner of speaking. He will not bother us anymore."

"Excellent. Well, since the dirty business has concluded..." Camael shifts her sore shoulders and sighs. "I shall begin the sorting of the dead. We will burn the demon bodies and hold a twelve-hour vigil for each of our brethren who perished today. All deserve the highest honor."

Murmurs go through the crowd. One of the seraphs raises his hand. "Eldest sister, if I may be so forthcoming... only twelve hours? Is it not customary to hold a one-week dirge for any angel who perishes, no matter the reason?"

Camael stares at the young man for several seconds. "Aye. However... given the number of us that have fallen... t'would be decades before we finally concluded our mourning. We might finish by the end of next year if I expedite the..."

The Archangel of Divination trails off. Looks of anguish cross the faces of those present. Several cherubs shake their heads, unable to believe my words. She closes her eyes. _Tsk. Only twelve hours... such a short vigil for each of our brethren would sully the light of Heaven. Have I forgotten how important every one of our lives is?_

After a moment, she opens her eyes.

"I apologize. It occurs to me that we mustn't desecrate the dead any further than the graverobber already has. If we were to only mourn for twelve hours, I believe that would give the demons a victory, one they do not deserve. Nay. Instead, perish the thought. We will offer a _two_ week vigil for each of the fallen. We will etch their names into every rock, tree, and carving in Heaven. Never will we forget them or their noble sacrifices."

Sighs of relief pass through the crowds. Despite the dead lying around unmoving, several angels openly smile at my words.

Camael smiles back, as do Gabriel and Raphael. However, in her heart, an inferno of rage burns.

_Valac surprised me, but in the end, he was a false prophet; a devil consigned to the eternal flame. We will mourn for every single fallen angel, no matter the circumstances. We will rebuild and heal the wounds of today at any cost._

_And... for those demons who escaped... we will hunt them. We will scorch their burrows, tear them limb-from-limb, and make them rue the day they turned their backs on us._

_I will kill every last demon, and nobody will be able to stop me._

Camael's smile stays frozen on her face, even as she balls her hand into a fist.

"Sister?" Sophia pokes me. "Is something the matter?"

"No. Let us begin gathering the bodies. The sooner we begin, the sooner we may rest."
