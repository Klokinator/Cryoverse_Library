Part 338: Tension and Longing

Jason teleports himself and Belial back to the edge of the Fortress of Retribution, just inside its crystalline walls, but away from the populated areas. He observes the inside of the Hatoraxia for a few moments using his Wordsmithing and listens to the conversation between Kordonis and Miikil while his mind-wife works in secret.

"Alright," Phoebe says. "I've updated the files. You should be able to transform all the existing 'Spatial Pylons' into their updated configuration."

These pylons, the same ones she made during the Stormbringer Invasion to 'harden' the space around Tarus II and make it impervious to Warpers opening portals, now sit in hundreds of pre-selected locations around the world of Tarus II, all casting a net of spatial-strengthening magic to envelop the planet.

Thanks to her technical expertise, Phoebe adds a new function to them, one which requires a mass-update of all their internal workings.

Jason nods, while listening to Kordonis's words. "Alright. Thanks, honey."

Belial raises an eyebrow when she hears Jason say that, but immediately realizes he's talking to his mind-wife and not her.

"What's up?" Belial asks. "You planning something?"

"The Volgrim came here ahead of schedule," Jason answers, "But I had Phoebe prep a possible solution to them in case they ever were to reveal themselves. While we chatted with that Kordonis fellow, she finished her work. One moment."

Jason inhales deeply and closes his eyes. He focuses his mind, reaching into the files his mind-wife has given him, and producing a technical readout of the Spatial Pylons before and after Phoebe's updates.

After absorbing these thoughts into his short term memory, a feeling of technical expertise momentarily swallows the Wordsmith's thoughts. He opens his eyes and utters a Word of Power.

"**Update.**"

His spell permeates the hardened spatial area surrounding his world, infiltrates its central nexus, and travels around to all of the many hundreds of Spatial Pylons dotted around the Planet's crust. 

Moments later, the Wordsmith nods.

"Done."

Belial raises an eyebrow. She glances around, from left to right, and even up toward the distant speck of light representing the Volgrim spaceship.

"Uhh... what did you do?"

"From our perspective, nothing has changed." Jason explains. "But from the perspective of anyone more than half a mile above Tarus II's atmosphere, our planet will start to become hazy before turning completely invisible. No technological or magical item will be able to pierce the veil and observe us from afar. It should cut down on Volgrim spying. I also have to make something similar for Kelkin, but the moonbase isn't nearly as high of a priority."

"Oh!" Belial says. "I understood about half of that. If it was Phoebe talking, I'm pretty sure I'd have understood nothing. You're good at this. You should become a teacher."

Jason rolls his eyes. "Teachers don't get paid enough. Wouldn't suit me. Err, well, they _didn't_ get paid enough in the society I came from. I'll have to change that in mine."

Jason and Belial fall silent. They watch the sky for a few minutes until the speck of light vanishes, when Kordonis's spaceship jumps to Warp and leaves the system.

"How troublesome." Jason mutters. "The Volgrim are onto me, but we're not ready to deal with them. I don't have any defenses set up. The only reason they haven't attacked is because they probably aren't too sure of what I can do. Hopefully, my little planet-go-bye-bye trick will worry them a little bit. According to what I just heard, they aren't even able to replicate my little 'Laprox Field,' though they've theorized it's possible."

"The Volgrim want to use you." Belial comments. "Don't trust them. They made all sorts of promises to the demons. They told Satan he could rule over the Earth if he helped them, but they failed to mention they would eventually glass the planet and leave him with a lifeless rock. The only reason they haven't attacked you is because Unarin sees you as a useful tool."

"Do you know Unarin pretty well?" Jason asks.

"Not especially." Belial answers. "I've spoken to him only a few times. Or rather, Satan did. I didn't have anything to say. I just watched them talk while I stood off to the side like a good girl."

She pauses.

"I must say though, Unarin never dismissed me. He seemed to think my shapeshifting abilities were quite formidable. He even commented to Satan that if I were to partner with the Changeling division, he would repay the demons greatly."

"Changelings." Jason repeats, his tone pensive. "I assume Satan didn't take Unarin up on that offer?"

"No, _I_ didn't." Belial corrects him. "I have known many liars, deceivers, and backstabbers. I never trusted Unarin from the moment I laid eyes on him. He was polite to me... perhaps too polite. He never looked at me in a sexual way, or like a piece of meat, but perhaps as something lower... a barely functional tool. He looks at all 'mud-dwellers' in that way. To Unarin, and the rest of the Volgrim, you're either insignificant, or useful. You're not a living, breathing entity with a right to live."

Jason listens quietly to her words. He waits for her to finish, then raises a question.

"Can you show me what the Volgrim leaders look like? With your shapeshifting powers?"

Belial blinks. "Oh? You want me to play dress-up for you? This is a first."

"I only want to know what my enemies look like," Jason says, waving her teasing away with a hand. "You don't have to if you don't want to. I can always extract images from someone's brain, or something."

The former Second Emperor smiles cutely. She wraps her arm around Jason's waist and pulls herself right up next to him. "If you say _please_, I won't tell Phoebe. It can be our little secret."

The Wordsmith rolls his eyes. "My mind-wife is always watching, Sam. She keeps me honest. Now, knock it off, will ya?"

"Am I not cute?" Belial asks, pulling away. "Come on, be honest. Most men can't help but stare when I walk past. It's not insulting or anything. I actually find it quite flattering."

"Oh, you're extremely attractive," Jason says, "and I might sneak peeks if I weren't married. But, eh. I dunno. You're always 'out there,' you know? You don't leave much to the imagination."

Belial glances at the tiny strips of cloth covering her boobs. "Hmm. What, you like Phoebe because she wears more clothes?"

"I like Phoebe because she's the woman I love." Jason explains. "She could wear anything and I'd stare- oh, dammit, now we're making her blush. This is causing my mind-wife secondhand embarrassment, you know."

The Wordsmith crosses his arms. "So, are you done horsing around? You're pretty, Sam, and that's the truth. But I'm a married man. So... let's stay friends, okay?"

"Monogamy is such a _human_ thing." Belial protests. "Most demons faff about with whoever they want. I'll never understand why humans insist on staying committed to one single person. Don't you know variety is the spice of life?"

"Are you even talking about you and me?" Jason asks, tilting his head slightly to the side. "...Or is this actually about Phoebe? Are you thinking if you can convince me to become polyamorous, then you and Phoebe might...?"

Belial blushes. "No! It's not like that! Geez, never mind. Forget I brought it up. I'll turn into the Volgrim suits, so let's drop the conversation."

Jason smirks. "Spirit-Phoebe says you're cute when you blush."

"AHEM! First Founder, Unarin! Get a good look at him!" Belial says, becoming even more flustered at having her thoughts seen through. She quickly activates her magic, changing her physique into that of the leader of Volgrimkind.

"Alright, alright, we'll move on," Jason says, as his smile recedes. In its place, a look of concentration appears as he examines Belial's transformed appearance. "He's shorter than I expected. Is this his actual body size?"

"Unarin is only about six and a half feet tall." Belial says, her voice a perfect imitation of Unarin's baritone. "Or, 198 centimeters."

Jason gestures to the thick hair-like tendrils spilling down 'Unarin's' front and back. "What are those?"

"These are called 'Haa'ti' by The Ascended." Belial says. "From what I understand, they have a bunch of useful properties. I've never examined them in-depth, though, so I don't know what exactly they do. I only know because I heard someone else mention them once."

"Haa'ti." Jason repeats. "I've actually heard about the Volgrim sub-species from Marie Becker. Do you know her?"

Belial shrugs. "Name doesn't ring a bell. But then again, I don't pay much attention to random people. If I don't talk to someone a lot, I tend to forget about them."

"...She was the leader of humanity at one point." Jason says. "During the Energy Wars. Apparently, she ruled Earth for quite a long time."

"Ohhhh..." Belial says. A faint light appears in her eyes. "Right... yeah... Marie Becker... I don't know. I sort of remember something about a Marie Becker... maybe. But look, Jason, I've met a _lot_ of human leaders. They all blend together after a while. When you've slept with as many humans and demons as I have, they stop feeling special or significant at a certain point. Arthur stands out, as did Joan of Arc... for similar reasons. Maybe a few other Heroes... but generally, normal humans who weren't Heroes? I didn't care much about them."

"Mmm. Fair enough." Jason says. "Err... I guess this is sort of rude to ask, so ignore it if you want, but why did you sleep with so many people? Do you still do that now?"

Belial shrugs. "Oh, I didn't love or care about pretty much any of them. There were a few humans and demons I liked a little... but they died. Then Satan died, too. And... well, I haven't felt much interest or passion ever since."

Belial's current appearance, that of Unarin, contrasts with the sadness in her eyes. She lowers her head and sighs.

"Succubi and incubi... we are different from other demons. We experience deep 'cravings' that prompt us to cavort with others. At the lower ranks, we aren't even immortal like other demons. We die if we don't absorb any 'life energy'. But since I'm an Emperor, I don't have to deal with that anymore. Sometimes the cravings flare up, but I just ignore them until they go away. I used to vent on my husband, but I can't really do that anymore. I've been frustrated and hollow for so long... I think I've even forgotten what it meant to love someone."

"They always die." Belial continues. "Everyone I love. Yet somehow, I stick around. Phoebe will die too, you know. I don't know when or how, but if I get too close, something will happen to her, like it did to Satan. That's why I have to keep my distance."

Belial demorphs from Unarin's form back to her original self, sighing deeply.

"Sorry. I shouldn't throw that on you."

"No." Jason says, offering a faint smile. "It's fine. I'm glad we're close enough that you feel secure around me. You can tell me stuff like this whenever you want. For what it's worth, I don't think you're cursed at all. Bad things happen, you know? Terrible things. Whole worlds die. Freaking space zombies attack. You spent a lot of good years with Satan, so if you continue to live on... so will he. In your heart."

"Sometimes..." Belial says, her tone heavy. "When... when the pain gets to be too much... I turn into him and stare at myself in the mirror. I make myself into my fallen husband, hoping to keep his memory intact."

She shakes her head.

"But it's not easy, Jason. I spent a few thousand years with Satan, following our exodus from Heaven. I've spent a hundred thousand without him. His memory... it grows fainter every day. I worry that if enough time passes, I'll forget him the same as I have everyone else. At some point, I thought I could even move on... but then... then Amelia returned. She revived him and turned him into her puppet."

The succubus plops down on a nearby rock, a boulder with a flat top.

"I wasn't prepared to see my husband. I was shocked. Elated. Terrified. Worried. So many emotions all at once. Then he attacked Phoebe, and... and I had to make painful choices. I knew if I killed him, he wouldn't die. He wouldn't _really_ die... but I'd already lost him once. And then... after everything concluded, and he was still alive... after Polaris's star detonated, I thought the Creator had finally showed me some faint mercy."

Tears well up in Belial's eyes.

"But... but he didn't! He took Satan away from me, again! I had to watch him die, turning to dust in my arms! He fell apart and I was- I was completely helpless again! I couldn't heal him, I couldn't do a thing! I was just useless, like always!"

Jason walks over to sit beside Belial. He wraps an arm around her shoulder, disables his T-REX, and pulls her in to cry against his side.

"It's okay. I'm here for you..." Jason says. "**Projection.**"

He summons the spiritual projection of his mind-wife, who also embraces Belial from the opposite side. Her body, faintly corporeal and able to interact with physical object to some small extent, projects an aura of warmth onto the succubus.

"We're both here for you." Phoebe says. "It's okay to cry, Sammy..."

"I just- I miss him so much!" Belial sobs. "The pain never goes away... it never stops hurting..."

Jason rubs her back and falls silent, allowing her to vent her feelings unabated for as long as she wants.

She hesitates for a moment before standing up, pulling away from Jason and Phoebe both.

"Did you... hear what Kordonis said? About Ose?"

"Yeah." Jason nods, remaining seated. "She's still alive. But we knew that already. She fell into Mephisto's clutches, the poor bastard."

"As much as I love that she ended up a slave to some other demon," Belial mutters, "it's not a bitter enough ending for her. Bael stole my chance to kill her. I still want revenge... not only on Lucifer, but Ose too."

"Yes. I'd like that." Belial says, steeling her eyes. "But, moving on. What do you think about Unarin's offer? He's giving you such a large 'donation,' the debt you'll accrue may prove insurmountable."

Phoebe nods. "The First Founder clearly wants to bind Jason to himself as tightly as possible. He wants to put humanity on a leash and make us thoroughly indebted to the Volgrim's 'goodwill.'"

"What comes after the Plague?" Jason ponders. "Let's say I do help the Volgrim fight this terrible zombie menace. What then? We can't seriously expect them to leave us be afterward, can we? We'll go from being the heroes who saved the galaxy to the Volgrim's enemies. It seems likely Unarin will simply turn on us right afterward."

They consisted of five factions, each warring against each other some some small or large extent, but when faced with an external enemy, they united in an instant. They placed shackles on us to limit our development so we couldn't threaten them... and I need not tell you about the Humanity Containment Project. They turned humans into little more than cattle and sheep for demons to eat, yet restricted your birthrates to the point where my people had to live on scraps."

Jason snorts. "I don't know how scary some stupid space zombies can be, but I bet I'll be able to clean them up pretty easily. However, it would be best if I took my time and made sure to leave them as a threat the Volgrim can't overlook. So long as I keep their threat at roughly equal levels to the Volgrim, both will have to leave humanity alone to fight each other."

How could a galaxy-class civilization fall so easily to a mere bunch of drooling, slowly-shambling zombies? Mephisto summons 'zombies.' Whatever the Plaguehosts are, they must be much more terrifying to have broken the Volgrim as badly as they have."

"I imagine Unarin will tell me more about the Plague when I meet him in person..." Jason ponders. "But... hmm."

"Problem?" Belial asks.

"It's just... I've had too many demons try to take over my brain and enslave me or Hope, recently. My powers would be far more valuable to Unarin if he could definitely control me. We're talking about a species which is basically a Class III on the Kardeshev Scale. Who knows what kind of crazy fucking mind-control techniques they might have? Nanites, brain implants, Psionic abilities, to name a few. They probably have shit I can't even imagine. If I go to meet Unarin in the heart of his Empire, do you _really_ think he'll be okay with just a simple 'chat,' especially when I pose a major risk to him?"

"If he fears you, he might take drastic measures." Phoebe points out. "Ose planned to capture me and Daisy both. She was going to use us to manipulate you."

Jason taps his lip. He frowns several times, thinking to himself about many possible eventualities.

"Say... do you think the Volgrim know about Hope?"

"They know about you." Phoebe says. "And they interrogated Ose. I'd say it's a 50/50 whether they do or don't."

He'd fear capturing you, because another clone could take your place, but would also be much angrier than you and out for revenge. He could kill you, but he'd never know if there were other Wordsmiths out there. Even if he captured you, one of your clones could free you, potentially without even breaking a sweat."

"But that's the thing... I only have one clone, Hope. And frankly... I don't want to make any others. Hell, without Solomon's Crown, I can't. The Crown made it possible to perfectly recreate every atom of my body, soul, and magic. Phoebe can replicate a lot of its functionality, but not its cloning potential."

The Volgrim have been around for millions of years, and Unarin has, too. We shouldn't underestimate the cunning of a monster who's led a civilization for eons."

We know they monitor the Hell Harbor system, because that's how they encountered you. They must have found out the truth when they interrogated Ose, Mephisto, and the others."
