Jason couldn't believe his eyes. It only took two short months for Hero City to become unrecognizable to him. Buildings ten stories tall loomed over areas where small houses and mud huts once stood.

Phoebe drove her new truck along, pointing out various additions to the city.

"...And over here, I made these things I like to call _Sidewalk-Water-Spitters_. If a building catches fire, we can hook up these long hollow snakes to them and shoot water all over the fire."

"Nice invention, Phoebe. I think the name's a bit convoluted, though. How about calling them _Fire Hydrants_?"

Phoebe beamed an award-winning smile at Jason. "Great thinking, Jason!"

Jason leaned back in his seat and smiled smugly. "I have my moments. How'd you managed to create these buildings so quickly? Normally, you'd need a team of a thousand workers slaving full time around the clock to build a single structure."

Phoebe shook her head. "Oh, not anymore. I created a device that creates stone and brick out of energy. I still haven't thought of a good name for it, but we can build walls by aiming a gun at them. The Crown is useful! I barely know what I'm doing half the time, and it fills in the gaps!"

Jason reached up and plucked Solomon's Crown off his head, then stared at it absentmindedly. "Yeah. It's a damn shame we only have one of them."

A minute passed. As Phoebe and Jason stared at the road, Jason scratched his head.

"I don't know why I never tried doing this. **Copy!**"

Solomon's Crown stayed in Jason's left hand, but in his right, a second crown materialized, identical to the first.

"I can't tell if I'm smart for thinking to do this, or stupid for not trying it sooner. Oh, well." Jason stuck the new crown on his head and glanced around.

Nothing felt different.

_Mental Map._ Jason thought.

No change.

Phoebe glanced at Jason as she pulled into a garage next to their home. "A second Crown?! That's great!"

"It's... a dud."

Jason's voice flattened as he pulled the new crown off his head. "Doesn't work. It looks identical to the original, but it's a useless piece of gold. Fuck. I bet it's because the Crown is a magical artifact."

"Oh. Never mind, then." Phoebe leaned back and stretched in her seat. "Aaahn... god, I'm tired, Jason. How about you?"

Jason handed her the fake crown. "I'm tired too — long day. I can't rest just yet, though. We don't know if demons will attack or not."

The two of them hopped out of the truck. Phoebe and Jason donned each of their crowns. She seemed disappointed that hers was little more than an ornament, but it made her look like a queen, so she rolled with it.

"Jason, can we visit the Factory? I want to talk to Hans."

"Sure. I've got nothing better to do, for now."

"Hey, cutie, yer back!" A voice from behind made Jason jump in fright. He whirled around to see an odd-looking lizard standing behind him.

Ah, he was a salamander. His red-scales glowed under the waning sun.

"Is this yer boyfriend?"

Phoebe walked around the back of her truck and wrapped herself around Jason's right arm. "That's right! This is Jason, my fiance. Jason, this pretty boy here is Ogie. He also has a wife named Jeera."

"Uh, pleased to meet you." Jason held his left hand out, and the salamander stared at it with suspicion.

"Wassat? You want a handout, kid? I ain't made of money. Jeez, I thought the Hero would have enough to get by without begging."

"It's a greeting. We're supposed to, uh, shake hands."

"A greeting? But we already did that!"

Jason retracted his hand. "N-never mind."

The salamander smiled at Jason with an odd look in his eye, then turned to Phoebe's truck. "What the hey? Did you get this thing refurbished by someone? Looks brand stinkin' new!"

"I kinda destroyed my old ride," Phoebe said. "Sorry. Jason built me a new one."

"Ah. No worries, then. I'll fix this lady the same as I did the other. Trucks're all the same to me." Ogie's tongue flicked out. "Saaay, you wouldn't happen to have any of those sweet-rolls left, would ya?"

Phoebe's body sagged. "Sorry. I'll make some tomorrow, Ogie."

"No problem! I shouldn't eat so many anyway. Jeera says I'm puttin' on the pounds! Bahaha!" The Salamander reared up to almost Jason's height and rubbed his gut. "It's better if I take a couple o' days off the sweets!"

...

Several minutes later, Phoebe and Jason strolled down the street, hand in hand. Three Felaris boys batted a ball around with their paws, trying to play kitty-in-the-middle. The unfortunate middle-child swiped at the ball but always came up short. "No fair! You guys are too fast!"

Jason gazed around at the gaggle of humans, monsters, and other mythical creatures alike all hanging out and playing together.

"You must have found a lot of monsters willing to join our cause while I was gone."

Phoebe shook her head. "Not especially. Many of the newcomers lived on human worlds Neil reclaimed. A small number moved out of the Labyrinth at the recommendation of family members already living here."

The Factory, a giant building that took up an entire city block, rose just ahead. "I take it everything has gone well, then?"

Phoebe didn't reply for a few seconds. "Aside from Beelzebub's ambush, yes. Perhaps too well. I constantly worry that the peace and harmony we're enjoying won't last."

"You can make anything endure, but you have to struggle and sweat."

"Well, I'm willing to do that much."

They entered the factory through a set of automatic doors at the nearest corner, only to hear someone swearing in a German accent.

"Verdammt! Get off mein vorkbench! Pest!"

A Felaris girl hissed at Hans as he squirted her with a water bottle. "Kss! Jerk! Lemme sleep here! Leave me alone!"

"You're in zee way!" He squirted the cat-woman again, and she swiped at him with her sharp claws.

"Hans!" Phoebe walked over to him, mostly ignoring the cat-person. "What's going on here?"

She had hardly asked the question before the Felaris girl casually batted a nearby beaker filled with blue liquid off the table. Hans' eyes bulged as he tried to catch it, but he was too late. It fell off the desk and crashed to the floor, erupting in a smoky fizzle.

"Gahhh! Zat culture took two weeks to mature! Zees pest is causing me nothing but troubles!"

The felaris girl licked her paw innocently, flicking her tail around. "It was shiny."

"Sandy..." Phoebe rubbed her head and sighed. "Can't you go curl up in a corner and let Mr. Wagner work in peace? He's a busy man."

The cat-girl pouted. "I don't wanna! It's warm on the desk!"

Her face was extremely catlike, with a mouth and nose that jutted out and eyes like slits. However, her 'paws' resembled human hands with short talons sticking out. A thin layer of fur coated Sandy's body, but she wore human-like clothing. She was a weird amalgamation of human and cat that made Jason a little uncomfortable.

Phoebe crossed her arms. "Go home. Don't make me grab a bucket of water."

The Felaris girl met Phoebe's gaze for a few moments, then hopped off the desk and walked past Jason, groaning all the while. "What a paaaain."

After she left, Hans grumbled and cursed as he leaned down and tried to scoop some of the already-dried liquid into a beaker. He wasn't successful. "Shiza. Two weeks of work lost. Verdammt Felaris, always causing me trouble."

The thin, wiry German stood up and suddenly noticed Jason's presence. "Oh, Mein Fuhrer, apologies for mein conduct. I wasn't expecting any guests so late in zee day."

"Hans, I told you not to call me that."

He frowned, then waved off the complaint. "Right right, it slipped mein mind. I meant to say Mein Lieber, ja."

"Yeah. Anyway, Phoebe, why'd you want to come here?"

She paused to look around Hans' workshop. "Where's that new design I had you working on?"

"Oh! Zee new suit! Ja, I made some progress on zee thing, but it was quite difficult to tweak. Zee technology is beyond mein level of expertise."

Hans walked over to a workbench and picked up a circular object with several wires sticking out of it. It was about the same width and circular shape as a compact disc, but with a height of thirty stacked atop one another.

"Zee protective capabilities are limited, but I believe zat with time, it could be a formidable weapon against zee demons."

He placed it in Phoebe's outstretched palm. She studied it for a moment, looking everywhere at the seamless object with only a faint groove where the top popped off.

"Hmm. Looks the same as when I left it with you."

"Ja, ja. I have no Crown of Knowledge like you, so I make do with what I have." He walked over and picked up a clipboard with fifty papers stuffed in it, and handed it to Phoebe. "Here are zee new armor designs."

Phoebe stared at the page for three seconds, then shrugged. "Um, Jason... I can't read his language. Not without the Crown, anyway."

It was only now that Jason realized all the writing on the page was in German. He started to remove the Crown, but paused. _Hang on a moment, I could give Phoebe the Crown, but isn't there a better way?_ Jason thought.

Naturally.

"**Languages.**"

Jason spoke the word, aiming at the three of their minds simultaneously. After a moment, Phoebe blinked and rubbed her head. "Aaaaooow... what was that?"

"I think you can read German without the crown now. Give it a shot."

Phoebe nodded and glanced at the clipboard. "Oh! I can read it perfectly! Wow... Wordsmithing is versatile."

"To say the least."

Jason scanned the documents along with her as she flipped the pages. Thankfully, the Crown allowed him to read at lightning speed and comprehend the information at levels beyond his brain's usual potential. Otherwise, he would fall behind his wife. Even without the Crown, she devoured the information with ease.

The paper read:

_The Environmental Adaptative Armor is a nano-technology-based suit that can fit a user of any size by forming dermal shielding around their body. It's lightweight and acts as a second skin, protecting the user from physical damage. While the suit has limited power, it's possible to extend the duration of the power cell by placing mobile power transmitters nearby._

Jason noted with interest that its functionality resembled his old, discarded lightsaber design.

_I propose that we create different suit variants for different combat units to maximize their warfare capabilities. The demons have hundreds of monster types at their disposal, and so should we. These suits have a secondary advantage in that our soldiers can wear them under their civilian uniforms and activate the armor even during a surprise demon attack._

He continued reading the rest of the report, glancing every so often at Phoebe's face.

_I'd never have thought of this. Phoebe, you're smart and beautiful, yet you don't give yourself enough credit for creativity._ Jason thought.

Phoebe finished reading the documents. "Excellent work, Hans. You've outdone yourself. Continue working on the new armor. Do you have anyone working on those Recall Markers?"

"Ja. They're already done."

Hans reached under his desk and plopped a tiny pill-sized piece of metal in Phoebe's hand.

She smiled and hopped excitedly. "Oh, Hans! I knew I could count on you!"

"Ja, ja, German engineering is truly something to behold with awe."

...

Later, Phoebe and Jason trudged toward their palace, arm in arm. She looked at him for a moment, then looked away, as if she had something to say but didn't know how to say it.

"What? Felaris got your tongue?"

She glanced over to where the three boys were playing. Now they were swinging sticks at each other and pretending they were sword-fighters.

"J-Jason... kids are nice, aren't they?"

"Huh? Oh, sure. Why do you ask?"

A long pause followed.

The wind rippled down the street, colder than when Jason left. Phoebe shivered and pulled a little closer.

"W-well, you know... kids are... they're innocent and fun, and they remind me of my childhood."

"Uh-huh..."

_She's trying to take this conversation somewhere. I don't think I need the Crown of Solomon to follow the dots._ Jason thought.

"Say, Phoebe... I was gone for two whole months. I don't suppose you got a new boyfriend or fiance in that time, didja?"

Immediately she recoiled in horror. "What?! No! I-I would never!"

"I know. I'm messing around with you."

The palace doors approached. They climbed the ten steps toward the doors, but Jason paused before entering.

"You like kids, huh?"

Phoebe blushed madly. "Sure. Kids are wonderful..."

"Great. Let's get married next week."

Dead silence.

Phoebe's face froze into a mixture of a thousand different emotions.

"Y-y-you don't mean... _next_ week, _next week_?!"

"Yeah. As in, seven days from now. I thought about you every day while I was gone. I was all alone in the Labyrinth, and then when I was fighting Satan, and his Demon Council appeared, Beelzebub said he attacked the Core. For a while, I thought... I thought maybe... you'd..."

Jason's voice caught.

_I almost lost Phoebe. She worried about me, too. I don't want that feeling, not ever again._ Jason thought.

Phoebe swallowed hard. "Jason. I love you. I don't want to wait another week."

"Alright. Me neither. Let's get married tomorrow."

She smiled widely. "No, we can put off the marriage until next week. But, tonight. Tonight. Let's make a baby."

Oh.

Oh. _That's_ what she meant.

Sometimes Jason forgot that he was still only an eighteen-year-old guy.

"What about your vow of-"

She pressed a finger to his lips. "The Gods are dead. The Church is gone. I persisted in chastity for a hundred thousand years. It's time for me to free myself from the past."

Jason smiled, as Amelia's face appeared in his mind. Mentally, he forced himself to wipe her away.

"Yeah. Me too."

"Amelia. Amelia."

Hope muttered in his sleep before lurching up in bed, waking up from a deep dream filled with fear and anguish.

"Amelia! She's dead. I killed her. No. Hoarhiim did. Jason did. Why? She didn't need to die. There could have been another way. Wordsmithing. It could change her mind. I could have made her a better person!"

Hope swept his feet over the side of the bed and exhaled. "She's gone. There's nothing I could have done. Nothing at all."

His gaze wandered across the room to a mirror on the wall, barely visible in the darkness. Only a sliver of light from the doorway illuminated the room.

_I don't even know whose face gazes back at me. I'm Hope, not Jason. Jason loses one woman but returns home to another. I get nobody. I don't matter to him. I'm just a copy. A hallucination._ Hope thought.

Hope climbed out of bed and wandered over to the doorway, cracking it open. He peeked out to see Neil sleeping on his desk. His hand rested on a freshly written piece of paper. Hope slipped out of the room, walked over, and glanced at it.

A wall of dead soldier names appeared on the left side of the page, along with a few checkmarks for only a small portion of them on the right. At the top of the page, the title read, _List of confirmed dead. Check off if condolences were sent._

Hope looked away from the page and stifled a sigh. _Five thousand dead. My failure. Jason's failure. Instead of saving lives, we've only taken them. We tracked Amelia down and murdered her, and in the process, left the people in the Core defenseless. No wonder Neil was angry. Who could blame him?_ Hope thought.

_We should have stayed here. Amelia would have done _whatever_ to the demons, and we could have protected the Core. Hell, I probably led the demons right to her. Ahh! Not me, Jason. Jason did that. I can't keep pretending we're the same person._ Hope thought.

Hope slipped back into the room and yawned, sitting on the bed in the darkness.

_Amelia. I want you back. I made a mistake. I can't live without you. Jason can keep Phoebe, but I want you. There has to be something I can do with Wordsmithing!_ Hope thought.

Almost immediately, an obvious idea came to him.

_Of course. Wordsmithing is the power of the gods. I can bring her back, just like that._ Hope thought.

A confident smile spread across Hope's face. He aimed his mind at the center of the room. Gathering his thoughts, he pictured Amelia, her smile, her adult body, and everything else about her.

"**Resurrect.**"

For a moment, nothing happened. Then, in the darkness, a _fwoomph_ of air rushed in, and a woman's body appeared and fell to the ground.

Hope's heart caught.

_I... I did it! I did it!!!_ Hope thought.

"Amelia! Amelia!" He jumped off the bed and rushed over to her. "Hey, wake up! **Light!**"

The room brightened dramatically, as if he had activated a hundred-watt lightbulb.

When he finally caught sight of Amelia, the color drained from Hope's face. Her frigid body lay limp in his arms.

"No... no no no... Amelia. Wake up. Why are you so cold? **Resurrect! RESURRECT!**"

He yelled again and again, but her body stayed still in his arms. Everything about her was identical to the woman in his mind, but still, she slumped unmovingly.

"Hope?" A voice from the doorway made the clone freeze. Neil stepped in, wincing at the bright light. He stopped and stared in silence at the woman in Hope's arms. "No. You didn't."

Hope whirled around. Panic spread across his face.

"I... I needed to t-try! I had to see if I could bring her back! I can't! She's dead!!!" Hope's body shook as he tried to rub warmth into her skin. "She's so cold, Neil! I thought Wordsmithing had all the answers! I thought it could do anything!!!"

Tears streamed down his cheeks. He pressed the dead woman against his chest. "Again. I feel like I've lost her again."

After a moment, Neil leaned down and rested his hand on Hope's shoulder. "I'm... I'm sorry. I wondered privately if you could bring back the deceased. It seems even you have a limit. Hope... I lost my wife and children to the passage of time. I know what it means to lose someone you love. To want to do anything to see them again, yet lack the power to bring them back."

Hope stared silently at Amelia's face. "She looks peaceful. Like she could wake up at any moment."

"They always do," Neil murmured. "But they won't. They can't. The ability to bring back the dead is a power reserved only for the Creator."

Neil squatted down to Hope's level. "The only way you can save people from death is by protecting them. If you fail at that, with all the power you possess, then you will suffer and wallow in pain for the rest of your life. Just as I have."

"You?" Hope turned to stare at Neil's stoic face. "What do you mean?"

"A General leads his troops to death. Any victory earned without losses is a miracle. He must be willing to lose some of his best men. In that sense, I have killed many. Their passing weighs on my soul. The Creator will judge me when I fade."

Hope nodded slowly and looked away. "I don't believe in God. But, then again, maybe I don't know anything about the universe. Maybe you're right."

Neil reached over and brushed Amelia's hair from her face. "Come now. You were never able to bury her, were you? Dig the hole yourself. Put the dirt over her body. Burn her sleeping image into your mind and never forget. Her memory will give you the strength to protect the others you care about."

A long moment passed. Hope nodded.

"Don't you worry. I will _never_ forget the woman I love."
