Upon awakening, Jason's eyes fluttered open, and a yawn escaped his mouth. He gazed at the ceiling for several seconds as memories of the previous day entered his mind. After being chased by monsters and stumbling onto a magical orb of light, he had been so exhausted that he slept like an anvil. Not even a marching band with a full orchestra could have woken him up.

Without thinking, he turned his head to the left and winced as the brilliant light from his orb blinded him. "Gah!"

After a few seconds, his eyes adjusted to its light. When he picked up the orb, he noticed it felt much colder than it had yesterday. Most likely, he had finally recovered from the cryogenic sickness that had stolen his sense of touch away. Compared to how disoriented he had felt when he fell asleep, he felt like a new man.

His eyes lingered on the orb. He squinted mightily and pulled it closer to peer inside of it. He didn't believe it was an object that could be described by science. It gave him abilities that were beyond human. It must have been some legendary S-Tier item, but he hadn't a clue where it came from.

The closer he looked, the more details he was able to pick out. Inside the orb were hundreds, thousands, perhaps even millions of microscopic life forms. Like worms, racing in all directions, the minute organisms zipped around, moving to and fro, and their excitement increased the closer he stared.

A feeling in his body made him pull the orb away. A strange sound, like that of a gnat, buzzed in his ear. He swatted at it, but the noise stayed and coalesced into something more akin to a voice.

"...you?"

The voice was a whisper in his ear. He could barely make out a single word.

"...are you?"

It continued speaking, and he strained to hear it.

"Who are you?" The voice cleared up and spoke directly to him. The timbre was that of a middle-aged man, deep, and full of knowledge. He had experienced many things in his life, yet there was also an animalistic growl in his voice, as if he had merged with the consciousness of an animal spirit.

_Me? I'm Jason Hiro. I'm a time traveler, I suppose._ Jason answered the voice, figuring it must be coming from the orb.

The voice didn't respond, and the energy from the orb dissipated, leaving him wondering who it was. Perhaps the mysterious man was satisfied with his answer. Maybe he'd never know.

He climbed off the dusty bed and twitched his nose in disgust at his clothes. They were still covered in the flaky debris from the cryopod, as was he. His body felt gross, but until now, he hadn't had his wits about him. Figuring it was worth a shot, he spoke a single word to nobody in particular. "**Clean.**"

As expected, his T-shirt, jeans, and underwear instantly changed and became as clean as if he had tossed them in a washer and dryer for an hour. He repeated the process, but for his body, and smiled as he also ended up as clean as if he had just climbed out of the shower. Heck, he felt completely refreshed!

He took a moment to reorient himself, then glanced at the several cold slices of pizza leftover from earlier.

_Should I eat them?_ Jason wondered.

_Nah. With my power, I shouldn't stoop to eating leftovers._ he decided.

"**Pizza.**"

Once again, a sumptuous pie materialized in midair and landed atop the previous box. He didn't hesitate before diving into a second meal. Pizza had always been his favorite food. Even before he entered college, he tried to eat it as often as possible.

It only took him a few minutes to eat most of the food. After he finished, he rubbed his stomach and pondered the meaning of life for a moment.

Then, he directed his gaze to the leftovers.

_If I leave this food behind, I'll be putting myself in unnecessary danger. The smell of leftovers might attract those monsters. I'd prefer it if they never found out I was here._ Jason thought.

"**Erase.**"

With a single word, he eradicated the existence of the pizza and banished it to the shadow realm. Several seconds passed as he contemplated how he could figure out his powers without any trouble. Eventually, he shrugged and jumped to his feet.

_I can't stay here forever. Even if this place looks abandoned, a monster might stumble in here by complete accident. I would be much safer if I could locate other humans. Surely I can't be the only person left alive, right?_ Jason thought with determination.

A minute later, he stepped out of the facility and held the glowing orb close to his chest, allowing it to light his path. It didn't take long before he arrived back in the El-Dorado treasure room and decided to head down a different passageway. He had no idea where the other two led to, but if the storage facility was a dead-end, and if the place he originally entered from led back to the statue monster, he'd take his chances with one of the other two passages.

As he traveled along, he slowly realized he'd entered familiar territory. The walls of the Labyrinth were earthen again, and the glowing rocks seemed to have vanished. The hallway continued for at least a few miles before opening into a series of corridors and doors. Something about the place gave him the creeps. It resembled a _prison_.

A dozen doors lined the right wall, and at the end of the row was another entrance, far bigger than the rest. He walked up to the nearest one and shoved it. Unlike every other door until now, this one didn't budge.

He pushed harder and shoved with all his strength, but it still wouldn't move. Finally, he pressed his back against it, and grunted out loud, "Okay, orb! I need your strength! Give me the power to open this goddamn door—!"

His words cut off abruptly as it suddenly yanked inward, making him stumble back and slam onto the ground. His spine screamed in pain, but after a few moments, he breathed a little easier and pulled himself to his feet.

Only, this time, he froze in horror. What awaited him was... no... he didn't want to believe it. At the back of the room, there was a cryopod, identical to the one he had left. It was broken and torn to pieces.

The walls were covered in blood.

The room yawned before him. The smell of iron forced him to stifle his gag reflex, and chunks of pizza threatened to crawl back up his throat. The sight inside of this room made him never want to eat meat again.

The smell of death was thick, though not fresh. Much of the blood was hardened, crusty, and brown. If someone were to try and clean the room, they would need a paint scraper, and it'd be a hell of a job. The corpse torn to pieces and scattered all over the place was unrecognizable and desiccated. Whatever had killed them, it had happened a long time ago. The cryopod looked like something had smashed through the window on the front and torn the occupant out with force.

Saying a quick prayer for their soul, Jason quickly stepped back out of the room and leaned against the hallway door, and his breathing arrived in shallow gasps. _If that room housed one of the other cryopod occupants, then that can only mean..._ he thought with dread.

He didn't want to check, but he had to. The room next door was in a similar situation. A violent murder, under the same circumstances, began to reveal a pattern. He could barely make out long, blonde hair on a severed head across the room, but he didn't want to go anywhere near the corpse. He quickly checked the other chambers and found a similar situation in the third, fourth, and fifth doors.

The sixth, however, was different.

Inside, the cryopod was intact, and opened up. Whoever had been inside must have escaped, since there wasn't a horrible bloody mess to be seen anywhere. There was a journal on the floor, placed directly in the center of the room, laying in such a way that anyone walking in the room would see it immediately, and it was facing the door, so they'd know to pick it up.

While it felt like this was a booby trap, curiosity was Jason's weakness, so he crept over and nudged the book with his foot. No Indiana Jones-style boulders came rolling at him, so it was probably safe. He quickly snatched the journal up and retreated to the hallway, his heart a pounding mess.

The first page had some writing, so he carefully read its contents.

_Date: Unknown._

_Location: Unknown._

_Mental state: Hopeless._

_My body is weak. I haven't eaten anything since I emerged from cryostasis, and if I don't find something soon, I'm going to die. I'm ready. I have to accept it. I always wanted to grow old and die in my sleep, but that isn't going to happen._

_Anya and Faraday were my friends. That thing, the monster... Gressil. It took them. Their screams were awful. What a horrible way to go. Waking up, the jaws of a demon tearing them to pieces before they even have a chance to fight back, and all they could do was let out pitiful screams._

_I don't want to go out like them. I have to try and escape, or die trying. If I can stab it once, before it kills me, I'll at least have my revenge._

_Well, it's about time. My body's getting weaker. If I don't go now, I won't ever be able to leave. If only I could see my daughter again... just one more time._

_Amy. I'm sorry, sweetheart. I hope Cryotek's payout helped you. I hope you lived a good life, but... daddy won't be coming home after all._

_I deserve what's coming._

After reading it, he flipped to the next page. Only two words were legible.

_Umi watches._

Over and over, but the handwriting style was different. It wasn't as feminine. Perhaps someone else had found the notebook and scrawled the words?

Every page had the same words repeated. _Umi watches._ A hundred times per page. The further through the notebook he flipped, the more frantic the words became.

Silently, he set the notebook on the floor where he had found it, and said yet another prayer. He didn't know what had happened to that man, but he hoped he had eventually found peace.

As he left the room, he spun those two foreboding words around in his head, like marshmallows over a fire. Looking back on things now, he'd felt that same sensation of eyes tracking him everywhere he went. Was it just his imagination? Paranoid delusions from the ever-present darkness shrouding the Labyrinth?

The final rooms held no surprises. Apparently, only one man had managed to escape without the monster tearing him to pieces. He offered more prayers for the fallen and sighed. He wasn't even a religious person, and in fact, he was something of an atheist, yet now he found himself wishing a god existed to save people from this hellish nightmare.

Finally, only one door was left; the one opposite from where he had started. Whatever awaited him on the other side, hopefully his trusty Orb of Light could protect him.

The door slid open easily as he pressed it inward. For a moment, confusion overtook him as he looked around the room. These gave way to terror and panic once he realized why it seemed so familiar.

A cryopod rested against the far wall. He quickly walked over to it and examined it, spotting an open hatch on the back.

Jerking his head to the left, he spotted a second closed door. The other Cryopod rooms didn't have any entrances other than the main lobby, but this room did.

He was in his original Cryopod room. But the door that he had just entered through was not here before.

_The Labyrinth is shifting dynamically. The idea makes no sense, but neither does shooting light out of my hands or my sudden ability to summon pizzas._ Jason thought.

If his theory was right, then he had a few options. First, he could return to the El-Dorado room and pass through the final door he had missed. Second, he could travel past the statue monster room and go back to the giant door, hoping the smaller door on the right from earlier would take him somewhere new. Third, he could try and use his abilities to shove open the supermassive door instead. He doubted that was doable.

_If these are my options, then the choice is simple; no way am I going to take a chance with statue monsters! I'd like my innards to stay inside my body, thank you very much._ Jason decided.

Turning around, he left his cryopod room behind and headed back through the cryopod chambers toward the El-Dorado room. However, his heart sank. The long corridor back was gone.

_As expected, the Labyrinth has shifted. I guessed correctly._ Jason thought.

He cautiously and silently sneaked around, checking this passage and that. Dozens of doors greeted him. He didn't open them.

After a few minutes of wandering around, he stopped and listened. A sound of feet pattering approached. Two pairs, in fact. It wouldn't take long before whoever, or _whatever_ was approaching spotted the light from his orb.

"Oy, Tvoorik! Didn't ya notice? Them doors was open. What idiot leaves the doors open?"

English. Jason recognized their words. The speaker's voice didn't sound human, however. His timbre reminded Jason of a wolf's growl. He quickly whispered to his orb, "**Dim.**"

Its light dissipated to nearly nothing, allowing him to see a flickering of torchlight approaching. He ducked around a corner and hid behind a wall outcropping, making himself as tiny as possible while hiding the orb in his shirt.

Another voice replied. "Are ya kiddin', Ylvusk? Ain't nobody dumb enough to leave a door open 'round these parts. Gressil'd kill us!"

The footsteps drew closer. Jason's heart smashed around in his chest like a pack of rats trying to escape. As long as the voices walked straight down the corridor, they wouldn't see him.

"Yeah, but this place is the Popsicle Block. I thought only one fool was still left in 'ere?"

Jason's eyes widened as the two beings appeared, one of them holding a torch. _Imps._ He'd read enough fantasy to know the difference between various monsters when he saw them. They were, without a doubt, not humans. Their short little bodies, perhaps three feet tall, waddled along, and their fat eyes rolled around in their heads. Bright red skin, wispy hair, and two tiny little horns gave Jason all the other visual cues he needed to fit the puzzle pieces together.

_They're demons!_ he realized.

The two imps stopped and sniffed. Tvoorik spoke; a hint of wariness in his voice. "You ever wonder what Gressil does with 'is victims? I bet he lets 'em go. You know how Gressil is."

"Oh, come on," Ylvusk groaned. "Keep your conspiracies to yourself."

"Nah, nah, I'm serious," Tvoorik continued. "He's been offin' the popsicles one by one over the last few years. But I got ta' thinkin'... what if he wasn't offin' 'em, eh? What if he was faking everything? Putting on an act?"

Ylvusk's expression turned sour. "_Why_ would Gressil do that?"

"Dunno! I'm just thinkin' out loud, ya know? Don't tell me you don't have any doubts!"

"I don't," Ylvusk said, shaking his head. "Shut your trap and keep walking. We've got three more patrols to do before we earn our allotment for the day."

"Psh. You know that's just how the higher-ups keep us in line, don'tcha? It's the ol' carrot and stick treatment. I read about it in a book once!"

"Oh, stuff it, ya stupid knob! I know you ain't never read a book in your whole life! Don't throw the shrakh all over me just 'cause it works on the other dummies."

"I'm serious!"

"And I'm a succubus! Come on, move it along! I ain't got all day to listen to your globberin'."

The two imps continued walking down the hallway. Jason held his breath the entire time until their voices faded into the distance.

Once he couldn't hear their voices anymore, he sneaked down several hallways, taking random directions as he tried to find a way out of this labyrinthine series of passages. The different hallways blended together. Soon, the glowing rocks appeared once again, illuminating different paths.

Suddenly, he arrived somewhere completely unexpected.

A massive chamber, over one hundred feet square, held a statue of a young girl with an umbrella. While she raised it above her head, gold-colored water bubbled up from the top of it and dripped down, barely missing her body. Thousands of flowers surrounded her, but the ones closest to her lay withered and dead.

The look on her face was that of acceptance. She'd seen things in her life, things she couldn't help. Her eyes were young, yet worn out, tired of a fear-filled existence. Most importantly, she was _human_ and not a misshapen monster. The realization that the only humans Jason had found in this Labyrinth were statues and corpses filled him with dread.

A giant golden door across the room caught his attention, but he ignored it. Something about the girl pulled his eyes to her.

The closer he walked to her, the more he noticed that the flowers, choked and withered though they were, slowly grew back as the light from his orb touched them. This effect was more muted as he walked nearer to the statue and discovered why. In the girl's other hand, the one not holding the umbrella, she clutched a black orb. Unlike his, hers sucked the light in around it.

_An orb of life, and an orb of death. The two are opposites. One drains life; the other breathes consciousness back into the world._ Jason realized.

He didn't want to go anywhere near that orb of darkness. Everything about it screamed _danger_. If he were the protagonist in an action movie, it would surely be the ultimate final weapon of a villain.

"Why, hello there."

A voice spoke up from somewhere in the room. Jason dropped to a crouching position to make himself a smaller target as he looked around. Nobody else was there.

"Come, now. Don't be rude."

The voice continued, and now Jason realized it wasn't speaking to him audibly, but inside his mind.

"Who's speaking? What do you want?" Jason answered the voice, and turned uneasily to the orb. _It can't be._ he thought.

A face appeared in his mind, one of a man shrouded in darkness. He smiled, and the whites of his teeth spread open, just below the fire burning inside his eyes.

"What do _I_ want? No. What do _you_ want? Speak truth."

Jason considered his question for all of three seconds, before standing back up to his full height.

"I'd kill for another pizza." he replied.
