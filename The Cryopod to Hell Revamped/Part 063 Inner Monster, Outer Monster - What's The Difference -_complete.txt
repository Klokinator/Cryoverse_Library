The walls of Meldora, little more than low-quality bricks slapped together with mud, stood a mere seven-feet tall. Hope peeked out from the forest and squinted at the four-dozen orcs milling about outside the city's gate. Alongside them were several goblins and even a harpy perched on the wall. She didn't have a good foothold on the soggy bricks, but she clutched them tightly with her foot-claws anyway.

One orc, dressed in slightly more elaborate armor compared to the rest, laughed. "Bahaha! Stupid Lorvos! He think we save him? What idiot! Me no stick neck out for bloodskin!"

"You said it, boss," a goblin laughed. "Hope the fleshbags gut him real good! That's what he gets for getting caught, am I right? Hehehehe!"

Hope frowned. _Wow. Tons of security. Shouldn't be a problem to slip by, but if this is the group outside, I can only imagine how many will be inside the walls._ Hope thought.

He whispered under his breath, "**Invisible.**" His body became transparent, little more than a blur in the wind. He dashed from the tree-cover to the city without being noticed and hopped the wall. Using his Wordsmithing, he spoke. "**Convert. Convert. Convert. Convert.**"

Over and over, for each guard outside, Hope said the word. It took him almost a minute, and he coughed. _Aren't there supposed to be 50,000 monsters in this city? Jesus Christ. It'll take me forever to convert them all. There has to be a better way._ Hope thought.

Immediately, an idea appeared in his head.

_If I say the word 'Convert,' it will create an active effect. However, maybe I can make it passive instead. That's what I did with my telekinesis. I might be able to mind-control enemies by looking at them. Hopefully, that will work._ Hope thought.

Nodding to himself, Hope uttered a word of power. "**Conversion.**"

He slid up against a straw hut. An orc grunted inside the hut, and Hope could barely make out a man's muffled whimpers. Hope's stomach churned as he realized what the monster was doing to the man. _Disgusting. What the hell? Maybe Neil was right about them. Do I want pigs like these on my side?_ Hope thought.

He aimed his mind at the source of the grunting, then slipped around and entered the front. The man was tied down to the table while the orc had his 'fun.'

"Release him."

Hope spoke, and the orc paused. It pulled away from the man. "Yes, master. Me do as you say."

"Untie and ungag him. Christ. That's horrible. You know what? Go jump in a pool of ice-water. Freeze to death for all I care."

The orc kept his back to Hope, but nodded dumbly. "Yes, master. Me free hooman. Me freeze in water."

Hope watched silently as the orc untied the man, then walked outside. The man lay on the table shivering and whimpering for over a minute. "Hurts... hurts..."

"**Heal. Forget.**" Hope Wordsmiths and gnashed his teeth together. "Maybe if you forget the trauma, you can live a normal life once I clear out this city."

Before leaving, Hope frowned. _I shouldn't leave him here. An orc might find him. I can't waste time running him back to the portal either, but maybe I can speed up the process._ Hope thought.

Hope spoke a single word. "**Return.**"

The man opened his mouth in surprise and vanished a moment later.

Hope spoke again. "**Locate.**" His mind traveled out of the city and flew over to Neil's camp, where the man appeared in the middle of some confused soldiers. The soldiers stared at the man, and he stared at them. Several seconds passed before the soldiers took him to see Neil.

_Great. Neil will understand._ Hope thought.

Hope pulled away and returned to his body. _No time to waste._ Hope thought.

He slipped outside the tent and glanced up at the planet's sun. It beat down on the surface like a vengeful phoenix. The heat must have been a hundred degrees out, and it was worse inside the buildings.

The houses on the outskirts of the city were little more than tents and huts, but deeper inside the city, they transformed into mud, brick, and stone. One of them, a church, stood taller than all the rest. It was only now that Hope spotted the source of the fire from earlier. A massive pile of human bodies sat outside the church, its flames rising dozens of feet into the air. Nearby orcs grabbed human hostages, bound their limbs together, and hurled them onto the bonfire to burn alive.

The victim's screams sent chills down Hope's spine.

"Oh, god. Oh, god. I'm gonna be sick."

Hope watched in horror as a little girl screamed and cried. An orc carried her bound body to the flaming heap, and her eyes widened in terror. Several of her friends lay motionless on the mound, their lives long gone.

Neil's words came back to Hope. _Demons respect strength._ Hope thought.

Hope's stomach trembled. _Strength? Is tying up defenseless children and hurling them onto a fire considered strong?! It's cowardly! Pathetic!_ Hope thought.

The orc grinned as he walked toward the fire. "After cook you good, you taste real nice. Burned human meat the best. Kids taste like yum. Hehe."

Hope aimed his mind at the orc. _You're mine._ Hope thought.

The orc paused in front of the fire. His eyes glazed over. "Uhh... what was doing?"

Hope looked around at the hundreds of orcs standing around, laughing about this and that. One of them glanced at his ally with the girl. "Hey! Mulpu! What you wait for? Throw on cook meat and grab next one!"

Hope shivered. _Even invisible, I can't walk over there and talk to him to tell him what to do. Oh! I should order him around with my mind!_ Hope thought.

"**Telepathy.**"

Hope aimed his mind at the big orc again. _Bring the girl to the green tent, then attack that loudmouth. Tell him you want to keep the little girl for yourself. Call him barf-skin. That should piss him off._ Hope thought.

Mulpu nodded dumbly. He turned around, walked toward Hope, and set the crying child down.

The other orcs scowled at him. "What big idea, Mulpu?! Follow order, stupid hog-fucker!"

Mulpu reared up and roared. "Shut up, barf-skin! This girl mine! Me fight for her!"

The other orc cocked his head. "Boorf-skin? What that?"

Hope facepalmed. _They're so stupid they can't even recognize an insult. Mulpu, tell him his mother was a goblin._ Hope thought.

"Your mother a goblin!! She ugly like demon ass!"

The other orc's eyes widened. He yanked a sword out of a nearby barrel and raised it above his head. "How dare you insult mother! Me make rug from your skin!"

The two orcs leaped into combat, howling like angry wolves. While they fought, Hope reached down and picked up the little girl. Her eyes opened in terror as the invisible assailant grabbed her. "Eee-!"

"Don't speak," Hope whispered. "I'm a friend. I'm here to save you."

Despite the terror on her face, the child nodded and stayed silent.

Hope turned her invisible as well, and dashed away to a nearby hut. He was relieved to see nobody inside.

"**Revert. Revert.**" He undid the invisibility on both of them, then untied her wrists and feet. "Hey there, cutie. What's your name? I'm Hope."

The child, barely seven years old, stared at him silently for several seconds. "M-Marisa..."

"Great. Marisa, who are the people that run the village? Do you know?"

Marisa stared emptily at him. "Mama. They hurt mama. And papa. They put them in the fire."

"I know, sweetheart. I'm sorry. You know what? Never mind the questions. I'm gonna put you somewhere safe, okay? **Return.**"

He sent the little girl back to Neil's tents. "Two down, God knows how many to go."

Hope made himself invisible once again and slipped out of the tent. This time, he walked over to the tallest stone-building in the city.

"**Adhere.**"

With a single word, Hope made his hands and feet sticky and began climbing the walls like a spider.

Several seconds passed. Once Hope reached a decent vantage point, he gazed intently at the city sprawled out beneath him.

Mulpu's body lay motionless. Orcs surrounded and kicked him, battering him left and right. "Stupid shrakh! Fell for a hooman, did ya? Bahaha!"

Hope didn't blink. He muttered under his breath, "Piece of _shit_ Orc. Fuck him. He deserved to die."

He froze.

_Whoa? Did I say that? That's not me. I shouldn't like death. He's an orc. He doesn't know any better. I sentenced him to death. Even if I didn't kill him myself, I ordered him to die. That's sick. Messed-up._ Hope thought.

He didn't have time to dwell on the thought. Several orcs yelled at once. "Oy, Ligna! The tiny one got away! She musta crawled!"

A giant orc with a black ponytail, apparently named Ligna, scowled. "What?! She couldn't have gotten far! Look around! Search the tents! We don't waste little person meat around here!"

Hope scrutinized the large orc. _He must be their commander._ Hope thought.

A sound behind Hope made him shiver. He turned his head and nearly screamed. A goblin climbed the wall beside him, sniffing the air. "Human... I smell a human..."

Hope gathered his wits and mind-controlled the goblin. _Tell me where the human leaders of the city are._ Hope thought.

The ugly little monster paused and blinked. His eyes glazed over. "Leaders. City. Burned alive. Only weak ones left."

_What?! That's awful! How many humans are left?_ Hope thought.

The goblin blinked slowly. "Only a few thousand. Takin' 'em to the fire pit, we are. Not long now. Tasty meat. Lorvos is gone. No reason for us to stay. Return to Labyrinth."

Several people screamed. The orcs readied themselves to hurl the next batch of squirming people into the blaze.

"No!" Hope leaped off the building and slowed his descent with telekinesis. _You, orc. Release her. You, orc. Release him._ Hope thought.

Hope quickly sent thoughts and orders to each one. The dozen orcs blinked their eyes and yawned. "Huh? Why me burn bodies? No need burn. Put on ground. Save for later."

Ligna spotted the new batch of turncoats. "Aw, come on! Not you hound-fuckers too! Put the bodies in the fire! It ain't hard to do."

Hope hardened his eyes. _Kill your leader. He's in the way._ Hope thought.

The twelve orcs immediately spun and drew their swords, then advanced on the commander. His annoyance vanished, and deadly anger replaced it.

"Hey. What are you shrakh-heads planning? This some kinda mutiny?"

His subordinates charged him, but the commander didn't raise a weapon. Instead, he dropped to one knee, making himself a smaller target. The first orc that drew near got a fist to the groin. Ligna flung that one behind himself with an expert-level maneuver that would make a martial-arts master weep with joy. The next eight followed similar fates. By the time he got to the last three, the commander snarled.

"You lot are actin' like you ain't never trained before! Mindless idiots! Who taught you to fight? I'll have his head!"

Hope quickly returned the humans on the ground to Neil's camp while Ligna's was busy. _Interesting. Their cognitive abilities decrease dramatically once I mind control them. I've seen orcs fight before. These guys are pathetic. I guess mind control isn't the catch-all solution I thought it was._ Hope thought.

The commander took out two of the last three at the same time, then punched the final orc's head so hard it flew off and crashed into the dirt. The headless body collapsed, and Ligna stared at it silently.

"Somethin's goin' on here. I don't like it. Smells like magic."

He spotted the missing humans. "What? Where'd the tarks go?"

Hope continued to watch from beside the fire-pit. _The orc commander, Ligna, is more intelligent than his underlings. He can form full sentences. He took out his minions like they were nothing. I'll control him and see what happens._ Hope thought.

A moment later, Hope connected to Ligna's mind. _Obey._ Hope thought.

The big brute took a step back and recoiled, then winced. "Ohh, head hurts. What the devil?"

Several orcs and goblins came running up from the city entrance where Hope had arrived. "Boss! Boss! You okay? Why followers attack?"

Ligna rubbed his eyes. "Gah. I hear a voice. In my head. Shrakh. I don't want... to listen..."

Hope frowned. _He's resisting my Wordsmithing? He's only an orc. That shouldn't be poss-_ Hope thought.

Suddenly, Ligna's voice roared in Hope's mind. _GET OUT OF MY HEAAAD!!_

Hope stumbled back and landed on the ground with a _thud_, disturbing the dirt. He yelped in surprise, causing several of the newcomers to turn toward him. One goblin yelled, "Oy! Did you 'ear that?"

"Hooman voice! Someone's there!"

"I don't see nothin'."

Ligna shook the fog out of his head. "Idiots! He's hiding! Look for him! Might be trickin' us. Use your noses!"

The goblins sniffed the air, and the orcs narrowed their eyes as they squinted and tried to spot him. Hope jumped to his feet, and one of the orcs pointed. "There! The dirt! It moved!"

"I saw it too! Somethin's movin' in the air!"

Ligna punched his palm. "I knew it. Some tark is tryin'a screw with me. Get him! Don't you shrakh-heads dare let him escape!"

Hope quickly levitated in the air with telekinesis, but in doing so, he spotted a horde of orcs approaching the burning pile. Someone must have signaled them.

One of the goblins sniffed. "Something's moving. The air's shaking. I see it... I see the human!"

Hope winced. The monsters began to converge on his position as the goblin kept pointing at him. _I guess even invisibility isn't infallible. If the goblin's using his nose, though..._ Hope thought.

"**Anosmia. STENCH!**"

With two words, Hope sealed off his sense of smell and unleashed a vile odor that raced around the town like a mad hatter. The entire army crumpled to its knees. Even the orcs, who loved the smell of dead bodies, couldn't control themselves. They doubled over and gagged violently, wretching up meals from the last few days all at the same time, which only added to the horrid stink in the air.

Hope gazed at the crowd of enemies. _I feel nothing toward them, and I can't excuse their actions anymore. They deserve all the pain they're suffering. Neil is right._ Hope thought.

The Wordsmith began mind-controlling orcs one-by-one. _Fight your fellow orc. Fight. Fight. Kill each other. Do the world a favor and bring an end to your disgusting existences._ Hope thought.

Despite the tears in their eyes, each mind-controlled orc stumbled to his feet and drew his sword, then attacked the enemy closest to him. Chaos ensued.

Swords and steel met flesh and bone. Orcs and goblins alike screamed in pain as their limbs ended up hacked to pieces and sprayed all over the ground. Their blood mixed with the dirt, creating muddy trenches that sucked their feet to the ground. Several orcs slipped and fell in the chaos, only for their mind-controlled allies to pounce at them and end their lives.

Hope grimaced at the death occurring all around him. However, a hint of excitement appeared in his eyes. _Yes. Yes! Tear each other apart. I'll save the humans while you lot suffer and gag._ Hope thought.

He hovered higher into the air and used his Wordsmithing to locate any survivors he could, then teleported them back to Neil's camp. Any time an orc emerged from a tent or stumbled toward the chaotic battle, Hope mind-controlled and ordered them to fight their former allies.

_Hahaha... I'm having fun. Watching disgusting creatures tear each other apart isn't so bad. I was wrong all along. I need to listen to Neil more often. I shouldn't compare orcs to fairies or other cute creatures. They might all be monsters, but some are more monstrous than others._ Hope thought.
