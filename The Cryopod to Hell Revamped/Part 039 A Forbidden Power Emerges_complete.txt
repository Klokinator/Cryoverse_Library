Jason's heart stopped for three seconds. Amelia stared at him silently. The wind swept across the plains, blowing dead leaves and grass between them.

"Die?" Amelia asked. "Are you trying to kill me with Wordsmithing?"

One of Jason's fingers twitched. A bead of sweat dripped from his forehead to his chin. He yelled again. "**DIE!!**"

The word of power faded into the wind. Amelia drew Dellfingler and loosely held him at her side. "Well. How about that? The Creator had a sense of humor. It seems whatever godly abilities he gave you; an easy killing word wasn't one of them."

Jason's right foot slid back slowly. _What the hell?! It wasn't supposed to be like this! Jason should have been able to kill her with one word!_ Jason thought.

Amelia noticed the confusion on Jason's face. "Jason, _sweetie_, I know you're a little baffled, but try to keep your head on straight. After that preachy speech, I didn't expect you to act like a little chicken-shit."

Slowly, Jason nodded, forcing himself to accept the outcome. Raising his palm, he telekinetically-yanked his lightsaber to his hand and shifted the backpack around to adjust its weight.

_Jason didn't know if he could beat her. He had wanted to end the battle with one word... but now he had no choice._ Jason thought.

Blaarjiim appeared in Jason's mind. "I pegged you as many things, mortal. A coward was never one of them. Of course, I can hardly blame you. You are no Joan. You will be unable to beat my Queen in physical combat. When she finishes, she will crush and consume you... forcing you to be her slave for all eternity."

"Slave?" Jason echoed his words. "What's that about?"

Amelia crouched down, bundling power into her legs. "You're about to find out!"

She jumped at him. The fifty-foot gap between them diminished frighteningly fast! With a twitch of his unencumbered left-hand, Jason sent a telekinetic shockwave down from the sky, smashing her into the ground mid-dash. Amelia's quick yelp of surprise as she swallowed dirt gave him enough time to jump back and raise his lightsaber defensively. The blade popped out as she staggered to her feet.

Amelia spat soil and wiped a few blades of grass from her face. "More cowardice. Do better!"

She leaped at him again, but when Jason sent another wave of telekinesis at her, she vanished, and only Dellfingler's voice yelling, "Look out, kid! From the left!" allowed him to dodge right.

Her fist slammed into Jason's kidney, sending him sprawling in the opposite direction. He righted himself in midair, cushioning his landing with telekinesis and saving himself from a bone-crushing ground-slam, just in time to hear a verbal facepalm as Dellfingler yelled out, "No, stupid! MY left!"

_You should have specified!!_ Jason mentally screamed at the sword.

"Above!" Dellfingler yelled again, as Amelia sliced at him from the sky. Jason telekinetically shoved himself to the right, barely dodging as her attack smashed the ground.

Amelia paused for a moment to yell at Dellfingler. "Stop telegraphing my attacks, fucker! You're making me hurt him!"

Jason frowned in confusion. _What the hell did she mean by that?_ Jason thought.

Amelia didn't give Jason the time to ponder that question. She sheathed Dellfingler and balled up her fists. "Whatever! I don't need a sword anyway!"

Jason leaped to his feet and Wordsmithed again. "**Speed. Speed. Enhance. Perception.**" He couldn't match her with his current speed, but maybe if he enhanced his physical abilities, that would change.

Amelia heard him Wordsmithing and scowled. "Finally resorting to Wordsmithing boosts? Maybe now I'll have some fun!"

Jason's ribs were killing him. Where he got punched felt like she had jammed a hundred knives inside. He healed himself and grimaced as his energy dropped a little bit. _The damage had been extensive._ Jason thought.

Amelia hunched down, assuming a new combat stance that kept her low to the ground. She faked left, then rushed right, closing the thirty-foot gap in a second. She skittered around like a spider, causing her movements to become erratic and difficult to follow. This time, Jason sent out a telekinetic wave in all directions away from his body, which was weaker but provided him better protection from all sides.

Amelia tried to swing her fist at Jason's right side, but his telekinetic wave knocked her off-balance. He quickly dodged her attack and grabbed her with his telekinesis, then threw her straight up into the air. 

_Chance!_

Jason yanked Amelia downward, flinging her into the ground with his telekinesis.

_Whump!_

She struck the dirt like a sack of potatoes, gasping in surprise as he knocked the wind out of her.

Jason lunged forth; lightsaber aimed downward to drive through her. The blade of holy energy slammed into Amelia's stomach, piercing through her and impaling her body into the ground. She opened her mouth to scream, but nothing came out. 

"Whoa, boy! Brutal!" Dellfingler offered a few words of disapproval as Jason deactivated the lightsaber and hopped away. Just in time, too. Amelia's flailing legs missed him by an inch.

She writhed on the ground, clutching her stomach and gasping. "Nnng! Ahh! J-Jason! It... it hurts! Ahh!" 

Her pitiful cries nearly wrenched Jason's heart in two.

"Imagine how all the humans felt as you tore them apart," Jason replied, steeling himself. 

_Amelia wasn't Jason's friend. He couldn't pity her. It was like Michael had said: Heroes had to sacrifice their ideals._ Jason thought.

Before she could try another trick, Jason sent a continual wave of telekinesis at her, pressing her body against the grass and pinning her in place. His lightsaber activated, and he took a step toward her.

Amelia squirmed, trying to stand up, to move, to flee. "Jason! No, p-please! Don't do this!"

Jason faltered, pausing for a moment. "If I don't kill you here, you'll continue ravaging the cosmos."

"I won't! Please! I promise... I'll be a good girl... Jason..."

_No. Jason couldn't listen. She was... she was trying to manipulate him._ Jason thought.

Jason pressed a little harder with his telekinesis, easing his body down onto her wriggling form and holding the tip of his lightsaber to her forehead. Amelia went still and stared up at him. She inhaled sharply. "Fine. Do it, then. I knew all along... you're as evil as Joan. All heroes are. You c-can pretend to be righteous agents of justice... but you're no better than me."

"I haven't killed millions, let alone billions," Jason retorted, gritting his teeth. "You have. Don't pretend we're the same."

"I'm a hundred thousand years old, Jason." She didn't flinch as the energy from his lightsaber nicked her forehead. "You don't know how I've lived. Joan was a mass-murderer. You haven't killed anyone yet... but I'll be your first. And after me, how many more? You think you can stop after killing me?"

Jason's hand twitched. The saber pulled on his arm like an anvil, threatening to fall and impale Amelia's head. He raised it an inch.

_Dammit. She had to be lying._ Jason thought.

Her body shifted under him. It was distracting. He started thinking about other things and- _Focus! Even if Amelia was a woman, that didn't mean..._ Jason thought.

Amelia's chin trembled. "Why do you hesitate? Do it. Be a _Hero_. Claim your first victim, just as Joan did. She slew countless demons, telling herself that her actions were in service of _God_. That's how it always begins. You start off doing the right thing, trying to put an end to evil, but-"

"Shut up, Amelia!" Jason forced his jaw to set, moving the lightsaber close enough that her forehead started to warm. She whimpered, and a tear rolled down her face.

"Jason... you're hurting me. Why are you so cruel?" Slowly, Amelia raised her hand, trying not to provoke him. She touched his chest. "We don't... have to be enemies."

Jason couldn't take it anymore. Rage boiled inside of him. With a flourish, he lunged the lightsaber down, chopping off her arm and jumping away. Amelia screamed in pain and clutched her stump. 

Bile formed in Jason's stomach. He wanted to vomit. Her touch sickened him. But... he had lashed out at her.

"It burns!" Amelia whimpered and clutched her wound. "Stop hurting me! Finish me or leave!"

...

Fuck. Now it came down to the wire. Jason didn't want to murder Amelia.  He knew she was manipulating him. Maybe it had been years of conditioning from public school. _Don't hit girls,_ the teacher would always tell the boys. It had been drummed into him from kindergarten to adulthood. 

_No. Jason was making excuses. Not only did killing violate his ideals, but he secretly thought Amelia didn't deserve to die. What if she had lived a painful life? What if she was right about Heroes? It wasn't his place to pass judgment on her actions._ Jason thought.

_You can't let her murder anyone else!_ Another voice yelled in Jason's mind. _If not you, who will stop her? Who will prevent her from killing millions more?!_

_Maybe the demons will,_ Jason retorted. _How should he know?!_

Jason couldn't make up his mind. The voices within him conflicted, making it impossible to direct his blade.

Sighing, Jason pressed a button and deactivated his lightsaber. It slid back into his belt, and he stared at the girl lying helpless in the grass. 

"I can't kill you, Amelia. Nor do I want to maim and torture you, either. I don't know what to do. **Regenerate.**"

Her arm began growing back, sans a sleeve. To Jason's surprise, the hole in her stomach appeared to have already healed. _When? No. That didn't matter._ Jason thought.

Jason held his hand out. "Are you okay?"

Amelia's face, appearing as a series of colors and heat shapes, formed an expression he didn't recognize. "You're healing me?"

"I don't want to hurt you. I don't want to kill you. I don't even want to fight you. When it comes down to it, I don't know if it's my right to deal with you at all. I believe, however, that no matter the awful things you've done... you can redeem yourself."

She stared at him incredulously. "What? _How_?"

"I don't have an answer, Amelia. If you've devoured ten million souls, then perhaps you need to save ten million others. You're immortal. It... it could be possible."

Scoffing, Amelia pulled herself into a sitting position and rubbed her arm, staring at the ground. "You're a hopeless romantic, Jason. Maybe I was wrong about you. You're not like Joan at all."

Dellfingler piped up. "Aw, wow! This is great. I hoped you two would kiss and make up."

Amelia stared silently at Jason's shoes for a moment. "You know, Jason... when you pulled me out of the statue, I was thrilled. It only lasted a few days, but I enjoyed our relationship. I remembered what Joan did to me, but her torture felt like an eternity past."

A crow cried out across the plain. "_Caw! Caw-caw!_"

Amelia continued. "That was months ago. In a lifetime of a hundred thousand years, you're a leaf on the wind. Here one second, gone the next."

Something about her tone caught Jason's attention. "What do you mean? Do you think you can't redeem yourself?"

"I'm not you, Jason," Amelia retorted. She looked up at him, and her eyes narrowed. "There isn't a shred of goodness in me. Thank Joan for that."

"Amelia... if you-"

The grass behind Jason shifted. Before he could react, several objects leaped up and lanced through his backpack, piercing his chest organs all at once.

"Wh...?"

Amelia stood up. "Shadow-bending. A power only those unified with darkness can use. Don't say I didn't warn you."

The invisible objects retracted, yanking Jason onto his back, unable to move. Beneath him, his backpack's power source noticeably cooled as the safeguards kicked into place, and its fusion cells died off.

Jason coughed blood. _P-paralyzed... again?! No... oh no... this couldn't be happening!_ Jason thought.

Jason's spherical vision began to flutter. Something about the shadows sucked the energy right out of him. _He was running low... on... mana..._ Jason thought.

Amelia drew Dellfingler and pointed him at Jason's chest. "I know you have feelings for me, Jason. You're too childish to admit it. Luckily, that was a weakness I could exploit. You had me worried for a moment."

"Don't do it!" Dellfingler howled in protest. "You don't have to be evil, darling! Save him! Do the right-"

"Shut up!" Amelia stabbed Dellfingler at him, aiming to plunge him into Jason's heart.

"Nooo!" The sword screamed in protest.

Jason closed his eyes.

_Goddammit, Jason. You're a fucking idiot. Amelia's going to kill you._ Jason thought.

_Your ideals failed, once again. You shouldn't have opened up your heart._ Jason thought.

_Michael was right._ Jason thought.

With his last ounce of strength, Jason ground his teeth together.

"**CATHERIIINE!!!**"

Amelia stopped, holding Dellfingler an inch from Jason's heart. The heat drained from her face.

"What... what name... did you say...?"

An object began to form in Jason's right hand. 

Jason stared at her emotionlessly. His back rapidly healed as energy poured into his body.

"I trusted you, Amelia. You have no-one left to blame but yourself for this."

Her eyes darkened. She gazed in horror at Jason's new weapon.

"Im... impossible. That sword... is..."
