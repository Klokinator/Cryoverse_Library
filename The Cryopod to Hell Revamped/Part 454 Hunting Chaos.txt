Part 454: Hunting Chaos

_Just outside the Labyrinth Core, amidst the throng of demons and monsters heading into and out of humanity's stronghold._

A curious trio of unlikely allies figuratively joins hands to complete a critical mission: Hunt down and kill Gressil, the Emperor of Chaos. These three figures consist of Creator Demila, Benjamin Brown, and Siddhartha Gautama, also known as Buddha.

The three people exit the Core and wade through the typical torrent of incoming monsters and demons that frequen the Core's entrace. They head down several nearby tunnels and eventually reach an area where the nearby population has dwindled significantly, with only the casual goblin or demon grunt walking past them and shooting curious glances at Demila in particular.

Demila and Benjamin both turn to Buddha.

[You are the one who knows how to locate Gressil.] Demila says, her tone revealing little faith in Buddha's abilities. [_If_ you are capable of doing so, lead us there.]

"I am not a bloodhound." Buddha says mildly. "I cannot pinpoint the Envoy of Chaos. I can only bring us to the location where Karma no longer holds any effect. Once we reach that vicinity, it will be up to you two to uncover his location."

"Me as well?" Benjamin asks, crossing his arms. "I'm a soldier, not a scout."

"Your vision is augmented." Buddha points out. "Demila's psionic senses are powerful, and her eyesight is just as formidable as yours, but you have your own unique capabilities."

"Fair enough." Benjamin says gruffly. "Let's find this guy and get back home in time for dinner."

Buddha falls silent.

He stands silently for a full minute, slowly swiveling around in place while aiming his eyes in every conceivable direction. The other two wait patiently.

Finally, Buddha nods while looking up and to a diagonal direction, as if peering through the Labyrinth's walls. "That way."

Demila notes the direction of his gaze. [Not near the prison complex. It seems Gressil has abandoned it.]

"Makes sense." Benjamin says. "I hear the humans went in and cleaned up shop. They pulled out all of Gressil's prisoners while rescuing Hope Hiro."

Demila's eye twitches. [Yes. Hope Hiro. Indeed.]

"Hm?" Benjamin says frowning. "You have a problem with him?"

Unaware of Hope Hiro's attack on the Volgrim world of Serris, Benjamin has no idea why Demila holds such visible animosity toward his name. She doesn't explain, either.

[It is none of your business, mud-dweller.]

"Suit yourself." Benjamin replies, rolling his eyes.

The trio begins the work of navigating the Labyrinth. Despite its twisting paths and confusing layout, Demila's psionic senses easily map out the Labyrinth for a hundred miles in every direction. If she were to concentrate harder, she might even be able to extend her senses to its most distant ends.

They turn right. They turn left. The ground sometimes slopes upward, and sometimes downward. They even pass stairs carved out by goblins, though these will eventually break apart and regrow as the Labyrinth continues regenerating itself over time. Like a living organism, Hell feeds on the life forces of the microscopic denizens walking through its hallways.

As the trio travels, they pass through many different biomes adapted for specific subspecies of monsters. Benjamin looks around with interest at the surroundings.

"Have you explored Hell before?" Buddha asks, after they arrive at the third biome, an aquatic area where mermaids and sirens chirp and play together.

This particular biome glows under the radiance of surprisingly bright lightbugs, which flicker and fly throughout the air, crawling on surfaces to provide light for the surrounding areas.

"Not particularly." Benjamin says. "Occasionally, I entered the Labyrinth to visit................ _friends_. But otherwise I kept to myself." 

Demila snorts. [You intended to speak of copulation partners. You mud-dwellers all swim in filth.]

Buddha ignores her comments. "You were once humanity's champion, considered the strongest man on Earth, given the lack of a living Hero. Why did you ultimately turn aside from your hatred of demons to fraternize with them?"

"You have me all wrong." Benjamin says calmly. "I never hated demonkind. I fought for life and liberty. I fought for the sake of humanity and to defend our interests. But once we lost the war... it didn't matter anymore. We had no more interests worth defending."

[And so, you began your pursuit of demonic females to ravage.] Demila says, curling up her eyes in dismissive amusement. [A typical primitive male.]

"What would you know about human sexual activities?" Benjamin challenges. "100,000 years is a long time to live without love. I met a few women I particularly liked, we enjoyed ourselves and had good times together. There's nothing wrong with that."

"Indeed." Buddha affirms. "I was not challenging your right to do any of those things. Indeed, you had no more reason to hold animosity toward the demons. However..."

Buddha pauses to look at Benjamin meaningfully.

"While you and your extensive family lived well on Hell Harbor, the rest of humanity lived in slavery under demonkind. I find it odd you would simply allow that to happen. Why did you not advocate for their release? For more humane treatment?"

"Again. I'm a soldier." Benjamin says, in a somewhat defensive tone. "I complained at the start, but it had no effect. Marie Becker had more authority than me. She carved out a niche for her Cybernites. She didn't care about the rest of the ordinary humans. In fact, I'm not sure if she ever did in the first place..."

"I think I understand now." Buddha says with a faint smile. "You are only one man. You could not save humanity alone, and your list of allies had thinned considerably."

"Yeah..." Ben replies, lowering his gaze. "I just dealt with the aftermath... in my own way. Fornicating, drinking, reveling. Better than wallowing in self-pity."

"I am not alien to such actions." Buddha says quietly. "When I lost Tamael, I turned to such activities to dull my memories. My attempts failed. We all have our own ways of coping with trauma."

"You sounds like a psychiatrist." Benjamin grumbles.

"I dabbled in the field for a few decades." Buddha replies. "As you said. When you've lived a long time, you have to cope in your own way."

The two men share a look of understanding, having both gone through somewhat similar situations across their long lives.

They proceed forward, occasionally chit-chatting about this or that. At one point, they enter a biome that truly surprises Benjamin.

"What in the literal Hell?" The Vanguard gasps. "There's lava inside the Labyrinth? And thunder?!"

Before the trio, a land of thunder and fire boils and crackles. Cries and screeches erupt in the distance from unseen giant monsters as they detect the presence of three intruders upon their domain. The truly massive inner space does not regenerate like the rest of the Labyrinth would, but remains unmoving, perhaps held at bay by the strong presences contained within.

"This is one of the Drakelands." Buddha explains. "It is where many Elemental Wyverns live, including the types that attacked Tarus II not long ago."

"I never knew something like this could exist in the Labyrinth." Benjamin mutters. "Amazing..."

Demila narrows her six eyes at Buddha. [You have only just revived. You have not been alive for 100,000 years. Yet, somehow you know of this place. How suspicious...]

Buddha chuckles. "This body I've possessed belongs to a man known as Makoto Ueda. He spent 100,000 years as a transformed monster working under the individual known as Bahamut. He himself does not remember those years well, but I am more than capable of peering into his memories."

Once again, Demila tries to read Buddha's mind, but all she can find are Makoto Ueda's memories. This time, she peers a little deeper and finds that Buddha is in fact telling the truth.

[What a wonderful deal for you.] Demila snarks. [You steal a body and reap all its benefits. You truly are an undying abomination.]

"Someday, that may come to an end." Buddha says, his tone distant. "Perhaps sooner rather than later."

The trio exits the Drakelands biome. They continue traveling, passing more and more areas as they speed up their pace.

Hours pass. Soon, an entire day. Despite his earlier comment about dinner, Benjamin Brown doesn't truly think their search would be completed quickly. He munches on some granola bars he brought along, looking around with his keen eyes while Demila scans the surrounding area.

"Still nothing?" Buddha asks. "We'll reach the first layer of the Chaos boundary soon. After that, my link to Karma will begin to weaken. Gressil is a much stronger Envoy than me, after all."

[I cannot detect his presence.] Demila says, while continuously sweeping her Psionic Sense around the nearby region, occasionally focusing on one anomaly or another. [Either he is not here, or he is exceptionally good at hiding.]

"Gressil is somewhere nearby." Buddha says with absolute certainty. "We may even begin to feel the touch of his chaotic energy soon."

A few minutes later, as Buddha predicted, a tingly, unpleasant feeling tickles the minds of everyone present. Were they magi, they would start to feel the connection to their magic supply weaken. As normal humans, and a Psion, all they feel instead are odd sensations of sadness, anger, and despair come bubbling forth, unbidden.

Benjamin's guilt surfaces to the top of his mind. He silently reflects on all his past failures to protect humanity, as well as how he ultimately turned to the side of demonkind.

Budda does not feel any immediate sensations due to his link to Karma. However, even with that protection in place, he begins to feel uncomfortable as that connection weakens due to the suppressive force pushing down on him.

Demila becomes noticeably more antsy, more on-edge. Her movements turn twitchier as she quickly looks around, imagining distant voices whispering that don't exist.

[How discomforting.] Demila hisses. [We must be within Gressil's domain now, yet I still cannot sense him!]

"Chaos is formless. It is everywhere, yet nowhere." Buddha says sagely. "Chaos is entropy, and entropy values disorder. Try to shield your mind, False Creator. Do not allow yourself to be distracted by stray thoughts. The more you fall into disorder, the lower your strength will be."

[I know fully well what to do.] Demila snaps. [Do not speak down to me, mud-dweller. You have no idea of the strength a 7th Level Psion wields!]

"Your anger is unwarranted." Buddha says. "Try to remain calm. Not even your soul is impervious to the effects of Chaos."

Demila blinks. She shakes her head, then blinks again.

[I am... fully in control of my thoughts.]

"For now." Buddha nods. "For now..."

They walk deeper and deeper into the depth of Gressil's chaotic aura. Unaware of his exact position, the humans eventually stop talking as a strange silence lingers in the air. No longer do any other life forms stir in the area. Occasionally, they will find bugs lying dead on the floor, but never will they encounter even a single random goblin, imp or other demon or monster.

Benjamin's hands ball into fists. He flicks his eyes around, looking both forward and back as they walk down one random, featureless hallway after another. Still, nobody sees Gressil.

"We're getting close." Buddha says uneasily. "My link to Karma has reached its limit. It cannot be suppressed any further, but all that remains is a single thread of light leading out of the chaos. I have guided us this far. I will be relying on the two of you to root out our foe."

Demila starts to reply, but before she can, a figure walks out from a corner up ahead, startling everyone due to his brazenness.

"That won't be necessary," says Gressil, the Emperor of Chaos. "Welcome, friends. I've been watching you for a while now."

Demila blinks. The fact Gressil dares to simply walk in front of the group and openly show his faces to them makes her feel somewhat wary. But, in the end, he is still only a Demon Emperor. She does not think much of his threat.

[Gressil.] Demila says authoritatively. [You are suspected of engineering multiple crimes against the Volgrim Empire, including the creation of the Kolvaxian menace. You are to submit to my arrest, or you will face death.]

Gressil glances at Demila for a moment, but then turns his attention to Buddha.

"Haha. Well done, Buddha. Well done. You've led her right to me, as planned. That Psion is so headstrong, she couldn't see what you were actually planning. Her arrogance was her undoing."

Buddha frowns. "Hm?"

Demila whips her head toward him. [What did he say? Are you planning to betray me, mud-dweller?!]

"No." Buddha says simply. "Gressil is lying. Do not fall for his tricks so easily, Demila."

Gressil gasps. "Buddha! You would deny our long friendship? After all of these years? Haha. You don't need to fear that Psion any longer. Now that I am here, I will save you if she makes a move. It is all within our plan."

"What the hell are you talking about?" Benjamin Brown interjects. "Quit trying to play with our minds, Gressil. You're not as smart as you think."

Gressil turns to the human. His expression darkens.

"**Quiet.**"

A chilly sensation washes down Benjamin Brown's back, as if he were a child in a remote village suddenly faced with the appearance of a hungry grizzly bear on an isolated mountain path. He involuntarily takes a step back, but then certain neural compounds inside his cerebral stimulator activate, dulling his sense of fear. He quickly stands back up and reasserts himself.

"Nice tricks. Your 'chaos energy' is really something. It's all about tricking the weak-minded. But I've met all kinds of demons in my life. Everyone has a gimmick. Color me unimpressed."

Demila seems to realize the truth behind Benjamin and Buddha's words. She nods slowly, then returns her attention to Gressil, while he begins to slowly walk toward the trio, each footstep hitting the ground with a heavy _thud._

[Very clever, demon. It seems my mind is not impervious to your tricks. But if tricks are all you have, you will be lucky to survive this elder's wrath for even one standard time unit without falling to dust!]

Abruptly, Demila vanishes. She reappears directly in front of Gressil with her palm raised. In the instant between instants, she brings her hand down with the force of a megaton hammer, slapping directly at Gressil's face far too quickly for him or anyone else to even follow the movement.

**BOOOM!!**

Demila's casual slap fractures space in the area. She blows apart the Labyrinth in front of herself as if unleashing a focused explosive strike, and the backwind from her attack races down the corridor behind her to knock Buddha off his feet. Benjamin only remains standing thanks to his excellent sense of balance and the weight of his metallic bones keeping him firmly planted in place.

However, Demila's attack... strikes nothing!

Gressil disappears, as if his body were atomized. Demila scowls when she realizes what has happened.

[An illusion. Quit playing tricks, demon!]

Benjamin turns around and offers a helping hand to Buddha. He pulls the former Hero to his feet, and the two of them glance past Demila at the massive crater in the Labyrinth's floors, ceiling, and walls left behind from her attack. Jagged pieces of dirt crumble in the aftermath and fall to the floor, turning to dust on impact.

"That Psion is one scary bitch." Benjamin grumbles. "I've had angry women slap me before, but I don't think I'd survive pissing her off."

Buddha doesn't answer. He frowns as a strange feeling wells up in his chest...

Suddenly, Buddha spins on his heel to face the hallway in the direction they came. He then bends over backward as an invisible fist tears at the air where his head was only an instant before, narrowly avoiding an instant death. With unnatural agility, Buddha kicks off the floor and reverse-flips backward, while Benjamin also detects the attack.

"There you are!" Benjamin roars. The technology in his iris modules allow him to detect even the faintest fluctuations of mana in the air, and he instantly homes in on the identity of Buddha's attacker.

Without hesitation, Benjamin leaps forward and raises his fist.

_THUNK!_

He strikes at the empty air and sends a figure stumbling backward. The illusory wall around that figure vanishes, revealing none other than Gressil's true body, no longer obscured by his magic.

Benjamin presses the assault. He continues punching and kicking at Gressil, but the Emperor of Chaos doesn't allow him any additional cheap hits. The two of them engage in a brief but vicious dance of melee combat, with Benjamin roaring in rage as he tries to get an edge on the fearsome monster before him.

Abruptly, Demila appears. The Psion casually raises her palm once more, and slaps downward, making Benjamin's heartrate spike.

He doesn't get a second to scream.

Demila violently slaps Gressil's true body, bashing him against the wall while releasing another catastrophic explosion of energy, one which sends not only Buddha flying, but Benjamin Brown too. The Psion batters Gressil against the wall, this time successfully connecting with her attack which once again breaks apart the Labyrinth's foundations and sends shockwaves surging outward in all directions.

An instant later, she blows away the dust to gaze upon Gressil's broken, fallen figure. With both his arms shattered and his legs twisted into pretzels, Gressil appears a sorry sight.

[And there it is.] Demila says victoriously. [One slap was all it took. For your crimes against the Volgrim Empire, you will suffer tremendously.]

Demila reaches out her hand. She casually yanks Gressil into her grasp, clamping her grip onto his throat and dangling his broken, useless body as if he were a coat placed upon a hanger.

At the same time, Benjamin, who was the closest to her strike aside from Gressil himself, coughs out a wad of blood. He staggers to his feet and holds his chest, wincing at the injuries to his internal organs. Already, his internal reconstructive nanites have begun to repair the damage, but it will take him an hour or two to return to pristine condition.

"Cough! Shit, Buddha, you okay?"

Benjamin looks to the side, where he sees an armored figure laying on the ground. Buddha, having worn a T-REX activator beneath his clothing, survives the impact with minimal injuries. He lifts himself to his feet and cracks his neck. "It seems our Psion compatriot does not know how to attack without endangering her allies."

Demila, still holding Gressil's broken form, sneers. [You are both fine. If you'd like, I can teleport you back to Tarus II. I'm sure Emperor Belial would be happy- hm?]

She frowns.

Demila looks at Gressil's body in surprise, noticing the fact that he appears to be... quite dead.

Completely, utterly dead.

Not only that, but his body appears decayed enough that it doesn't seem as if Demila killed him herself. Rather, he seems to have died several days before.

[What in the Founders' name?]

Demila drops the Emperor of Chaos's body, allowing him to collapse to the floor. Her pupils shrink and enlarge several times as she makes a strange discovery.

[Impossible...]

"What is it?" Benjamin asks, slowly walking over while holding his stomach. "Did you really kill him in one slap?"

[This makes no sense.] Demila mentally whispers. [His body. It has the same soul signature. It truly is Gressil. But... the one I killed is one who existed several rotational cycles ago. This reminds me of a phenomenon I read about in the Psionic Archives, a state of temporal paradox only achievable by the most enlightened of Temporal Manipulators...]

Buddha's stomach churns.

"...Karma is the force of order. It is the force of stability. But Chaos is the force of disorder and entropy. Gressil can already see into the future. Perhaps the barrier to time itself has begun to weaken..."

Hardly have the words left Buddha's mouth before two figures suddenly appear at the far edges of both craters. Gressil reveals himself in two locations at once, startling the trio.

[You're still alive.] Demila says grimly. [What have you done? Have you achieved the state of Temporal Ascension?!]

The Gressil nearest to her chuckles.

"Oh, Demila. I am not a Psion. Your rules do not apply to me. But then again... I suppose _no_ rules do."

Demila smiles with her eyes.

[We will see about that.]

She vanishes from her position, intending to reappear before Gressil and slap him to death again. But this time, she materializes in front of a wall, making her blink in surprise.

[Hm?! I jumped to the wrong coordinate? Impossible. I haven't made such an amateur mistake in a million cycles!]

Flustered by this minor failure, Demila becomes incensed.

[You did this to me!] She roars at Gressil. [Your damned mud-magic is interfering with my mind! But demonic tricks won't make a difference when compared with overwhelming STRENGTH!]

Once again, Demila flickers toward Gressil. This time, she succeeds and swings her palm down, but as she does, a figure appears behind her with his fist raised.

**CRACK!!**

Demila's attack harmlessly whiffs past an illusion, but Gressil's sneak attack connects with the back of Demila's head, pounding her straight downward into the Labyrinth's soil!

_Thunk!_

[Aargh!]

Demila howls more in rage than pain, while Gressil stands amidst the wreckage of her third attack, smiling casually.

"No need to end things quickly. We're only just getting started here, _Psion_."
