"WARNING! THERMONUCLEAR DETONATION DETECTED! SEEK COVER!"

Centurion roared in Hope's ear like a tiger howling for blood. Hope's eyes bolted open, and he swiveled around to see Beelzebub's entire body convulsing.

"Holy shit! Samantha, look out!"

"FIVE SECONDS TO DETONATION. FOUR. THREE..."

Instinctively, Hope aimed his mind at Samantha. "[SHIELD! SHIELD! SHIELD! SHI-]"

Hope's mind melted away. A supernova exploded ten feet from his face, and every cell in his body screamed in agony.

Hope opened his eyes. Everything was still.

The taste of metal permeated every corner of his mouth. A hundred bells clanged in his ears. His vision was little more than a wild splash of colors and images. Cat. Dog. Car. Plane. Cloud. Tree.

Hope couldn't move. There was no pain, but invisible anvils crushed him against the floor.

Somewhere between one second and one eternity passed. Hope opened his mouth to speak, but whatever came out, he couldn't hear it.

Finally, the colors melted away. Black and white flashes of heat replaced them, followed by a sensation of burning across his skin.

"Ahhunn..."

Hope let out a pitiful whimper. At least he could speak.

"-ope? Hope?"

Someone's voice spoke to him.

"...God?"

"No. It's me, Samantha. Are you okay? I healed you as best I could."

Hope couldn't see her at all. He blinked his eyes. "I'm blind."

"You'll have to fix that. Anything involving a brain is beyond me. I did what I could with the burns and broken bones."

"**Heal.**"

Hope croaked out a word of power, causing his vision to return. The darkness of the Labyrinth replaced the pure black and bright white from before. It took him several seconds to pick out Samantha's face from the gloom surrounding them.

"Hey, are you okay?"

Hope reached up to rub his face. His hands scratched his skin, reminding him of pressing sandpaper together. He winced and pulled himself up.

"What happened?"

Only now could Hope fully understand the scene around him. He sat not in a small room like before, but a massive cavern. There were no walls anywhere in sight. The floor around him was pure black and covered in a cross between ash and soot. One look at his body revealed his tuxedo was in tatters, with only a few strips clinging to his skin through static electricity. Similarly, Samantha's white bridesmaid dress was gone, burned to a crisp. She didn't notice.

Samantha shook her head. "I think... I think Beelzebub blew himself up. You saved me. I would have died if it weren't for you." She smacked her lips. "Eugh. I'm nauseous. My stomach won't stop turning."

Hope spoke again. "**Light.**"

A bright light appeared above him, illuminating the floor around them. A shiver went down Hope's spine.

Hope still couldn't see the walls or the ceiling; only the ground stretching out endlessly into the darkness. Beelzebub was also nowhere to be seen. Hope thought he had died.

"Incredible," A deep voice said from behind Hope. He leaped to his feet and spun around to see Barbatos—no, rather Uriel, the Archangel, standing and staring at the cavern around them. She looked up, down, left, and right. "Such devastation. Thou shouldst consider thyself lucky I was here to protect thee, young Hero."

"Protect me? I shielded myself."

The Archangel chuckled. "Heh... amusing. I created several Light Walls at the moment of Beelzebub's detonation and enveloped us in their protection. I didn't particularly want to protect Satan's whore, but I knew ye would think better of me if I did."

Hope glanced at Samantha and shrugged. "I mean, the Archangel called you a whore, but she did save us, too. Do those cancel out?"

"Uriel didn't save my life out of the kindness of her heart. She isn't good enough to do that." Samantha crossed her arms and glared at the Archangel. "Still, thank you. I would be dead if it weren't for you."

Uriel ignored the succubus. "Tell me, Hero, now that thou hath seen the devastation bloodskins can unleash, will ye stand apart from the demon whore at thy side and join me? If ye striketh down Belial, the temptress of man, thou shalt earn thy place among the legends of times past."

The color drained from Hope's face. "What the hell? After saving Samantha, now you want me to kill her?! Are you insane?"

The Archangel shook her head. "Hardly. 'Tis a reasonable proposal. Since time immemorial, angels and humans have always walked arm in arm as brothers and sisters. Perhaps after a hundred thousand years, humans may have forgotten, but I have not. I want nothing more than to save thy people from slavery at the hands of the bloodskins."

Samantha rolled her eyes. "Oh, that's _rich_. You're the ones who enslaved imps in the first place. Slavery wouldn't exist if it weren't for you."

"Quiet, wench. I have little patience for thy silver-tongue."

"What?! You're the liar! All you utter are falsehoods!"

Hope watched silently as the Archangel and Demon Emperor bickered, each trying to compel him to join their side. Thinking back on what he had witnessed, not once had he seen Samantha kill or injure someone else. On the other hand, Uriel's aura dripped with bloodlust and murder. If Hope had to compare the two of them, he couldn't see himself sympathizing with the Archangel.

However, Uriel's hatred wasn't likely to have sprouted from nowhere. The fact that a Demon Duke, Barbatos, had trapped her within his body implied she had met some horrible end at his hands. Who could say if her quest for vengeance was unwarranted? Hope had already realized, unlike Jason, that killing was sometimes necessary.

After all, not even one day ago, Hope had killed hundreds of orcs to save a human village. If Uriel was a monster, then Hope shared a similar stain on his conscience.

One lingering issue, however, was that the Archangel pursued a grievance from a hundred thousand years in the past. None of the demons alive today had anything to do with the murder of the angels. It wasn't right to judge children for the sins of their ancestors!

...

No. Hope was overlooking something. Samantha was an Emperor. She was old enough to remember the angels and the war, along with their destruction. Surely the other Dukes and Emperors also remembered. In that case, Uriel's grievance was legitimate. The same corrupt leaders of demonkind who had exterminated the Angels and Gods continued to live today.

Hope paused for a moment to itch his arms. They tingled every so often, causing him to notice other weird sensations in his body.

While Samantha and Uriel took menacing steps toward each other and yelled, Hope glanced down at his arms. With the light illuminating them, he saw...

_What the hell?_ Hope thought.

His skin was mottled and gross. Tiny worm-like objects crawled all over his limbs and chest. Bugs? Spiders? No, something else.

...

_Son of a bitch. That's right. I was wearing the T-REX when Beelzebub exploded. What the hell would a nuclear blast do to nanites?_ Hope thought.

Hope slapped the button on his chest. Nothing happened.

His armor was destroyed. What if the nanites had embedded themselves inside him?

"**Observe.**"

Hope spoke aloud, making the other two turn to look at him.

His body's status popped up in front of him, detailing his current state via a series of images and diagrams.

_Physical Condition: 45%._

_Stage 3 Radiation Poisoning detected_

_Stage 3 Cancer of the kidneys, lungs, heart, and brain detected._

_Lymphomatic cells detected within 14% of the bloodstream._

_1,603,978,801 defective nanites detected throughout body._

"Holy fuck," Hope muttered aloud. He turned to Samantha. "**Observe.**"

Her condition came up.

_Physical Condition: 90%._

_Stage 1 Radiation Poisoning Detected._

_Stage 1 Cancer of the heart detected._

_Lymphomatic cells detected throughout 3% of the body._

Samantha raised an eyebrow. "Is something wrong?"

"You're in better condition than me. Beelzebub's attack infected my entire body with cancer. It also did something to my suit, since it embedded over a billion nanites into my bloodstream."

"Are you okay?"

Hope shook his head. "I don't know how to target something the size of a nanite. Maybe if I had the Crown, things would be different."

Uriel sighed. "I apologize, young Hero. I wish I could have protected thee. Now, the horrors of the bloodskins will affect thee for the rest of thy life, as they have mine."

Hope rubbed his head. "Actually, I'm pretty sure once I return to the Core, Jason can heal me."

Uriel's somber expression froze. "Oh."

The Archangel's eyes shifted. Hope couldn't see her face, but her expression seemed to be something akin to... disappointment? Why that emotion, of all the possibilities? Hope would have expected her to be glad that he hadn't suffered any permanent damage.

_Something doesn't add up. I can't bring myself to trust this Archangel. Ever since I first laid my eyes on her, she's acted in her self-interest. She doesn't look at me like a person. She thinks of me as a tool._ Hope thought.

_Just like Jason does._ Hope added.

But Hope wasn't a tool. He was flesh and blood human. He was sick and tired of people treating him like a nobody, or a servant who must do this and that. _Protect the Core, Hope. It's your job, Hope. Do this, Hope. Do that, Hope._

_Shut the fuck up, all of you. I barely survived a nuclear blast, and Uriel only looks relieved that she can still use me. She's happy I'm not... defective._ Hope thought bitterly.

_If I'm going to kill any demons, it'll be for my sake, not hers._ He decided.

Hope shook his head. "Come on, Samantha. Let's return to the Core. I'm not helping this Archangel. I've heard enough."

Uriel narrowed her eyes. "Thou art leaving?"

"Yeah. I am."

Samantha met Hope's eyes. "Hope, we came here to fight Uriel. We can't leave."

Hope shook his head. "I'm not interested in dueling an Archangel. She saved my life, after all. Both of you have history, and I'm not going to piece together who started what. However, I'm not going to help her kill anyone either. Her quarrel with demons isn't mine."

The Archangel took two steps toward him. "Disrespectful child! How dare thou turn down my alliance! Thou art a Hero. Angels created the Heroes. Thy life would be insignificant and worthless, were it not for our sacrifices!"

"I beg your pardon?"

"I speak the truth, boy. Thou art little more than a child who has grown attached to the likes of bloodskins. I know not why or how, but I won't allow thee to walk away. Thy lineage is mine to control! Thy _life_ is mine to use as I see fit."

Samantha paled. "W-what? That's terrible! Even for you-"

"SILENCE!"

Uriel flicked her eyes toward the succubus. The ground shifted under Samantha, and a dozen knives burst out of the Labyrinth's floor to rush at her.

With her body weakened by radiation sickness, Samantha couldn't react in time. The knives impaled her limbs, spun her around in midair, and slammed her into the dirt. Samantha coughed up blood. She wriggled to try and free herself, but she couldn't move.

"Samantha!" Hope turned to run to her, but an invisible wall blocked his path. "Uriel! Let her go! What's wrong with you?!"

"Hero, thou art a misguided fool. If I cannot change thy mind, then I will at least make certain the bloodskins cannot seize thy power for themselves!"

A warning tingled in the back of Hope's mind. He ducked reflexively as a knife sailed over the back of his head and landed in Uriel's palm. She frowned. "Impossible. How could a mere human dodge an attack like that?"

A buzzing feeling hissed throughout Hope's body. He jumped to the side, ducked, and hopped two feet into the air to avoid several shards of metal flying at him from his left and right.

As his body moved on its own, a realization struck Hope. Something was different. He had never moved like this before, not once in his life. He wasn't athletic, even after Wordsmithing his body to be more physically fit.

_What's going on?_ Hope thought.

Hope didn't have time to think. He evaded several more shards of metal and aimed his mind at his body. "**Strength! Speed! Enhance!**"

This time, when Hope sensed danger approaching his body, he snatched the handle of a knife before it could impale his back.

Uriel's tongue flicked out. "What happened? How have thy movements quickened? How art thou keeping up with my attacks?!"

Hope shrugged. "Dunno."

Before she could say anything else, Hope aimed his palm at her. "**FIRE!**"

A blast of Holy Energy sprayed out and flew at the patchy-skinned demon-angel hybrid. She watched the beam approach and caught it in her palm, absorbing its energy without taking damage.

"Silly boy. I am an angel. Manipulating holy magic is child's play."

Hope flicked his eyes to Samantha. Uriel hadn't paid any attention to the succubus since she had engaged in battle with him, and Samantha had been slowly molding and shifting her body to free herself from the knives.

_I'll give her more time, then._ Hope thought.

"Hey, Uriel. Doesn't it piss you off that you're trapped in that hideous demon body? I bet it's still better looking than your original form."

The Archangel gnashed her teeth. "Impudent shit! When I walked the cosmos, all knew of my name and bowed at my feet! I stood atop a precipice of beauty, surrounded by the likes of the Valkyries and the Earthmother herself! A shriveled little boy like thyself would have counted himself lucky to court someone of my class!"

More danger appeared in Hope's mind, but this time he could almost make out a voice, but he didn't have time to listen intently to what it said. He ducked and dodged several knife attacks, then turned to Uriel.

"You only think of me as a weapon against demonkind. Maybe I'm naive, but I always thought angels sat on clouds and played harps. You're more of a demon than the demons I've met so far."

"Speak not of what ye don't know!" Uriel bellowed. She stomped her foot, causing the ground to shake violently, knocking Hope off his feet. "The damned bloodskins took everything from me! EVERYTHING! If ye choose not to be my ally, then thou art my enemy! Burn in the lake of fire!"

She formed several signs with her hands, and flames surged from her chest, screaming across the short gap toward Hope. No time to dodge!

_I'm dead. I know I'm dead._ Hope thought.

Hope tucked himself into a ball and flinched. Warmth washed over his body.

A moment passed. Two. Three.

Uriel spoke again, her voice softer now. "That fire was over eight hundred degrees. How is he still...?"

Hope blinked and patted himself all over.

_She's right. What the hell? How did I survive that?_ Hope thought.

A voice spoke in his head again.

_PILOT AGHSK NNBSSPP._

Hope recognized the voice as Centurion. Why could he hear him?

Centurion cleared up.

_PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR, HOPE HIRO. THE TRANSFORMING REPLICANT EXOSUIT WAS DAMAGED DURING THE NUCLEAR ATTACK. ALL SYSTEMS HAVE SWITCHED TO BACKUP MODE. I HAVE ADJUSTED MY OPERATING PARAMETERS ACCORDINGLY._

It took Hope a moment to understand Centurion's words. "You... you merged with my body?"

_AFFIRMATIVE. CENTURION'S SYSTEMS ARE UNABLE TO CONNECT TO THE EXTERNAL FUSION POWER SOURCE. CENTURION HAS ADJUSTED ITS SYSTEMS TO DERIVE POWER FROM YOUR BIOLOGICAL PROCESSES. EXCESS BODILY WASTE WILL BE EJECTED AS A HARMLESS AEROSOL BYPRODUCT._

Hope jumped to his feet and felt his arms again. Within seconds, the bumpiness on his skin from before vanished, replaced instead with a clean, metallic texture resembling armor.

A warning signal appeared in Hope's mind again. He was so distracted by his thoughts that he didn't move quickly enough. Uriel's knife stabbed into his spine, only to bounce off his skin and hit the ground with a _clang_.

_I get it now. Centurion's nanites have merged with my body to form invisible armor._ Hope thought.

Hope's vision shifted. Something moved around in front of him. He swatted at the air, but quickly realized his eyes themselves were changing.

_ADJUSTING OLFACTORY, VISUAL, AND AUDITORY RECEPTORS TO OPTIMUM LEVELS. PROCESS COMPLETE. ENABLING NEURAL CONTROL OF CENTURION'S PRIMARY AND SECONDARY SYSTEMS. ONLINE._ Centurion announced.

Hope blinked in surprise. _Holy shit, does that mean I can control Centurion with my thoughts?_ He thought.

Centurion replied to Hope's mental query. _AFFIRMATIVE, PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR. CENTURION'S SYSTEMS AND SUBSYSTEMS ARE NOW CAPABLE OF ACCEPTING MENTAL STIMULI AS COMMANDS._

Uriel scowled. "Thou have yet to activate thy Wordsmithing, yet somehow, thou hath vastly improved thy fighting skills since awakening! How is this possible?!"

What happened next surprised Hope. Samantha, having shifted her body enough to melt around the knives, leaped from the ground and transformed her arms into two swords. She chopped down and hacked off Uriel's right arm. The sudden assault caused the Archangel to scream in pain and retreat several steps while forming an invisible wall to block Samantha's ambush.

Samantha transformed her hands from blades into hammers. She smashed against Uriel's invisible wall, battering it so hard that shockwaves rippled outward, making Hope's insides quiver.

Centurion evaluated the situation. _ARCHANGEL-HYBRID DESIGNATED URIEL HAS FORMED A WALL OF SOLID LIGHT. ERROR. THIS EXCEEDS CENTURION'S TECHNICAL PARAMETERS. UNABLE TO EXPLAIN THE PHENOMENA._

"Uriel's using magic," Hope said. "I don't know how her magic works, but it seems she can control metal, fire, and light."

Uriel staggered back, and Samantha changed her method of attack. She dived into the ground and burrowed inside the scorched floor of the Labyrinth. Uriel flicked her gaze to where Samantha had disappeared.

"Tch, foolish female." Uriel motioned with her hands, and the ground trembled. A moment later, Samantha exploded from the dirt, almost as if something had spit her with incredible force. She sailed into the darkness and landed somewhere unseen.

Hope's vision suddenly changed, startling him—the world shifted to hues of red and black.

_THERMAL VISION ACTIVATED._ Centurion announced.

"Uh. Thanks, Centurion."

A blip of red in the darkness caught Hope's eye. Samantha slinked around, careful to stay in the shadows where Uriel couldn't see her.

The succubus made a motion with her hands and raced at Uriel. Her speed multiplied by a factor of ten. She covered the quarter-mile in under a second and slammed against the Archangel's invisible wall, breaking through it as if it was made of glass. She crashed into the Archangel with thunderous strength, causing both women to tumble and roll into one another. Before Uriel could react, Samantha stretched, bent, and twisted, wrapping herself around her opponent like Silly String.

Uriel gasped and struggled, trying desperately to unravel the demoness from her body. "Get... off... me!"

Samantha's fist transformed into a hammer again. She smashed it against Uriel's face over and over, pounding and pummeling the Archangel senseless.

Uriel motioned with her hands and quickly formed a single magic sign, despite Samantha's intense barrage. Two winged figures of light appeared; the same ones that had stood beside her before the blast went off.

"Samantha! Look out!"

The blank face of one opened up. A hole appeared in its head, and it fired several shots of holy energy at its master.

The bolts slammed against Samantha's body. She screamed. "Aiieee!!"

The succubus turned to swing her hammer at the winged pseudo-angel, but the other jumped forward and summoned a sword of light. It chopped off her hand, causing her to scream again.

"Paha, foolish bloodskin! BURN!" Uriel formed another sign to summon fire from her pores. She released it outward, scorching every inch of the succubus wrapped around her. This time, Samantha's anguished cry churned Hope's stomach. Her pitiful wailing made Hope realize she couldn't hold out much longer.

Hope had seen enough.

"**Remove.**"

With a single word, Hope teleported Samantha off Uriel's body and over to him. Her stretched form was covered in blood and burns, making her look like a mass of burned Jello. Hope could barely tell where her head began and her legs ended.

Samantha slowly twisted herself back into a humanoid shape, but her badly burned body made Hope's stomach lurch. The succubus clutched her severed hand and winced. "Ahh, it burns! Shit... shit..."

"**Regenerate. Clothes.**" Hope quickly healed her arm and covered her naked body with a simple jumpsuit. Then, he stepped between her and Uriel. "That's enough. You've both gotten the anger out of your systems. Uriel, if you hurt my friend again, I'll have to get involved."

The Archangel's eyes exploded with rage. "No! That's it! I've had it with thy cowardice! I'm done playing nice! Thou hath made thy bed, now sleep in it, _forever_!"

Uriel formed signs once again. Hope grimaced.

_There's no backing down for me, now. I have to fight the Archangel._ Hope thought.

Hope looked at Samantha. "Sorry. I can't protect you and fight at the same time. **Return.**"

"Wai-!" Samantha started to say something, but disappeared and returned to the Core.

Centurion boomed in Hope's mind. _PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR! CENTURION DETECTS A BUILDUP OF ENERGY INSIDE THE ARCHANGEL-HYBRID ENEMY!_

"Yeah, I bet you fuckin' do. Let's take this cunt down a peg or two."
