The vision lingered in Jason's mind, becoming a memory that felt almost as if he had lived it himself. It wasn't only Uzziel and the Archangels who had lost Samael, but Jason as well. Irrational as it seemed, and despite how little he knew him, Samael had seemed like a stand-up guy, one willing to do anything to protect his sister's life. He hadn't deserved to die in such a terrible manner.

After a few seconds, Jason materialized before the bonfire and immediately turned to Raphael.

"You didn't cry," Jason said.

Raphael blinked slowly. "Apologies, Jason, but I don't follow."

"Samael died, but you barely acknowledged his death. I know people suffer in different ways, but you hardly mentioned it," Jason observed.

"Jason," Solomon whispered, a note of warning in his voice. "Don't be disrespectful."

"'Tis fine, Knowledge Seeker," Raphael replied. "As for the young one's inquiry, I did not break down in tears because, long ago, I lost millions of family members all at once. Even now, a hundred thousand years later, my grief is so overwhelming that it never leaves."

Gabriel took a step forward and rested his hand on Raphael's back. "Raphael is my elder brother, but on a scale of immortality, years count for little. We might as well be the same age. The difference is that Raphael remembers all those who died before we gave up our memories, while Michael and I do not. I am certain the burden he carries places tremendous stress on him."

Raphael bowed his head. "Mmm. 'Twas essential that at least one person remembered the ancient histories. The pain I bear is nothing compared to the thought of forgetting my beautiful sisters, Muriel and Cassiel, let alone millions of other angels."

At the mention of Muriel and Cassiel, Michael grimaced. He plopped down on his log to Jason's right, pulled out his sword, and stabbed it into the ground. He began to wring and squeeze the hilt as though it were someone's neck.

"If only I could remember the ancient histories. Sometimes I see pale flashes of faces. Distant afterimages. A faint smile, a memory of happiness and bliss. They're always a galaxy away, yet so close that I wish I could reach out and touch them," Michael said.

Raphael flicked his eyes to Michael. "Of all the angels, 'twas essential thou should forget the past. I'm sorry, brother, but I did what I could to—"

Michael suddenly leaped to his feet. "Gah! Why, Raphael?! Why can't I remember?! Our people are extinct! We're confined to these torturous rings, unable to fly freely or cut down the ones who did this to us! Surely, thou cans't finally lay bare the reasons we lost our memories! It isn't as if the Titans are waiting for us to break the treaty anymore! They're dead too!"

Raphael stared evenly at Michael. His ancient eyes slowly sealed shut. He bowed his head, rested an elbow on his knee, and placed his face in his palm.

"I'm sorry, brother. The treaty was... it was..." Raphael's voice trailed off.

"Thou need not explain," Gabriel said. "Michael. Calm thyself."

"I won't," Michael growled. "I wish to hear about our past! I wish to know what secrets were so significant that Raphael wouldn't speak a word to his brothers about them! Where does thy loyalty lie, Raphael? With thy kin, or with the Titans?!"

Slowly, Raphael raised his head.

"Treaty? There was no such thing."

Michael blinked. He pulled back slightly. "What? Thou speaketh nonsense."

"Nay, I speak the truth," Raphael whispered. "What good would a treaty do if only one person knew the reasons behind its existence? No. The reason the angels and Titans gave up our memories was... it was... to prevent..."

Raphael's voice cracked. "No. I won't say. Michael. Please don't press me for answers. Thou dost think answers will ease thy pain. Thou dost hope they will give thy spirit closure. They will not. I pay the price for every moment of my continued existence, knowing what I do. I do not wish for thee to suffer more than is absolutely necessary."

Raphael's words chilled the air around the campfire. Michael met Raphael's gaze. Their eyes spoke to each other for a few moments before he opened his mouth. "Would... woulds't thou tell Gabriel of our past? Ever? Under any circumstance?"

"I might," Raphael replied. His voice was little more than a murmur in the wind. "But t'would only be as a last resort."

"A last resort," Michael said, echoing Raphael. "Yet, never woulds't thee speak of the past to me."

"Nay. Never," Raphael said.

Michael's lip trembled. He slowly turned to stare into the bonfire. After a moment, his chest began to shake. He squeezed the hilt of his sword, and a tear dripped down his face.

"I... I did something. It... it must... must have been terrible," Michael whispered.

Raphael stared at his brother. Michael tried to contain his emotions, but slowly, his stalwart facade began to crack around the edges.

"Do not blame thyself, Michael. The Ancient Times... the Dragon Wars... the Titan Wars... many tragedies of unfathomable scale unfolded there. Thy actions were hardly—"

"Stop," Michael said. He raised his armored hand to wipe at his eyes. "I know the truth now. It... it only makes sense — the way thou regarded me with suspicion. The way thou never spake of the Ancient Wars when I was present. Muriel. Cassiel. Did... did I... toward them..."

Raphael's eyes widened. "Enough, Michael. Thy curiosity will only lead to ruin."

"I knew it!" Michael leaped to his feet. "'Twas my fault, wasn't it?! I killed Cassiel! I killed Muriel! Why?! Why would I do such a thing?! Answer me! Tell me—"

"MICHAEL!"

Raphael leaped to his feet, mirroring Michael. Out of nowhere, the old man's dismal appearance changed. A dark cloud of miasma sprang up around him.

"I AM THY ELDEST BROTHER! CEASE THIS LINE OF QUESTIONING AT ONCE!" Raphael roared.

Raphael's body stayed the same size, but somehow his presence seemed to quadruple in magnitude, dwarfing even Gabriel. The Archangel of Power took a step back in fright, tripped over his log, and landed on his ass.

Michael's anguish turned to terror as his elder brother lurched toward him. Instinctively, Michael grabbed for his sword, but he clumsily batted it aside, then rethought challenging Raphael and cowered under his brother's overwhelming pressure.

"B-brother Raphael! Stop! I yield!" Michael cried.

Raphael's teeth gnashed together. He reached a hand toward Michael as if to strangle the life out of him.

Suddenly, Solomon left Jason's side. He jumped in front of Raphael and puffed out his chest. "Raphael! Stop! Stop! You're going too far!"

Expressions of malevolence, hatred, disgust, and rage passed over Raphael's face, all within a single second. However, his eyes glazed over when he recognized Solomon. The Archangel of Wisdom recoiled in surprise. His aura shrank once again, and the terrifying specter of Death itself morphed back into that of a tired, weak old man.

"O-oh. Solo...mon," Raphael whispered. He shakily leaned on his staff and sat back down. A look of horror spread across his face. He grabbed at his robes and dabbed his face dry. "I... I didn't mean to... to lose my..."

...

Jason didn't know how to feel or what to think.

Raphael, the kindly old man, was hiding more than Jason had expected. Thanks to Michael's line of questioning, he could take a few stabs in the dark at what happened during or before the Ancient Wars.

Michael was involved, somehow, in killing Muriel and Cassiel. _Why?_ Jason wondered. Even if that were the case, Raphael didn't seem to blame him for it, yet he had exploded in rage at Michael for bringing it up.

_Does that mean Raphael blames his brother for their deaths? Perhaps there's another reason. The truth could hold sinister consequences for the future,_ Jason thought.

Everything Jason learned about the angels, more and more, turned him toward the belief that they were not and were never agents of righteousness or virtue. They were selfish, vain, and petty, like numerous people he had met during his life. Hell, the Archangels were worse in many ways because they're immortal demigods with explosive magical powers. Their potential to cause damage far exceeds that of the average human.

Michael lay on the ground shivering in a ball for half a minute, trembling and sweating. He covered his face with his hands and mumbled incoherently, though Jason couldn't make out his words. Gabriel, on the other hand, recovered relatively quickly. He cautiously pulled himself to a sitting position on his log and smoothed his robes over and over. Like a nervous tic, the Archangel of Power rubbed his robes repeatedly while flicking his eyes between Raphael and Michael.

Solomon pulled Jason aside and walked them a short distance away from the fire.

"Jason. Try not to make any hasty conclusions about what just happened. The Archangels are not in their best states after living inside the three rings, alone, for a hundred thousand years. Such isolation would cause anyone to... ah... lose a few marbles," Solomon said.

Jason bit his lip. "Didn't Bahamut wear all three rings constantly? I thought they could communicate if that were the case."

"No. The Archangels only can do so now because of my crown. I linked their spiritual energies with mine using my artifact. Look, all I ask is that you don't think ill of them because of one minor scuffle," Solomon explained.

"_Minor?_" Jason asked, nudging his head toward Raphael. "The old man went berserk! He was going to kill Michael!"

Solomon didn't reply for a few seconds. When he finally responded, he chose his words carefully.

"I... I don't think... Raphael would hurt Michael. I think he... ah... intended to wipe Michael's mind," Solomon said.

Jason's heart nearly stopped.

"Raphael would do that? To Michael?" Jason asked.

Solomon looked Jason dead in the eye. His expression was graver than anything Jason had seen in his entire life. "Raphael would do a great many things, Jason. You know the saying. _The road to Hell is paved with good intentions._"

Jason's heart trembled. "What are you implying?"

"I didn't live through the Ancient past, Jason. However..."

Solomon glanced behind them a hundred feet away at Raphael's bowed form. The old man silently stared into the bonfire, consumed by his thoughts.

A look of fear passed over Solomon's face. He didn't want Raphael to hear him.

Solomon lowered his voice. "Jason. My crown is the receptacle for hundreds of thousands of memories, from beings long-dead. As I said when you first entered this thought space, its visualizations of the past are built upon the memories of those whose memories I copied."

Jason nodded slowly. "Okay. So what does that mean?"

Solomon lowered his voice. "Anyone who wears my crown, even for a fleeting moment, gives their memories to me. Anyone I touched during my lifetime gave not only their knowledge to me, but the knowledge of any souls they had consumed or manipulated as well. As I told you at the beginning, Jason... I have access to the memories of all three Archangels now."

...

Jason's jaw slackened.

"You... you know..."

"About the Ancient Wars, yes. I know Raphael's secrets, including _the big one_," Solomon replied.

Solomon's eyes darkened.

"Say nothing to the old man. Don't ask me to tell you what he knows. I won't utter a word. All I will say is that Raphael is right to conceal the past. The secrets he holds should never come to light. I regret probing his mind," Solomon warned.

"If you know his secret, why not me as well?" Jason challenged him.

The king shook his head in response. "It isn't like that. I learned his past unwillingly as a result of my powers. The danger lies not in you or I knowing his secret, but in someone else obtaining that knowledge. Especially Gabriel, Michael... or the demons. The fewer who know it, the fewer who can turn the past to their advantage."

Solomon squeezed Jason's shoulder. He forced a smile so painfully disingenuous that it made Jason's eye twitch.

"Cheer up, Jason. I'm going to give you another vision," Solomon said.

Jason smacked his lips while trying to rub the taste of sand off his tongue. "I don't think I want to see anything else."

"You do," Solomon replied encouragingly. "Don't forget why you came here. You need to know about the past if you wish to change the future. Have no fear. I will guide you to the best of my ability."

Solomon and Jason turned back to the Archangels. Michael had already pulled himself up to sit on his log the same as his brothers, but his look of total dejection reminded Jason of an abused dog. He slumped forward with his shoulders sagging and stared at the ground emptily. Raphael gazed into the fire, a look of heartache etched into every corner of his face. Gabriel, however, merely looked confused. As a third-party, he doesn't appear to have the emotional involvement Raphael and Michael do about the Ancient Wars, though Jason could see gears turning in his head as he tried to untangle their conflict.

"Let us return to the Wordsmith's visions," Solomon said, jarring everyone awake. All three Archangels simultaneously turned to look at him, remembering where they were.

"Oh, right. Yes, yes, of course," Raphael muttered. "The visions. Where were we?"

Michael lowered his head again. "As if it matters."

Gabriel and Raphael ignored Michael's brooding. The Archangel of Power coughed into his fist. "Ah, ahem, right... well, I suppose the next major event would be... err..."

"We'll return to Satan's perspective," Solomon said. "The point of these visions is to give Jason a view into the ancient history, and I'd like to keep things balanced."

Raphael shakily lit more tobacco and inhaled the odor in his pipe. "Yes, balance, of course. Go on, then."

Jason glanced at Michael. The look on his face reminded Jason of someone trying to figure out how they ended up on trial for the death penalty, as if he were trying to unravel what would make Raphael hate him so much.

_Does Raphael hate Michael, I wonder?_ Jason thought. _Perhaps it's less about hatred, and more about how much Raphael loved his sisters, Cassiel and Muriel, more than life itself. Since Raphael loves Michael as much as he did them, his mind and heart war with one another, trying to decide whether to love Michael as a brother or hate him as their murderer._

_Or, maybe it's none of those things. I don't know enough about their history to cast judgment._

Solomon weaved magic signs with his hands. "Careful. This next vision is going to be a bit... _disorienting._"

"I hate it when you say crap like that," Jason muttered.

The world went dark, and once again, Jason left the bonfire, but this time, he was worried about what its state would be when he returned.
