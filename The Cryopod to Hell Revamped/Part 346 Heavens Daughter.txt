Part 346: Heaven's Daughter

Raphael, Uriel, and Michael all stare in absolute shock at the woman curled up in the corner of the room, her arms wrapped around her legs. Cassiel, apparently known as Heaven's Daughter, blinks her watery eyes as her gaze flicks between the three Archangels, yet she says nothing. Instead, a look of deep shame appears on her face. She lowers her head again, burying her face into her knees and tightening her arms around her legs.

"How can the Daughter of Heaven be here?" Michael whispers. "We... we were certain she perished..."

Jason allows the Archangels a few moments to look at Cassiel, but seeing her clear discomfort, he doesn't invite them inside. Instead, he ushers them back outside and closes the door before conjuring a sound isolation barrier.

"Heaven's Daughter?" Jason asks. "So you do know her. I asked Gabriel, but he kept his lips sealed. **Projection. Projection.**"

Jason conjures Gabriel and his Mind-Wife, Phoebe, into the physical realm. When they appear, Gabriel towers over the others, nearly double their height. Aware of his immense height difference, he drops down to sit on the dirt.

"I hath spoken with Heaven's Daughter." Gabriel says. "Our sister... had little to say. She barely spoke a few words before withdrawing."

"Where dids't thou find her?" Raphael asks. "Gressil's prison?"

Jason nods. "I assembled a platoon of soldiers to rescue Hope and several humans from the Labyrinth. While we were there, we uncovered a locked room Gressil clearly didn't want anyone to enter. Cassiel was inside."

Raphael frowns. "Cassiel?"

"That is the name the humans have decided to call her." Gabriel quickly interjects. "Considering the torture she has suffered, I thought it prudent to allow her to keep her identity hidden, until she decided to speak her name, herself."

Michael's expression becomes heavy. "...I have spoken with Heaven's Daughter on many occasions. She used to wear my ring on her finger and talk to me while she went about her business. She always had such a... bubbly personality. So full of life and joy. But now..."

"A dark cloud pervades her gaze." Raphael says, sighing deeply. "Tell me more about the state thou hath discovered her in, Wordsmith."

Jason nods. He spends the next several minutes detailing what happened in Faith's End to all of the Archangels, including the revelations about Lucifer being trapped inside of Gressil's body, and Gressil's apparent ability to nullify magical energy.

"Tsk." Uriel hisses. "I am only a little familiar with Heaven's Daughter. Rumors of her name reached Barbatos on a few occasions... but I never had the privilege to speak with or see her in person. Now that I have... she seems so... frail."

"She was not like that in the past." Raphael murmurs. "She was the mightiest of Lazarites. She earned countless titles for her bravery in battle. Daughter of Heaven. Child of the Light. As a mere Lazarite, she even slew Emperor Red Raven in one-on-one combat, earning herself the title of Blood Reaper. Previously, only the Archangels could accomplish such a feat."

Jason widens his eyes in surprise. "What the hell? She's that impressive of a figure? Could that be why Gressil... kept her alive?"

Raphael's expression turns grim. He nods slowly, disgusted by the notion. "What an unforgivable hellspawn Gressil is. To bring the Child of Light to such depths of despair... I can only imagine the horrible cruelty he heaped upon her."

"Undoubtedly, she suffers not only from the trauma of physical and mental torture, but the burden of our expectations..." Michael murmurs. "For her to have been unable to free herself and eventually learn that she is the last living angel... she bears a weight heavier than any mountain."

All of the Archangels fall silent.

"It doesn't matter if she is the greatest Archangel or the weakest Lazarite." Phoebe says, piping up in the absence of conversation. "She has suffered terribly. If she wants to stay silent, then we must allow her to do so. Let us hope that Soleil will be able to offer her some comfort."

Raphael blinks. "Soleil? Ah! Was she the other angel sitting beside the Child of Light?"

"Soleil is not an angel, but a construct." Jason explains. "I wanted to give Cassiel a friend she could lean on and confide in. Soleil is simply the first idea that popped into my head."

Jason explains Soleil's purpose and capabilities, leading the Archangels to nod along to his words.

"I see." Raphael says. "Aye, t'was a thoughtful measure to take, Wordsmith. I doubt... 'Cassiel'... would enjoy the company of demons or monsters. As for humans... I dare not postulate."

"Why dids't thou nameth her Cassiel?" Michael asks. "The name of my fallen sister... an Archangel... 'tis not appropriate for a mere Lazarite-"

"It is a fine name." Raphael interjects. "The Child of Light is not to be compared to other Lazarites. Her control of the Lazarite Essence towers above those of her kin. For her to use an Archangel's name as an alias, I do not object."

Michael opens his mouth to retort, but thinks better of it.

"...Thou art right, eldest brother. Our precious sister deserves accolades. She carries our hopes and dreams. Even if all of us should perish, she may still bear our torch. And if she wishes to remain on the sidelines, she has also earned that right."

"The old distinctions of purebloods and human-born matter little." Gabriel muses. "What remains of our people all stand here, upon this world. We are nothing more than a sliver of our former strength. We must respect Cassiel's wishes, no matter what they be."

All of the Archangels reach a consensus, leaving Jason to shrug and go along with them.

"I won't lie. I'm still interested in what makes Cassiel so... special. What is this 'Lazarite Essence' you spoke of? Can you at least tell me that much?"

All four of the Archangels become quiet. They look thoughtfully at one another, as if unsure what they should say.

Raphael waves his hand. "Bah. There is no point in hiding the truth. The Lazarite Essence is the core of their power structure. It was intended to be a simple power that any human could master, upon resurrecting in a Lazarite body. Humans are naturally incapable of understanding complex magic, especially those who die later in life. But the Child of Light was different."

"She died when she was still only a babe." Michael explains. "As such, she was able to grasp hold of important concepts more easily than adult humans could. Of course, countless children perished and revived as Lazarites, so that situation was not particularly unique, but she also..."

He pauses.

"She also... possessed a certain affinity to holy magic. She was able to control it in a more unique manner than other humans could ever hope to copy. As such, she became the mightiest wielder of the Lazarite's Essence."

"Alright, but what _is_ this whole 'essence' thing?" Jason asks. "Just give it to me straight."

"Very well." Raphael responds. He summons a tiny, illusory image of Cassiel above his palm, levitating her like a poseable action figure. "All Lazarites possessed the ability to summon holy magic into existence. We taught them to use this ability in order to don weapons and armor, protecting themselves and increasing their fighting capabilities."

Raphael alters Cassiel's figure, making shining golden armor materialize onto her body, its appearance resembling hardened mana of average strength.

"This would allow any Lazarite to conjure swords, hammers, spears, shields, and other simple implements into existence to battle their demonic foes. The problem came in that the vast majority were simply incapable of conjuring anything more complicated, than bows and arrows. The moment they tried to create complex forms of mana, their efforts fell apart."

"And Cassiel was different?" Jason asks.

"Oh, yes. Very much so." Raphael affirms. "She was the Child of Light! Her ability to conjure and control holy energy went well beyond the limits of all other Lazarites. Indeed, she was even more capable than the Valkyries, and her proficiency nearly reached the level of my brothers and sisters among the Archangels! She was truly an elite!"

Michael smiles. "I admired her tenacity and willpower. She never stopped learning. No matter what humans invented, she would learn to craft weapons and implements similar to those found on Earth. By the time of the Energy Wars, she had become so deadly in battle that Demon Emperors regularly avoided her whenever she appeared."

"We had to engage in some level of subterfuge to keep the demons guessing." Raphael says with a smirk. "We placed Cassiel under the care of humans on occasion, hiding her identity with special talismans, so she could locate and sneak up on demons to kill them. Once the Blood Reaper appeared on the field of battle, even an Archangel would seem preferable to fight."

"She always did detest demons, from the depths of her soul." Gabriel muses. "Not dissimilar to..."

He trails off, leaving the sentence to linger in the air.

"Not dissimilar to who?" Jason asks.

"If Cassiel wishes to say, so she shall." Raphael says, falling silent.

"She really does sound incredible..." Phoebe says. "What a tragedy for her to end up like this. I wonder how she ended up trapped with Gressil? How did she fall into his clutches?"

"Gressil can nullify magical energy." Jason says. "He probably caught her offguard. It's not too hard to imagine."

"Mmm." Michael grunts, looking at Cassiel's abode. "I... wish to speak to my sister alone. Wordsmith, brothers, sister, woulds't thou allow me a few minutes with her?"

Jason and the others nod.

"Go ahead. All of you can take turns." Jason says. "But... try not to overwhelm her. If she doesn't want to talk, don't force her to speak."

"I know." Michael says, his tone softening. "I won't do anything that will hurt... Cassiel's soul. I just wish to spend a little time with her."

Michael steps out of the sound isolation bubble and walks over to Cassiel's door. After stepping inside, he closes it, leaving the others to wonder what they might discuss.

After Michael leaves, Raphael turns to Jason.

"Regarding other matters, there is something I wish to discuss, Wordsmith. Verily, I hath failed to mention this before, but my brother, sister, and I observed the First Emperor of demonkind on Hell Harbor. His title..."

Raphael pauses abruptly, as if momentarily confused.

"His title is... 'twas... erm..."

The old man hems and haws for a few long moments before frowning.

"I... cannot recall. I forgot to mention him before, though the reason why escapes me. He appeared at the end of our battle against Ose. He spoke to her and said..."

The Archangel of Wisdom pauses once again.

"Verily, he spake to her. I watched their exchange. I listened... but regarding the contents of their discussion..."

"I cannot recall either," Uriel says with a frown. "I do not remember what he looked like, or what he said. I remember only that he appeared and said... _something._"

Jason listens to their conversation with rapt attention.

"...You mean to tell me two Archangels have become totally forgetful? Maybe I can help. **Remember.**"

Jason aims his magic at Raphael's mind, intending to make the old Archangel remember just what, exactly he observed. However, instead of revitalizing Raphael's thoughts, his vision dims considerably.

"Erm... forgiveth me, Wordsmith. What matter was I speaking about?"

"The First Emperor." Jason says, his frown deepening. "I just- don't you remember anything?"

"First Emperor?" Raphael repeats. "Dost though referreth to Satan the Devil? What of him?"

"I don't know." Jason replies. "I assume you meant someone else. What the hell is going on? Why are you forgetting, all of a sudden? You brought him up!"

Raphael stares at the Wordsmith as if gazing at an idiot. He turns to Uriel.

"Sister. What nonsense does the Wordsmith speak of?"

"The First Emperor." Uriel repeats. "He was... we saw him, brother. I believe? He was on Hell Harbor."

"Satan the Devil is alive?" Raphael asks, nearly jumping out of his skin. "I thought he perished along with the Black Queen!"

""Uh... well, he _did..._" Jason interrupts. "I don't think you guys are talking about Satan... so let's just drop the subject. But, uh... regarding the Black Queen... boy, do I have something that will get your noggins jogging."

With the Archangels having left immediately following Stormbringer, none of them know of Amelia's reappearance, her subsequent assistance in locating Hope, and the revelation that she is not, in fact, actually Amelia. Annette, the sister of Debra, turns out to be a huge surprise for the Archangels, as Jason's story leaves them reeling from one twist after another.

"For there to be such a matter!" Raphael exclaims. "Verily, thy clone hath gotten up to some tomfoolery!"

"Yes." Jason affirms. "But I don't blame him. I blame myself. I blame myself for a _lot_ of things that have gone wrong in the last six years."

The Wordsmith sighs.

"In the meantime, Uriel, why don't you have Gabriel's ring back? I expect you'll be sticking around here for a while?"

The Archangel of Retribution crosses her arms.

"Indeed I shall, Wordsmith. I still hunger for my spears to taste the flesh of bloodskin Emperors, but for now, I must rest. Transforming into the Cherubiim has drained my soul, and without my original body, my soul's recovery speed shall prove quite slow."

"Can you transform again?" Jason asks.

"...Perhaps. 'Tis within the realm of possibility, but it shall require a not-insignificant sum of time. In the worst case, I may require several months, years, or even decades to transform again."

"And Uriel shall require my assistance, as well as Michael or Gabriel's," Raphael explains. "'Tis impossible for one or two Archangels to transform into a Triad State. The Ophaniim, Seraphiim, and Cherubiim are all mighty forms that would cause despair to fall upon the demon Emperors."

"I assume each of the three forms has their own advantages and disadvantages?" Jason asks, probing for more information. 

"Aye, they do. But for now, only the Cherubiim and Seraphiim lay within reach. None of the Ophaphs remain among the living. Not Samael, not Cassiel, nor Muriel, so that transformation has been lost forever. And as for the Seraphiim..."

Raphael chuckles coyly.

"Well, it has a few merits."

"I imagine so." Jason replies.

None of the Archangels or Jason bother talking about Cassiel or the First Emperor again, especially as Raphael appears to have completely forgotten what he was saying. Jason chalks his memory loss up to dementia and decides to say nothing, not wanting to insult the old man.

A few minutes later, Jason passes Gabriel's ring to Uriel, and she conjures a summon identical to his original form. While lacking all his physical strength and other attributes, the fact he can interact with the physical realm for the first time in millennia makes Gabriel noticeably happier. He pokes the dirt with his fingers, then scoops it up and allows it to fall from his palm.

"Mmm. Physical sensations. How long has it been...?"

While Gabriel casually plays with his sense of touch, Raphael turns to another topic.

"Jason. During the meeting between Confessor Vulpanix and Ose, I overheard an important piece of intelligence."

He leans toward the Wordsmith with a grave look on his face.

"The Volgrim... have Excalibur in their possession."

"What? They do?" Jason gasps. "Why would Vulpanix mention something like that?"

"Because." Raphael answers. "The demons know not of Camael's Cube, nor do the Volgrim. 'Tis thy secret weapon. However, due to this misunderstanding, Ose let slip that she believed thou possessed Excalibur, and Vulpanix set the record straight. Currently, Excalibur resides in some sort of secret Volgrim vault; so claimeth the Confessor."

Jason frowns. "A secret Volgrim vault. Hmm. If I were to obtain Excalibur, would that make any sort of difference? Can I even wield a weapon of that power level?"

Raphael nods. "Heroes are generally capable of doing so. Excalibur still possesses the intact soul of King Arthur. However, thy ability to master it is limited by thy control of mana. And forgive this old bird for his bluntness, but thy control is quite... _shoddy._ Compared to Arthur himself, thou art barely a hatchling. Thy instinctual control of mana is all but nonexistent, and thy internal reserves are quite minuscule. Still, Excalibur is always useful as a simple mana battery. Compared to Camael's Cube, it is a 'perfect' artifact that does not leak mana. It is the mightiest of my sister's creations for a reason."

"Obtaining Excalibur needs to become a high priority for thee." Uriel says. "The Volgrim have possessed it for a hundred millennia. What use they have obtained from it, we do not know. T'would be unfortunate if they had obtained a method to tap into its mana reserves."

"Well. I can't use 'Locate' to find Excalibur." Jason mutters while rubbing his chin. "I'll have to put it on the backburner for now. Uplifting humanity is my primary goal."

Jason continues to talk with the Archangels, exchanging ideas about how to proceed.

While talking, Jason's T-REX beeps three times, catching his attention. The Wordsmith activates it, covering his body in nanite armor.

Once the T-REX helmet covers his head and face, a message pops up, along with Physical-Phoebe's face.

"Hey! Jason, you won't believe who popped by!"

"Who?" Jason asks.

"Blinker! She said Marie Becker had something she wanted to give you. Said it was urgent, so I contacted you right away."

"Marie Becker..." Jason mutters. "Sounds like trouble. Let me see what it is. Where is Blinker now?"

"She's at the Core. Mind popping over?"

"Sure thing, honey."

Jason turns off the call, deactivates his helmet, and smiles apologetically at the Archangels.

"'Scuse me a minute, everyone. I have a friend waiting for me in the Core. Blinker's back, and she has a present from Marie Becker."

Raphael waves Jason away. "Yes, yes. You may go. We shall continue to wait for our time to speak with Cassiel."

"Alright. I'll be back soon."

Jason offers a wave goodbye, then utters a Word of Power.

"**Return.**"

His body, and Spirit-Phoebe's, both vanish from Tarus II and instantly reappear inside the Labyrinth Core, atop an elevated outcropping on the wall where nobody is allowed to enter, lest Jason teleport inside of them.

The Wordsmith levitates to the floor and trots over to his wife, where he finds Blinker sitting on her shoulder, back in her tiny fairy-form.

"Blinker!" Jason says. "Heya, girl. How are you doing? That baby bump looks a little bigger than I remember."

Blinker shrugs. "Hey, Jason. I'm doing alright. Still trying to find happiness... you know how it is."

Jason's face twitches, making his forced smile more than a little uneasy.

"Haha... yeah. I do."

Neither of them mentions Kar's name, but both of them think it.

Blinker sighs. She motions with her hands and summons a rip in space, where a small black 'bead' the size of a marble falls out of a dimensional storage space and levitates in the air.

Jason gently plucks the bead out of the air with two fingers and frowns.

"Uh. What's this?"

"It's... something really exciting." Blinker says, without a hint of a smile. "You'll hate what you see, Word-man."

"What do you mean?" He asks. "I don't follow."

"It's some sort of computer... data... _thingy_..." Blinker says. "I don't know all the details, okay? Miss Marie just told me to tell you it has a recording of something really important on it. Specifically..."

Blinker leans forward and crinkles her nose.

"...It's a recording of a really big, important Volgrim meeting. The Founders addressed their entire species, collectively. And they talked about _you._ A lot."

The hair raises on the back of Jason's neck.

"Oh. Oh, yeah. That... certainly does sound _important_."

"And scary." Phoebe affirms. "Let's not delay. I want to hear what they had to say."

"As do I." Jason concludes.
