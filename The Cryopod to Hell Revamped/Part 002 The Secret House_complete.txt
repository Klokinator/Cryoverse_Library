The soles of Jason's feet slapped on the floor as he walked along a narrow, lightly illuminated corridor. The sanded dirt path beneath him began to feel less like soft dirt, and more like coarse limestone. The orb in his right hand illuminated the way forward, but also seemed redundant, given the walls had glowing rocks embedded all over the place. Where had those been when he had fled that damn statue monster?

Within a few minutes of walking, Jason headed right, then left, following nothing but his intuition as he made his way down one hallway and another. To his surprise, he ended up standing before a metallic frame wrapping around the entirety of the corridor. The square slabs of metal gave off an impression of being unlike any metal he'd seen before. He took a moment to evaluate them, only to raise his eyes and stare into a pitch-black room—one illuminated only by his glowing orb. The rocks that had led him here no longer provided any light.

Stepping inside, Jason swept his eyes in a wide arc and frowned. The place looked like an abandoned storehouse. Piles of scrap metal lay scattered about here and there. The metallic floor exposed itself only after he left footprints in the thick layer of dust shrouding it. The ceiling, too, almost reminded him of the Cryotek facility, in that it too had a slight metallic sheen to it. Rising nearly forty feet above him, it towered imposingly, making the storage facility seem far bigger than he might normally expect.

"Hello?" Jason whispered into the void, but his voice traveled only a few feet forward before the neverending darkness swallowed it whole.

Slowly, he made his way forward, taking care not to step on any metal shards. The last thing he needed was to hobble himself and catch an infection. Each movement of his feet brought him further into the depths of the storage. Eventually, he reached the opposite wall, nearly two hundred feet from where he had entered.

The facility was quite large. _Who built it? What is it doing in a place like this, where freaky statue and shadow monsters roam freely? What is its purpose?_ Jason wondered.

As he conjured questions one after the other, he lowered his gaze and froze in place as one particular chunk of metal caught his eye. Unlike the others, this one had a certain shape to it, almost as if someone had crafted it intentionally.

Leaning down, Jason bent over to pick it up. A jolt of awe shot through his back as he quickly recognized its purpose.

"It's a gun!" he exclaimed.

Indeed. Unlike any weapon Jason had seen or imagined, this weapon had a slender, circular handle, allowing him to wrap his hand around it. It contoured to his hand perfectly, making him wonder if someone had custom-built it, knowing he would come here. The most surprising thing was that it didn't appear to have anywhere to insert bullets or ammunition.

_It appears to be... a laser gun? A space-age weapon?_ Jason thought.

No matter how he tried to explain its appearance, the fact remained that this weapon was far removed from any design he'd seen outside of movies and comics. Without hesitation, he aimed it at the wall and pressed his finger against what he assumed was the trigger.

_Click._

Nothing happened.

Jason stared at the weapon for a moment before sighing. It was dead now. Whatever battery or power source it once had, it had long since lost its charge.

Still, seeing the blaster sent a shiver down his spine. The design was unconventional. The long needle-tip sticking out of its front appeared alien in origin, making him wonder whether a human had crafted it... or something else.

_Perhaps those monsters I've seen might know something about it,_ Jason thought.

With a shrug, he dropped the weapon and continued on his way. It wouldn't be useful to him, so there was no reason to carry it around. He needed his hands free for other purposes.

He took a moment to glance around the facility again, but its size was simply too huge for even his magical orb to light up everything. After a minute of thinking, a lightbulb appeared above his head.

_I spoke a word when that shadow monster attacked me. "Light." When I spoke, a blast of light fired from my palm and incinerated it. Does that mean I can do it again?_ Jason thought, considering the possibility.

With a shrug, he spoke up, elevating his voice a little louder than usual.

"**Light.**"

A flash of brilliance escaped his body and fired into the distance! After a moment, it halted at the center of the room and began brightening. He winced as the brilliant light easily dwarfed his orb and illuminated every corner of the massive facility.

Only then did Jason finally get a good look at the entire chamber. Piles of metal stacked up to the ceiling at random intervals, while other areas had only small mounds of finished gadgets, all of varying quality.

He spent several minutes walking around, picking up anything he could find. However, no matter where he looked, all he found were depleted weapons and random devices; their glory days long since faded. None of them served him any purpose, so he left them behind.

As he stumbled around, fatigue swelled within his body. He was still suffering from cryogenic sickness, but already he'd fled from a statue monster and fought off a shadow monster as well.

Jason was hungry, thirsty, and dead tired.

After skimming the various scrap piles, he eventually plopped his butt down on the filthy floor and sighed.

_I don't know where I am, but I know it's not the Cryotek facility. How did I get here? What happened while I was in suspended animation? Where are all the people, and why do monsters lurk around every corner?_ Jason wondered desperately.

He wanted to cry, but he was too dehydrated to muster any tears. Instead, he stared at his feet, wondering what he could do to alleviate his pain and suffering.

Minutes passed.

An idea occurred to him.

_I spoke the word 'Light' earlier and summoned light. Could another word have a similar effect?_ Jason thought.

Figuring it couldn't hurt to try, he spoke again.

"**Pizza.**"

_Fwoop!_

Hardly had the word left his mouth before a giant pizza twice as big as anything he'd received from any restaurant materialized before him. Jason jumped in fright as the unexpected gift from the heavens fell down and slapped against the floor, scattering dust in every direction. The giant cardboard box encircling it had no logos, nor markings of any sort, giving it the impression that the Creator himself had crafted it.

He stared for a moment, wide-eyed, as the smell of a hundred mouth-watering toppings wafted toward his nose.

_I summoned a pizza? I actually did it! Holy shit!_ Jason thought in amazement.

He reached a trembling hand toward the box, and a sumptuous sight greeted him as he opened the lid. He'd always been a Supreme pizza lover, but not even he had ever imagined there could be so many toppings on a single pie.

In the blink of an eye, his hands moved faster than his mind could react. He shoved huge masses of cheese, meat, and vegetables into his face. Every bite intoxicated him. He almost began to lose himself as he rapidly regained his stamina and filled up his empty belly.

Ten minutes later, Jason flopped backward and gasped for breath. His body felt ten pounds heavier. The exhaustion from before faded away, and a comfortable sensation of satiation replaced it.

"**Bed.**"

He spoke another word, and a mattress appeared behind him. It took all of his remaining strength, but he managed to crawl atop it and drift away... away to a place where the world once wasn't so scary, and the monsters weren't out to eat him.

_Ah. The simple times. How I long for them..._ Jason thought as sleep claimed him.

Zzz...
