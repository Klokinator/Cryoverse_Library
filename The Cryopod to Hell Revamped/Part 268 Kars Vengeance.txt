Part 268: Kar's Vengeance

_Tarus II, to the west of Hero City. Second day, first hour._

Darkness covers the land. With the clock having struck one hour past midnight, the night-time gloom spreads out in all directions, illuminated only by red-hot bullets whizzing through the air, magical fireballs exploding against metallic armor, or the spotlights of Phoebe's mechanized war machines.

Half of the Horned Forest blazes uncontrollably, lit aflame a few hours before by unscrupulous Burner Grunts. From the forest's confines, countless huge and horrifying creatures flee, some enraged, others terrified. A fifteen-foot-tall Buffalo-looking animal with two gigantic horns and a mouthful of knife-shaped teeth charges toward humanity's embattlements. Before the Troopers can gun it down, it stomps ten men and women to death, killing them mercilessly. Eventually, a nearby mechanoid whirls around, fires twice into the creature's skull, and puts it down for good.

Phoebe Hiro leans heavily against a furred Felaris soldier, a female of her kind. The cat-like biped helps ease Phoebe into a chair on the backlines, allowing the exhausted Commander to slump in its embrace and breathe heavily.

"Mraww! Are you okay, Miss Hiro? You need to sleep! You've been awake for over twenty-four hours!"

Phoebe closes her eyes. She swallows several breaths while listening to the distant screams of her dying friends and comrades.

"The... the demons... they're coming faster than ever... cough. I can't sleep now. They need me."

"They need a leader who can think properly," The Felaris lady says. "You're much too tired for that!"

It takes the Hero's Wife several seconds to reply. She forces her eyes open for a moment before they close again of their own volition. "No... I can't sleep. The, the uh... the injector. Give me the injector."

"The injector? What injector? I don't understand..." The Felaris female meows.

"It's in that toolkit over there," Phoebe explains, vaguely gesticulating in the direction of several black chests. "The adrenaline injector. You know what a hypodermic needle is, right, Sandy?"

Sandy, the Felaris, nods. "Yes! Big, long, pointy needle. But miss Hiro, this isn't good for you! If you give yourself adrenaline, you'll only exhaust your body further."

"Haha..." Phoebe laughs, her voice strained. "I'd rather end up in a coma than dead. My husband can always Wordsmith me back to optimal condition, as can Belial's magic."

"Well... if you insist..." Sandy meows. She walks over to the metal chests, pops them open, and starts rifling around inside. It only takes her a minute to locate a small syringe filled with golden liquid, tucked inside a transparent box. She pulls it out and extracts the syringe, then walks over to Phoebe. "Mraww! Here is the needle. Be careful! Don't hurt yourself with it."

Phoebe takes the needle and sighs. "Thanks. This is, without a doubt... the worst part."

After sucking in a deep breath, Phoebe grits her teeth and plunges the needle into her heart, then squeezes the fluid into her body.

"Aaaaargh! Ahhh! Goddamned, shit-licking, cow-balls!"

Tears flow freely from Phoebe's eyes as her special solution rips through her blood vessels and all throughout her cardiovascular system. She quickly pulls the needle out and throws it aside, then jumps to her feet and kneels forward.

"Huuack! Huurk!"

She vomits onto the grass several times from the sheer shock of so much energy traveling within her veins. Sandy watches nervously, clasping her paws together as she tries to assess Phoebe's health.

"M-miss Hiro? Are you okay? Here, have some water!"

She grabs a nearby bottle of cold water and holds it out. Phoebe snatches it away, tilts her head back, and dumps half of the bottle into her mouth, while the rest washes across her face and spills onto her chest.

"Kuhak! Oh, heavens... that feeling is one I'll never enjoy..."

After rinsing the puke out of her mouth, Phoebe stands up straight, a forced alertness in her gaze. She blinks several times and shakes her head ferociously.

"Okay. Okay! I'm awake. Thank you, Sandy. You helped me a lot."

The Felaris woman nods. "Anything to help, miss Hiro. I'm just sorry I can't help on the frontlines. I'm only a relief worker..."

"It takes all types of personnel to fight a war," Phoebe replies. "Your work here is invaluable. I'm going to head back to the frontlines, now."

"Okay! Stay safe, miss Hiro."

As Sandy waves goodbye, Phoebe stumbles away. Her stomach burns with an electrical pain, forcing her eyes open in spite of the exhaustion wracking her brain.

"I'm only buying myself another hour or two..." Phoebe mutters. "My next crash will be way worse."

She heads in the direction of a communication and armory depot, one of the many mobile platforms built to service the battlefield in the long-term. A giant square surface with dozens of boxes of weapons, ammo, and supplies rests atop a drivable vehicle, allowing it to move backward or forward as the battle-lines shift.

Phoebe climbs onto the moving platform and walks over to a group of officers clustered together. The moment they spot their Commander, they spin on their heels and salute. "Commander Hiro on deck!"

The vomit and water splashed all over Phoebe's face doesn't bother her officers in the slightest. A single glance at her baggy eyes tells them that despite getting a short sleep-break earlier themselves, their Commander has taken no rest whatsoever, and worked far harder than all of them combined.

"Status update." Phoebe mutters.

"Commander," A nearby male officer says. "We're losing ground more quickly now that night has fallen. Already, we've pulled the frontlines back another hundred and fifty feet in the past hour. The demons have begun mixing shadow-walkers into their army, allowing them to take out our troopers with carefully placed ambushes."

A female officer nods. "On the southern front, Lieutenant Samuel has managed to gain ground on the demonic hordes. After Captain Yamir reinforced him mid-day, they started working together to set up reinforced walls and battlements as per your specification."

"On the Northern Front, we've successfully launched five of the ten Covenant ships. We've sent 100,000 citizens offworld, and are loading the second group now," the First Officer continues. He holds out a datapad with several numbers and diagrams. "Here are the manifests, Commander."

"I don't need to see them," Phoebe says, ignoring the man's outstretched hand. "Elder Skarde knows what he's doing. What about the Eastern Front? Has Kar managed to kill both Battle Brothers yet?"

"I'm afraid not, Commander," The female officer says. "Their battle still rages. Fortunately, because of how intense the fighting has become, it's significantly cut our losses on that side. Any demons who try to bypass King Kar's battle end up dead. Apparently, Kar has declared that anyone who interferes before his battle finishes will die. Our troops have decided to standby and wait."

"That _is_ good news," Phoebe says, forcing a smile. "Fewer casualties are always worth praising. What about Samantha? Belial, I mean. Is she still alive?"

The man nods. "Yes. Alive and well, I might add. I could be wrong, but she seems to be rampaging even more fiercely than when she started. Apparently, the demonic forces cannot kill her, and have thus chosen to instead slow her down. Reports have it that several Burrowers have taken to manipulating the terrain around her to stymie her movements, but these actions have only enraged her further, making her attacks even more vicious and violent. Were it not for Belial's assistance, I fear the demonic armies would have poured across our barriers several hours ago. They hold a tremendous advantage during the cover of night."

Phoebe's energy-filled eyes lose some of their luster. "...Even more vicious and violent, you say."

"Commander?"

"No, it's... it's nothing. I just... I feel a bit helpless and angry right now. Belial shouldn't have to step in for us. This is my failing. If I'd prepared better, maybe we could have defended ourselves better... maybe the hospital wouldn't have..."

The female officer shakes her head. "Do not blame yourself, Commander. You've done all that you could. We can still win. Your husband is the Hero! Once he returns... he'll certainly save us from this hell."

"Yes. I hope you're right," Phoebe mutters.

After listening to a few more reports, Phoebe walks over to the munitions chests and grabs a beaten-up exosuit controller, the same one she's worn all day. She sticks her arms through the straps, then connects them behind her back. After fitting it snugly against her chest, Phoebe taps its ignition button twice, causing nanites to erupt from its core and spread across her body.

After her nanosuit solidifies, Phoebe starts to leave. Before she can depart the communication platform, the female officer from before steps in front of her.

"Commander. I mean no disrespect, but you're in no shape to return to the field."

"I'm not going to fight," Phoebe replies. "I'm only wearing this for my protection. I know damn well I can't shoot when my body aches as badly as it does right now."

"Alright, I understand," The officer says. "Just... take care, Commander. We need you right now. If something happens and you fall tonight... we'll lose this war in the same hour. You're our moral support, so please don't do anything that will get yourself killed."

"You don't need to tell me twice," Phoebe replies, squeezing her subordinate's shoulder. As she leaves, she taps the side of her helmet. "Let me know if anything important comes up."

"Like your husband returning?"

"Exactly."

Humanity's Commander leaves the depot. With an exosuit wrapped around her skin, Phoebe's speed increases substantially, allowing her to jog at a casual twenty miles an hour. 

She arrives at the midlines, where three Planet-Walkers continue firing into the distant demonic hordes, only ever pausing to reload. However, a fourth mech lays on its side some distance away, a huge hole torn through its cockpit.

"What happened here?" Phoebe barks, while walking up to a group of flustered mechanics.

"Commander!" A Salamander yells. The yellow-scaled monster doesn't even bother with a salute, instead continuing to frantically dig around inside the mech's internal circuitry. "It shuckin' sucks! A group o' dem' Burrowers and Brutes emerged from the backline. They done popped up in our blind spot and attacked Gold Three. Pilot Daniels died during the ambush."

The Salamander gestures to his left with a quick head-nod. Phoebe follows his gaze to find a half-dozen dead demons laying in a pile, each one with blaster holes in their chests.

"God... god damn it..." Phoebe growls. "Can you fix it? These Planet-Walkers are our strongest assault mechs!"

"We'll need sev'ral hours, ma'am," The Salamander drawls. "I called up Ogie, but it'll be a bit 'fore he can make it here. He knows this stuff way better'n me."

Phoebe's expression clouds over with doubt. "We don't have hours. I need this thing back up and shooting, pronto. Can you hack together a solution?"

The Salamander shrugs. "Dunno, ma'am. I could maybe jump in 'n make it go on auto-pilot, but it'll be way slower 'n less reliable without a pilot."

"That'll have to do," Phoebe replies, nodding confidently. "Make it so. I'll check back later."

After finishing with the Salamander, Phoebe recalls her helmet and rubs her head as a bit of her energy fades away.

"I just... have to hold on... until Jason returns. If he doesn't... then we're all doomed."

_On the eastern side of Hero City._

Deafening shockwaves erupt, flattening the fields between humanity and the demons. Both sides keep their distance, watching with a mixture of shock and horror as Kar, King of all Monsters, engages in combat with the Battle Brothers, Zamiel and Duriel.

The two Demon Barons take beating after beating. Kar punches Duriel's stomach so hard that the younger Battle Brother flies three hundred feet straight backward, bounces off the ground, and skids another hundred feet before eventually sliding to a stop. Kar doesn't even pause before tail-whipping Zamiel, sending him flying in the opposite direction. He, too, forms an intimate relationship with Tarus II's terrain as he lands face-first in the dirt, shearing much of the skin off his cheeks, forehead, and nose.

Before Duriel can get back up, Kar jumps into the air with his fist raised. He falls upon the younger, bigger brother like a meteor, pounding him against the planet's soil with enough force to knock dozens of distant humans and demons off their feet.

The humans' battle line, not far west of Kar and the Battle Brothers' positions, remains tense as they watch the brutal display of power unfolding before them. "Kar is incredible! I haven't even seen Blinker help him much over these last eight hours; he's been treating those two Barons like his personal punching bags the whole time!"

"Yes," A nearby kobold grunts, "but don't you find it odd? The Battle Brothers are only Barons, yet they're still putting up a hell of a fight. Any other Baron would have died by now."

On the demons' side of the battlefield, Ose's armies stand at the ready, eying the battle with caution.

"Remember," A low-ranking Baron says, "don't take a single step forward! I'm not going to die in some useless, unwinnable fight. We don't wanna lose any more troops to the Sphinx, so we'll just keep our distance."

"You're a coward!" A nearby Demon Lord yells. "You're a Baron, too! Go help the Battle Brothers!"

"Screw that! Can't you tell something is different about Zamiel and Duriel?" The Baron retorts. "Those two are WAY stronger than me! If I go over there, the Sphinx'll kill me without batting an eye! I'm as useless as you against someone like the Monster King!"

The Lord snarls. "Shrakh! Since when were monsters better than demons? Everyone knows monsters are low-class rats."

Not far behind that Demon Lord, a pair of Orcs glance at one another meaningfully.

"Hmph. Stupid demon. He not know glory of Elder Orc and Ancient Orc," the first one mutters.

"Quiet," The other Orc grunts. "That a big secret. We no talk about the Ancient Ones here."

"Oops. Sorry."

Both of them grin evilly, but fall silent, saying nothing else.

Meanwhile, Kar continues kicking and punching Duriel. He grabs the giant Baron by the neck, lifts him overhead, and slams him into the dirt, over and over.

_Whump! Whump! Whump!_

The sound of a body hitting the topsoil makes all the distant participants cringe sympathetically, even the humans.

"Hurgh! You are a durable little snake," Kar says, as he holds Duriel's limp body high. He punches Duriel's demonstone armor, causing a gong-like sound to ring out over the battlefield.

_Bwang!_

Duriel coughs. "Ugh..."

At that moment, a dozen spectral hammers form overhead, each one five feet long with quarter-ton heads attached to their handles. They fly at Kar, forcing him to spin around and hold Duriel up as a shield. Instead of striking the Battle Brother, the hammers curve around and crash into Kar.

_Boom-boom-boom!_

Kar drops Duriel reflexively and staggers backward, but appears relatively uninjured. Before he can grab the younger brother, another hammer violently flies in an upward curving arc, punts Duriel skyward, and sends him flying into the demons' battle lines.

"Oh, shrakh!" A goblin screams. Too late, before he can shove his way through the throng of Ose's assembled army, Duriel hurtles into the mass of enemies, crushing and killing half a dozen of them.

Zamiel, still badly injured, staggers toward Kar while weaving hand signs. His eyes flash red as he summons another wave of hammers and sends them hurtling toward Kar.

"Hurgh!" Kar grunts. "More useless tricks! You never know when to give up, murdering bastards."

The hammers crash against Kar's scales, but he wades through them easily, shrugging off the hits as if they were pebbles glancing off a windshield.

Kar glances back at the demon army, watching as Duriel jumps to his feet, completely healed of his injuries. The Battle Brother roars in rage and charges at the Sphinx again. "Stupid croc! You keep letting me heal! I'll make you regret that decision!"

Instead of fear or panic, a casual smirk passes over Kar's face. "I have broken your bodies dozens of times now. Finishing the two of you off is a simple matter for me. What you have failed to realize is that my only goal is to cause you pain."

Duriel summons a one-ton greathammer. He grabs it, hefts it high, and swings it down, intending to shatter Kar's skull.

The crocodile raises his arm. When the hammer reaches striking distance, he backhands it, tearing the weapon's head off and flinging it to the side. The remaining stump whacks Kar's chest with the piddling impact force of a whiffle bat.

"You slaughtered my family..." Kar grunts, as he balls his right claw into a fist.

"You tortured them, maimed them, and caused them immense suffering," He continues, as he rears his arm back.

"And so I must pay you back in kind, ten times over... for each life you ended!" Kar roars, while lunging forward.

_BOOM!_

He socks Duriel in the stomach, once again sending the Battle Brother careening backward. Duriel again crashes into the demons, this time crushing two dozen of his comrades into meat paste. He grinds to a stop atop their meaty chunks, gasping for breath.

"What I do not know... is why!" Kar yells, turning to face Zamiel. "Why did you kill my people?! Why did you murder the crocodiles?"

The older brother, still badly injured, clutches his broken ribs and wheezes out a laugh. "Hahaha... you know we can heal by absorbing blood, so you keep using our bodies as battering rams against our fellow demons. All this, just for your _pathetic_ revenge. You are a- _cough_! You are a miserable little reptile."

"Of course I am miserable..." Kar growls. A look of hatred envelops his face, causing his eyes to shrink to the narrowest of slits. "Pyra was a fine female. She was my beloved mate. She never hurt anyone in all her life, yet you butchered her! You crushed her eggs, tied her down, and impaled each of her limbs with your weapons! Why?! What was her sin? What were any of my people's sins?!"

Zamiel laughs again. "Sins? Pahaha! You're an imbecile. _Cough!_ What makes you think we needed a reason? We killed the crocodiles because it was _fun._"

"Liar!"

Kar leaps toward Zamiel. His speed, as swift as the wind, brings him into striking range in less than a second. All Zamiel sees is a blur of motion before Kar backhands him and sends him spinning and careening into the demon army once again.

Crocodile tears well up in Kar's eyes. "Huuuurgh! I never even got the chance to say goodbye! All I could do was hug my wife's corpse! Even if you die a million times, it won't be enough to make up for her suffering!"

Instead of running forward, Duriel climbs out of the bloody, meaty pit, and stands before the demonic army. He seems to realize something and grins sadistically.

"Ohhh, yeah. Pyra. Psh, you think me and big bro would remember one slime-scale's name? As if. I gutted so many nasty crocs, I couldn't tell you which of the ugly ones were female, and which were male. They all looked and smelled the same ta' me."

Zamiel, too, having crushed a bunch of demons to death, climbs out of their blood-soaked bodies and walks over to his brother's side. "Dur-Dur's got a point, croc-face! You might be the strongest crocodile of 'em all, but you're still a peace-loving simpleton. That's why you won't kill us!"

Kar pauses. The Reptile King straightens his posture and glares at both Battle Brothers with murderous intent.

"Hurgh. You are wrong. I _will_ kill you. But before then, I will break your bodies so that your souls may learn humility. I will make you experience the same suffering that every single one of my people did."

"Big words..." Zamiel mutters.

Both brothers look at each other, then nod.

"Alright Croc. We're done playin' games," Duriel says, curling his lips into a sneer. "You think you're big shit, but you ain't. We've been lettin' you knock us around for a good, long while now, all to see just how strong you've gotten."

"Turns out, you're tougher than we ever could've imagined," Zamiel adds. "But in the end, you're still a stupid croc. You're 'bout to find out how we took down all of your species in one day. Fifty thousand reptiles. Didn't even bat an eye."

Both brothers inhale in unison. Their armor audibly creaks, while popping sounds erupt from their joints. They raise their arms and flex their muscles, shifting their skin into a deathly, pale white color. This change makes their blood-red eyes stand out even more, giving them an appearance distinctive from all other demons.

Zamiel laughs. "Hahaha! Let's see what you're made of now, tough guy!"

They charge toward Kar, covering hundreds of feet in seconds. Their boots leave deep depressions in the flattened soil, each step bringing them thirty feet closer to the Sphinx.

Kar frowns. "Hurgh. So be it. Let me reveal your misplaced confidence to the world!"

Again, both sides enter a brutal melee. Kar's fists shake the planet while sending shockwaves out that knock humans, demons, and monsters off their feet. He summons rocks onto his fists and batters Duriel from left to right, but to Kar's surprise, Duriel takes the hits like a champ. Every time Kar knocks him back, Duriel stomps his feet into the dirt and holds his own, no longer allowing himself to go flying.

Zamiel too, doesn't bother with summoning weapons from above. Instead, he conjures a powerful greathammer, one with a solid demonstone shaft and head, and proceeds to attack Kar while he's busy with Duriel.

_Bang!_

Zamiel's first strike crashes against Kar's back, momentarily jarring the Sphinx's senses. Zamiel lands two more hits before Kar whirls around and spin-kicks the older brother in the chest, but the attack only pushes Zamiel back a few feet while he doesn't lose his footing.

Kar's animal instinct activates, processing the changes in his opponents. _Hurgh. The Battle Bastards have suddenly increased their power tremendously. Why has their skin turned pure white? Is this a limited-time transformation? Their bodies have become as mighty as a top-ranked Demon Duke!_

Instead of going to town on Duriel, Kar starts exercising more caution. He continues fighting both brothers, but it doesn't escape his notice that their speed and strength have skyrocketed, putting them on par with Barbatos.

"Hurgh! How tricky! It seems you two have been hiding some secrets," Kar remarks. Duriel swings a greatsword at him, but Kar instantly reinforces his arm with a layer of diamond to block the strike. "But I wonder how long you can hold on!"

...

Minutes pass. An hour.

Kar continues to breathe evenly, but to his shock, not once does he manage to send either brother flying. Hundreds of blows get exchanged between himself and his mortal enemies, but the Crocodile realizes they must have been holding back earlier. Their durability easily approaches his level.

"What's wrong, croc?" Zamiel laughs. "Not having fun anymore? What was all that nonsense you spouted earlier? Something about making our souls learn humility? Get real! Your power may have reached the level of a Demon Emperor, but it ain't enough to kill the likes of us!"

The brothers momentarily pull back and circle around Kar like a pair of wolves looking for an opening.

Kar assumes a defensive stance. His crocodilian eyelids flick over his eyes for a split-second. During that instant, both brothers lunge at him!

_Crash! Thunk! Bang!_

They beat and slice Kar a dozen times, finally drawing blood. Before Kar's blood can hit the ground, they both retreat while motioning with their hands, causing the plasma to fly up and into their mouths.

"Mmm! That's **good**!" Zamiel laughs, his pupils dilating. "Your blood tastes much better than your wife's!"

Duriel smacks his lips. "Nice and sticky, just the way I like it."

Kar, lightly injured, easily seals his wounds with his reptilian physiology. The sudden attack only momentarily startles him, but it doesn't cause him any long-lasting injuries.

"Hurgh. I see now. Your powers... they revolve around blood. How could I not see before? Blood doesn't only regenerate your injuries. It must also empower you."

"Wow! You figured it out!" Zamiel laughs, while sarcastically clapping. "Good job, croccy cunt!"

Duriel grins, exposing his teeth. "That's right, Monster Chump. Me 'n Zammy get way stronger each time we drink blood. And guess who got us to our current level? Bahaha! All your worthless croc friends!"

Zamiel lowers his voice. "You wanted to know why we killed the crocs? There's your answer, croc-master. We didn't go there to waste our time slaughtering some useless 'reps. We killed each one and drank their blood! When we started, we were low-level Barons. Weak, useless. Now? We're stronger than most Dukes."

Kar's expression turns to one of horror. "You filthy, evil wretches! HURGH! Crocodilian blood... is sacred!"

"And tasty!" Zamiel laughs. He and Duriel keep their distance, even as Kar starts to stomp toward them. "Just imagine how strong we'll be once we reach the ranks of Duke and Emperor! Our bodies will become invincible, just like Bael! And it's all thanks to your generous family, donating their slimy juices to us! What kind citizens!"

Before Kar can reach the Battle Brothers, a female voice suddenly speaks.

"I've heard enough!"

A tiny figure pops into existence next to Zamiel's face. He barely gets a second to react before she rears back and swings her arm, summoning a fist-shaped projection to crash into his face.

_WHUMP!!_

Zamiel goes flying for the first time in an hour. Taken completely by surprise, he spins helplessly through the air and crashes into the dirt, only to immediately jump to his feet a second later.

"Grr!! Nice ambush! Sneaky fairy!"

Blinker disappears, then reappears four inches from Duriel's face. Once again, she rears back her fist, but when she lunges forward, the projection doesn't fly out of her hand. Instead, it appears from the air in front of Duriel's chest.

_Thunk!_

She sends the younger brother flying by tricking him with misdirection. The unexpected frontal assault knocks the wind out of Duriel, making it tough for him to jump up even once he hits the dirt and skids to a stop.

"I won't listen to any more of your filth!" Blinker declares. "Kar! We have to stop playing around. These are the two who killed your species. Let's work together to send them to the Great Beyond!"

Kar hesitates. "Butterfly. This is not your battle. They are mine to kill."

"Wrong," Blinker replies. She teleports to her husband's side, reaches up, and touches his face. "Our friends are dying on the northern, southern, and western fronts. Every minute you drag out this fight, more of them will perish. Are their lives worth the cost of dragging out your revenge? What would Pyra say?"

The reptile shudders. 

"N-no. No... Pyra would not like that I used her death as an excuse to sate my bloodlust. Not if it cost the lives of my friends."

Blinker smiles gently. "You're not invincible, gator-man. You're suffering. Everyone can see it. Don't kill these losers just to fulfill some twisted revenge. Do it to prevent any further deaths."

The Sphinx lowers his head. After a few moments, he raises it, then watches as Duriel and Zamiel fall into step beside one another while gazing at him and Blinker warily.

"I am sorry, Butterfly," Kar murmurs. "I acted selfishly earlier. All my life, I have thirsted for a chance to exact my revenge. I never imagined a time would come when my strength alone was not enough to end their lives. Please assist me."

"You've got it, honey bunches." Blinker says. She kisses his cheek, then activates her fairy magic, turning herself invisible.

With his mind recentered, Kar assumes a dominant stance.

"Hurgh. No more games, Bastard Brothers. I will kill you here and now. After that, I will wipe away your armies and make all who have invaded my world rue the day they crossed me."

The Battle Brothers don't move. They keep their distance and instead smile.

"So you're done screwing around, huh?" Zamiel asks. "What a coincidence. So are we."

From the ground in front of them, amidst the darkness of the night, a ghastly specter emerges. Mephisto, the Duke of Mist, rises from the planet's depths while clasping his bony fingers and cackling gleefully.

"Kekeke! Lassst of the Crocodilesss. I have awaited thisss day for agesss. You have marinated yourssself perfectly. You have sssurpasssed my every expectation. Now, watch asss I bring you to the brink of dessspair!"

Mephisto motions with his hands. The dirt around him shifts and rumbles.

From the underground depths, a ghastly scream cries out in pain.

"No! Please, spare us! Spare the children!"

Kar freezes in place. He watches, horrified, as dozens of figures crawl out of the ground, all of them covered in ghastly wounds, with exposed bones poking out of skin and scales.

"No... no... no..."

The Sphinx takes a step back. Tears well up in his eyes as the figures begin limping and crawling toward him, all of them only able to move thanks to Mephisto's necromantic magic.

"You graverobber..." Kar whispers. "You... you stole them away..."

Kar's long-dead kin, the ancient crocodiles, make their way in his direction. Some of them cry out in pain. Others yell profanity at him, blaming him for their deaths.

The nearest crocodile, a female with faded red scales, gurgles in her throat. "Kar... Kar... is that you? It hurts... my whole body... hurts..."

Kar presses his fists against the sides of his head. "No! Nooo! Not Pyra too! You abhorrent, scum-sucking flesh-pirate! Mephistoooo!! I'M GOING TO **RIP YOU APAAAART**!"
