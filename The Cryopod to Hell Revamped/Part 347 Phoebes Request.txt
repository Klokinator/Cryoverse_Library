Part 347: Phoebe's Request

Jason and his spirit-wife join her physical self and Blinker to walk over to a nearby computer terminal. Despite the data-bead's odd shape and seeming lack of any way to put it into a computer, Phoebe still figures out how to activate it. She takes it from Jason, holds it near the terminal, and a wireless transmission transfers between the nearby interface and the flat screen before them.

Several nearby personnel shoot curious glances in the Wordsmith's direction when the 100-inch screen lights up with the faces of strange, never-before-seen aliens. When they start speaking, an automated filter inside of the file translates their words into English, allowing the humans to follow along. As Jason, his two wives, and Blinker watch, a video recording taken from an intercepted broadcast plays out. The Volgrim Founders identify themselves and speak, one after the other, of the many matters plaguing their society.

Jason learns more about the Plague than he ever did before, including the name the Volgrim have given them, 'Kolvaxians.' He finally obtains a true idea of just how badly the Volgrim have been beaten by the monstrous Plague, and how much ground they've lost. A cold sweat goes down his back when he hears that the Kolvaxians have taken over more than 10% of the Milky Way's inhabitable worlds, or caused others to fall to ruin.

The more he hears, the graver his expression becomes.

"Kolvaxians. Seven Great Wars. Sentinels. God, damn. This is a lot to take in."

Physical-Phoebe chews her lip. "Blinker, did you see this transmission already?"

The Monster Queen nods. "Yeah. Miss Marie showed it to me. It's grim stuff, alright."

"This 'total rendition' term is the same as what Raphael mentioned," Spirit-Phoebe says. "To think the Volgrim would not only have the power to annihilate entire worlds, but the willpower to do so. What a tragedy, and a waste."

"I doubt they'd go to such lengths unless absolutely necessary." Jason says. "These Kolvaxians must be more frightening than any nightmare we can dream up."

"The 'Sentinels' also seem alarming." Physical-Phoebe adds. "They killed 97% of all Volgrim, and they weren't even destroyed. They're just... inactive. Hidden somewhere. They sound even scarier than the Kolvaxians."

"Let's not overlook an important tidbit." Jason says, rewinding to an earlier point in the speech. "This Muuxunuu lady says the Volgrim didn't create the Kolvaxians. They're supposedly invaders from another galaxy. Is she telling the truth?"

"Why would she lie?" Blinker asks. "She's talking to her own people."

"Yes. But sometimes leaders do lie, especially if the purpose is to prevent mass panic. It's entirely possible she and the other Volgrim are making up the Kolvaxian's origins. They may originate from a Volgrim research facility, but the higher-ups don't want the common folk to know. Back when I was a kid, the president of my own country lied about finding 'weapons of mass destruction' in another country to justify invading them. Don't count out the possibility of a well-told lie."

"Raphael found Kolvaxians in the Cosmic Realm." Spirit-Phoebe points out. "Is it possible they originated from there? Or perhaps, they did come from another galaxy and they somehow found their way into the Cosmic Realm? Hell, they might have ended up in there by accident."

"...Or someone put them there _deliberately._" Jason says, lowering his voice. "Maybe the Psions tried to contain the Kolvaxians inside the Cosmic Realm. Or maybe when they condemned a world to Total Rendition, it ended up blasting a hole in the Cosmic Realm big enough for the Plague to slip inside. We don't have anything to go off except Muuxunuu's words. Let's keep watching."

More details emerge as the group of four observe. They learn that a pair of Executors died and became weapons used by the Kolvaxians. They learn of ten ships known by the code-name of Refuge. The fact the Volgrim are considering fleeing the Milky Way leaves them even more concerned than before.

"We absolutely cannot underestimate these Kolvaxians." Jason says. "They're bringing a Type III civilization to the brink of despair. The Volgrim seemed unstoppable before, yet they cannot stop the spread of these invaders."

Some of the subjects the Founders discuss go right over Jason's head, as he isn't familiar with Volgrim society. Unsure of how Psion powers work, the details regarding Specialist Psions mean little to the Wordsmith, though he gathers based on the audience's reaction that this must be quite a surprise to the important delegates in the audience.

"It sounds like becoming a specialist is simultaneously easier and a faster route to achieve power," Jason says, "but it comes at the cost of a diverse arsenal of abilities. Do any of you know how Psion powers work?"

The women all shake their heads in unison.

"No idea." Physical-Phoebe says.

"Not a clue." Her spiritual self confirms.

"In that case, I'll have to grill Raphael and the other Archangels." Jason says. "I can't deal with threats I know nothing about."

"It seems the Volgrim don't have a viable method to quickly travel between galaxies." Spirit-Phoebe says. "They sent probes to the other galaxies in the Local Group, but beyond that, the distance is too vast. They don't have any way to colonize them or assess their threat levels without risking losing the people they send."

"Would it be in bad taste to say these Kolvaxians might be an ironic fate heaped upon the Volgrim by their own hand?" Jason ponders. "Imagine if their own probes caused the Kolvaxians to come here. That would mean they really kicked the wrong can of shit."

"Sounds like the Plague is either super smart, or super-adaptable." Blinker says, thinking out loud. "Plus, they must be strong as heck, if they were able to kill two 'Executors.' I wonder how strong an Executor is..."

When Unarin brings up humanity again, Jason can't help but scowl at the First Founder's digitally projected face.

"A small scale conflict! The war that destroyed our world was barely even a trifle in Unarin's eyes! Get a load of this prick!"

"To us, the Energy Wars were a terrifying matter of life and death. But to the Volgrim, we probably weren't more than a minor distraction requiring a few small-scale forces to subjugate." Physical-Phoebe says. "We just aren't a threat to them."

"Maybe we _weren't_, but we are _now._" Jason says, quietly resolving his heart.

Finally, the recording reaches a point that makes all four viewers lean forward and watch with rapt attention. Unarin arrives, along with Dosena, and the two of them start speaking to the rest of their species the details they've uncovered about Jason.

They talk of of the Wordsmith's appearances in Marie's facility, his battle against their forces in Hell Harbor, and even the Cherubiim's later appearance.

"I've been too sloppy." Jason says with a grimace. "But to put all of these pieces together, Unarin is no idiot. He's the leader of a species that once controlled the entirety of a frigging galaxy. I guess it's to be expected his intelligence-gathering capabilities would be this frightening."

"He seems to be a little scared of you." Physical-Phoebe says. "That's at least somewhat... comforting. I suppose."

"Or distressing." Jason mutters. "Scared dogs tend to bite when they're cornered. Damn. He even knows I caused the Polaris star to go supernova. How the hell does he put all these pieces together?"

"He knows about Hope, too." Blinker says, quietly. "Isn't Hope still injured? I heard Gressil tortured him."

"Did Phoebe tell you that?" Jason asks, glancing at his wife.

"Nope. Miss Marie did." Blinker replies. "She... keeps an eye on you."

"Hmph. I'll bet she does."

The group continues watching. Even crazier facts come to light, making Jason widen his eyes.

"Seriously! He's afraid I might have more clones besides Hope? That's ridiculous! Just one clone is enough to make me tear my hair out!"

"It's a reasonable assumption." Physical-Phoebe says. "You know the limits of your powers, but Unarin doesn't. He can only guess based on what his interrogation of Ose revealed. And it's clear she hid things from him because she still wants to obtain your powers for demonkind, not the Volgrim."

"Still, the fact remains that Unarin is scared of me." Jason says. "And that's good. He should be. I'll make humanity so terrifying he won't dare to mess with us. Once we stomp down the Kolvaxians, there won't be anything he can do to defeat us."

The Wordsmith glances at his wife.

"I'll just have to make sure to protect you even more than I do now. I have no doubt Unarin would try to capture and turn you into a weakness for me. I won't let him touch you, though. No matter what."

Physical-Phoebe smiles. "You're so romantic."

When the recording continues again, Jason nearly has a heart attack.

"What the fuck?! SOLOMON'S CROWN?! How in the goddamn shit-fucking-shit did Unarin get the Crown?? Is that why it randomly disappeared? I thought Solomon sent himself away!"

"Maybe... a Psion... stole it from you?" Spirit-Phoebe asks, shrugging heavily. "I wasn't there, so I can only guess."

"Yeah... perhaps it was a Psion..." Jason mutters, clearly unconvinced. "Either way, I have to get the Crown back, and Excalibur, too. They're too dangerous to remain in enemy hands."

The next subject of War Provisions doesn't interest the Wordsmith and his cohorts much, but it does contextualize to them the value of the 100 provisions Kordonis offered. Dosena also mentions Excalibur, confirming its presence inside some unspecified 'secure vaults,' but this doesn't surprise Jason given what Raphael told him.

Unarin then discusses how to deal with Jason. When he explains his plans, Jason frowns deeply.

"I see. So he _doesn't_ want to fairly parley with me after all. He wants to control me. And this bit about Changelings... he really think he can so easily infiltrate _my_ civilization? I won't let him."

"We'll have to come up with ways to detect Changelings." Spirit-Phoebe says. "But I don't know anything about them, so we'll have to capture a few and experiment on them to find out how they differ from humans."

"Not just humans," Physical-Phoebe says. "Monsters and demons too. It seems Changelings can shift their appearances to all sorts of different forms. I'll bet Samantha would know more about how their transformations work, so we can ask her to tell us about them."

"Good idea."

When Unarin concludes the assembly speech and turns to the audience to ask questions, more tidbits emerge regarding the capabilities of Jason's potential enemies.

"It seems the mightiest Psion, Second Founder Dosena, never leaves Volgarius." Jason muses. "But that also means she'll be their final defense. I wonder how strong she is compared to the Cherubiim, let alone me."

"This 'Order to Chaos' book sounds interesting." Spirit-Phoebe chirps. "Look at how Unarin evaluates our entire society! He even gleans details that go well beyond the surface! I never knew architecture could have such an interesting side to it!"

"I'd like to get my hands on that tome." Physical-Phoebe adds. "It sounds like an interesting read, at the least."

Blinker pokes Phoebe's cheek. "No problem! Miss Marie thought you'd want to read it, so she included a copy of Order to Chaos along with the recording. It's really easy to find, since it's a mainstay and required reading for all Volgrim. It's kind of like... like..."

"Like their Bible?" Jason asks.

"Yeah!" Blinker exclaims. "It's like a holy book! The Volgrim revere their First Founder more than you'd find comfortable."

"Great." Jason says. "I'm sure both Phoebes will love it. I'll pass, though. I've got more important things to worry about."

He pauses.

"...Kind of rude to say I'm lazy, though. I mean, he's not wrong, but damn. Talk about slapping a man right in his ego."

"What's the matter?" Physical-Phoebe asks, poking her husband's ribs teasingly. "Are you mad the big mean Volgrim Founder called you out? You'll just have to work harder to break that image!"

The Wordsmith smiles and rolls his eyes. "Yeah, yeah. I know."

The recording concludes, and the group of four find themselves at a momentary loss for words. The revelations contained within shock them to their cores, making them re-evaluate the situation significantly.

"Solomon's Crown... Excalibur... Changelings... and the Kolvaxians, too." Jason says, trying not to sound too worn-out. "I knew the Volgrim wouldn't be easy to deal with, but this is worse than I expected."

"Well, at least you have advance warning," Blinker says with a shrug. She kicks her feet back and forth while sitting on Phoebe's shoulder, as if splashing them in a small pond of water. "Don't say I never help you out."

"I'd never say that." Jason chuckles. "You've helped me out more than I deserve. It's me who owes you."

Spirit-Phoebe coughs.

"Say, um. Jason? Other-Phoebe? While we're all here, there's something I've been wanting to talk about."

"Yeah?" Jason asks, turning to his soul-wife's projection. "Is something eating at you?"

"Well. Yes, actually." She answers. "It's... it's just... I want a new name."

"You do?" Physical-Phoebe asks. "Why?"

"I think you know why." Spirit-Phoebe says. "It's confusing having there be two of us. And, well, Hope has a different name from Jason, right? I'd like a name of my own. We're similar to each other, yes... but I feel as if I've started to become my own person, recently. I just want a new name, that's all. I want to feel distinct from you."

Jason listens attentively. "That's totally fine. You can change your name, no problem. What would you like to go by?"

Spirit-Phoebe pauses.

"I've... given it some thought. I don't want my name to be as big of a change as Hope's is compared to yours. I just want to be a _little_ different. How about... Daphne? Or Fiona? Or maybe even Penny?"

"Daphne sounds good." Jason says. "It'll take some getting used to, but I like it."

"I like Penny!" Blinker says. "That's a heckin' cute name!"

"Fiona doesn't sound too different from Phoebe." Physical-Phoebe says. "Yet it also sounds distinct."

"Which one are you leaning toward most?" Jason asks.

His mind-wife chuckles nervously. "Well, uh, I was leaning toward Fiona, personally. Is it weird if I just agree with my other self?"

"Not at all." Jason answers. "I like Fiona. That's a great name, and it fits you well!"

"Fiona." Phoebe repeats, looking at her spirit-self. "Yeah. I can get behind that."

"I liked Penny more." Blinker complains. "But hey! Maybe I'll name one of my baby crocs Penny instead!"

Blinker rubs her belly, smiling warmly at the thought of her future children.

"That's a great idea." Fiona says. "Make sure you bring your kids to see us when you finally pop them out. I'll be their aunt! Me and... and Phoebe both!"

"Yeah. Me and Fiona!" Phoebe says, repeating her spirit-self's name to get a better 'taste' for it. "We'll both be their aunts!"

Jason laughs. "Well, at least we've gotten all that resolved. Thanks for bringing the recording by, Blinker. You should probably head back to Marie's laboratory, soon. I have to prep for the demon invasion."

Blinker's smile instantly evaporates. "What?! Another demon invasion? What are you talking about, Word-man?!"

Jason smacks his forehead. "Oh, yeah. I didn't tell you. Uhh... the demons are probably going to attack Tarus II again, but this time, all of the Hidden Emperors will join the attack. So, I'm currently planning our defenses out so they'll fail."

"That's terrible!" Blinker gasps. "Haven't they hurt you enough? I won't forgive them! I HATE demons!!"

The fairy's expression turns vicious. For a brief moment, a flash of absolute bloodthirstiness washes across Blinker's face as countless suppressed emotions rush to the surface.

"Calm down, calm down." Phoebe says, raising a finger to touch Blinker's tiny arm. "Don't get angry. It won't be good for your babies. Jason and I will take care of the demons, this time. We have a new weapon, after all."

"I can't go back to Marie, not now!" Blinker snarls. "Those demon-bastards... they took Kar from me! I need to pay them back tenfold! Especially Mephisto, that absolute little shithead!"

"You and me both." Jason says with a nod. "I'll bet the Battle Brothers are still alive, too. Mephisto probably reanimated their bodies. I'll make sure to crush them for you and deliver their heads on platters."

Blinker crosses her arms. She flies into the air, rising off Phoebe's shoulder to hover between the Wordsmith and his wife.

"No need! I'm joining you! I'll kill the rat bastards myself!"

"Uhh..." Jason says, frowning at the fairy. "You're pregnant. You shouldn't do anything that will risk your children's lives."

"I'm barely pregnant." Blinker retorts. "I'm not at a point where I can't kick some demon butt! Besides. How will I be able to look Kar's babies in their cute little eyes if I let their father's killer roam free? Zamiel's days are numbered, and so are Mephisto's!"

Jason glances at Fiona, then Phoebe. Both of his wives shrug at him.

"I think it's fine." Fiona says. "Blinker, you can stay, but don't go charging into battle. This isn't the same as the Stormbringer Attack. We're not fighting one or two strong enemies, but dozens. I don't even want to imagine what would happen if two or more Emperors ganged up on you."

Blinker's enthusiasm deflates visibly.

"Well... alright. I'll just have to be careful, then. But Word-man, how exactly are you gonna deal with a bunch of Emperors? We barely made it when only Ose was attacking us!"

All three women turn to look at Jason. He shrugs nonchalantly.

"Killing all those Emperors is probably impossible, even for me. If I rely on my new weapon's energy, I might be able to smite them all dead, but that would drain its reserves, possibly until they run dry. I can't afford to waste mana like that, not with the Volgrim breathing down my neck. Even though that's the case, there is another option."

Phoebe raises an eyebrow. "And that would be?"

"Scaring the absolute _shit_ out of the Hidden Emperors." Jason says, with a smile. "Confessor Vulpanix assured the demons I don't possess Excalibur, and that is true. However, based on what I've heard, the demons don't... _trust_ the Volgrim. They certainly don't _like_ them. All it will take is one little seed of doubt and their bravado will come crumbling down..."

Jason cracks his neck. He holds out his hands, face-up.

"**Sword.**"

In the air before him, Jason's Word of Power conjures a perfect-looking replica of Excalibur. The blade falls a few inches and lands atop his palms with a _plop._

Fiona cocks her head. "A fake Excalibur?"

"Sure." Jason says. "The thing about war is it's not always about what you have, but what your opponent _thinks_ you have. Solomon may have slapped me in the face when he left, but he was a good teacher. Sun Tzu once said that all warfare is based on deception, and to that, I agree."

Jason utters additional Words of Power. "**Strengthen. Reinforce. Reinforce. Sharpen. Attune. Crystallize...**"

Over the next several minutes, Jason alters the simple-looking blade in his hands to the point that all the magically sensitive creatures in and around the Core turn in his direction, surprised by the powerful emanation of holy energy coming from the object in his hands.

Jason not only strengthens the blade until it becomes nearly as durable as Living Moldanium, but he makes it ultra-sharp, to the extent it will never dull, no matter how many limbs it severs or however many blunted surfaces it strikes. Just like he once did with Dellfingler, Jason creates a blade capable of cutting nearly anything.

Additionally, Jason attunes the blade so that it emanates Camael's Cube's aura, but with a mana-flavor identical to that of Excalibur!

"It's nowhere near as powerful as Excalibur, nor does it have the heroic blade's powers." Jason says with a smile. "But it doesn't have to, because I'll pair it with a certain Black Hole Construct."

"A what?" Blinker asks.

"I'll let you meet him later." Jason replies. "For now, just trust me. This isn't my only measure, but it will absolutely prove among the most effective. If there's one thing Solomon made clear to me over the years, it's that no Hero has inspired as much terror and as many sleepless nights as the famous Hero-King. The demons have been sleeping a little too easy lately, so I think it's time we reawaken their dormant nightmares. Don't you?"

Blinker nods excitedly. "Heck yes! Scare those demons, Word-man!"

Fiona exhales. "And here I once thought you were a little slow on the uptake, Jason. Have you been holding back, all this time?"

"I have." He replies. "I never wanted to be the guy who stepped up to the plate and took control, but I was just denying reality. Now I know what has to be done. The demons will suffer for killing my friends and family."

He looks at Blinker.

"My little girl, and your husband. We'll make them pay."

The Monster Queen nods.

"Yeah. We will."
