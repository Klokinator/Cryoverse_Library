"Welcome to Maiura, Your Majesty," the soldier on the other side of the portal said with a nod.

Hope blushed. "Ah, sorry, you have me confused with Jason. I'm his clone. He's the king."

The guard blinked twice. "Oh, sorry. I couldn't... err..."

"Tell me apart. Right. I get that a lot."

Hope inhaled deeply, smelling the fragrance of a delightful pink and green planet. "Officer, can you direct me to Commander Neil?"

The guard scratched his chin uneasily. "Sorry, sir. I don't know where he is off the top of my head. If you give me five minutes, I can ask around and find out."

"No need," Hope replied. "I'll find him myself."

The Wordsmith paced down the steps and glanced back at the Core, visible through the portal. _This is the 32nd world we've reclaimed since I- no, since Jason first emerged from the Cryopod. It's beautiful._ Hope thought.

Hope turned away from the Core and jogged down the steps, taking them two and three at a time. Dozens upon dozens of uniformed men, women, and monsters ran around, looks of trepidation on their faces. They were all armed with a mixture of weaponry, and Hope paused, sensing an aura of fear in the camp.

He continued walking for a minute or two and paused to stare at the distant vistas of mountains and trees, along with several large animals reminiscent of dinosaurs. However, no matter where he looked, Hope didn't see Neil.

Hope shrugged. "**Locate.**"

It barely took ten seconds to discover Neil in a tent half a mile away. The army's commander flailed his arms and yelled angrily at a group of men and a demon, but Hope pulled away before he could hear what was going on. _It's not polite to intrude on conversations with Wordsmithing. At least, not when it comes to my friends._ Hope thought.

Not two minutes later, Hope finished jogging through the makeshift camp and paused outside Neil's tent. Neil's outraged voice was muffled somewhat by the tent's fabric. What made Hope pause, though, was a giant cloud of smoke in the distance, which was now much more visible at the edge of the camp where Neil's tent stood.

_A forest fire?_ Hope shook his head. _No, something else. That looks like the direction of this world's capital city._ Hope thought.

He lifted open the tent flap and stepped inside. A few people glanced at Hope, but Neil didn't notice him. The Commander stood with several men and a familiar-looking demon. Neil gesticulated wildly and pointed at the demon. "-fault! You didn't tell us the demons would fight back so fiercely! Why are they guarding this world more than they did the others?"

The demon rubbed his hands together. "Look, I'm sorry, okay? Demons need human souls. If you guys keep liberating worlds, they're gonna get desperate. It's that simple."

Hope's eyes widened. _Ah, I recognize him. Didn't I- err, didn't Jason chat with him a few months back? His name is Paimon. He was part of the assault force that attacked the Core. Jason interrogated and let him go. I didn't even know he returned._ Hope thought.

Neil cursed. "Shit. We lost thirty soldiers. How long did Lorvos torture those innocents before he killed them?"

Paimon shook his head. "A while. At least a month. What are you gonna do with him?"

"I'll have to think of a fitting punishment. Many of the bodies we recovered already melted together thanks to his infernal magic."

Neil paused to take a breath, and one of the men nudged the commander. He turned around and raised an eyebrow at Hope. "I expected you an hour ago. Where the hell have you been?"

"Busy, sorry. There was a cave-in at a mine. I was pulling people out. I didn't realize your message was urgent."

"Tch. It sounds like the messenger boy didn't understand the meaning of an S.O.S! Whatever. You're too late to do anything now."

Hope glanced around at the group of people, as well as Paimon. "What happened? What's going on?"

Several of the lower-ranked officers turned away, unable to meet Hope's eyes. Neil replied, "Demonic brutality, of course. I expected to liberate this world quickly, but it turns out the demons anticipated our movements. They were waiting for us."

"Waiting?"

Neil turned to Paimon. "Tell him."

The young demon, still dressed in a smart suit, yet appearing smaller than the first time Hope remembered meeting him, slicked down his black hair. "Hey, Jason. Long time, no see."

Hope smiled. "Wrong guy. I'm Jason's clone. Call me 'Hope.'"

Paimon blinked. "Hope?"

Neil snapped his fingers. "It's a long story. Let's get on with your report. Small talk can come later."

Paimon nodded. "Right, sorry. Anyway, I had a few comrades who told me this was an outer rim human world. It's supposed to be one the Council didn't care about, but I think my friends lied to me."

"Why?"

"Dunno."

Paimon hesitated.

"Okay, I _do_ know. I don't wanna say it, though."

Neil crossed his arms. "Paimon is a traitor to his people. A good soldier, willing to join the forces of humanity. The other demons discovered that fact and fed him false intel. They wanted to ambush us on this planet."

"P-pretty much," Paimon said. "Sorry. They played me for a fool."

Neil waved his hand. "It's not your fault. I thought the demons might try something sneaky. That's why I set up camp before we traveled to the city."

Hope waved his hands. "Hold on; I don't understand. What happened? The demons attacked us?"

The Commander and the Demon Lord nodded simultaneously. "Aye," Paimon replied. "You're lucky I was there to help. There were fifty-thousand monsters and a bunch of low-level demons, plus a Demon Lord named Lorvos. I managed to knock him out and capture him. We can't return to the city, though."

"Why not?"

Hope's blood chilled. He glanced at the tent doorway and remembered the smoke in the distance. "Don't tell me the monsters are still there!"

"They outnumber us ten to one," Neil replied, clicking his tongue in exasperation. "Even if we had superior weaponry, which we don't, we couldn't beat them. We had to pull back to the safety of the portal, but I imagine they'll hit us here in the next few hours."

Sweat rolled down Hope's back. "Why the laid-back atmosphere in camp? We need to retreat or attack them now. We can't simply sit around and do nothing!"

One of the other men in the room spoke up, though he and his peers had been quiet until now. "Hope, Paimon captured Lorvos. That means we have a bargaining chip. I think the top-level monsters are waiting to hear our demands."

Hope scoffed. "A bargaining chip? Come on! That can't be all you've got. Demons will abandon one Lord if it means tearing apart humans."

"True," Neil said. "But we have two other sources of protection. The demons are hesitant to attack us directly as of late, likely because Jason destroyed Polaris. Not only that, but as that succubus stated, supposedly we're under her protection."

Hope mentally recalled the succubus's name. "Samantha, you mean? Belial? But she isn't here now."

"They don't have to know that."

"Right."

Neil punched his palm. "It took you an hour to arrive, but now that you're here, I say it's time for a counter-attack. We've been thinking of some ways we can pay the bastards back. Since you're the Wordsmith, maybe you have a few ideas?"

Hope paused to consider his options.

_I haven't used my powers much since Jason created me. The lack of Solomon's Crown is my biggest hindrance compared to him, since the crown allows him to visualize complex machinery and the like. However, in all other respects, my abilities and mana capacity are equal to his._ Hope thought.

Hope nodded. "I have a few ideas. I can teleport, put people to sleep, read minds, release holy energy, use telekinesis..."

"Excellent. You're an intelligent fighter, Hope. I believe in you." Neil rubbed his hands together. "Would you be able to go in and thin the herd a bit? If you can kill half of the demons in the city, that will make things a lot easier for us."

Hope blanched. "W-what? Kill them? Are you sure that's necessary? I could knock them out, put them to sleep, change their allegiances, and all sorts of other-"

Neil scowled. "They're enemy combatants, Hope. If you put them to sleep, they'll wake up. Do you expect us to capture every enemy we meet? We haven't the prison capacity for such an operation."

Paimon squirmed in embarrassment. "Neil, Jason converted me to humanity's side with words. Maybe Hope could do the same for these monsters."

Irritation appeared on Neil's face. "Do you expect Hope to sit down and have a twenty-minute chat with fifty thousand monsters one at a time?"

"...I suppose not."

Neil waved his hand. "Fantastic. I'm interested in recruiting the strong ones, but the cannon fodder will do little more than devour our food reserves and tax our system. Right now, what we need are humans. They'll ally with us, as the others have before, and won't be a security threat once inside our borders."

Paimon looked away, unable to meet Neil's gaze. The atmosphere in the room turned tense. Several men glanced at the Demon Lord, unsure how he'd react. Neil ignored Paimon and focused on Hope.

"You need to thin the monsters out and save as many humans as possible. Can you do that for me?"

Hope shook his head. "I won't kill, Neil. I can't. It's not right."

The room went silent. The bystanders held their breath. The nearest spectators winced as their emotions washed across their faces.

_Hope told the Commander 'no.' Yikes._ someone in the room thought.

Neil glanced around. "Would everyone give Hope and me a few minutes of privacy?"

Wordlessly, everyone nodded and shuffled out of the room, including Paimon. The demon sent a look of concern toward Hope before he left, but Hope held his ground and showed no anxiety.

After everyone disappeared, Neil rubbed his face. "Hope. I understand. I know why you think the way you do. You don't want to kill because killing is wrong. Am I correct?"

"Yes. I won't budge on this, Neil."

Neil licked his lips. "Is that _you_ speaking, or Jason?"

"What do you mean? I can be my own person while also sharing a value system with Jason."

The commander folded his hands behind his back and strolled to the center of the lightly furnished tent. Only a few chairs and a table sat on the side opposite the entrance. "Hope. Don't you think that perhaps Jason does things the wrong way, on occasion?"

"I don't follow."

"A lot of people died because Jason was unwilling to kill. He was unwilling to kill Amelia to save humanity, but he was also unwilling to kill demons to protect her as well. When your values contradict themselves by their internal logic, don't you think that's a problem?"

Hope shifted on his feet. "Okay. I... I see your point, somewhat. However, I don't know if I can-"

"You _can,_" Neil said, interrupting him. "We're in the middle of a war, Hope. Jason might run around flitting about with women, but you're better than him. Do you want the demons to tear apart the Core? Do you want us to appear weak?"

"No."

"Well, if you make them take a nap, that's what they'll think. Demons respect strength. You have to set aside your emotions and put them in their place."

"What about Paimon?" Hope countered. "I talked to him and convinced him to join our side."

Neil's lip twitched. "Can you convert trillions of demons to our side? Can you ensure the safety of humanity by talking to our enemies and making them see the error of their ways?"

The Commander took a few steps forward and lowered his voice. "You don't think I _trust_ Paimon, do you? Have you any idea how many humans he killed before he joined our cause?"

"I don't," Hope said, swallowing hard. "But Paimon was indoctrinated into demonic beliefs and values from birth. He didn't know better."

"You're naive," Neil countered. "There are two types of men in this world: Those who have taken another man's life, and those who haven't. Once you kill someone, no matter who they are, something _changes_ inside you. That's the way humans are. That's the sin we share with demons. Paimon can convert to our side, but how many human souls did he devour to become a Lord?"

Hope opened his mouth, but Neil cut him off. "At least a thousand. That's how many human souls it takes to mutate into a Lord. How many men do you know who've killed a thousand people, hmm?"

"None," Hope replied, reluctantly.

"Then you understand," Neil said. "Go to the capital, Meldora. It's not far from here. Kill as many enemies as possible with your Wordsmithing. When you finish-"

"No."

Hope stared at the Commander silently. "There's another way. I can wipe their minds. I can make them loyal to us with Wordsmithing."

Neil stared at the Wordsmith for several seconds. "How?"

"I can influence and change their minds with Wordsmithing. Well, I think I can. It's worth a try."

"And if that fails?"

Hope bit his lip. "I guess I'll have to try your plan, then."

The two didn't say anything for a long minute. Neil broke the silence. "Fine. Far be it for a simple man like me to question the _mighty_ Wordsmith. But before you go, why not test your theory on Lorvos? If you can convert him, perhaps you will succeed at turning the rest."

Hope nodded. "Good idea."

They turned to walk out of the tent, but Neil hesitated. "If you succeed, then your power will be even more frightening. The ability to take free will, a gift given by God, should not rest in the hands of any man."

"I don't disagree, but if the alternative is killing, wouldn't you agree changing someone's free will is the morally superior option?"

Neil touched his lips. "Is it? I wonder."

Neil and Hope eventually arrived at a tent on the other side of the camp. Paimon and the others stayed behind, given the Commander had told them it was a private matter. Two guards outside the tent pulled its flap open, and Hope stepped inside, followed by Neil.

"Here he is," Neil said, gesturing to a bound demon. The big brute, only somewhat smaller than Bael, thrashed around on the floor, grunting through a gag over his mouth. His hands were bound firmly behind his back, and his legs were shackled tightly together. Black hair and a torn-apart set of light, leather armor were all that covered him.

"Wow. Lorvos is massive. How the hell did Paimon beat him?"

"Paimon is a proficient fighter, especially for a Lord. He has command over water and ice. Lorvos is a physical fighter, so Paimon jammed ice all throughout his body. Nearly killed him. The big guy didn't stand a fighting chance. When their boss went down, the enemy monsters didn't dare to stand up to Paimon."

Hope knelt and pulled the gag out of the demon's mouth. The lunkhead scowled at him. "Filthy fleshbag! Take these bloody cuffs off, and I'll tear you apart! I'll shove a thousand maggots up yer arse!"

Hope cocked his head. "You hate humans a lot, don't you?"

"No shit, stupid! Lean a little closer, and I'll bite yer fuckin' ear off!"

Neil curled up his lip. "Quite the foul-mouth, isn't he? Go ahead. Do it."

Hope hesitated. "Lorvos, can't you sit still for a minute? I want to talk."

"Eat my ass! I ain't talkin' with no skarn-droppin' fleshbag!"

"I could kill you right here," Hope warned him. "Do you still want to be feisty?"

Lorvos grinned. "Hehehe, tryin'a scare me, eh? I see the weakness in your eyes, you little shit. You ain't got the heart to off me."

Lorvos glanced at Neil. "Not like that one over _there._ He's got a hard edge to 'im. Even if you did kill me, I'd consider it an honor! I died after killin' hundreds of your kind!"

Hope winced. "You did?"

"Course I did. Meldora was ripe for the takin'. Raped a bunch of sows, gutted a few crawlers, and they begged for mercy the whole time. That's what you want me to do, innit? Beg?! As if!"

Hope swallowed. "I wanted to talk. Maybe that wasn't the right thing to do. You aren't like Paimon at all. He was reasonable."

Lorvos's smile vanished. "Paimon. Filthy devil-damned traitor. Turned his back on demons to grovel at the feet of fleshbags, he did. Don't think the rest of us are like 'im. There's always a few weaklings in the crowd. I call 'em arena-droppings, since we feed 'em to the hellhounds. You can spew honeyed words at one or two of us, but the rest'll gut'ya in your sleep."

A moment passed. Neil pursed his lips. "Heard enough, yet?"

Hope stood up. "I guess."

"Why are you waiting? Use your Wordsmithing."

Lorvos and Hope stared at each other silently. The former grinned evilly, while the latter stared blankly, trying to parse what he'd heard.

"You heard the big boss! Smith me with your words... whatever the hell that means."

"Maybe I should kill him," Hope said. "Maybe you're right, after all, Neil. Do I want someone like this on my side?"

"I don't," Neil said. "But you're the Wordsmith. You make the call."

Hope sucked air through his teeth. "I came here to test my Wordsmithing, and that's what I'll do."

He closed his eyes and inhaled. "**Convert.**"

Energy traveled from his body into Lorvos.

The Demon Lord's evil smile slipped away. He blinked. "What was that? What'd you do to me? I felt somethin'... somethin' tickle my head."

His eye twitched. Hope watched intently. "Who do you serve?"

The big demon raised an eyebrow. "Uh, you, of course, dumbass. Why'd you put me in these cuffs, anyway? You testing me or somethin'?"

"You don't remember?"

Lorvos shifted his wrists around and moved his eyes from right to left. He scrunched his forehead together. "Hmm... ahhh... I feel like there was somethin'. I fought another Lord. Lost. Bastard iced me up. Can't remember why, though."

Neil exhaled. "It worked."

Hope forced a smile. "Yeah. Lorvos is trying to fit past events into his new persona. He's justifying his past actions in a new light."

Lorvos continued to stare at the humans. "What're you babblin' about? Are you gonna leave me all tied up like this forever? My wrists are killin' me. What's a fella gotta do to get some fresh air around here?"

"Should I release him?" Hope asked.

"You tell me," Neil replied. "Think he's safe?"

Hope reached out with telekinesis and shattered the bindings on Lorvos's arm, and flinched, waiting for the demon to attack him.

The attack didn't come.

Lorvos rubbed his wrists. "Cripes, my skin's chafed as hell. Next time when you're messin' around, don't make the bindings so tight. And what about my ankles, eh?"

Hope shrugged. He tore the bindings off. "How many humans have you killed?"

Lorvos hopped to his feet and stretched. "Humans? Oh, a few thousand. That was a long time ago, though. They were our enemies. Why d'ya ask?"

"You're not going to hurt humans anymore, are you?"

"Of course not, dumbass." Lorvos rolled his eyes. "Humans are friends, not food. New creed. That's how it goes."

"Sure. Whatever you say, Lorvos." Hope stared at the big demon for several seconds, then shook his head. "Can't believe it worked. Wordsmithing is something else."

"Huh? Whaddya mean?"

"Nothing."

Hope turned to Neil. "Meldora, then. I'll go alone. Let's hope I can convert fifty thousand monsters to my side by the end of today."

Neil nodded. "Yes, let's. Still, don't let your guard down. Anything could happen. And I hope for your sake you're ready for whatever you see in that city. I've... heard rumors. Paimon told me some unsettling things."

"I can handle it," Hope retorted. "I'll be back before you know it."

"Godspeed."
