"A valiant effort, Satan," Hoarhiim whispered. "But ultimately, your struggle will prove fruitless."

A five-hundred-foot wall of water tore across the landscape at over three hundred miles an hour. Hoarhiim glanced down at his brother, Blaarjiim, whose limbs were bound together. Blaarjiim rolled around in the crater below, struggling to break free. 

"This will not kill you, Blaarjiim... but it will hurt."

Blaarjiim's eyes widened. "Don't leave me here! Hoarhiim! Even for you, this is-"

"Think of it as retribution for the lives you've ended. A little pain won't be the end of you."

"Hoarhiim? HOARHIIM!!"

The Deity of Light ignored his brother's screams of anguish and continued levitating into the sky as the seawall approached. A flash of movement caught Hoarhiim's eye. An armored figure appeared, yanked Beelzebub out of the hole Belial pummeled him into and vanished, stepping through a portal.

_Oh? It seems Barbatos understood after all._ Hoarhiim thought.

Below Hoarhiim, the seawater ripped up the land, flooded into the crater, and crushed Blaarjiim and Amelia with unrelenting force. Blaarjiim's screams cut off halfway as tens of millions of gallons of seawater pulverized him without restraint.

...

Elsewhere, Joan writhed in agony on the ground as her leg healed, but it regenerated far too slowly for her to save herself from the wall of water. She tucked her body into the fetal position, making herself as small as possible. Catherine stabbed into the ground before her, erecting a powerful barrier of Holy Magic around the Hero.

Diablo inhaled mightily and unleashed a blast of fire at the oncoming seawall, hoping to slow it down or make it split around him, but the oncoming sea swallowed his flames without remorse. At the last second, he jammed every tentacle into the ground, intending to root himself like an anchor. Instead, the millions of tons of water tore him away when the sea impacted, yanking him off his feet and pulling him across the plains. The Archdemon tumbled and swirled inside the vortex, spinning his body around several times per second as he was dragged and beaten across the land. Eventually, a massive mountain appeared before the Emperor, and he slammed into it with enough force to generate a localized earthquake.

Diablo's four main limbs dug into the mountainside as he struggled to hold on for dear life. The seawater pounded him for several minutes, yanking his immense body left and right with the force of a hundred hurricanes.

Eventually, the height of the water began to dip. Time passed, and it lowered from five-hundred-feet to four hundred, three hundred, and so on.

Half an hour later, the Third Emperor sagged to the mud, his strength sapped dry. The tsunami reduced to the depth of a puddle compared to the colossal Archdemon, but Diablo lost his ability to move.

Hoarhiim, flying high in the sky, frowned. _Perhaps I was wrong. The Archdemon has nearly been beaten already. I might not have to stop him myself._ Hoarhiim thought.

The Deity of Light glanced at the crater below, now filled with water. His brother was nowhere to be seen.

"**Locate.**"

Hoarhiim's mind traveled across the battlefield, toward the direction the water surged. Twenty miles away, sprawled against a rock, Amelia's body lay in a ditch, Blaarjiim's orb gone from her grasp.

"**Teleport.**"

Hoarhiim transmitted the thirty miles in an instant, arriving at Amelia's bound, soaking wet body. Her dress was waterlogged beyond belief, and she gasped for breath, occasionally coughing up fluid as she sputtered and wheezed.

"Ja... Jas...on."

Hoarhiim shook his head. "I am not Jason. Not right now. Where is Blaarjiim?"

Amelia lay still, her wrists still bound behind her back by Hoarhiim's unbreakable shackles.

"G-gone. Kah! Where am... I?"

Hoarhiim aimed his palm at her. "**Release.**" 

The shackles vanished, and Hoarhiim took a knee. "Were you working with my brother?"

Amelia's body trembled. She shivered from the cold. "S-s-suh- sorry... I d-didn't want t-to wake up... but he entered my mind. It was awful."

"I did something similar with Jason. I assumed you and Blaarjiim were at odds with each other."

Slowly, Amelia pressed her back against the rock. She mopped her hair out of her face. "What happened? I don't... I don't know how I got here. Why am I s-soaking wet?"

"**Dry. Warm.**" Hoarhiim aimed two words of power at the young woman, and immediately her condition improved. "It seems my brother intended to take over your mind and force you to kill Jason. His desire for power is insatiable."

Amelia pulled herself up and dusted her now-dry clothes off. "I... I see. Um. Hoarhiim?"

"Yes?"

Amelia fiddled with her fingers for a moment. 

"Is, err, Jason in there? Is he listening?"

"No. The Hero is dormant, and will stay so until I've finished."

"Okay." Amelia splashed her feet in the muddy ground absentmindedly. "Look... I've done some bad things."

"To say the least," Hoarhiim replied blandly.

"I killed many people. So many that I couldn't count them if I wanted to."

"Blaarjiim's influence, no doubt."

She nodded. "Yes, but I was complicit. He didn't force me to become a mass-murderer."

Surprisingly, Hoarhiim smiled. "Agreed. You did that much of your own volition."

Amelia turned away to gaze at the massive Archdemon slowly standing up in the distance. "Jason said I could be forgiven. He said I c-could redeem myself... in spite of what I've-"

"Jason is wrong," Hoarhiim said, cutting her off. "You cannot be redeemed. Once evil, always evil."

Amelia blinked in surprise. "Huh? Wait, I thought you were..."

"Going to forgive you? No. I cannot offer repentance for your life of evil, and neither can Jason. No act of goodness can redeem you for murdering tens of millions. No matter how many you save, no matter how many you heal, no matter how often you pray to gods that no longer exist... you are utterly irredeemable."

Amelia leaned back to gaze up at the man towering over her. "H...Hoarhiim? What are you saying? After drying me off, helping me up, and freeing me from those shackles, I thought you'd-"

"You thought wrong. I only freed you because you don't pose a threat to me." Hoarhiim turned away from Amelia and the Archdemon and stared into the distance, his piercing gaze fixating on his brother's orb, over a mile away. Even in the shadow of the rising sun, the light-consuming void of Blaarjiim's orb was unmistakable. "I've made my decision. No apology you make will sway my soul. Go to my brother and-"

Suddenly, from the direction of the Archdemon, a horrible monstrous scream erupted in the din. Something alien and decidedly _non-demon_ howled in rage. Amelia and Hoarhiim both ceased their conversation for a moment and gasped.

A gigantic dragon, made entirely of bone and decomposed skin, burst from the ground and leaped at Diablo. The winged beast, nearly as big as the Third Emperor, ripped and tore at Diablo viciously. Atop the dragon rode Mephisto, Duke of Mist.

"Kss! Behold! My mossst prized posssesssion! The corpssse of the lassst dragon! The ultimate weapon to ssslay my enemiesss!"

The undead dragon unleashed a torrent of acrid green balefire upon the Archdemon, draconic breath which melted flesh and bone alike. Diablo roared in rage and blocked the gout of flame with one of his arms. The limb melted into a puddle before him, proving useless against the dragon's breath. Diablo howled angrily and jumped at the dragon.

Diablo grappled the undead behemoth with his three remaining arms, while simultaneously lashing his tentacles out and biting at the monster, only to yank back and hiss in pain as mouthfuls of acid dissolved their teeth away. The tentacles wilted and melted apart, flopping to the ground uselessly.

Amelia's eyes widened. "No! I... I can't believe it!"

Hoarhiim, too, stared in shock. "The last dragon?! But... that means..."

Mephisto cackled gleefully. "Keh keh keh! A torrent of sssoulsss, leap forth from the heart of thisss world!"

Immediately, hundreds of ghouls burst from underground, climbing and covering the Archdemon from head to toe, biting and clawing at him. Diablo released one arm to try and tear them off, but doing so only freed the dragon's head.

"**GRRRROOOOAAARR!**" 

The undead colossus reared its head back and unloaded a torrent of acid-breath right into Diablo's chest. Diablo wheezed and gasped, trying to block the breath, but as his chest melted away, his grip weakened. The ghouls banded together and pulled him to the ground.

For one moment, Amelia sat beside Hoarhiim. In the next, she vanished. The young woman exploded in rage and charged toward the battle of the goliaths.

"Leviathan..." Hoarhiim muttered. "You... you were defiled... by the grave-robber. Such sin. Such misery."

Atop the mountains, Satan and Belial watched in stunned silence as Mephisto's ultimate trump card tore Diablo to shreds.

"Mephisto... he..." Satan grappled for words as the Archdemon began to revert to his base form, all of his energy exhausted. "When did Mephisto get hold of the last dragon?"

Belial shook her head slowly. "I never knew. I don't think anyone did."

Satan's hand clenched into a fist. "If he wields Leviathan's power, then Mephisto's might exceeds that of any mere Duke. He may even rank higher than an Emperor. Higher than me!"

"Leviathan appears soulless," Belial pointed out. "He's undead. Perhaps his powers aren't as great as they once were."

Satan calmed down noticeably as he spotted Amelia racing toward Mephisto. "Hm. Yes. Perhaps. _She's_ quite angry, though."

"Can you blame her?" Belial murmured. "I doubt she'll be able to rationalize away her pain."

...

In the fields below, Joan's leg finally finished regenerating. She patted Catherine and offered a prayer to the gods. "I thank thee for saving my life... sister."

The Hero jumped to her feet, shifting her weight uncomfortably as she adjusted to the regrown limb. Immediately, her eyes locked onto Amelia. "Unbelievable. A mighty demon, a tainted dragon, and the Black Witch. I shan't watch idly as three vile enemies of God battle amongst one another."

It did not escape the Hero's notice that the Archdemon was beginning to revert. Indeed, as Joan started energizing her weapon, she checked him off in her mind, turning her attention instead to the Black Witch. "The Dragon will have to wait."

Satan leaped off the mountain, racing down it at faster than free-fall speeds as his Eight vectors simultaneously carried Belial and yanked into the cliffs, pulling him with incredible force.

He came to a screeching halt and lowered Belial to the ground.

Suddenly, Barbatos appeared in front of them and stepped out from a Labyrinth portal. The Duke of Steel stood imposingly over the former First Emperor. "Satan. We leave. No more fighting."

Satan paused and frowned. "What? Are you giving me an order?"

"Enough death. Enough sorrow. Agares is dead. Beelzebub unconscious. Diablo defeated. The battle is over."

Satan clenched his teeth and looked past Barbatos as Amelia leaped atop the dragon and began swinging wildly at Mephisto. "Now, you listen here-"

_WHAM_

Barbatos moved instantly, so fast Satan could not react. A punch with the force of a tactical nuke pounded the First Emperor back against the mountainside, blowing out several metric tons of rock and stone in all directions. Satan instantly died. His broken corpse rested lifelessly against the cliff's face, sending horror coursing through Belial's veins.

The Duke of Steel pointed at Belial. "Go. Portal. Take Satan."

Belial stared at him for several seconds with a dumbstruck look etched into every corner of her face. She nodded. "Uh... uh huh..."

Barbatos turned away from her without another word and plodded across the field to Diablo's shrunken body. The Third Emperor lay comatose in the mud. Mephisto's ghouls left to focus their attention on the new threat.

Amelia rode the bucking dragon, swinging and punching at Mephisto and screaming incoherently. "I'LL KILL YOU! I'LL FUCKING TEAR YOU APART! HOW DARE YOU!!"

Her attacks passed through his ghostly body over and over, and the cloaked demon cackled. "Keh keh keh! I merely reconssstituted a dead corpssse into sssomething ussseful! You ssshould be happy to sssee your friend one lassst time!"

Tears poured down Amelia's face as hundreds of ghouls began climbing the dragon to jump and bite at her. Unlike before, however, her wrath boiled like lava poured into a pot of water, igniting her latent powers. Thousands of shadow-javelins rushed from her body in all directions as she continued to swing at Mephisto. Each partisan impaled one ghoul after another. Her mind focused on nothing but rage as she swung and kicked, yet still, the Duke of Mist stood and laughed maniacally.

Suddenly, a holy spear flew across the field, impaling Amelia's chest. The Black Witch stopped mid-swing and gasped. A chain of light yanked her off the dragon with animalistic force.

Amelia sailed hundreds of feet away and slammed into the mucked-up ground with terrific power, blasting muddy dirt in every direction. She barely caught sight of a massive armored man as the man yanked on the chain again, hurling her body into the air before slamming her into the ground repeatedly.

Mephisto cackled with glee. "Kekekeke! What deliciousss irony that the Black Witch'sss sssummoned warrior hasss turned againssst her!"

Amelia panted and tried to pull the spear of light from her chest. "Ar-Arthur! Stop! I order you to stop!"

The Hero stomped toward Amelia, grabbed her by the throat, and lifted her into the air. "I am not Arthur." 

The voice, Amelia realized with a start, was all too familiar.

"J-Joan? No! Nooo!!!"

She tried to kick at the Hero, but Joan slammed Amelia into the ground and summoned spear after spear, rapidly impaling and stabbing the screaming Black Witch with righteous fury. "Enemy of God! Thy time to live is over! I will send thee to the realm of death wherefore thou belong!"

Amelia screamed in terror. "Please!! Nooo!" 

...

Across the plains, Hoarhiim looked away. Sorrow etched into his face. "Such pain. Such ruin. I will transform this planet into a resting place for the damned. I'm sorry, Jason. Amelia, no matter how you care for her, brought this fate upon herself. Perhaps... someday... you will forgive me."

Hoarhiim closed his eyes. 

The sun appeared over the horizon. Its rays of light warmed the land, invigorated the soggy soil, and awakened the planet's creatures.

"That which gives this planet life shall become its reckoning," Hoarhiim muttered softly.

He aimed his mind toward the distant sun, tens of millions of miles away. His mind traveled across the blackness of space toward the lustrous star which gave the world energy and life.

"I curse this planet... all those who live on it... as well as myself... with death."

"**SUPERNOVA!**"
