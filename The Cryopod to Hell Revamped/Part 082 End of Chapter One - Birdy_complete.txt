_Click. Clack. Click. Clack._

Ose's high heels slapped against the floor as she strode into the deepest, darkest region of the Labyrinth. While she walked, monsters and demons ogled her with looks of lust and curiosity. _I wonder what the newest Emperor is doing down here,_ their eyes seemed to say.

Ose ignored the covetous gazes of grunts. The sounds of prisoners screaming in pain as demons tortured, raped, and devoured them filled the air, making her quicken her pace.

_I can't wait to leave,_ Ose thought to herself. One man's cry for pity suddenly cut off, making her pause in front of a cell before continuing onward. It wasn't fear or terror that tugged at Ose's mind, but disgust. 

Faith's End, the Labyrinth's sole prison complex, was a mass of tangled rooms and corridors, extending for miles in any given direction. Rats scurried about here and there, while every so often, a goblin would race across the ceiling and duck into a hole. Their kind lived in the bedrock of the Labyrinth.

A pungent odor struck Ose's nose. She didn't react visibly, since that would be undignified, but her throat caught nonetheless. _Rotting flesh. You'd think these worthless goblins would at least clean up the messes in the cells after a demon has his fun._ Ose thought.

Ose's heartbeat slowly increased in tempo as she continued rounding corners. Her destination drew nearer. _No rats. No goblins. No signs of life. This way is the correct path. Unfortunately._ Ose thought.

The sounds of screams faded away behind Ose. The further she walked down this particular corridor, the quieter the Labyrinth became. Where lit torches once lined the walls, maintained by goblins, darkness loomed instead. She entered the Forbidden Area, the place where the master of Faith's End lurked.

Silence swallowed the world around her. Soon, the clicking of her high heels pounded like drums, echoing against the walls while making her hands twitch.

She paused for a moment to collect herself. _There's no reason to be afraid. You're an Emperor now. Calm down. Breathe. Inhale._ Ose thought.

_I'm not the scared little girl I once was._

_Those days were a long, long time ago._

Ose began walking again. This time, it was her pounding heart, not her heels, that boomed in her ears.

A man shrieked in agony up ahead, piercing the silence. Ose flinched instinctively. His cry of pain came from a door further up the corridor. The door was one of many lining the walls, nondescript in appearance, and seemingly unimportant. However, Ose spotted a faint stream of light emanating from a crack at the door's bottom, revealing its _occupied_ status.

Slowly, tentatively, Ose took one step, then another, carefully pressing her heels down so that she didn't make a noise.

The man bellowed again, his voice hoarse. "Oh, god! Please! NO! NOT THAT! ANYTHING BUT- GAAAAAHH!!!"

Ose winced. Even outside the door, the sound of burning, blistering flesh reached her ears. The sizzling noise made her stomach roil.

Ose placed her hand on the door handle, inhaled, and yanked it open.

"Gressil!"

She spoke a single word while intending to add more, but after saying the warden's name, she lost her nerve.

Inside the room, a fireplace stood lit, causing smoke to fill the air. Given the Labyrinth's lack of chimneys, the gag-inducing black smoke washed across her face, tearing the breath from her chest.

Two round pillars stood in the center of the room, each one as wide as a tree. They rose from the ceiling to the floor, nearly fifteen feet tall in total. A human male and female, each in their 30's, stood chained with their backs to each pillar: the man on the left, and the woman on the right. With their naked bodies covered in blood, cuts, and bruises, the pitiful nature of their torture sent nausea crawling up Ose's stomach.

The man and woman hung limply, their broken arms twisted behind themselves and suspended with chains and shackles to the pillars. The man wheezed and gasped, a fresh burn still festering on his body, while the woman rested motionless against her pillar, dead.

Ose's eyes didn't linger on the humans, or the table of torture tools standing between the pillars and the door, but instead, traveled toward the prison warden. 

The master of the dungeon.

The cruelest, vilest, most despicable demon in the Labyrinth.

_Gressil._

The 9-foot tall demon, a two-headed monstrosity with a birdlike-beak on his right head, and a horrifying melted face on his left, turned to face the Emperor.

"Ose. How rude of you not to knock."

Glowing red eyes dotted Gressil's four arms, his chest, and legs. Each glowing eyeball swiveled and spun in seemingly random directions. Underneath Gressil's flesh, lumps formed and disappeared, as if something were trying to escape the warden's body. His left head slumped forward, its eyes closed. The eye on its forehead stared ahead blankly and didn't glow like the others scattered around Gressil's body. The entire head appeared to be sleeping, but Ose knew better. 

The Emperor of Infiltration swallowed slowly and smoothed her uniform. "I... apologize. I didn't mean to... to interrupt your _playtime._"

Gressil cocked his long, pterodactyl-like head. "Aren't we formal, today. Have you come to join in on the torture?"

The warden's monotone voice never failed to send a spasm of nerves racing down Ose's spine. "N-no. I know you've been busy... _brother_. You probably haven't heard the news."

Gressil raised his clawed hand to scratch his beak. "You'd be shocked how few people want to talk to me these days."

Ose somehow managed a weak smile. "They're missing out."

A moment passed. Gressil's eyes lowered to the table between him and Ose. He slowly brushed a claw across the assortment of bloodied knives and forks sitting atop it as he considered which toy to use next. "Don't keep me in suspense. It's not often I get a visit from my little sister."

"I'd never dream of making you wait."

A trickle of sweat dripped down Ose's face.

"I-I'm an Emperor now."

Gressil didn't meet her gaze. Instead, he plucked a knife from his table, then reached over and caressed the man's chin. "Is that all?"

"I came to order you- that is, to ask for something. I need a favor."

The human trembled as Gressil's claw dug into the underside of his chin. "No... please... not the knife again... mercy-"

"Quiet. I'm talking to my sister. Don't be _rude,_ or I won't let you die when I'm done."

Gressil's words brought the human's complaints to an instant halt. His desire to pass away overrode all the logic centers of his brain.

Gressil continued to play with the man's chin. "A favor. How strange. Now, what, pray tell, could an almighty Emperor of the demons need from little old me? I'm nothing more than a humble flesh-melder."

"I wouldn't normally, Gressil. I hate to impose. You don't have to. In fact, never mind. This- this was a mistake." 

Ose's nerves got the better of her. She started to step out of the doorway, but Gressil's voice stopped her dead in her tracks.

"Why are you leaving?"

Ose stopped, one foot primed and ready to drag her out the door. Every fiber of her being screamed at her to leave, but her body wouldn't move.

"I... I... I'm not- the demons- there was an accident. Barbatos. Uriel. He fell asleep. There-"

Ose babbled incoherently. Her mind began to race as a sickening, primal fear seeped into her mind. Nothing scared her more than being in the presence of her disgusting, torturous, barbaric older brother.

Gressil held up a claw. "Oh. I thought you were going to pester me about something important."

Ose gathered her courage and turned to face Gressil. "It _is_ important. I defeated Uriel... but the Archangel killed half of the Lords, and most of the Barons. Our ranks have been decimated. And on top of that... we still have the humans to deal with."

Gressil's casual demeanor fizzled away, and his expression turned icy. He pulled away from his prisoner and rose to his full height. "Oh? Humans, you say?"

"You _know_ what's going on," Ose growled. "It's _your_ fault we're in this predicament."

Gressil feigned ignorance. "Is it? My memory is quite poor these days."

Ose took a step toward her brother. "You were supposed to guard the last cryogenic container. How did a human manage to slip past you? That shouldn't be possible!"

Gressil smiled. "Heh... heh heh... amusing. Is that what you think? A little rat skittered by when I turned my back?"

"What?" Ose's breath caught. 

The Emperor of Infiltration stared at her brother for a moment. A sinking realization appeared in the back of her mind.

"You... you let the human go? Even for you... that's..."

Gressil laughed.

"Haha. Come, now, Ose. Ask yourself: why would I let an insignificant fleshbag slip past me? Isn't the answer obvious?"

The prison warden slowly waggles a finger at Ose.

"...Because I thought it would be fun."

Ose stared, dumbfounded. "Fun? You let a Hero roam around for _fun_?! He's destroying everything!"

Gressil smiled. "I know. That's the point, sister. All your silly machinations, your little tricks and schemes... they always did annoy me."

Ose lowered her eyes. Rage boiled in her mind, but she forced herself to suppress it. Even as a freshly minted Emperor, she hadn't the power to punish her brother for the wrench he tossed into her plans.

"_You_ annoy _me_, Gressil."

Gressil laughed. "Haha. Is that all you have to say? I find your courage lacking. Stop wasting my time. If you've no more business here, then leave."

"Fine. Far be it for me to keep you from your _hobby_," Ose said, rolling her eyes. "With the demons decimated, we need new forces. The humans have already begun making advanced technology, and I believe they will soon improve even faster. I would like for you to make me a new demon warrior. Something that can stand up to technologically advanced humans."

Gressil smiled. "Haha. No."

"What? No?! What do you mean?"

He turned to the human male and bent down to wipe the knife on the man's shoulder. "Your request doesn't interest me. I despise your orderliness and the way you treat humans like livestock. They deserve torture and death. Nothing more, nothing less."

Ose calmed her nerves. "I agree. I always have. However, I have to be practical. Demons need humans for soul energy. If we kill them all off-"

Gressil suddenly swung his hand around. He backhanded the deceased human woman with enough force to rip her head off and send it flying. It smashed against the wall with a tremendous _bang_ and exploded into a mass of bone and gore.

The prison warden clenched his fist and breathed through his nostrils like a mad dog. "There is no _if_. Demons do not require soul energy to live. Your need for control disgusts me. You have to put _everything_ into a box, don't you, little sister?" 

Gressil rose to his full height again, but this time a menacing look appeared in his eyes. "You think you're better than the other demons. Better than _me_. Isn't that right?"

Ose slid back half an inch. "N-no. I didn't say that..."

"You don't have to." Gressil began walking around the table. He approached his sister, and any trace of strength left her body. The massive monstrosity towered over her. His shadow stretched out like an invisible hand and crushed her spirit with its oppressive weight. "I know your thoughts. I know you better than you do yourself. You require validation — approval from the other demons. You wish to prove that the other Emperors were nothing compared to you. Isn't that right? Answer me. ANSWER ME!"

Any semblance of calmness left Gressil's voice. He lunged a hand out with frightening speed and grabbed his sister by the throat, then slammed her against the wall. Immediately, Ose clawed and grabbed at him, but no matter how she wriggled, she couldn't pull his hand away.

"B-brother... you're... hurting me..."

"Good. You deserve to suffer. While you forget your roots, I live them. Every breath I take is a reminder of what the humans did to me. They can never suffer enough." Gressil's tongue flicked out and licked his beak in a circular motion. "I'm a freak — a sick, twisted reminder of what humans do to our people. And you... you want to... to reward them! You want to give them cushy lives so they will spawn more of their brood!"

"No...! That isn't right... kah..." Ose wriggled and tried to kick her brother away, but she couldn't tap into the electricity inside of her body. Her strength reserves diminished every second, leaving her defenseless before Gressil. "Th-think of the demons... we have to-"

"Forget the demons! They can burn for all I care! I'll throw them into the Lake of Fire myself if that's what it takes!" The eyes on Gressil's body began swiveling and spinning in a frenzy. Their colors brightened dramatically.

Finally, Ose had enough. She ground her teeth together. "Gressil... what would... would mother say... if she saw you hurting me like this?"

A moment passed. Gressil's eyes flicked to his second head. A moment later, it rose and blinked its eyes. A soothing female voice spoke from its lips. "Gressil... what are you doing to your sister?"

"Mother... mother..." Gressil released his sister and took a step back. "No! You filthy bitch! How... how _dare_ you wake mother up!"

Gressil's lower left arm swung up and punched his beak-head with staggering force. He stumbled back and gasped. "Augh!"

"You worm! I take one little nap, and you start strangling your sister?! Some demon you are! You're a failure! A disgrace!!"

Gressil reeled around as his second head berated him. "No... no... I didn't mean anything by it, mother. Please... please don't yell..." He pressed himself in a corner and covered his head with his hands.

"Don't you call me 'mother,' you pathetic insect! You call me by my name!" The second head screeched like a banshee, berating him mercilessly. "You can't do anything right! Freak! Monster!!"

Ose winced at the cruelty of the second head. "Mother, please don't hurt Gressil. He didn't mean anything by it... you know how he is."

The second head went quiet. Gressil continued to shrink into a ball and whimper, but the second head took control of his body and forced him to stand and walk over to Ose. "Oh, my beloved daughter. Why haven't you stopped by recently?"

Gressil peeked through his fingers at Ose, as if to beg her for help. However, Ose locked eyes with his second head. "Sorry, but I've been busy. Um... I'm an Emperor now! Just like you used to be!"

Her mother smiled. "Oh, that's wonderful, darling. It's good to see you devoting yourself to the path of power." Her expression hardened. "Perhaps someday, your incompetent brother will amount to something!"

"Moth- I mean... Lucifer... please..." Gressil whimpered like a beaten dog.

Lucifer ignored his pleas and continued fawning over Ose. "My precious little girl, it is wonderful to see you again. If you ever need anything, be sure and let me know."

A spark appeared in Ose's eyes. "Ah. Actually, mother, there is one matter I need assistance with. Gressil said no, though. He doesn't want to help."

"I see. Well, whatever it is, I'll straighten him right up. Don't you worry! Anything for my little genius daughter!"

Ose smiled uneasily. "Mother... don't go overboard, please."

"Gressil will be fine," Lucifer replied. "More importantly, how are you an Emperor, now? Did you finally eliminate that empty-headed dullard, Diablo? Or perhaps Satan's little skank, Belial?"

"No. There was an insurgency among the humans that forced Diablo to use his Omega form. He went into a coma afterward."

"Mmm. So it's only temporary. You'd best finish him off while you can, dear. The last thing I want is my little pumpkin getting demoted! Don't taint my legacy with simple mistakes."

"I'd never dream of it," Ose replied with a smile.

Before she left, Ose stole a glance at her brother's cowering face. He continued to mutter words under his breath, but Ose couldn't make them out. "Gressil? Are you alright?"

The formerly intimidating prison warden didn't reply. He continued to cower behind his hands, terrified of his second head.

Ose shrugged. She left the room and slowly paced down the hallway. The sound of Lucifer screeching at her brother sent pins and needles through her body, but she forced herself to ignore it.

_Gressil chose to escalate the situation. I only did what I needed to calm him down. Now that I have what I need, I won't have to talk to him again._ Ose thought.

Ose's expression turned to steel.

_Besides. Gressil killed our mother. He deserves to suffer._ Ose thought.

"Aaaooo..." Gressil whimpered and squirmed for several long minutes, even when his mother yawned, closed her eyes, and went to sleep. The memory of her abuse lingered in Gressil's mind.

"Damn you... Ose... waking mother like that. How could you be so cruel?"

Tears streamed down the deformed demon's beak and plopped onto his shriveled body. He shook and moaned for several seconds before finally pulling himself to his feet. 

He stared emptily at the human man, who was now incoherent and muttering quietly to himself. Gressil pressed his toothy beak together and glared at his second head. _Lucifer's_ head.

"Callous... vile... despicable..."

Gressil rose to a standing position. "Birdy. I need Birdy. Where is Birdy?"

He walked around the table, grabbed the man's head, and tore it off without the slightest effort. Shakily, having done his good deed for the day, Gressil walked over to the wall opposite the door and pulled a rack of keys off a handle.

"Birdy... Birdy... I need Birdy..."

That was right. Birdy wasn't like his other prisoners. Birdy was special. Birdy had been his favorite for a very long time.

Birdy always made Gressil feel better, especially when Mother emerged.

Gressil threw the door open and stumbled out into the darkness. He sniffed the air, feeling apprehensive about the direction Ose went.

Satisfied his sister headed toward the exit, Gressil's eyes flicked left and right. He walked in the opposite direction. His two-toed feet, shaped more like a dinosaur's than those of a demon, _clomped_ like horse-hooves against the floor. He leaned against the wall as he walked.

"Birdy... Birdy... need Birdy. She'll make me... make me feel good... not like mother... mother only hurts me..."

Gressil glanced around to make sure nobody was following him. He eventually stopped before one door, among many. It appeared unimportant, given it had the same markings as all the others. However, a faint series of scratches above the wooden doorframe revealed a name if one looked closely enough.

_Birdy._

Three dozen locks littered the side of the door, as well as the top and bottom, making entry incredibly tricky if one lacked all the keys.

Gressil's anxiousness melted away. He flashed a sinister grin and quickly unbolted the door, then started releasing one lock after another.

His heart skipped several beats. "Birdy is waiting. She hears me unlocking the door. She can't wait to see me!"

A childlike excitement gripped the hideous demon as he unhitched the final lock. After suffering all the humiliation from his mother, and after Ose laughed and mocked him... he'd have the final laugh.

Gressil slowly opened the door. It creaked loudly as its rusty hinges ground together.

A pitch-black room awaited. Even Gressil's demonic eyes couldn't pierce the veil of shadow.

"Birrrrdyyyy..." Gressil stepped inside the room. The sound of something scratching the ground caught his attention.

Birdy was awake.

A muted whimper caught his ear. Gressil sighed. "Oh, Birdy. It's been a while, my pretty. I've had the worst day. You wouldn't believe it."

Gressil pushed the door shut. The pitch-blackness of the room swallowed up his form. Only his glowing eyes remained, like smoldering embers to light up the night.

The sound of chains clinking excited him. Birdy pressed herself against the wall with what little strength she had left.

Keeping his voice low, Gressil leaned down and caressed her broken, lacerated leg. She quivered and squeaked in fright.

"We're going to have fun, Birdy. I'll hurt you in ways I've never tried before. You won't forget tonight... not for another ten thousand years."
