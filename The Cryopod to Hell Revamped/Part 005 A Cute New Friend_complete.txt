Jason didn't take long to end up sitting cross-legged on the ground, mindlessly tracing circles on the smooth dirt floor with his finger. His mind churned through hundreds of thoughts, one after the other, while he tried to figure a way out of this mess. Hoarhiim and Blaarjiim's orbs sat a few feet in front of him, putting out and sucking in the light around themselves, respectively.

_The Golden Doors only opened inward. I shoved them shut earlier, and they don't have handles, so I don't know how the hell I'm going to pull them open to escape. Once I make it outside, I'm reasonably sure that Hoarhiim's power-up will improve my body to the point that I can either fight the monsters or evade them and flee. Probably..._ Jason thought.

He narrowed his eyes and focused, and the mental map from Solomon's Crown appeared once more. The Labyrinth stretched for miles in every direction, almost as if Hell were inside the hollowed-out remains of a planet. But if that was the case, why was there gravity pulling him to the ground?

While his mental map made it seem like he was at the center of a cube, in reality, it probably wasn't displaying the Labyrinth's layout past a certain point. It could go on for hundreds, thousands, perhaps even hundreds of thousands of miles past the edge of his mental map.

Hoarhiim stirred. "What is on your mind?"

"A lot," Jason replied, replying noncommittally. His mental map had started showing more than just a single red dot indicating his location; now it displayed several other points of interest, including a few that he could only assume were the legendary weapons and armor he had noticed when he first picked up Hoarhiim. However, they were considerably further from his current location than he'd like.

"Hey, Hoarhiim. Do I have to hold you to access your power?"

He shook his head. "We are bound together, so distance is not a barrier. However, if you attempt to use my power without physically touching my orb, the connection will be substantially weakened."

"I see."

Another few minutes passed. The monsters outside seemed to have fallen silent, making no noise at all. Jason didn't know if they were waiting for him anymore. Maybe they had given up and left.

Standing up, Jason started pacing back and forth, chewing on his thumb as he examined the door and used the Crown to consider his options.

_When I first picked up Hoarhiim, I killed that shadow-monster, but there was a trigger involved. I spoke a single word out loud. Why? What prompted me to do that?_ Jason thought.

He stopped and pivoted on his heel to face the entrance head-on. Raising his arm, he aimed his palm at the door.

"**Light.**"

Immediately, a light like the Sun radiated from his hand, reflected off the shiny golden door, and blasted him in the eyes, blinding him. "Geyaugh!"

Stumbling back, Jason fell on his ass and rubbed his eyes fiercely. "Fuck! Shit! Goddamnit! Worthless piece of garbage, Jason!"

After a minute, he blinked his eyes open, but he could barely see anything. Hoarhiim appeared in his mind and shook his head. "Tsk. You've seared your retinas, Jason."

"No shit!"

He closed his eyes again and thought of the word he'd spoken. _Light._ He had said it, and a light appeared. He'd already made himself a pizza and a bed. What else could he summon with his words?

Stumbling forward, Jason leaned against the wall and pulled himself to a standing position, keeping his eyes closed the whole time. He aimed his hand at the other wall. "**Rock.**"

An indistinct _whoomph_ sounded off in front of him, and something clattered to the ground. Hoarhiim inhaled sharply. "Brother, did you see that?"

Blaarjiim nodded slowly. "I saw it, but I don't believe it."

"Quiet," Jason muttered at them. "I'm thinking."

His mind raced. _I said the word 'light,' and created light. Then I said the word 'rock,' and a rock appeared. Does that mean that any word I say will make something happen related to its meaning?_ Jason thought.

Deep in his chest, his heart slammed like a jackhammer.

"**Sword.**"

This time, after the _whoomph_, a metallic object clattered to the ground. Jason jumped at the sound.

"Merciful Creator," Hoarhiim whispered.

_Perhaps an abstract concept will work?_ Jason thought.

He took a few moments to focus on his burning eyes. In the depths of his mind, he imagined each one restoring to perfection, and his pain fading away.

"**Heal.**"

This time, a crackling sensation appeared in his eyes, but it faded after just a second. When he blinked his eyes open, he could see once again. In fact, his vision was damn-near 20/20.

"Holy shit!" The curse burst out of him as he regained his vision and caught sight of the sword laying on the ground. The weapon's design was nothing special, but it looked like it could deal some damage. He snatched it up and hefted it around. The sword wasn't heavy, perhaps a few pounds at most, but just touching the blade nearly split his finger open.

Blaarjiim eyeballed the sword. "You spoke a word and forged that weapon out of nothing. I've been alive a long time, mortal, and never have I seen a spell that could do such a thing."

His hands started to shake. "H-Hoarhiim, am I doing this because of you? Did you give me this power?"

The light-being shook his head. "Nay. It is a power that you already possessed. I have only given you enough spiritual energy to realize your potential."

"No kidding," Jason whispered.

He swung the sword at the wall, and when it hit, it cut a few inches inside the stone and got stuck. Damn! It was sharp as hell!

After yanking it around for a moment, he wrenched it back out. "**Scabbard.**"

This time, instead of appearing in midair, he felt a small displacement of weight on his body, glanced down, and marveled at how the sheath had emerged at his side, looping around him with a belt to hold it sturdy. A smile formed on his face as he slipped the sword into its holder and grabbed the orbs up.

"**Container.**"

This time, two round holsters appeared on his left side. They were slightly bigger than the two orbs, with a little flip-up latch to hold them in place. He put Blaarjiim in the rear and Hoarhiim in the front. Plenty of light shone through to illuminate the path before him, and Hoarhiim nodded approvingly.

"Well done, Jason. You're learning how your power works at an incredible speed."

"Thanks, Hoarhiim," Jason said, only to pause and think about it a moment. "I can manifest objects and perform other miracles by simply speaking a word. What would you call a power like this?"

Blaarjiim and Hoarhiim stared at each other for a moment. The dark one answered first. "Err, 'word magic?'"

Hoarhiim smacked his forehead. "Terrible! The title of such powerful magic demands more majesty! This ability is unlike anything we've ever seen before. It can forge weapons from little more than Jason's spiritual energy! It's a power beyond anything the ancient Heroes could have ever dreamed up."

"Thanks, Hoarhiim."

Jason blushed a little at his savior's sudden enthusiasm and drifted back to thinking about what he should name his power.

_It can forge weapons out of midair, huh?_ That sounded pretty badass. Forging. Blacksmithing. It was as if he were a weaponsmith, crafting a world that bowed before him.

Heh, he liked that. Now he knew what to call his power.

"Blaarjiim, Hoarhiim, I've made up my mind. I'm going to call it 'Wordsmithing.'"

The shit-eating grin on his face couldn't be contained. Already his mind bubbled with the possibilities his new power gave him.

Turning around, he faced the door.

"Let's get out of here. **Open.**"

Seconds passed.

The grin on his face faded as the door didn't budge.

He tried again. "**Open.**"

Still nothing. Frustrated, this time, he tried yelling it. "**OPEN!**"

Finally, a reaction. The door shuddered slightly and nudged inward for all of two inches before coming to a stop. Outside, the screams of monsters and demons began anew as they realized he was trying to exit the room. Several skeletons started pounding on the door, while other monsters banged on drums and hollered like banshees. Now that the door was cracked ever so slightly, he could hear voices screaming out things like, "Man filth! Come out, coward!" Swords stabbed through the opening as hundreds of monsters screamed and shouted.

Jason wiped his forehead. His body felt fatigued, and all he had done was speak a single word. Hoarhiim took notice.

"Be careful, Jason. It appears your ability to Wordsmith isn't unlimited. Using magic drains the spiritual energy from your body, including that which I am giving you. If you keep spellcasting recklessly, you'll drain your reserves and leave yourself open to attack."

He was right, as always. Jason nodded along with his words, then stood a little straighter and took several deep breaths. Unless he was mistaken, the volume of his voice had increased the energy used, but also the effect. Then, that meant...

No, what was he thinking? If he used all his energy just opening the door, he'd be a sitting duck. The monsters would pour inside while he was gasping for breath on the ground and gut him like a pig.

Yeah, that was the key. If he opened it inward, he was inviting them to carve off the juiciest part of his body. But if he forcefully opened it outward, he could blow away the ones at the door and dash for the exit.

But how?

The Crown sent countless thoughts toward him, and a moment later, an idea sprang into his mind.

Raising his arm, he aimed it at the door. "**Push!**"

A wave of energy surged from his body, like a blast of wind, and smashed against the door. It impacted it with a bang, then knocked him back slightly. He quickly regained his footing and examined the door. It was rattling a little, but other than closing it the rest of the way, he didn't think his magic had much effect.

Blaarjiim smirked. "You'll need more power than that, mortal."

"Right, right."

The knockback was a problem. If he turned the juice up, he wouldn't be able to keep his balance. The door was exceptionally resistant to any form of attack. Gold was a soft metal, so given the door's defensive properties, it must only be painted gold, but forged from something far more resilient.

Aiming his hand one more time, this time, he prepared a shield for himself. "**Solidify. Root.**"

Dense rock sprouts from the ground, climbed up his body, and wrapped around his flesh to hold him in place. With his frame now firmly rooted, he took the deepest breath of his life.

"**PUUUSH!!!**"

A stupendous wave of energy exploded from his body, comparable to the kinetic energy of ten hurricanes, fifty tsunamis, and a giant tornado. The wave of power instantly blasted the giant golden doors off their frames, smashed through the enemies in front, and sent them careening through the wall of monsters awaiting his exit. Before he could see what was happening outside, the magic force ricocheted back against his body. It ripped his hardened form from the ground, flung him back against Solomon's Throne, destroyed his makeshift armor, and knocked the wind out of him.

A moment later, Jason blinked his eyes.

He couldn't feel anything. He couldn't move his head, or anything else.

No, that wasn't right. He felt warm. Hot, even. It was as if someone had poured gasoline on him and lit a fire. The burning agony was like nothing he'd ever felt in his life.

It hurt.

It hurt!!!

"Ney-ungh-"

A pitiful whimper choked from his throat. The world rushed back to him, and he slowly managed to discern that the side of his head was resting on the ground.

Hoarhiim said something, but he couldn't hear him. He couldn't see him, either.

His figure appeared in front of him, but it was foggy and indistinct. He was... waving his hand?

"-son. Jason. Jason!" He yelled and made him flinch, the slight movement sending spasms of pain across his entire body. "Jason! Gods, your body- it's..."

"How brutal," Blaarjiim said, as he appeared beside his brother. "The mortal is still alive, isn't he?"

"Barely," Hoarhiim whispered. "I think Jason shattered every bone in his body. He's not going to make it, I'm afraid."

"Well, heal him, idiot!" Blaarjiim screamed at his brother in anger.

_When did he become so concerned about my well-being?_ Jason thought.

"Don't tell me you've forgotten how to do that?!"

"I haven't forgotten," Hoarhiim said quietly. "But he used up much of my energy during that attack. The best I could do is stem the bleeding. It doesn't matter anyway, brother. Look."

Hoarhiim pointed somewhere past where Jason could look. He tried to follow his finger, but he couldn't move his head at all. He could only estimate he was pointing at the doorway.

Blaarjiim's voice softened. "Ah, what a shame. It looks like you didn't get them all, mortal. We had fun while it lasted."

From the doorway, the sound of breathing emerged. Something took a step forward, its foot causing a _whoomph_ on the ground. "Man-filth."

Jason managed to wheeze out a question. "Whuh ish et?"

Hoarhiim turned his back on him. "I'm sorry, Jason. It's an orc. There's nothing we can do to help you. Close your eyes. Maybe he'll kill you quickly."

"Kuh... kull muh?"

His mouth wouldn't form words. He couldn't move at all. All he could do was lie still and listen in terror as the monster took slow, menacing footsteps toward him.

"You killed friends. Me lose many blood brothers! You pay! Bahahah!" The monster laughed, and Jason could only thank whatever god might exist that he couldn't see the monster's face.

_I'm going to die now._ Jason thought.

More footsteps. The orc paused. "Mormok, you live too?"

A second orc voice spoke. "Yup. Human not kill everyone. Skugs alive. Diablo be very angry about losses though."

"Diablo need stuff it," The first orc said. "Let's gut filthy human 'n give him to boss."

The two of them chuckled and took a few steps forward. Soon Jason could hear their breathing. They were less than ten feet away now.

"Hehe. Man-filth piss himself. Scared? Should be."

Suddenly, from outside, a bone-chilling scream from another orc rang out. "Aaah! No, stay back! Get away!"

Both orcs paused, probably to look back. The first one sniffed the air. "What that?"

The orc outside screamed again, but his voice cut off midway as a sickening _crunch_ filled the din. The sound of snapping bones turned Jason's blood to ice.

The second orc spoke, but his voice was trembling. "H-hey, Ugna. I-isn't this room that scary place? Gressil says no come here."

The first orc's breathing quickened. "Let's take look. Someone's 'bout to die, and it ain't me."

"B-but Ugna..."

"No buts! I ain't 'fraid of nothin'!"

Both orcs turned around and headed for the entrance, leaving Jason to bleed out on the floor. He didn't know whether to be relieved or terrified at this turn of events.

Hoarhiim nudged Blaarjiim. "Brother, is that-"

"Quiet! I'm trying to listen!" The dark orb walked over and leaned against a barrier, as if trying to see past the entrance. His vision must have been limited in some way. "This... this could be..."

The orcs stopped at the entryway. Ugna, the first orc, sniffed the air again. "Where everybody go? Where skugs? Where kobolds?"

"U-Ugna, we should leave. Look. Look!" Mormok, the second orc, yelled at his comrade frantically. "The statue! It's broken! It's-"

His voice halted much too fast for him to have simply stopped mid-sentence. The sound of something wet and damp hit the floor. Ugna howled in terror.

"No... no! NO!! It's the Black Witch! Get away from me! Filthy human devil!" He swung a sword at someone out of Jason's sight, banging it against the wall in the process, but a moment later, his voice went silent.

Blaarjiim and Hoarhiim gaped at something unseen.

The awful sound of bones snapping and sinew twisting filled the air again. This time, however, it was accompanied by another sound: _Someone smacking their lips._

"Mmm, yummy! Teehee!"

A little girl's voice rang out in the now-silent hallway.

The sound of clicking, like high heels on stone, slowly approached him.

"Hello? Are you alive, mister?"

The little girl skipped toward him, kicking pebbles and loose debris out of her way. Her pitter-patter of footsteps reached him within a few seconds. "Mister?"

"Wung-guh..." Jason wheezed something out, but it wasn't much more than a painful gasp.

A moment later, a pair of black platform shoes, and some pale white legs appeared in front of him. Two inches above the ankles, a long fluffy black skirt started, but he couldn't see much beyond that. The girl knelt down, and finally, her skirted knees came into view. He raised his eyes to look at her, but she was still too high up for him to see her face. He could just barely make out a black dress with red ribbons all over the place and fluffy white frills lining her arms and stockings.

She reached forward and poked his chest. The slight tremor sent lightning through his body. "Hmm. Not dead, huh? You won't put up much a fight. Oh! What's this?" She shuffled slightly and reached toward his belt. A second later she squealed excitedly. "Ooooh! Blaarjiim? Hoarhiim?! You're both here!"

Blaarjiim forced himself to look calm, but Jason could vaguely make out a look of joy on his face. "My queen. It is wonderful to see you once again. Long have I awaited your return."

"Oh, you! You're always so stuffy! Why are you in this guy's belt, though?" A moment passed. Her voice pitch lowered. "Did he... _steal_ you?" The shift was frightening.

Blaarjiim paused for a moment, before turning deadly serious. "Aye, Lady Amelia. This mortal stole me from your grasp, and sought to take my power for himself. I can only imagine what nefarious deeds he was plotting."

"Muh-hung!" Jason choked out a protest, but it didn't amount to anything decipherable.

Amelia's knuckles cracked. "How... how dare you. Taking away my eternal partner while I was trapped in that awful statue... I'll make you regret this."

She started to stand up, but Blaarjiim interjected before she could turn violent. "Just a joke, my queen. A jest. He is the one who freed you from your prison."

"Yes!" Hoarhiim interjected. "This human is named Jason. He has a unique magical power called Wordsmithing."

Amelia tapped her shoes on the ground. "Oh! I see, now. Haha! Blaarjiim, since when were you one to tell jokes?"

Blaarjiim bowed slightly. "Your radiant smile is worth everything, my queen. However, I must recommend that you heal him at once. His life force is slipping away."

The girl hemmed and hawed for a moment. "Do I have to, Blaarjiim? He looks pitiful and weak."

Hoarhiim lowered his voice. "As a long-time friend, I would ask the same of you, Amelia. I have attached to Jason. I like him."

A moment passed. Finally, Amelia sighed. "Okay, fine. Blaarjiim, give me your power again. I'll heal him up in a jiffy."

What came next shocked Jason. An awful pain erupted in his chest, as if a trillion watts of electricity were coursing through every nerve in his body. He opened his mouth to scream, but nothing came out.

A minute passed. His bones wrenched back together violently, and his skin folded back on itself. He writhed on the ground, whimpering like a beaten dog, even after his body finished stitching back together.

"Oooh, sorry about that," Amelia said. "The phantom pain won't last long, though!"

Eventually, as she promised, his anguish subsided. He flopped onto his back and breathed heavily, staring up at the ceiling. Finally, he turned his head to examine his savior. She was just a child, perhaps twelve years old. Her black dress was an elaborate, gothic-lolita style thing, with red lacing and frills on the edges. Atop her head was a cute little fluffy red ribbon. She looked adorable.

Then she smiled, and somehow it was the creepiest thing he'd seen in his life.

"Aren't you going to thank me?"

Several moments passed as Jason stared at the strangely dressed young girl. The gaze she sent in his direction indicated a certain casual air he'd never seen in a child before. He probably shouldn't call her a child, since there was only six years difference between them, but she was very short, standing well under five feet tall.

Amelia tapped her foot. "Hellooo? Is anyone home? I healed you. Where are your manners?"

"What? Oh, right! Thanks." Jason quickly jumped to his feet, only for his legs to give out midway into the hop, making him stumble forward and bash his knees on the ground.

He could barely stand. His limbs had the elasticity of wet spaghetti, rather than the strength of bones and muscles. Every breath threatened to rob his last bit of energy from him.

Hoarhiim grunted. "Careful, Jason. Even if Lady Amelia healed you, your mind still has some leftover trauma from the grievous wounds you suffered. Further, you have almost no spiritual energy left at all. It's a miracle you haven't fallen into a coma."

"A coma?" Jason repeated his words disbelievingly. "I feel weak as hell, but that seems a little extreme."

To his surprise, Amelia stepped forward and grabbed his arm with both her hands, helping him to his feet. As she did, a slight warmth entered his body, making him realize how cold he felt.

Amelia stepped back, and Jason noticed that Hoarhiim and Blaarjiim's orbs were floating in midair, though she grabbed one in each hand right as he spotted them. "Jason's your name, hm? Well, I'll give you a freebie. Call it a reward for releasing me."

"Generous as always, my queen," Blaarjiim murmured. "Be thankful, Jason. The Black Witch is very picky with whom she rewards."

Jason rose to his full height of six-foot, two inches, only to realize he towered over the little girl. She took a step back in surprise. "Oh, my. You're taller than I expected. Somewhat skinny, though. Not much in the way of muscles."

Self-consciously, Jason rubbed his bicep. "Um, yeah. I, err, never saw the need to work out. Haha."

The two of them evaluated each other for a moment before Jason decided to ask a question. "You said I freed you. How?"

Wordlessly, Amelia pointed to the exit of Solomon's Tomb. "Out there. You broke the statue."

As Jason followed her finger, he spotted splatters of blood and gore all around where the Golden Doors once stood. A green leg lay against the wall, looking as if a horrifying monster attacked and devoured whomever it once belonged to.

Slowly, Amelia and Jason walked over to the exit. She handed him Hoarhiim's orb, then pointed at the center area. To his astonishment, one of the doors had embedded itself in the wall across the room, while the other had crashed into the ground and buried itself several feet deep.

_He knew he had shoved them with his "push" spell, but he didn't expect to send them flying off their hinges like that._ Jason thought.

His eyes looked away from the doors and toward the carnage in the room. Skeletons were crushed into dust, orcs and centaurs contorted into various unnatural shapes, and several monsters were little more than a collection of body parts strewn all over the ground.

And the statue! Where once a beautiful figure of a girl in a dress once stood, with water billowing from her umbrella- _hey, wait a minute!_ Jason thought.

He quickly looked at Amelia, comparing her to his memory of the statue. "Hold on, are you telling me you were the statue itself?"

Amelia smiled. "Mhm! And you released me! That was super nice of you!"

_But, I mean, I didn't do it on purpose,_ Jason started to say. Blaarjiim appeared in his mind and glared at him, waggling his finger.

Right, duh. _Go with the flow, Jason. You did it on purpose._ Jason thought.

He quickly wiped at his nose. "Yeah, nah, it was no big deal. Blaarjiim suggested smashing the statue, so I thought it would be cool. Everything got a little out of hand with the monsters, but you saved me, so I'd call us even."

Amelia's smile disappeared. "Uh huh... sure."

Even as Blaarjiim grinned at him for making him look good, Jason swept his gaze around the room once more. _Yeah, Amelia saved me, or at least I think she did. But... what about those awful chewing noises?_ Jason thought.

Maybe he had heard wrong. After all, he was in a state of delirium. Still, it couldn't hurt to ask, could it?

"So, uh, Amelia."

"Hmm?" She walked forward and nudged an Orc corpse with her foot.

"You healed me, so you have magical energy too, right? Magical powers?"

She spun around in a circle on her heel like a ballerina. "Mhm! I've got all sorts of powers! Why do you ask?"

"Well..." Jason turned his gaze to one of the horribly broken Orc bodies nearby. "These monsters... they're from fairy tales and fantasy stories. I never thought in my life that they truly existed. Yet, you can fight them... but how?"

She stopped spinning to frown at him. "What a silly question. Hoarhiim, haven't you taught anything to this child? Surely you've at least told him about magic!"

The white orb sighed. "I tried, but we didn't have time to explain."

Tapping my lip, Jason recalled what he had told him before. "Hoarhiim, you mentioned spiritual energy once already, then Amelia did, too. What is that? Is it like mana?"

Amelia cocked her head slightly. "Mah-nuh?"

Aw, nuts. She had no idea what he was talking about. "It's this mechanic from video games. It's like a meter that allows you to cast spells and stuff. When it runs out, you can't use magic anymore, and end up a sitting duck."

Hoarhiim squinted. "I am not sure what you're talking about, but the concept is mostly correct. Spiritual Energy is the power that allows demons and angels to cast spells. Humans typically have very little, but even if they had more, they could not use magic."

"Oh. But Amelia has magic, and so do I. Are we special or something?"

Amelia's mouth went taut. "Special. In a way, yes. Certainly, you are. As for me, I'd rather not say."

Once again, she gave off an odd feeling Jason couldn't quite describe in words. "You're a peculiar little girl."

Her expression shifted to annoyance. "Hey! You're the child here! I'll have you know my body is immortal. I'm tens of thousands of years old."

"Really?" Jason eyed her carefully. "I guess anything is possible with magic."

She quickly looked away and blushed. "Y-yeah. Just drop it, okay? I don't like this topic. Tell me about your magic."

_She's self-conscious about her looks,_ Jason thought, making a mental note. _That's how most women are, I guess._

"I call my power Wordsmithing. I can make things happen by speaking a single word."

Amelia continued looking away for a moment, then suddenly jerked back to meet his gaze. "Wait, you _used a word_? Not your hands?"

Jason echoed her confusion. "What do you mean, 'my hands'?"

Hoarhiim cleared his throat. "Ahem, well, you see, Jason, every form of magic requires a specific amount of time, hand sign combinations, and spiritual energy from the user. Your power is very irregular in that respect. I've never seen someone, especially a human, who could use magic just by speaking a word."

"Yes," Blaarjiim said. "And creating items out of thin air! That should certainly be impossible."

Amelia raised an eyebrow. "Show me."

All eyes turned to Jason. "W-well, see, I don't have any energy left. Even if I did, I don't know what to make."

Amelia leaned toward him, reached her free hand forward, and poked him in the chest. A sudden rush of mana poured into his body. Jason stood awed by how revitalized he felt. "Have you tested your abilities yet?"

"No. I only just realized what my power was right before I sent the Golden Doors flying. I haven't had time to experiment."

Amelia nodded and started pacing back and forth. "I see. Blaarjiim, surely you've evaluated the boy. What can he do?"

Jason hated it when this little girl called him _boy_. She probably did it to make him self-conscious.

Blaarjiim smoothed down his shirt, or whatever. He only saw him as a vague black apparition, but apparently, he wore clothes. "Hmm, yes. He can heal himself, he created a sword and a rock, and he sent the doors flying. I don't know what his limits are, but based on what I've seen, I'd be surprised if he had any at all."

Amelia paused her walking to kick an orc's helmet out of the way. "No limits? Absurd. Every magi has limits, especially if they're human. I need to test him."

She turned to face Jason.

"Make me a chair," Amelia pointed to an empty spot a few feet away. "Right there."

"A chair? That seems random."

She shook her head. "I want to sit. Hurry up! Unless you think you can't."

"Fine, geez." Jason reached his free hand out and aimed it at a bare patch of floor near a crushed skeleton and a beheaded horse. The sight of the corpses all around hardly fazed him anymore. "**Chair.**"

His heart skipped a beat as once again, air rushed into the spot he aimed his hand at, and a chair formed in midair. It dropped a few inches, landing perfectly on its four legs.

Amelia scowled. "You call that a chair? Look at it! I'll get splinters on my legs if I sit there! Did you envision a pauper's chair? It doesn't even have a cushion! Make another one, and make it fit for a queen!"

"A queen, huh?"

Jason stared at the chair thoughtfully. He hadn't thought of anything specific when he first used his spell, but this time...

The Crown of Solomon seemed to speak to him, bringing up a mental list of ten thousand chairs. He closed his eyes and focused, bringing up an image in his mind.

With a flourish, he jerked his hand out again. "Behold! I will summon a chair far greater than any you've seen before! Prepare yourself, Lady Amelia! Hah hah hah! **Chair!**"

A _thunk_ of something hitting the ground made him jerk his eyes open. Amelia stared at him, disgust on her face. "What was with that grandiose speech? Never do that again."

"Uh, sorry." Jason's eyes flicked from her to the chair and- HOLY MOLY!

A throne, something that would make King Solomon red with envy, sat on the ground amidst the corpses. The chair's vibrant golden color mixed with its bright-red fabric and elaborate stitchings to create an awe-inspiring image. Skulls made of silver and bronze stood atop the side pegs near the head, giving off a feeling as if it once belonged to a powerful necromancer.

Amelia noticed Jason wasn't looking at her anymore, and when she turned and spotted the chair, she jumped in surprise. "Whoa! You made THAT?!"

Jason pulled away and swept his hair back with his hand. "Heh, yeah. Now you're beginning to see the true extent of my power. Something like this is trivial for a god, like me. I can create anything with the power of a single word."

Amelia's excitement flattened as she turned to stare at him. "Jason. You're doing the thing again. Don't."

His shoulders slumped a little. "Okay. Sorry."

She perked back up and skipped gaily to the chair, spinning and sitting down in one smooth motion. The instant she leaned back, she moaned out loud, "Oh, goodness, this throne is soooo comfortable! I feel like it was made for me!"

Jason resisted the urge to boast once more. Instead, while Amelia rubbed all over the chair, smiling ear to ear and praising his handiwork, other thoughts came to his mind.

_I created that chair out of nothing. This power is unusual enough that Hoarhiim and Blaarjiim both saw fit to comment on it. What other properties could it possibly hold?_ Jason thought.

Silently, he pondered a hundred different options, each one more interesting than the former. Raising his hand, he pointed a finger toward the opposite wall and muttered a word. "**Fire.**"

A tiny _poof_ of flame spat from his finger and fizzled away.

This time, he tried again, but louder. "**Fire.**"

A stream of flame shot out, traveled around ten feet, and fizzled away.

By now, Amelia had calmed down, and she watched him with interest. "Whatcha doin'?"

"Trying new things. **Fire!**"

Jason felt his energy decrease substantially, but a plume of flame spewed from his finger, forming a laser of fire that jetted across the room and carves a line in the wall as he moved his finger. A few seconds later, it cut off, forcing him to take a breath. _That was exhausting._ Jason thought.

"Jason," Hoarhiim spoke up, surprising him. "Take Lady Amelia to the entrance of Solomon's Tomb, then use every ounce of energy in your body with that Fire spell. I want to see what happens. Don't hold back."

Frowning, Jason glanced at Hoarhiim's orb. "That's dangerous. I don't know if I can control my power. Besides, I might accidentally hurt Amelia."

Blaarjiim guffawed. "HAH! This mortal has a sense of humor! Flames can't harm the Black Witch, boy. Do as Hoarhiim says. The three of us all want to see what happens."

Indeed, Amelia leaned forward and rested her head and hand on her knee as she stared at Jason with interest. "Go on. Let's see it."

Jason nodded slowly. "Okay. Let's clear the room, then."

...

A minute later, Amelia stood beside Jason as he aimed his hand toward the room of corpses and blood.

"This should be a heckuva way to clean the place. Hell doesn't have many janitors."

"Mmm," Amelia muttered.

To be honest, Jason was scared. The last time he had gone all out with an attack, he had nearly killed himself. Still, Amelia was here. The orbs insisted he wouldn't hurt her with his fire, and he knew she could heal him if things went south, but still... he was a little worried.

He stuck Hoarhiim into his belt pouch and raised both arms, then took a deep breath. Using every pore of his body, he screamed with all his might, "**FIIIIRE!!!!**"

What followed next was terrifying. A flood of flame surged from his body, screamed as if it were a living being, and tore through everything in its path. It leaped from object to object, swallowed the golden throne and melted it to slag in an instant, crawled across walls and the ceiling, and raced down the hallway across the room in search of more prey. Everything in the path of the flames screamed for mercy as it burned and devoured all it saw.

It was... it was incredible! It was terrifying! If he kept this up, he'd turn the whole Labyrinth into ash! He had to stop it!! But how?! How could he-

Suddenly, a sharp pain erupted at the base of his neck. He stumbled forward, and the flames cut off. His body went limp, and as he fell, Amelia caught him before he could plunge into the lake of fire.

Everything started to go black as Jason passed out. He slowly realized that Amelia must have knocked him out with a judo chop to his neck. The last thing he saw before he went under was a worried look on her face.

"Who _are_ you?"

Then the sweet release of sleep took him.
