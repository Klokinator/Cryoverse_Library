Part 433: S.W.O.R.D.

Both shocked and curious, Hope slowly approaches the statue of Uzziel. It perfectly mimics her original appearance in every way, while at the same time being made entirely out of specially-grown wood. Like a tree grown over time to somehow perfectly form the shape of a living person, Uzziel stands in place, her sightless eyes gazing vacantly past Hope to a world beyond his comprehension.

"She's dead?" Hope asks. "What do you mean? Why can I feel a trace of her energy still here?"

[There is far more than a 'trace' of the Earthmother's aura here.] Vulpanix says contemptuously. [You are simply too spiritually dull to sense it. Indeed, her affinity to life has enhanced the growth of all living things in Heaven. Even this underground arboretum.]

"But you said she was dead." Hope points out. "So... how can that be?"

Vulpanix draws nearer to Uzziel's statue. She looks at the Archangel's ring, but does not touch it.

[That is a somewhat complicated question to answer. You see, the Archangel of Creation experienced a violent fit of rage during the final moments of the Final Battle in Heaven. In an unexpected, uncharacteristic manner unbefitting the personality we had all grown accustomed to, she erupted with a bloody wrath, tearing apart several Demon Emperors, many Dukes, and countless other lower demons. She obliterated Heaven by drawing upon its internal mana reserves, then detonated her soul to unleash one last ultimate attack.]

Vulpanix slowly shakes her head.

[The Volgrim were not privy to that final battle. No, we arrived after its conclusion, only to witness the mountains of corpses that lay piled atop one another. We saw the aftermath of her outburst. Not myself, as I was not yet formed, but I have read the reports and seen the holo-images. It was a... gruesome sight. Quite horrifying, if I'm being quite honest.]

She continues. [The Earthmother died, along with all her brothers and sisters. The Archangels ended up inside their rings. Since the demons had fought that final battle, they held dominion over the rings and refused to give them up without... concessions. Unarin paid quite a price to obtain Uzziel's ring, but left the others to the demons.]

"You paid a hefty price for a dead Archangel." Hope repeats. "Yet the implication seems to be that she is not truly dead."

[Her soul was shattered to dust.] Vulpanix explains. [The other Archangels also died, and for the first few millennia, they did not awaken inside their rings either. But eventually, they did. Uzziel did not. Her broken, shattered soul remains inside this ring. And that is one of the reasons why I've brought you here today.]

She turns to look at Hope.

[The Earthmother is technically dead. But she is not _truly_ dead. We have sustained and nourished her soul over the eons. We placed her ring inside Heaven and anchored this realm to a world rich in life energy, all in the hopes of someday bringing her back.]

"You... want to use her powers?" Hope asks. "And you want me to use my Wordsmithing to try and revive her."

[The Archangel of Creation is not like the other Archangels.] Vulpanix explains. [She possesses the Creator's Seed. While we once sought to obtain the Codex, the key of life, from Emperor Lucifer, her creation abilities are but a pale shadow of Uzziel's. Lucifer cannot create new life. She can only corrupt existing life into forms which already exist. But Uzziel... she can create _something_ from _nothing_. And that is a power we must obtain if we are to defeat the Plague.]

Hope crosses his arms. He smirks at Vulpanix.

"Look at you, twisting the truth. You want me to believe you need Uzziel to fight the Plague, but that isn't the case. She might prove useful there, but that's hardly why you first picked up her ring. Considering the Plague didn't start popping up until a _few thousand years_ after the Energy Wars, and considering you paid for her ring _right_ after the Energy Wars, you must have wanted her abilities for another reason."

Hope begins to walk back and forth, pacing in front of Vulpanix while hemming and hawing.

"Hmm. Now, if I were a galactic empire in full control of the Milky Way, what would be my number one goal moving forward after defeating humanity? _Ah, I know._ I would want to augment my empire's strength further. Uzziel's powers would certainly come in handy for something like that. I could genetically strengthen the Psions, making them even more unstoppable. The Technopaths could become not just intelligent, but physically mighty as well. The Ascended, the Dolgrimites, the Changelings... there would be so many ways I could use her power, it's no wonder I'd want her ring for myself."

Vulpanix narrows her eyes. [And what of it?]

Hope stops to face her again.

"The answer is obvious. You don't need Uzziel to revive so you can defeat the Plague. You need her to revive so you can escape the Milky Way if things go south. Then you can put her to 'good use' empowering your species by force. She won't even be able to resist. She's not a short-term gain for you, but a long-term one. And that changes the equation for what value I provide, doesn't it?"

He continues. "If she were only useful in the short-term, I wouldn't be able to extract many benefits for myself. But since she will provide you with powerful upgrades over the coming eternity, I think I'm entitled to far more benefits than you were going to sucker me into accepting."

[You are surprisingly agile-minded when it comes to your greed.] Vulpanix replies blandly.

Hope doesn't reply. Instead he walks over to the statue and hovers up to its splayed-out hand. The statue's fourth finger holds Uzziel's ring, and he observes it for a moment.

"Will anything happen to her if I remove the ring?"

[The statue stabilizes her condition.] Vulpanix answers. [But you may remove the ring for a short while. It will not affect the Earthmother negatively.]

Hope nods. He starts to reach for the ring, but pauses.

"...You're really okay with me taking the ring? What if I teleport away? I could screw you over pretty badly."

[You might not be the most intelligent entity in the Milky Way,] Vulpanix says, [but you have much to gain by working with us and much to lose by making us your enemy. I trust you would not do something so imbecilic.]

"Mmm. Fair enough. I am pretty good at... gauging the odds of my actions." Hope says, his words slightly cryptic.

He plucks the ring off the statue's hand, then slides it onto his finger.

After a few moments, Hope immerses his consciousness inside the ring's interior. What he finds is a broken and shattered world. While all of the Archangels tend to live within pure quite voids inside their rings, Uzziel's ring appears more like a shattered pane of glass, with giant cracks and fissures inside the ring's interior.

Hope easily finds Uzziel's soul. He appears before her in an instant, frowning as he looks at her horrifically deformed and mutilated 'body'. Arms and legs grow out of places they definitely shouldn't. Her 'body,' which is actually just a representation of her soul remains, twitches every so often. Two heads sprout from her shoulders, with each of them sporting shuffled facial features. One of the heads has seven eyes. The other has nine. And neither of them have their noses or mouths in the correct places.

Slightly disgusted by the sight, Hope sighs to himself.

_Every vision Solomon showed me demonstrated that Uzziel was a kind and loving person. For her to end up like this... it's just not fair._

He retracts his consciousness and reappears in the material realm where Vulpanix gazes at him in disinterest.

[Quite a pitiful sight, isn't it?] Vulpanix asks. [I am unable to connect to Archangel Uzziel's soul. No Volgrim can. We have had to experiment on humans to create temporary beings who were capable of interfacing with her soul. Unfortunately, their bodies destabilized, so we could only obtain verbal and telepathic reports of what they saw before they ultimately died.]

Hope licks his lips. "I... I don't know if I can bring her back to life. She's still... alive. Sort of. But even if I can heal her soul, she won't be the same. She's been suffering like that since the end of the Energy Wars. I'd rather just... end her life. Give her peace."

Vulpanix's expression sours. [Do that and you will receive nothing from us. You may find the Earthmother's predicament painful to look at, but compared to the fate of the galaxy, it is nothing at all.]

"Even if I bring her back," Hope points out, "that doesn't mean she'll be capable of doing anything but crying or wallowing in depression. She'll be a broken shell of her former self."

Vulpanix shakes her head. [You are laboring under a misconception. We do not need Uzziel to act consciously in order for the Volgrim to make use of her abilities.]

Hope frowns.

He thinks about the implication behind Vulpanix's words for several seconds before curling his upper lip in disgust.

"You... you want me to give you a way to use Uzziel's power freely? Without her consent?"

[Exactly.] Vulpanix says, caring little for his evident disgust. [You could make a device of a magical or mundane nature. It would allow our people to interface with the Earthmother's powers. We could use them as we pleased. The better the device you create, the more concessions we are willing to give.]

She pauses, noticing the look on Hope's face.

[Get over yourself, mud-dweller.] Vulpanix snaps. [Your aversion to this minor action is revolting. The Plague captures living sentients and turns them into mindless drones, making them fight to capture and kill their enemies. Consider how many lives you could save with the Earthmother's powers, then get to thinking about how to satisfy our request. Considering how many lives you have taken since your cloning event, I do not imagine you are as averse to this task as you pretend.]

Hope's frown lessens somewhat, but he still feels relatively mixed on the issue.

"I... I might be able to heal her soul. Maybe I could make a device capable of wielding her creation powers. But... alright, let's talk business. What's in it for me?"

[You know, I respect a greedy person.] Vulpanix muses. [You're always certain of where you stand with them. Follow me. I'll take you to the next location of interest.]

She pauses to gesture at the statue. Hope nods and place Uzziel's ring back on its finger. Then, Vulpanix leads him a little deeper into the Arboretum. They round a pillar to find a door leading to a spiral staircase descending into the wall, and the depths of Heaven.

Hope follows Vulpanix. They quickly move through the stairwell, its interior illuminated by glowing mana-stones charged via Heaven's energy. Before long, they arrive inside a small room with another glowing portal, though this one is colored an ominous blood-red instead of purple or blue.

The Wordsmith watches as Vulpanix steps through the portal.

For a brief moment, he hangs back, thinking about shutting the portal off, stealing Uzziel's ring, and leaving.

_Her ring could benefit humanity tremendously. Plus... she doesn't deserve to be used and abused by these goddamn aliens. She should be with her brothers and sister._

But he doesn't do that. Instead, he flies toward the portal.

_If there's an ambush waiting for me on the other side, I'll make Vulpanix regret crossing me. She has no idea the surprises I can unleash if she sours on our 'cooperation.'_

He travels through the portal and arrives inside a steel-laden factory, a bunker of some sort with conduits of blue light and energy running along the walls. Hope glances around, sensing a tremendous amount of magical energy in the area.

"We're... back on Serris?" Hope asks. "That's odd. I... I sense countless Heroic Artifacts! But I couldn't before."

[We are indeed back on Serris. In a manner of speaking.] Vulpanix says, gesturing to a group of Technopaths standing at attention just a hundred feet away. The Technopaths look at Vulpanix, but don't notice Hope at all, given his currently invisible body. [We are inside a... Special Containment Zone. This is the Sentient Weapon Observation and Research Division. S.W.O.R.D. for short."

A pulse of magical energy collides with Hope's barrier, momentarily making him visible to the Technopaths. They notice the disturbance in the air and look in his direction, but he turns invisible again a half-breath later.

"The hell?"

Hope glares at Vulpanix, but she doesn't pay his annoyance any heed. [I can already see you. What does it matter if these Technopaths can too? They are senior researchers, the cream of the crop. The best and brightest in the Volgrim Empire. You should let me introduce you to them.]

"Not interested." Hope gruffly says. "Let's get on with actual business."

[Fine. But I do insist you learn the name of at least one person here.] Vulpanix says. She directs her eyes to a certain Technopath, someone with powerful-looking steel legs, a burned body, and a deformed face that makes her barely even identifiable as a female. [Do you know this one?]

Hope frowns. She does indeed look a little familiar, if only due to her highly distinctive appearance.

"I... think I've seen her somewhere before."

[She is Psymin Miralax. One of the last living Celestial Designers. The Celestials were a group of Technopaths who created the Sentinels. Not many of them remain. Only a handful, as you humans would say.]

"I think I saw her during Unarin's broadcast..." Hope murmurs, before uttering a Word of Power. "**Memory.** Ah, yeah. He mentioned her name. She must be pretty important."

[Important. Yes. That is one way to put it.] Vulpanix says quietly. [I am a mighty 7th-Level Psion. Yet if the Plague were to strike Volgarius, Founders Unarin and Dosena would toss me aside, along with all the others of my rank and below, so long as it ensured the survival of the Celestial Designers. Their importance may even be greater than the Executors themselves. And for your information, Founder Cuanali is also a Celestial Designer. That means Psymin Miralax is regarded nearly as highly as our Fourth Founder herself.]

Hope raises his eyebrows. Hearing such reverence in Vulpanix's tone, he realizes that not only would the Volgrim gladly throw Vulpanix aside like garbage if it meant protecting Psymin's life, Vulpanix herself would not even complain! She would probably consider it an honor to die in the service of such a superior specimen like Psymin.

_Perhaps Vulpanix herself regards Psymin Miralax as humanity would a 'God,'_ Hope muses to himself.

The Technopaths standing at attention tilt their heads slightly. Then they turn and walk away, dismissed via a private telepathic message from Vulpanix.

[We will enter now.] Vulpanix says.

They walk through the only doorway leading out of the gate transfer room. As they pass through the exit, Hope feels a shift in the air, as if he has passed through a bubble, or the membrane of a biological organism.

A torrent of holy energy suddenly buffets his position, making Hope visibly wince. As if he has stepped into the periphery of a storm, Hope can practically feel the winds of mana crashing against his barrier. He hastily strengthens it with his Wordsmithing while looking around the room.

What he finds leaves him in awe.

Hope now stands on a small outcropping of a giant wall, one that drops a hundred stories above and below his current position. Every ten meters, Hope will see a different hole in the wall, and this goes on in a spherical shape all around the interior of a truly unfathomably gigantic facility.

Now standing inside a space seemingly blasted apart via a nuke into a perfectly spherical shape, Hope finds that he has truly entered the Vault itself. Each hole on the wall is covered by energy-imbued glass, containing most but not all of the mana of countless Heroic Artifacts. Truly, even if he were to try and count them with his eyes, Hope might not even be able to guess their total number.

"What..." Hope whispers. "What am I looking at? Are ALL of those holes filled with Heroic Artifacts? There must be thousands of them!"

[Not quite.] Vulpanix says. [The maximum capacity of the Vault is seventy-three thousand artifacts. At present, only two percent of the alcoves possess an artifact. Given how many Heroes have walked Earth's surface, we will never be able to fill it up.]

Hope's eyes sweep across the opposite walls and the walls around him, but ultimately it's a single beam of light in the exact center of the facility that draws his attention.

A torrent of magical energy travels from the ceiling of the spherical interior to the floor. And smack in the middle of this torrent of power, a powerful artifact levitates.

Excalibur.

Hope's heart quickens. He gazes at the artifact greedily, but quickly sobers himself up.

"That's Excalibur? But the beam of magical energy... it's almost like..."

[As if Excalibur is powering the Vault?] Vulpanix challenges. [That's right. It is. Excalibur provides power for not only the Vault, but the whole of Serris as well. Its reserves are truly beyond comprehension.]

Excalibur dumps ambient energy into the local environment. At the same time, high-technology-based energy-siphons greedily slurp up Excalibur's excesses in order to transmit that power elsewhere.

[Excalibur is perhaps the most remarkable of all Camael's creations.] Vulpanix remarks solemnly. [It absorbs residual ambient mana into itself, and when its reserves are full, it simply releases that power for us to use. And the best part? Even though the Volgrim cannot properly utilize mana for our own purposes, we all still possess mana in our bio-vessels. The greater the number of Volgrim who live on this planet, the more ambient mana we release, allowing Excalibur to further augment its energy reserves.]

She pauses.

[In short, it practically bypasses the laws of thermodynamics!]

Hope becomes more excited at this explanation, but that feeling quickly vanishes, replaced instead with a sinking realization.

_There's no way I can obtain Excalibur easily._ He thinks. _It has too many applications for the Volgrim to just hand it over. They're going to want me to pay an exorbitant cost!_

This realization causes him to experience several mixed feelings. He eyes Vulpanix from the side, but makes no particular moves, waiting to hear what the Confessor has to say next.

[Excalibur's energy production is truly impressive.] Vulpanix says, turning to face the Wordsmith. [But... it can be replaced. Taking energy from a local star is not too difficult for us Volgrim. Creating a Dyson Node is something we do all the time to transmit energy to our populated worlds. In that respect, Excalibur is far from irreplaceable...]

"But?" Hope asks, prodding her along that train of thought.

[_But_ that does not mean we have the resources to throw around casually creating Dyson Nodes.] Vulpanix retorts. [If you want the artifacts you seek, you will have to not only assist us with Uzziel, as I previously requested, but a few other matters as well. For instance, building the infrastructure to replace Excalibur's functionality. If you can use its magical reserves to augment your Wordsmithing, this should be but a _trifle_ of a favor.]

Hope's lips begin to feel dry again. He cautiously licks them, then presses them together.

"Is that all? Repair Uzziel's ring, build the energy infrastructure necessary for you to replace Excalibur, and perhaps some other... miscellaneous tasks?"

[Yes...] Vulpanix says slowly. [Other miscellaneous tasks.]

"How many? And to what extent?" Hope asks cautiously.

Vulpanix does not immediately answer. She fans out her fingernails and idly examines them, looking away from Hope as if he suddenly isn't all that important in her eyes.

[You seek a short term venture, it would seem. What we want is much longer-term. It would be a shame for you to waste your Wordsmithing on mere idle pursuits, don't you think? This war between humanity and demonkind, is it not rather petty? The basis for cooperation exists, but why do you hesitate to go any further?]

Hope narrows his eyes.

"Why? Because your species has dominated the Milky Way for the past several eons. Any gains I might make from this transaction will surely pale compared to those you will earn. On top of that, the Volgrim have not only tested their inventions on humans in the past, you still do so even now! And you really expect me to trust you when the terms are so beneficial for you but so limited for me?"

Vulpanix snorts. She and Hope fully put their attention on one another, having finally reached the bargaining part of their meeting.

[You want to speak of benefits? Let us not bring up how heavily Excalibur will inflate your strength. What of the Dominion Rod? It is largely useless for us, yet its ability to control and influence human minds will surely prove potent in your hands. And Solomon's Crown! Need I say any more about putting the greatest library of knowledge atop your head? These three artifacts alone should never be possessed by a single individual, lest they go power-mad and try to conquer the galaxy. Yet you act as if you are being slighted!]

"You're trading away three artifacts that either have no use to you or can be replaced for an Archangel's power that will _vastly_ enhance the Volgrim Empire's strength over time." Hope argues back, standing his ground. "You lose practically nothing but you gain a great deal in return!"

The two of them begin pushing and pulling, each one trying to gain leverage for their own side.

"You want to use Uzziel's power to create more folded spaces like Heaven!" Hope exclaims. "Who knows what dastardly secrets you'll be able to hide all across the Milky Way?!"

[And you can already do such a thing with your own power!] Vulpanix retorts. [The fact you haven't already shows that we Volgrim are superior! We know how to properly use the strength we possess!]

"Long-term cooperation benefits you far more than it does me!"

[If you think this is the only card we hold that you might want, you are sorely mistaken! Helping us without complaint will give you access to our other stores in the future!]

"You want a subservient Wordsmith slave you can hand some crumbs and get back gold from." Hope says. "Your sincerity isn't nearly enough. Show me more."

Vulpanix becomes quiet.

She eyes the Wordsmith with an ambivalent gaze, her eyes betraying no particular emotion.

[I do like a greedy person.] Vulpanix says quietly. [You always know where you stand with them. But sometimes, one can take their greed a little... _too_ far.]

"You think I'm asking for too much?" Hope replies. "I'm sure an intergalactic empire can pull a few more goodies out of its ass. Let's not play stupid."

Vulpanix doesn't budge. She merely stands firm, waiting for the Wordsmith to relent.

[Our terms are generous. What we ask of you is far from excessive. Do not press your luck, Second Wordsmith.]

The corner of Hope's eye twitches. 

That term she keeps using.

It didn't particularly bother him at first, but in this moment, it truly grates on his nerves.

Second Wordsmith.

_Second_ Wordsmith.

Always the follow-up choice. Always reminded of his inferiority.

He knows that the only reason the Volgrim approached him is because their first pick, Jason, would never have agreed to their terms.

Always, Jason gets the good parts of life, while Hope gets the leftovers.

A moment of silence passes between the Wordsmith and Confessor. Perhaps sensing the change in atmosphere, she cocks her head.

[Do you... disagree?]

Hope's eyes flicker. His pupils dilate, ever so slightly, a fact that Vulpanix does not miss.

"You know..." Hope murmurs. "I've treated you with respect this entire time. I've never implied that you were below me, or less than me, or inferior to me. But always, you look down on me. You look down on humanity."

[You are mud-dwellers. This is the natural way of things.] Vulpanix says, her tone frosting over. [Do not ruin this opportunity for yourself. It will not end well for y-]

Hope accelerates.

From a standstill, his body suddenly and violently races forward at the speed of sound.

Like a cannonball fired when nobody expected it, he blasts away from his position, his hand aimed at Vulpanix's chest.

**THOOM!!**

The Wordsmith crashes into the Confessor's body, exploding her like a bomb and spraying her entrails all across the area. He flies forward and slams into the opposite wall, his 'invincible' body taking no damage from the collision while causing alarms to ring out inside the facility.

His heart racing, Hope turns around to see what little is left of the Confessor sliding down the nearby walls.

"I reject your terms." Hope hisses, smiling evilly.

However.

The air shivers. It vibrates, like churning water seen through an aquarium's glass.

Vulpanix steps out of a tear in the fabric of space and idly glances at the remains of her 'other self' sliding down the walls.

[As I said, Wordsmith. You have made a _foolish_ choice.] She says, smiling with her eyes.
