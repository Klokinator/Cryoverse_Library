Jason stared blankly at Amelia as she took a step back. "You brought this upon yourself."

In his hand, a sword of pure blackness formed. It was a weapon familiar to Amelia, one she had seen long ago when Joan tore her to shreds.

_Catherine. The Sword From Heaven._ Jason thought.

"No! No!!" Amelia shrieked as she backed away even further. "How can you—where did you get that?!"

The Wordsmith rose, and his wounds healed. Dark energy rushed into his skin, and his eyes shifted from white to grey to black. "**Achilles. Orion. Hermes.**"

Several pieces of armor appeared in midair, wrapping around and encasing his body in darkness. "The Armor of Achilles, the Belt of Orion, and the Boots of Hermes. All legendary treasures from on high, all of them ruined by this godforsaken hell. If I must wear them to defeat you, I will."

The Black Queen raised Dellfingler defensively. Her nervousness and fear threatened to paralyze her. "W-what are these artifacts, Blaarjiim? I've... I've never seen anything like them."

Blaarjiim shook his head. "Neither have I, my queen. If they're even close to the level of the Sword of Heaven, then this battle won't last—"

Jason leaped at Amelia, screaming with a pitch similar to a minotaur. "**GROOAAR!**" He swung his sword down, and Amelia barely raised Dellfingler in time to block. The impact of their weapons flattened the grass around them.

"Ow! Oh god, that hurt!" Dellfingler cried out in pain, but barely got a second's reprieve before Jason's sword turned into a blur of motion. _TING-TING-TWANG THUD!_

Amelia deflected three of Jason's attacks before dodging the fourth and jumping back. The Wordsmith smashed his sword into the ground, spraying dirt in a wide arc. After a moment, his tongue snaked out. "**Where do you think you're going?**"

Blaarjiim assessed the situation. "My queen, something about those artifacts is corrupted. They're poisoning the mortal's mind. He's not fighting like he normally does."

Jason cackled evilly. "**Hahaha! You don't say! Oh, Amelia! I haven't forgotten what you DID to me!**" He stood up straight and aimed the sword away from his body, as if lowering his defenses. "**For thirty days, I lay in that fucking bed! Paralyzed, unable to speak, barely able to think!**"

Jason vanished, startling Amelia and Blaarjiim with his speed. A moment later, he reappeared behind the Black Queen and licked his lips. "**I'll make you SUFFER!**"

Jason grabbed Amelia by the hair. He ignored her scream of fright as he flipped her over his head and slammed her into the ground. Thanks to Amelia's enhanced body, the attack barely hurt her, but fear still appeared in her eyes. She swung Dellfingler and sliced the back of Jason's legs, but he didn't even wince in pain. The Wordsmith released Amelia for a moment, fell forward, and healed the wound with his Wordsmithing. "**Heal.**"

Dellfingler yelled, "W-watch out, honey! He's strong as the Devil himself! That dark energy's messing with his head!"

Amelia dodged another grab as Jason swiped at her. He grew enraged, and his hands turned into the claws of a werewolf. He gripped the corrupted Sword of Heaven more tightly and spun to face her. "**I released you from that statue, and look at how you repaid me! You split my throat, broke my back, and left me to die!**"

Amelia's hand trembled as she gripped Dellfingler. "That's not true! I healed you! I even made sure everyone knew you were lying outside the Core!"

"**LIAR!**" Jason stomped toward her, and his dark energy shifted around him, transforming him from a man into something resembling a Shade. His body became slightly fuzzy and indistinct, and then morphed into a shadow of a woman.

Amelia's eyes widened in terror. "N-no! Joan! Stay away!" She took a step back, failed to notice a rock, and tripped. At that moment, Jason lunged forward, clearing the short gap instantaneously.

"My queen!"

The Sword of Heaven swung up, lopped off Amelia's right arm with one movement, then reversed direction and cut off her other arm. She flopped onto her back and screamed in pain. "Aaiie! No, Joan, please!! It hurts!!"

Jason grinned devilishly and yanked Dellfingler out of her dismembered hand.

"You'd better stop this right now, you little punk!" Dellfingler roared.

Jason ignored the sword's protests and plunged Dellfingler into Amelia's stomach, then stabbed the Sword of Heaven into her heart, pinning Amelia to the ground.

"Aaaah! Uwaaa! It hurts! Hnnngh!"

It took several seconds before she could do anything other than cry and choke for breath. Jason leaned over her body and smirked. "**Not so tough now, are you? Fucking bitch. Do you think this ends here?! I've barely begun!**"

The Black Queen wiggled and tried to kick Jason, but he wrapped his legs around hers, pinning her completely. She whimpered in return. "Joan... please... I'm sorry. I'm sorry. I didn't mean—"

"**I'm not Joan! Stop calling me that!**" Jason gnashed his teeth. "**You hated me because I was weak, but now I'm unstoppable! It takes a monster to kill a monster! Beg for mercy!**"

Jason leaned imposingly over Amelia's helpless body. His expression changed slightly as his eyes traveled from her legs all the way up to her face. "**Maybe I've been doing this all wrong. There's a way to get out of your little predicament, Amelia. If you stay by my side and promise to be my whore, I'll make sure nobody hurts you. Not the demons, not Joan... nobody but me. You get to live; we get to be together...**"

His face approached hers. Amelia squirmed and glanced down at her arm stumps. They still hadn't grown back yet. Catherine's holy energy, driven straight into Amelia's heart, prevented her body from regenerating.

Jason caressed Amelia's thighs. Slowly, his werewolf-like claws roamed up her body to her breasts, only to come to a stop on her bloody arm stumps. The mortal pressed her against the ground, digging into her wounds.

Amelia cried out in pain. "Eeeyahh!!"

Jason softened his voice. "**We don't have to hurt each other anymore. Just say you want me, Amelia.**" He leaned down to kiss her, but Amelia wrenched her head away.

"Stay away! You're not Jason. I hate you." Tears formed in her eyes as she tried to avoid his touch. "I knew it. You're no better than Joan."

Her voice shook, despite her attempt to dampen her terror. "I cared about you. For a day or two, maybe it would have worked. But I knew, once I found out you were a Hero, it was over between us. I finally understand what that armor is. It's not making you evil, Jason... it's drawing out your deepest desires."

Jason faltered, pulling back a few inches. He scowled. "**Lies. The armor is evil, not I.**"

"No, you are." Amelia turned to face him defiantly, their faces separated by inches. "If I'm wrong, then kill me. Don't turn me into a plaything. Take the sword and cut my head off, then destroy the rest of my body with your Wordsmithing."

The Wordsmith stared at Amelia for several long moments, taking shallow breaths. "**I... I don't want to.**"

A moment passed.

Amelia nodded knowingly.

"You said the armor would give you the strength to kill me, but that's not true. The last thing you want is for me to die. You want my body; nothing else. Admit it. The corrupted armor brings out your selfishness, your repressed thoughts. That's all it does. It isn't _making_ you evil, Jason. Those thoughts were there all along."

He jumped off her and took a few steps back. "**Y-you're wrong! All wrong!**"

"I'm not!" Amelia didn't kick or try to stand. Instead, she glanced away, across the field and into the distance. "You could have been honest, Jason. If there was even a drop of love in your heart or a pale shadow of empathy for others, you could have told me you loved me."

She inhaled painfully, the swords still impaling her feminine form to the ground. "But now you can't. You've done the unforgivable. Look at yourself. You wanted to be a righteous Hero who fought for truth and goodness, but you're as much of a failure as all the previous ones were! It's true, Jason. I wanted to enslave you, make you into a weapon against the Emperors. The difference is that I have no path to redemption. I'm evil. Stained."

"**Amelia...**" Jason whispered, his eyes downcast. "**I... I didn't mean to hurt you.**"

Amelia stared up at the sky, the sun waning in the distance. "I deserve what you've done to me. But... you were supposed to be better." She choked and teared up. "You were supposed to be better! You were supposed to be a good man! Why did you have to change?! I hate you! I hate everything about you!!"

Amelia kicked and squirmed violently for several seconds, futilely trying to break free of her impalement. She failed.

The Wordsmith said nothing for several moments, as Amelia sobbed and gasped. Despite his demonic, monstrous appearance, his aura shrunk until it was no longer recognizable. The intimidating monster faded away, revealing a broken husk of a man.

Jason raised his head and walked to Amelia, then slowly knelt beside her. His voice normalized as his negative energy faded. "You're... you're right. I can't apologize enough. I don't know what to say."

He reached over and yanked the swords out of Amelia's heart and stomach, sending blood flying. Then he tossed the weapons to the side. "**Heal.**"

Amelia stared at him silently as he sat beside her. Her arms rapidly regenerated. Despite their battle only a minute before, she lay still and didn't move to attack him.

"Jason?"

He avoided her gaze. "Don't look at me."

"I'm not mad."

"You should be. I am."

Jason leaned over and started yanking on the Boots of Hermes. He struggled for a solid minute before sighing. "I can't... can't get these fucking things off." His voice cracked as he fought back tears.

Amelia pulled herself into a sitting position. She silently took Dellfingler and sheathed him inside his scabbard. "Jason. I've never seen you in as much pain as you are right now. Look at me."

He turned away, putting his back to her instead. "I don't want to. Everything you said was right. All your fears... all your suspicions. You were right to hate me."

"That's not what I said," Amelia replied, chewing her lip. "I never hated you, Jason. Not until today. Not until you attacked me. Not until you brought out the evil within yourself. Still... I would be hypocritical if I acted like you were the only one at fault."

His voice was numb. "I suppose."

Amelia pulled herself a little closer and wrapped her arms around Jason. "You love me... don't you?"

He didn't answer.

"You can say it, Jason. Now is the time for honesty."

While she wrapped herself around Jason's back, he rubbed tears from his eyes and stifled a sob. "I.... I did. I do. Maybe. I don't know. What I did wasn't loving at all. If this armor brings out my inner thoughts, then I'm just a sick, evil man. I'm worse than the demons."

"I wouldn't go that far."

The Black Queen pulled away from Jason. She sat and stared at his back for an entire minute. Already, the waning sun cast long shadows across the plains. She looked up at the darkening sky and sighed.

"Jason. I want you to kill me."

The Wordsmith sniffled, then turned to look at her. "What? I can't. I won't."

"You need to. I'm a mass-murderer. I won't stop until I've devoured the universe."

Another minute passed. Jason stroked the dead, brown grass. "Don't ask me to hurt you."

Amelia narrowed her eyes. She climbed to her feet and walked over to Catherine. The sword lay on the ground a dozen feet away.

With a light kick, Amelia sent the sword clattering over to Jason. "Pick it up. This time, finish what you started."

"I won't." Jason turned away again and stared into the distance.

"Pick the fucking sword up."

"No."

Amelia shook her head. "You almost had me. Goddamnit Jason. I told you to stop hurting me, not to spare my life! There's a difference!"

He didn't reply for a few moments. "To me, those are the same thing."

"They aren't."

Blaarjiim watched silently, trying to understand his queen's thoughts. Never before had they been so chaotic and disorganized. Confusion and contradictions swirled around in her mind.

"My queen, please, stop and think. Take the Wordsmith's life. Enslave him like we planned. Then, you can—"

Amelia muttered under her breath, "No, Blaarjiim. I won't. I've made up my mind."

"You wish to die? Why?! Don't throw your life away!"

She ignored him. "Goddamn you, Jason. Finish me off. You're a Hero. I'm a monster. It's your destiny. Your job."

He crossed his arms. "Fuck destiny."

Amelia inhaled. "Fine. I'll beat you to death. How about that?"

The Wordsmith shrugged and looked away. "Go ahead. I don't care anymore."

His apathy pushed her over the edge. "Argh!! One little failure, and you fall to pieces. Pathetic! If you won't fight me willingly, then I'll make you!"

Amelia unsheathed Dellfingler, then rushed at Jason. She struck the front of his chest armor, punched his stomach, and sent him careening head over heels several feet away. He landed in a patch of dirt and lay limp.

Jason rolled over and shook his head, unharmed by her attack. "I won't fight you."

"Bastard! Stop mocking me!" Amelia forced anger into her voice, but her heart wasn't in it. She wanted to fight him, but only because she didn't know how to deal with her feelings either.

Jason pulled himself up. "Your attack lacked conviction. Hurry up and kill me, Amelia. I know you want to."

"Ooooh!" She howled in frustration, then stabbed Dellfingler into the dirt. "I can't! I can't do it if you won't fight back! Come on! Summon Joan again!"

Jason didn't reply. His emotions faded away, leaving nothing behind. He became little more than a sailor floating atop an ocean of despair.

Amelia sank to the ground. She buried her head in her hands. "I can't do it, Blaarjiim. I can't kill him. What changed? Why... why?"

Begrudgingly, the orb of darkness offered his opinion. "You wanted honesty from the mortal. Despite the unhealthy way it manifested, you got what you wished for. Haah... perhaps Dellfingler was right. Kiss and make up, or go your separate ways."

Time passed. The young woman and man sat across from each other, a fifty-foot gap separating them. Neither looked the other in the eye.

Amelia blinked and raised her head. "I can't kill Jason. He won't kill me. There's only one thing I can do."

She formed signs with her hand and whispered. Beside her, dark energy coalesced into the form of a familiar red-skinned demon. His short, stocky body and strangely ill-fitting suit combined to give him something of a comical appearance.

The newcomer yawned. "Aaah, geez. What's the big idea? Why'd you wake me up?"

Jason, hearing the unfamiliar voice, looked up. "A demon...? Who?"

A twinkle of recognition appeared in the newcomer's eyes. "Oh? Is this that guy you were telling me about?"

Amelia stared sullenly at the ground. "Yes."

"Great! Might as well introduce myself, then. 'Sup, kid. I'm Satan, the former First Emperor of the demons. You must be the Wordsmith. The Black Queen kept _blubbering_ about you."

Jason jumped to his feet and looked at Amelia in confusion. "The First Emperor? You're allies with _him_?! But... but he's supposed to be dead! You killed him!"

Satan rubbed his muscular bicep. "The reports of my demise have been greatly exaggerated."

The Wordsmith eyed the First Emperor cautiously. While Jason towered over Satan, standing seven feet tall to the Devil's below-six-foot stature, the demon's stocky body and nonchalant attitude gave him a far more intimidating presence.

Amelia touched Satan's leg. "I can't fight Jason anymore. Please... finish the job. Leave him almost dead so I can—"

Satan rolled his eyes. "Steal his soul. Duh. I know the drill."

The former First Emperor raised his wrist to stare at a strange-looking analog wristwatch, one without any moving parts. Then, he crossed the field, closing the gap between himself and the Wordsmith. "I don't get out often. Give me some fun, chum. If you don't, I'll hurt ya ten times worse than you could ever imagine."

Amelia turned away and closed her eyes.

"I can't watch."
