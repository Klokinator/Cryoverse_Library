Part 364: Humanity's Rise!

Two weeks pass.

In this time, Jason and the Seven Hells begin working together to rebuild Tarus II, relocate the refugees from the Hell of Isolation, and place the human refugees Jason personally teleported to Tarus II elsewhere on the planet.

During this time, all manner of events occur.

Hope returns from the Core with Neil in tow. Shocked, many people welcome back their commander with open arms, hugging and laughing with him due to the joy of his survival. For his part, Neil quietly accepts everyone's feelings, all while his eyes twinkle with insight. He evaluates the rapid changes occurring throughout human society with an air of aloofness. But privately, his thoughts darken as he observes the large numbers of demons working alongside his fellow men and women.

All across Tarus II, housing projects begin and end in rapid succession, bolstered by the efforts of demons and humans alike. To Jason's surprise, Phoebe takes the reins with excessive gumption, organizing the myriad of settlements inside and outside the reinforced crystal dome to ensure every bit of space is used wisely. With the amount of humans exceeding forty-seven million, they end up needing to cram a number nearly four times the population of Tokyo into a space only half the size of that city. Needless to say, this proves impossible to do in such a short time, so Jason makes several compromises.

Not only do forty-seven million humans populate Tarus II, but ten million monsters, and just as many demons, most of them from the Hells of Lust and Isolation, respectively.

With so many people trying to pack themselves into such a small space, Phoebe inevitably relents and begins building huge apartment complexes and individual houses well outside the crystal dome. Before long, the number of people living inside the dome drops dramatically, and the number outside skyrockets.

Despite the frantic energy and rapid expansion of Tarus II's controlled borders, most people remain hopeful and optimistic. The vague and distant specter of a possible alien invasion doesn't rest within their area of control, so the majority of citizens, no matter their species, leave such worries to the higher-ups. Generally, this means the Wordsmiths and the Demon Emperors.

In addition to housing, a thriving economy based on bartering springs up out of necessity. While the Wordsmith might be able to create unlimited energy for humanity, thanks to his replication of extremely safe micro-nuclear power plants, that doesn't mean he has the time, interest, or attention span to spend all day creating materials for humanity. And even if he did possess such inclinations, he still would opt to stand aside and let a certain level of self governance take hold.

Jason's vision proves far-sighted. Restaurants pop up here and there. Stores, markets, and other such establishments come into popularity as the military and civilian populaces find themselves in desperate need of ways to spend their downtime, re-energize, and ready themselves for more work.

In just two weeks, a tremendous amount of work becomes available, and essential, for everyone to complete. Nobody is spared from helping to rebuild and organize humanity's homeworld. Even the children pitch in. As for the elderly, the disabled, and the infirm, none of them exist!

Through the Wordsmith's unique powers, several useful and esoteric facilities spring up, all of them similar in their backend functionality to the Belial Booster.

One of these structures, referred to by the common folk as the Bodily Rejuvenation Depot, draws its power from the collective mana of every human, monster, and demon on Tarus II. By paying a certain number of Merit Tokens, obtained through services to the collective good of humanity, elderly humans can restore their bodies back to their youth while also obtaining dozens of years of additional lifespan! While demons and monsters don't need to use this facility, they still marvel at how the Wordsmith's versatile magic can improve the collective whole of humanity.

"Amazing! Humanity has always suffered from deaths caused by old age, but doesn't this facility essentially make humans immortal?"

"Immortal? Yeah... but then again, it can only restore a few dozen people a day. It's also not as convenient as making humans immortal from the get-go..."

"Doesn't matter. Humans have always been inferior to demons and monsters because they can't live long enough to accumulate centuries of wisdom. How frightening will they become when their collective intelligence is allowed to develop for hundreds of years?"

Conversations like this play out all across Tarus II, as well as inside the Labyrinth. The defeated Emperors can only smile bitterly, knowing they have no way to stop humanity's accumulation of power. Even if they destroyed the Bodily Rejuvenation Depot, Jason or Hope could easily create another one. If they wanted, they could even make humans individually immortal one at a time, through their Wordsmithing.

Nobody even doubts that the Wordsmiths haven't already done so to the people closest to them. For example, Jason already privately made his wife immortal, and Hope did the same for Neil Adams and Annette. Who can say how many others received these special privileges?

While an undying body might seem incredible to humans, the demons and monsters don't think such thoughts. After all, most monsters and demons are already immortal themselves. But even if one lives well for 100,000 years, a random meteor might crash into their planet, an earthquake might kill them, or another monster or demon might strike them down in combat!

Therefore, the Bodily Rejuvenation Depot might end up being a truly empowering piece of magical technology, but ultimately, all it really does is finally give the humans a partial solution to a problem only they have suffered, bringing their lifespans to the same level as demonkind, monsterkind, and the Volgrim.

However, in addition to the Bodily Rejuvenation Depot, another type of special facility makes itself available! And this one draws huge crowds of not only humans, but members of other species!

Much like how the Belial Booster can boost the ranks of demons from Grunt to Emperor, a series of similar Body Booster facilities spring up, all of them with a slightly different capability, yet ultimately not all that dissimilar to the Belial Booster.

The Body Boosters use magical power to strengthen the metaphysical attributes of those who step inside. Not only can any average man or woman gain super strength, or speed, or perception, but demons and monsters can, as well!

Much like the blood-body strengthening techniques used by the Hell of Blood, Body Boosters allow anyone to obtain permanent bodily alterations to make themselves stronger than the mortal coil would typically allow. However, there are a few downsides to these incredible facilities.

Even with humanity's collective mana rising over time, and slowly funneling into Camael's Cube, the amount of expenditure still dwarfs the amount collected. Jason personally uses the Cube to speed up his own production of high-concept structures, such as the Belial Booster, while the Bodily Rejuvenation Depot itself also pulls from this wellspring of mana to give humans greater life.

On the other hand, the Body Booster facilities do not pull from humanity's collective mana reserves. Instead, like the Belial Booster, they require an individual with strong bodies, minds, or mana to step inside and temporarily drain themselves of their power, transferring it to another individual. Unlike the Belial Booster though, this effect is not only demon-to-demon, but any species to any other species!

Sometimes, Demon Lords will transfer part of their strength to humans.

Other times, Minotaurs will do the same. Or Goblins. Or Harpies.

Of course, weakening oneself for several days or even weeks would ultimately be an act that leaves that individual defenseless. So why would anyone go to such lengths for strangers? Especially, why would demons and monsters purposefully bolster the strength of weak and pathetic humans?!

The answer lays in the system of Merit Tokens.

Conceived by Neil Adams not long after his return, this unique currency becomes the strongest possible method of exchange among all the species situated on Tarus II. All one has to do is perform a service that collectively assists the entirety of humanity, even in a small way, and they will receive Merit Tokens.

For strong individuals, such as high-ranking demons, powerful monsters, and talented humans, the first batches of tokens are all too easy to obtain.

Through the digital Centurion database, thousands of small, medium, and large-scale missions appear, all of them placed by the upper echelons of humanity's leaders. These consist of many different tasks, some immense, others simple.

Constructing housing and other such structures are the most common. Any able-bodied person can contribute to a construction task, earning a portion of the Merit Token reward for themselves. If 200 people construct a building worth 100 Merit Tokens, they each obtain half a token individually. If some people contribute more than the others, they might obtain a larger portion of the share, or even a unique bonus reward, assuming they somehow innovate on the process or come up with unique solutions to the problem. Most especially, if these unique solutions can transfer to other construction projects, these creative types can obtain small but repeated amounts of tokens... similar to royalties from patents!

While Phoebe Hiro might be the smartest human on Tarus II, she is far from omniscient. Through the efforts of many other people, humanity's processes become more refined over time, allowing construction projects to speed up to half and even a quarter of their starting estimates! Buildings that once took two days to make now only require half a day. Of course, if people were to use old-style construction technique and tools, it would take weeks or even months to lay down building foundations, build structural skeletons, construct the walls and ceiling, lay piping and wiring, and other such minutiae. But even with construction drones capable of beaming buildings into existence, they can't construct a city instantly.

Construction is not the only method to obtain Merit Tokens. In fact, one of the most efficient ways non-humans can obtain tokens is by donating their strength through the Belial Booster and Body Booster facilities. By doing so, they can obtain dozens of tokens at once, if not hundreds, depending on how big of a boost they give to other sentient beings.

The most frightening part of these Merit Tokens, conceived by Neil, is how aggressively they begin to make the demons and monsters compete to help bolster humanity's power!

Not only can the tokens be exchanged for necessities and luxuries, but also extremely difficult to obtain items, such as permanent boosts from the Wordsmith himself!

Indeed, while Demon Emperors might not be able to boost each other's power through the Belial Boosters, nor will they genuinely desire to boost one another's body, mind, or soul... they can still exchange these tokens for the ultimate boost! A Word of Power granted by one of the Wordsmiths!

Among all the Hidden Emperors, Fae is the very first one to successfully obtain 1,000 Merit Tokens. She immediately obtains the choice of using these tokens for herself, or her minions.

By exchanging 1,000 Merit Tokens for a boost from the Wordsmith, she can augment her body, mind, or soul to become more formidable! She can enhance her perception speed, her physical strength, or any number of other attributes!

But, she can also spend those points to boost the Dukes, Barons, and Lords of her own Hell.

And this, therein, creates a minor complication. A conundrum which befuddles the Hidden Emperors.

Neil, in his genius, not only created the Merit Token system, but also placed a price on using the coveted Belial Booster. Since the molten energy in Tarus II's core takes a significant amount of time to regenerate each time the Belial Booster activates, demons can only use it once every few days. And since Emperors give much bigger boosts to their subordinates than Dukes or Barons, it becomes obvious that they will want to spend their energy boosting subordinates through the Belial Booster rather than through Body Boosters.

But, thanks to Jason's foresight, the Emperors know that the ones granted first access to the Belial Booster will obviously be those who have curried the most favor with the Wordsmith. Be it hunting down Yama or Mephisto, or who can help rebuild humanity the quickest, this measure proves to be quite subjective. Thus, obtaining 1,000 Merit Tokens is not as easy as it sounds. Some of the Emperors who haven't bootlicked the Wordsmith enough can only reluctantly use the Body Boosters to empower humanity's allies instead of the Belial Booster for their own minions. In this way, they can obtain Merit Tokens too!

Neil's insidious plan allows Jason to do the unthinkable.

He can charge Emperors Merit Tokens for the right to use the Belial Booster!

_That's right! For the low-low price of just 500 Merit Tokens, you can boost your subordinates to the ranks of Baron, Duke, or even Emperor! Ah, but are you first in line? Have you played well with the Wordsmiths and kissed their boots enough? Do you even **have** 500 tokens? You should probably earn enough tokens first before coming here! And hey, why spend 500 Merit Tokens to boost a subordinate when you can spend 1,000 to boost yourself? Keep donating your power to Jason's subordinates through the Body Boosters, that way you'll have enough tokens to fulfill your own ambitions!_

These words, while never outright stated by any of humanity's leaders, still ring in the thoughts of the Hidden Emperors.

Through the power of implications, the people controlling the Belial Boosters and the Merit Boards obtain a small but reliable method of forcing the Emperors to submit to humanity, again and again. Even the most haughty Emperors, such as Gorn, can't help but frown at the realization they're being used.

But even if they are, will they push back?

No!

In truth, while they might be vastly boosting humanity's power more than their own, in the end, these old bastards have remained stagnant in their strength for tens of thousands of years!

Fae fought during the Energy Wars as an Emperor. Aside from the exchanging of different powers through Auger, her own strength hasn't increased even in the slightest for 100,000 years. For her, obtaining more strength through any means, even if only a slight boost, tickles her greed more than any other incentive ever could.

The same is true of all the other Demon Emperors.

A truly rare situation occurs, upending the balance of power in all the Seven Hells. With the exception of Mephisto and Yama, the Hidden Emperors have to engage in a fierce competition between each other to exchange for the benefit of their Hells!

To boost themselves or their subordinates, which option will they take?

Even the greediest Emperors cannot instantly choose. After all, the cost to boost a Duke to an Emperor is half the cost to boost themselves by a marginal amount, but the result is that their Hell may obtain a new Emperor-level powerhouse! This could mean as much as a tenfold boost in power!

Faced with this choice, even Fae can only hesitate, an ugly expression of indecision plastered across her face.

[Dammit. Auger, quit messing with me. I want that boost! The Wordsmith could make me thirty percent tougher and harder to kill. Why should I care about boosting one of our Dukes? They might not even become an Emperor! Then we'll have to spend another 500 Merit Tokens in a few weeks to boost them again! It's better to benefit myself for 1,000 Merit Tokens instead!]

[Don't be so greedy.] Auger transmits telepathically. [The Duke of Contagion is close to the rank of Emperor. I believe that with one boost from you, he could finally evolve. Our Hell is already stronger than the other Hells, with more Emperors than the competitors. But if you waste 1,000 points on yourself, the Hell of Calamity might obtain another Emperor, matching our strength.]

[The Hell of Calamity? Aw, Christ, here we go again!] Fae groans. [Gorn is half-dead. Yumagi got blown to atoms, and even his soul was scattered. Don't forget, even the Orcs are starting to compete for dominance within their Hell. Emperor Serena isn't a factor, and Crow remains the lone powerhouse. Me and Yardrat can match her, no problem! Especially if I get that boost!]

Auger sighs. [We need to stay ahead of the other Hells, Fae. Look at the bigger picture. You were the only Emperor to obtain a free session inside the Belial Booster, and instead, you raised a different Hell's Baron to Duke. With her enhanced abilities, Mara has increased the powers of two humans so far, earning Merit Tokens for herself and the Hell of Calamity. Meanwhile, Yardrat already exchanged 500 Merit Tokens to boost one of our own Dukes. After you do the same, then you can exchange 1,000 for a boost to yourself.]

[Yardrat failed, though!] Fae complains. [He tried boosting the Duke of Hailstorms to the rank of Emperor, but it didn't work! If I use my tokens on the Duke of Contagion and he also fails to advance-]

[Then the next boost will surely raise him.] Auger interrupts. [No more complaints, Fae. If your empowerment fails to uplift the Duke of Contagion, then I will allow you to bolster your own strength in the next round. That is the limit of my magnanimity.]

[Since when did you become my broodfather?] Fae pouts. [Maybe I'll just boost myself, anyway! If you want to blow your wad on some stupid Duke, then get down here and earn your own damn Merit Tokens!]

For a brief moment, Auger remains quiet.

When he next speaks, Fae's protests catch in her throat, and her bones feel a cold chill.

[Emperor of Terror. **Do not forget your position.** You submitted to me. Your powers lay within my grasp. So long as I will it, I can take away **all** of your abilities and leave you a hollow shell. You will do as I command. You will not speak another word of complaint... or if you do, I will find _another_ to take your place. I'm sure there are plenty of Dukes who would be willing to step into the role of the next Emperor of Terror.]

Fae's teeth clench together. A bone-drenching sensation of coldness freezes her in place and forces her to lower her head.

[Y-yeah... of course. Sorry, Auger. I'll, ah... I'll boost the Duke of Contagion, next.]

[Indeed, you will. As for my coming there in person, hmph. You know well why I cannot do that. I will remain here, in solace, as always.]

The mental connection severs, making Fae's eye's flinch.

"...Goddammit. I signed my soul away to that old bastard when I was only a Baron. Now I'm stuck to him for the rest of eternity! What a stupid choice. Wish I could go back in time and slap my dipshit younger self."

After a brief pause, her expression turns gloomy.

"Then again, maybe not. I'd probably never have become an Emperor without Auger's help. I'd have been cast aside during some lame battle against those ancient humans, and that would have been the end of my life. Better to be a lion licking the paws of a Hellhound than a kitten thrown into the meat grinder."

With a long sigh, Fae travels to the Belial Booster facility, exchanges 500 Merit Tokens, and successfully uplifts her subordinate, Dagon, to the rank of Emperor. He evolves from the Duke of Contagion to the Emperor of Pathogens, successfully making himself the third direct Emperor subordinate to Auger. Of course, this does not include the secretive and insane Stitched Emperor, Glinch, but Auger never considered him anything more than a final defense for if the Fourth Hell came under attack.

At the same time as Fae successfully raises the first Duke to the rank of Emperor since the Hell and Humanity Alliance formed, a different Emperor begrudgingly enters the Body Booster facility, where he finds a dark-skinned human male waiting for him.

"Emperor Kristoff." The man says, appraising him carefully. "I have 250 Merit Tokens with your name on them."

The Emperor of Thirst glances at the man before him. "It's my third time boosting a human. What's your name?"

"Lieutenant Samuel." The man answers. "I've been saving up my tokens for a while now. I'm looking forward to this."

Samuel exposes a white, toothy smile. Despite his hatred of demons, the thought that he can exchange a mere 250 Merit Tokens for a powerup from a Demon Emperor fills him with a sense of indescribable satisfaction. Of course, he could also exchange only 100 tokens for a Duke-level powerup, or 50 from a Baron, or even 10 from a Lord, but why would he do that? Those boosts might make him relatively strong, but compared to the power a single Emperor can give him, a power which he might someday use to exact his revenge, he would much rather obtain a truly legendary amount of strength.

"Are you sure you can handle what I'm offering?" Kristoff asks. "That other woman... Corporal Hurent... she suffered some injuries and had to pay a visit to the hospital to fix herself up. Something about her bones not being able to handle the swelling of her muscles."

"No pain, no gain." Samuel replies, his voice calm. "Do you want my tokens, or not?"

Kristoff snorts. "Obtaining strength similar to mine for a few measly tokens. Without the Wordsmith, you humans would never be able to obtain such a boon, not even if you poured all of your blood and organs onto the dirt."

"Strength is only a means to an end." Samuel says. "That's the problem with you demons. You pursue strength just for the sake of ruling with an iron fist. But I'm not like you. I pursue my own goals. I actually have a reason for my existence."

Kristoff doesn't miss the gleam of hatred in Samuel's eyes.

"Let me guess. You lost someone important because of my people. And now, in a delicious twist of irony, your chance of obtaining vengeance comes by borrowing the powers of your enemies. Fate is full of all sorts of surprises."

Samuel remains quiet. He gestures to the two power-exchange pods.

"We done bullshitting? Let's get this over with. I can't stand jabbering with demons."

"Suit yourself." Kristoff replies. The polite and elegant-looking Emperor steps into the pod on the left, while Samuel enters the one on the right.

Not long after, they both step out, with Kristoff looking half-dead, while Samuel appears exactly the opposite. He grins viciously and pumps his muscles, bellowing at the sky.

"It's Samuel's time to shine!" The Lieutenant roars, before storming out of the building past a crowd of gleeful onlookers.

After he leaves, Kristoff sighs. The Emperor of Thirst glances at his own Merit Token reserves, which have finally reached 1,000 points, but only feels a sense of gloominess in his heart.

"...Oh, Fenrir. How I miss you."
