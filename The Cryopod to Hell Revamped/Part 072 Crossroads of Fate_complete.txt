Two combatants faced each other on an empty battlefield. After scorching away every trace of life for several miles in every direction, Beelzebub's attack had obliterated a circular cavern the size of a small city, giving Hope and Uriel plenty of space to move around.

The Archangel wove a dozen hand signs, causing a chunk of the ground behind her to jump into the air. Fragments shot from it rapid-fire and sailed at Hope like an earthen machine gun.

Hope dived to the side, allowing Uriel's dirt bullets to miss him by an inch. "Centurion! I could use a little help, here!"

_EVALUATION OF THE ENEMY COMBATANT REQUIRED. PLEASE CONTINUE TO FIGHT SO THAT CENTURION'S ANALYSIS SYSTEMS CAN DEVELOP A STRATEGY TO COUNTER THEIR ATTACK._ Centurion announced.

"I don't have time for that!" Hope aimed his palm at Uriel. "**Freeze!**"

A wall of ice instantly enveloped the Archangel. Hope dashed toward her, but the Archangel shattered her restraints instantly, causing the Wordsmith to retreat. Uriel punched her broken prison, sending a hundred icy shards flying at Hope. The Wordsmith tucked his face between his arms, allowing the ice bullets to explode against his armored limbs.

"That backfired," Hope grunted.

Uriel's light-form angels flew into the air. The female opened her head again and began firing holy energy at Hope, tracking his movements as he ducked and dodged around. The other angel summoned a bow and arrow, then flew around to Hope's backside, flanking him in the process.

_WARNING, PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR! THE ENEMY HAS SURROUNDED US!_ Centurion warned.

"No shit!"

Hope aimed his mind at Unit Two while still dodging her attacks. He grabbed her with telekinesis and yanked hard. She lurched downward and piledrove into the ground, breaking several limbs in the process.

Unit One aimed an arrow and fired. It left the bow at twice the speed of sound and struck Hope square in the back, blowing the wind out of his body and sending him sprawling in the dirt.

_MINOR DAMAGE SUSTAINED, PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR! BEWARE OF ATTACKS FROM THE REAR!_ Centurion warned.

Hope coughed violently. "Kah... n-no kidding. You think?!"

His eyes shifted again. A graphic interface popped up, highlighting several areas on Uriel's body. _PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR, I HAVE LOCATED SEVERAL STRESS POINTS ON THE PRIMARY TARGET! STRIKE THEM TO CAUSE SEVERE DAMAGE!_ Centurion announced.

"Finally, something useful."

Hope spun on his heel and batted another of Unit One's arrows out of the air, but he hit it in such a way that it bounced off his hand and slammed into Uriel's chest. The Archangel tripped and fell on her rear.

"Argh! Unit One! Don't strike me, imbecile!"

Hope teleported atop the Archangel's shoulders, making her jerk her head back in surprise. "What-?!"

The Wordsmith stabbed his armored hand down and plunged it deep into her shoulder. "**EXPLODE!**"

Hope's hand detonated with the force of a stick of dynamite, blasting him up into the air. He careened around wildly and smashed onto his face, twenty feet away.

_WARNING! MINOR DAMAGE SUFFERED! DEPLOYING NANITES TO EXPEDITE THE RECOVERY PROCESS!_ Centurion announced.

"Thangs, Senshureeon," Hope drawled. It took him a moment to shake off the disorientation from his unexpected flight. He clawed himself to his feet a moment later and scowled at the Archangel as she stood up and dusted herself off at the same time.

"Thou art an innovative opponent, Wordsmith. I forgot short-range teleportation was one of thy abilities."

Uriel's severed arm, while gruesome, only required a few moments to heal. Hope watched silently as drops of water fell away, and a new arm regrew itself.

_Damn. Uriel can regenerate her wounds using water magic. If I'm not mistaken, that means she has control over light, metal, fire, water, and earth. Ridiculous. I thought most magic users specialized in one or two elements!_ Hope thought.

Centurion replied. _THE ARCHANGEL-TYPE ENEMY POSSESSES A WIDE VARIETY OF ATTACKS AND DEFENSES. IT IS A FORMIDABLE OPPONENT._ Centurion said.

_Yeah, thanks for that, Captain Obvious._ Hope thought.

Uriel narrowed her eyes, sending more pieces of metal flying at Hope. He swatted them out of the air, batting them away as if he were a pro-Badminton player. "I'm _so_ sick of your bullshit!"

Hope teleported again, this time in front of her. Uriel lunged her hand to grab at his neck, but Hope teleported behind her, making her miss. He slapped his palm against her back. "**SEAL!**"

The Archangel hopped away. "Had ye chosen to listen, I wouldn't have to strike ye dead!" Her eyes darted to more metal on the ground, then back to Hope.

Nothing happened.

Hope took two steps back and narrowed his eyes. "Give me your best shot."

The Archangel paused for a moment, then formed a hand sign.

Nothing happened.

"My magic. Why can't I use it? My reserves shouldn't be anywhere close to empty."

A smile crept over Hope's face. "That's what I thought. It only occurred to me a few seconds ago, but I should be able to disable the magic powers of others. You're defenseless."

Uriel stiffened. A bead of sweat trickled down her face. "Shut up, Artorias. This development was _not_ within thy predictions."

Hope cocked his head. "My name is Hope, and I don't recall predicting anything."

"Imbecile, I am not referring to thee," Uriel said. She waved a finger, summoning Unit One to her side, his bow and arrow held at the ready. Unit Two limped toward Uriel as well. The construct motioned with her hands and healed herself, repairing her broken limbs within a few seconds.

Hope exhaled. "Now that I've taken out your magic, you won't be any trouble at all. How about I beat some sense into you? After what you did to Samantha, I won't forgive you easily."

"Thou art amusing. I need not use magic to defeat thee, Wordsmith." Uriel glanced at Unit One. "Summon two spears and pass them to me."

Unit One nodded. His bow vanished, and two spears appeared, one in each hand. He gave them to Uriel, and she expertly spun around, twirling each one like an extension of her body.

"I held off a hundred thousand bloodskins all by myself during the War in Heaven. My mighty spears, Gungnir and Gae Bolg, were weapons crafted by Camael, the Archangel of Divination. While I no longer possess my artifacts of power, fake weapons like these will still be enough to display my combat prowess."

"Camael? Never heard of him."

"_Her._ Camael was my wise sister, the one who gave every Archangel and Hero their soul-bound weapons and items. Perhaps, had thou not evolved into a demon worshipper, ye might possess an artifact of thy own."

Hope shrugged. "Technically, Jason has Solomon's Crown."

The Archangel gurgled unpleasantly, a sound vaguely reminiscent of a laugh. "Were my sister to recommend an artifact for thee, I'd suggest a shovel. That way, I could dig ye a grave!"

Uriel lunged at Hope with frightening speed. Both of her Pseudo-angels flew into the air, getting distance from their creator.

Hope dodged the first spear, but his reflexes weren't fast enough to duck the second. Uriel stabbed his stomach with her left-hand spear, then lifted him into the air and slammed him into the ground as if he were a hammer.

_WARNING, PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR! MODERATE DAMAGE SUSTAIN-_ Centurion began.

_I know!!_ Hope thought.

Hope's eyes widened. One of Uriel's spears lunged at his mouth as he lay on the ground. He flinched and jerked his head to the side. The spear stabbed the ground beside him, rattling his teeth from the force of the blow.

Hope bellowed, "**PUSH!!**"

A wave of energy surged from his body, throwing the Archangel a few feet into the air, just long enough for Hope to press a foot into the dirt and kick away, then backflip off his hands into a standing position.

He wheezed painfully. _Uriel's movements are totally different from before! I thought she was a mage-type, but she's a warrior-type too! How can Barbatos's giant body move like that?!_ Hope thought.

The moment the Archangel hit the ground, she jumped at him again, raising both spears above her head to slam against his body.

"**BARRIER!**" Hope yelled again, creating a wall in front of himself, anything to get a little breathing room. To Hope's surprise, Uriel stabbed the wall with a spear and vaulted over it, flinging herself into the air like a bat outta hell.

_Christ's sake! Did Uriel invent parkour? She's ridiculous!_ Hope thought.

Uriel fell toward Hope again. This time he stepped deftly to the side and slapped Uriel's spear away, catching her off-balance and making her slam onto her shoulder.

"**GRAVITY!**"

Hope yelled again, increasing the gravity around her body by a factor of ten. The Archangel shook wooziness from her head and climbed to her feet. Her movements slowed dramatically.

"Haah... thou art a worthy opponent, little Hero. I applaud thy improvisation skills, at the least."

Hope retreated several steps. "Yeah? Well, you're, uh... really good with spears!"

_EXCELLENT RETORT, PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR._ Centurion said.

_Oh, shut up, Centurion._ Hope thought.

Several minutes passed.

The Archangel slowly breathed, as did Hope. Both of them eyed each other, sizing up their conditions.

Uriel was the first to break the silence. "When I gaze upon thee, I cannot help but see the mirror image of King Arthur. He was a valiant warrior and the mightiest servant of the gods. Tis' a shame that thou will not change thy mind and abandon thy demonic comrades. Were ye to do so, I could see thy feats eventually eclipsing the Hero King's."

Hope pointed at the Archangel. "I don't fight because it's my duty or because people tell me I must. I do it because I want to protect those I love. You expect me to commit genocide for a matter unrelated to my life. As long as that is true, I will resist."

"Hmph. Thou mayest think my methods barbaric, but tis' far from the truth. Perhaps ye believe slaughtering trillions of bloodskins to be an unspeakable atrocity?"

Hope nodded. "An atrocity. Yeah. That's a pleasant way of phrasing the issue."

"Yet thou dost ignore the billions of humans and angels slaughtered during the Energy Wars. Thy decision is based on ignorance. Undoubtedly, thou must not know the lengths demonkind went to, to eliminate the humans and angels."

Hope shook his head. "I don't know the details or the number of lives lost, no. It was a war, though. People die during wars. That's why I want to prevent another one. Besides, demons didn't kill us all. Millions and millions of humans are still alive today. You're proposing something far more extreme than what they did."

Uriel scowled. Her eyes turned into slivers of red beneath her black helmet. "Thou dost believe that the demons did not kill all of thy people, so inevitably, the demons spared humanity out of pity? Nay. The demon leaders were eager to wipe out not only the angels but the humans as well. 'Twas only due to the actions of several key figures that humanity still lived on today."

Uriel continued. "I lived inside this Duke's mind when the great purges occurred. The mind wipes — the cullings. The cowardly human leaders sought out only their own benefits. They sold out the rest of their species to become soul food for the bloodskins. They agreed to imprison their fellow man and woman across a hundred planets. They cozied themselves up inside of ivory towers of riches and opulence and left the other ninety-nine percent of their people to rot under bloodskin rule."

The Archangel lowered her eyes and shook her head. "Thy people only continue to walk the cosmos because they hold some value for the demon leaders. Souls give demons power. The more of thy people that live, the more power the demons will amass. Thou art little more than food in their eyes. They turn thee into spiritual energy so that they can become Lords, Barons, Dukes, and Emperors."

Several moments passed. Hope swallowed. "You're talking about... the River Styx."

"Aye. Angels served no purpose to the bloodskins. They killed my people off without batting an eye. The only reason the fiends have yet to rain down upon your pathetic fortress is that they have yet to formulate an efficient method of breaking thy spirit. Eventually, they will. You will all perish, and they will enslave the remnants once more. Is that the future you want?"

Hope hesitated. A thousand questions appeared in his mind. "Well... no, but I still don't think you're going about this the right way."

The Archangel nodded. "Perhaps not. However, my time is limited. Right now, Barbatos slumbers within me. While he rests, I control his overwhelming power. I must strike a blow, hard and fast, against the filthy bloodskins. I must wipe out their upper ranks, then work my way down. I don't believe I can kill them all, but without leaders, they will present less of a threat to humanity. I must beseech thee to assist me. Failing that... stand aside. Allow me to complete my task at hand."

Hope met the Archangel's gaze. "You want me to let you go? To allow you to kill them?"

"Aye. Thou need not draw blood, but simply allow me to do so in thy stead. I see the sin trapped within thy gaze. Thou hath done something similar before. Repeat that which ye already know, and soon, the demons will become little more than a faint nightmare in thy past."

"Uriel..." Hope trailed off. His throat dried out as he considered her words.

_I didn't kill the orcs on Maiura. I only made them kill each other. However, even if I didn't plunge a knife into each one's gut, the result was the same. After everything the demons have done... after all the things Neil has told me... is Uriel correct? Is she right?_ Hope thought.

Hope relaxed his posture. He aimed his mind at Uriel. "**Normalize.**"

The gravity around Uriel returned to normal, and her powers returned as well.

"I'm sorry, Uriel. I can't help you. I won't kill if it isn't necessary... but at the same time, I can't deny that you have a point. I'll let you do what you must. Just stay away from my friends. Samantha is not your enemy."

The Archangel evaluated him for a moment. "I apologize, Wordsmith. I misjudged thee. Thou may presently lack some modicum of resolve, but the look of a great Hero rests within thy gaze. As you request, I will accept thy terms. I will not kill Belial, the Second Emperor, unless she gets in my way. However, I have my own request to beg of thee."

"Oh yeah? And what's that?"

Uriel stabbed a finger at Hope. "The Temptress of man must be watched by you at all times. She is dangerous, vicious, and a psychopathic monster capable of inflicting incredible harm upon thy resistance. I care not if thou believe she hath turned over a new leaf. I know well the extent of Belial's treachery. Have ye yet witnessed her transformation powers? Her ability to perfectly mimic anyone she wishes?"

"No. I haven't. So, what, Samantha is a shapeshifter or something?"

"Indeed. Belial might be called the Emperor of Passion by her fellow bloodskins, but in my lifetime, we referred to her by another title. _The Great Deceiver._ Her ability to infiltrate and destroy humans and angels from the inside was unparalleled. The Titans conducted regular drills to determine if she were in their midst. She single-handedly slew hundreds of angels via backstabbing and betrayal."

Uriel stomped toward the human and loomed over him. "I will leave the filthy worm alive... but thou must always keep thy gaze trained on her. All bloodskins are equally evil, but some are worse than others."

Hope nodded. "Fine. I promise."

"Good," Uriel said with a smile.

Hope turned his back to her and walked a few feet away. The Archangel held up her hand.

"Wait. I would ask one last favor of thee before ye return to thy people. Thou need not accept if ye find my request troublesome."

Hope tilted his head back. "Oh? What is it?"

"The bloodskins bar entry to Hell Harbor via powerful magic. Beelzebub's attack obliterated Grigori, Diablo's personal Warper. Finding another Keymaster could prove troublesome for me, especially after Beelzebub's attack. Might I trouble thee to open a gate to Hell Harbor via thy Wordsmithing?"

Hope frowned. He paused for a moment to consider Uriel's request.

"Hell Harbor? I don't know what that place is. I won't be any help."

Uriel touched her chest. "I will provide thee with the planet's location. Use thy magic to search my memories."

"Err... I don't know if I can... do that..." Hope trailed off. He turned back to Uriel, a look of curiosity on his face. "...But I suppose I can try."

...

Several seconds later, the two of them stood two feet apart. Hope pressed his hand against the Duke's patchy white and red chest. "**Remember.**"

Immediately, images of an unfamiliar world danced in Hope's mind. Demons and monsters living together in harmony. Beautiful fields, clouds, an off-color sky...

_Found it._ Hope thought.

The vision shifted. Hell Harbor vanished, and something else replaced it.

A different memory. Not something from Barbatos, but a memory from Uriel.

"No, stop. Not that one." Uriel's body twitched. "Don't... don't look at it. Don't look!"

Hope tried to pull away. His body refused.

"I... I can't! Th-this emotion! It's so raw! It's-"

The world shifted around Hope. Uriel's memory, an awful nightmare from her past, materialized around him physically, almost as if he were living it himself.

A series of words appeared in his mind.

_The War in Heaven._
