Hope frowned in annoyance. _The heater in Neil's SUV isn't working. It's freezing cold outside._ Hope thought.

Neil and Hope drove toward Meldora, the city on Maiura Hope had liberated not even a week ago, and the harsh bite of a snowstorm chewed through the rig's steel-clad exterior, chilling the inside of the car. Maiura's weather cycle could be described as chaotic at best, and already they were experiencing its worst days.

Hope rubbed his arm as he drove. Centurion spoke to him inside his mind. _PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR, YOUR BODILY TEMPERATURE IS FOUR DEGREES BELOW OPTIMAL LEVELS. WOULD YOU LIKE ME TO REDUCE MY THERMAL DAMPENING?_

Hope spoke out loud. "You can do that?"

_AFFIRMATIVE._

"Don't bother. **Warm.**"

The temperature in the car increased noticeably. Neil glanced at Hope and shivered. "Who are you talking to?"

"It's a long story, Neil. You interrogated Samantha upon her return, right?"

"Mhm."

"Well, I was wearing Phoebe's new nanosuit when Beelzebub detonated his body. I don't know exactly what happened, but the radiation must have embedded the suit's nanites into my bloodstream. I can hear Centurion in my mind."

Neil pursed his lips. "I see. So the reason your shoulder felt like steel was..."

"The nanites hardened my skin. It's as if I'm always wearing the suit. It gave me a bunch of other perks too. The downside is that I had to suffer radiation poisoning to wear it."

Centurion replied. _NEGATIVE. THIS CENTURION UNIT HAS SCRUBBED ALL CANCEROUS CELLS FROM PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR'S BODY. PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR IS RUNNING AT 100% EFFICIENCY._

Hope blinked. "Uh. Never mind."

"Never mind, what?"

"Centurion healed me. I thought I'd need Jason and the Crown to purge the radiation from my body. It's my lucky day."

Neil loosened his suit collar. "So you say. How are you holding up? I mean, you fought an Archangel. That couldn't have been easy."

"It wasn't. Uriel nearly beat me several times. I stripped her powers away with my Wordsmithing, and even then, her melee combat skills were ridiculous."

"You can do that? Remove someone's magic?"

"Apparently."

A moment passed. Neil glanced out the window at the sea of snow surrounding them. The capital city of Meldora loomed in the distance. It was a tiny village compared to Hero City, but still the most populated settlement on Maiura. The torches at its gates signaled the watchful eye of its few remaining soldiers.

"Have you told Jason about this newfound aspect of Wordsmithing?"

"Nope. I wanted to talk to you first."

"Good. That's very good." Neil said. He eased back in his seat and smiled. "You're advancing quickly. It wasn't long ago that Jason barely held his own against the First Emperor of the demons and the Black Queen, and now you're beating an Archangel without any help."

"Technically, Samantha was there. She did a bit of damage."

"Sure, but you sent her away to face the enemy alone. That was brave of you."

Hope hesitated. A few thoughts rustled under the surface, making him wonder how he should respond.

"I only wanted to protect Belial. She might be an Emperor, but the matchup against Uriel's powers was uneven."

"Protecting those weaker than you is admirable as well."

"Thanks."

"Mhm."

Meldora drew ever closer. Soon it was only half a mile away. Hope could faintly make out the torches of men standing in the snow, peering at their vehicle.

"I let Uriel go."

"Oh? Why?"

"She showed me a vision. An image of the past. Something revolving around the demons and how they attacked Heaven. I believe her grievances were... legitimate."

Neil stared ahead stonily. "Do you think you did the right thing by walking away?"

"Kinda. I don't know. I even opened a portal to Hell Harbor to give her access to the demon homeworld. I didn't know that was even a place until she told me."

"Why didn't you go with her if you thought she was doing the right thing?"

The city gate came into view, and Meldora's soldiers barely gave them a once-over before letting them through. The poor men were freezing alive. As they drove past, Hope spoke a word of power to warm their bones and dry their clothes. The feeling might not last long, but it was the least he could do.

Hope drove further into the city and slowed to a crawl as he scanned for people walking in the road. Given the hellish sleet falling from the sky, he wasn't surprised by the empty streets as far as the eye could see.

"Like I said. Uriel's grievance seems legitimate... but it isn't mine. The demons didn't do those awful things to me."

Neil's voice softened. "But they did. By annihilating the angels and gods, the demons won the war and enslaved humanity. I think you should have helped her kill as many of the bastards as humanly possible. It's only my opinion, though."

"I don't like killing, Neil. Not unless I have to."

"So you say. Ah, right there. That building." Neil gestured toward a wooden structure covered in snow. Smoke belched from a chimney above, while light shined through the windows. "That's the place."

"Doesn't look like much. Pretty small."

"Yeah, you're not wrong. However, after all the killing those orcs did, Meldora is a bit of a ghost town. We'll have lots of privacy inside."

Neil and Hope hopped out, and the bitter cold plunged through Hope's veins like a hundred daggers. Wind beat his face, and snow battered their car. 

Hope shouted over the wind. "Christ! Why couldn't you have picked a pub in a nice, warm, tropical location?!"

"Haha! Because everyone would be there!" Neil replied.

Hope ignored Centurion's second offer to warm his body. The ten feet to the door took longer than he'd like, and by the time they stepped inside, his boots were already waterlogged.

Neil slammed the door shut behind them. The pub was empty, save one man sitting at the counter, his legs propped up on the stool beside him. He looked at them as they entered. "Oh? Does ye need a drink in this storm? Be ye humans or demons, laddies?"

"Human. Very, very human," Neil said.

"Take a seat then. I'll have the missus pop over in a jiffy."

"Sure."

Neil gestured to a spot off in the corner. The warmth of the fireplace next to the booth felt amazing. As soon as they sat down, Hope Wordsmiths for Neil and himself. "**Dry. Warm. Clean.**"

Their clothes and bodies flashed clean themselves. Neil glanced at his boots, which were now in mint condition. "Damn. Not bad. I could get used to this."

"I find my magic hard to use, sometimes. Too many possibilities. The thought of all the effects my lips can unleash makes my head spin."

"Too many choices?"

"Exactly. I suffer from choice paralysis. There are so many ways I can Wordsmith that I can't wrap my mind around _what_ to say."

"You thought of taking Uriel's powers when you fought her. That was clever."

Hope's gaze wandered to a window behind Neil. From inside the pub, the snow looked almost pleasant, like something he'd see on a Christmas card back home.

"Sure, but I feel stupid for not thinking of it sooner. Or at least, for Jason not thinking of such a thing. It would have made battling Satan and his minions much easier."

"To say the least," Neil replied. "If you remove a demon's magic, they should become weak enough that even a slightly enhanced human can fight them on equal terms. Still, you shouldn't beat yourself up over it. Divorce yourself from Jason's actions and yours. You're two different people who do things uniquely."

"I keep telling myself that."

Both of them sat in silence for a few moments. Eventually, Hope broke the ice. "Neil. Do you remember how I told you I let Uriel go? How she showed me a vision of the past?"

Neil barely reacted. "Sure. What about it?"

"Have you ever heard of the War in Heaven?"

"I have not."

"Me neither. It seems to have been a huge event." Hope rubbed his chin thoughtfully. "The demons launched an assault on the angels and gods. Uriel was the frontline soldier. She slew tens of thousands of demons and prevented them from entering a gate to Heaven, one similar to our warp-gates in the Core."

Neil smiled. "She sounds like a brave warrior."

"From what I saw, she was. Uriel stood against the horde all by herself. However... someone attacked her from behind. They, uh, stabbed her in the back and allowed the demons to pour through."

"Who?"

Hope didn't hesitate. "Samantha. She's the one responsible for the demons killing off the angels."

Neil stared at Hope in surprise. "I expected you to say many names... but now I don't know what to think. That succubus was the backstabber?"

"Yeah. She's a shape-shifter. She can mimic anyone's appearance. I never realized. How can we trust someone like that?"

Before Neil could reply, the bar owner's wife came over and plunked down two fat mugs of beer. She smiled sweetly at Neil, but he ignored her and continued to stare at Hope incredulously. The lady rolled her eyes and walked away.

The commander shoved one of the beers at Hope. "Here. You need one of these."

"Yeah. This time, at least. Christ. How am I going to tell Jason? She stuck the knife in Uriel's back and twisted. Trusting a demon is hard enough, but a shape-shifting succubus?"

Neil sipped from his mug and averted his eyes from Hope's. "Succubi can manipulate the emotions and feelings of the opposite sex. It's like I warned you, Hope. She's twice as dangerous now that you've told me about her past. On top of manipulating my men, she could be plotting to kill me and take over my position. Or you. Or Jason. Hell, even Phoebe."

"I don't know if I'd go that far."

Neil's eyes lowered to stare at the table. "I know what you're thinking. People can change. Hell, even demons can change. Look at Paimon. Until that bastard, Lorvos, gutted him, Paimon was our guy. I wouldn't want to suspect the demon female just because of some vision from an Archangel."

"Right. That's my point. Think of how devastated Phoebe and Jason would feel if we just walked up and said, 'Yo, we think your succubus friend is plotting to take over the Core.' They'd call us conspiracy theorists."

"Mmm." Neil and Hope paused to take a drink. The moment the liquid hit Hope's tongue, a gagging reflex started to take hold. Centurion yelled in his mind. _WARNING! INVOLUNTARY EXPULSION REFLEX DETECTED! LIMITING THE EFFECTS OF TOXIC SUBSTANCE K3-11248. TOXIN NEUTRALIZED! NO ILL EFFECTS INCURRED!_

Hope stared at the beer silently. It tasted like water now. _Thanks, Centurion._ Hope thought.

Neil set his glass down and smiled at Hope's half-empty cup. "By the way, I know you were a little depressed at the wedding. It was all over your face."

Hope waved his concerns away. "Aw, Neil. It's no big deal. I'm happy for Jason and Phoebe. They deserve each other."

"Sure, sure. But you need to think about yourself. Did I ever introduce you to my assistant, Debra? She sometimes pops over at the Rust Bucket."

"Uh, I dunno. Maybe once or twice."

"Great! Well, she has this cute sister. Single. Why don't you let me introduce you to her sometime? I think you'll like her. Looks a bit like Amelia. Might help you get over your loss."

Hope released a long-held breath. "I'm fine, Neil. Thanks. I don't need a woman in my life right now."

"Shut up, you terrible liar, you." Neil laughed, shaking his head. "We ought to play poker sometime. I'd never lose to someone with such a poor poker-face."

"Don't be so sure," Hope countered. "I can always give myself a royal flush with Wordsmithing."

"Heh, _right_. If cheating is allowed, I'll have a look at your cards to even the playing field."

They both laughed at his little joke. Afterward, Hope finished off his beer in one big gulp. Centurion told him it had nullified the alcohol content, so it appeared Hope couldn't get drunk anymore. That was either good or bad, but he didn't know which.

Neil set his empty mug down. "Hope, I have to ask you... where do you think we should go from here?"

"I don't follow."

"The demons. They run everything. Holing up in the Core won't last us forever. If your suspicions about the succubus are right, then for all we know, the demons could be plotting to take us out in our sleep. We won't know what hit us."

Hope rubbed his chin thoughtfully. "If that was their intention, why wouldn't Samantha have finished us off after Beelzebub's attack?"

"I considered that very thought. But remember, the goal of the demons isn't to kill humans. They need to make an example out of us. We're supposed to be their food and their source of soul energy. If Samantha- well, if Belial decided to kill us, it wouldn't prevent more humans from eventually rising to fight again."

"Sure. But the only reason humans can combat the demons is that Jason freed the best and brightest from Bahamut. He made the weaponry we use to stand half a chance against our oppressors. If she kills him and me, then..."

Suddenly something clicked in Hope's mind.

"Holy shit, Neil. I just realized. If Jason and I die, humanity is doomed. That's not conceit speaking. Without our Wordsmithing, humans can't fight back against the demons."

Neil waggled a finger. "Not true. We can reach a point where humans have the technological prowess to stand against our enemies without assistance. The problem is that while the demons have gained power over a hundred thousand years, we've lost it. It will take a great deal of time to restore a sense of balance between the sides."

"I guess so. I'm not trying to make myself sound ultra-important, but I feel like the demons are going to target us, the Wordsmiths, eventually. If that happens, humanity loses."

"You're not wrong. That will certainly be the case for the forseeable-"

Neil paused. He frowned and reached into his pocket to pull out a palm-sized square gadget. The little black box had a screen on it, and reminded Hope of something from Earth...

Ah. It was a pager. Err, why would Neil have one of those?

The commander glanced at the small display on the front. "Son of a bitch. We finally sit down to talk, and someone messages me from camp. I guess we have to go."

"Is that a pager?"

"No clue. I asked Jason to make a device that would allow people to reach me anywhere. He created this. It uses magic to receive short messages."

"So... it's a magical pager."

"I suppose."

Sighing, Hope climbed out of the booth. "Well, okay, then. Nothing to do about it. Let's go see what they want."

Henry and Jason leaned forward and stared at the screen. A video began to play. The quality was abysmal, with artifacts and glitches present. It was in color, but file degradation had rendered a lot of the information difficult to see.

"**Repair.**"

Jason spoke a word, and instantly the screen cleared up.

The scene appeared to be a barren, rocky planet. Several massive skyscrapers loomed in the distance, but their designs were unlike anything Jason had seen before, made mostly of glass and metal. They twisted into the sky and formed several ornate shapes. Very different from the tall rectangles on Earth.

A thousand demons stood, weapons and claws at the ready. Scythers, shadow-walkers, and a dozen other types all stood in a military formation and prepared to attack.

One demon Jason didn't recognize stood in the front. He was a fellow with some facial hair and a nasty scar across his eye, and judging by his horns, he must have been a Baron, possibly a Duke. He pointed toward a solitary hooded figure. "I've heard enough from you, paleskin. I won't allow my people to stay and cower in the Labyrinth for all eternity! I won't allow you to banish us so that you can take over the galaxy for yourselves!"

The figure's hooded face was impossible to make out. However, beneath his hood, Jason spotted six faintly luminous eyes, arranged vertically in two rows across what must have been his head. 

The hooded figure 'spoke,' but his voice seemed to come from everywhere, and not from the place he'd expect his mouth to be.

[Yuia knugh sha 'iaftat, ganun. Wa ha'a a s'aasx.]

The crown translated for Jason.

_You know the rules, demon. We have a treaty._

The young demon took a menacing step toward the mysterious figure. "I don't give a shit about your treaty. You lied to us! You gave us a few planets as a pittance, and kept the rest of the galaxy to yourselves! The demons and Volgrim were supposed to be allies! Equals!"

[Duat Duaftftu knugh aftuias shut? Aftuias xuia'... untiaftu'gunasuun?]

_Does Diablo know about this? About your... insubordination?_

"We don't care about Diablo, do we, Lempo? Besides. Diablo agreed to the treaty without consulting the rest of our people. We want to rewrite the terms!"

_The terms were fair. You have no say in the matter. I will forgive this breach of ethics... but only once. Leave._
