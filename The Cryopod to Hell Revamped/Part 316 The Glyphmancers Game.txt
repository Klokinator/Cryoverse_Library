Part 316: The Glyphmancer's Game

_Hell Harbor, at the epicenter of a scorched battlefield._

Emperor Yumagi, the Glyphmancer, levitates off the ground. He slowly floats up into the sky, facing across from Archangel Uriel and her two construct-brothers. Multiple beads of light shine on his forehead, each one telling the tale of a pre-activated Tarot Card augmenting his abilities... or perhaps waiting to be cast.

"My, my, my..." Yumagi says, as he sweeps his eyes around the ravaged world. "Look at all the damage you've done in just twenty short minutes. You angels are all so uncivilized."

Uriel points one of her light-spears at Yumagi. "Are any others of thy kind coming? I have been hoping to see more bloodskin Emperors, yet they continue to hide behind trees or lurk in the shadows. And now, one imbecile actually dares to face three Archangels alone. I know not whether thou art bold, arrogant or foolhardy, but I suspect a combination of all three."

Yumagi gently runs his fingers through his pretty white hair. "To defeat a few half-dead Archangels, I alone am enough. Of course, three against one is hardly a fair battle, so why don't we start by laying a few ground rules, hm?"

Suddenly, he pulls his hand away from his hair and flicks his fingers, causing a Platinum-colored Tarot Card to appear in his grasp. This movement, as if he had pulled it from his ear, gives the technique a street magician feeling, catching Uriel and her brothers off-guard.

"Pharaoh's Insight, **Evoke!**"

A flash of light ignites on Yumagi's forehead. His jewelry trembles for a moment before a sphere of golden light explodes from his body and rockets outward in a huge radius, traveling all directions at once. In the blink of an eye, it covers several miles of Hell Harbor's surface, causing every demon, angel, vampire, and monster within its radius to flinch reflexively.

Once the light fades away, Uriel frowns. She looks left and right, yet notices no obvious changes. "What...?"

Michael appears just as confused, but Raphael doesn't. The Archangel of Wisdom's expression becomes ugly.

"Soul shackles! That demon placed some sort of restriction on our souls!"

Yumagi smiles. "Indeed, I did. I don't like an unfair battle, you see, so I've placed some limitations on our battle. Don't worry, unlike you unruly _pigeons_, this Emperor is an impartial demon who only fights fairly."

Uriel gnashes her teeth. "A lie if I've ever heard one! Ahhh!"

She rockets toward Yumagi and stabs her lances at his chest, but surprisingly, Yumagi doesn't move at all. He merely looks away and winces, tensing his body up as those holy energy-laden spears impale his stomach and tear through his back.

"Aaargh!"

Yumagi falls away from the spears and plummets toward the ground, where he hits it with a _bang!_ The other Emperors gasp in horror as Uriel raises her spears once again.

"That stupid imbecile!" Emperor Fae yells. "What is Yumagi doing, playing games with an Archangel? She'll rip him apart!"

Uriel lifts her spears above her head and prepares to charge at Yumagi again, but unexpectedly, her body remains rooted in place. She continues to hold her spears high, but for some reason, no matter how hard she tries, she fails to move.

"Nnn! What in Heaven's name?! Brother! I can't move! Something's wrong!"

Yumagi, laying on the ground, stands up and clutches the bloody wound on his stomach.

"Cough... cough... yes, that's right, Archangel. You didn't let me speak the rules first, so now you've trapped yourself until I make my move. Tsk, tsk. You truly are a moronic blowhard."

After shooting Uriel a sly grin, Yumagi touches one of the glowing red dots on his forehead.

"You attacked me, and I didn't defend. I activate my trap card, Bedeviled Naga!"

A blur of blackened, shadowy demonic power engulfs Yumagi. Instantly, his grievous wounds heal, restoring him back to pristine condition. At the same time, one of the glowing dots on his forehead fades away, leaving him with a dozen others.

Uriel fights with all her strength, but her body doesn't move an inch. No matter how hard she tries, she fails to lower her arms and remains stuck with her spear held high.

"What devilry is this?" Uriel asks, clearly shocked by whatever hex Yumagi has cast upon her. "It cannot hold me forever, bloodskin! My Archangel soul is far stronger than thine!"

Yumagi chuckles. He levitates into the air once more, taking up a position some fifty feet in front of Uriel.

"Hehehe. Yes, indeed, your soul is quite powerful. But the Pharaoh's Insight does not care about one's mind, body, or soul. It balances the scales so that everyone has the opportunity to properly show their skills."

Slowly, leisurely, Yumagi flicks his fingers again, causing another card to appear in his grasp. He momentarily frowns, then tosses it away, causing it to explode into particles of red light. After that, he resumes speaking.

"If you weren't a complete idiot, you might have waited for your brother to investigate the restrictions on your Soul Shackles. Ah, but I shall magnanimously explain the rules for you now, even after you so rudely attacked me like the dumb brute you are."

He continues. "The rules are simple, really. Once someone makes a move, they cannot make another until everyone else in the battle has made their move. We take turns, you see? It's so much better than some speedster beating the devil out of someone and them being unable to fight back. It puts us all on a nice, fair, level playing field. Now that you have performed your first attack, Uriel, your brothers may make their moves. As for me, my turn has not yet ended, for I merely activated a pre-planned effect which took place before the restriction went up."

Uriel's scowl deepens. "We must... take turns?! What sort of absurd nonsense is this, bloodskin? I need not abide by any rules thou hath placed upon me!"

"Oh, but you _do,_" Yumagi says, as a sinister grin crosses his face. "These restrictions will remain until one of us defeats the other. And of course, if I defeat you, I will destroy your body, and those of your brothers. If you defeat me, you may destroy my body, as well. A fair trade, if I don't say so myself."

"Demons know nothing of fairness," Uriel snaps. "All of thy people art liars, deceivers, and jackals."

Yumagi's grin fades away, and in its place, a placid look of disgust replaces it.

"Coming from the likes of an Archangel, that is an insult I cannot tolerate."

The Glyphmancer shoots a look toward Michael and Raphael.

"Come then, you two; It's your turn to act. But be careful, for you only get one chance to end this Emperor's life."

"One chance, _indeed,_" Michael snarls. The Archangel of Courage raises his sword and flaps his wings.

"Wait!" Raphael says. "Brother, thou-"

Raphael fails to stop Michael's momentum. The Archangel of Courage dives toward Yumagi with his sword held at the ready. In the blink of an eye, he slashes horizontally, and his blade finds its mark, instantly beheading the Glyphmancer!

"Damned bloodskin!" Michael roars. "Thy games mean nothing to me!"

He slows to a stop and spins to face his opponent once again. However, just before Michael can raise his weapon, his body freezes mid-movement, and he becomes bound by the same restriction holding Uriel in place.

In that moment, Yumagi's head falls from his shoulders, but one of the lights on his forehead dims, and a wave of shadow travels from his body to grab his head, place it back atop his shoulders, and instantly heal the wound.

The Glyphmancer swallows heavily. He rubs his fingers across the place where Michael slashed and shoots Michael a wry grin.

"Impressive speed, Archangel," Yumagi says. "But speed alone will never defeat me. Since you separated my head from my shoulders, Shadow Bind put it back in its rightful place. You activated yet another of my trap cards."

Michael frowns deeply. "Twice, thou hath lost thy life, but the number of lights on thy forehead hath also dimmed. I need only slay thy frail body eleven times more to end thy existence!"

"Perhaps." Yumagi replies, his tone mysterious.

Raphael motions with his hands. An instant later, he casts a wide-area illusion spell, one which teleports Uriel and Michael away from their current positions. At the same time, twenty-five copies of Uriel, of Michael, and of Raphael appear all across the battlefield. Uriel's copies remain frozen in place with her weapon held overhead, while Michael's stand at the ready, with his sword half-held in an attacking stance.

The Archangel of Wisdom, on the other hand, levitates in place, each of his twenty-five copies merely crossing their arms to look down upon the Glyphmancer.

"Thy abilities are quite curious," Raphael says, all of his clones speaking in unison. "But this old man has observed many strange and frightening powers in his life. My vision remains just as acute today as ten and twenty eons ago, demon. Thy Soul Shackles are far from invincible, especially as they bind thee to the same limitations as we."

A flicker of doubt crosses Yumagi's face. He glances at each of Raphael's clones, trying to pinpoint which one is the real Archangel. In the end, he fails, making his eyes twitch.

"Clever, Archangel of Wisdom. But now that the three of you have made your moves, I can make mine."

"Oh?" Uriel says. "Twice, thou dids't activate an ability to heal thy slain self, yet still, thou claimeth thou hath a turn to take?"

"Funny you ask," Yumagi says, his smile returning in full force. "Any cards I played before the Pharaoh's Insight activated haven't been bound by the restrictions. Of course, if you had prepared any abilities before, then you would be able to activate them out of turn, too. That's why my rules are fair."

"A game where one side knows the rules in advance well before the other players, and who is able to make preparations before them, is no fair game at all," Raphael chides. "But worry not. This old man is well known for his magnanimity. He knows the young and stupid must sometimes cheat if they wish to beat their grandfather in a 'friendly' game."

"I do not cheat!" Yumagi shouts, momentarily losing his cool. "The rules are fair! They bind me, and they bind you!"

All of Raphael's clones avert their eyes.

"So thou claimeth."

"Bastardly Archangel! I have given you the courtesy of explaining the rules!" Yumagi shouts, all but blowing his top. "From here on out, I won't give you that luxury! Let's see if you can figure out the rules on your own!"

Yumagi flicks his fingers, causing a platinum-colored card to appear in his grasp. Fervent greed clouds his expression. He holds the card up high and laughs uproariously. "You don't like playing games, Archangels? Well, too bad! I make the rules here, and you have to follow them! Ramses' Staff, **Evoke!**"

Uriel and Michael tense up, awaiting some stupendous super-attack to fall upon them. Instead, a wave of blood-red magic travels out of Yumagi's body and flies toward the other Demon Emperors below him. All at once, their fatigue vanishes, along with any lingering weaknesses plaguing them. They blink in surprise, momentarily stunned.

"Hey! Yumagi! What did you do to us?" Wolfram asks.

"Is it not obvious?" Yumagi asks, as he winks at his comrades. "My game is fair, and the rules are set. All of you are a part of the rules, too. Naturally, to keep things fair, you may attack whomever you wish. There are no 'teams' in this game."

Emperor Serena, levitating a sizable distance from the battlefield, blinks her sightless eyes. "Yumagi! Are you stating that every person here also gets a turn?"

"Of course!" Yumagi answers. "And as you can see, I have not stacked the deck in anyone's favor. You may all attack whomever you wish. Naturally, I advise that you strike the Archangels down before pursuing any... _personal_ vendettas."

Michael scoffs. "Hah! More rules that favor thee! Truly, bloodskins are all cheaters and villains!"

"I do not cheat," Yumagi repeats, once again taking great offense to that accusation. "I merely set the rules beforehand. Now then, everyone, join me in attacking whomever you desire."

Wolfram crosses his arms. "I thought you said 'you alone' were enough?"

"Oh, I am." Yumagi answers. "I have balanced the scales all by myself, have I not? Before I showed up, the lot of you were, how shall I say it? _Getting your asses kicked._ You can thank me later."

Emperor Lupus snorts. "He's not wrong."

Shortly after, the remaining Demon Emperors take up offensive stances. Lupus and Wolfram stick together, as do Yardrat and Fae. Serena remains in the distance, unable to offer much offensive support. In total, six Emperors face off against the three Archangels.

The Emperors communicate with each other telepathically. They keep their plans to themselves, debating how exactly they should finish off their most hated enemies.

[Once we slay Uriel, the other two will disperse.] Yardrat says. [But killing her won't be easy. There are too many of Raphael's illusory clones present. It will take us each one attack to whittle the clones down.]

[We could use a wide-area attack,] Fae says. [I'm sure Auger has something perfect for us. If nothing else, I could grab one of the useless bystanders, and...]

She falls silent, allowing her implication to hang, unspoken.

[Let's not go that far, Emperor of Terror,] Lupus says. [All three Angels are currently frozen. They cannot make any moves, but undoubtedly, they've started preparing their own plans to deal with us. I suspect Raphael's clones will make for difficult opponents.]

[We aren't the only ones in this battle,] Wolfram says, casting a glance to the south. [We should drag Guura and Melody over here. Those lazy bums are sitting and eating food while we fight the Archangels!]

[Don't bother,] Lupus says to her fellow werewolf. [The Hell of Isolation hates getting involved in anything like this. Considering what happened to Emperor Shax, you can hardly blame them.]

[I'll blame them all I damn well want!] Wolfram shouts telepathically. [They're so stuck in the past-]

"Your time is almost up!" Yumagi says to the other Emperors. "If you don't make your moves in the next minute, the next round of actions will start!"

"What?!" Fae shouts. "What do you mean? Why would our turn suddenly end?"

"Oh, goodness, it must have slipped my mind," Yumagi says, while fanning out and examining his finely curated nails. "You see, there is a five minute time limit for every round. So, if nobody makes a move in those five minutes, they forfeit their turn and the next round will begin."

"'Must have _slipped your mind_?!' You stupid jackass!" Fae roars. "Spell out the devil-damned rules next time before leaving us grasping at straws! Shit, everyone, just attack! Beat the crap out of the clones before our turns end!"

All at once, pandemonium ensues as the other five Emperors race into the fray. Yardrat conjures a ball of temporal energy that he throws into a dense cluster of six Archangel copies. It tears open a rift in space, eviscerating their bodies, but the different versions of Raphael, Uriel, and Michael prove to be only illusory copies.

At the same time, Wolfram slashes his claws with reckless abandon. He releases shockwaves of demonic power that tear through the air and strike three more copies, causing them to burst into particles of light before his body freezes up and his turn concludes.

Lupus, as a defensive-oriented fighter, doesn't possess any wide-area abilities. She only ends up tearing through one of the illusions, but to her shock, she crashes into a physical body, making her cry out in surprise.

"Michael! I found Michael! He's right here!"

With only Fae and Serena not having taken their turns, the Emperor of Terror scowls angrily.

"Shit!" Fae curses. "I have to attack Michael, but my powers are no good against powerful opponents! I massacre hordes of weaklings; that's what I'm good at!"

After a brief moment, Fae closes her eyes and sends a petition to her leader, Emperor Auger.

[Hey! Auger! I know you're watching this battle! Give me an ability I can use! I'm useless out here!]

The Emperor of Distribution's response comes back swiftly.

[Your opponents are powerful. I will acquiesce. I have selected nine potential powers that will assist you. Choose two, as per what your body can handle.]

He transmits a series of images, thoughts, and concepts to Fae, making a vicious grin cross her face.

[Oooh! Those two! They're perfect! But give me that other one, also. Just take away my 'bomb' power for now. It's useless here!]

[I give and I take.] Auger replies, his voice the epitome of calm. [Show these lesser Hells the might of the Fourth Hell.]

[Oh, I'll 'punish' these Archangels,] Fae says, as demonic power leaves her body, followed by three surges of new power. [I'll punish them until they beg for MERCY!]

Their discussion happens in the blink of an eye. The moment Auger distributes three new powers to his minion, Fae opens her eyes, revealing her glowing red irises. She motions with her hands, causing bones to burst out of her skin and grow rapidly, hardening into formidable bone armor. A long spike sticks out of her right wrist, resembling a makeshift shank.

At the same time, a hurricane of wind summons behind Fae. She fires it forward and rides it like a surfer, rushing toward Michael's exposed body with her wrist pulled back.

"Hey, Archangel! Piss off and die, will ya?!"

Unable to move, Michael can only close his eyes as the Emperor of Terror flies at him. An instant later, she rams the bone-shank into his heart, instantly destroying his construct body and causing all of his illusions to disperse, leaving behind three or four dozen copies of Raphael and Uriel.

After impaling the Archangel of Courage, Fae forcibly grinds to a halt, restricted by the Soul Shackles placed upon her.

"I got one of the bastards! You bums can thank me later, bahahaha!"

Several of Raphael's clones shoot disinterested glances at Michael's destroyed body as it fades away. Given Uriel can simply resummon him, his 'death' is only transient, but even so, it still represents a significant loss to Uriel's fighting power.

_My illusions were intended to confuse the demons and make them waste their turn,_ Raphael thinks. _But through a stroke of luck, they succeeded in taking out my brother's construct. Uriel will need to spend a turn resummoning him. That means in this next round, only Michael and I will be able to attack._

The Archangel of Wisdom rapidly plays out dozens of possible attacking and defensive strategies in his head. He takes special note of the abilities Fae unleashed, frowning deeply.

_Emperor Auger. That child did not exist during the Energy Wars. If he did, he was not an Emperor at the time. I am unfamiliar with his abilities, but Fae's powers do not seem to follow the common demonic power types. I have never known a demon to be capable of manipulating bone and wind... so how coulds't she obtain these two foreign powers? Auger must be responsible, in some way..._

While Fae celebrates her first blood, Emperor Serena continues to stay out of the battle. Her powers, like Fae's, make her fare poorly against powerful individuals, such as the Archangels. Unlike Fae, however, she does not have Emperor Auger to swap out powers as required.

_Fae and Yardrat will be the key players in defeating the Archangels,_ Serena thinks. _The werewolves may prove useful, but their contributions in this fight should end up inferior to Fae, Yardrat, and especially Yumagi._

She stares blankly into space, but her spiritual perception sweeps across Yumagi's body as he remains frozen in place.

_I have only spoken to Yumagi once, many eras ago, during the Energy Wars. He is a complete recluse, and I cannot imagine him stepping out of the shadows to join us for demonkind's sake. From what I have gathered, he is a selfish bastard even compared to the many selfish bastards among the other Hidden Emperors. He must have come here for some other reason, but as for why, I cannot tell._

As the last seconds count down, Yumagi begins to move again. He offers a cold smile to everyone present.

"The first round has ended. Now, the second may begin. But be careful! Those who take their turn first may make an initial gain, but they will also be sitting ducks for their opponents to counter-attack!"

Wolfram, originally intending to charge forward and attack, pauses to shoot a suspicious glance at Yumagi.

"What do you mean?"

"Isn't it obvious?" Yumagi counters. "If you attack, your body becomes frozen. It may be better for your opponent to attack first. Ah, but there is that pesky five minute round limit... you can't wait too long, lest you waste your turn! And I won't be telling you when the round timers run out after this, because that simply wouldn't be any fun!"

"Whose side are you on?!" Wolfram roars. "We're all demons here! We're your allies!"

"In this game, there _are_ no allies," Yumagi says, while glancing at Wolfram from the corner of his eye. "There are only enemies and _future_ enemies. Really... do you trust that the demons to your right and left won't take a chance to attack you when your guard is lowered? The angels may fight together as one, but we have never been more divided."

"Knock it off with the riddles!" Wolfram retorts. "I came here to fight, not faff about with the likes of you! One way or another, I'm killing me an Archangel today! Bahaha!"

While the two of them talk, Uriel motions with her hands. She re-summons Michael's construct, along with her orb and cube constructs. Despite summoning three at once, this only counts as a single action, freezing her in place.

Similarly, Raphael motions with his hands to conjure yet another twenty-five copies of Michael's newly summoned figure, along with replacements for the losses his copies and Uriel's both suffered. Once again, seventy-five Archangels hover in the sky, all of them appearing to be the genuine articles.

In unison, all twenty-five of Michael's copies swoop toward the Demon Emperors, including Yumagi, the werewolves, Fae, and Yardrat.

Instinctively, they all raise their fists to meet the incoming threats, all except for Yumagi, who remains motionless.

"Only one of them is the real Michael!" Lupus shouts. "The others are fakes!"

Her words cause an unanswered question to spring into the minds of the other Emperors.

_Sure, but WHICH one is the real one?!_

In unison, 'Michael' delivers twenty-five slashes at every Emperor on the scene, excluding Selena, who continues to sit out the battle. His many copies phase harmlessly through the bodies of the Emperors, all except for Yardrat, who cries out in pain.

"Aaaargh!"

Despite successfully protecting his neck, Yardrat fails to shield the rest of his body. A violent sword-slash cuts across his chest, spraying blood and entrails out in a violent spurt. He instantly stumbles forward and freezes, while his bodily fluids continue to drain.

"Huh-help! Aaugh! The Archangel- he got me!"

The other Emperors try to move, but they also find themselves frozen!

"What the-?!" Wolfram shouts. "I didn't even do anything! How can my turn be over?! This makes no sense!"

Yumagi chuckles. "All of you defended yourselves. Doing so is considered using up your turn. I must have forgotten to mention that part! Oopsie~!"

"You devil-damned prick!" Fae screams. "Yardrat's gonna bleed out and die! Do something!"

The Glyphmancer rolls his eyes. "Oh, if you insist, I suppose I can use up my turn to save Auger's precious number two. By the way, Raphael. That was an excellent feint your brother used. Could you have possibly seen through the rules of my little game before these addlebrained Emperors did?"

The Archangel of Wisdom's twenty-five copies all smile in unison.

"As this old one said before... he has seen many strange and terrifying powers. Thine are not as unique as thou thinketh."

Yumagi nods.

"So it would seem. It's so much more fun playing games with a smart person. There just aren't enough of them around to give me a challenge."

The Glyphmancer lightly shrugs, then flicks his fingers, causing a bronze card to appear in his grasp.

"Oooh, tough luck, Yardy-boy. This won't heal you all that much, but it should staunch the bleeding a bit. Next time, try not to spill your guts. Golden Fire Rhino, **Imbue!**"

The card explodes into particles of light, and a miniature, blood-colored rhinoceros wreathed in flame rushes toward Yardrat. The Emperor of the Void flinches in alarm, but cannot move to avoid the incoming attack.

"Yumagi! How dare you attack-!"

Before Yardrat can finish his sentence, the rhino leaps onto his chest and transforms into a fiery tattoo. Yardrat closes his eyes and screeches in pain, while his open wounds rapidly burn closed, preventing further blood-loss. The sheer agony of having his chest forcibly burned nearly makes him pass out, but he manages to remain conscious through his force of will.

"No need to thank me," Yumagi says. "But I just saved your life. Did you really think I'd be such a bastard that I would attack a 'future enemy' while he was defenseless? That wouldn't be very _fair_ of me now, would it? Hahaha!"

Yardrat grits his teeth. His body trembles from the pain as he forces a pained smile.

"Y-yes. Thank... you... for your... help... Yumagi..."

"You are most certainly welcome!" Yumagi says. "Ah, but now that we've all made our moves, the round won't end until the five minutes are up. It seems _someone_ is choosing to sit idly on the sidelines!"

The Glyphmancer casts a casual glance back at Emperor Serena. She simply crosses her arms and continues to levitate, making no move to attack.

"I am useless in this sort of battle," Serena says, her soft voice gracefully touching the ears of those present. "Unless one of you wants to experience the realm beyond Demon Emperor for a half-hour or so, before dying a death most unbearable, I will continue to remain a spectator."

Nobody volunteers to accept her offer, so she falls silent.

Two minutes later, Yumagi smiles.

"Alright, round three can begin! I hope you've all been paying attention, because now, you should understand the majority of the rules for this engagement. No more complaining from this point onward!"

Wolfram glowers at Yumagi.

"Tsk. When this is over, you and I should have a little 'chat' about your so-called 'fairness,' Glyphmancer."

Yumagi flips his hair.

"You may do as you please, Wolfy-Boy."

"...Don't call me that."
