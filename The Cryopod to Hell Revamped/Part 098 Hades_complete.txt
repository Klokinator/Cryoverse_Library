The moment the vision began, Jason realized something was horribly wrong. The darkness of a cave yawned out before him. Agares stood by his side as they headed into the depths of the Earth, but before Jason had time to gather his location, something smashed into his head with the force of a freight train.

"Gaaaugh! Nnn... huwaaah!"

Jason stumbled forward and lost control of his body. The ground rushed at his face, and he hit it with a sickening thud. His nose cracked, blinding him with pain.

Agares' voice called out from somewhere, but Jason's tears blinded him. "Satan? Whoa! What happened? Hey, Belial, something happened to the boss!"

Footsteps approached. Jason gasped for breath while clutching his chest with one hand and his face with the other. His broken nose forced his eyes shut with all the agony it could muster. Electricity coursed through his body, tearing apart his blood cells and rewiring his neurons to deliver unending torture unlike anything he had ever experienced. Jason had had every bone in his body broken twice, both times were his fault, and the pain Satan felt now was somehow ten times worse than either of them.

Someone grabbed his shoulders. "Satan? What's the matter?!" Belial's voice rang out in alarm.

Satan could not form a coherent reply. "Hnng, buhuhu, gaah! Hur-hurts!"

After a moment, the pain lessened dramatically. Jason took a sharp breath as the sensation of magic entered his body. His lungs burned. He felt like his ribs had somehow impaled through both of them.

He winced and opened his eyes to see Belial in her tigerskin robes, her brow furrowed in concentration. A drop of sweat dripped down her forehead as she channeled magic into his body.

"Gah! Your mana passages are a bloody mess," Belial said. Agares stood beside her, watching intently to see if he would recover.

Something had happened to Satan, and it hurt terribly, but the confusion running through his thoughts mirrored Jason's. Satan didn't know what was shredding his body any more than Jason did.

Jason cautiously flipped onto his back and stared at the ceiling while gasping for breath. Belial and Agares hovered over him, and eventually, the pain faded to a dull throbbing in the back of his throat.

"Boss! You okay?" Agares asked.

"Y-yeah. I... I dunno what happened, Scales. Felt like someone threw me into a boiling geyser for a minute or two," Satan replied.

Belial scrounged up her face. "Well, your magic pathways were jumbled up. I pulled out a bunch of weird, shifty stuff from your body. You should be okay now."

"My fuckin' nose—"

Satan started to say something but stopped mid-sentence. Above him, a long, spindly arm drifted into view. The appendage shifted colors slowly, from green to red to blue to pink; it cycled through all the colors of the rainbow over and over. It reached toward the ceiling ten feet above, and as it drew near, the hand fanned out to reveal five fingers, each ending in a ghastly-looking talon.

"Your nose...?" Belial leaned in to examine him. "Mmm. It's broken. I think I can patch it up."

Satan ignored Belial and focused all his attention on the floating arm. After a moment, another emerged from behind his back. Then another, and another...

Eight arms twisted and bent in midair, writhing weirdly as they sometimes locked hands with each other, and other times reached out to touch the walls.

Belial finished weaving a magic sign, and the pain in Jason's nose vanished as it popped back into place. Blinking, he flicked his gaze toward her. "Hey, toots... do... do you see the arms?"

Belial raised an eyebrow. "The arms? Your arms? Obviously. Do you have a concussion?"

Agares nodded. "We see both of them, boss."

"No, dumbasses. Not _these_ arms—" Satan tried to move his arms to wave at them, but instead, two of the rainbow-colored octopus-style limbs lowered and waved in Agares and Belial's faces. As they did, Jason noticed the limbs were partially transparent, like they belonged to a ghost.

Belial stared through the limbs, not noticing them at all. "What do you mean, 'not _these_ arms,' Satan? Are you okay?"

Jason continued trying to move his flesh-limbs, but nothing happened. Instead, the ghost-limbs danced around. Concentrating, he furrowed his brow to gaze at Belial. The hand closest to the demoness reached toward her to caress her face. Belial flinched and swatted her cheek, but her hand passed through his.

_Yes. That's right. My limb. Mine,_ Satan thought.

Jason knew what they were. Vectors. Samael's vectors. He had fought Satan once before, and he had been torn apart with them. Satan of the ancient past didn't fully understand them yet, but Jason did.

"Whoa..." Satan whispered. "I can't believe it."

Belial swatted her cheek again, then stood up and took a step back, flicking her gaze around the room to look for her unknown assailant. "What the Devil keeps touching me?"

Satan tried to stand up, but instead of pushing off the ground with his flesh and blood limbs, his new ethereal ones did the job. This resulted in him levitating into a standing position.

Agares' eyes widened. "Uh, uh... boss? You're floating. You're floating!"

It took a moment, but eventually, Jason regained the ability to move Satan's regular arms. He reached up and rubbed his nose. "Yeah. How about that?"

Satan's thoughts bubbled in Jason's mind. _That ugly chicken must've iced over. Guess his friends took him out, eh? Are these the Vectors he mentioned? Pretty cool. It seems I'm the only one who can see 'em._

Satan adjusted his coat and cracked his neck. "Thanks for the fix-up, doll. I'm feelin' pretty damn good right about now. Let's have a chat with our new friends."

Belial blinked. "Huh? Oh. The imps, right."

The three of them turned and continued into the cave. Satan's thoughts began to go wild.

_I guess that Blood Contract did the trick. Anything I write in the notes will happen, so when I said the Archangel's soul would become mine, it must've given me his powers too. That's handier than I expected. I'll have to play around with his abilities later,_ Satan thought.

_Hm. Now that I think about it, Blood Contracts are pretty convenient. Sure, I've gotta convince someone to sign, but once they do, I own their ass forever. The other demons can't put a hand on me. Even Valac don't seem all that tough now that I have Samael's powers._

Satan narrowed his eyes. _Wish I'd known what I know now when I first got the power. I'd have bound the souls of Agares, Belial, and all the others to me. Shit. They probably won't sign a second contract. Oh well. At least they can't attack me. That's good enough for now. As for the rookies... hmm..._

The tunnel warmed steadily as they descended into the planet. Eventually, they arrived at Leviathan's lair. Already, several hundred imps milled around, waiting for Satan's arrival. The sheer-drop from the cliff edge was gone. At some point, somebody must have smoothed it out, because now a stony ramp descended the side of the bluff to the ground forty feet below.

Jason walked down it, with Belial and Agares trailing him. A few torches lined the wall so that the imps wouldn't blindly wander about, but the dragon was still damn hard to see. His black body seemed to suck the light from the room and absorb it. Even if Jason squinted, he could barely see the dragon's prone form as anything other than a part of the void.

When Jason arrived at the bottom, he stopped to look around the room. "Hm? Why are there so many imps?"

Belial asked, "I beg your pardon?"

"I thought Leviathan was going to power them up. Why are they all still unmutated?" Satan asked.

Agares shrugged. "No idea. Let's ask."

Nodding, Satan headed across the room. Leviathan's head lay on the ground. He snored quietly, though it was hard to see how he could sleep with some of the imps screeching loudly to one another.

"Hey! Dragon! Wake up!"

Satan yelled, surprising Jason with his boldness. He showed no fear as he awakened the primeval monster that could kill him effortlessly.

Several seconds passed. Satan started to open his mouth, but Leviathan rumbled deep in his stomach. "**I heard you the first time.**"

Swallowing, Satan's voice shook slightly. "O-oh. Well, ah, I was kinda w-wondering... if, uh... you could..."

His confidence vanished instantly as Leviathan's eyes opened. The winged behemoth slowly raised his head and yawned, unleashing a torrent of flame upon the ceiling. The canopy turned red hot. It only lasted for an instant, but the temperature in the room jumped fifteen degrees.

Leviathan blinked slowly. "**Why have you awoken me?**"

A lump formed in Jason's throat. "The, the, uh... the imps! They're still... you know... _imps_."

The dragon blinked. "**And?**"

"Well, we killed a bunch of angels... I thought you could, uh, you know... power the other imps up? Like you did the rest of us?" Satan asked.

Several seconds passed. The dragon flared his nostrils. "**Who said I would do such a thing? Do you think yourself heir to my power? Does it belong to you?**"

"Huh? No! No, haha, of course not!" Satan rubbed his head sheepishly. "Ahh, forget it! I don't know what I was thinkin'."

Leviathan gazed at him. Ten seconds passed. Sweat dripped down the back of Satan's neck.

"**What are those?**"

The dragon's eyes followed the movements of Satan's vectors as they danced about.

"These? You can see them?" Satan asked.

Leviathan blinked slowly. "**Mmm. Unless my eyes deceive me, Samael had a set of limbs similar to yours.**"

Satan nodded. "Samael died. I killed him."

"**Oh? Most impressive.**"

Leviathan's tongue snaked out of his mouth and through his teeth, wetting his monstrous lips.

"**I underestimated you, Satan. No matter how you did it, killing an Archangel is no mean feat. Millions of dragons far beyond a level of power you could comprehend have tried and failed. Only two Archangels have ever fallen, and neither perished to the claws of my brothers and sisters.**"

Leviathan leaned his head to the side and scratched the top of it against a wall. A horrible grinding sound radiated outward as scale meets stone, causing every demon in the room to press their palms against their ears.

After the dragon spent a few moments satisfying his urge, he spoke again. "**If you can kill one Archangel, perhaps you could do the same for others. To be honest, I thought of your kind as a joke at first. I imagined it would be amusing to send you to die at the hands of the Archangels. I'm reconsidering my opinions.**"

Jason's jaw slackened. "D-does that mean... you'll power us up after all? If you do, we can kick some angel ass!"

"**No. I won't,**" Leviathan replied. "**It would be too tiresome to transfer my power hundreds of times. There are too many of you. Instead, I'll teach one of your kind my ways. They can evolve the rest. I look forward to seeing what you'll accomplish.**"

Satan crossed his arms. "All I know is that the angels captured two of my friends. We need to rescue them as soon as possible. Teach me, and I'll power up my comrades lickety-split."

Leviathan chortled in his throat, a nasty sound like metal ripping in half. "**Kukuku... not you, Satan. You've too much on your plate, and your mana capacity is quite low. Leading the rest of your species won't give you enough time to manipulate their spiritual energy. No, instead, I'll pick a subordinate. I'll choose...**"

The dragon's fiery eyes swept across the room. His vision illuminated everywhere he looked, revealing looks of terror on the faces of all the imps who weren't yet used to him. Even the other demons who had met him once before appeared a little frightened.

However, two demons showed no fear. Two of them didn't cower or flinch when the dragon's gaze met theirs: Valac and Lucifer.

"**Mmm. A Manipulator of the Damned, and a female with an all-seeing eye. Interesting.**"

Satan followed Leviathan's gaze. Immediately, his expression soured. He turned to the dragon and kept his voice low. "Come on, why you gotta do me like this? Can't you pick someone else?"

Leviathan cocked his head. "**My powers of aura manipulation are no trifle. They will kill a weakling instantly, and they require an ironclad will to use properly. Only one of those two will do.**"

A minute passed. Satan grumbled to himself as the dragon's gaze beat down on him.

_Stupid dragon. Cripes. I guess I have to choose Lucifer. Valac doesn't have to follow my orders at all. He's a loose cannon. If anyone's gonna have the powers of the dragon, they'd better be under my thumb,_ Satan thought.

"You're saying I can't have the power, right?" Satan asked.

"**Correct.**"

"Fine. Give it to Lucifer. She's the second-best pick," Satan said.

Jason glanced at Lucifer. "Hey! Lucy! Get your funbags over here! Mister Dragon has a present for ya," Satan called.

The demoness rolled her third eye. "_I heard_. Fine. Do what you will, oh mighty dragon."

Lucifer walked over to Leviathan, ignoring Satan's presence entirely. Leviathan shifted his body, moved his front claw toward the demoness, and touched her chest.

After a moment, Lucifer took a step back. "Oh? Is that all? I expected more of a—"

Lucifer stopped mid-sentence. All the strength in her body vanished, and she slumped to the ground, unconscious.

The dragon chortled again, making that horrible sound in his throat. "**Kukuku... this female will require hibernation for a few days. Begone, all of you. Leave me in peace so that I may rest. When she awakens, Lucifer will be the one who handles the future of demonkind. Use her new powers well, Satan.**"

Leviathan pulled back and yawned, releasing another wave of fire at the ceiling. The temperature in the room rose to well over a hundred degrees, sucking the moisture from everyone's bodies. Several imps passed out from heat exhaustion, and even Jason began sweating uncontrollably.

"Y-yeah, can do, big guy. Have a nice nap," Satan said.

A while later, all of the imps paced around in the snow, shivering as they stayed close to several firepits scattered around a makeshift camp. The cold of winter already reached below zero, but the winds aren't yet chilly enough to extinguish the flames.

The demons had already evolved to ignore the cold, but the imps weren't as hardy. One imp nudged another. "Me freezing tits off! Heaven never this cold!"

"Stop your bitching!" Satan snapped at him. "If you want to return to Heaven, go ahead. They'll slit your throat without a second thought."

The imp swallowed hard, rethought his life's decisions, and wandered away to another fire pit.

"You seem more hostile than usual," Agares remarked. Much like the unmutated imps, he stayed close to the fire since his reptilian blood makes him sensitive to the cold. "Is something on your mind?"

Jason swore under his breath. "No, things are just fucking dandy— OF COURSE I'm angry!" Satan snapped. "Tens of thousands of imps are still in Heaven. They'll all be dead soon, assuming I've judged the angels correctly. On top of that, our enemy captured Deebs and Bael, and now the dragon gave Lucifer some special power. Everything sucks, chum."

"I think you're acting a little overdramatic," Agares replied. "We survived. Lots of demons died, but we have a dragon on our side. Instead of worrying constantly, you should focus on creating a home for us. We need to get out of the cold and the heat and make a nest where the angels won't find us."

"We need to save Deebs and Bael," Satan muttered. "That other crap can wait."

"Patience. Use your head. We can't return to Heaven until all the imps are at the same level as you and me. Seven of us barely held the angels back, and that was only because of the stunt you pulled with Samael. You said he was dead, right?" Agares asked.

Satan squirmed slightly. "Err, yeah. Probably."

"How do you know?" Agares asked.

"I have my ways. I made Samael sign that magical contract thingy. When he died, I knew," Satan replied.

Agares rubbed his lip. "Mmm. Interesting. If I died, then you would know as well?"

"Probably," Satan said.

"Well, at least that's one positive thing to come from your power," Agares said.

Satan crossed his arms. "Yeah? Only one? I saved us from the angels, remember?"

Agares's casual smile faded away. "You tricked us into signing our souls over to you. No matter the reason, such deception is going to cause a lot of resentment. I don't know if we can trust you or not, but you didn't give us a choice. At this point, I can only hope you'll do the right thing when an opportunity arises."

"I'm selfish. I'll admit that much," Satan replied. "But I've got the best interests of our people at heart. Don't you dare doubt me now."

Agares bristled at his words. "As I said, I don't have a choice in the matter. Diablo and Bael are trapped in Heaven. If you plan to save them, you'd better figure something out soon."

"I'm still thinking," Satan muttered in response. "Gimme time. I always come up with something."

"Right. Always the genius," Agares muttered.

Several minutes passed. Jason turned and wandered away, leaving Agares beside the fire. As he walked, Satan's thoughts formed again.

_Everyone doubts me. The dragon. Valac. Lucifer. Even Agares. I'm starting to think they might be right,_ Satan thought.

Satan rubbed his chin as he trudged through the snow.

_I saved a few hundred imps. Big fuckin' deal. Whoop de doo. I got two of my promising warriors caught in the process. I did kill an Archangel indirectly, but that don't make up for Deebs and Bael. I gotta do better, man. I gotta work my tush off and send those ugly chickens packing,_ Satan thought.

Satan stopped a short distance away from another campfire. At this one, only imps milled around. None of the seven mutated demons were in the vicinity.

_Come to think of it, once Lucifer wakes up, she's gonna be a pain in my ass. She'll boost these rookies up, but they might not follow my orders. Instead of only Valac, I'll have hundreds of rebellious bastards plotting to take my spot. I need to take action now, while they're still dumb as rocks,_ Satan thought.

Satan motioned with his hand. A Blood Contract appeared in the air, and words magically appeared as Satan gazed at it. The language was a guttural, demonic-looking batch of letters, each with pointed edges. The uppercase G resembled a curled claw, while the W's reminded Jason of demonic horns.

Thanks to Solomon's Crown, and the fact he was in a vision of the past, Jason quickly scanned the words.

_Bla bla bla, sign your soul over to me until one of us dies, bla bla bla, give me all your powers and abilities on death, bla bla bla..._

Jason skimmed the contents of the magical scroll. It appeared to be slightly different from the ones Satan had had Agares, Belial, and the others sign before. The new contract included a clause about taking people's abilities on death. _Perhaps he used the same one on Samael?_ Jason wondered.

Satan stood up straight, adjusted his coat, and tossed the scroll in the air. Two of his vectors zipped forward and grabbed it before it could land in the snow, and he swaggered toward the fire pit where the imps were chatting.

"Mornin' chums. How are you young bucks doin' today?" Satan asked.

Several imps spun to look at him as he stopped at the edge of the melted snow. One of them leaned forward. "You big boss? Big boss Satan?"

"Mhm, that's me, lil' guy. And boy, do I have a job for you! Though, I dunno if you're cut out for it," Satan replied.

Satan paused for a moment. He pretended to hem and haw while evaluating the imps.

Unsurprisingly, his reverse psychology worked. Every imp at the campfire turned to look at him. He captured their attention immediately.

The nearest imp raised his hand. "Ooh, ooh! Me! Pick me!"

A second imp scowled at the first. "No way! Belmor stupid! Me smarter!"

Within seconds, all of the imps began howling and arguing with each other over who would get to work for Satan. The Devil's thoughts rang clear in his head. _Heh. It's like taking souls from babies._

"Alright, all of you, shaddup. If you wanna be eligible to be my right-hand soldiers, you dummies hafta sign this here scroll first, okay?" Satan asked.

Satan's invisible arms lowered the Blood Contract down, and a third arm held out the feather for signing.

The first imp scratched his head. "What mean by 'sign?' How do?"

Satan kneeled to the imp's level. "It ain't tough. Just poke your finger, then use your blood to draw a smiley face. Easy peasy."
