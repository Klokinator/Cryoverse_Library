The Crown of Solomon is a weapon unlike any other, but only in the hands of a Wordsmith. It allows him to imagine the inner workings of complex mechanical objects, perform Ph.D. and Doctorate level math, and even comprehend the concepts of string theory. His mind compresses and expands, making him wince as he imagines all the parts and pieces that would go into a weapon like a Lightsaber. Despite being a fictional weapon, he rapidly manages to craft a functional prototype in mere seconds.

When he opens his eyes and holds out his hand, a small cylinder, perhaps the size of a flashlight, appears in midair and lands in his palm. He stares, wide-eyed, at the silver cylinder he once dreamed about as a child. 

His heart skips a beat. _A lightsaber. It's a goddamn lightsaber! This is the coolest moment of my life!_

He hesitates to activate it, but when he does, ecstasy overtakes him. A bright white blade of light shoots out, and a wave of heat begins emanating from the weapon. 

Amelia stares, wide-eyed. "What is that?"

"A lightsaber. It can cut through anything. Metal, rock, you name it. It's the greatest melee weapon that has ever existed."

The stupid grin on his face begins to slip. The sound his new weapon makes isn't quite right. It's less of a gentle hum, and more like a fireplace rapidly crackling after tossing a bunch of kindling on top. The white color also seems odd, given he envisioned blue in his mind.

He stabs it to the ground, and the moment the saber touches the dirt and grass, a small wildfire ignites, making him pull it out and step back. A second later, the saber makes a sputtering noise, then deactivates. No matter how he shakes it or presses the activate button, it won't reignite.

The Crown gives him an answer as he scrutinizes the small weapon. _Battery depleted._

_Seriously? I activated it for all of ten seconds! How much energy does it use? Perhaps sustaining a beam of energy takes a lot more power than a quick energy pulse like that of a blaster rifle, so it dies rapidly. Geez. I suppose having a cool weapon isn't in the cards for me._

He hooks it through his belt and sighs. "That's a bust. Let's go already. **Extinguish.**" The fire at their feet snuffs out, bringing his smile back. _I don't need a lightsaber anyway. I have Wordsmithing._

The steps leading up to the portal take only a few seconds to climb. Once they make it to the top, he almost bursts into laughter. The frozen looks of fear and awe on the faces of the monsters inside are comical in a way he's never seen before.

The three of them step through the portal, and the _whoomph_ of air pressure inside makes his ears pop. One of the monsters slowly points at him and says something, but he doesn't catch it. After a moment, he realizes the monster isn't gesturing toward him, but Kar.

"...new leader! Behold his aura!" The gangly-haired monster continues to point at Kar, and several others nearby nod simultaneously.

"Yes, he has the power of the Sphinx. That must be how he opened the portal without our assistance. Only a Sphinx could have the wisdom to do such a thing."

He opens his mouth to retort. "Actually-"

"All bow before the new Sphinx! May he show mercy upon us!" The monsters in the room all fall to their knees, prostrating themselves before them.

"Fuck it," he mutters. He nudges Kar with his elbow. "You can take the credit for this one."

"Hurgh. Your generosity knows no limits."

"Shut up, scaly."

Amelia pokes him in the back, _hard_. "Jason. Rude!"

...

The sudden shift in the monsters' personalities surprises him. Where before they were vicious and looked like they wanted to tear the flesh from their bodies, they now smile and nod politely. Kar notices his confusion. "Monsters have a hierarchy, just as demons, angels, and humans do. The King or Queen leads all monsters, and that one's aura tends to pass down through to the rest. A wicked king will lead to violence, but one who is peaceful will lead to unity and order."

He frowns. "Why is the Sphinx the leader of all monsters?"

"Hurgh. Before, t'was Dragons who sat atop the hierarchy, but as they have long since disappeared from the galaxy, 'tis the Sphinx who leads. The fewer members a species has, the more potent their magical power becomes. I am the only Sphinx; therefore, I must rule."

Amelia pipes up. "Dragons used to lead, huh? Why don't monsters slaughter others of their species to condense their power? Wouldn't that improve their magical abilities dramatically?"

A hissing to his side makes him flinch in surprise. A giant cobra slinks up beside them and flicks its tongue out. "That would be pointlesss... There can only be one rightful king. Even orcssss, without any magical power, can be ussseful in an army."

Kar barely even looks at the freakishly long cobra. "Hurgh. Exactly. A bloody war over monarchical power would serve no purpose. Besides, some monsters birth vast litters of spawnlings, while others, such as the Sphinx, cannot procreate. Our species' self-regulation determines the leader without any artificial assistance. We don't expect a non-monster to understand our ways."

He nods along, forming links in his mind. _Monsters, demons, humans, angels, Titans. Each of them works together to form a complex, symbiotic relationship. The Energy Wars must have been unthinkable in scope for the Titans and angels to die off._

Kar trots away to speak with some other monsters, leaving Amelia and him alone together.

Dellfingler shrills out, "Oh boy! I hope we're going on an adventure! What do you guys think, eh?! Amelia, sweetheart, I can't wait for you to wrap those cute little hands around my-"

Before he can finish, she smacks the sword. "Hush! Lewd!"

He chuckles. "Heh. I guess we're going to go out and start looking around, huh?"

Amelia's expression grows more serious. "Jason, I have to ask you something."

"What?"

"It's regarding what we discussed while you ate." She pauses and crosses her hands in front of herself. A trio of goblins pushes past them, nudging her against him. Amelia squirms and pulls away, and her cheeks flush red. "Um, there's a lot of others here. Can we talk somewhere more private?"

"Yeah. That's fine." He swallows slowly, unsure what she wants to discuss. _Lately, she hasn't been the bubbly, explosive person she was when we first met. She's grown more subdued, especially since Bahamut's visions took hold of her._

...

A few minutes later, they step outside of the Great Doors, which are still wide-open from when Kar flung them inward. The darkness of the corridor leading to his cryopod stretches out before them. He makes a mental note to start installing lights all around the Labyrinth where possible.

Amelia taps her foot on the ground. She forces an awkward smile, then loops her hands behind her back. "L-look, um, I don't know how to ask this... it's about what Hoarhiim showed you."

His back muscles tense up reflexively. "What about it?"

"What memories did you see? You saw what I used to look like, but Blaarjiim mentioned something to me while we were walking through the forest, and it was a little unsettling."

_Now I understand the look on her face. It isn't just nerves, but a lack of faith that's bugging her. She doesn't trust me, given everything she's experienced over the last day or so. Can I blame her?_

"Hoarhiim showed me the events leading up to your encounter with Joan, Amelia. He... he showed me how she hurt you."

Amelia's breath catches. "Hoarhiim showed you our fight?"

"Yeah."

Her lip quivers. "Wh... why wouldn't you just ask me about it?"

"I didn't want to push you away, Amelia. You don't want to talk about Joan, that much is clear. Anyway, Hoarhiim offered to show me. I didn't ask him."

Amelia's eyes drift down to the ball of light resting in his belt pouch. "A likely story. Hoarhiim! Don't you have anything to say for yourself?"

He half-expects him to reply, but as before, Hoarhiim stays silent. Blaarjiim appears in their minds. "Hm. Odd. I cannot sense my brother's presence. He must be sleeping again."

"Sleeping?"

Blaarjiim grins. "When you first met him, he was in a similar state. If something disturbs my brother, or he feels rejected, he goes into isolation. His psyche is too fragile for my liking."

A realization hits him. _Rejected? You... you don't mean...Guilt hits me in waves. Hoarhiim showed that memory of Amelia for a reason, yet I spit in his face. I ignored him and called him a liar. He's always been there for me, but I didn't just doubt him yesterday, I outright accused him of deceiving me. Is that why he's gone silent?_

Amelia studies him intently. "Why did Hoorthiim show you my battle with Joan? He wouldn't do something like that without a good reason."

_It occurs to me what she's prodding for._

"Hoarhiim wanted to explain to me why you were, uh, hurting so badly. I didn't understand your trauma until he-"

"Bullshit." Amelia grits her teeth. "Hoarhiim isn't one to meddle like that. He thinks he's an agent of justice, a force of good against evil. He'd only show you my past if..." She trails off and looks down. A spark of recognition appears in her eyes. "He didn't just show you my fight with Joan."

"What? No, that was all he showed me, Amelia. Why don't you believe me?"

Amelia's jaw slacks, she tilts her head to look at Blaarjiim's orb. "You're right, Blaarjiim. He must have shown Jason something else."

_Too late, I realize lying wasn't the best option._

"Fine, he showed me one other thing. It wasn't that big of a deal, but you were looking for a lost treasure or something, and some goblins got in your way."

Instantly, Amelia takes a step back. Her jaw drops. "I knew it! You were lying to me!"

"I-I wasn't lying, Amelia! Really! Honest to god!" He starts to reach toward her, but she swats his hand away.

"It all makes sense now, Jason. Blaarjiim was right about you. You're a Hero, just like Joan. You keep trying to get close to me so you can hurt me again. If you thought my past didn't matter, you wouldn't lie about it."

_Things are getting out of control. She's not listening to me again._

"Amelia, please, talk this out with me. Calm down."

She freezes and gawks for several seconds. "You... you used your Wordsmithing on me during the trial! Blaarjiim told me about it! I started acting unnaturally, and I lost all ability to feel my emotions! I couldn't let out the pain inside! What did you do to me?!"

"I calmed you down and undid it a few minutes later, that's all. Amelia, I swear! I didn't want you to hurt yourself-"

"Liar! Jason never tells the truth!" Tears well up in Amelia's eyes. "I... I can't believe you. I thought we were friends, but you used your powers on me just because you were scared. You _should_ be scared! I'm powerful! I'm not a little kid you can look down on!"

"I never said you were, Amelia..."

"Your eyes say it all! You always look down on others, and on me! All you care about is looks; you even said so yourself! Huhk-" Amelia's heel on her shoe catches on a patch of uneven dirt, and she stumbles to the ground. "Ahh! I can't believe you'd do this to me! Liar!"

Blaarjiim appears in their minds, and a sinister grin appears on his face. "Hohoho, my queen is angry at you. She's about to blow, Jason. How will you stop her this time?"

_Blaarjiim! That bastard! Everything clicks into place. Blaarjiim must be manipulating Amelia to turn her against me. I made a few mistakes, but he keeps weaving a web of conspiracy around my actions, turning me into a villain before her eyes._

"Amelia! Blaarjiim is behind all of this! He's trying to tear us apart, can't you see-"

Before he can finish, Amelia's eyes widen. She jumps to her feet. Her breathing comes in shallow bursts. "Now you're trying to lie about my best friend? Who are you, Jason?! I've known Blaarjiim for thousands of years, while you've barely been around for three days. I have no reason to believe you over him."

_I can't think of a response. I watch in horror as she draws Dellfingler from her belt._ 

The sword hollers in surprise. "Whoa, lassie! Let's calm down and think things over! No reason to-"

"Shut up, sword. You're a weapon. You do as I command." Amelia gnashes her teeth together. Her face is horrifying; an angry amalgamation of emotions he's never seen before.

_She won't listen to reason. I have to Wordsmith and stop her._

He begins, "**Calm-**"

Before the word leaves his mouth, Amelia lunges and pounds her palm into his chest. Several spinning, blurred images of the world swirl around him. His body comes to a sudden stop, and several moments pass before his brain can catch up.

Then the pain hits. He's upside down. Every inch of his body screams for mercy. He's in the same situation as when he first met Amelia, only now he has no recourse. His body groans and flops over. He ends up face-first on the ground, with his nose and mouth inhaling and swallowing dirt. He can't see more than a few inches ahead. His vision pulses red. _Blood? Is that blood pouring from my face? Where am I, again?!_

"You were right, Blaarjiim. I sensed the magical buildup. He was going to use his Wordsmithing on me."

Amelia walks over and spins him onto his back with a quick kick. His body flops to the side uselessly, and he can't cry out in pain. _My lung must have collapsed. I have maybe a minute to live._

His eyes swivel to look at Amelia. She looks terrifying, like a specter of Death. Dellfingler, in her hands, reminds him of a scythe. _She's going to reap my soul!_

"What do you mean?" Amelia holds the sword to his chest, but pauses to stare at Blaarjiim.

His face appears in their minds. "If you kill the mortal, his Heroic Aura will pass on to another. You'll have to face an unknown Hero eventually, one who will have the upper hand in a fight."

Amelia nods absentmindedly. Her heart has hardened, and she seems to think very little of killing. "I see. Thank you, Blaarjiim. You've given me an idea."

Dellfingler pivots in her hand, and she aims it at the side of his neck. "Wordsmithing. You must have felt pretty invincible with that power, just as you did when you tortured me, all those years ago. Heroes love to rule and flaunt their power."

The sword flicks to the side, and his eyes bulge as he feels the blade cut across his throat. Warmth spills out over his neck. "Kahk! Amel- kkuh!"

_I'm going to die. I'm going to die! I'm going to die!!_

_Only now do I understand the foolishness of my actions. Had I paid more attention to the heart of the girl I cared about or had I heeded Hoorthiim's warning, I'd never have ended up in this situation._

Amelia steps over him, straddling her legs across both sides of his body. He watches in silence as his life ebbs away. _Seconds remain._

She sheathes Dellfingler and holds her free hand up, pointing it at his chest. After making a few quick motions, she mutters something under her breath, and the pain in his body fades.

His throat stops gushing blood. His eyes begin to slump as a wave of exhaustion takes him, and Amelia sighs.

"Now you'll never hurt me ever again."
