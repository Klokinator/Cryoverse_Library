Part 315: Rescue Success

_Tarus II, at the western side of the Fortress of Retribution, in the Hospital Division._

Thousands of wounded soldiers lay in makeshift hospital beds assembled not through Jason Hiro's Wordsmithing, but through his wife's molecular assemblers. After returning from Faith's End with the angel, Phoebe Hiro quickly got to work with her assemblers, building simple but comfortable and sterile hospital beds.

Once Jason returned, he assisted her by wielding Camael's Cube to conjure a massive square hospital structure with separated rooms for all the potential patients. Now, Belial and the other injured troopers lay on their beds, either groaning in pain or simply flinching silently while sparks of agony flicker through their nervous systems.

Jason steps into Belial's room, alone, and closes the door. He shoots a sympathetic glance at her missing right arm and grimaces.

"Damn. What the hell happened after I left? I thought we might lose a few people, but more than a thousand men and women ended up dying. Phoebe's barely holding it together."

The Wordsmith sets a chair beside Belial's bed, takes a seat, and leans over her missing arm. After concentrating his mind for a moment, he utters a Word of Power. "**Regenerate.**"

Belial's eyes loll back into her head for several seconds as a fresh new arm regrows from her mutilated stump. The pain of regrowing a new arm mixes with the tingling sensations of ten thousand spiders crawling inside her blood vessels.

Once the succubi's arm fully regenerates, she coughs weakly and sags back against the pillow.

"Ugh... it was Lucifer. She turned out to be... stronger than I expected. She used one of her signature abilities on me, something called the Demonic-"

"Demonic Trance," Jason interrupts. "I'm familiar with it."

Belial blinks in confusion. "You are? But how?"

"Solomon's visions. He showed me all sorts of stuff from the Ancient Era," Jason says. "What happened after that?"

Belial nods, then explains to Jason all the happenings after he left. The more she talks, the more thoughtful and contemplative he becomes.

After Belial finishes, the Wordsmith looks away. He gazes at the empty wall and rubs his chin, falling deep into thought.

"Interesting." Jason mutters. "I greatly underestimated Gressil's abilities."

"What do you mean?" Belial asks. "I fought Lucifer, not Gressil."

"I wouldn't be so sure of that." Jason says, turning his attention back to the former Second Emperor. "Gressil does indeed possess illusion abilities, though to what extent, I can't be certain. My mind-wife wasn't able to watch what happened as that strange anti-energy aura put her and Gabriel into some sort of induced coma, but she and I started talking after I returned. I'm now eighty percent certain that Gressil is somehow nullifying magic in a vast radius around himself. More importantly, he, Lucifer, and whoever else might be in his body are immune to this nullification effect."

Belial frowns. "Are you kidding me? Faith's End is massive, Jason. My magic went inert from the moment we entered its corridors until the moment we left. I've heard of area-type abilities, but that huge of a radius is unthinkable!"

"The facts tell the truth," Jason says. "Of course, Gressil himself might not be the one outputting the nullification. Don't forget, Ose is his sister. Maybe she made him a device that nullifies magic. Or, hell, maybe there's some special power binding Faith's End to which he's become immune. That's why I'm only eighty percent certain. It might be that some part of Faith's End itself nullifies mana, but Gressil has adapted to its effect over time."

Belial shifts uncomfortably. "I really hope you're wrong, Jason. If Gressil can nullify magic in a massive radius around himself, he's your worst enemy. The moment he gets near you, you won't be able to Wordsmith. Not only that, but he's terrifyingly strong, too. I would absolutely wallop him in a fist-fight, sure, and it wouldn't even be close... but if I have no abilities at my disposal, while he can teleport around and take me by surprise, I won't stand much of a chance."

Jason flicks his eyes around the hospital room.

"Illusion powers are stronger than I thought. You can deceive a person's senses, hide your position, and strike from the shadows. When coupled with temporal abilities, particularly teleportation, they become downright frightening. Gressil isn't just a demon, he's like some sort of sick, sadistic horror monster. The moment you detect his presence, you're already too late, and you can't escape."

Jason pauses for a moment, then nods, as if hearing someone else speak. "That's a good point. Phoebe says- hold on, one second. **Projection. Projection.**"

The Wordsmith utters two Words of Power, causing his mind-wife and Gabriel to materialize as ghostly figures on his right and left. The Archangel of Power finds his head sticking through the roof, given his immense height. He opts to sit on the floor, bringing his head to the same height as Jason's.

"As I was saying," Spirit-Phoebe explains, "It's not all dire. Technology still works against Gressil. You said some of our troopers managed to shoot him. Therefore, we can probably prepare countermeasures for if we have to fight him again. Maybe we can even detect him in advance."

"That's going to be difficult," Belial says. "I can't detect Gressil's aura at all. Not only does that anti-magic field dampen magical powers, but it also dampens my demonic senses. It makes Gressil invisible to me. You shouldn't be shocked if he can also evade other forms of detection."

Spirit Phoebe sits on the bed beside Belial. She lightly touches the succubus's regrown arm, but her finger passes through Belial's skin.

"I'll figure something out," Phoebe says. "Never underestimate the power of human technology."

"I'm sure you will," Belial says, flashing Phoebe a charming smile.

After discussing Gressil a bit more, Belial changes the topic.

"Jason, what exactly _are_ those strange clones of yours? Can they Wordsmith, too?"

The Hero shakes his head. "No. They're not actually clones of me in any way, shape, or form. They're more like... chimeras. Or constructs. I guess you could call them Black Hole Constructs."

Belial stares at him. "I have no idea what you're talking about."

"I used an idea Spirit-Phoebe gave me to make them," Jason explains. "They are not truly biological beings. Rather, they started out as self-forming black holes that tore apart a bunch of monsters in the Core. After absorbing all their flesh and DNA, I changed the 'Singularities' to assume a human form, and ended up making them look like me out of convenience. They aren't Wordsmiths, nor are they human, demon, or even monster. They're just... _things_."

"Jason gave them a minor form of sentience," Spirit-Phoebe adds. "They will follow basic orders, but they don't need to eat, sleep, or do anything else. Their bodies are insanely durable, potentially at the level of your average Demon Duke, but they can't go much further beyond that. They're useful bodyguards for the Core."

"Wow..." Belial mutters. "Would have been nice to have them when the Core was first attacked. Can you make more?"

Jason nods slowly. "I can. But... their creation process is a bit gruesome. They need a lot of... living flesh."

"So?" Belial scoffs. "Just Wordsmith some flesh, then."

Jason shakes his head. "It will lead to inferior constructs. Based on the mathematical diagrams Phoebe showed me, entities which have existed and formed a soul naturally have more potent 'life energy' than things I summon with my Wordsmithing. Technically, this includes Hope."

Belial scrunches up her face. "Wait, are you saying Hope isn't a living, breathing person like you?"

"No, not quite." Jason continues. "I don't fully understand the details... but at the moment of Hope's creation, he was simply an exact copy of me. The longer he continued to live, the more life energy he gained until he became fully sentient. If I wanted to Wordsmith creatures, monsters, demons, or whatever else into existence, only to consume them for the sake of these Black Hole Constructs... I'd have to let them live normal lives for a few years. I'd still end up killing sentient creatures, in the end."

Belial gazes at the Hero for a moment before averting her eyes.

"Well. I guess it's better this way."

"What do you mean?" Jason asks.

"I thought you'd completely lost all your morals after Daisy's death... but deep down, you still don't love killing."

At the mention of his daughter's name, Jason's eye twitches, but he doesn't otherwise display any outward emotion.

"Of course I don't. I don't enjoy any of _this._ I have only decided to do what I absolutely must. If that means exterminating ninety percent of the demons, then so be it. That doesn't mean I want to create an army of chimera-monsters."

Gabriel glances to his side at the Hero, then looks away, remaining silent.

"So. How is Hope?" Belial asks, changing the topic once again. "Have you... healed him?"

"I have." Jason answers. "But he fell into a temporary coma. According to Doctor Fathy, he's suffered violent trauma to his whole body, but especially his mind. It might be a day or two before he awakens. Amelia is with him now."

"And what about that angel we rescued from Faith's End?" Belial asks. "How is she?"

"Well, she's awake, which is probably better than Hope's condition," Jason replies. "But she won't say a word to anyone. Phoebe's with her, now, trying to offer her some comfort."

Belial nods. She shoots a suspicious glance at Gabriel.

"You there, Archangel. This angel we've rescued. Do you know her?"

Gabriel's eyes meet Belial's.

"At the height of angelic power, the number of Lazarites in Heaven numbered more than two hundred million. I barely knew a fraction of them."

"Right." Belial says. "But what about this girl, specifically? It seems odd that Gressil would simply have some random girl locked away so tightly in his prison cell. Why her? Her identity must be somewhat important."

"No Lazarite ever stood above the lowliest pureblood," Gabriel says. "Be they resurrected kings, senators, politicians, or popes. The mightiest Lazarite was little more than a disposable soldier compared to the precious few purebloods left after the War in Heaven."

Belial's gaze hardens. "That's strange. I asked if you knew her, twice, yet both times, you dodged the question. Come now, Archangel, it's a simple yes or no answer. Do you know the woman or _not_?"

Jason and Spirit-Phoebe both shoot Gabriel looks of surprise. The Archangel's expression remains aloof, but his eyes imperceptibly flick away, avoiding Belial's gaze.

"I cannot say."

Before Belial can speak, Phoebe pipes up. "So you _do_ know? Who is she, then? Why are you so tight-lipped?"

Gabriel shakes his head.

"'Tis not my place to say. That sister... has suffered immeasurably. Verily, she hath earned the right to a lifetime of peace. Please do not aggrieve her. She is a good woman."

"What's her name?" Jason asks.

"If thou wisheth to know, thou must asketh her thyself." Gabriel says.

No matter how much the other three push or prod Gabriel, he doesn't budge. He remains tight-lipped, making them wonder just who, exactly, the mutilated girl they found in Faith's End might be.

For a brief moment, Jason considers using his Wordsmithing to look into Gabriel's memories. The Archangel of Power turns his head to look at the Wordsmith right as those thoughts cross Jason's mind.

"Hero. Thou claimeth thyself to be a righteous man. Dost thou thinketh me a servant of evil? If I choose not to speak of my sister's identity, then forcibly taking that information from me would violate every principle of goodness. Why should a woman who has suffered so much be forced to take up an identity she may not want any longer? Has she not suffered enough?"

Jason's eye twitches. He looks away from Gabriel and nods.

"Alright. Have it your way, then. If the girl tells us who she is, then that will mean we've earned her trust. And if not, then that's fine too. She _is_ the last living angel, after all."

Gabriel faintly nods. "Mm."

After a few more minutes of chatting, Belial swings her legs out of bed and stands up.

"Alright. Enough talking. I want to see how Hope and the angel girl are doing, not to mention all the injured soldiers. Don't tell me you came here to chat with me while they suffered out there?"

"I figured you could take a little pain," Jason says. "So I came here to heal you afterward. Everyone else is already good to go."

Belial wrinkles her nose, then lightly socks Jason's arm.

"Leaving me for last? _Dick_."

Jason chuckles. "I'm glad you came out alright, Belial. If I hadn't sent my constructs..."

Belial's expression softens.

"Lucifer's hellhound would have killed me. Thanks, Jason. For a moment, when you suddenly appeared in front of me, I even felt the same way I used to around Satan."

"What? You wanna gimme a big kiss?" Jason asks.

"Well, I _am_ a succubus. But we'll see. I still like Phoebe more than you!"

Jason glances at Spirit-Phoebe. She smirks triumphantly, as if having won some unspoken competition.

"Fair enough," Jason says. "I like her more, too."

"Everyone does!" Belial laughs.

_Deep inside a hidden alcove nestled within Faith's End._

"Worthless brat!" Lucifer shrieks. 

The Emperor of Providence swings her fist into Gressil's face, knocking both of them off their feet. The Emperor of Chaos whimpers in pain as he tries to protect his head with his two right arms, but Lucifer's two left arms yank his aside and smack him around while she lets loose her anger.

"You let my baby girl die!!" Lucifer screams. "My pretty little Ose! Your perfect sister, far superior to the likes of you! You worthless shit! How dare you fail as her brother?!"

Gressil whimpers. "Mother, please, she's not dead! She's... she's been resurrected in Bael's body!"

"WHAT?!" Lucifer roars. "That fat-ass blob of idiot-soup? Are you trying to piss me off even more? How could my precious little girl deserve to wallow in that lard-tub's nasty, fat, disgusting body?! She deserves better!!"

Gressil winces. "I... I know you are angry, mother, but it's not all bad. Bael's body is invincible. Ose still possesses all her latent powers, so she's actually stronger-"

"No excuses!" Lucifer roars, slapping Gressil's face once again. "Her body is no longer perfect, and that's that! My poor sweet baby is trapped in an ugly, flabby, sack of shit, and there's nothing I can do to help her! Oh, such sadness! Such woe!"

Lucifer continues to scream and rant for a while, before turning her attention back to Gressil. 

"This is all your fault! How could you let Ose die?! I ought to kill you right here and now, brat!"

"Mother... you must try to calm down..." Gressil whispers. "Y-you're a Demon Emperor, so surely, you should think of your dignity-"

_WHAM!_

Lucifer blasts Gressil in the face, making both of them stumble against a wall.

"No more excuses from you!" Lucifer shrieks. "I can't stand the sound of your grating voice! You're like a stupid puppy that won't stop barking! Not a cute one, like my Hellhounds, but an ugly, dumb, shitty little Chihuahua!"

As Lucifer screams at Gressil, a faint look of rage spreads across his face. The entire right side of his body shakes and trembles as he struggles against an ancient fear and hatred boiling within his stomach.

"Stupid, stupid, stupid!" Lucifer continues. "That's what you are! An insignificant little moron who only plays with his stupid butterflies! You're no demon, you're a chicken wearing a demon's skin! A disgrace!"

The more she rants, the more Gressil's rage boils.

In an instant, his eyes snap open.

The Emperor of Chaos lunges at his mother and grabs her by the neck. He smashes her head against the wall with one hand, then uses his other as a bludgeon to repeatedly beat and batter her face, over and over.

"SHUT UP! SHUT UP! SHUTUPSHUTUPSHUTUP!!!"

Gressil roars in a voice filled with unending malice and hatred. He completely overtakes Lucifer in that moment, beating her so badly that some of her teeth fly out. His mother lifts her arms to try and fight back, but they fail to respond, overpowered by the energy flowing through Gressil's side of their body.

Again and again, Gressil rattles and shakes the Labyrinth with his punches, pulverizing her head as if it were nothing more than a punching bag made of feathers. He beats Lucifer so badly that, within just a couple of minutes, her head becomes a misshapen lump of flesh and bone, unrecognizable to anyone who knew its previous appearance.

"YOU NEVER LOVED ME!" Gressil screams. "I DESERVED HAPPINESS TOO, YOU WICKED WITCH! I AM NOT INFERIOR! YOU ARE! THAT'S WHY I KILLED YOU! I'LL KILL YOU AGAIN! I'LL RIP YOU TO PIECES!!"

Finally, with one last, violent spurt of rage, Gressil jams his fingers into Lucifer's neck. He impales her throat with all his strength, grabs onto her spinal cord, and squeezes, shattering it to powder.

An instant later, Gressil yanks his hand back, tears her head off his shoulders, and hurls it against the far wall.

"AHHHH!!"

Gressil bellows at her severed head, lifts his foot, and stomps down.

_Splat._

He crushes Lucifer's head without a shred of mercy, ending her existence right then and there.

Several long seconds after, Gressil's blood-tinted vision loses some of its redness. He pants heavily, gasping for breath while gazing at the mashed-up remains of his mother's head.

Finally he sags back against the wall and sinks to the floor, ignoring the blood pouring from her severed neck.

He says nothing.

His countenance becomes despondent.

Silently, he stares ahead, while a wave of exhaustion overtakes him.

"I always hated you. I still do. Why... why can I never rid myself of you... why did the Creator put me into your claws... why did he meld us together? Did I do something reprehensible to deserve this fate?"

As Gressil speaks, the bloody stump beside his head begins to shift and morph. Slowly but surely, a new head regrows from the missing one's place. Lucifer's face takes shape as she regenerates in full, returning to her former appearance.

However, she does not scream or rant. Instead, her head lolls forward, and she falls asleep, leaving Gressil with that same silence, the only time he can ever feel at peace.

The Emperor of Chaos shivers. A tear streaks down his face as he rocks back and forth.

"I just want to get rid of her. I hate her. I can't stand her... yet nothing I do works. I'm trapped."

His pupils dilate.

"You lied to me. You said if I did as you said, I could get rid of her. But it didn't work. It made us... _one_. Why did you lie to me? Why did you whisper those false words in my ear? Were you only using me, from the very beginning?"

Gressil's teeth chatter. He numbly gazes at the bloody puddle on the ground, feeling a deep sense of emptiness in his heart.

"Don't even speak to me. You lie. You _always_ lie. You can't see the future at all. You just like torturing me. Well, I've had enough of you. I only care about _me_, now. I want to watch everyone _else_ suffer. I want them all to experience Chaos. That's what the world deserves... after what it did to me."

The prison warden's eyes harden.

"That's what they _all_ deserve."
