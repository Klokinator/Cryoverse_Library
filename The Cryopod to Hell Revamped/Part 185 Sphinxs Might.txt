Part 185: Sphinx's Might

Kar and I travel through the Labyrinth, our boots clomping against the hardened dirt floors with heavy _thuds_. Both of us keep our nanite armor ready to activate in case of an enemy attack, but thanks to my Wordsmithing, we avoid potential ambushers by literal miles.

Sitting on Kar's shoulder is his diminutive little fairy wife, Blinker. She says nothing, opting to instead observe quietly as Kar and I head toward our ultimate destination: His mother's secret base.

"**Scan.**"

I speak a word of power, making my magical energy surge into the surroundings, alerting me to a group of enemies in an upcoming junction. 

"**Distract.**"

Kar and I walk toward the T-shaped passage, where several dozen orcs sit on their asses, shooting the shit with one another.

"-what me said! Stupid Jooka no give me earned meat!"

"Stop whining! You get meat when you done guttin' them slaves!"

Two orcs argue with one another about some gruesome act of violence. Rather than listen to their conversation, I tune them out.

Kar and I walk toward the junction, but our loud footsteps don't attract any of the orcs' attention. They chat idly as the giant crocodile and delicious-tasting human walk right past them and continue on our merry way.

Half a minute later, Kar shakes his head. "Hurgh. Your ability never fails to amaze me, Turtle. Those orcs didn't see us at all."

I nod. "Solomon and I found that the intent behind my words matters. When I said 'distract,' I imagined that those orcs were unable to detect us and that they would be distracted by some banal observations. As luck would have it, they ended up being too engrossed in their conversation to notice us."

Blinker, still sitting on Kar's shoulder, pipes up, a hint of curiosity in her eyes. "Ohh! So you didn't know what would distract them, only that they shouldn't notice us?"

"Pretty much. Wordsmithing isn't just powerful; it's convenient, too."

I turn to look at Blinker, only to notice a wicked gleam in her eyes. "Sooo... your Wordsmithing can do _anything?_"

"Uh... I mean, it has a few limits. Like, I can't instantly kill anyone. I can't bring back the dead, either."

"Right, right," Blinker says, waving her hands. "Forget that boring stuff. Can it make people more attractive?"

I frown. "...Yeah. When I first discovered my power, I changed my body to look more manly. I feel a little awkward bringing it up, since it was a bit juvenile-"

"Have you ever Wordsmithed Phoebe? You know... to make her prettier!"

Blinker's question nearly makes me choke on my saliva. "Kuh! What?! Why would you even think that?!"

Suddenly, inside my mind, the phantom Phoebe I created earlier today materializes with a smirk on her face. "Yeah, Jason. _Did_ you do any Wordsmithing to me? I could swear I woke up one day with bigger boobs."

_No, absolutely not!_ I retort, dread building in the back of my mind. _I told you already; you were imagining things!_

"Haha! Sure, sure," Phoebe says, laughing into her fist. "I believe you. You're too much of a _gentleman_ to do something like that."

I stare ahead stonily, looking not _at_ Blinker, but _through_ her. My eyes glaze over as a sad realization hits me.

_Shit. Phoebe knows._

Blinker snickers like a hyena. "Hahaha! I knew it! Look at his face, Kar! He's blushing like crazy!"

Kar curls his lip upward as he looks at me. His gaze contains a hint of admiration, as well as jealousy. "If only I had such power..."

"You guys have the wrong idea..." I mutter, but it's too late. I've been found out. I'll have to apologize to Phoebe's consciousness later.

I mean, hell, I made myself better looking for Phoebe! What's wrong with a few tiny tweaks to suit my tastes? Sheesh, can't a guy catch a break around here?

...

Several minutes pass.

Blinker continues to chuckle to herself, pleased at her brilliant deductions. However, Kar falls silent. A look of annoyance spreads across his face as we wander from hallway to hallway.

"Hurgh. Was it here? Or maybe we should have taken a left at that last junction. Stupid Labyrinth! I've spent so much time on Tarus II that my instincts have become dull. I can't remember where the entrance is..."

I frown. "What do you mean, Kar? Can't you find the path to your mother's base?"

Kar slows to a stop at yet another nondescript junction. His reptilian eyes sweep from left to right as he peers into the all-encompassing darkness surrounding us. "The last time I saw Mother was six years ago, shortly before I met you. Monsters and demons instinctively know how to navigate the Labyrinth, but only if we live here permanently. Those who leave to live on planets will often lose their affinity to the Labyrinth's magic."

I nod. "Interesting. I didn't know that. Is there anything I can do to help? Maybe I can keep my eyes peeled to help you out."

Slowly, Kar nods. "Hurgh... scattered around the various corners of Hell are little holes in the Labyrinth's walls. We lizardmen shrink down and enter them, allowing us to use the secret passages for our protection. Few monsters and demons are capable of shrinking to a small enough size to enter, allowing us to conceal our movements."

"Why do you lizardmen have to hide, anyway? I thought all monsters got along with each other?"

Instead of Kar, it's Blinker who responds. Her giddy mood vanishes instantly. "Not true, mister 'smith. Every day, small-scale wars pop up here and there. Demons love preying on mermaids, and orcs hunt any Hellhounds they can find, especially if they split off from a pack. The monsters you know about are the teeniest, tiniest slice of the monster pie. There are whole worlds full of monsters even I ain't never seen."

Kar nods. "The lizardmen and crocodiles were once little more than simple-minded creatures roaming about on Earth. It was our Mother, the divine goddess, Marie, who pulled us out of the swamps and rivers. She gave us life and allowed us to walk with our heads held high among the other monsters. However, due to our incredible strength, some did not appreciate our presence."

Realization dawns on me. "Your ancestors were actual crocodiles and lizards? Four-legged reptiles that crawled around on their bellies?"

"Indeed. Most monsters arose from the primordial soup of life. Our beginnings were as undignified as that of humans, demons, and all the other sentients. Evolution and mutation gave rise to higher species. Over time, we reached our present status through blood, sweat, and tears."

Kar turns to me as a solemn look appears in his eyes.

"...at least, that's what Mother says. I dunno. I don't read many books."

I sigh. 

_Kar is, without a doubt... a giant lunkhead._

"Right. On that note, what happened to all the other crocodiles? I assume the lizardmen and crocodiles are different species, right? You're not a mutated lizardman, are you?"

Kar continues to stare at me. After several seconds, he looks away. "No. I am not. Among my people, I was, at best, an average warrior. I stood at neither the top of my clan nor the bottom. Each crocodilian warrior possessed the strength of a Demon Lord from birth, and we grew stronger over time. Because of this unique trait, many demons grew to fear and hate us. Two demon Barons, twin brothers, decided to act on their hatred. They arrived during the night and slaughtered my kin like sheep."

Kar's face twists and scrunches together, transforming into a flood of rage.

"They killed my wife and her brood. I returned to find her bloodied body laying in the grass, our spawn dead in her arms. I could only watch as... as the life ebbed from her eyes..."

Kar stares at the ground. Tears do not well up in his eyes, and neither does his voice crack. However, he speaks more slowly and inflects each word more precisely than usual.

"I... I swore that I would not die until I took revenge for their deaths. I know not what their killers look like, only their names."

I reach over and rest my hand on Kar's shoulder. "Who are they? Tell me. I'll help you find them."

Kar exhales hot air through his nose. "Duriel and Zamiel. The Battle Brothers. I know very little about them. They possess excellent weapon skills, and they love killing in all its forms. They torture, maim, and hunt prisoners for sport. They're sick, vile little creatures. When I wrap my claws around their skinny little necks..."

Kar grabs at the air, pretending for a moment that he's twisting the life out of his worst enemies. I watch, silently, saying nothing. After a few moments, Kar shakes his head.

"Hurgh. My apologies, little Turtle. I... I should not heave this load onto your shoulders."

"Don't worry about it," I say, dismissing Kar's concerns. "We're friends. If you want to get revenge, I won't stop you. Hell, I'll help in any way I can. These guys sound like true-blue bastards."

Kar raises his head and eyeballs me for a moment. "Haha... you're too kind. I know you, Turtle. You hate violence. You would never kill someone, not even for revenge. You are a better person than me."

I shake my head. "That isn't true. If I were in your shoes, and if someone hurt my family, my wife, and daughter... I don't know what I'd do. Every man draws a line in the sand somewhere. If someone crosses it, they'll pay with their life."

Kar nods. "Hurgh. Still, if I someday meet my mate's murderer... what should I do? Should I forgive them? Should I kill them? Even if I do, it won't bring my dear Pyra back. I have a new wife now, Blinker, and I love her with all my scales. Sometimes... sometimes I wonder if..."

Kar trails off. It takes me a moment to understand his unspoken words. When I do, I immediately shake my head. "No, no! Don't even think like that, Kar! Your wife would be satisfied that you've moved on. She wouldn't hate you for trying to find happiness."

Blinker crosses her arms. "Jason's right. You can't keep beating yourself up, Kar-Kar! When we find those Battle Bitches, I'll tear 'em limb from limb! I'll give 'em the ol' five-six; the seven-eight! We're in this together! Don't even think about getting all mopey on me!"

Kar sighs. "I do not have the same confidence as either of you. Every time I see myself in the mirror, guilt wracks my body. It is difficult moving on when I have lost so much."

I glance at Blinker, and she meets my gaze. For a brief instant, the two of us share our unspoken thoughts. I begin to understand how hard it was for her to marry Kar when he still suffers from the loss of his previous wife. He has yet to forget his beloved Pyra.

I wonder if Blinker has resigned herself to only ever being Kar's second lover?

...

We continue walking through the Labyrinth. This time, I try to help Kar by using my Wordsmithing more frequently.

"**Investigate. Inspect.**"

I aim my mind at the walls around us, searching for hidden tunnels and passages. However, no matter where I look, I don't detect the mouse-sized holes Kar mentioned.

"Are we in the right area?" I ask. 

Kar shrugs. "Maybe not. I barely recognize this area."

Blinker gestures to our right. "We're gettin' pretty close to the flame regions. Tons of hotheaded demons live there, plus salamanders and baby drakes."

I scratch my thin thoughtfully. "Aren't dragons extinct? Why would these little half-drakes pop up on Tarus II, and here in the Labyrinth?"

"They aren't genuine dragons," Blinker replies. "Think of drakes as mutated imitations created by Lucifer."

"Lucifer? The Emperor of Providence? Isn't she dead?"

"That's what everyone says," Blinker replies, shrugging. "I heard Lucifer died during the Energy Wars. Her son turned on her, killing her before she could react. I don't know much 'bout them demons, though. We fairies like to keep to ourselves."

"That sucks," I reply, not caring one way or the other. "Demons can't even trust their closest family members. That says something about their nature."

Kar scoffs. "Pft. Humans aren't much better, Turtle. Your species lies, cheats, and backstabs one another for pieces of gold. I've seen it many times on worlds we've liberated from the demons."

"Touche," I mutter. "So how are we going to find this teeny little hidden entrance? We've wandered through fifty miles of the Labyrinth today, and so far, we've come up empty-handed."

"Let's go back to the Core," Kar mutters. "My mother told me she hid a passage to her base somewhere nearby the center of the Labyrinth, so if we keep looking around, we're sure to find it."

I sigh. "Sure. We'll do that, then."

Without any other options, we turn around and head back. Hardly have we rounded three corners before I slow to a stop and hold my palm up. "Wait. Something's wrong."

Kar tenses up. He balls his hands into fists and glances around. "Hurgh! The Labyrinth shifted. Our return path changed."

I nod. "**Map.**"

After speaking a word of power, a mental map appears in my mind. It resembles the map Solomon's Crown used to show me, but it's incomplete and only allows me to see areas of the Labyrinth I've already explored.

"Shit. We only stood idly for a few minutes to chat, but during that time, fifteen different hallways shifted directions. It'll take us twice as long to return to the Core as it took us to get here."

"Phooey. Stupid maze," Blinker grumbles. "Whoever created this place was a real jerkface."

_Clink. Clink. Clink._

My ears pick up a strange sound in the darkness ahead. Narrowing my eyes, I speak a word of power. "**Visibility.**"

Instantly, one hundred feet of the Labyrinth's tunnels illuminate under a magical light. I take a step back in surprise as fifty skeleton warriors shamble toward us, each one equipped with bronze weapons and armor.

Kar frowns. "Hurgh. Mephisto's minions. Where did they come from? It's not often we'll see so many skeletons massing together like this."

"Doesn't matter," I reply, aiming a palm forward. "**PUSH!**"

A blast of telekinetic energy fires from my hand. The shambling bonewalkers explode into a million pieces, sending their ossein scattering backward to crash against the wall. Their swords, shields, and light leather armor clang and clatter against one another, only to fall to the ground, motionless.

"Kar blinks. "Whoa. Turtle... you... killed them!"

I shake my head. "No. Skeletons are undead. I returned them to the grave. I'm not a killer if the thing I kill has already died."

Kar nods. "Mmm. You are indeed crafty in how you bend the rules. I approve."

"What? I'm not bending anything! Everything I said was correct!"

Before Kar can retort, the sound of dozens of footsteps behind us causes our party to whip around. From the direction of the fire caves, more than forty monsters appear.

Flame salamanders, orcs, goblins, and even a minotaur emerge from the darkness. They rush toward us and slow to a stop some fifty feet away.

"I knew I smelled a stinky fleshbag!" One of the goblins shrieks. "It's meat time, boys!"

"I'll go first!" The minotaur yells. "Nobody can withstand my might! As the son of the Iron Horn Clan, I dare any of you to stand in my way!"

The other monsters pause for a moment, allowing the minotaur to take the lead. He steps in front of his comrades and spins a giant war axe in front of himself. It strikes the Labyrinth walls repeatedly, shearing off chunks of hardened dirt to spray all over the floor. "You there! The big green one! Give up the fleshbag at your side, or meet your end at my axe!"

Despite the minotaur's imposing words and chiseled body, I don't feel a drop of fear toward him. Having fought Satan, the First Emperor of all demons, and having witnessed the might of demons from the Ancient Era, this 'son of the Iron Horn Clan' appears about as threatening to me as a toddler with a spoon.

I raise my hand, intending to knock the monsters out with my Wordsmithing, but Kar shakes his head. "No, Turtle. You are my king. I cannot allow you to stoop to the level of mere ruffians. Allow me."

Kar gently plucks Blinker off his shoulder and sets her on mine. He strides forward and spreads his arms out to taunt the minotaur. "Hmph! A little soft-horn like you wishes to challenge me? Don't you know who I am?"

The minotaur flares his nostrils. "Mrrr! You're a slithering little lizard, green one! How can you beat me without using a weapon?"

Kar pauses. He scratches his head awkwardly. "Erm. Well, I have my fists. That should be enough."

The minotaur stomps his hoof. "Bahaha! Stupid reptile! No scale can withstand the might of my demonstone axe! My ancestors have passed this weapon down for fifteen generations! If you can withstand one strike from it, I'll admit defeat right here and now!"

Kar's eyes brighten. "Oh? That'll make things a lot easier. Alright. Hit me, then."

The minotaur falters. "...What?"

"Hit me. Do not worry. I'll be fine."

...

The minotaur glances back at the other monsters. He meets the gaze of one of the salamanders, who merely flicks his tongue before bowing his head.

After a moment, the minotaur returns his attention to Kar. "Hmph! If it's death you want, then death you shall receive!"

The bull-headed creature wraps his human-like hands around his axe's handle and stomps his hooves. He lowers his head slightly and charges at Kar with the blade raised above his head.

"Die!!"

Kar doesn't move. Instead, he eyes the weapon curiously, wondering what effect it'll have when it reaches him.

When he reaches striking range, the minotaur slashes downward, bringing his weapon to crash against Kar's chest.

Blinker stares, unworried, as her husband absorbs the full brunt of the minotaur's charge.

_Crack!_

The demonstone weapon, a nigh unbreakable object, shatters on impact. It explodes into countless fragments, sending shards scattering all over the goddamn place, while Kar doesn't even flinch.

The minotaur crashes against Kar's body, only to fall back as if he'd run full-speed into a steel wall. He hits the ground, hard, landing on his ass with a heavy _whump_. "Aaugh! Shit! What the devil-?!"

The minotaur takes a moment to collect himself, only to gaze in horror at the remains of his beloved axe. The broken weapon, now a facsimile of a shattered mirror, seemingly taunts him as it lays scattered about the hallway.

Kar closes his eyes. "Hmph. That is what happens when a lowborn like you dares raise a weapon against the King of All Monsters."

Suddenly, the minotaur's eyebrows jump in astonishment. "Th-the King of All Monsters?! Could it be...?"

Behind him, several goblins stare, their eyes as wide as saucers. "Shrakh! No way! Ain't that lizard Kar? THE Kar?"

The other goblin nods hurriedly. "Kar? He's the new Sphinx! Stinky badgers! What're we doin' tryin' to pick a fight with him?! If we piss the Sphinx off, we're dead meat!"

One of the salamanders, the one who met the gaze of the minotaur only moments before, quickly skitters forward and bows its head respectfully. "Oh, great Kar! Kar the benevolent! Kar the magnificent! Please, forgive us for our ignorance, for we did not know it was you!"

Kar smirks. He crosses his arms and assumes a haughty stance. "Hurgh. Stupid little weaklings. Geez. I was just taking my pet human out for a walk, and you chumps wanted to make trouble for me. I oughta rip off your limbs and feed them to the basilisks."

I frown. _Pet human?_

The salamander repeatedly kowtows, smacking its head against the ground. "Please forgive us! Show us mercy! We would never intentionally attack someone as magnificent as you!"

Kar uncrosses his arms and waves a hand flippantly. "Very well. Hurgh. If you do me a small favor, I'll forgive ya."

"Yes! Of course! Anything to please you, your eminence!! We are at your service!"

The salamander shoots a glance toward the other monsters. Having understood his intent, all of them run forward to bow before Kar.

"Heh. My pet human and I are looking for holes in the Labyrinth walls. Have any of you pups seen one?"

The goblins glance around. They shrug, not knowing what Kar is referring to. After all, in the darkness of the Labyrinth, observing the path directly in front of oneself is hard enough, let alone tiny holes in the wall.

However, to my surprise, an orc raises his head. "Ooh! Me know! Me seen one! Not far from here!"

Kar nods. "Good! Good. I'm glad you maggots haven't disappointed me. Now, I'll let you live. Take me there."

The orc nods, eager to please. He quickly jumps to his feet and walks toward us, a look of excitement on his face. "It big honor to work for strong boss like you! How you break big axe?"

Kar shrugs. "Dunno. I didn't even feel it."

"Ooh!"

All the monsters crow in admiration. Even the minotaur, still miffed at his humiliating defeat, can't help but shoot a gaze of awe at his senior crocodile.

I understand what happened. As demons increase their rank, their bodies become more resilient and durable in every way. Their minds sharpen, and their looks typically improve, too. 

Monsters are similar. Kar now stands atop the monster hierarchy, reaching the level of a Demon Duke. His body is powerful enough to resist a cannonball to the face, let alone an axe-swing from a Lord-level minotaur. If a tank from my era shot him with an artillery shell, it's unlikely he'd be injured at all.

He's become far stronger than when he first obtained the Sphinx's power six years ago, having absorbed all of its essence and practiced daily.

Similarly, so have I.

"Lead the way," Kar says, gesturing to the orc. "I ain't got all day, whelp. I've wasted half my morning scouring these halls."

...

Two hours later.

The orc, Kugol, leads us down several winding passages. The salamanders fall into place behind us, keeping their heads bowed respectfully while plodding along on all four of their legs. The big-headed monsters look almost comical, given how defeated they look.

"Right here," Kugol says, stopping before a wall. I frown as we walk up to the spot he indicates.

"Are you kidding me?" I mutter.

We passed this area earlier, but I didn't notice the hole. It seems my Wordsmithing isn't as omniscient as I first thought. Ugh. There's nothing worse than needlessly wasting my time.

Kar, however, doesn't remember passing through this hallway. "Hurgh. Thank you, little orc. You deserve praise for being such a smart warrior."

The orc clasps his hands together. He grins, revealing his two lower tusks that stick out of his mouth. "Hoo! Please, big Kar! Me no want live in Hell! Me want serve big strong leader like you!"

Kar nods. "Mmm. It just so happens that I've been looking for capable warriors. Go to the Labyrinth Core. Give them this."

Kar reaches into a pouch on his belt. He pulls out half a dozen circular coins, each crafted from wood with a carving of a crocodile's head on their fronts. 

He hands the wooden coins to each of the orcs, and the first two Salamanders who first came to bow before him. "Tell 'em Kar sent you."

The monsters stare, wide-eyed, at the fancy-looking coins. "B-but! If we do that, those lasers will kill us!" One of the salamanders sputters. "We can't approach the Core! Everyone knows it's too dangerous!"

"You'll be fine," Kar says, dismissing their concerns. "Now, go! I've got shit to do, pup!"

Immediately, the trailing monsters quiver in fear at Kar's sudden outburst. They gently pluck the coins from Kar's palm and scurry away, leaving the two of us in silence.

I exhale a breath of turbid air. "_Pet human_, eh?"

Kar snorts. "I'm the brains and the brawn, Turtle. That makes you my pet."

"I don't know about that."

"Hurgh. I do."

Ignoring my annoyance, Kar returns his attention to the hole in the wall. He motions with his hands and begins casting a spell. Moments later, his body rapidly shrinks, transforming him from a ten-foot-tall behemoth into an eight-inch-tall midget.

"Your turn!" Kar squeaks from my feet. "Let's get a move on!"

Blinker flutters off my shoulder and lands beside him. Now, she towers over him by over an inch, making him the small fry in their relationship.

"Alright. Here goes nothing. **Shrink!**"

I speak a word of power, and seconds later, my skin, bones, internal organs, and even my clothing and equipment start to compress together, shrinking me to Kar's height.

A minute later, I stand just under six inches tall. To my dismay, even shrunken down, Kar still towers over me.

"Hurgh. I wasn't sure if your magic would work, Turtle. Good work."

I nod. "Hey, those monsters... won't they get shredded by the laser turrets? Aren't you worried about them?"

My surprisingly squeaky voice unnerves me, but I ignore it.

Surprised by my topic switch, Kar shrugs. "Rabbit made those coins for me. They allow their holder to pass through the turrets without activating them. Pretty handy!"

"Huh. Hope thinks of everything..." I mutter.

...

Our adventuring party enters the hole in the wall. Along the way, we find ourselves besieged by freakishly huge bugs, spiders, and all other manners of creepy, crawly critters.

Kar dispatches them with his earth manipulation magic, while I merely kill them with Wordsmithing. A shudder goes down my spine whenever we find a spider. I'm deathly afraid of arachnids... especially when they're half as tall as me!

It takes us two days to reach our destination. I'm astonished to find that the tunnels inside the walls are a hundred times more elaborate than I first expected, with steel reinforcement along the walls, floor, and ceiling to prevent the Labyrinth from regrowing its insides.

"My mother is a brilliant woman!" Kar boasts. "She knows all about this Labyrinth. She helped us lizardmen engineer our secret tunnels so demons and monsters wouldn't be able to find us. I can't wait for you to meet her!"

Blinker pipes up, a hint of nervousness in her voice. "Oh, gosh, Kar! I just realized I'm about to meet my mother-in-law! Eeeh! I forgot to wear something cute and eye-catching! Oh no, what if she thinks I'm ugly? She might reject our marriage!"

Kar laughs. "Hah! My mother is a kind, benevolent woman. She would never think such a terrible thing, let alone say it. Her kindness is overflowing, like a river in a desert. When you meet her, you'll understand just how great she is!"

I nod. "Marie sounds like a delightful person. I can't wait to meet her."

...

"We're here!"

Kar slows to a stop before a massive chamber; one also plated with metal to prevent the Labyrinth from growing back. However, despite its imposing size, it only stands a couple of feet tall and wide. Size is only relative to one's height, after all.

I glance around the empty room and frown. "What do you mean? There's nothing here."

"Haha... my mother is a genius," Kar says, puffing up his chest. "See that little bump over there?"

I follow Kar's gaze as he gestures toward a tiny depression in the metal-covered ground. Barely a tenth of an inch deep, it didn't register as important in my mind, not until Kar pointed it out.

"What about it?"

"That is a teleportation glyph!" Kar says, chuckling. "My mother placed a few of these throughout the Labyrinth. If we step on it, it will take us to her base!"

"Oh," I say, feeling slightly impressed. I walk over to the dip in the metal and glance down. "How do we activate it?"

"Just step on it. When you do-"

Kar starts to say something. Before he finishes, I step on the pad, only to stumble forward awkwardly as my surroundings instantly shift!

I flop forward and fall on my knees, hitting a smoothly-tiled floor with a thud.

"Gah!"

A squeak of pain escapes my lips. I quickly jump to my feet, blushing like a madman at my awkwardness. A moment later, my eyes widen.

The smooth, metallic room I stood in just moments before is gone. In its place is a truly colossal, gigantic, unthinkably vast chamber. Brilliantly bright light shines on me from every direction, amplified by the pure white coloring inside the room. I stagger slightly as my eyes adjust to the room's brightness.

"OH? WHO'S THIS?"

A voice as loud as an explosion smashes into me from above, nearly blowing out my eardrums. I fall on my ass and wince as I raise my eyes upward.

There, a human as tall as a mountain stands idly, staring at me with only a slight hint of curiosity.

"WHO ARE YOU?" The speaker asks.

Due to their ear-shattering loudness, it takes me a moment to recognize the speaker is a woman, someone with long, brown hair.

"J-Jason!" I squeak, trying to come anywhere close to the person's volume. "I came here with Kar!"

"OH! IT'S BEEN A WHILE SINCE I SAW LITTLE KAR," The woman says, her voice still buffeting me like a hurricane. "WHERE ARE MY MANNERS? MY NAME IS REBECCA! NICE TO MEET YOU, JASON."

I squint and gaze up at the woman's face. Due to the shift from the Labyrinth's darkness into this blindingly bright new world, it takes me a few moments, but eventually, I manage to identify her appearance.

The woman wears thick-rimmed glasses and a white lab coat. Her curly brown hair falls to her shoulders, and her big, wide eyes give her mature face an aura of innocence and curiosity.

However, what sends a bolt of lightning through my heart is a sudden realization that I know this woman! I recognize her!

She's Rebecca... the same woman who put me in the Cryopod six years ago!

"Turtle," Kar says, his voice coming from behind. "You should have waited for me to finish explaining. Hurgh. Judging by your expression, you are not used to the loudness of enlarged beings. Return your body to its original size, and the pain will disappear."

I nod, my thoughts still racing. "I... I... yeah. Sure. One moment. **Normalize.**"

Within seconds, my body begins to expand. I lean forward and gasp in horror as I suddenly realize our teleportation platform was a pedestal in the middle of the room! I'm going to fall!

Before my rapidly expanding body tumbles over the edge, I feel a pair of hands grab me by my chest and slow my descent. "Whoa, whoa! Careful, there!"

Rebecca catches me an instant before disaster strikes. She eases me to the ground, where I finish morphing into my seven-foot-tall height.

I assume a standing position, only to find that Rebecca's former mountain-like body is now a full two feet smaller than mine. She raises her eyes to examine me, only to take a step back. "Oh, wow! I didn't expect you to be so tall, Mister Jason, sir!"

I blink. "Rebecca. You used to work at Cryotek industries. Do you remember me?"

A moment passes. Rebecca frowns as she stares at me. "Uhm... Cryotek... Cryotek... gosh, did I? That was such a long time ago..."

As she wracks her brain, I suddenly have an urge to smack myself. _Ugh! That was over 100,000 years ago! How would Rebecca remember events from so long ago? No, more importantly, how is she still alive? Shouldn't she have died ages ago?_

Kar enlarges himself, transforming once again into his ten-foot-tall reptilian body. Unlike me, he deftly hops off the teleportation pedestal mid-transformation and lands on his feet, thus riding out the rest of his enlargement without anyone else's help.

"Hurgh. Little Orchid, I have brought back the human my mother wished to see. He is the Hero she has been looking for!"

Rebecca's eyes light up like the 4th of July. She takes a step back and re-evaluates my tall, muscular body. "Ohhh! Now I remember you! Yes, Marie has mentioned you a few times before, but I never got to see any pictures. Wow, you are very handsome in person!"

I chuckle. "Eh... hehe... uhm, we've met before. You helped put me in a cryogenic freezing pod. That was 100,000 years ago, though... so it's no wonder you don't recognize me."

Rebecca nods. "Mmm. When I transferred my mind, the memory backups from my biological life became a little fragmented. I have a perfect memory from the moment of my mechanical inception, but before that, everything is a little bit hazy. Sorry!"

I raise an eyebrow. "What? Mechanical inception? Biological mind? You're... not a human?"

Rebecca nods. "Oh, yes, of course, I am! It's just, due to limitations on the human body, lifespan, and other such things, all of us scientists working under Marie placed our souls into android vessels. You'll understand, soon enough."

Rebecca gestures behind me. I follow her movements, turn around, and stare in disbelief.

The transporter room Kar and I have arrived in isn't even worth mentioning compared to the view through the window outside. While several computer terminals sit inside our arrival point, several thousand people walk to and fro outside our room. I watch them as they go about their day, each one wearing a white lab coat or military fatigues. 

Floating robotic drones follow along behind the head of each person, serving as their assistants. Some scientists stand at computer terminals and write reports with their minds, while others operate futuristic-looking machines, each of which I can't begin to comprehend. One device summons a robotic arm in midair, but the scientist merely examines it for a moment before shaking his head and tossing it in a garbage bin.

As I stare blankly at the facility, Rebecca's voice pipes up behind me.

"Welcome to the Remnant Oasis!" Rebecca says, her voice tinged with pride. "We're all that's left of humanity's glorious past!"

I swallow ten thousand questions, unable to voice them for fear of embarrassing myself.

"Wow... this place is really something."
