Solomon's Crown worked its magic, allowing a new vision to appear. Once Jason's eyes adjusted, he quickly discovered he was no longer in Uzziel's body. Instead, his head hovered less than a foot off the ground. He walked along, taking tiny little steps, and every so often, a red-skinned arm with five chubby fingers reached out to grab things in front of him.

Jason was in an imp's body. But which one?

"Oy, Satan!" A nasally voice addressed him, helpfully answering Jason's question. He turned to face the speaker, who turned out to be several inches taller than him. Jason hadn't a clue who he or she was. All of the little red buggers looked identical to him.

"Find grub?" Satan asked. Jason's mouth moved along with his, just as it did when he was inside Uzziel's body.

"Mhm! Big and juicy! Agares lift rock and they crawl everywhere."

Jason's stomach growled. "Good find. Me hungry."

Jason toddled after the other imp, whose name he still didn't know. His stumpy little legs were so small that hopping over pebbles and stones made him feel like an Olympic gymnast. _Christ, seeing things from an imp's perspective makes me appreciate standing seven feet tall,_ Jason thought.

The air was noticeably chillier than when he was last inside Uzziel's body. Clouds obscured the sun above, and every shadow sent a shiver down his back. If Jason had to guess, he'd say several months or years had passed since the last vision.

A thinning forest with many dead trees loomed ahead. Each one seemed ten times taller than it should be, likely due to Jason's short stature. Satan glanced at the treetops above, showing several crows perched on various branches. All of them eyed Jason with cold, calculating eyes. They were scavengers, and they were waiting for his death.

Dry grass cracked and popped under their feet. Jason turned to look at the other imp. "Bael. You not cold?"

"Nah. Me fine. You guys bunch of wimps."

Satan rubbed his arms and shivered, forcing Jason to mimic him. A tingling of worry seemed to buzz in the back of his mind, as if time was running out, and he didn't know what to do.

Eventually, after climbing over tree roots and sliding under logs, Bael and Jason stopped in a small clearing where the rest of the imps clapped their hands giddily.

"Ah, Satan. You hungry?" One of the imps clutched several wriggling bugs in one of his hands. "Me find big blue beetles. Whole bunch of them!"

"Mmm. Them look tasty," Jason said, salivating at the little— no! _Augh! Gross! Don't tell me Satan's going to eat—_ Jason's thoughts screamed in desperation. _AHHH no, no, NO!!_

Satan lunged his hand out and grabbed the six-legged critter with his fat fingers and drooled. "You find best bugs, Agares."

"Yeah, me do. Not many around."

As Satan brought the bug closer to his mouth, violent spasms of fear washed through Jason's mind. _No, god, please! Don't eat it! Oh, for fuck's sake!_

_CHOMP._

Half of the wriggling insect exploded in Jason's mouth. His teeth mangled and chewed it into progressively tinier pieces as its guts spilled through his teeth. Every time a new chunk of the bug hit his tongue, Jason desperately tried to spit it out, but he couldn't! He couldn't leave the vision!

_Solomon!! You did this deliberately! You wanted me to suffer!!_ Jason thought bitterly.

When Satan finally swallowed, the gory hunks of the bug rolled down Jason's throat, sticking to his esophagus as they fell. Satan smacked his lips, _Jason's_ lips, and just as Jason felt relief that it was over... Satan shoved the rest of the bug inside.

_CHOMP._

_I want to die!! Please, God, kill me now! I don't deserve this!!_ Jason screamed silently.

...

By the time the fifth insect went down, Jason felt numb. Perhaps it was Stockholm syndrome, but he was almost growing to enjoy the taste. No, that couldn't be right. It was awful. _Come on, Me. Take your mind off the bitter flavor. Think of Phoebe. Think of Daisy,_ Jason coached himself.

While Jason tried to cope with the horrible things Satan had stuffed in his mouth, Satan smacked his lips pleasantly. "Very tasty. Want more."

Agares shook his head. "That all me found. Too cold. Bugs hiding very sneaky."

The imps chittered and chattered amongst themselves for a few minutes, but then Jason cocked his head. "Hmm? Where Lucy? Where Deebs?"

Satan glanced around, and Jason counted the imps. Two of them were, indeed, missing.

"They go explore. Lucy say she saw cave," Agares replied. "Maybe cave warm. Diablo say he want protect Lucy."

"Cave? Hmm..." Satan trailed off and tapped his lip. "Me want see. Where they go?"

Agares pointed toward a nearby mountain. "That way."

"Okay. We go take look."

All the imps nodded and followed after Jason. Agares and he trotted toward the mountain, saying nothing to each other as they walked. The muttering of various imps behind them turned into a dull hum as they whispered about this and that. Jason ignored them.

After several minutes, Agares' eyes darted to the side. "Oooh!" He trotted to the left and leaned down beside a stump to look at something.

"What see?" Jason asked.

Agares grinned and plucked a tiny animal off the tree bark. It wiggled in his hand but didn't try to flee. "Look! Lissard!"

"Look tasty. You gonna eat it?"

"No! Lissards are friend!" Agares shielded it protectively. "They eat bugs. Just like us!"

Jason shook his head. "Yeah. Sure. You weird."

Agares petted the little creature, then placed it back on the log and puttered over to Jason. "We almost there. Big mountain just past trees."

After a few more minutes of walking, they stepped out of the tree cover and entered the shadow of a grassy cliff. Unlike Mount Sinai, this one appeared much smaller, perhaps only a few hundred feet tall. Calling it a 'mountain' might be giving it too much credit, but it was also a little too big to be a hill...

Whatever.

Amidst the greenery coating the mega-hill, two red spots halfway up popped out like black dots on a white piece of paper. Lucy and Diablo slowly headed toward the top, but the tall grass made it hard for them to move. Their climbing was slow and labored as they waded through obstacles in their path.

"Yo! Diablo!" Bael stepped ahead of the group and yelled, but his shrill voice barely carried up the hill. "Hey! Ugly! Stupid!"

Bael howled several insults, but the climbers didn't hear him. He gave up and tossed his hands in the air. "Buttheads. Hate when peeps ignore me. Sheesh."

"We follow them," Jason said, gesturing toward the fellow imps. "Nothing better do."

Agares nodded. "Okay. Sound good— ah! Valac! What you doing?!"

Jason turned around to spot one of the imps holding a dead rabbit by the ears. Unlike the others, this imp's appearance was somewhat deformed. His jaw jutted forward noticeably, giving him one hell of an underbite. He didn't answer, but instead opted to slowly claw out the rabbit's innards, throwing them on the ground bit by bit.

"Valac. You not waste food. That bad," Jason said. "Stop it."

The creepy imp raised his eyes to meet Jason's. "It no scream."

"Huh? The bunny?"

"Yes."

Jason opened his mouth to say something else but thought better of it. "Never mind. Let's go..."

Soon, all the imps began climbing the hill. A quick glance backward revealed Valac trailing the rest. He was much slower since he continued to hold the rabbit.

_What the hell is his deal?_ Jason wondered.

Jason didn't get an answer. As the imps ascended, one of the two they were following glanced down and spotted them. He (or she) waved, then grabbed the other and pointed at them.

It didn't take long for them to climb down and meet the group.

"Why you follow us?" Diablo asked. Jason only knew it was him because his voice was slightly deeper than Lucy's, though it was still pretty squeaky.

"Me heard you find cave. Me want see."

Diablo shook his head. "No. Very dark. Too scary. Me help Lucy look for berries now. Forget cave."

Jason crossed his arms. "Me leader. Me decide if scary or not."

Diablo tried to nod coolly, but a hint of fear bubbled in his eyes. "O-okay. You big boss."

Jason followed after Bael and Lucy, noting with interest that she took the lead. Sometimes she pointed out landmarks or scratches she had left in the ground, but when Jason followed her gaze, he didn't see anything until they drew closer. Her eyesight was spectacular.

Eventually, all the imps arrived at a small hole in the side of the mountain. Standing perhaps five-feet-tall, the most surprising thing about the opening was the heat warming Jason's skin. As if the depths of the Earth were yawning, a mild warmth radiated out, allowing him to stop shivering.

Still, that familiar buzzing alarm poked at the back of his mind.

_Don't enter,_ Jason thought to himself. _Diablo's right. This cave is dangerous._

"Diablo right," Satan said, as if he could hear Jason's words. "Too scary. Warm, though. We stay here tonight."

Someone shoved Jason aside. Bael strode several feet into the cave and puffed out his chest. "Pft. You guys big scared! Warm means comfy! Me go into cave alone!"

Several imps fidgeted. Something about Bael's haughty tone made Satan narrow his eyes. "Me no scared."

"Yeah-huh!"

"Nuh-uh." Jason stomped over to Bael and glared at him. "You no leader. Me leader."

"Leader too scared go inside," Bael said, snickering to himself. "Hehe, you little kitty cat."

Unsurprisingly, Satan took the bait. Jason turned around and beckoned to the other imps. "Me not kitty. Come on. We go deeper."

Despite all the warning signs, the other imps nodded. Everyone entered the cave, staying near to the wall as they walked. Within a minute, darkness swallowed them. The sounds of breathing, bare feet shuffling against the floor, and hands scraping the walls were all Jason heard as they traveled.

The temperature rose. Thanks to the lack of a breeze, soon Jason started sweating bricks. He mopped his face down, realizing that they might be heading toward an active volcano or an underground lava geyser.

However, the thought that this heat came from the Earth itself was far too comforting to be the truth. Jason began to wonder if he was hearing things. The distant sound of breathing made him twitch as he walked. _Where are we going? Shouldn't we turn back?_

"Excited?" Bael whispered from behind him, quiet enough that only Jason could hear him.

"No," Jason replied, keeping his voice low. "We need turn back."

"Me gots good feeling, boss."

"Me got real _bad_ feeling," Jason muttered, his voice hoarse.

Suddenly, as Jason stepped forward, the floor disappeared.

"Wha-?!"

Jason let out a yelp of fright as he fell into an abyss. Before he could scream, he hit a wall and rolled downward.

_THUD! CRACK!_

Jason bounced off something unseen and smashed into the ground. His body came to an abrupt halt as waves of agony coursed through him.

It took several moments before Jason could think.

Everything hurt — his body ached. Jason thought he might have broken something...

"Nnn..."

Satan let out a pitiful whimper. The bumpy, spiky ground pressed against his face and stomach, taunting him.

After a few moments, Jason froze. His blood turned to ice.

_I'm not alone._

Jason raised his head slowly. In front of him, something absolutely shitfucking _massive_ shifted around. It barely made a sound, but even in pitch-black darkness, Jason could see the slightest hint of motion.

Whatever the _thing_ was, it was gigantic. A hundred feet tall. Perhaps more.

"**Hmm?**"

A single grunt rumbled in the darkness. The beast spoke, its tone so deep that words couldn't describe it. Jason's bones rattled from the weight of its voice. He slowly curled into a ball to make himself as tiny as possible.

_Don't see me... please... oh god... what the hell is this monster?!_ Jason thought desperately.

"**Is someone there?**"

The voice spoke again. It knew.

The air shifted. Two specks of orange appeared, perhaps twenty feet away. They slowly moved toward Jason, sending waves of terror through his body.

Eyes.

Two eyes, each one as big as Jason's head, with slits for pupils, narrowed in on his tiny, pathetic form. Light emanated from them, illuminating his naked, defenseless body.

Jason trembled, unable to speak.

Whatever the creature was, it knew Jason was here.

"**Do you think yourself invisible? You shine as bright as a star to my penetrating gaze.**"

"I... I..."

Satan tried to reply. Instead, he stuttered and stammered.

From the entrance above, a tiny voice squeaked out. "Hey! Boss! You okay?!"

The gargantuan monster flicked its gaze upward. "**Others? Invaders, come to fight? Perhaps I should retaliate.**"

"N-no..." Jason whispered, flinching as the creature stared at him again. "N-no fight... no hurt, please..."

"**Your scent is unknown to me,**" the monster said. "**What do you call yourself, little red blob?**"

"I-i-imp! Me called imp... not taste good! Very nasty!"

The monster snorted and recoiled slightly. "**That name, I have not heard it. I know of rat and bat and cat, of deer and bear and boar... but imp is one I've never heard before. Stand up, 'imp,' so that I may study you more closely.**"

Jason nodded, unable to deny the creature's request. He grunted in pain and staggered to his feet, leaving his broken right arm to hang limp. He could only clutch his shoulder to try and stifle the pain.

"**Ugly little wretch. Weak and scared. You're no threat. Why have you come?**"

Jason shook his head slowly, unsure of what to say. "Cold outside. F-felt warm in cave. Did not m-mean wake up... very sorry!"

"**You stumbled in by accident? Tch. I thought you were agents of the angels, but you're little more than skinny rats. Leave me. I wish to rest.**"

The monster started to turn its head away.

"Angels? Big ugly chickens? You no like?"

The monster stopped before fully turning away. It snorted as if to chuckle. A puff of fire burst from its nostrils, illuminating its face and allowing Jason a glimpse of its body. Now Jason saw that it was a massive dragon, pure black across its entire body, except for its glowing orange eyes. Its scales, black as the night, seemed to swallow the light from the fire instantly, returning darkness to the room.

The size of the dragon's head made Jason swallow a lump of fear. It could devour him whole without a second thought. Worse, however, was the sense that he had seen it before. The dragon appeared familiar, but he couldn't place the memory...

"**Angels are the enemies of all living creatures,**" the dragon said. "**You. Me. Everything. They slew my people one by one. They would kill me as well, were they to discover my existence.**"

The dragon slowly turned to look behind Jason toward the opening above. Several rocks crumbled downward, bounced off the walls, and hit the ground below. A grinding sound followed as an imp slid down the slope. They hit the ground a few feet behind Jason, and squeaked out, "Oy, boss! Boss! Are you—"

The speaker, Bael, froze mid-sentence. Jason couldn't see him, but the dragon's gaze fixated behind him.

Bael whimpered. "Buh-buh... boss?"

"Me here," Jason whispered. The dragon hadn't tried to eat him yet. It didn't appear aggressive, though Jason couldn't know if that was what Satan was thinking. It was a terrifying monster, to be sure, but if it wanted them dead, they wouldn't still be standing.

"**Another imp. How many more will arrive? Call to them. Tell them to come.**"

The dragon's orders were absolute. Jason nodded slowly, forced himself to turn his back to the creature, and yelled toward the others, "M-me okay! Everyone come!"

As if the floodgates of Hell had opened, several sounds of sliding followed in quick succession. Unlike Jason's accidental stumbling into the abyss, the other imps managed to glide to the bottom without hurting themselves.

Every single one of them froze in terror when they arrived, as the dragon scanned the group with its reptilian eyes.

"**One... two... three... hmm. Why are fifteen imps traveling together?**"

"Big chickens kick us around. Mudder say we leave."

Satan seemed to have adjusted to the fact the dragon wasn't going to eat him. He took this in stride, but still flinched when it lurched toward him.

"**You lived among the angels? I knew I smelled their scent.**"

"Chickens not our friends," Satan replied. "Chickens beat us. Make us work. Kick around and hurt."

"**I understand,**" the dragon replied. "**The angels enjoy playing the role of gods. They summon creatures from the void to serve them, then toss us aside when they tire of us. They did so for my people, and now for yours.**"

A moment passed. The dragon snorted again, sending plumes of fire over their heads. "**Bah. The old wars are a distant memory. Perhaps I could kill the angels, but I haven't the will to try. Sleep was comforting. I dreamed of my mother, and my brothers, and my sisters too. I dreamed of those lost and those who would never see the light of day.**"

Satisfied with itself, the dragon sighed. "**Haah... I decided long ago to burrow under this mountain and live my life in solitude. The bedrock, infused with the mana of the Earth, concealed my presence well. After millions of years, the angels have yet to find me.**"

Satan cocked his head. "You hide? Scared of chickens? Like me?"

"**Scared... frightened... perhaps... perhaps not...**"

The dragon pulled back and reared up on its legs, stretching itself to the ceiling above. Several explosions went off as its joints popped into place — the behemoth grunted with satisfaction.

"**I am Leviathan, the last of the dragons. For every brother and sister that died, my strength increased as their mana spread among the survivors. With all of them gone, I am at the peak of my power. However... I consider myself a practical thinker. Battling the angels was a futile endeavor. Not only would I need to combat them, but also their allies, the Titans. I might win. I might not. Other than satisfying a sense of justice, what purpose would killing them serve?**"

Satan glanced at the other imps. All of them stared with rapt attention at the dragon, afraid to take their eyes off it. Satan returned his gaze to Leviathan.

"Words too big. Not understand."

Leviathan stared at the ceiling. Seconds passed — a minute.

Slumping to the ground, he lowered his body and returned his attention to the imps. "**The angels are afraid of losing their power. Whenever they form a sentient being, it turns against them. That's why they made pathetic servants like you. Weak, unable to fight back. Easy to push around.**"

The dragon curled back his lips, exposing his teeth in a sinister grin. Even in the darkness of the cave, the pure white stood out, illuminated by the faint light coming from his eyes.

"**Hehe... I have a great idea. A little... _poetic justice_, if you will. Wouldn't it be funny if, for the third time, one of their creations turned against them? What a twist of irony that would be.**"

Satan blinked and cocked his head. "What mean?"

"**I've decided to take you under my wing, little 'imp.' You can't fight the angels as you are now, but, in time, that will change. I'll make it change. You will battle them in my absence and make the angels suffer. Those who took my family from me... you will take theirs from them. Come. Step forward. I have a gift for each of you.**"

Jason nodded. His body moved on its own, unwilling to disobey the dragon's orders. One step became two, then three. Without intending to, he took twenty steps forward. The dragon's glowing orange orbs were closer now than ever before. From the darkness, a slight movement caught Jason's eye. Leviathan pulled his head back and thrust a massive claw forward, one that stood ten times taller than Jason. His four-taloned appendage was barely visible in the dark, but its frightening size made him wet himself.

The dragon could kill Jason in an instant. Instead, he wanted to give him a gift. What could it be?

Jason found out as the claw lightly nudged his forehead.

Cosmic power flooded into Jason's mind, body, and soul. Waves of mana coursed through his veins, bursting every blood vessel in his body, rupturing his heart, and reforming them a moment later. He tried to scream, but couldn't. One moment he was standing, and in the next, he ended up on the ground.

Bones snapped — muscles popped. Jason writhed and wriggled, unable to control his spasming form. The dragon's energy burned like a wildfire, searing his skin off and growing it back. The cycle repeated over and over for what felt like an eternity. In reality, the process barely took a minute.

The pain was mind-shattering agony, yet also exquisite ecstasy. Once it faded away, violent rapture embroiled Jason's thoughts. The most wonderful food, best sex, and memorable moments of his life did little to compare to Leviathan's power.

Jason knew, from the most primal corners of his mind, that the power Leviathan had shared with him was but a sliver of his whole — a fraction of a fraction.

Jason had only tasted Leviathan's power... but he wanted more.

Slowly, Jason staggered to his feet. A rush of sensory information flooded his mind. His hearing, more acute, detected the slightest whimpers of fear from the imps behind him. The dragon's drumming heart was like an anvil crashing into the Earth.

Jason's sight, smell, and hearing increased tenfold. Suddenly, the darkness melted away. He saw far more clearly, as though several small candles illuminated the room. The dragon, once a specter in the gloom, now became visible. Jason beheld his majesty in its entirety. While crouched low to take up as little space as possible, Leviathan's body was hundreds of feet from his head to his tail. His wings, though folded against his back, must stretched a hundred feet to the right and the left.

Jason had never seen something so incredible in all his life. Leviathan, more than any being Jason had ever imagined, was a dark god, capable of slaughtering all those who slighted him.

He chose Jason. He favored him.

_I'm... lucky,_ Jason thought.

Turning around, Jason faced the imps behind him. Suddenly, it hit him: he had grown. He was at least four feet tall now.

The imps squinted to stare at him, but their vision was far worse than his. It was likely they could only make out his hazy red figure in the darkness.

"B-big boss? That you?" Agares asked.

"Yep. It's me," Jason replied. His voice was suddenly much deeper, dramatically so. The change startled him, but he adjusted to it. "Step forward. Leviathan is going to power us up."

The dragon chuckled. "**Hm. Hm. Your cognitive abilities have increased. Likewise, you have gained magical powers. You are too weak to handle any more of my power, but you have enough to cause some mischief. Doubtless, your abilities are already far beyond your comprehension. It will take time for you to adjust.**"

Agares inched toward the dragon. Leviathan reached his claw out and touched the imp's head. Just as Jason had, Agares stumbled onto his back, wheezing and gasping. His body slowly increased in size, his skin melted off and grew back, and within a minute, he lay on the ground, breathing hard.

When Agares rose to stand beside Jason, Jason looked his naked body up and down. Like Jason's, it coursed with raw energy. His eyes flicked around the room to examine the dragon, and he turned to Jason.

"Satan. What... what's happened to me? To you?"

Jason smiled. "We've been given a second lot on life, chum. We share a common enemy with the dragon. I'll explain after he boosts the others."

Both of them stood and watched. Bael came next. Diablo. Lucy. Belial. Valac.

After all fifteen of them finished, they examined one another. Power flowed from their veins, but unlike before, they had distinct appearances and stood apart visually. Bael towered a foot above the rest. Hair covered Diablo's face. Belial transformed into a sexy goddess.

Jason's eyes scanned all of them, past the four-breasted figure of Lucy, toward the final remaining demon, Valac. Unlike the rest of them, he continued to writhe on the ground. His wounds only partially healed, leaving patches of bone visible. Two arms sprouted under the others, giving him four in total, in addition to his legs.

Valac's skin hissed. Pieces began to flake off. Jason glanced at the dragon. "Hey! What's wrong with Valac? He's falling apart!"

Leviathan shook his head. "**Someone was bound to reject my gift. Count yourselves lucky that only one of you will perish. In exchange, the rest will experience power they couldn't imagine.**"

Jason walked over and knelt next to Valac. Before he could try and touch him, he lunged out and grabbed Jason's ankle with a skeletal hand, startling him.

"Nehehe.... endless pain and the fear of death, enrich my body and give me breath. Send to darkness, put to sleep, burn the bodies, their souls I _reap_."

Jason jumped backward in fright. Valac chuckled quietly and pulled himself into a sitting position as even more of his skin began to melt away. Soon, only bits of guts and entrails stuck to his bones underneath, held to him by the thinnest pieces of muscled sinew.

Valac rose. He stood nearly as tall at Bael, but unlike the massive demon behind Jason, Valac appeared thin and gangly, due to being a walking skeleton. His teeth chattered, leaving Jason to wonder how he could speak without a voice box.

"V-Valac? Are you alright?"

The skeleton cocked his head. "I am fine. Sublime. Don't worry about me. We have angels to kill, hehe..."

Leviathan narrowed his eyes. "**Interesting. This one has adapted. All of you have. By now, you should be able to sense the mana within yourselves. Reach inside. Summon your aspects.**"

Everyone glanced at each other for a moment and nodded. They all began weaving signs simultaneously. Lucy, the four-breasted demoness, gasped and recoiled as a third eye emerged on her forehead. Belial changed shape, morphing into Diablo. Valac hissed as a massive bone-scythe appeared in his grasp, along with a glowing blue lantern in the other.

As for Jason, when he formed the signs, a magical scroll appeared in midair. It hovered for a moment, then fell, but he caught it before it hit the ground.

Jason unraveled the scroll to read it, but nothing appeared. It was blank.

Despite this, Jason smiled. "Ah, so that's how it is."

_I don't know why I'm smiling. I haven't a clue what the scroll does— ah, right... I forgot. I'm Jason. Not Satan. I keep mixing up my mind with his. These visions were blurring the line between reality and fantasy for me. I was starting to lose the ability to tell one from the other,_ Jason thought.

Satan raised his fist. "Alright, you bunch of idiots. Listen up. I've got a plan."

"A plan?" Belial asked. She and Lucy appeared disinterested in their nakedness, despite standing amongst a group full of men. Perhaps they didn't yet understand the concept of shame.

"That's right, hot stuff. This dragon wants us to fight the angels. Stupid. We can't beat 'em like we are now. What we can do, though, was make some noise. Cause some trouble. We gotta get the rest of our people outta Heaven. You feel me?"

Agares nodded. "Sounds good, boss. Whatever you say, I'm in."

"Great. Here's the plan..."

Satan knelt and began drawing pictures in the dirt. The demons leaned over to watch — all of them except for Valac. He stood and gazed at them... staring with hollow eyes... never moving...

Like a harbinger of death.
