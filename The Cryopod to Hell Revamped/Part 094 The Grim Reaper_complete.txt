Diablo grabbed Satan's shoulder. "Don't say that! You're leading everyone. If we work together, we can kill this beast."

"My friends are dying!" Satan yelled, turning to face him. "And I can't help them fight!"

"If you can't fight, lead," Agares said, having finished his morphing. "Diablo and I are useless, too. You're fighting to save us. Make us proud," Agares added.

Satan nodded. "Alright. I'll do what I can, chums. Stay out of the way, and don't let this big, furry bastard spot you."

Turning his attention to the bear, Satan quickly waded through the snow, taking in the scene as best as he could. Belial's arms, once fists, had become two deadly blades. Instead of punching the bear, she stabbed and lanced it all over, bouncing out of its reach whenever it drew near, and ducking under its legs to inflict damage on its softer underbelly. Sadly, her attacks seemed little more than pinpricks against its bulk. Spots of blood dotted the beast's hide all over, but none seemed deep enough to cause significant damage.

Bael continued grabbing boulders and flinging them at the bear, but they glanced off its thick, wooly fur, causing light injuries at best.

From Jason's perspective, as a human from the future, it was almost comical seeing the demons of the ancient era get their asses handed to them by this wild animal. However, Satan's beating heart pounded in Jason's chest, as well as his own. A tear dripped down Satan's face as the bear jumped backward and crushed a demon standing behind it. The poor fellow never saw his death coming.

_I'm failing them,_ Satan thought, his heart screaming in anguish. _If only I had more power. If only..._

Suddenly, out of nowhere, Valac rushed past Satan. He reached the bear with alarming speed, ducked its swinging claws, and reared back his bone-scythe.

_Swish._

Valac slashed the bear's stomach.

All the fight in the animal vanished. Its momentum came to a crashing halt, and the light vanished from its eyes. The beast stumbled forward and collapsed on the ground. Everyone, Jason included, stared in shock at Valac. The cloaked demon grinned evilly, exposing his jawbone underneath his hood. He motioned with his hand, causing his scythe to vanish and his lantern to appear. An unearthly moaning sound erupted from the beast, as a ghostly form appeared from its body and was sucked into Valac's lamp.

"Mmm. A delicious soul for the Grim Reaper. It will make a fine addition to my collection," Valac said.

"W-what..." Bael gawked at Valac. "He... he killed... the bear... in one hit."

"Unbelievable," someone whispered.

"Did you see that?"

Several demons whispered to each other as they gazed at Valac.

Valac trudged toward Satan, deliberately taking his time. When he arrived, he whispered, "You can be the 'leader,' Satan, but now everyone will know who _you_ answer to... hehehe..."

Valac slowly wandered behind Satan off into the distance, ignoring Agares and Diablo. As he left, a hollow pit formed in Jason's stomach.

Belial approached Satan, a look of concern on her face. "Satan? What did he say?"

Satan wet his lips. "S-something about repaying a favor," he replied.

"Oh," Belial said.

Satan wiped sweat from his forehead and slowed his beating heart. _Valac saved my life, and the others as well. But... he also embarrassed me,_ Satan thought. He balled his hand into a fist. _Damn you, Valac. You got me,_ Satan thought.

....................................................................

Later, Satan gathered the remaining demons.

"Where's Lucy?" Satan asked.

Diablo stared at Satan. "She stayed behind. I think she was going to-"

"I'm right here," a woman spoke up, turning Satan's attention away from Diablo. Lucifer strode into the group. "Did you have fun with that cave bear?"

"We lost eight people. More than half of our fighting force," Satan replied.

"Wonderful. Now we won't have any dead weight slowing us down," Lucifer replied blandly. "Are you going to tally my points?"

Satan glowered at her. "The points don't matter anymore. We all got a feel for our powers... ahh, fuck it. Where are your kills?"

The demoness grinned, revealing her teeth. "Everyone, follow me."

The demons did as she asked.

A few minutes later, all of the demons arrived at an enormous pile of bird corpses. Satan stared at it for several seconds, trying to guess how many were there. The miniature mountain of feathers and flesh proved too intimidating for words to describe it.

Lucifer gazed at Satan smugly. "Fourteen thousand, six hundred, and ninety-one."

"What? You killed _that_ many birds?" Satan asked.

"Indeed. As such, I'll be taking the role of leader. Those were the rules, weren't they? Unless you did better than me, _Satan_," Lucifer stated.

Satan shook his head while still gaping at the pile of bird corpses. "No. I didn't. You won the competition without a doubt."

Despite the snobbish look on Lucifer's face, Satan was the one who smiled.

"Ahh, about the competition. I forgot to mention there were a few _extra_ rules added."

"What do you mean?" Lucifer asked, gazing at Satan suspiciously. "You didn't mention any extra rules."

Satan's smile widened. The thought of putting this conceited woman in her place made him giddy with joy. _But I did, girl! It's all right here in my Blood Contract,_ Satan thought.

Waving his hands, Satan produced his magical scroll once again. The words on it, written in a strange, demonic language, glowed ominously along with everyone's signatures.

Lucifer stared at the contract, but she didn't seem to understand the words. "Explain. What rules are you talking about?"

"Oh, nothing too important! Well, by signing the contract, you agreed to obey my orders for the rest of our lives, and that you would never make any attempt on my life. Simple stuff like that," Satan said.

All the demons nearby gasped. Belial's lip quivered. "Satan! Y-you tricked us! You-"

"I did what was necessary," Satan said, cutting Belial off. "Once I don't have to worry about any betrayals, we can progress toward saving the rest of our kin."

Agares was next to speak. "Unthinkable. I can't believe you'd do this, Satan. We trusted you. It wasn't enough to prevent a betrayal, but you also made it so we had to follow your every order?!"

Bael punched his palm. "Pah! You think I care?! That stupid paper don't mean nothin'! I'll knock your head off right now!"

Bael, a powerful Demon Baron, charged at Satan, but the leader didn't flinch. The moment Bael reared back to swing, his body froze, as if a thousand ghosts had suddenly appeared to hold him in place.

"Huh? I-I can't move! What the fuck is this?!" Bael shouted.

"It's Satan's ability," Diablo murmured. "You sly hound. You acted like you were powerless ever since meeting the dragon, but that scroll had a purpose. Amazing."

Smiling, Satan bowed dramatically. "Yes, yes, praise me more, chums. You can all be pissed if you like, but nobody can nullify my contracts, not even me. You're stuck like this for the rest of your lives."

Belial took a step back and covered her chest. "S-so, you can make us do anything you want? _Anything_?"

"That's not important!" Lucifer yelled. "Valac didn't sign! That means he got off scot-free while we didn't! Outrageous! I have an urge to splatter you all over this forest, you damned-"

Satan ignored her and looked around, but Valac was nowhere to be seen.

"Let's all move past the trickery. You can't undo it, and in time you'll come to find I had my reasons. More importantly, now that everyone has witnessed the potential of my contracts, you understand the threat it poses. I want to weaponize it against the angels," Satan said.

Lucifer shut up. Despite her anger, Satan's words piqued her curiosity.

"Will it allow us to strike a blow against Samael?" Lucifer asked.

Satan grinned. "Hehe. Well, it's funny you mention him."
