Part 361: Fallen World

Outside one of the four warpgates positioned at the 'corners' of Hell, a massive group of monster and demon refugees lays, sits, or stands in one of the dozens of alcoves, hallways, and other adjacent areas to that part of the Labyrinth. These beings, the survivors of the Kolvaxian invasion, scream and cry while lamenting the fall of their world.

Not since the Energy Wars has the Hell of Isolation suffered such a devastating loss. Its members now amount to less than twenty percent of its original numbers, making them feel scattered, lost, and disorganized.

Emperor Melody, a muscular demoness with long black hair stretching down to her waist, gem-studded teeth, and plenty of additional piercings all over her body, sits despondently on the dirt floor. While other demons around her cry and sob, she instead becomes half-catatonic, dazed by the deaths of her fellow demons.

Not only does she not know what fate ultimately befell Emperors Guura and Viola, but she cannot mentally cope with having been forced to kill a million of her own people to save them from being assimilated into the Plague's biomass.

"Gone... they're all gone..." Melody mumbles incoherently, her thoughts a slurry of despair and bitterness. "I couldn't... save them..."

While Melody battles with feelings of inadequacy and uselessness, a portal opens up at the far side of the lone warpgate's nexus, amidst a hollow spot in the crowd of demons and monsters.

Through that portal, a Demon Emperor steps out.

Yardrat, Emperor of the Void, only glances at the Grunts and Lords closest to him before flickering forward to arrive at Melody's side.

"Melody. We came as soon as we heard. How is the situation?"

Yardrat's portal remains open. After he teleports over to Melody, dozens of other high-level Demon Emperors step through, including Fae, Gorn, and Kristoff. Once they emerge, so too does the Wordsmith, as well as Raphael. The Archangel of Wisdom insists on observing these happenings with his own eyes.

"You're too late." Melody hisses through her teeth. "You're too late! They're all gone. It's over. Those monsters got Viola, and Guura, and Dorma, and... and everybody!"

Yardrat flicks his eyes toward the disabled portal and shivers.

"I'm sorry. If you had warned me sooner, I could have opened dozens of portals to evacuate-"

"WELL I DIDN'T!" Melody bellows, raising her head to shriek at Yardrat. "I didn't, okay?? It all happened so fast! By the time I heard what was going on, half the planet had already been taken over! I was scared! I could barely even figure out what was happening before the Plague pushed us back to the Warpgate! Nobody told me it would go down like this! Nobody warned me!"

Yardrat flinches. He takes a step back and raises his palm.

"Sorry. I didn't mean to act pushy. It wasn't your fault... never mind."

While Yardrat gathers the details, the Wordsmith and Raphael exchange glances, having heard Melody's vague explanation even from a short distance away.

"The Kolvaxians..." Jason mutters. "This is so strange. Why did they attack Sharmur? And right when all the Emperors came to Tarus II?"

Raphael shakes his head. "I know not the answer, Wordsmith. Verily, this timing art coincidental. Coulds't one of the Emperors hath planted a Kolvaxian on Sharmur, somehow?"

"Doesn't seem likely." Jason mutters. "This is the first time any of the demons have even seen one. I'll have to ask the Volgrim. They might have some ideas."

The Hidden Emperors surround Melody and try to comfort her, but none of their words make her feel any better. Among them, Gorn crosses his arms and crooks a faint smile as he sees the devastation.

_Good. Good... this will incentivize the different Hells to work together._ Gorn thinks. _Now the others will understand the Threat we face more viscerally. In these times, a capable leader will need to step up... and who better than me?_

Confident in his chances of working his way up to seize control of all Seven Hells, Gorn says nothing to Melody, opting to allow his peers to offer their words of comfort.

At the same time, the Wordsmith utters a Word of Power, allowing him to look into the last 24 hours of memories of several nearby demons. He quickly obtains a massive amount of emotionally packed and fragmented information, but sifts through it with Fiona's help to look at how, exactly, the Plague spread.

"Hmm..." Jason mutters, keeping his voice low. "It seems the Plague started with only a handful of infected, then spread rapidly across the capitol city of Sharmur. There aren't any eyewitnesses here from those first few hours, though. They must have been... yeah. But how did the Plague spread across the whole planet so quickly?"

"'Tis beyond my understanding." Raphael replies. "If we could safely travel to Sharmur, we might be able to examine the infection's aftermath to postulate how it spread. Otherwise..."

"I don't want to go there myself. Not even in a soul-form." Jason says. "For all I know, the Plague might have some way of detecting souls. Especially as it just devoured multiple Demon Emperors."

After thirty minutes, Jason and the demons manage to console the survivors of the Sharmur attack. Jason courteously invites them all to live on Tarus II, a simple matter given the huge amount of free space on his world, and Melody accepts.

"I don't know!" Melody shouts, bitterness lingering in her voice. "What the hell is up with all of you? Why is the Wordsmith and an Archangel here? I thought you guys were going to war with each other today!"

Yardrat crosses his arms. "Yes. About that... remember Ose?"

He and the other Emperors bring Melody up to speed. She listens, dumbstruck, as they inform her that they successfully tricked Ose and got her killed. Now, they have to hunt down Mephisto, Yama, and the others who attacked Tarus II. The more they talk, the more tongue-tied Melody becomes.

"This is insanity." She mutters. "If only my Hell had gone with you. Then Guura, Viola, and the others wouldn't have..."

"It's better this way." Jason says. "Yes, you did lose several friends. But if all the elites from Sharmur had gone to Tarus II, there wouldn't have been anyone left to save those you did when the Kolvaxians attacked. In the end, you still managed to save a fifth of your people. That's worth something, right?"

"A fifth." Melody grunts. "What a relief."

Jason doesn't offer her any other words. Like how he struggles with the deaths of those who served under him during Stormbringer, Jason finds a certain amount of parallel thinking with Melody, now that the two of them have both suffered a massive loss of life under their watch.

"We can't change the past." Jason says. "But we can move toward a better future. What do you say? Do you want to set up a new life on Tarus II? I won't treat you badly."

"Sure..." Melody sighs. "Thanks. I owe you."

She leaves to talk to the surviving elites from her Hell, while Jason and the other Emperors mingle, wondering what to do next.

"Everything I said before still stands." Jason reiterates. "You guys need to track down Mephisto and the rest. I can handle the relocation of the demons and monsters from Sharmur. As for what comes after that, I'll start drawing up plans on how you can help humanity rebuild-"

Right in the middle of the Wordsmith talking, he receives a telepathic transmission. Not words, but images and mental impressions. The Wordsmith abruptly pauses to listen as his strongest Black Hole Construct, Arthur, transmits information to him.

The Wordsmith blinks slowly.

"...What? Yama did? And where is Hope now?"

The next transmission makes him wet his lips. He turns to the Emperors and shrugs.

"I, ah, have something to deal with. You know what to do. You can meet me on Tarus II, or in the Core. We'll talk again soon."

Without waiting for a response, the Wordsmith blinks away, leaving Raphael behind to stand awkwardly in the middle of countless former enemies.

The Archangel of Wisdom snorts. "Hmph. I shalt take my leave as well."

He opens a portal back to Tarus II, steps through, and vanishes.

After he disappears, Melody frowns.

"Say. Are we cool with Raphael now, or...? You know, since he and the other two Archangels killed most of the demons on Hell Harbor."

"To be honest," Kristoff replies, "it still isn't clear to me how we should treat the Archangels."

"Huh." Melody mutters. "Neat."

_Some time earlier, in a distant part of the Labyrinth._

Yama and his Shades stand in a pitch-black room while awaiting a signal from the other Hidden Emperors to invade Tarus II. Among his Shades are several former Warpers, demonesses capable of opening portals to the soon-to-be-attacked planet ruled by humanity.

"It should not be much longer, now." Yama hisses, rubbing his shadowy claws together in anticipation. "Those other Emperors sure are dragging their feet! They always waste our time, as if we haven't many fun activities to participate in!"

Yama's mood is lower than it has been in tens of thousands of years. Thanks to Bael's summoning of Satan, he no longer has the ability to capture more females for his harem. Instead, he can only 'enjoy' those who already exist, a torturous notion to a greedy sex-fiend like himself.

"That devil-damned Satan..." Yama curses. "How could he return and do this to us? He was long dead! Whatever trick that Bael used, he really did bring him back! Bael is not as simple as he seems. He must have only been pretending to be a huge idiot, all this time! In actuality, he is a genius, waiting for the right moment to seize power over all Seven Hells!"

One of Yama's Shades leans toward him.

"Master. Surely, thou art wrong. Bael is far from intelligent. Everyone knows that! How could he hath changed his intellect in so short a time?"

The Emperor of Shadows turns to look at who spoke. When he sees the speaker is none other than the very first Shade he ever converted, he doesn't rebuke her. She, alone, holds the right to talk back to him.

"Hmph. Oh, Red, you still don't know how demons act, after all these years. Isn't it obvious? That Bael must have become smarter after swapping bodies with Ose! Her brain juices made him a genius! That's why he could use Satan to toy with us in the way he did! He-"

A flicker of light momentarily lights up the darkness of the room. Before Yama or the others can flinch or shrink back, the light vanishes, and in its place, a bipedal figure stands.

A blonde-haired human, lacking any armor on his body, with piercing blue eyes and a week's worth of scruff covering his face and chin, smirks when he appears.

"Surprise. It's me."

Yama jumps in alarm. "What! A human! No, not just any human, we sense the Heroic Aura! He's the Wordsmith!!"

"Wordsmith!" Countless Shades shriek in rage. "KILL!"

At once, a deluge of shadows plunges toward Hope Hiro, engulfing him from all directions to ensure an instant death.

However, the Shades encounter an impenetrable defense when they crash against a force field surrounding Hope's entire body. The Wordsmith chuckles coldly to himself.

"Haha... as if I would teleport here unprepared. Say goodbye, Yama. Your miserable life ends today."

Yama shrieks in rage. "Wordsmith!! Bastard, we'll capture you and make you our slave! Coming here alone will be the last mistake you ever make!"

Yama conjures his powers of darkness, firing off powerful shadow-lances to crash against Hope's mana barrier. The impacts cause his shield to crackle and hiss, but in the end, none of them pierce through.

Hope stands calmly and watches while Yama's forces lob shadow bombs at him, strike him with their fists and claws, and screech profanities, hoping to weaken his resolve and perhaps even frighten him.

The Wordsmith's cold laughter freezes the blackened hearts of Yama's minions. Realizing they can't even harm Hope, they instantly pivot and turn to flee, leaving without any parting words.

However, barely have Yama and his women made it a hundred yards before they crash against another mana barrier, one engulfing the entire area and locking them in place!

"No!" Yama exclaims, shock in the crafty old Emperor's heart. "Dammit! The Wordsmith caught us unprepared! Quickly, attack one spot! If we can make a small hole, we can burrow through and escape!"

From behind Yama, Hope laughs. "Heh heh... are you sure about that? **Teleport.**"

Hope arrives directly behind Yama. He holds his hand up high, then utters another word of power.

"**LIGHT!**"

Instantly, a brilliantly glowing orb materializes in Hope's grasp, blasting sunlight in all directions, irradiating Yama's body and burning him alive.

"Aaaaaiiieeee!" Yama shrieks, his voice turning shrill as his body starts to rapidly evaporate. "Stop! Pleeeaaaase! You're killing us!"

"That _is_ the idea." Hope retorts. "But don't worry. I'll make sure to drag out the pain a while longer. You killed a lot of soldiers in that hospital. They were good friends of mine. Your death will be one of torture and suffering."

Yama's Shades struggle and writhe. Many of them burn to ash under Hope's divine light, while others manage to hold on a while longer. Yama himself screams and pleads for mercy, yet no matter how he begs, Hope doesn't let up.

"Please! Aaaugh! It burns! We can help you! We will be your slave!"

"I'd rather put a bullet in my own head than work with a demon." Hope says. "There's no escaping your fate, shadow bastard."

Suddenly, as if through a moment of clarity, Yama remembers something.

"Wait! W-we have information! You want to learn about- aaargh! About Solomon's Crown, don't you?!"

Hope frowns.

"Solomon's Crown? I already know where it is. The Volgrim took it."

"No, please spare us! We know a secret! A secreeet! If you kill us, you'll never know!"

The Wordsmith hesitates for a moment, then deliberately lowers the power of his light orb, giving Yama a delightful reprieve. The light continues to scorch and burn Yama's body, but compared to before, the difference is like night and day.

Yama gasps for breath. His tortured red eyes flick toward Hope, feeling a glimmer of a chance for survival.

"Speak." Hope says. "Tell me what you know about Solomon's Crown. If your answer doesn't absolutely knock my socks off, I'll kill you in the next instant!"

Yama nods repeatedly. "Yes, yes, of course, master! I-it's... it's Gressil! Gressil took the Crown! We saw it happen!"

Hope jerks in fright, causing his light magic to shatter in his grasp. He takes a step back from Yama and gasps.

"What? Gressil? You're lying!"

With Hope's divine energy no longer irradiating his whole body, Yama and the Shades regain their ability to breathe, even though the aftereffects of the light still leave their shadowy figures scarred and deformed.

"We would not dare!" Yama protests. "We saw it happen! Gressil captured you, then he threw the Crown aside. We would never touch a holy object, but we kept our eyes on it. Later, Gressil led the Changelings to the Crown so they could take it back to Volgarius. We swear this is all true!"

Hope's heart thumps erratically as he not only reflects on what Yama is saying, but mentally recalls the horrific torture Gressil unleashed on him. Without intending to, Hope's suffers multiple flashbacks of those dark days, making him grit his teeth and sweat as he envisions the saws cutting through his skin and bone to amputate his limbs.

"Nnn... it's not true..."

As if receiving a second chance at life, Yama furiously grabs onto the opportunity. "There's more! Spare us, and we'll tell you everything!"

Hope shakes away the thoughts plaguing his heart. His eyes flare with rage as he gazes at the hateful apparition of a shadow in the dark.

"You... you're trying to trick me!"

"We aren't! We pledge this to you!" Yama pleads. "We know the truth about you, Hope. You are nothing but a tool to Jason! He wishes to use you, but he doesn't care about you at all. With our help, you can overturn his rule! Here, another token of our sincerity..."

Yama pauses before continuing. "Satan... First Emperor Satan... he is alive. We saw him with our own eyes!"

"Satan?" Hope repeats. "How can I believe you? I killed- Jason killed Satan on Polaris! Belial saw him turn to ash in her arms!"

Yama takes a few long moments to collect himself. In the back of his mind, he sneers.

_Got him._

"We speak the truth, Wordsmith." Yama says, crawling across the ground on his belly to bow at Hope's feet. "We saw Bael summon Satan from the Great Beyond. Somehow, Bael has gained Satan's full power! Not just him, but Emperor Shax as well. We are not certain how, or why, but Bael has unlocked new powers. He can revive dead demons where even Mephisto never could."

Hope narrows his eyes. He stares at the Emperor groveling at his feet, all while rubbing his chin thoughtfully.

"You saw both of these things, then? You seem to be quite sneaky. Are you good at observing others without them noticing?"

"Oh, yes, yes of course we are, master!" Yama exclaims, cozying up to Hope like a beaten dog. "We do not want to die! We can spy for you! We can be your slave! We know all sorts of dirty secrets! With us helping you, you can take back humanity from the other Wordsmith!"

"I don't need your help for any of that." Hope snorts. "But I'll tell you what. If you can answer one question for me... I'll spare your life."

"One question?" Yama asks, feeling a hint of worry in his cold, lifeless heart. "We... we will do our best to answer, no matter what you ask."

Hope nods.

"Where is Neil? Neil Adams. The commander of humanity's military. Supposedly, he died during the Stormbringer Attack. Personally, I think he survived, and the demons captured him. If you can tell me where he is, I'll spare you."

Yama's eyes widen. A feeling of glee swallows his heart.

"Y-yes! Neil Adams! We know who he is! We know where to find him! Ose locked him away, and she hid him using her brother's magic, but we know everything that happens inside the Labyrinth!"

So excited is Yama at the prospect of surviving that he can't help it when his voice trembles. He quickly tells Hope the location where Neil is hiding, to which Hope nods.

"Good. You'd better pray you aren't lying. Since you've been useful to me, I will uphold my end of the bargain. You can go free... but you'll be on your own if Jason comes after you."

Hope turns to walk away. Right when he's about to teleport, Yama slithers forward.

"W-wait, master! If you leave us like this, the other Wordsmith will find and kill us! We can be so much more useful to you alive than dead! Why don't you protect us with your Wordsmithing magic? Hide us so the other Wordsmith can't find us. Then you can come to us whenever you need information. Our skills are hard to come by!"

Hope frowns.

"You want me to... protect you?"

"Yes! Yes!" Yama exclaims. "Just hide us so the Wordsmith can't Locate us. We know how that part of your magic works. We have seen Jason use it several times."

Hope falls silent. For several minutes, he mentally debates Yama's request, before nodding.

"Alright. **Shroud.** I've put a layer of protection on your soul. As long as Jason doesn't know it exists, he won't be able to find you and undo it. But since I know about it... hehe. You'll never be able to hide from me. Keep collecting information. If I ever need anything... I'll be in touch."

"Yes, master. Yes!" Yama hisses. "We were right to follow you! Thank you for your benevolence!"

"Alright, shut up." Hope snaps. "If you keep fawning, you'll make me vomit. Get out of my sight."

Without another word, Yama meekly nods, then slithers away, his surviving Shades following in hot pursuit.

After the Emperor of Shadows disappears, Hope snorts to himself. "Little rat. He thinks he can use me for protection? I'll use him instead. Then, when I don't need him anymore, I'll discard him like trash alongside the road. Yama's days are numbered."

Hope once again prepares to teleport away, but at that moment, Arthur appears.

Jason's Black Hole Construct flickers into existence before Hope, slightly startling the Wordsmith. The construct glances around, as if searching for Yama.

"Don't bother." Hope says, reading Arthur's intentions. "Yama got away. He's harder to track than I expected. Also, tell Jason I found Neil, and I'll bring him home, soon. As for you, you can go back to Tarus II ahead of me. I don't need your help."

Arthur doesn't speak. The construct merely nods at Hope before flickering away, traveling at close to the speed of light as it returns from the direction it came.

"...Stupid machine." Hope mutters. "**Teleport.**"
