His stomach churned. His mind spun. It took all of his concentration to break away from the scene of horror before him, and to speak a word of power.

"**Take.**" 

A moment later, Hoarhiim's orb appeared in his hand, and he retracted his vision from the grassy field to return to his body.

He was sickened. Disgusted. Nauseated. 

After using '**Locate**' to find Amelia, he ended up witnessing a scene he'd never forget.

Amelia, her sky-blue sundress covered in blood, was chasing after a family of humans with a psychotic grin plastered across her face. Her expression reminded him of scary clowns from horror movies he saw in his youth. _Unnatural._

What planet was she on? What reason could she have for performing such horrible, vile acts? What could motivate her to walk such a path of evil?

Sweat glided from his forehead and dripped past his eyes. He stopped at the edge of the forest and leaned against a tree. _Amelia isn't the sweet little girl she pretended to be,_ he thought to himself. _Everyone has been warning me, but I ignored them. I didn't want to believe the truth._

_Amelia isn't just a liar; she's dangerous. She tore me apart in less than a second and left me to die. I can't allow her to slaughter human settlements for her sick, demented plans!_

 But... how could he stop her? She outclassed him in speed, strength, and every other category. She had magic, too; he knew that much for sure. Could she match his Wordsmithing? Possibly not.

He was vulnerable. Wordsmithing required him to speak and form words. One broken jaw, and he was out of the fight. He had to protect his neck, head, and lungs, at the absolute minimum. If that was the case, then it seemed he needed a helmet.

"What do you think I should do, Hoarhiim?" 

Silence followed as the glowing white orb offered no response. He guessed he wasn't going to say anything, after all — not even a thank-you for the rescue.

_Maybe he hates me._

After a moment of silence, he stuck Hoarhiim's orb in his belt-pouch. His hand brushed against the faulty lightsaber, and he decided that now was a good time to try fixing it. He needed an edge against Amelia. She was too dangerous for him to ignore anymore.

When he pressed the lightsaber's activation button, it only made a sputtering noise, popped out for a split second, then retracted again — dead battery.

"**Recharge.**"

After saying a single word, he watched as the weapon activated, and the beam shot out like it did before. However, once again, after fewer than ten seconds, it sputtered and died. 

_He could recharge the lightsaber's battery, but the amount of power it needed was ridiculous. Unless he planned to say 'recharge' every ten seconds during a battle, the saber was functionally useless._

Maybe there was a better way to power it. He activated the Crown and began perusing his options.

_A power cell sitting a distance away could beam the energy required to his lightsaber wirelessly, but the power draw was too high. He'd have to turn it off every couple of minutes and let it charge to full. Worse, the further he moved from the power source, the less energy that would transmit._

_There was also the option of adding a much bigger, bulkier battery. He might be able to create a fusion power core and attach it to the bottom of the saber, but that would make it bottom-heavy and awkward to swing. The saber also would look like an inverted hammer, which would be super dumb._

Hmm. Maybe there was another option.

Oh! He could create a portable battery and maybe wear it like a backpack! Then he could attach it to his lightsaber with a thin cord, and he could even use a wireless charging mode if the situation called for it! Brilliant!

"**Battery.**" He spoke a word of power and created a fat, highly energized cell with straps to wear on his back. The battery was a little warm, but not uncomfortably so. He then created a cord, altered the lightsaber a bit, and attached the cord to its underside.

With a flick of his thumb, he pressed the ignition button and watched as the lightsaber sprang to life.

This time, several minutes passed, and the sword continued to hum audibly, sounding far stabler than it did before. He swiped at the ground a few times and smiled as it finally served its purpose. Best of all, he could attach and detach the charging cord using telekinesis!

_Wow. That was a surprisingly easy fix. The battery was pretty bulky, but giving himself more physical power with Wordsmithing was child's play._

"**Strength!**"

His physical strength increased by around 40%, turning him into a man capable of lifting over a ton. The stress on his muscles nearly burst his arteries, but he managed to solve his problems with a few rounds of healing and conditioning.

Satisfied, he nodded and continued into the woods, slashing at trees every so often while he waited to see if the battery would die. Indeed, after fifteen minutes, it finally did, but he could refill it instantly with Wordsmithing. Fifteen minutes was nothing compared to ten seconds — a win in his book.

_Depression took him as the trees loomed from both sides. I'm making a better weapon, just in case I have to fight Amelia. Am I going to cut her down with this laser sword? Is that the sort of person I'll have to become?_

_Heroes shouldn't kill. They're supposed to be saviors, arbiters of justice. Superman never killed anyone, unless it was the absolute last option. Not even Batman murdered, unless a particularly edgy author was writing the comic._

A second voice appeared in his mind. _Those superheroes were comic book characters. Stop living in the fantasy world, Jason. To be a hero, you have to make sacrifices. Remember what Michael told you? There's no way you can argue with an Archangel who lived for tens of billions of years._

He shook his head. _It's not about being like Batman or Superman. Yeah, they're only comic book heroes, but they're also inspirational. I can't inspire others to commit genocide! If I kill Amelia, who's to say I won't kill someone else, and another person, and another after them? Where will it end?_

_You're a gas,_ his second inner voice snorted. _If the good guys never kill, then how will evil ever be annihilated? If we didn't kill Hitler, then who knows how many more millions he'd have gone on to exterminate?_

He frowned. _Hitler killed himself._

His second inner voice hesitated. _...Nobody likes a smartass._

Rolling his eyes, he gazed at the sky.

_Joan was supposedly a Hero, but she annihilated tens of millions of demons. By my reckoning, that made her a genocidal maniac._

She was the only Hero he knew anything about, but already she'd soured his image of what it meant to be a champion of justice. How could someone call themselves a 'good guy' if they committed acts of evil? Wasn't the point to be better than enemies?

Maybe he was naive. He came from a world where the 'good guys' dropped two atomic bombs on an enemy to cow them into submission and used internment camps just like the dictators they opposed. Maybe his idea of justice was infantile. He probably needed to grow up and face the facts; violence was a necessary evil.

But...

...That didn't mean he had to like it.

...................................................

Half an hour later, he emerged from the woods, climbed up the canyon stairs, and stopped to watch a Harpy playing horseshoes with a ten-year-old girl. The two were having great fun, and it made him realize that the monsters weren't as different from humans as their looks would suggest. Heck, the Harpy was even letting the kid win. Adorable.

"**Locate.**" He spoke the word to the wind and aimed his thoughts at Phoebe. His mental vision traveled into the air and zoomed around to a small training area behind the castle. He paused mid-flight and watched as Phoebe swung a sword repeatedly into a training dummy, adding several chop marks into an already diced-up frame. The poor thing was on its last legs, and about ready to fall apart.

Once his mental vision returned, he summoned a pair of wings and flew over the castle, waving politely at the citizens below who gazed up and gawked at him.

He swung left over the castle and smiled at the sound of Phoebe's grunting as she continued to chop the wooden dummy. "Haah! Hyah!"

Landing silently about fifty feet away, he quietly magicked his wings away, then Wordsmithed at the dummy. "**Repair.**" It shifted and grew back again, catching her off guard. Phoebe stopped swinging and gawked at the mannequin, then spotted him out of the corner of her eye. "Ah! Your Grace! How long have you-?!"

"I just got here a second ago. Sorry to scare you." He smiled for a moment to disarm her anxiety before continuing. "Getting some exercise, huh? You probably needed it, after being cooped up with me for a month."

"I... I trained when I could." Phoebe lowered her sword and stared at him. "Your health was my primary concern."

"Yeah, I know. I, ah... I wanted to talk to you about that." Casually, he started closing the gap between them, walking about a step per second. "Look, uh, about earlier-"

"Twas nothing worth mentioning," Phoebe said, clamming up suddenly. "I... I barely even thought about it! You had just woken up, and your nerves were a bit frazzled. No man could be blamed for rushing to judgment."

"Hah... rushing? No, no. I spent that entire month thinking, Phoebe." He stopped in front of her and stared down at the nearly six-foot-tall blob of heat before him. She was taller than most women, but not quite at his level. "Phoebe, I can't take 'no' for an answer. Maybe I'm just a young, idiot guy, but I care for you. I don't want anything to happen to you. I want to protect you."

Her cheeks flared-up. "That is quite kind of you, Your Grace."

"No, you're the kind one, since you selflessly took care of me without anyone asking you. You could have left at any time, but you didn't. Why not?"

Phoebe stammered and reached up to brush a stray hair from her face. "Well.... you see... you're the Hero. If you die, then humanity has no hope..."

"Yeah? So you only care about me since I'm the Hero, and not for any other reason?"

She wrapped both of her hands tightly around her sword's hilt. "I didn't say that."

"Then, why?"

"Just because."

"That's not a good enough answer."

He sighed and stooped down to his knees so he could look up into her eyes, though it was mostly pointless given he couldn't see anything. Oh well.

"Phoebe, I'm not good at awkward things, so I'll spit it out. I love you. I don't want anything to happen to you, and most importantly, I want you by my side. You're not only a friend or a caregiver, but someone more precious to me. I hope you feel the same-"

He paused mid-sentence as Phoebe suddenly reached a hand up to wipe her eyes.

"Are... are you crying?"

She sniffled. "N-no..."

"I think you are."

"I'm not!" She stomped her foot and looked away. "Augh! Jason, I'm a hand-maiden! You're royalty! It can't work between us!"

"Yes, it can, dammit! I was born believing that as long as two people cared for each other, it didn't matter what their status was!"

She growled. "I was taught the opposite! Only marry within your class!"

"Marry?" He balked suddenly. "Whoa, I- I didn't want to move that quickly."

She took a sudden step backward. "You what?! But... a woman must be chaste until she enters wedlock!"

Only now did he realize the situation he was in. Phoebe came from a different time period than he did, and her expectations of a relationship followed from her life growing up. She wasn't just old-fashioned, she was _ancient-fashioned_.

He, on the other hand, grew up in a time of casual romance and constant divorce. Marriage was little more than a minor formality for two people who had been together for several years already. For Phoebe, marriage was the very first step.

So, if that was the case, he had two options: he could change her stance, or he could accept it. He didn't know what the correct choice was.

Phoebe knelt as well, meeting him at the same eye level. "Your Gr-, ah, Jason... if you wish it, I can be your Mistress. I... I never expected a king to take me in such a way, and it does betray my convictions, but if it's for your happiness, then-"

"No, I can't do that. That would only hurt you." He cut her off before she could finish. "You know, Phoebe, you're right. I'm taking things too fast. I'm immature, still. I have much to learn."

"We all do," She replied, nodding. "In truth, I feel the same toward you that you do me, but with God as my witness, a woman cannot shirk her sworn duties. I must protect and care for the Hero, no matter what form he or she may take. That has become a personal task above, and beyond the creed I swore my life on as a child."

Phoebe slowly tilted her head up to look at the clouds. "It was so long ago that I've forgotten the words. Does the oath bind me to my dying breath?"

"Perhaps, Phoebe. Perhaps."

Without meaning to, he stretched his hand out and took hers. It was an unconscious movement, but unlike before, she didn't pull away. Instead, she lowered her head to stare into his eyes. "Jason?"

"I don't care what form your oath takes, Phoebe. I want you to be with me. That's all."

She smiled sweetly. "And so, I shall." A moment passed as they stared into each other's eyes. Phoebe swallowed. "C-close your eyes."

"Okay."

He closed them, but his eyelids didn't limit his spherical vision. He watched in silence as Phoebe leaned forward and pressed her lips against his.

A moment later, he pulled her against him and finally released his burning passion upon her lips. He knew what he wanted, and he wouldn't take no for an answer.

_Tradition be damned._
