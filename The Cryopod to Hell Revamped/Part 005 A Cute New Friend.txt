It doesn't take long before I end up sitting cross-legged on the ground, mindlessly tracing circles on the smooth dirt floor with my finger. My mind churns through hundreds of thoughts, one after the other, while I try to figure out a way out of this mess. Hoarhiim and Blaarjiim's orbs sit a few feet in front of me, putting out and sucking in the light around themselves, respectively.

_The Golden Doors only open inward. I shoved them shut earlier, and they don't have handles, so I don't know how the hell I'm going to pull them open to escape. Once I make it outside, I'm reasonably sure that Hoarhiim's power-up will improve my body to the point that I can either fight the monsters or evade them and flee. Probably..._

I narrow my eyes and focus, and the mental map from Solomon's Crown appears once more. The Labyrinth stretches for miles in every direction, almost as if Hell is inside the hollowed-out remains of a planet. But if that's the case, why is there gravity pulling me to the ground? 

While my mental map makes it seem like I'm at the center of a cube, in reality, it probably isn't displaying the Labyrinth's layout past a certain point. It could go on for hundreds, thousands, perhaps even hundreds of thousands of miles past the edge of my mental map.

Hoarhiim stirs. "What is on your mind?"

"A lot," I say, replying noncommittally. My mental map has started showing more than just a single red dot indicating my location; now it displays several other points of interest, including a few that I can only assume are the legendary weapons and armor I noticed when I first picked up Hoarhiim. However, they are considerably further from my current location than I'd like.

"Hey, Hoarhiim. Do I have to hold you to access your power?"

He shakes his head. "We are bound together, so distance is not a barrier. However, if you attempt to use my power without physically touching my orb, the connection will be substantially weakened."

"I see."

Another few minutes pass. The monsters outside seem to have fallen silent, making no noise at all. I don't know if they're waiting for me anymore. Maybe they gave up and left.

Standing up, I start pacing back and forth, chewing on my thumb as I examine the door and use the Crown to consider my options.

_When I first picked up Hoarhiim, I killed that shadow-monster, but there was a trigger involved. I spoke a single word out loud. Why? What prompted me to do that?_

I stop and pivot on my heel to face the entrance head-on. Raising my arm, I aim my palm at the door.

"**Light.**"

Immediately, a light like the Sun radiates from my hand, reflects off the shiny golden door, and blasts me in the eyes, blinding me. "Geyaugh!"

Stumbling back, I fall on my ass and rub my eyes fiercely. "Fuck! Shit! Goddamnit! Worthless piece of garbage, Jason!"

After a minute, I blink my eyes open, but I can barely see anything. Hoarhiim appears in my mind and shakes his head. "Tsk. You've seared your retinas, Jason."

"No shit!" 

I close my eyes again and think of the word I spoke. _Light._ I said it, and a light appeared. I've already made myself a pizza and a bed. What else can I summon with my words?

Stumbling forward, I lean against the wall and pull myself to a standing position, keeping my eyes closed the whole time. I aim my hand at the other wall. "**Rock.**"

An indistinct _whoomph_ sounds off in front of me, and something clatters to the ground. Hoarhiim inhales sharply. "Brother, did you see that?"

Blaarjiim nods slowly. "I saw it, but I don't believe it."

"Quiet," I mutter at them. "I'm thinking."

My mind races. _I said the word 'light,' and created light. Then I said the word 'rock,' and a rock appeared. Does that mean that any word I say will make something happen related to its meaning?_

Deep in my chest, my heart slams like a jackhammer.

"**Sword.**"

This time, after the _whoomph_, a metallic object clatters to the ground. I jump at the sound.

"Merciful Creator," Hoarhiim whispers.

_Perhaps an abstract concept will work?_

I take a few moments to focus on my burning eyes. In the depths of my mind, I imagine each one restoring to perfection, and my pain fading away.

"**Heal.**" 

This time, a crackling sensation appears in my eyes, but it fades after just a second. When I blink my eyes open, I can see once again. In fact, my vision is damn-near 20/20.

"Holy shit!" The curse bursts out of me as I regain my vision and catch sight of the sword laying on the ground. The weapon's design is nothing special, but it looks like it can deal some damage. I snatch it up and heft it around. The sword isn't heavy, perhaps a few pounds at most, but just touching the blade nearly splits my finger open.

Blaarjiim eyeballs the sword. "You spoke a word and forged that weapon out of nothing. I've been alive a long time, mortal, and never have I seen a spell that could do such a thing."

My hands start to shake. "H-Hoarhiim, am I doing this because of you? Did you give me this power?"

The light-being shakes his head. "Nay. It is a power that you already possessed. I have only given you enough spiritual energy to realize your potential."

"No kidding," I whisper.

I swing the sword at the wall, and when it hits, it cuts a few inches inside the stone and gets stuck. Damn! It's sharp as hell!

After yanking it around for a moment, I wrench it back out. "**Scabbard.**"

This time, instead of appearing in midair, I feel a small displacement of weight on my body, glance down, and marvel at how the sheath has emerged at my side, looping around me with a belt to hold sturdy. A smile forms on my face as I slip the sword into its holder and grab the orbs up.

"**Container.**"

This time, two round holsters appear on my left side. They're slightly bigger than the two orbs, with a little flip-up latch to hold them in place. I put Blaarjiim in the rear and Hoarhiim in the front. Plenty of light shines through to illuminate the path before me, and Hoarhiim nods approvingly.

"Well done, Jason. You're learning how your power works at an incredible speed."

"Thanks, Hoarhiim," I say, only to pause and think about it a moment. "I can manifest objects and perform other miracles by simply speaking a word. What would you call a power like this?"

Blaarjiim and Hoarhiim stare at each other for a moment. The dark one answers first. "Err, 'word magic?'"

Hoarhiim smacks his forehead. "Terrible! The title of such powerful magic demands more majesty! This ability is unlike anything we've ever seen before. It can forge weapons from little more than Jason's spiritual energy! It's a power beyond anything the ancient Heroes could have ever dreamed up."

"Thanks, Hoarhiim." 

I blush a little at my savior's sudden enthusiasm and drift back to thinking about what I should name my power.

_It can forge weapons out of midair, huh?_ That sounds pretty badass. Forging. Blacksmithing. It's as if I am a weaponsmith, crafting a world that bows before me.

Heh, I like that. Now I know what to call my power.

"Blaarjiim, Hoarhiim, I've made up my mind. I'm going to call it 'Wordsmithing.'"

The shit-eating grin on my face can't be contained. Already my mind bubbles with the possibilities my new power gives me.

Turning around, I face the door. 

"Let's get out of here. **Open.**"

Seconds pass. 

The grin on my face fades as the door doesn't budge. 

I try again. "**Open.**"

Still nothing. Frustrated, this time, I try yelling it. "**OPEN!**"

Finally, a reaction. The door shudders slightly and nudges inward for all of two inches before coming to a stop. Outside, the screams of monsters and demons begin anew as they realize I'm trying to exit the room. Several skeletons start pounding on the door, while other monsters bang on drums and holler like banshees. Now that the door is cracked ever so slightly, I can hear voices screaming out things like, "Man filth! Come out, coward!" Swords stab through the opening as hundreds of monsters scream and shout.

I wipe my forehead. My body feels fatigued, and all I did was speak a single word. Hoarhiim takes notice.

"Be careful, Jason. It appears your ability to Wordsmith isn't unlimited. Using magic drains the spiritual energy from your body, including that which I am giving you. If you keep spellcasting recklessly, you'll drain your reserves and leave yourself open to attack."

He's right, as always. I nod along with his words, then stand a little straighter and take several deep breaths. Unless I'm mistaken, the volume of my voice increased the energy used, but also the effect. Then, that means...

No, what am I thinking? If I use all my energy just opening the door, I'll be a sitting duck. The monsters will pour inside while I'm gasping for breath on the ground and gut me like a pig. 

Yeah, that's the key. If I open it inward, I'm inviting them to carve off the juiciest part of my body. But if I forcefully open it outward, I can blow away the ones at the door and dash for the exit.

But how?

The Crown sends countless thoughts toward me, and a moment later, an idea springs into my mind.

Raising my arm, I aim it at the door. "**Push!**"

A wave of energy surges from my body, like a blast of wind, and smashes against the door. It impacts it with a bang, then knocks me back slightly. I quickly regain my footing and examine the door. It's rattling a little, but other than closing it the rest of the way, I don't think my magic had much effect.

Blaarjiim smirks. "You'll need more power than that, mortal."

"Right, right."

The knockback is a problem. If I turn the juice up, I won't be able to keep my balance. The door is exceptionally resistant to any form of attack. Gold is a soft metal, so given the door's defensive properties, it must only be painted gold, but forged from something far more resilient.

Aiming my hand one more time, this time, I prepare a shield for myself. "**Solidify. Root.**"

Dense rock sprouts from the ground, climbs up my body, and wraps around my flesh to hold me in place. With my frame now firmly rooted, I take the deepest breath of my life.

"**PUUUSH!!!**"

A stupendous wave of energy explodes from my body, comparable to the kinetic energy of ten hurricanes, fifty tsunamis, and a giant tornado. The wave of power instantly blasts the giant golden doors off their frames, smashes through the enemies in front, and sends them careening through the wall of monsters awaiting my exit. Before I can see what's happening outside, the magic force ricochets back against my body. It rips my hardened form from the ground, flings me back against Solomon's Throne, destroys my makeshift armor, and knocks the wind out of me.

A moment later, I blink my eyes.

I can't feel anything. I can't move my head, or anything else. 

No, that's not right. I feel warm. Hot, even. It's as if someone has poured gasoline on me and lit a fire. The burning agony is like nothing I've ever felt in my life.

It hurts.

It hurts!!!

"Ney-ungh-"

A pitiful whimper chokes from my throat. The world rushes back to me, and I slowly manage to discern that the side of my head is resting on the ground.

Hoarhiim says something, but I can't hear him. I can't see him, either.

His figure appears in front of me, but it's foggy and indistinct. He's... waving his hand?

"-son. Jason. Jason!" He yells and makes me flinch, the slight movement sending spasms of pain across my entire body. "Jason! Gods, your body- it's..."

"How brutal," Blaarjiim says, as he appears beside his brother. "The mortal is still alive, isn't he?"

"Barely," Hoarhiim whispers. "I think Jason shattered every bone in his body. He's not going to make it, I'm afraid."

"Well, heal him, idiot!" Blaarjiim screams at his brother in anger. 

_When did he become so concerned about my well-being?_

"Don't tell me you've forgotten how to do that?!"

"I haven't forgotten," Hoarhiim says quietly. "But he used up much of my energy during that attack. The best I could do is stem the bleeding. It doesn't matter anyway, brother. Look."

Hoarhiim points somewhere past where I can look. I try to follow his finger, but I can't move my head at all. I can only estimate he's pointing at the doorway.

Blaarjiim's voice softens. "Ah, what a shame. It looks like you didn't get them all, mortal. We had fun while it lasted."

From the doorway, the sound of breathing emerges. Something takes a step forward, its foot causing a _whoomph_ on the ground. "Man-filth."

I manage to wheeze out a question. "Whuh ish et?"

Hoarhiim turns his back on me. "I'm sorry, Jason. It's an orc. There's nothing we can do to help you. Close your eyes. Maybe he'll kill you quickly."

"Kuh... kull muh?"

My mouth won't form words. I can't move at all. All I can do is lie still and listen in terror as the monster takes slow, menacing footsteps toward me.

"You killed friends. Me lose many blood brothers! You pay! Bahahah!" The monster laughs, and I can only thank whatever god might exist that I can't see the monster's face.

_I'm going to die now._

More footsteps. The orc pauses. "Mormok, you live too?"

A second orc voice speaks. "Yup. Human not kill everyone. Skugs alive. Diablo be very angry about losses though."

"Diablo need stuff it," The first orc says. "Let's gut filthy human 'n give him to boss."

The two of them chuckle and take a few steps forward. Soon I can hear their breathing. They're less than ten feet away now.

"Hehe. Man-filth piss himself. Scared? Should be."

Suddenly, from outside, a bone-chilling scream from another orc rings out. "Aaah! No, stay back! Get away!"

Both orcs pause, probably to look back. The first one sniffs the air. "What that?"

The orc outside screams again, but his voice cuts off midway as a sickening _crunch_ fills the din. The sound of snapping bones turns my blood to ice.

The second orc speaks, but his voice is trembling. "H-hey, Ugna. I-isn't this room that scary place? Gressil says no come here."

The first orc's breathing quickens. "Let's take look. Someone's 'bout to die, and it ain't me."

"B-but Ugna..."

"No buts! I ain't 'fraid of nothin'!"

Both orcs turn around and head for the entrance, leaving me to bleed out on the floor. I don't know whether to be relieved or terrified at this turn of events.

Hoarhiim nudges Blaarjiim. "Brother, is that-"

"Quiet! I'm trying to listen!" The dark orb walks over and leans against a barrier, as if trying to see past the entrance. His vision must be limited in some way. "This... this could be..."

The orcs stop at the entryway. Ugna, the first orc, sniffs the air again. "Where everybody go? Where skugs? Where kobolds?"

"U-Ugna, we should leave. Look. Look!" Mormok, the second orc, yells at his comrade frantically. "The statue! It's broken! It's-"

His voice halts much too fast for him to have simply stopped mid-sentence. The sound of something wet and damp hits the floor. Ugna howls in terror.

"No... no! NO!! It's the Black Witch! Get away from me! Filthy human devil!" He swings a sword at someone out of my sight, banging it against the wall in the process, but a moment later, his voice goes silent.

Blaarjiim and Hoarhiim gape at something unseen. 

The awful sound of bones snapping and sinew twisting fills the air again. This time, however, it's accompanied by another sound: _Someone smacking their lips._

"Mmm, yummy! Teehee!"

A little girl's voice rings out in the now-silent hallway.

The sound of clicking, like high heels on stone, slowly approaches me.

"Hello? Are you alive, mister?"

The little girl skips toward me, kicking pebbles and loose debris out of her way. Her pitter-patter of footsteps reaches me within a few seconds. "Mister?"

"Wung-guh..." I wheeze something out, but it isn't much more than a painful gasp.

A moment later, a pair of black platform shoes, and some pale white legs appear in front of me. Two inches above the ankles, a long fluffy black skirt starts, but I can't see much beyond that. The girl kneels down, and finally, her skirted knees come into view. I raise my eyes to look at her, but she's still too high up for me to see her face. I can just barely make out a black dress with red ribbons all over the place and fluffy white frills lining her arms and stockings.

She reaches forward and pokes my chest. The slight tremor sends lightning through my body. "Hmm. Not dead, huh? You won't put up much a fight. Oh! What's this?" She shuffles slightly and reaches toward my belt. A second later she squeals excitedly. "Ooooh! Blaarjiim? Hoarhiim?! You're both here!"

Blaarjiim forces himself to look calm, but I can vaguely make out a look of joy on his face. "My queen. It is wonderful to see you once again. Long have I awaited your return."

"Oh, you! You're always so stuffy! Why are you in this guy's belt, though?" A moment passes. Her voice pitch lowers. "Did he... _steal_ you?" The shift is frightening.

Blaarjiim pauses for a moment, before turning deadly serious. "Aye, Lady Amelia. This mortal stole me from your grasp, and sought to take my power for himself. I can only imagine what nefarious deeds he was plotting."

"Muh-hung!" I choke out a protest, but it doesn't amount to anything decipherable.

Amelia's knuckles crack. "How... how dare you. Taking away my eternal partner while I was trapped in that awful statue... I'll make you regret this."

She starts to stand up, but Blaarjiim interjects before she can turn violent. "Just a joke, my queen. A jest. He is the one who freed you from your prison."

"Yes!" Hoarhiim interjects. "This human is named Jason. He has a unique magical power called Wordsmithing."

Amelia taps her shoes on the ground. "Oh! I see, now. Haha! Blaarjiim, since when, were you one to tell jokes?"

Blaarjiim bows slightly. "Your radiant smile is worth everything, my queen. However, I must recommend that you heal him at once. His life force is slipping away."

The girl hems and haws for a moment. "Do I have to, Blaarjiim? He looks pitiful and weak."

Hoarhiim lowers his voice. "As a long-time friend, I would ask the same of you, Amelia. I have attached to Jason. I like him."

A moment passes. Finally, Amelia sighs. "Okay, fine. Blaarjiim, give me your power again. I'll heal him up in a jiffy."

What comes next shocks me. An awful pain erupts in my chest, as if a trillion watts of electricity were coursing through every nerve in my body. I open my mouth to scream, but nothing comes out.

A minute passes. My bones wrench back together violently, and my skin folds back on itself. I writhe on the ground, whimpering like a beaten dog, even after my body finishes stitching back together.

"Oooh, sorry about that," Amelia says. "The phantom pain won't last long, though!"

Eventually, as she promised, my anguish subsides. I flop onto my back and breathe heavily, staring up at the ceiling. Finally, I turn my head to examine my savior. She's just a child, perhaps twelve years old. Her black dress is an elaborate, gothic-lolita style thing, with red lacing and frills on the edges. Atop her head is a cute little fluffy red ribbon. She looks adorable.

Then she smiles, and somehow it's the creepiest thing I've seen in my life.

"Aren't you going to thank me?"

Several moments pass as I stare at the strangely dressed young girl. The gaze she sends in my direction indicates a certain casual air I've never seen in a child before. I probably shouldn't call her a child, since there's only six years difference between us, but she's very short, standing well under five feet tall.

Amelia taps her foot. "Hellooo? Is anyone home? I healed you. Where are your manners?"

"What? Oh, right! Thanks." I quickly jump to my feet, only for my legs to give out midway into the hop, making me stumble forward and bash my knees on the ground.

I can barely stand. My limbs have the elasticity of wet spaghetti, rather than the strength of bones and muscles. Every breath threatens to rob my last bit of energy from me.

Hoarhiim grunts. "Careful, Jason. Even if Lady Amelia healed you, your mind still has some leftover trauma from the grievous wounds you suffered. Further, you have almost no spiritual energy left at all. It's a miracle you haven't fallen into a coma."

"A coma?" I repeat his words disbelievingly. "I feel weak as hell, but that seems a little extreme."

To my surprise, Amelia steps forward and grabs my arm with both her hands, helping me to my feet. As she does, a slight warmth enters my body, making me realize how cold I feel.

Amelia steps back, and I notice that Hoarhiim and Blaarjiim's orbs are floating in midair, though she grabs one in each hand right as I spot them. "Jason's your name, hm? Well, I'll give you a freebie. Call it a reward for releasing me."

"Generous as always, my queen," Blaarjiim murmurs. "Be thankful, Jason. The Black Witch is very picky with whom she rewards."

I rise to my full height of six-foot, two inches, only to realize I tower over the little girl. She takes a step back in surprise. "Oh, my. You're taller than I expected. Somewhat skinny, though. Not much in the way of muscles."

Self-consciously, I rub my bicep. "Um, yeah. I, err, never saw the need to work out. Haha."

The two of us evaluate each other for a moment before I decide to ask a question. "You said I freed you. How?"

Wordlessly, she points to the exit of Solomon's Tomb. "Out there. You broke the statue."

As I follow her finger, I spot splatters of blood and gore all around where the Golden Doors once stood. A green leg lies against the wall, looking as if a horrifying monster attacked and devoured whomever it once belonged to.

Slowly, she and I walk over to the exit. She hands me Hoarhiim's orb, then points at the center area. To my astonishment, one of the doors has embedded itself in the wall across the room, while the other has crashed into the ground and buried itself several feet deep.

_I knew I shoved them with my "push" spell, but I didn't expect to send them flying off their hinges like that._

My eyes look away from the doors and toward the carnage in the room. Skeletons are crushed into dust, orcs and centaurs contort into various unnatural shapes, and several monsters are little more than a collection of body parts strewn all over the ground.

And the statue! Where once a beautiful figure of a girl in a dress once stood, with water billowing from her umbrella- _hey, wait a minute!_

I quickly look at Amelia, comparing her to my memory of the statue. "Hold on, are you telling me you were the statue itself?"

Amelia smiles. "Mhm! And you released me! That was super nice of you!"

_But, I mean, I didn't do it on purpose_, I start to say. Blaarjiim appears in my mind and glares at me, waggling his finger.

Right, duh. _Go with the flow, Jason. You did it on purpose._

I quickly wipe at my nose. "Yeah, nah, it was no big deal. Blaarjiim suggested smashing the statue, so I thought it would be cool. Everything got a little out of hand with the monsters, but you saved me, so I'd call us even."

Amelia's smile disappears. "Uh huh... sure."

Even as Blaarjiim grins at me for making him look good, I sweep my gaze around the room once more. _Yeah, Amelia saved me, or at least I think she did. But... what about those awful chewing noises?_

Maybe I heard wrong. After all, I was in a state of delirium. Still, it can't hurt to ask, can it?

"So, uh, Amelia."

"Hmm?" She walks forward and nudges an Orc corpse with her foot.

"You healed me, so you have magical energy too, right? Magical powers?"

She spins around in a circle on her heel like a ballerina. "Mhm! I've got all sorts of powers! Why do you ask?"

"Well..." I turn my gaze to one of the horribly broken Orc bodies nearby. "These monsters... they're from fairy tales and fantasy stories. I never thought in my life that they truly existed. Yet, you can fight them... but how?"

She stops spinning to frown at me. "What a silly question. Hoarhiim, haven't you taught anything to this child? Surely you've at least told him about magic!"

The white orb sighs. "I tried, but we didn't have time to explain."

Tapping my lip, I recall what he told me before. "Hoarhiim, you mentioned spiritual energy once already, then Amelia did, too. What is that? Is it like mana?"

Amelia cocks her head slightly. "Mah-nuh?"

Aw, nuts. She has no idea what I'm talking about. "It's this mechanic from video games. It's like a meter that allows you to cast spells and stuff. When it runs out, you can't use magic anymore, and end up a sitting duck."

Hoarhiim squints. "I am not sure what you're talking about, but the concept is mostly correct. Spiritual Energy is the power that allows demons and angels to cast spells. Humans typically have very little, but even if they had more, they could not use magic."

"Oh. But Amelia has magic, and so do I. Are we special or something?"

Amelia's mouth goes taut. "Special. In a way, yes. Certainly, you are. As for me, I'd rather not say."

Once again, she gives off an odd feeling I can't quite describe in words. "You're a peculiar little girl."

Her expression shifts to annoyance. "Hey! You're the child here! I'll have you know my body is immortal. I'm tens of thousands of years old."

"Really?" I eye her carefully. "I guess anything is possible with magic."

She quickly looks away and blushes. "Y-yeah. Just drop it, okay? I don't like this topic. Tell me about your magic."

_She's self-conscious about her looks,_ I think to myself, making a mental note. _That's how most women are, I guess._

"I call my power Wordsmithing. I can make things happen by speaking a single word."

Amelia continues looking away for a moment, then suddenly jerks back to meet my gaze. "Wait, you _used a word_? Not your hands?"

I echo her confusion. "What do you mean, 'my hands'?"

Hoarhiim clears his throat. "Ahem, well, you see, Jason, every form of magic requires a specific amount of time, hand sign combinations, and spiritual energy from the user. Your power is very irregular in that respect. I've never seen someone, especially a human, who could use magic just by speaking a word."

"Yes," Blaarjiim says. "And creating items out of thin air! That should certainly be impossible."

Amelia raises an eyebrow. "Show me."

All eyes turn to me. "W-well, see, I don't have any energy left. Even if I did, I don't know what to make."

Amelia leans toward me, reaches her free hand forward, and pokes me in the chest. A sudden rush of mana pours into my body. I stand awed by how revitalized I feel. "Have you tested your abilities yet?"

"No. I only just realized what my power was right before I sent the Golden Doors flying. I haven't had time to experiment."

Amelia nods and starts pacing back and forth. "I see. Blaarjiim, surely you've evaluated the boy. What can he do?"

I hate it when this little girl calls me _boy_. She probably does it to make me self-conscious. 

Blaarjiim smoothes down his shirt, or whatever. I only see him as a vague black apparition, but apparently, he wears clothes. "Hmm, yes. He can heal himself, he created a sword and a rock, and he sent the doors flying. I don't know what his limits are, but based on what I've seen, I'd be surprised if he had any at all."

Amelia pauses her walking to kick an orc's helmet out of the way. "No limits? Absurd. Every magi has limits, especially if they're human. I need to test him."

She turns to face me.

"Make me a chair," Amelia points to an empty spot a few feet away. "Right there."

"A chair? That seems random."

She shakes her head. "I want to sit. Hurry up! Unless you think you can't."

"Fine, geez." I reach my free hand out and aim it at a bare patch of floor near a crushed skeleton and a beheaded horse. The sight of the corpses all around hardly fazes me anymore. "**Chair.**"

My heart skips a beat as once again, air rushes into the spot I aimed my hand at, and a chair forms in midair. It drops a few inches, landing perfectly on its four legs.

Amelia scowls. "You call that a chair? Look at it! I'll get splinters on my legs if I sit there! Did you envision a pauper's chair? It doesn't even have a cushion! Make another one, and make it fit for a queen!"

"A queen, huh?"

I stare at the chair thoughtfully. I hadn't thought of anything specific when I first used my spell, but this time...

The Crown of Solomon seems to speak to me, bringing up a mental list of ten thousand chairs. I close my eyes and focus, bringing up an image in my mind.

With a flourish, I jerk my hand out again. "Behold! I will summon a chair far greater than any you've seen before! Prepare yourself, Lady Amelia! Hah hah hah! **Chair!**"

A _thunk_ of something hitting the ground makes me jerk my eyes open. Amelia stares at me, disgust on her face. "What was with that grandiose speech? Never do that again."

"Uh, sorry." My eyes flick from her to the chair and- HOLY MOLY!

A throne, something that would make King Solomon red with envy, sits on the ground amidst the corpses. The chair's vibrant golden color mixes with its bright-red fabric and elaborate stitchings to create an awe-inspiring image. Skulls made of silver and bronze stand atop the side pegs near the head, giving off a feeling as if it once belonged to a powerful necromancer.

Amelia notices I'm not looking at her anymore, and when she turns and spots the chair, she jumps in surprise. "Whoa! You made THAT?!"

I pull away and sweep my hair back with my hand. "Heh, yeah. Now you're beginning to see the true extent of my power. Something like this is trivial for a god, like me. I can create anything with the power of a single word."

Amelia's excitement flattens as she turns to stare at me. "Jason. You're doing the thing again. Don't."

My shoulders slump a little. "Okay. Sorry."

She perks back up and skips gaily to the chair, spinning and sitting down in one smooth motion. The instant she leans back, she moans out loud, "Oh, goodness, this throne is soooo comfortable! I feel like it was made for me!"

I resist the urge to boast once more. Instead, while Amelia rubs all over the chair, smiling ear to ear and praising my handiwork, other thoughts come to my mind.

_I created that chair out of nothing. This power is unusual enough that Hoarhiim and Blaarjiim both saw fit to comment on it. What other properties could it possibly hold?_

Silently, I ponder a hundred different options, each one more interesting than the former. Raising my hand, I point a finger toward the opposite wall and mutter a word. "**Fire.**"

A tiny _poof_ of flame spits from my finger and fizzles away.

This time, I try again, but louder. "**Fire.**"

A stream of flame shoots out, travels around ten feet, and fizzles away.

By now, Amelia has calmed down, and she watches me with interest. "Whatcha doin'?"

"Trying new things. **Fire!**"

I feel my energy decrease substantially, but a plume of flame spews from my finger, forming a laser of fire that jets across the room and carves a line in the wall as I move my finger. A few seconds later, it cuts off, forcing me to take a breath. _That was exhausting._

"Jason," Hoarhiim speaks up, surprising me. "Take Lady Amelia to the entrance of Solomon's Tomb, then use every ounce of energy in your body with that Fire spell. I want to see what happens. Don't hold back."

Frowning, I glance at Hoarhiim's orb. "That's dangerous. I don't know if I can control my power. Besides, I might accidentally hurt Amelia."

Blaarjiim guffaws. "HAH! This mortal has a sense of humor! Flames can't harm the Black Witch, boy. Do as Hoarhiim says. The three of us all want to see what happens."

Indeed, Amelia leans forward and rests her head and hand on her knee as she stares at me with interest. "Go on. Let's see it."

I nod slowly. "Okay. Let's clear the room, then."

...

A minute later, Amelia stands beside me as I aim my hand toward the room of corpses and blood.

"This should be a heckuva way to clean the place. Hell doesn't have many janitors."

"Mmm," Amelia mutters.

To be honest, I'm scared. The last time I went all out with an attack, I nearly killed myself. Still, Amelia's here. The orbs insist I won't hurt her with my fire, and I know she can heal me if things go south, but still... I'm a little worried.

I stick Hoarhiim into my belt pouch and raise both arms, then take a deep breath. Using every pore of my body, I scream with all my might, "**FIIIIRE!!!!**"

What follows next is terrifying. A flood of flame surges from my body, screams as if it were a living being, and tears through everything in its path. It leaps from object to object, swallows the golden throne and melts it to slag in an instant, crawls across walls and the ceiling, and races down the hallway across the room in search of more prey. Everything in the path of the flames screams for mercy as it burns and devours all it sees.

It's... it's incredible! It's terrifying! If I keep this up, I'll turn the whole Labyrinth into ash! I have to stop it!! But how?! How can I-

Suddenly, a sharp pain erupts at the base of my neck. I stumble forward, and the flames cut off. My body goes limp, and as I fall, Amelia catches me before I can plunge into the lake of fire.

Everything starts to go black as I pass out. I slowly realize that Amelia must have knocked me out with a judo chop to my neck. The last thing I see before I go under is a worried look on her face.

"Who _are_ you?"

Then the sweet release of sleep takes me.
