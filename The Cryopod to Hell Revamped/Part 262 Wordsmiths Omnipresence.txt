_First Day: Sixteenth Hour._

Sunlight shines upon Tarus II's surface. At 4 PM, the midday sun beats down overhead, but its rays barely warm the planet's surface, given the early springtime chill.

Ying-Ying, one of Hell's countless Warpers, breathes heavily as she motions with her hands. She hides behind a tree at the edge of the Horned Forest, to the west of Hero City and the east of the Warpgate Encampment. With only half a mile or so separating her from humanity's backline troops, she grits her teeth and perspires.

"Okay... here goes."

Her entire body a bundle of nerves, Ying-Ying finishes her casting motion, then nods toward two other Warpers nearby, both of them also keeping themselves concealed. In unison, they open three portals to the Labyrinth, causing ten-foot-tall shimmering rips in space to materialize. During the dead of night, these gates would draw countless eyes, but with a sunny sky overhead, it takes the nearby human troops half a minute to notice the flood of enemies rushing toward their flank.

That half-minute is all the Warpers need for their ambush to succeed and a wall of demons to crash against the humans, killing dozens of troopers before they even realize what happened.

After summoning their portal, Ying-Ying and the other two Warpers dash deeper into the forest, making sure to get out of the line of fire and escape with their lives.

"Run, run!" Ying-Ying shouts, while fleeing as swiftly as her grunt-body can manage. Lacking the speed and strength mutations of Lords, Barons, Dukes, and Emperors, mere grunts like herself can barely run any faster than humans. Still, with a flood of Mephisto's undead, Scythers, Brutes, and Orcs pouring out of the three portals they left behind, Ying-Ying and her two comrades manage to make a clean getaway before the shooting starts.

After two minutes of running, all three of the Warpers suddenly stumble over their feet and fall to the ground in unison.

"Aaargh!" One of Ying-Ying's companions shouts. The young male Warper, a good-looking fellow with a buzzed head of hair, clutches his heart while writhing on the ground. "H-hurts! It hurts! What's happening?!"

Ying-Ying grits her teeth and closes her eyes. As a senior Warper, she's already summoned dozens of portals during the Stormbringer War today.

"It's... ahh... it's normal! Just give it a minute! Back... backlash! The Wordsmith sealed our spatial powers!"

Ying-Ying swallows several breaths, forcing her heart to calm down. Pins and needles wrack her body as she tries to calm the agonizing pain of having her powers sealed mid-cast. Even so, she manages to pull herself to her feet well before the other two.

"We won't... won't be able to use our powers for a while," Ying-Ying explains, as she helps the other two to their feet. "The Wordsmith's sealing abilities only last a little while, and then they wear off. Not only that, but any second now-"

Before Ying-Ying can finish her sentence, a sensation like rushing air grabs her body and yanks her backward.

_Foop!_

Ying-Ying teleports into the Labyrinth, followed by the other two Warpers. They appear inside some random pitch-black corridor, completely alone, with no other demons nearby. A split-second later, the demons and monsters who made it through their three portals only minutes earlier pop into existence around them, teleported into the Labyrinth via the Wordsmith's power. It's only thanks to their demonic heritage that they can see through the gloom surrounding each other without a need for light.

The good-looking male Warper continues clutching his chest. "W-wait... that's it? We're back inside the Labyrinth... after all of that trouble! It took us an hour to get to that forest!"

"Yeah!" The other Warper complains. "What the hell?! This sucks! All those goons we summoned ended up here, too! That was a total waste of time!"

Ying-Ying shakes her head. "It isn't. I don't understand Ose's plans, but once we recover our powers, we're going right back to Tarus II. You all memorized the forest's position, right?"

The first Warper nods, a weary expression plastered across his face. "Yeah! I'm not some amateur, okay?"

"Right," Ying-Ying says, ignoring his annoyed tone. "Now that you two have visited that spot, your job will be to keep opening portals there. It'll take about an hour for your powers to recover, but keep trying. Once you can open a portal again, send these guys through. Keep summoning, and don't stop."

"What's the point of that?!" The second Warper asks. "Seriously, if that stinking Wordsmith is going to just keep teleporting us away-"

"No complaints!" Ying-Ying snaps. "You have your job, and I have mine! I need to find the next group and lead them to their designated position. The more advantageous spots where we can summon portals, the more our forces can spread out and surround the fleshbags' city! Capturing that city is our goal, and that's all you need to worry about. Now, are you done whining?"

The other two Warpers lower their heads. "Yes, elder sister."

"Great! I'm leaving."

Ying-Ying pauses for a moment to address the other demons and creatures nearby. "You guys, stay here. Wait for these two to open their portals, then jump through. Kill any fleshbags you see. When the Wordsmith sends you back, you keep waiting and return again. Got it?!"

The other demons nod dumbly, shrugging as if they haven't a care in the world.

Satisfied, Ying-Ying nods her head, then breaks into a brisk run as she uses her intuitive demonic understanding of the Labyrinth to plot a course through its vast, interlinked hallways.

After leaving the others behind, Ying-Ying starts weaving left, right, going up and down various slopes and stairways, slowly making her way toward a distant destination.

As the Warper runs, one of her crystal earrings pulses with mana. Ying-Ying pauses midstride and slows to a stop. A look of elation flits across her face as she touches the crystal, opening up a spiritual connection.

"Mara! How are things?"

Mara, possessing a soul crystal of her own, communicates with her sister telepathically.

"Hey, sis. I'm with Artorias still; we're near the city's southern side. I wanted to make sure you were okay."

Ying-Ying nods. "I'm great! Just set up two more portal summoners! I'm on my way to the Return Station now."

"Excellent," Mara replies. "I'm glad things are going well for you. I got word from Ose a few minutes ago; It seems the number of portals closing have started to slow. She thinks we've finally started tiring out the Wordsmith. I have to say, his commitment to not killing any demons is bordering on the ludicrous. He keeps teleporting huge batches of our troops back to the Labyrinth, but I'm starting to wonder how in the devil he has so much mana avail- ah! One sec!"

Mara's line goes quiet, but Ying-Ying doesn't worry. She resumes running toward the Return Station, a place where she can jump back to Tarus II, even with her powers sealed.

After two minutes, Mara reconnects. "Sorry. A group of fleshbag soldiers ambushed me."

"You okay?" Ying-Ying asks.

"Yup, just dandy. Ripped them to pieces, no problem. Anyway, where was I? Oh, right. Ose says the Wordsmith's sealing activity seems to be slowing. I'm surprised it took this bloody long, but at least it finally happened. I was starting to think he'd tapped into some sort of unlimited power source, but I guess he was just a bit more resilient than we expected."

"So, maybe soon, he won't be able to seal my powers again?" Asks Ying-Ying.

"I don't know. Perhaps. There's something funny going on, but I can't figure out what it is. Ose's keeping oddly tight-lipped. Stinking bitch. _Devils_, I can't stand her. The fact Bael chose her as the Third Emperor grinds my gears so much..."

Mara curses a few more times, then returns her focus to the current situation.

"_Ahem_. I managed to piece together one tidbit, something Artorias mentioned in passing. It seems Ose gave Orias a secret ace in the hole. I don't know what it is, but Orias might be the key to everything. I expect some big moves to start soon."

"Noted," Ying-Ying replies, her face filled with determination. "Orias is super reliable. We can count on him!"

"Indeed," Mara confirms. "I've spoken with him many times. Bit of a recluse, but he's got a good head on his shoulders. Any plan hinging on him is practically foolproof. Anyway, I've talked enough. Gotta go. Artorias is ready to move out."

"Okay. Love you, sis! Stay safe!" Ying-Ying says.

"Love you too," Mara concludes.

Ying-Ying's earring fades in color as its mana signature dies off.

As she runs, Ying-Ying's thoughts turn complex.

_This whole war is so strange! First, Ose and most of the Dukes got their butts beat by the Monster King, then Bael lost an eye to those crafty fleshbags... now we all need to rely on a Baron for some reason._

_I wonder what the heck Ose's plan is with Orias? He's not the strongest Baron, but his powers are pretty versatile, kind of like Mara's. Maybe it has something to do with his gems? I bet he's going to capture the Hero's wife and daughter! Yeah, and then the Wordsmith will have to concede!_

Ying-Ying nods to herself, uncertain if she's right, but confident _something_ must be up.

After running for another twenty minutes, she slows down before a seemingly nondescript wall at a random spot in the Labyrinth. She glances at it for all of two seconds before putting her hand on a barely visible ink blotch splattered on the Labyrinth's earthen surface.

Suddenly, a flash of light engulfs her.

_Fwish!_

Ying-Ying disappears from that corridor and travel several hundred miles across the Labyrinth, instantly arriving inside one of the Warpers' many secret dwellings, far from the general demonic population. She trots down the well-illuminated tunnel, one with torch sconces stuck to the walls, all while passing goblins along the way.

These goblins chitter at her while keeping out of her way. She flashes a cute smile at them, ignoring their repulsive green skin and mottled faces. "Hi Jun-Jun! Koopi! Slapster!"

The goblins wave back and mumble their own greetings.

Soon, Ying-Ying arrives in a wide open area, one illuminated with magical purple flames, each fire belched from the mouths of lion statues placed around the room's edges. Dozens of Warpers stand in a circle around one demoness at the center, an ancient-looking Baron with sagging skin and long, grey hair.

The crowd parts for Ying-Ying as she approaches the old woman from behind. "Grandma Ahriman! I'm back!"

Ying-Ying slows to a stop, and the old woman turns around, revealing her still-functioning right eye, as well as her clawed-out left eye, lost during the Energy Wars, 100,000 years ago.

"Oh, Ying-Ying," The old woman says, her voice gravelly and cracked. "_Cough, cough._ You're back. I've been waiting."

"Sorry!" Ying-Ying says, sticking her tongue out cutely. She smiles bashfully at the crowd of Warpers around her, making some of the hot-blooded males and females flare up with lust. "I set up those two juniors at the forest. What next?"

Ahriman uses her long, pointy nails to scratch behind her ear. "Mmm. The return speed has slowed considerably. It appears that the Wordsmith is either running out of mana, or running out of stamina. Either way, it's our good fortune. We've already set up seven hundred and twelve warp points around the humans' main city, but Ose wants at least two thousand before we go all-in on the final assault."

"Sounds like we've got a ways to go," Ying-Ying says, her tone pensive. "I can't tap into my powers yet, but it's only been about thirty minutes. They should return soon."

"I've just received an interesting bit of information from Emperor Yama..." Ahriman says, pausing to emphasize her following words. "He hatched a plan to capture the Hero's daughter. The plan failed. It turns out the little brat possesses Heroic abilities, much like her father, the Wordsmith. I'm sure you can understand the significance of this finding."

Several Warpers nearby gasp in shock, some of them leaning more toward surprise, while others shudder in horror.

"...So, a third Hero, then..." Ying-Ying mutters. "Jason, Hope, and Jason's daughter. I thought the ancient records said there could only ever be one Hero at a time?"

Ahriman nods slowly. "Never, in all my years, have I seen two Heroes at once. However, since we know Jason cloned himself to create Hope, the Council has decided to consider the possibility that one or both Wordsmiths might be using their Wordsmithing to grant Heroic abilities to non-Hero humans. If that is the case, then there could be others we've overlooked; not just Jason's daughter."

"...Frightening..." Ying-Ying mutters, shivering slightly. "What does that mean for us? Are we changing the plan?"

"No. Ose's orders remain unchanged," Ahriman answers. "I'm merely informing you of this new discovery, given your position among the Warpers. When I transport you back to Tarus II, please spread this information among our forces."

"I'm on it," Ying-Ying says with a nod. "And I'm ready for action!"

"Good. I'll send you, Cypher, and Jerald. I'm sure you know where to take them next."

Ying-Ying smiles. "Yes!"

"Great. Alright, you two, join hands with Ying-Ying, circle around me, and allow me a brief moment to cast my spatial magic."

The other two Warpers, Cypher and Jerald, nod politely at their clan sister, then interlock their hands to form a triangle around Grandma Ahriman.

After a minute of weaving magical signs, Ahriman finishes mumbling demonic words under her breath, then raises both of her palms in the air.

"Go!"

Ying-Ying, Jerald, and Cypher shimmer out of reality, traveling through a wormhole to Tarus II and leaving the other Warpers behind.

After they disappear, Ahriman shudders visibly, causing two nearby Warpers to run over and grab her by the arms. "Grandmother! You're exhausted! Take a break, we beg of you!"

Ahriman sags into their grips, sweating heavily. "I'm just... just a little tired, that's all. How can I take it easy when so many of my children are risking their lives out there? Grab me a fresh cup of virgin blood. I just need to wet my tongue a bit; then I can continue."

"Yes, grandmother."

As the two men help ease Ahriman into a wooden chair, the old demoness sighs.

"Ahh, to live long enough to finally see the complete eradication of humanity's Heroes. I thought I'd succeeded already, but it seems the Creator played a trick on me. At least now I can see that day a second time... haha."

_Tarus II, Hero City's General Hospital._

Belial walks into Kar's hospital room at the furthest part of the Eastern Wing, a haggard look etched onto her face. She arrives to find Daisy and Rosalia sitting beside the comatose Monster King's bed, with the rotund demoness looking somewhat tired from having spent the last forty-something hours awake, while Daisy appears noticeably chipper in comparison.

Rosalia turns her head to look at Belial. "...Find anyone?"

Belial groans. "No. They're all dead. Every single devil-damned soldier."

She plops onto the visitor's sofa and rubs her eyes.

"I'm way past running on fumes. I feel worse than I did at any other point in my whole life. I've drained my mana past absolute zero, I haven't slept a wink in five days, and to make matters worse, it feels like there's a bloody turtle expanding inside my chest. It seems like something's going to burst out of my chest."

"That doesn't sound good," Rosalia says. She stands up and walks over to the Second Emperor to examine her condition. "Here, look into my eyes. Let me feel your back. Does it hurt when I press here?"

Belial starts to push Rosalia away, but finds herself too tired to resist. Wearily, she submits to the impromptu examination, bending any which way Rosalia asks.

"Ahh! Hurts... right there. Where you touched."

Rosalia kneels down and squints to look at Belial's skin. "Devils! Look at this discoloration! There's a black mark on your ribcage, and it looks like it's moving around."

"There is?" Belial asks. Her words carry a hint of alarm, but with her energy drained, she can barely pay attention. "...Damn. Probably those Shades. They... they must have hit me with a... with a shadow infection... or something."

"I'm not familiar with this condition at all," Rosalia says, shrugging helplessly. She turns to look at Daisy. "Hey, bra- err, kid! Hero's daughter! Come over here and work your magic on Aunt Belial, will ya?"

Reflexively, Rosalia corrects herself, having realized that antagonizing the child with unknown magical abilities and a penchant for torturing mean people with spankings probably isn't the brightest idea.

Daisy smiles sweetly. "Okay! But only because it's Aunt S'matha! If it was the fat lady in pain, I wouldn't help at all!"

Rosalia scowls. "Shut up. It's not like I'd want your help anyway, you... you _sweet little darling_."

The chubby demoness grumbles under her breath, while Daisy hops off her chair and toddles over to Belial. Daisy walks around Belial several times, gingerly touches the wound, and frowns.

"Can't do nothin'," Daisy proclaims. "It's not an ouchie. It's a... thingy. A mean thingy. Doesn't want me to get close. Says it'll hurt Aunt S'matha if I try to make it cry."

Rosalia's mouth lolls open. "...Huh? What are you babbling about? Are you saying you can heal all kinds of crazy injuries, just like the Second Emperor can, but now you're bloody useless?!"

Daisy ignores Rosalia's taunts. "Dunno. I can't do nothin', and that's that. Not very dope of me, I know..."

Several seconds of awkward silence follow. 

Rosalia lowers her head and sighs. "I'm sorry, Second Emperor. If she can't do anything, and I certainly can't, then..."

"Don't worry," Belial says. "I just need to rest my eyes for a bit. Once I recover some of my mana, I'll rip this parasite out myself."

The Second Emperor's eyes open and close heavily as she struggles to stay awake. While she maintains her delicate balancing act, Daisy shrugs and wanders over to Kar's side. 

"Since Grandpa Gator is sleeping, would it be bad if I woke him up?"

Rosalia barely pays any attention to the child. "Huh? You can't wake Kar up, silly. He's comatose, like Belial was not long ago, Kar can't wake up because he suffered a bad head injury... wait..."

Hardly has Rosalia said those words before she whips her head in Daisy's direction, just in time to see the child climb on a stepstool next to Kar's hospital bed. Daisy reaches over to the crocodile's face and pokes him several times.

"Grandpa Gator! Helloooo! Wakey, wakey! Are you still sleepy? Grandpaaaa!"

Stunned into silence, Rosalia watches as Daisy does to Kar the exact same thing she did to Samantha many days before, injecting healing magic directly into his brain. The girl uses her magic with such ease that she doesn't even seem to realize what she's doing, reviving Kar bit by bit, until finally...

"...Hurgh."

Kar croaks out a single word, then slowly blinks his eyes.

"Noisy. Why is it so loud in here all of a sudden?"

The crocodile tilts his head slightly to the side, where he spots Daisy.

"Hurgh. Turtledove? Nnn, my chest hurts. Why are you here? What happened to Thundertongue?"

"You're awake!" Daisy says, beaming a smile full of sunshine. "You were hurt pretty bad, but no worries! I healed you right up! No more head-ouchies!"

She pauses for a moment before poking a few other spots on the crocodile's body. "Hmm, but there's an ouchie here, and one there... and one here, too! Ooh, this boo-boo looks bad..."

With each poke, Kar's vitality returns more and more, putting a spark of light in his eyes. Eventually, he sits up in the bed and curls the sides of his mouth into a thankful croco-grin. 

"Hurgh! Turtledove! I did not know you could heal people! And look, Big Boobs is here too, along with Sugar! Where is Butterfly, though?"

"You mean Grandma Fairy?" Daisy asks.

"Yes, hurgh. Have you seen her?"

"Not since last night," Daisy replies. "A bunch of stuff happened. Lots of people got boo-boos. Lots of people... died."

As if suddenly remembering the war's existence, a look of alarm appears in Kar's eyes. "Ah! Big Boobs! Tell me what happened! Did Thundertongue make it past the Core? Is she on our world, now?"

Belial, her eyes half-closed, barely manages a nod. "...Yeah. Um... a lot happened. So... Rosalia... you tell him."

Rosalia sits next to Belial, hesitantly deciding to allow the Second Emperor to lean her head against her shoulder. Belial practically melts against Rosalia's arm as waves of exhaustion hit her one by one.

_Odd,_ Rosalia thinks. _Running out of Mana would certainly tire any demon out, as would going without five days of sleep, but that shouldn't be the case for an Emperor. There must be something more to her shadow-infection._

She ignores those thoughts and turns her attention to Kar.

"Well, uh, mister Monster King, sir, it's like this, you see... after Ose knocked you out, the demons attacked the Core. I wasn't there personally, but some of the soldiers who came back told me about Bael's transformation..."

She spends twenty minutes explaining to Kar the events of the day, mostly pulling from secondhand accounts she heard in passing. The more Rosalia speaks, the more alarmed Kar becomes.

"Hurgh! The demons captured Ox-arms? They took over the Core, and now they're surrounding the city with portals? This is bad! Terrible!"

He climbs out of bed, shakes a bit of numbness out of his legs, and flexes his arms.

"Filthy hellspawn! Hurgh! To think the only way they could make it here was after they defeated me! Well, too bad! I survived, and now I'm as good as new, all because of Turtledove's magic! I won't stand here any longer and let those scheming bastards kill even one more of my friends! This king has never felt as angry as he does right at this moment! HURGH!!"

Kar pauses for a moment, then frowns.

"...Hurgh. Well, I suppose that's not entirely true. There was _one_ other instance where I was angrier than now."

With a shake of his head, Kar strides toward the doorway, pausing only to pat Rosalia's head. "Thank you for the information, Sugar. Your assistance was greatly appreciated."

Rosalia blushes. "M-my name, though. It's... it's Rosalia."

"Hurgh. I know. That is what I said, silly Sugar."

With a chuckle, Kar continues on his way and strides out of the room, pausing only to grimace at the horrific scenes of bloody carnage in the hallway outside. Seeing many of his pet humans dead causes the Sphinx to ball his claws into fists and roar in rage.

"Demon scum! You will all PAY!"

A moment later, Kar rushes away, leaving Rosalia and the other two alone.

Daisy plops her butt on Kar's hospital bed and hangs her tiny legs over the side, kicking them back and forth nonchalantly.

Rosalia sighs. "What now? Should we make for the shelters?"

Daisy shrugs. "Nah! We should wait here. Daddy's gonna call soon."

"Daddy?" Rosalia asks, clearly skeptical. "You mean the Wordsmith, Jason?"

"Duh! Jeez, you're not very smart!" Daisy cackles, taunting the demon nurse with a smug grin. "My daddy is the bestest daddy ever! He can do anything! He's awesome; totally dope!"

"...I'm sure he is." Rosalia replies, glowering at the precocious child.

Like clockwork, a minute later, Daisy tilts her head upward. "Daddy!"

Jason's voice speaks within the room, projected magically from a distant location. "Daisy! Err, how... how did you know I was going to call? Never mind, that's not important. Rosalia, did Samantha find any survivors?"

Rosalia shakes her head. "She didn't, Mister Wordsmith, sir. Everyone in the hospital has died."

"Damn..." Jason replies, his voice tinged with regret. "I'm trying to project my powers everywhere at once, but it's taking a toll on me. My head hurts, my eyes are going fuzzy, and I can barely even think. I've got mana to spare, but my brain has a hard limit."

While Jason speaks, a spark appears in Rosalia's eyes. "Oh! Uh, I don't mean to interrupt, Mister Wordsmith, Hero-man, sir... but um... the Second Emperor! I think the Shades infected her with something! She's way more tired than any Emperor ought to be, even under the current circumstances. Can you help her?"

"What, seriously?" Jason asks. "Goddamn, these demons are so... err, sorry."

"It's fine," Rosalia replies, lowering her head. "I'm... I'm sure you didn't mean _me_."

"Yeah, but still. It's not good to generalize," Jason explains. "Good demons exist. Personally, I believe that demons and humans could coexist, given the right incentives. We just need to stop ripping each other apart. Better said than done, though. Anyway, let me just... **Inspect.**"

A Word of Power travels instantly across time and space, pressing itself invisibly against Belial's body.

Not long after, Jason speaks again. "Creepy. It's some sort of... magical nutrient-parasite. It's feeding on Samantha's demonic mana. Don't worry, though. This little jerk won't hurt her any longer! **Sever! Expel. Heal!**"

Instantly, the parasite loses its hold on Belial's internal organs, teleports out of her body, and fizzles to ash under the daylight streaming into the room from outside. Belial's wounds heal, restoring her body to near-perfect working condition.

The Second Emperor stirs, looking noticeably less lethargic. "...Huh? Strange. I suddenly feel a bit better than before."

"Let's improve your situation even further," Jason says, startling Belial. "My mana is limitless! **Energize!**"

A flood of magical energy fires from Jason' position, tears across the fabric of reality, and floods into Belial's heart. Any remaining lethargy vanishes from her face, making her jump to her feet, as if compelled by the God of Vitality himself.

"I'm awake! Whoa! What the heck- what did you do, Jason?"

"Nothing much," Jason says, trying not to sound smug. "I gave you, like, one and a half drops of primordial energy. No big deal."

"Wow!" Daisy chirps. "That's so dope, daddy! You're wicked cool!"

"Eh... yeah. Thanks, sweetheart," Jason laughs. "Belial, I can't waste any more time. There's no reason for you to stay at the hospital. You're a Demon Emperor; we could use your strength on the frontlines. I'll keep healing people and sealing the demons inside the Labyrinth... but I need you to take out the big threats among our enemies. If you can't bring yourself to kill them, then at least slow them down."

Belial snorts. "No problem, I can handle that. But what about you? Are you going to get your hands dirty?"

Jason hesitates.

"...If I must."

"Good," Belial replies, puffing out her chest. "I think you should know something, Jason, but please don't tell Phoebe. I've already killed once today. I slew three of Yama's Shades. Given how I've broken the vow I made to my deceased sister, I hope that will convince you of how dire our situation is."

"It does, and I know," Jason says. "Take care. Daisy, you and Rosalia should go to the shelters, okay? It will be safe there."

"Okay, daddy! Bye-bye!"

Daisy waves at the air cheerfully, then lowers her hand. As Jason's voice fades away, Daisy's smile disappears, and a look of sadness replaces it.

"Daddy was really scared. He's tired, too. There are bad people everywhere, Aunt S'matha..."

"I know," Belial says, as she trots over, picks Daisy up, and hugs her gently. "But don't worry. Now that your auntie and grandpa are about to join the battle, we're sure to win! You just go to the shelter with Rosalia, okay? Be good, and don't cause her any trouble."

Daisy dugs Belial back. "Okay. Stay safe, Aunt S'matha. It wouldn't be very dope if a bad guy hurt you."

"Haha. No, it wouldn't."

A minute later, Belial punches the wall, using her incredible strength to blast open a path to the outside world for Rosalia and Daisy. As they leave, Belial perks her ears up, but even with her Emperor-level hearing, she doesn't detect any distant gunfire.

"Hmm. The demons have already surrounded Hero City, but they haven't yet broken our perimeter. Daisy and Rosalia are going south, to the underground pyramid shelters... but I should head west. That's where the strongest demons are sure to appear. They'll want to reinforce their main forces to form a bulwark, and thus, force their way past our defenses."

Swiveling on her heel, Belial heads back into the hospital. She steps into the main corridor and stomps westward, avoiding the bodies of her fallen friends and comrades. 

"_Tch._ Damn! Never have I felt so worthless as I do right now. I saved all of these soldiers' lives, but failed to protect them from that filthy Shade. _Yama._ This time, you've royally pissed me off. Your whores killed my friends. They butchered these helpless humans mercilessly, all for a chance to boost your harem. You disgusting, wretched little _insect._ If I make it out of this war, and trust me, _I will_... I'm coming for you. Your so-called Hidden Hell means nothing to me. I'll rip it stone from stone. I'll pay you back tenfold for your atrocities!"

Belial gnashes her teeth. Her blood-red eyes turn pitch-black for a moment as a sense of humiliation and regret burns inside her heart like a roaring inferno. Her Demon Emperor aura surges into the heavens, and she lets out a bloodthirsty roar!

"Gaaaah!! I hate you! I hate you more than life itself! I'm sick of standing by, watching while the people I love die! My sister, my husband, and all the others, too! They died, while I survived, and for what reason?! None! Their deaths were senseless, a sacrifice to spare me!"

"I never wanted their mercy! Why couldn't I have perished along with Satan? Why must I suffer and languish in loneliness?! If you want to fight me, then so be it! I'll give you a devil-damned fight! I'll make you all regret initiating this war! I'll show you why I'm the Second Emperor of demonkind!"

Without another word, Belial storms forward, the sheer magnitude of her demonic aura forcing the walls away from her, crumbling the hospital's foundations to powder.

As she walks, her hatred becomes a smoldering furnace, one fueled by her shame and former sense of helplessness.

However.

Once Belial arrives at the central corridor, just before the crumbled remains of the hospital's eastern wing, she pauses and flicks her head to the left.

"Who goes there? Show yourself!"

A tiny figure half-buried under the rubble flinches. After hearing her voice, the figure lifts up its head, revealing the face of Grima, the hospital's receptionist goblin. Still dressed in his suit, Grima appears to be in surprisingly good condition, despite all of the dust and crumbled powder covering him. He stands inside the reception area and blinks twice while gazing at Belial.

"Oh, Second Emperor. You're alive? I just returned and found the hospital in shambles. What happened? Why is everyone dead?"

Belial gazes at the goblin in confusion. "_I'm_ alive? I thought you perished, Grima! I finished a full walkabout not fifteen minutes ago and couldn't find anyone still breathing! Where have you been?"

The goblin dusts himself off. He reaches into the pile of rubble he just climbed out of, fishes around inside it for a moment, then extracts an electronic datapad.

"My apologies. I left last night to deal with some errands, and only just now came back. I never expected the hospital to end up in ruins, but oddly, the rest of the city seems fine."

"...That's my fault," Belial mutters, looking away. Her rage fades somewhat as she releases a sigh. "Yama's Shades attacked us. They tried to capture Jason's daughter, but she surprised everyone when she revealed she had Heroic powers, just like her father. She beat the tar out of her attacker, but it was too little, too late. The Shades had already butchered all of our patients. Only Daisy, Rosalia, and myself survived, along with the Monster King, Kar."

"Goodness!" Grima exclaims. He hastily starts tapping on his datapad while shaking his head. "That doesn't sound good at all, not one bit. With all of our patients dead, what are we to do? The teleportation array has broken down, so the Rescue Modules won't save any other troopers' lives..."

"I know," Belial says, shrugging helplessly. "I'm going to go and fight on the frontlines. Jason is assisting our forces remotely by healing our troops and teleporting the demons back to the Labyrinth. You should go to the shelters, Grima. If you see any other hospital workers on the way... tell them what happened."

Grima nods. "Yes. Thank you, Second Emperor. I will do that."

"Great. I'm off, then," Belial says. She gives a quick goodbye wave, then exits through the hospital's main doors, leaving the goblin behind.

After Belial disappears, Grima stares at her afterimage for several minutes, then raises his datapad and continues tapping its surface, typing out a string of notes.

"Interesting. I did not anticipate the Shadow Emperor would make such a bold move. And the Hero's daughter possessing magical abilities? This is high-priority information. My value continues to increase. If humanity survives Stormbringer, this report will boost my prestige considerably."

The little goblin falls silent and continues tapping out a message on his datapad. After concluding, he saves the file for later, then slides the datapad into one of his suit's inner pockets.

"Hm. With this hospital destroyed, where can I gather additional intelligence? Now is not the time for caution. Perhaps the western front? No. If I am seen, it may expose my identity. Should I try scouting the eastern edge of Hero City? Negative. I cannot risk the demons seeing me, either. They seem to be encircling the city from all angles. Escape is impossible."

Grima steps out of the rubble and exits the hospital. After pausing for a few seconds, he starts off toward the northern side of the city.

"Perhaps in the event of a total societal collapse, my best course of action would be to remain in a position close to the Covenant Ships. Then, if an evacuation proves necessary, I will ensure my survival to deliver this report."

"Yes. I believe preserving my life is the most important feat I can perform at this time."

Slowly, the goblin shambles away from the hospital, off toward the camouflaged spaceships at Hero City's northern edge.

All the while, the Stormbringer war continues to rage, unabated.
