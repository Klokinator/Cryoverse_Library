Part 141: Enter the Dragon (Part 2)

Satan skitters down the steep bluff leading into the dragon's inner chamber, but his Vectors prevent him from suffering any injuries when he lands at the bottom, as they absorb the brunt of the impact. Belial follows after him, stretches out her legs, and zig-zags from left to right. She dives into a roll and jumps to her feet, using her superior strength and cat-like agility to her advantage.

Solomon and I hover to the base of the cliff. When we land, I watch as the demons freeze in place. Both of them slow their breathing as they gaze ahead into the darkness.

"Is... is he here?" Belial asks. "I don't see him."

"He's here..." Satan murmurs. "I still sense his overwhelming power..."

A shadow shifts barely fifty feet in front of us.

Leviathan's monstrously huge head, seemingly twice as big as when I last saw him, raises off the dirt and swivels to face the intruders. The dragon's white teeth sparkle in the darkness of the cave, but their appearance fills the demons with dread instead of glee.

"**Hoh. Look who's come to say hello.**"

Leviathan's eyes shift from Satan to Belial, and back to the former again. He stares at the crystal in Satan's grasp for a moment.

"**I assumed you perished at the hands of your tormentors. Why have you returned?**"

Satan shakes off his nerves. With a smile, he struts forward and bows deeply.

"O-oh, great Leviathan! It is I, Satan, your loyal servant! This unworthy demon stands before you today to ask-"

"**Stop.**"

Leviathan growls in annoyance.

"**That disgusting, grandiose tone does not suit you. Speak plainly, lest I scorch your bones to ash.**"

Satan falters. He pauses mid-sentence, winces, and lurches back to his feet. "Right. Yeah, I knew that. Uhh... hell, I planned a whole speech and everything..."

Leviathan sighs. "**Kuu... spare me. You've come to ask me for some ridiculous boon, acting as though I owed you anything more than a polite greeting. It's not often I meet someone with a death wish.**"

"Err... well..." Satan pales, unsure how to reply. He shoots a glimpse at Belial, who shrugs at him. "It's... okay, look. I do need a favor, alright? I know you're an all-powerful dragon. I will pay you back in any way that I can."

The dark dragon rolls his eyes. Sarcasm drips from every word. "**Oh, I see. You'll owe me a favor. Your bargaining posture is highly _dubious_.**"

Satan clenches his hands into fists. "I know I don't have much to offer, dammit! Can't you hear me out?! My girl is dead. I need to bring her back. If you can do something to revive her-"

A horrible sound rips through the air, one of bone scraping across rock and soil. Leviathan rises onto his haunches, stretches out his front leg, and points a claw at Satan.

"**Quiet, red rat. I care not for you, nor your brothers and sisters. I know well why you've come. When you accepted my power, you laid bare your thoughts, desires, and darkest fears. Reviving this lifeless female appears to be the sole request bubbling to the top of your feeble mind. Whatever happened to the grandiose promises you made? Where are the mountains of angel bodies now that you have reached some minor crest of power?**"

Satan winces. "Hey, bub, don't be a dick. I know you've been living under a mountain, but my people have been kicking ass out there! We've killed hundreds of angels!"

Leviathan chuckles, sending flames spewing from the sides of his mouth.

"**Hundreds.**"

He shakes his head slowly.

"**There are millions of angels still alive, Satan. In the beginning, there were billions. You've made no progress since we last spoke, besides increasing your power and influence. I'm... disappointed.**"

Satan clenches his teeth together, then swings his finger toward the dragon.

"You ain't done anything either, dragon! Now that I can sense mana levels, I know the truth. You're way stronger than all of the angels combined! Why don't you pop out of this mountain and kill 'em yourself if you want 'em dead so bad, eh?!"

Leviathan goes quiet. He stares at Satan for several long seconds, making the Devil's bravado falter slightly.

"**Do... not... test me... imp. Have you forgotten? I care little for war and violence. Such were the ways of my brothers and sisters. I sleep because I enjoy the peace and quiet of my cozy cave. My grudge against the angels is little more than a distant memory, a faint scar which occasionally awakens a smoldering of anger in my chest.**"

Leviathan lowers his head and moves closer to the two demons. "**Perhaps I possess the power to slaughter all of the angels, and perhaps I don't. Destroying them myself doesn't interest me, and that's all that matters. As for you, if you want something, then you should know that dropping to your knees and begging for it won't be enough. I expect results. If you can't give me something of equivalent value, I would never consider helping you.**"

Satan winces at the dragon's verbal barrage. "What do you mean? All I want is for you to bring my girl back! I know it's probably not the easiest thing in the world, but surely-"

"**Do not patronize me when you have so little knowledge of the universe,**" Leviathan hisses. A flash of rage passes across his face. Flames belch from the corners of his mouth. "**Fool. Once someone dies, their soul enters the Great Beyond. If you had captured hers before it fled, I could have revived her with ease. Instead, you brought me nothing but her lifeless husk. The only way to revive this female would be via the usage of Equivalent Exchange, and there's _no_ possibility I'd waste my life on some trifle for your amusement.**" 

Satan stares at the dragon for a few moments in silence.

"You... you mean... you'd have to..."

"**To give my life for your mate's, yes. Hah! Imagine that! A mighty dragon, the last in the universe, wasting his life on some frail mortal! 
There's only one reason I'd do such a thing, and you've proven it's beyond your power to achieve.**"

Belial clears her throat. When she speaks, her voice cracks. "O-only one reason... and... what would that be?"

Leviathan snorts. "**Slaughtering all of the angels, as I originally ordered. The Cherubs, the Seraphs, and the Archangels, too. A task far beyond what the whole of your species could ever accomplish.**"

Satan and Belial stare, bewildered, at Leviathan's smirking face.

"You're... joking... right?" Satan asks. "To bring back Nerissa, you want me to kill all the angels?"

Leviathan chuckles. "**Kukuku... that's right. What's the matter, Satan? Where did your pomposity go? Haven't got what it takes? Two thousand years, and all you've managed is killing a handful of Cherubs! Perhaps that's giving you too much credit. I'd wager they were only Acolytes!**"

The dragon chuckles quietly to himself, but in the darkness, Satan smiles.

"Heh. That's all you want, dragon? A few dead Archangels? The extinction of demonkind's greatest enemy?"

Leviathan pauses. He cocks his head. "**Hmm? More bravado?**"

"No. It's funny you mentioned the Archangels. I have a surprise for you."

Satan glances at Belial for a moment, then returns his gaze to Leviathan, as if to say, _well, she'd have found out sooner or later._

"I've already defeated one of the Archangels and taken his soul for myself."

The dragon blinks slowly.

"**Did you? Ahh. I see, now. The souls inside your body... one of them shines brighter than the rest. I paid them little heed before, but now...**"

Leviathan lowers his head and brings his eye close to Satan, casting glowing orange light on both of the demons, as well as myself and Solomon.

"**Which Archangel was it? Raphael? No, you don't possess a millionth the battle experience he does. Gabriel? No. You're much too weak to face-off against a battle-hardened warrior the likes of him. Perhaps the weakest, Camael? No, you stated your victim was male. Hmm. Consider me intrigued. Who is it?**"

Satan taps his head. "Samael. The Archangel of Change."

Leviathan blinks. He pulls away from Satan, slowly.

"**Samael? There's no chance he would associate with the others. I can only assume you ambushed him when his guard was down. Is his spirit intact, or have you shattered his soul?**"

Satan shakes his head. "No, he's fine. I can talk to him, usually. He's been awfully quiet since I arrived here, though."

"**Indeed.**"

Leviathan tilts his head left and right. Several emotions pass over the dragon's face as his eye scans Satan's body, but I can't tell what they are.

"**Samael. Have you nothing to say to me, your old friend? Has time worn down our bond beyond recognition? Or are you simply speechless that we may speak yet again?**"

Leviathan's words send a tingle of worry through my mind. I glance at Solomon. "Wait, what's he saying? Is Leviathan joking?"

Solomon doesn't reply. He continues to gaze at the scene before us and ignore me.

Satan shivers as Leviathan's glowing orange eye dresses him down. "Hey, no need to stare, alright? Only I can hear him. Samael-"

"**Fool. As I told you before, Satan, your thoughts lie bare before me. If you can hear Samael in your mind, then I can, as well. Samael! Why do you remain silent? Speak up!**"

...

If Samael hears Leviathan's words, he shows no sign of it. The Archangel doesn't say anything, even as the dragon continues to pester him.

Satan eyes Leviathan suspiciously. "Hold on, why are you calling Samael an 'old friend'?"

Leviathan ignores Satan's question. His eye-slit narrows, projecting a concentrated beam of light on Satan. A moment later, the dragon taps one of his claws on the ground. A pulse of energy shoots from his eye and slams into Satan, knocking the Devil backward as it catches him off-guard. Satan gasps in surprise and digs his Vectors into the ground, catching himself a moment later.

"Cripes!! What's the big idea, you ugly-"

Satan stops mid-sentence.

Three feet before him, an astral body materializes, one born of pure mana. Samael, the Archangel of Change, cowers in terror. His six-foot-tall form appears diminutive as he shivers in fright under the dragon's gaze.

"S-stay... stay b-back! Foul... ancient... black beast! S-servant of darkness! Blackheart! M-murdering fiend!!"

Tears flow from Samael's eyes as he tries to make himself as small as possible. Every bone in his body rattles and shakes as fear spreads throughout his very being. Never once does he open his eyes. He shields his head and ducks beneath his arms, almost as if waiting for the dragon to unleash infernal flames and incinerate him.

Belial's jaw drops. "That's _Samael_?! Satan! I can't believe you never told me!"

Satan nods slowly. Her words only half-penetrate his swirling thoughts. "Yeah... I... had my reasons. Leviathan! What did you... no... _how_ did you bring Samael here?"

The dragon flicks his eye to Satan for a moment, then back to the Archangel cowering in terror. "**I'm projecting his spirit into the realm of the living. A trifle, for one as mighty as I. More importantly...**"

Leviathan trails off. Confusion stretches across the dragon's face as he slowly pans his head around to scan Samael's cowering form from several directions.

"**My savior... why do you tremble and shiver before me? What slight have I committed to merit such a reaction?**"

Samael jerks his head up. His eyes bulge as he meets the dragon's gaze. "D-do not call me friend, monster! I am n-no ally of thine! I know well the devastation thy kind hath brought upon the universe!! A thousand deaths would be too slight a punishment for thee!"

The dragon's jaw slackens. His expression turns from visible confusion to pure bewilderment.

"**I... I do not understand. Satan, you said his spirit was intact when you killed him. What devilry have you foisted upon my compatriot for him to act in such a manner?**"

Satan scratches his head. "Bloody hell, I have no idea. I thought all the Archangels were your enemies. Now you're getting all chummy with Samael. When I lived in Heaven, none of the angels even mentioned the dragons. Samael certainly didn't."

"**Heaven?**"

"The place where the angels live. You ain't heard of it?"

Leviathan blinks slowly. "**I have not. Hm. More has changed than I expected. Samael, calm down. There's no need to worry. I am not going to hurt you.**"

Samael's knees shake. "Thy words are poison, winged one! I will not allow thee to use me as a pawn in thy games! I will never turn against my family for the sake of... of a dragon!!"

The dragon falters. "**You... you wound me, Samael. Never did I expect to hear such awfulness escape your lips. Tell me, brother, what changed? What has turned you to the other side? Does Raphael now possess leverage over you? The Samael I remember would never convert to the bloodthirsty ways of the other Archangels.**"

Pins and needles tingle in my hands and feet. I glance at Solomon nervously. "Hey, what is _happening?_ Is the dragon lying?"

Solomon shakes his head, but he doesn't reply.

Samael slowly raises himself to a standing position. His body continues to tremble, but he forces himself to meet the dragon's gaze. "W-what wicked lies art thou attempting to whisper in my ear, foul beast? My brothers and sisters are not the bloodthirsty ones. The dragons were! That is why we exterminated them!"

The dragon narrows his eyes. A menacing expression passes across his face. "**My name is Leviathan. Use it.**"

"I care not what thy name is!"

The dragon curls up his lips. Venomous rage appears on his face. 

"**I understand. Something has gone horribly wrong. You cannot be the same Samael I once knew. After all, you _gave_ me my name. You, Cassiel, and Muriel were the only Archangels sympathetic to our plight. Have you forgotten them too, Samael? Have you forgotten your two beloved sisters?! ANSWER ME!**"

Leviathan's voice triples in intensity. He lunges toward Samael, and his pupils dilate to fill up his entire eye.

"**What changed?! What turned you into this vicious, hollow being?! Where has your empathy gone? Why have you forsaken me?!**"

Samael winces as the dragon bellows at him. He takes a step back and stares wide-eyed at the super-massive being challenging his core beliefs.

"S-stop! I won't listen to thy lies!! Satan, we must leave at once, and warn my family! They have to hunt this creature down before it can commit another genocide!!"

Samael turns to plead with Satan, but the Devil stares at him tight-lipped, a look of concentration on his face.

"No, Samael. Leviathan, calm down. Do not blame him. I've just remembered something my ol' chum here told me."

Leviathan flicks his gaze to the Devil. "**Do you know what happened to Samael?**"

Satan nods slowly. "Maybe. Samael told me once... after the war between dragons, Titans, and the angels, Raphael did some stuff. He erased the memories belonging to everyone but himself. That includes Samael's."

The dragon's rage subsides. He pulls his head back and exhales a thin cloud of smoke.

"**I see. That... that explains a great many things.**"

Samael shoots a dirty look back at Satan. "Do not attempt to sully my brother's name, Satan! He only did what was needed to protect our people. Because of the memory-wipes, we were able to live in peace. It was only because we erased our pasts that angels and Titans were able to reach a peaceful resolution. Raphael is a hero!"

Silence fills the room.

Leviathan snorts, sending a small plume of flame erupting from his nose. It splashes against the ceiling before dissipating a moment later.

"**Hmph. A hero, you say? You always used to say that whichever side won the war would rewrite the history books as they pleased. I never understood your meaning until this very moment.**"

Leviathan lowers his head. A listless look appears in his eyes.

"**I know not what led you to accept a mind-wipe, Samael. Perhaps you did it to ensure my existence would never be uncovered. If so, I thank you. It would be yet another boon in the long line of gifts you gave me. Thanks to your concealment magic, the other Archangels never discovered me, despite living beneath their gazes. Still, I find it hard to believe you would ever concede to your brother. Especially after... the fate... which befell Cassiel and Muriel.**"

Samael stares at the dragon for a moment.

"Cassiel... Muriel... what of them? What do you know, dragon?"

"**I ask the same question of you, Samael. I possess memories of the primordial era, but it seems you do not. Tell me, old friend, what memories rest in your bosom? Do you still remember the throbbing in your chest as you held their bodies in your arms?**"

Samael's lips quiver. "Their... bodies?"

Samael echoes Leviathan. A look of worry embeds itself in his forehead.

"I know only what Raphael has told me. Cassiel and Muriel perished at the claws of the dragons. I... I tried to save them, but failed. S-surely, you do not expect me to believe otherwise?"

Leviathan sighs.

The dragon's head slumps down until it rests on the ground.

"**How... tragic. To think... while I rested peacefully beneath this mountain, Raphael stole away the most precious memories of your beloved sisters. Once, Cassiel and Muriel held the most important place in your heart. You doted on them, and they on you. The three of you sought to end the war between the angels and dragons, but the Seraphim and Cherubim would not listen. To them, the Ophanim's complaints would result in boredom and complacency. After all... your fellow Archangels were...**"

Before Leviathan can continue, a voice cries out from behind me. "Solomon!! What is the meaning of this?!"

I turn to see Raphael and Gabriel standing fifteen feet behind Solomon and me. I didn't hear them arrive, so quiet were their footsteps.

Solomon doesn't look at the newcomers. Instead, he motions with his hand and freezes the vision. "I'm merely showing Jason the entirety of the Ancient Era, Raphael... as I promised."

Raphael's eyes bulge in rage. "Stop projecting lies into the boy's mind! If thou continueth, thou shalt only further corrupt the Hero's imagination beyond-"

Solomon turns and raises his palm to silence Raphael. "There are no lies here, Raphael. I searched for decades before finally uncovering the truth of the universe. Worry not, for I will not show Jason that indefensible secret which you clutch closest to your breast. However... he deserves to know _everything_ else."

"He deserves nothing!" 

Raphael storms toward us, shoves me aside, and seizes Solomon by his coat.

"Bastard! How dare ye corrupt our legacy with these... these putrid falsehoods!! Everything thou hath shown the boy has led to him believe the demons were champions of justice, while the angels were violent psychopaths! Nothing could be further from the truth!"

Solomon's expression remains impassive. He doesn't try to pull away from Raphael's grip as the Archangel squeezes his robes tightly.

"You have done that yourself. I only wish to protect the rest of humankind from the terror of the demons. Defending the rose-tinted image of your people means little to me."

"Do not test me, Solomon."

Raphael's voice turns icy-cold.

"Thou kneweth this would happen from the beginning. Art thou in league with the demons, after all?! What are thy intentions?!"

Solomon purses his lips. He closes his eyes for a moment, and his body vanishes from Raphael's grasp, causing the old man to stumble as he grabs at the air wildly. Raphael manages to reorient himself as Solomon appears a few feet to my left. He adjusts his collar before speaking.

"I'm sorry, Raphael. The truth is that angels and demons were never all that different, despite your attempts to distort everyone's perceptions of the past. In a way, I am glad your people perished. I only wish the same could happen to the demons... especially those who stole my family from me. With Satan dead, half of my desire has been fulfilled, but as for the other half..."

I turn to my left. Solomon's face remains deathly calm. Something about his word touches a nerve and makes me uneasy.

"Are you lying to me, Solomon? Is Raphael? You could be showing me anything in these visions. I don't know what the truth is and isn't."

Solomon nods. "You're a bright one, Jason. Despite how Raphael belittles you by calling you 'child' and 'boy,' you have a man's intuition and hunger for the truth. Search your feelings. If these allegations against the angels are little more than lies, do you think Raphael would react in the manner he has?"

I nod.

"He might be pushing back against genuine lies, yes. Or... he might be trying to stop you from releasing damning truths that would paint angelkind in a negative light. I'm not certain which is correct."

Solomon smiles. "Aye. The truth can vary depending on the time of day and what direction the wind blows. I will state outright, Jason, that I have not shown you a single false vision, nor will I in the future. Raphael worked hard to bring about peace and prosperity to the angels, but I believe you would agree with the statement, 'the ends do not justify the means.' Raphael's method of enforcing order was... shall we say... totalitarian?"

Raphael goes quiet. Several seconds pass before he can muster a reply. 

"I did what I felt necessary to bring about peace."

Solomon nods. "Of course. And I will do the same."

He turns away from Raphael and motions with his hand, causing the vision to continue playing. Raphael balks and starts to say something, but Solomon waggles his finger and shakes his head.

"You're not convincing me of anything today, Raphael. Stand quietly and watch. What happens next will be of great interest to you."

...

I don't know what Solomon's game is, nor what his goals are, but I know the vision he's showing me now is the closest to some form of truth I've seen yet. Everything up to now has only been a precursor.

_The dragon must know something crucial._

Leviathan continues speaking.

"**After all... your fellow Archangels were... bloodthirsty killers.**"

Samael's voice comes out as little more than a whisper. "...Lies."

"**No. I speak the truth. Uzziel created the first dragon. You and the other Ophanim helped reshape Jörmungandr into a mighty being, one against which the precursor angels could test their might. Of course, at the time, the angels had been fighting amongst themselves for several ages. Raphael thought it prudent to create a scapegoat for their grievances to prevent the annihilation of all angels. A monster against whom he could direct their hatred. In that respect, he succeeded.**"

Leviathan continues. "**My mother was subjected to endless torture and agony. She withstood magical and physical attacks as they unleashed their basest cruelties against her, but their torment was ceaseless, never-ending. Countless millennia passed as they beat and battered her around, boiling a rage inside of her unlike any other. Only the Ophanim comprehended the barbarism on display and sought to end it. You protected her, at least for a while, and allowed her to give birth.**"

Samael stares at the dragon, rapt with attention. "Have... have thee any proof of these claims?"

Leviathan ignores his question. "**When the other Archangels discovered my mother had given birth, their rage tripled. They turned against your faction. They singled all the Ophanim for extermination. Haven't you ever wondered why angels could defeat dragons as mighty as Jörmungandr, if my kind were so overwhelmingly powerful? Have you, Samael?**"

Leviathan's voice tightens. "**We lost because of our peaceful nature. Dragons did not wish to fight. We did not desire death, pain, and vengeance. No, such darkness came from your people... from the angels.**"

Samael digs his feet into the dirt and gnashes his teeth together. "I don't believe thee. Everything thou hath said is far too convenient for my liking. How dare thou believeth I would turn against Raphael, simply because I do not know our history. I have no reason to believe thy words over his."

Leviathan nods. "**Indeed. I see that, now. However, if your memories have not been erased, but merely suppressed, then... perhaps...**"

The dragon closes his eyes. His scales press together as a look of concentration washes over his face.

"**Hmm... hng...**"

Samael raises an eyebrow.

"What art thou doing?"

Leviathan doesn't respond. Instead, he continues to concentrate.

"**Nnn... Satan... prepare thyself...**"

"Huh?" Satan replies. "For what-"

The dragon's eyes bolt open. A surge of blood-red energy discharges from his mind and slams into Satan, knocks the Devil off his feet, and throws him on his ass. Satan yelps in surprise as he loses his footing, but a moment later, he clutches his chest. "Gahh!! Sh-shit! Fucking... hell...!! HURTS!!"

If the look of pain on Satan's face is intense, the look on Samael's is ten magnitudes greater. The Archangel's astral body collapses to the ground. He writhes around and wraps his arms and wings around himself and curls into a ball. "Geyaaaahh!!! Ahhhhh!!!!"

Leviathan doesn't blink. He continues pointing his eye at Satan. "**Endure it. The holy magic binding Samael's mind is powerful and ancient. Annulling it will require considerable energy and effort.**"

Belial drops to her knees and cradles Satan's head in her arms as he continues to roll around. "Leviathan, stop! You're hurting him!"

"**I cannot stop,**" The dragon murmurs. He brings his eye closer to throw even more energy at the Devil. "**This is all I can do. I will remove the seal binding Samael's memories. Since I do not know the cipher which binds him, I must use brute force. Please, withstand the pain a little longer.**"

Satan and Samael roll on the ground, screaming and crying. Several minutes pass as Leviathan intensifies his mental barrage.

"Satan is going to die!" Belial yells, as tears form in her eyes. "Please stop! I beg you!!"

"**I'm nearly finished. Only a few seconds remain.**"

Behind me, Raphael gasps. "Unbelievable... he's... undoing..."

The dragon recoils. "**Kuh! Mother, guide me. I've finished!**"

Satan and Samael both cease their screaming and fall limp. The Devil's body sags against the cave floor as Belial wraps her hands beneath his head to support him.

"S-Satan. Lover. Are you alright? Say something, please!"

Satan grunts. "Guhh..."

Samael says nothing. The spirit-body of the Archangel flops lifelessly on the ground. 

Leviathan's gaze softens. He leers toward Samael. "**Old friend... you still draw breath. Please, speak. Do you remember me, now?**"

Samael's eyes stare ahead blankly.

After a moment, they refocus. His gaze raises, ever so slightly, to meet Leviathan's.

"Hah... haha..."

Leviathan watches.

"Hahahaha... ahaha..."

Samael slowly shifts his body. As Satan regains his bearings, the two demons turn to watch as Samael picks himself off the ground and rises to his feet.

"Ahahaha... haaahaha... AHAHAAHA!!!"

Samael's quiet chuckles suddenly explode into maniacal laughter.

"HAAHHAHAH!! AHAHAHA!!! HOHOHO?! Hahaha... hah..."

The Archangel sags to his knees. He rests his face in his palms as his body begins to shake and tremble.

"Nnn... guhh... no, no, no..."

"**Samael. What is wrong?**" The dragon asks. "**Why do you weep?**"

"Hnn... Cassiel... Muriel... guu... their blood... it was everywhere..."

Samael chokes back agony-drenched sobs. He wraps his wings around himself, as if to try and block the pain from reaching his heart.

He fails. His sobs grow more intense.

"Everything I fought for... EVERYTHING!! It's all gone... they're gone... the Ophanim were all slaughtered..."

Leviathan sucks in a breath. "**Tch. As I suspected.**"

Samael raises his head. His tear-stained face and agonized expression sends a pang of emotion through my heart as well. With a single glance, I finally see everything he's lost.

"Raphael... he... he tricked me..."

A sharp gasp behind me makes me turn and watch as Raphael takes a step back. The old man's eyes widen as he gazes at his brother.

"**He tricked you?**" Leviathan asks.

Samael nods. "He and the other Archangels... they held me down... they merged into the Seraphim and Cherubim. I couldn't fight back. Raphael laid his hands on me. He... he wiped my memories. I never wanted... I never agreed to... to his terms..."

My jaw drops.

"Raphael! Is that true?! Is Samael lying?!"

Raphael's eyes flick to me. The old man opens his mouth to speak, but his words come haltingly. "I... I didn't... he wouldn't listen to reason! He only wished to fight!"

"You forcibly erased your brother's memories! What the hell is wrong with you?!"

Gabriel reaches out and touches Raphael's arm. He gazes at me with an expression like that of a rock. No emotion graces his face. "Better to wipe Samael's memories than to kill him."

"That's no excuse! How could you do such a thing to him, after everything-"

Before I can continue, Samael screams in rage. "MICHAAAEL! He took them from me!! He took my beautiful sisters!! Ahhh!!!"

The Archangel punches the ground with all his strength, but his hand passes through it without causing a disturbance. Again and again, he wails and smashes his fists against the rocky cave floor, but never once do they make a sound.

Satan staggers to his feet. He and Belial gaze in stunned silence as the Archangel pours his heart out in emotionally-wrenching screams of anger and rage.

"-took them! Took them! TOOK THEM FROM ME!!"

Samael collapses. He falls onto his face and sobs, unable to muster the strength to rise to his feet.

Leviathan closes his eyes.

"**I am sorry, old friend. I knew of Michael's treachery, but... for you... experiencing those memories must feel like losing your sisters all over again.**"

Satan clenches his hands into fists. "Fucking... bastards. I can't believe it. The old man did something unforgivable to Samael, my friend. That worthless, senile piece of dirt..."

I glare daggers at Raphael. "He said it, but I'm starting to agree."

Raphael doesn't reply. Instead, he turns away and stares into the darkness, unable to meet my gaze.

Samael flops his head to the side. He stares at Leviathan, and his raging emotions seem to subside somewhat.

"L... Leviathan... that's right... I bestowed that name upon thee..."

"**You did.**"

"Muriel... she always... toward thee..."

Leviathan closes his eyes. "**Muriel was as dear to me as my beloved mother.**"

"I failed..."

"**No. I failed you, old friend. I left you to suffer in the hands of your brothers and sisters. I falsely assumed-**"

"I did not suffer," Samael says. The Archangel gingerly lifts himself off the ground and plops onto his butt. "Nay, I lived a wonderful life in Heaven. Too exquisite for one as wretched as myself. I did not deserve such frivolous joys as what Raphael bestowed upon me. Perhaps, my brother thought to atone for his sins by removing my memories. Perhaps he allowed me to live in the hopes that he could redeem his soul."

Samael's lip trembles.

"Unforgivable. Muriel and Cassiel perished at Michael's hands, yet my brother did not punish him. Raphael forced me to smile and treat my sister's murderer with undeserved respect. I never avenged them. I never paid Michael back... for what he _did_..."

Leviathan waits a few moments before opening his eyes. 

"**Mmm. But, now that your memories have returned...**"

"That's right — Thou art correct, young dragon. I owe Michael and Raphael a great debt. Nay, I owe _all_ the survivors of that horrid day a blood-debt. I must repay them ten times over for their genocides."

Leviathan nods slowly, but for once, he hesitates.

"**I, too, often feel the ache for vengeance, Samael. However, violence and bloodshed only further a perpetual cycle. Perhaps you should consider alternatives for settling your dispute.**"

Samael wipes the tears from his face. "Should I? Have thee any to suggest?"

"**No. I do not. Still, I wonder what Muriel and Cassiel would do, were they in your position.**"

Samael shakes his head. "I cannot pretend to know their thoughts. They were peaceful, loving beings. Naturally, that is what made them easy _targets_ for Michael's greed. They are not me, and I am not them."

The Archangel turns to look at Satan. He chuckles wryly. "Ironic, isn't it, Satan? Once, I believed that being devoured by thee was a curse worse than death. Now I know that the Creator's will still guides me to this day. 'Twas destiny that our fates intertwined."

Satan nods. "Yeah. You're right. Now that you have your memories back, I see a path I hadn't considered before."

"Oh? Please, elaborate."

Satan smiles. He clamps his hands together, stretches them out, and pops his knuckles. "Mmm. Well, you see, demons don't exactly like the angels. We've got a grudge against them, and it ain't going away anytime soon."

Samael nods. "Go on..."

"Well, your story pissed me off, Sammy-boy. Got me all fired up. I don't think I can go another day without tearing some angel assholes to shreds."

Leviathan sighs. "**A _crude_ metaphor, but expected from the likes of you.**"

Satan turns to the dragon. "Leviathan. You said you'd bring my girl back if I killed the Archangels, didn't you?"

Leviathan blinks. "**I said I would _consider_ it.**"

"Good enough for me. I didn't want to go all-out before, but I've changed my mind. There's no way I'm letting Raphael off easy after everything Sammy-boyo's said."

Belial nods. "I agree. The angels are pure scum. All they do is create and enslave other beings for their amusement. They're selfish people with god complexes. Even if they no longer have their memories, they're still guilty in my eyes for the atrocities they've committed."

With a pause, Belial blushes. "No offense, Samael."

"None taken," Samael replies. "Satan. Will thou assist me in my quest for vengeance? Will thou assist me in wiping out those who have wreaked havoc on the galaxy?"

Satan nods.

"I'll do you one better. I already have a plan."

"A plan?"

"Yep. But... I can't pull it off by myself. I'm gonna need all the help I can get."

Samael falls silent.

The Archangel of Change turns to look at Leviathan. A hundred emotions pass over his face.

"Leviathan. I was the one who gave thee thy name. However, thy mother also gave me a name. I never used to like it, but I've changed my mind, now."

"**Hm? She did?**"

"Aye. Due to how the Ophanim worked from the shadows, Jörmungandr called me by the name of _Hades_, king of the underworld."

Satan nods. "Hades. How very... un-angelic. Fitting."

Samael glances at his wings. "I hate my people. I always have. From this day forward, I will work tirelessly to bring them to their knees."

Leviathan curls his lips into a smile.

"**Welcome back, Hades, my beloved brother.**"

"Aye. I thank thee for saving me, Leviathan."
