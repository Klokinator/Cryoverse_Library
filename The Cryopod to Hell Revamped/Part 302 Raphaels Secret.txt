Part 302: Raphael's Secret

_Half a minute earlier._

Raphael, the Archangel of Wisdom, and Michael, the Archangel of Courage, assist their sister in combating the many Demon Emperors arrayed around them. While Uriel suffers the attacks of over half a dozen Emperors in unison, the two errant Archangel souls in her body manipulate two of the constructs she has summoned, in secret.

Raphael remotely controls the female construct. He does not give her specific commands, but instead gives vague orders to be as annoying as possible, slowing Fenrir down and causing her to lower her guard against the seemingly 'weak' and 'useless' construct.

Michael does the same, but with the male construct and Gorn.

"Remember, brother," Raphael says to Michael. "These demons are both sly and dangerous. Alone, our sister would lose against any three of them. Even Ose herself managed to defeat Uriel with less than half a dozen Dukes reinforcing her. If we hope to slay a few of these ancient monsters, we must use psychological warfare. Wait until I give the signal to take direct control of her Servants."

Michael nods. "In recent days, verily, I hath questioned thee many times, Raphael. But still, I have not lost faith in thy leadership. I know, in my heart, that all of thy seemingly wicked deeds must have been performed for some... some benefit to our people. Some selfless reasons. None hath suffered as much as thee."

"Good. That is good, Michael," Raphael says, smiling kindly. "No matter what, we are brothers. Archangels. _Equals._ We must ensure the monsters we created meet their ends at our hands. That is how we will do right by the galaxy we have harmed."

Michael nods. "Mmm."

They both fall silent, waiting for the right moment to strike.

At one point, five Emperors surround Uriel from all sides. Two orcs, two werewolves, and Yardrat all work together to launch what would, under normal circumstances, prove a deadly attack against the unprepared Uriel.

However, in the eyes of the Archangel of Wisdom, this change in the battle's dynamic proves nothing more than a mere trifle. He sees through the formation in less than a second.

[Sister. Swap with Michael.] He orders.

[Yes, brother.] Uriel responds.

Suddenly, Michael's soul-form vanishes and Uriel's appears. She stands at the alert, not a trace of worry on her face, while watching as Michael completely catches the demons off-guard. Michael's swordplay, peerless in its ability to cut and deflect, and perhaps faster than light itself, allows him to completely shatter the deadly formation surrounding his sister's body.

Less than five seconds later, Raphael speaks again.

[Michael. Swap with Uriel.]

Michael doesn't reply. He merely gives up control to his sister, trading places with his sister's soul.

These three Archangels, all errant souls, are simultaneously capable of controlling Barbatos's body at any moment and releasing their true powers. This feat, only possible due to more than a hundred millennia of Uriel reshaping Barbatos's essence to allow the flow of divine energy, gives them an unparalleled amount of flexibility in how their souls can flow through these pathways.

The moment Michael returns, Raphael gives another order.

"The time is right. Our enemies are distracted. We will take control of Uriel's Servants."

A faint smile crosses Michael's face.

"Verily, I shalt enjoy this."

Michael and Raphael both vanish, seizing full control of each of Uriel's constructs. Michael takes control of the masculine one, using its sword to decapitate Emperor Gorn in a single stroke. His swordplay, peerless and invisible to the untrained eye, ends the second-strongest Emperor's reign before he even knows what happened.

Raphael lacks such a swift killing move. But, in the end, his methods prove far more terrifying.

The Archangel of Wisdom becomes an illusion of himself. He reappears behind Emperor Fenrir's body and reaches out with his soul, grabbing onto hers before she can even detect his presence.

"Hellspawn." Raphael mutters. "Play with me a while."

In Fenrir's eyes, the world remains the same. She jumps away from the construct that grabbed her and shivers.

_That was close! What happened? How did this fake angel move fast enough to evade my senses?_

Fenrir strikes a defensive stance. She eyes the female construct with far more apprehension while ignoring the battle against Uriel.

"What are you?" Fenrir asks. "Speak, angel! You tried to trick me!"

Raphael chuckles quietly. "Hoho... you want to know this old man's identity?"

Before Fenrir's eyes, the space around Uriel's construct vibrates for a moment. Within a tenth of a second, the construct's form changes drastically, changing to reveal a figure feared by all Demon Emperors.

Fenrir sucks in a breath. "Raphael! No, that's impossible! We killed you! I saw your death with my own eyes!"

The Archangel nods somberly. "Aye, indeed my death came at the hands of Satan the Devil. 'Twas my great fortune that my soul ended up sealed within one of Camael's rings. And now, it has afforded this ancient one a rare opportunity to return from beyond the grave. Hoho!"

Fenrir's pupils dilate and contract several times in a fraction of a second. The terror of unexpectedly witnessing an additional Archangel throws her thoughts into chaos, making her realize all sorts of horrifying possibilities.

"No wonder Uriel came here alone! She wasn't by herself after all, she must have had..."

Fenrir pauses. She whips her head around to look at the other demons, only to realize something has gone horribly wrong. The night-time battlefield now appears completely empty, with no sounds of fighting anywhere in the vicinity.

A sinking feeling expands in the Emperor of the Night's stomach. She turns to look at Raphael again, only to see the old man standing calmly, smiling at her without a care in the world.

"Verily, thy thoughts are that of a greedy, selfish being," Raphael says, his words slow and measured. "Thy plans rest on the Hell of Blood's rise, its overtaking of the other Hells, and thy eventual climb to the position of Second Emperor. But ahh, what if thy plans were to fall apart here? Imagine the shame thou shalt feel when all thy machinations fall to dust? Such a tragic loss, no doubt."

"What did you do to everyone?" Fenrir asks, ignoring Raphael's taunts. "How did you hide them from me? No... did you put an illusion spell on _me?_"

Raphael's smile widens a little further. "This old angel is quite weak, and he is hard of hearing. Coulds't thou be experiencing some troubles, young one? Do feel free to speak up in the time thou have left. Thy existence will not be long for this world, after all."

"Don't screw with me!" Fenrir roars. "I'll rip you to pieces, Archangel!"

She charges at Raphael, conjuring all of her demonic power into a vicious soul attack. The moment she draws within range, Fenrir splits herself into six soul-forms, all of them traveling away from her original position to encircle the Archangel. Leathery, bat-like wings sprout from her back. She attacks the Archangel from all directions, giving him no room to escape.

An instant later, Fenrir crushes Raphael's body, splattering the old man into a bloody mess of guts and sinew.

However, Fenrir does not smile or gloat. Instead, her heart turns cold.

"That was too easy! Show yourself, Raphael! Don't try to fool me! I know you're still alive!"

The world around her, the meeting-place of the Hidden Emperors, becomes noticeably darker and more shrouded in fog. Raphael reappears, facing Fenrir's six selves without a hint of fear.

"Hoho! This old one simply wanted to play a little prank on thee! Hee-hee! Was it quite funny? Dids't thou experience the joy of a prank played well?"

"If you're going to fight me, then fight me!" Fenrir barks. "Stop wasting my time, wretched fossil! You belong in the dirt, along with the rest of your kind!"

Raphael's playful smile fades away. He eyes the demoness with an expression that rapidly grows darker and more malevolent.

"Yes. In the dirt, this old one belongs. Thou art right, hellspawn. Sadly, he must return from the abyss itself to avenge those he has failed. And so, here he stands, ready to personally wreak havoc on those who ended his happiness."

Raphael grabs the edges of his clothing. He slowly disrobes, revealing countless horrific scars on his chest, mottled patches of skin, and burn marks that no passage of time will ever heal. The skinny and frail-looking Archangel wears only a light set of pants to cover his dignity, but allows the Emperor of the Night to see his beaten and battered body.

"I have killed countless entities far mightier than thee, hellspawn. Worry not about thy so-called friends. Thy comrades will perish, as shalt thou, here and now. All thy ambitions will crumble before this ancient warrior."

"I won't die here!" Fenrir screeches. "But I **will** bury you where you stand!"

Fenrir and Raphael engage in a vicious battle to the death. Raphael's body, though seemingly weak, reveals a hidden wellspring of power as he regenerates from even the most violent and horrific deaths at Fenrir's claws.

For several hours, the two of them strike grievous blows against one another. Slowly, Fenrir's sanity begins to crack. She grows more and more desperate as the Archangel of Wisdom cunningly outwits her, dealing glancing blows that continue to slow her down more and more.

"How can you be so durable?!" Fenrir shrieks. "Just die already! DIE!!"

The world around her, having long-since faded into an infinite, blackened abyss, no longer reveals even a hint of the battlefield she once remembered. The fight against Uriel, once the most pressing thought on Fenrir's mind, becomes like an ancient memory.

A fist strikes Fenrir's face. She goes flying and crashes against the ground, pain blinding her vision and making it hard for her to see. At that moment, Raphael appears before her and grabs the top of her head.

"This is the part where I seize thy soul," Raphael says, smiling coyly.

The Archangel reaches out of himself and grabs onto Fenrir's soul, intending to rip it away from her body.

However, in that split-second, Fenrir's eyes turn as black as death itself.

"Taste your nightmare!" Fenrir screams, as she fires one last, desperate soul-attack at the Archangel holding on to her.

Raphael, unable to release the Emperor of the Night, shudders violently as this beam of darkness attacks his very core.

Suddenly, the pitch-black world around them changes to its total opposite; a stunningly bright realm of pure light. Fenrir's eyes burn from the radiance of a holy energy so pure it makes the Archangel of Wisdom's mana feel like puddles of mud splashed alongside the road.

In Fenrir's vision, a faint figure appears, a mere apparition, a long-lost shadow of power that none in the galaxy have ever observed, or still remember.

The Emperor of the Night trembles with fear.

"Who... who is that? Who's there?!"

Raphael convulses, while his hand remains stubbornly locked onto Fenrir's head.

The distant apparition, so powerful and pure that it fills Fenrir with a nameless terror, speaks softly to someone unseen.

"Why must you rebel? Have I not given you life? Have I not treated you with dignity and respect?"

The speaker receives no answer. Instead, a violent pain transmits through its consciousness directly to Fenrir's brain, making her shriek in pain.

"Eeeeyaaah!"

The Emperor of the Night watches in horror as the brilliant radiance surrounding her grows noticeably duller. The mighty presence fades away, and the blackness of the void once again swallows the brilliant light, leaving only the shuddering and suffering Raphael behind, along with herself.

Raphael finally recovers from her last-ditch attack. He opens his eyes and glares at her with a look of pure hatred.

"That vision was not thine to see."

Fenrir shudders. "You... you killed him! You murdered..."

Raphael gnashes his teeth. "That was my greatest secret, the one thing which should never be spoken of. For thy putrid eyes to observe it, thou shalt die a thousand deaths. I will not even consume thy wretched soul, but purify it to nothingness, forever."

Tears bubble up in Fenrir's eyes. "You killed him... you killed him... but he loved you! He loved you like his son!"

"**PERISH!**" Raphael howls, as he squeezes his hands, compresses her soul, and blasts it with the full might of his Archangel power.

"Aaaah!!"

Fenrir releases one last cry of pain before the light fades from her eyes. Her 'body,' the representation of her soul, scatters to ash in Raphael's palm. He remains standing while she crumbles to dust, staring hatefully at the fallen demoness.

"T'was no mistake..." Raphael murmurs. "I did what I had to. I made the choice to empower myself. T'was not wrong. It wasn't!"

The Archangel of Wisdom reflects for a moment on that moment in time, that ancient choice he made, one which changed the flow of power in the cosmos forever.

"...Only Solomon knows." Raphael whispers. "But I cannot kill him. I can only pray he keeps the secret safe, forever. 'Tis my one last desire."

As Raphael's soul-battle with Fenrir ends, his perception of time slows down, reverting to its normal state. The Illusory Realm he constructed, one which has allowed him to defeat even his mightiest foes, including Satan himself, grants Raphael yet another victory among the trillions of others.

Raphael returns to reality, watching as Fenrir's lifeless husk falls to the floor before him.

In the timeline of Uriel's battle, only a single second passed between the moment of Raphael touching Fenrir's soul and the fading of the light in her eyes. But to the Archangel and his victim, more than half a day had passed.

Raphael glances in Michael's direction, noting the fallen figure of Emperor Gorn.

[Good work, little brother,] Raphael says. [Thou hath performed admirably.]

He rears back his fist to destroy Fenrir's body, only to groan as he realizes it has already disappeared into the floor, along with Gorn's corpse.

For a brief instant, Raphael reflects on the secret Fenrir glimpsed, the one truth he never wishes anyone to know.

_As much as her soul would have empowered me, I cannot allow any others to remain in the mortal plane who know the truth._ Raphael thinks. _That is why... even Cassiel and Muriel..._

A flash of regret appears on Raphael's face.

_Perhaps t'was a mistake. Perhaps I should never have done what I did. But the dead can never return. That is the cruelty of this universe. That is the cruelty and unfairness we must all endure._

Raphael and Michael both fly back to Uriel's side, while the gazes of all the demons fall upon them.

_This old man cannot fix all of his mistakes,_ Raphael thinks. _But at the very least, he can purge these wretched sinners from existence!_
