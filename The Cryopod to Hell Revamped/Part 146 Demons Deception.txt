Uriel and Berith transform into living tornados. The Archangel of Protection spins Gae Bolg around her body three times faster than Sophia did her staff. The weapon slashes and stabs Berith dozens of times per second.

_Swish-slash swish-slash swish-slash!_

_Ting, ting, whiff!_

Berith engulfs her arm in hardened shadows. Even as Uriel presses her advantage, Berith digs her feet into the ground and deflects hundreds of spear attacks in the blink of an eye. Her hands move like lightning, making Uriel grin gleefully.

"Ohh! It seems you've gotten a little stronger since the last time we fought! I can almost tell the difference!"

"Don't mock me, you wretched chicken!" Berith screeches. She jerks her head to the side, narrowly avoiding Gae Bolg as the blood-red spear threatens to tear off a chunk of her face. Berith blinks her eyes, and a shadow-lance she prepared during a momentary lull in the battle lunges toward Uriel's back.

The Archangel instantly pivots around and backhands the shadow-attack, shattering it into a foggy mist. She resumes her unstoppable onslaught an instant later.

"Haha! Nice try! It's no wonder everyone refers to thee as the Emperor of Backstabbing! Thou art a foul rogue!"

Uriel switches to a one-handed style and begins focusing on rapid jabs and stabs. She attacks Berith from the front and right as she darts around, using her superior speed to flank and outmaneuver the demoness. With her free hand, Uriel conjures the elemental power of fire and enchants the tip of her spear, raising it to several hundred degrees.

Berith glares at Uriel as the superheated weapon nearly decapitates her several times within five seconds. "Getting serious now, are you? What's the matter? Have to bring out more power than usual against little old me?!"

"Not even!" Uriel cackles. "I only wish to end this farce quickly! There are so many bloodskins I'm _dying_ to introduce to my spears!"

"What an excellent choice of words!" Berith quips, as she ducks another jab. "Your life will surely come to an end today, along with those of your precious brothers and sisters!"

Uriel's smile vanishes. "Thou hath quite the deathwish, threatening my sisters like that, little bloodskin. Let's end this. Now."

Suddenly, both light constructs appear at Berith's left and right flank. The female summons a bow and arrow made of light, while the male creates a sword and shield.

Uriel brings forth her golden spear, Gungnir. She swishes it around in her left hand, then swaps it with Gae Bolg to her right.

"I think I'll try wielding my beloved children like this for now, hehe..."

A drop of sweat drips down Berith's face as she instantly evaluates the threats around her.

_It's now or never._

The instant Uriel starts to move, Berith claps her hands together. Gae Bolg swishes through the Emperor's neck, killing her instantly. Her body starts to fall backward, only to fizzle into ash.

Uriel's eyes widen in alarm. "What? That wasn't the real Berith?"

"You missed, little chicken!"

Berith's shadow clone disperses into the wind, while Berith's real body leaps from Uriel's shadow. She tackles the Archangel from behind, but before she can plunge her summoned shadow-dagger into Uriel's heart, the female light construct opens her head.

_KSSEEW!_

A blast of light magic fires from its face and pounds Berith's stomach, tearing her from Uriel's back. Berith splashes into the floodwaters and quickly melts into the shadow of a rock.

"Tch. Nice save, Unit Two!" Uriel says. "Unit One, guard my flank!"

The male construct nods. It jumps behind Uriel and presses its wings against hers.

From the depths of the foot-deep muddy floodwaters, two dozen shadow lances burst out, impaling Unit Two before she can react. Unit One deflects several lances with its shield and cuts down another with its sword, but a single spear slips past its defenses and impales the construct's stomach. It explodes into particles of light, leaving Uriel exposed.

The Archangel barely notices. She spends a few seconds batting away the deadly shadows as they fly toward her from every angle like the maw of a giant Venus Fly Trap. Berith dials up the intensity, but only manages to nick Uriel's arms once or twice.

Uriel leaps into the sky and bats away three more shadow-lances. "Gah! Come out and face me, impish bloodskin! Only a coward attacks from the shadows!"

Suddenly, a blob of blackness fires from behind Uriel's two levitating orbs. The softball-sized shadow, resembling a blob of ink, flies up at her with aimed precision.

"Shall thou never concede, bloodskin?!" Uriel says, lilting her voice. She smirks and spins her spear around, effortlessly slicing the shadow-ball apart before it can reach her.

However, her attack proves to be a huge mistake. As if popping a balloon, her spears burst the ball, detonating it like a grenade. Uriel gasps as a hundred tendrils of darkness spread out like a fishing net and wrap around her from every angle.

"By the Creator! What manner of- gah!"

The shadow-tendrils envelop her like an octopus grabbing a crab. Uriel's face turns blue as a staggering amount of force threatens to break her arms and ribs.

"Hahaha! I knew you'd fall for my trap!" Berith cackles, as she jumps out of the water, emerging from a shadow beneath the mud. "Your arrogance was your undoing, Uriel!"

Berith motions toward the Archangel of Protection, sending a dozen shadow spears from the water next to her toward the Archangel's head.

"Die!"

_Whiff! SHIK!_

Something flies down from the sky and rips through Berith's outstretched palm. Her hand tears from her body and slams into the floodwaters with a massive _splash!_ A split-second later, her shadow-spears fall apart.

"Gaaaugh! Spawn of a...!!"

Berith jumps back just in time to avoid two elemental arrows, as Hyacinth and Penelope arrive on the scene, along with Sophia and Catherine. All four Valkyries appear refreshed and energized, having had their wounds healed somewhere safe.

"Curses! CURSES!!" Berith howls in pain. She clutches the stump where her hand was only a moment earlier and turns to scream in rage at the newcomers. "You damned Valkyries! I buried two of you, and yet you still stand?! Even Valac's undead wouldn't be so tenacious!"

Hyacinth fires several arrows at Uriel with pinpoint precision. Each one tears through the spindly black limbs of Berith's shadow-ball. Once enough of the tendrils snap, Uriel breaks free of the others.

Penelope, Catherine, and Sophia land beside their sister and pull her to her feet. Hyacinth stays back and readies her bow. The four Valkyries and lone Archangel quickly advance toward the Emperor of Backstabbing, but they keep a cautious distance as well.

"What now, little demon wench?" Uriel asks, an evil glint in her eye. "It's five on one, and it appears thou dost require a hand!"

Berith's breathing catches as she glances at the stump where her right hand used to be. "D-don't worry about me. I'll be fine. We have a healer too, one whose abilities surpass any of you!"

"That won't matter if thou fail make it back to him alive!" Uriel cackles. She summons her two light constructs behind Berith, cutting off the Demon Emperor's route of escape. "With only one hand, thy incantations will be significantly impaired. Any last words?"

_FLASH!_

A spark of light erupts next to Berith. 

Orias, Lord of the Moon, reappears once again, but this time, his skin appears shiny and white. The midday sun gleams off his body, giving him the appearance of a living disco ball anywhere his skin exposes itself.

"Stick to the plan, Berith. You're not here to settle a score. I'll hold them off while you escape."

Berith whips her head to the left and glares at Orias. "Don't you dare order me around, little _Lord_! I'll defeat these five if it's the last thing I do!"

Orias's eyes flick toward Hyacinth, as she starts to draw her bow back. "You'll have an opportunity in the second wave. Stick to the plan. Besides, if they don't kill you, Satan will."

That gets Berith's attention. She tenses up at the mention of Satan and scowls angrily at Uriel. "Tch. Don't think I've finished with you just because of this interruption, Uriel. I'll have your head for breakfast tomorrow!"

With a flick of her wrist, Berith sucks into a shadow at her feet and vanishes. Uriel starts to grab for her, but the Archangel's movements prove too slow to close the gap.

"Gah! Fine, if the coward wants to run like a beaten dog, so be it! Sisters, tear this Lord to pieces! He won't be any trouble at all!"

Orias peeps out from beneath his hood, revealing that even his eyes have transformed into diamonds. 

"We will see."

Across the battlefield, Belial slips and slinks between various combatants. Thousands of demons and angels crash against each other, with the former losing their heads to weapons, while the latter perish to claws and teeth. One female angel screams in panic as three ravenous grunts grab her by the arms and yank her to the ground. Her screams fall silent as a dozen more leap atop her and begin ripping her to shreds.

Belial pauses to watch. Her stomach churns at the sight of the barbarism on display. She considers trying to help, but already, she's too late. The woman has died.

Suddenly, three of the grunts begin to scream. They leap off the deceased angel and claw at their faces. Chunks of her flesh and muscle hang from their mouths. Their bodies seize up as the holy energy trapped in her body flows into them.

"Gaa!! Ahhh!! Burns! It fucking burns!!"

Two of the grunts fall backward and begin violently spasming, but the third one takes a few extra seconds to join them.

Artorias bursts out of the ground, sensing their distress. "Gah! You idiots! Don't eat any angels! You know the rule!"

The other grunts stare at him guiltily, and quickly run away, but the damage has already been done. The first three demons cease their screaming and fall silent as the life vanishes from their body.

Belial stares at the dead grunts.

_I doubt I could have healed them. Even if I could... I wouldn't want to. What a brutal way to kill their enemy._

Artorias and Belial catch each other's eyes for a moment. He looks away, unable to meet her gaze. "Hurry up, Belial. Satan's plan hinges on you."

The Emperor of Passion nods. "Worry about yourself. There's no way I'll fail Satan when he needs me most."

Artorias returns the nod. A moment later, he dives underground, leaving the mangled angel corpse and her three attackers to cook under the sun.

Belial flicks her gaze around. Chester, leader of the Outer Defense Corps, cuts down three demon grunts at once, demonstrating his prowess at combat. When another attacks him from behind, Chester summons holy magic around his fist, forms an aetherial golden gauntlet, and spins around to deliver a brutal blow to his ambusher's face. The demon's head explodes, and he goes flying backward, unable to withstand Chester's might.

A hint of admiration creeps into Belial's eyes, but she quickly stifles it and slinks over to the dead angel female from before. The woman's face, though bloody, is still intact. Unfortunately, the same can't be said for everything below the chin. Her clothes are shredded, throat ripped out, and there isn't much left in her chest and waist areas either.

Belial flinches at the horrific brutality on display, but quickly steadies her mind. With a motion of her hands, the succubus heals the angel woman, repairing her body to perfection. Sadly, with her soul gone, not even Belial can revive her.

"You'll do," Belial whispers. "Sorry, but I'll be borrowing your face for a bit."

Two seconds later, Belial begins to sprout wings. Her clothing shifts and melts around her body, transforming into a set of fairly generic-looking robes like many of the female angels wear, minus the metal pauldrons and armor. Her skin shifts from red to white, and her eyes turn blue as well. Within seconds, she becomes a perfect facsimile of the dead woman.

The chaotic battle affords her a few seconds of transformation time unnoticed, but once she finishes, the demons around her quickly spot the 'angel' in their midst.

"Oi, there's a pretty one! She's mine!" 

"Screw you! I saw her first!"

Two grunts rush toward Belial, their teeth bared. They're too hungry for angel flesh to realize the living and dead angels before them look identical.

Belial sighs. She flicks her gaze toward them, and a moment later, her fist whips out like a freakish octopus tentacle.

_Wham! Wham!_

Belial ass-blasts the demons and sends them flying into their comrades. The two grunts flop to the ground, unconscious, along with a couple of badly broken jaws.

"Sick, disgusting animals," Belial seethes. She jumps to her feet, adjusts her wings, and flaps hard.

Having never copied an angel's body, she manages to lift into the air, but falls to the ground a moment later. Her inexperience at flying proves to be her undoing, as she tumbles to the bloodied plain below.

"Shit!"

Belial nearly crashes into the dirt but manages to catch herself thanks to her incredible strength and agility. The Emperor of Passion flicks her eyes around and realizes several demons have spotted her flying over their heads.

"Damn! Get that sexy one over there!"

"Oh, I can't wait to wrap my tongue around those thighs!"

Belial gnashes her teeth as, this time, two dozen grunts, and even a couple Lords come barreling toward her.

"Fucking hell. You want a piece of me? Bring it."

Belial stretches her arms out and grabs the sword of a nearby dead angel, along with his shield. It doesn't appear she needs them, since her fists can annihilate an army, but an instant later, I realize why she wants them.

After grabbing the shield, she flings her arm toward the nearest attacker.

_Whang!_

The white-and-gold shield smashes against his face and shoves him back, transforming him into a living mace. Belial lifts the demon into the air with her stretchy arms, and slams him down on two more grunts. Then, she turns her sword sideways and swings the flat of it around like a frying pan.

_Whap whap whap!_

Over and over, she slaps the demons targeting her with ferocious strength, sending them hurtling to the left and right.

One of the Lords pauses. I recognize him as Barbatos, the Lord of Iron. "Uh, oh! Shit! B-Belial? Is that you?!"

She snarls at him. "I'm undercover, you fucking moron!"

Barbatos's face turns white as snow. After realizing he nearly got his ass beat by an Emperor, the Lord meekly takes a few steps back and holds up his palms. "Ehh, haha... m-my bad. I'm gonna just... go the other direction. Nyehehe..."

Without another word, the bumbling idiot turns and runs away, leaving Belial by herself.

Other demons glance toward her, but seeing the pile of unconscious demons nearby, they think twice and continue toward the main angel attack party.

"Shit. My cover might be blown," Belial mutters. "I'll need to move fast."

Belial's eyes dart around, and her perception of the world slows somewhat. She picks out a dozen paths through the demons nearby and mentally plots a route to the entrance of Mount Sinai. There, two Titan women stand, giant earthen maces in hand, waiting for any demons to come close.

_Getting past those two won't be a problem,_ Belial thinks to herself. _They won't be looking for an angel woman._

No, instead, her eyes fixate on Uriel and the Valkyries, as the five of them furiously battle a single Baron.

Orias, Lord of the Moon, snaps his fingers, summoning fireballs from his left hand. The sudden barrage of heat drives Penelope and Sophia back as he shreds the nearby landscape. Catherine rushes toward him, but when she swings her sword down and tries to lop off his head, it deflects off the diamond-skin protecting his body and slides to the ground, not harming him in the slightest.

"How many powers does this demon have?!" Uriel shrieks angrily. "He's only a Lord! How can he be this strong?!"

"Strength is in the eye of the beholder," Orias mutters, as he swings his fist around and smashes it against Catherine's chestplate. He knocks the Valkyrie backward, but she manages to stop herself in midair and land gracefully, thanks to her wings. 

"Well, then I'd better slaughter thee before thou ascendeth any further!" Penelope yells. She runs toward Orias and swings her greatmace at him, but he backflips and dodges at the last second.

Belial bites her lip. _Orias can't win against an Archangel and the Valkyries. I should help him. No! I can't risk it! I need to get inside Heaven. Without me, everything we're doing now will fail._

Shaking her head, Belial quickly darts through the demons nearby, using her astounding speed to transform into a blur they can't follow with their eyes.

Chester, fighting at the outer ring of the angel's defensive perimeter, stabs his sword through the head of a Scyther and ducks a fireball aimed at his head. He quickly aims two fingers at the fire-type demon attacking him and lets two bolts of holy energy fly. They impact the demon and knock him off his feet, but don't deal enough damage to kill him.

Sweat drenches Chester's face. "By the Creator! Where are our reinforcements?! Where are the Archangels?!"

Belial appears at his side, startling him. She quickly bats aside a demon with her shield and answers his question. "Brother! Uriel told me we need to sound the retreat! The other Archangels aren't coming! We're to hold off the demons inside the caverns, with help from the narrow corridors!"

Chester blinks in surprise. "What! Melody, I've heard nothing of this! Why would Uriel sound the retreat? We can still win!"

A look of suspicion appears in Chester's eyes, but at that moment, three more demons pounce at him. Not one to fall for surprise attacks, Chester swiftly spins around and decapitates the first two, then slams the third one into the ground with his fist of holy energy. The last one's head pops like a grapefruit, spraying the ground with demon entrails.

Belial, too, quickly ducks and dodges a scyther hellbent on her death. She knocks his bladed arms aside and smacks him across the face with the flat of her sword. He flies into the flood of other demons and sprawls on the ground.

By the time Belial returns her attention to Chester, his look of suspicion has increased. "Thou hath certainly improved with the blade, Melody! I didn't know thy skill had reached such a level!"

Belial's heart freezes.

_Curses! He knows!_

She quickly blurts out the first thing on her mind. "Nothing like practical experience to bring out the best in me! Are we going to stay here and wait, or will we perish when the Demon Emperors show up?!"

Chester's suspicions deepen further. "Impossible. Thou hath Melody's face and voice, but thy strength and speech reveal thy true nature. Who art thou, bedeviled imposter?!"

Panic sets in. Belial's eyes flick to the angels behind Chester, all of whom are locked in life and death struggles with the demons. However, any moment, there's certain to be a lull in combat, and when they learn of Belial's existence from Chester...

Chester glances toward the demon Belial took down moments before and watches as he groggily rises to his feet. The gears turn in Chester's head as he realizes 'Melody' didn't even kill the little bastard. Not only is she more adept at battle than he remembers, but she also managed to knock a demon grunt out with a single attack.

Belial's jaw sets. When another demon lunges toward Chester, his concentration momentarily breaks as he guts the bloodskin and throws it to the ground.

_This man knows,_ Belial thinks to herself. _If he tells the others, they'll find out I'm a shapeshifter. The whole plan will fall apart. I can't let that happen._

Chester turns to face 'Melody.' As he opens his mouth to speak, Belial's fist flies out, purely on instinct.

_WHUMP!_

She side-swipes Chester with the force of a bulldozer and flings him into the mass of nearby demons, opposite the other angels. In the blink of an eye, Belial retracts her arm and shifts her facial features. Chester never even gets to scream as the demons pounce on him and tear him limb from limb.

Two seconds later, Belial's face finishes morphing into a perfect facsimile of Chester's. Her body shifts and bends, and she assumes his physique as well, though she's unable to duplicate his armor.

_Damn it. Damnit!!_

Tears boil in Belial's eyes, and nausea threatens to overtake her.

_I killed that man! I fucking killed him! I can't believe myself. I shouldn't have done that! There must have been another way... but what, and how?!_

The other Cherubs nearby finish dispatching their enemies. When they turn to face Chester, they spot Belial instead. Despite not wearing the same armor or wielding the same sword, the others have too much adrenaline pumping in their veins to pay attention to details. "Brother! Where are the reinforcements? What should we do?!"

Belial's chest freezes. After a moment, she forces herself to calm down. In a perfect facsimile of Chester's voice, she raises her sword and yells. "We must retreat! Uriel's orders are absolute!"

Two female Cherubs nearby merely nod. They shiver and shake as battle fatigue dulls their minds. "R-right! To where must we fall back?"

"T-to the cave entrance!" 'Chester' yells. "We'll hold them off in the caves! Go! Go!"

Belial catches the glances of two male Cherubs, who are also too preoccupied with fighting demons to notice their commander isn't quite the same as before. They nod. "As thou commandeth, brother!"

A shiver goes down Belial's back as the angels leap into the air and begin calling out in unison to those nearby. 

_Fuck. Fuck. Fuck. I killed that angel. I killed Chester! It doesn't matter if other demons tore him apart... it's my fault he died! Gahhh! I promised myself I'd never do something like that! What's wrong with me?!_

Belial starts to flap her wings, but thinks better of it, and instead lowers her body to the ground and darts across the landscape, covering an astonishing distance as she calls out to other angels to retreat.

Still, despite her anxiety, Belial forces her stomach to settle. As she runs, her face and body shift into that of another dead female angel she passes.

_Chester's identity won't last me long. This one will have to do._

---

Uriel's spears smash Orias back and forth like two cave bear paws battering a helpless field hare. Over and over, Uriel stabs and strikes the diamond-skinned Lord, knocking him around as if he were inside a pinball machine. She flings him into the air, flies above him, and kicks down, smashing him into the muddy waters below.

"Thou art a durable little gnat!" Uriel yells. "But I'll break through, eventually! And when I do, thy heartbeat shall cease in an instant!"

Orias leaps to his feet, mostly unharmed. He quickly shakes a bit of disorientation off and jumps back, narrowly evading Catherine's sword as she slashes at him.

_Thirty-five seconds remaining,_ Orias thinks to himself. _I only have a few more diamonds left. Once they crumble, I won't last long against the Valkyries. It's fortunate these five haven't figured out my powers, yet, but my luck won't last forever. That archer woman has excellent eyesight._

He flicks a gaze toward Hyacinth, who unleashes a volley of wind-arrows at him. Each one explodes against his chest and limbs like a heavy-caliber rifle. Her arrows hit him with enough force to knock him off his feet, but not even her strongest attacks deal any damage to the heavily armored Lord.

_Twenty seconds,_ Orias thinks. _Come on, Berith! Belial! Do your damn jobs so I can get out of here!_

Orias spares a glance at the five gems held in his palm. Three diamonds rest in his grasp, along with a ruby and a pitch-black onyx.

He quickly balls that hand into a fist and grimaces as he spots Penelope to his right. She heaves her greatmace into the air, and a sickening sense of dread washes over Orias.

_This one's gonna hurt._

_Swish- BOOOOM!_

Penelope's mace pounds the diamond-studded demon hard enough to drive him ten feet underground. Under normal circumstances, any other Lord would have become a pool of blood and pulverized bone after such an attack, but somehow Orias remains in one piece.

Still, the Lord of the Moon lays flat on his back. His eyes spin and tumble as he tries to make sense of his orientation. After a moment, he blinks and stares into the skies above, where hundreds of angels fly overhead, firing volleys of arrows at the demon below. On every side of Orias, the ground rises a few feet, giving him the impression that he's landed in something akin to a coffin.

"He's still alive?!" Uriel asks incredulously. "Even Gabriel would have felt that attack!"

Groggily, Orias gasps as the effects of his diamond-skin begin to wear off. His vision, partially obscured because of the gems protecting his eyes, return to normal as they melt away.

_I miscounted?! Damn! I need to activate the next one right now!_

He fumbles with the gems on his palm, only to realize with a sense of dread that he accidentally dropped them when Penelope struck.

Penelope raises her greatmace to her shoulder. The other two Valkyries, as well as Uriel, arrive at the top of the depression in the ground.

"His skin is different! I think thy attack broke his defenses!" Sophia cries out giddily. "Hit him again! Quickly!"

Orias's eyes widen.

_No! No! Those were all my diamonds! Where did they go? I need them!!_

The Valkyrie of Earth begins to raise her hammer, making ice creep into Orias's veins.

_Ahh, crap! This can't be where I die! I must survive! My gems need me!_

Instantly, his hand shoots up to pull back his hood. Several jewels of varying colors sit embedded in his face, from red to green to blue to yellow, too.

But, in his forehead, one particular gem rests. A beautiful, radiant, multicolored jewel unlike any of the others, and three times their size, as well.

Penelope starts to swing her greatmace down, but in the time it takes for her to clear the gap, Orias yanks the gem from his forehead, closes his palm around it, and snaps his fingers.

"Oh, Beauteous One, protect me!"

_Fwoosh!_

Time freezes.

The magic of the gem explodes in Orias's veins like Niagara Falls smashing against the Saharan desert. In a single second, Orias transforms from a Lord-level demon to a godlike deity.

Orias leaps out of the makeshift dirt coffin. His movements become like those of a practiced martial artist.

_Wham! Wham! Wham!_

Orias palm-strikes the three nearest Valkyries flat in the chest. They helplessly fly away, despite the dilated sense of time Orias is experiencing, and a moment later, he rears back his fist to aim it at Uriel.

**WHAM!!**

He punches the Archangel of Protection with all of his strength. She flies away so fast that her spears tear from her hands while remaining suspended in midair.

Even as Uriel and her three sisters go tumbling and twisting in four different directions, Orias's heart seemingly explodes in his chest.

"AAAAAAAAAAAUUUUUHHHHGHHH!!!"

The Lord of the Moon falls to the ground and lands on his knees. Veins burst in his eyes, flooding them with blood. Every muscle in his body writhes and twists as a godlike level of power threatens to tear him apart from the inside-out.

_Fuckfuckfuck!! My heart can't take it! Gotta leave, now!!_

Without aiming himself, Orias uses the last vestiges of his godly strength to plant one foot in the dirt and kick as hard as he can. He flies away from the angels right as time resumes to normal, landing somewhere amidst the horde of other demons.

_Whump, whump whump, wham!_

All three Valkyries come to a sudden stop, and Uriel lands face-down, ass-up in the mud, as stars dance in her eyes.

"Blurgh... h-huh...? What... where... w-what hit m-me?"

Uriel jumps to her feet thanks to her leftover adrenaline, but her legs buckle a moment later, and she lands on her hands and knees, breathing like she just ran an Olympic Marathon.

"Haah... haah..."

Uriel blinks several times as memories of what happened come rushing back.

"That... that little Lord... hit me? How?! I didn't even see him move!"

"Uriel!" Hyacinth calls out from above. She lands with a splash beside her sister and quickly wraps one arm around her sister's waist. "Get up! We have to leave. Chester called for a retreat!"

"...Leave? What? Why?"

Uriel tries to focus her vision, but her eyes continue to shake and tremble. She quickly splashes healing water on herself and mends her broken bones. 

"I don't know, but it seems the demons put up more of a resistance than we expected. We have to fall back."

"Nonsense!" Uriel yells, throwing Hyacinth's arm off her back. Uriel clambers to her feet and stares in disbelief at the retreating angels. Already, demons have begun taking territory their enemies gave up, as greedy looks wash over their faces. A few angels don't get the message in time and fall to the horde of blood-colored monsters overwhelming them.

"We were winning!" Uriel yells, rage entering her voice. "Since when did Chester become a coward?! When I get my hands on him..."

"There's no time for that, Uriel," Hyacinth says, a hard edge to her voice. "We have to get the others out of here. Demons will arrive within seconds."

"What about that rat we nearly killed?"

"Gone. I saw him... fly away. Or something. I couldn't follow his movements very well."

Uriel gnashes her teeth. "Fucking bastards. Damn. Damn! DAMN!! Alright, it's too late to change things. Let's retreat. I'll hold the demons off inside the caves."

Hyacinth nods. "A sound plan. Killing them all by thyself might even be possible."

"Damn right it is," Uriel says, as she balls her hands into fists. She aims her hands at Gungnir and Gae Bolg, causing the two spears to yank from the earth and fly toward her. When she catches them, Uriel turns to look at the other three Valkyries, who have only just managed to pull themselves together.

"Tch. When this battle finishes, I'll have to unleash my toughest training on all of them. They should have taken that little bastard's attack with ease."

Uriel and Hyacinth fly over to their sisters. After quickly healing their injuries, the five women take to the air and fly over the battlefield, dodging fireballs and rock-bullets from the demons below as they head for the entrance to Sinai.

"Ah!" Sophia says, alarm in her voice. "What about Astrea and Hestia?! They can't fit inside Sinai's entrance! If we leave them outside, they'll die!"

The two Titan women stand, clubs in hand, and usher angels inside the mountain. Both of them wear grim looks of determination.

"Tch. They wouldn't have had anything to worry about if Chester hadn't called for a retreat," Uriel says, a hint of bitterness in her voice. "I'll try to buy them time to escape, but there's no chance they will outrun the demons, let alone their hellhounds. I hope the Creator blesses them with a quick, painless death."

Sophia's face turns ashen, but she offers no complaint.

As the Valkyries and their sister arrive at the entrance, Uriel quickly begins ushering everyone inside the mountain. She stands alone, both spears in hand, and watches angrily as the angels give up land they should never have lost.

One female angel runs past Uriel, keeping her head ducked low. Uriel gives her only the slightest passing glance, but it won't be long before the Archangel wishes she'd paid more attention to the faces of those fleeing to the safety of Heaven.

The battle outside Sinai was only a precursor.

The real war begins now.
