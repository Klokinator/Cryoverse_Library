Part 300: First Hell's Return

The doors to the Hidden Emperor's meeting hall fly open, allowing 'Bael' to stride in first, followed by 'Ose.' As Bael enters, Emperor Gorn rises to his feet in surprise.

"Emperor Ose! Duke Bael!"

Ose, now inside of Bael's body, reveals a self-confident smirk. "Hello. Did anyone miss me?"

Some of the guests immediately detect a slight difference in the cadence of 'Bael's' voice. Given the clear and obvious signs of necromancy on the First Duke's body, they momentarily chalk up the discrepancies to his potentially altered vocal cords. After all, not all parts of an undead person's body retain their previous range of motion.

That is, until 'Bael' continues walking forward without announcing Ose's presence behind him.

"Oh, devils. Look at all of the prim and proper bedfellows sitting around, discussing how they might carve up the First Hell and divide the pie amongst themselves. I don't have to hear a word you've spoken to know exactly what sorts of things you lot might discuss. You're more predictable than the idiot whose body I've come to infest."

Ose's words instantly startle everyone in the room, including Benjamin Brown himself. The human stares with wide-eyed fascination at the figure of the First Duke, disbelief in his eyes.

"...Bael? No. You're not Bael. That manner of speaking. Could you be...?"

"Ose. Yes, you catch on fast." Ose says without missing a beat. "Mephisto performed a remarkable experiment and placed the soul of the smartest demon inside the most powerful body in all the Seven Hells. Truly, his genius knows no limits."

Gorn's eyes shift from Ose to her former body standing behind her, a frumpy frown on its face.

"Err... then... the soul animated inside Emperor Ose's body must be... Bael's?"

Bael raises his hand sheepishly and waves. "Uh. Sup, guys. I'm a girl now. Life comes at ya fast."

After the two of them have introduced themselves, another pair of figures walk into the room from outside. This time, several seated Emperors stand up in alarm as they recognize the distinctive mana signature of an Emperor-level monster, as well as Mephisto.

"That crazy bastard!" Emperor Fae shouts, a huge smile stretching across her face. "Hahaha! Mephisto did it! He reanimated King Kar to be his undying servant! This meeting is about to get mighty interesting, now! Bahaha!"

However, Kar also reveals a distinct change in his persona when he speaks.

"Wrong. I'm not Kar. I'm Zamiel. I just have to live inside this stupid lizard's skin cuz' the boss lost my original body."

Emperor Gorn, unlike many of his fellow demons, remains seated. He gazes at the newcomers with a mixture of fascination and worry. He gazes past the three mightiest newcomers to the comparatively diminutive Duke trailing behind them.

_Hmm? Duke Mephisto has not advanced himself to the rank of Emperor. I expected he would do so before coming here, as a show of authority. Why would he not evolve himself before we could state our disapproval? Perhaps he has some other plans... such as extending an olive branch of goodwill for us to hang from. No doubt, Mephisto intends to see which of us will speak out against him first before he dramatically evolves right before us. He's craftier than I thought._

Hardly have those thoughts passed through Gorn's mind before Yardrat vocalizes almost exactly the same thought.

"Duke of Mist! What a pleasant surprise. I see that you have succeeded in solidifying your base of power. You aren't even an Emperor yet, but you already personally wield the might of two Emperors and a Duke. Truly, your machinations startle this humble Emperor's soul."

Everyone waits for Mephisto to raise his voice and boast about his 'impressive accomplishments,' but he remains silent, bowing his head slightly, as if ashamed. Before anyone can reconcile this odd behavior, Ose suddenly bursts into laughter.

"Hahaha! Oh, yes. Mephisto's 'loyal servant,' you've got me! I am nothing more than this High Necromancer's weak-willed slave. Hahaha! **WRONG!** You imbeciles! My soul was much too powerful for that dull-witted buffoon to control, and so I have retained control of my mental faculties! Not only myself, but Bael, too! Mephisto does not control either of us in the slightest!"

Ose violently slaps her chest, while her expression doesn't shift even in the slightest from the impact. A powerful shockwave radiates outward, nudging all the seated guests in the room a little away from her and making them realize the true horror of the mightiest and smartest demons fused into one body can accomplish.

Emperor Fenrir's face pales visibly. "What? Mephisto does not control you? But how can that be? Necromancers should always..."

She pauses momentarily, while a series of thoughts run through her head. Then, she turns her head toward the Duke of Mist.

"You fool! You should have made yourself an Emperor before attempting to take over Ose and Bael's souls! Your soul was too weak to control theirs!"

Emperor Fenrir's grand ambitions, her plans to set up the pawn known as Mephisto on the seat of the Second Emperor while elevating herself to the position of Third instantly crumbles before her eyes. With Mephisto losing what should have been one of the mightiest personal power bases in the Seven Hells, her previous performance of a calm, collected, and well-in-charge Emperor reveals dozens of cracks to those observing her.

"Haha. How unfortunate." Gorn chuckles, as a broad smile crosses his face. "Sometimes, even the best laid plans can fall apart due to a subordinate's incompetence. Isn't that right, Fenrir?"

Some of the slower-witted guests finally realize, thanks to Fenrir and Gorn's words, just what her true plans were. They remain silent, listening to the exchange with looks of surprises on their face.

Ose rolls her eyes. "You always think you're smarter than you actually are, Fenrir. Don't even try to compare yourself to me."

She strides forward and arrives at the head of the table. Then, she turns her head toward Benjamin Brown.

"Move."

The human leader pauses for only a split-second before nodding his head, standing up, and gesturing to his daughter.

"Come, Lily. We must make room for Ose and Bael."

Despite his original intention to abdicate his seat to Mephisto when the Duke of Mist arrived, Benjamin immediately realizes that the necromancer has lost any chance of controlling the First Hell. Once again, his position has reverted to that of a mid-weight general, rather than a full-on commanding officer.

Lily nods, then stands up. "Yes, daddy."

After they vacate their chairs, Benjamin leads Lily around the table to where the Hells of Isolation and Lust sit. He takes a seat beside Bree and Fae, at the mid-section, with several seats between himself and Yardrat.

Fae smirks. "You got bitch-slapped, fleshie. Doesn't that just boil your blood?"

"I was only ever guiding the meeting in the First Hell's stead," Benjamin says, without a trace of regret on his face. "I never claimed to be a leader."

"Hmph. You're no fun."

Fae returns her gaze to the head of the table, where Ose takes her seat. Bael waddles over and plops down on her right-hand side, beside Emperor Fenrir.

He grins cheekily at the Emperor of the Night. "Hey. What's up, toots? You're lookin' perky today. And so am I! Finally got myself a big pair of honkers!"

Despite her poor mood and the realization that several of her ambitions have shattered, Fenrir can't help but send Bael a good-natured smile. "I suppose I could be worse, Duke of Pain. Or should I call you an Emperor? If you have taken up residence inside Ose's body, and if your aura is that of a Demon Emperor, then I suppose Hell's mightiest warrior must have finally taken up his rightful place among the rest of us."

Gorn raises an eyebrow. "Yes. I, too, wonder about that. And Ose, while your soul radiates the power of an Emperor, the rest of your aura is clearly only that of a Demon Duke. Hehe. Not to call your... _qualifications_ into question... but it does seem a bit odd for a Duke to lead this meeting."

Despite Ose possessing Bael's body, and despite the fat of his glob-like stomach, somehow, her posture reveals a sort of elegance and grace nobody has ever seen from the Duke of Pain. She folds her hands together and rests them on the table, while leaning forward and exposing a toothy smile.

"Yes, Gorn. I think you do make a good point. I, a mere Duke, dare to lead this council. I suppose you _should_ call into question my capabilities."

Yardrat frowns.

_Ose agreed with Emperor Gorn? To what end?_

Ose continues to smile, baring her teeth in an oddly demure manner. However, as the seconds pass, and her expression doesn't change, the other Emperors begin to feel a bit unsettled.

The Duke of Cuddles, Jahn, shifts in his seat. "Ose? Have you anything to add?"

Ose flicks her eyes to the Duke seated on her left.

"I was just mulling over a few random thoughts in my mind. Mostly, I was thinking about what a bunch of gossiping failures the group seated here today are. Rather than focusing on immediately exterminating the threat of the Hero, all of you would rather shuffle around pieces on a chessboard, playing the same old games you always have. Surely, before I came here, you lot were entertaining thoughts of giving the title of Second Emperor to whoever killed the Hero. No doubt, someone must have floated the idea to give the position to whoever killed Belial. Have I guessed correctly?"

Jahn's incubus tail flicks around behind himself, weaving through a small hole in the back of his chair. Its movements reveal the mild annoyance in his heart.

"Yes. We did. And your point?"

Ose's smile grows more wry. She slowly lowers her head and chuckles.

"So predictable, every last one of you. You know, I underestimated that boy, and I paid the ultimate price. Now, our enemy wields the greatest weapon demonkind has always feared. From the first time Camael's revealed Excalibur's power during the War in Heaven, to the mass extinction event caused when King Arthur slew seventeen Emperors from three separate Hells with a single stroke, we have never known any emotion but absolute terror in the face of Excalibur."

Ose's smile disappears.

"And yet right now, all of you are so calm, so collected, and so dignified! Have you grown soft? Have your brains turned to mush?! We are all in imminent peril, yet you have not immediately set aside your differences to strike down that human!"

Gorn raises his palm. "Calm down, Ose. We know the power Excalibur wields. That is why we must carefully decide on a course of action-"

"There is NO time for inaction!" Ose snaps. "You fool! Jason Hiro stole Excalibur from the Overlords. The OVERLORDS! Do you realize what that means?! Even if he doesn't kill us all, the Overlords might! The moment they discover the Divine Blade has gone missing, they will set upon the human worlds and annihilate all of human life. Then, they will storm the Labyrinth and execute every human on the spot! What will that mean for us? It will result in our loss of the souls which empower us! It will result in our total stagnation, and thus, our ultimate decline!"

Ose slaps her hand against the table. "Even if the Overlords don't kill us, according to the old treaty, they have every right to wipe out our soul supply if a human Hero emerges capable of threatening their rule! And with the soul supply gone, we will never again amount to anything in their eyes! Our situation is so dire that you cannot possibly fathom it, and yet still you lounge around, debating who will obtain this scrap of power or that!"

Yardrat speaks up. "But the First Emperor, surely he will not sit idly-"

"The First Emperor is neither omniscient nor omnipotent!" Ose replies, interrupting him mid-sentence. "He poses a grave threat to the Overlords, especially while they battle against the Plague, but if this Hero continues to gain power, the Overlords will have all the justification needed to cripple us forever! That is the fate we fear most! You cannot even begin to fathom the terrifying weapons those aliens possess!"

Ose steadies her breathing. Her expression becomes eerily calm as she returns to a posture of confidence.

"All of you only possess only the barest, most faint memories of the Overlords. They left enough behind to give you vague sensations of their capabilities. Diablo and I are not the same. I have worked with the Overlords on many occasions in my unique capacity as a technology-capable demoness. I have seen the horrifying might they wield. I know better than to second-guess whether they possesses the ability to annihilate us. Even with their war-time capabilities stretched thin, we are not their match. A toothless tiger is still more than capable of shredding you with its claws."

Emperor Fenrir slowly taps her fingers on the table. "You mentioned something about a 'plague.' What did you mean?"

For a few moments, Ose falls silent. She pauses, as if debating how to answer Fenrir's question, before ultimately shaking her head.

"I cannot say. I am not allowed to leak information of such a sensitive nature to anyone, least of all any of you. All I can say is that the foe our enemy faces is one so terrifying that, were we to face them, we would perish within a day."

"The threat is that significant?" Gorn asks, unsatisfied with Ose's explanation. "So, you mean to say the Overlords are not fighting a civil war within their own ranks. Could we not ally with their enemy-"

"NO!" Ose barks. "Open your ears, dullard! Look into my eyes! Do I sound as if I am speaking of an enemy that would befriend us? It would destroy us! We should be glad we have experienced 100,000 years of stability! Were it not for the Overlords, we may have already disappeared without knowing what struck us."

Ose pauses to add emphasis to her next statement.

"And besides... the Overlords are losing the war."

"What?! Losing?" Benjamin Brown replies, alarm in his voice. "Is the situation truly that dire?"

"It is." Ose answers, chilling the crowd. "If we somehow were to miraculously defeat the Overlords, we would instead be the ones fighting their war. Our military capabilities pale by several orders of magnitude compared to theirs. We have no space fleets to speak of. Even with my abilities, constructing enough starships for us to stand a chance would require centuries, time which we do not have."

"If the situation is as dire as you say," Yardrat interjects, "then how can we hold even the slightest hope? Are we not doomed no matter what we do?"

A lull appears in the conversation. Ose becomes uncharacteristically silent, while she continues to debate what she can and cannot say.

"I understand that many of you laughed at me for Stormbringer's failure. All my efforts, gone to waste. In every meaning of the world, that operation failed completely and utterly with no upsides. It was... a tragedy. But the tragedy you know of is only the tip of the iceberg."

She continues. "I had to try... to capture the Wordsmith. It is our only chance. The Wordsmith's power is something which most certainly _will_ reshape the balance of the war. The only question is whether demonkind would lose or win from this scenario."

Ose sighs. "Shortly before Stormbringer, Jason Hiro came to me in private. He sought a meeting with me, intending to strike up a peace treaty between demonkind and humanity. In exchange, he promised to grant us the ability to evolve our ranks without using the power of souls."

The First and Second Ancient orcs both stiffen in surprise. Huul voices her shock. "What?! To evolve without souls? Is such a feat even possible?"

Ose slowly nods. "If I had to wager a guess, I would say that it might be, but only through the Wordsmith's magic."

"We would become reliant on the Hero." Yardrat states, seeing the problem immediately. "But, considering the threat our galaxy faces, this... 'plague.' Does that not seem a worthy trade?"

"Under such a treaty, we would become subservient to the Hero." Gorn says. "Hmph. Prior to Stormbringer, the Hero was still a weak boy living a cushy life on Tarus II. Ose must have believed she could get a better deal for our people, and especially _herself_, if she managed to capture and enslave the Hero herself. I must say, in her shoes, I'd have done the same."

Emperor Fenrir's expression, unlike the rest, appears relatively calm.

"Perhaps. But now we've damned ourselves. Not only have we killed the Hero's daughter and made him our mortal enemy, but he has also obtained the most frightening weapon known to our people. Previously, we only faced extinction from this mysterious 'plague,' as well as a possible attempt on our species by the Overlords. Now we also face the wrath of a young hero led by King Arthur."

"I acted too greedily." Ose replies, her voice low. "If we were to somehow obtain the Hero's power, we could still succeed in my original plan. Obtaining the ability to empower any demon without souls would completely change the dynamic of our war. Forget the Overlords. I would not even fear their enemy if that were to happen. Demon Emperors are exceedingly powerful. Our biggest issue is that there are too few of you. Even a hundred Emperors would not pose much of a threat to the Overlords. But a thousand? A hundred thousand? Perhaps even a million? They would tremble if our shadow so much as grazed them."

"And consider the other possibility," Ose adds. "What if, through our experimentation, we discovered a method to break through our limiter? What if we could step into a realm beyond Demon Emperor?"

"Yes. That possibility does sound truly wonderful," Yardrat replies, smacking his lips derisively. "Oh, but what a coincidence, you intended to monopolize the Hero's power all to yourself. And Emperor Yama. We should be so fortunate if a few meager drops trickled down to the rest of the Seven Hells."

Ose chuckles wryly. "Hehe. Yes. I did intend to take all the power for myself, originally. You're not wrong. What can I say in my defense, but I am still a demon, in the end. Naturally, the tides have changed. We cannot afford to play nice with the Hero. We must kill him at once, before he reaches a power beyond our abilities to control... or one which will alert the Overlords to his presence."

Gorn cocks his head. "I do not understand, Ose. If the Hero is our only hope of defeating the Overlords and the Plague, then why would we even consider killing him? Surely, we should still make every attempt to capture him alive."

Ose gestures behind herself, to Mephisto, who continues to stand silently and watch the proceedings like a street beggar who has lost everything.

"We can still use the power of Wordsmithing even if Jason Hiro is dead. Previously, I would not have liked to give Mephisto such authority, but what choice do we have, now? We have to slay both Wordsmiths and use their powers through necromancy. We must move swiftly, without alerting the Overlords."

She pauses.

"...But, in fact, there is something I have neglected to mention. It is also possible the Overlords know of Jason Hiro's existence, or _will_ know, soon. I received a communication from a highly-ranked Overlord, someone who makes even me feel fear. She knows that we have lost control of the Core. I tried to handwave away the issues with talk of a minor human insurrection, but knowing this particular individual's deductive capabilities, she will bring an inspection team... and soon. I must intercept her arrival and ensure I deliver a narrative beneficial to our people."

"If the Overlords discover Jason Hiro's existence, let alone what his powers entail," Yardrat says, "what exactly will their response be, Ose? You know them better than the rest of us."

Ose lightly raps her fingers against the table.

"There are a few possibilities. Total annihilation of humanity is only one of them. Given their fear of Heroes, it is the most likely eventuality. However, when we consider just how poorly their war is going..."

She shakes her head.

"It is also possible, though not probable, that they will consider a treaty or enslavement of the Hero. They, too, may see what I see in his potential might wielded for their benefit. However, capturing or enslaving him will require the Founders to divert resources they simply don't have. Annihilation, by contrast, would be far simpler."

"If the Overlords obtain the Hero's power, then will we not simply cease to be any sort of threat?" Yardrat asks.

"Naturally," Gorn says, answering Yardrat's question. "No matter how you look at it, only Jason Hiro possesses the power to change this war and save the whole galaxy. Well, not just him, I suppose. There is also his clone..."

For a brief moment, all of the Emperors fall silent.

Bael, of all people, is the first to speak up.

"Err. I mean. Don't it just seem like y'all should give away one of the Wordshits to the Overdorks? Like, if they don't know his clone exists, we could be all secret-like and stash the bugger away for ourselves."

Ose turns to Bael and blinks. "That... that is a surprisingly good idea, Bael. But I thought you wanted us to kill the Wordsmith. Not capture him."

Bael shrugs. "I didn't know the 'sitch' was this complicated. All this yammerin' has my ears ringin'. Look, if these Motherlords find out about the Hero, then they're gonna wanna take him. But if they don't know we got one, we can be all sneaky and use that other kid ourselves. Seems pretty simple ta' me. Even Hell's biggest dummy can figure that much out."

Ose narrows her eyes. "Yes... Hell's biggest dummy, indeed."

After eying the Duke of Pain for a moment, she looks away.

"Well. Bael has brought up a decent backup option I hadn't considered. Never have two Heroes existed in tandem. Should the Overlords learn of the Hero's existence, we will have more possible avenues worth pursuing. In fact, now that he mentions it, we might even consider-"

Suddenly, right in the middle of Ose's speech, a deep rumble rattles the underground chamber, as if a bomb had detonated somewhere nearby. Ose pauses mid-sentence to flick her eyes around the room.

"What's going on?"

Every Emperor and Duke quickly rises to their feet. They go on the alert, not sure what just happened.

"Is it the Overlords?!" Lily asks, her voice tightening up in fright.

"I don't think so," Ose says. "But for a brief instant, I thought I detected Holy-"

**BOOOOM!!**

The roof of the chamber suddenly rips away, yanked upward into the sky. A violent shockwave blasts downward and flattens all the weakest demons, monsters, and humans present. Even Benjamin Brown barely manages to stabilize himself against the table, while his daughter's head bangs against it and instantly knocks her unconscious.

"HAHAHA!" Cackles a masculine voice from above. "Hello, little bloodskins! It seems I have located thy wretched hiding place!"

Every Emperor and Duke raises their eyes skyward, where they spot a single armored demon hovering in midair, the unmistakable power of a Demon Duke radiating from his body.

"Barbatos! No, wait, it's Uriel!" Yardrat shouts. "It's the Archangel! How did she get here without alerting us?!"

Uriel raises her hands overhead, conjuring a massive orb of holy energy.

"HAHAHA! Feel free to answer that question in the Great Beyond, after I damn thee to **OBLIVION!!**"
