Part 293: Retaking the Core

Due to the events of Stormbringer, humanity suffers a devastating loss. After well over two of the three million people on Tarus II perish, only half a million remain, with eighty percent of them being human, and the rest a mixture of monsters, along with a handful of allied demons.

Three days after Stormbringer's conclusion, outside the Fortress of Retribution's walls, every single member of humanity's survivors gather. With all of the injured and wounded healed by the Hero's seemingly limitless mana capacity, none appear injured on their bodies. However, their minds and souls are a different story. A downcast atmosphere lingers in the minds of every person present, with few exceptions.

Jason and Phoebe Hiro stand solemnly atop a fifty-foot-tall platform, allowing them to overlook the throng of humanity's last free individuals. Despite the possibility demonkind might attack again, nobody appears worried, having observed the Wordsmith's current level of power. With Camael's Cube at his beck and call, any invading force will surely perish instantly. None of the First Hell's survivors would be so stupid as to attack a world where their mightiest Emperor and Dukes fell.

A nearly invisible force-field hums in the skies above. It encompasses the entire planet, preventing Warpers from stepping foot on Tarus II's soil. Numerous magical embattlements dot the land. Their tremendous firepower and automated aim will allow them to repel any intruder who dares step foot on humanity's bulwark world without permission.

The Wordsmith and his wife, standing above their friends and comrades, heave sighs of regret.

"Everyone," The Wordsmith begins. "Thank you for coming. I owe you all an apology... from the bottom of my heart. I am sorry. I have failed you."

He pauses for a beat, allowing his words to sink into the ears of the half-million-strong audience.

"We've all lost people precious to us during the despicable Stormbringer Attack. Ose planned to capture Hope and I in order to enslave humanity. She failed, in the end... but our victory has proven hollow. The cost we paid was too high. Far, far too high."

"We have made gains," He continues. "I have acquired a device which will catapult humanity to the stars and beyond. However, this device cannot bring back those we've lost. Were you to place it in the hands of the old me, the one who had lost nothing and was too afraid to take any actions that might prod our enemies into action, I would have wasted its potential."

"Yes, the truth is, I could have prevented all of this, even with my previous abilities. I could have constructed a field that would keep Warpers away. I could have made the demons fear me to the extent of ever provoking my wrath. I could have claimed so many demon lives that they would never risk my ire falling upon them. But I didn't. Instead, I spent my days merrily keeping to myself and playing nice."

"That is why, starting today, I make a solemn promise to everyone here that I will no longer allow another one of our children to suffer because of my inaction. I will not allow my soldiers to suffer and die for a bleak future of merely existing. We have the right to live freely. We have the right to survive, and even to thrive!"

Jason's words project throughout the sky, allowing anyone on the planet to hear him, let alone the throng positioned directly before him.

Phoebe gazes at the side of her husband's face. For a brief moment, a sense of loss appears in her eyes.

The man she fell in love with has changed irreversibly. No longer do those same optimistic eyes look out at the world, hoping for a compromise. Instead, the rip his heart has endured has shaken him and torn away the sheet covering his vision, making him truly see reality for its stained, ugly truths.

The Wordsmith's wife bows her head.

_It's better this way._ She thinks. _I always tried to tell Jason he was too naïve. I just... I didn't want him to change this quickly... this abruptly. Not under these circumstances. The price we've paid was one I wish we hadn't._

Belial stands behind Phoebe, watching as the stone-faced mother stands silently beside her husband. She aches to comfort Phoebe, but with so many eyes focused on humanity's leaders, she doesn't feel right putting herself front and center. Especially with her identity as a Demon Emperor. In many ways, she feels even guiltier about the Stormbringer war than anyone else.

_I'm the Second Emperor. I should have helped defend the Core at the start. Beelzebub? Mephisto? Bael? I could have fought alongside Kar and easily beaten all of them back. Ose was the only demon capable of beating me, but with Kar and Blinker there... we could have overwhelmed her!_

Belial glances slightly to the right, where the Monster Queen sits on her shoulder, also watching the proceedings.

Belial touches Blinker's hair with her finger, offering what little emotional support she can. However, the Monster Queen appears unmoved. Blinker's gaze fixates on Jason's back, but her thoughts dwell elsewhere, imagining the children she could have raised with her husband.

"And that is why I will make the first strike today!" Jason proclaims, grabbing everyone's attention. "I will not allow these demons to so much as _sneer_ in our general direction. Today... without anyone else's help. I will steal back the Core. It is mine to take. It is OURS to take!"

The Wordsmith turns to his left, facing away from Phoebe on the platform.

"**Portal!**"

The space before him rips apart, momentarily revealing the stunned faces of several demons as they turn to look at the intruder. 

Without any fanfare, Jason activates his T-REX, enveloping himself in a sea of nanites. Then he steps through and closes the portal.

The audience, including Phoebe and Belial, appear momentarily stunned.

"What?" Belial whispers. "I didn't know Jason was going to do that!"

Phoebe blinks her eyes. "I-I... I didn't, either."

---

A single human steps inside the Labyrinth Core. Despite his face being exposed for the briefest of moments, none of the low-level grunts standing closest recognize him. By the time he enters the room, they can only gaze at the stupid fleshbag barging into their space before bursting into laughter.

"Oy, mates! Who's this bugger? What stupid fleshie thinks he can take us all on alone, eh?" Asks a Demon Lord nearby, an unpleasant-looking fellow with two tusk-like teeth jutting out of his lower jaw.

Almost fifty thousand demons walk and crawl around the Core's interior. After several days of cleanup, they've already pulled most of the debris outside and otherwise made the Core somewhat presentable. The louder the ruckus at the Wordsmith's location, the more heads that turn to face him.

The Demon Lord summons two bone spikes into his palms and laughs greedily. "Gut the bastard!"

He fires the spikes at Jason, even while a thousand other demons leap at the human in unison, all looking to bite off a piece of the Terran's armor and claim the easy kill for themselves.

An instant later, a silent explosion detonates inside the room. A flash of light spreads outward from the Wordsmith's body faster than the eye can follow. Any demon the expanding shockwave of holy energy touches disappears at the atomic level, vanishing from reality. 

One second later, the room becomes eerily silent. A few confused orcs, minotaurs, goblins, and other assorted monsters stand, frozen in a mixture of surprise and shock as the fifty thousand demons around them seemingly vanish into thin air.

"H-huh?" One minotaur grunts, as he flicks his head from side to side. "Where... did the others... go?"

The Wordsmith raises his finger. He summons a pebble of condensed dark energy, which suddenly races away from him toward the nearest monster, a goblin. Before the goblin can react, its head explodes, and its body gets sucked into the small black orb, which grows minutely bigger.

Chaos erupts in the Core as the monsters realize the human that has appeared is the one who killed all of the demons and is now aiming for them! The bravest monsters charge Jason, but the rest flee toward the Core's exit. Some even rush toward an open portal targeted at another world somewhere in the Milky Way.

However, their speeds pale in comparison to the seemingly sentient orb of darkness. Like a miniature black hole hellbent on devouring all the Wordsmith's enemies, it zips around at the speed of sound, ripping apart anyone in its path and killing them before they can voice a grunt of pain.

One minute later, every enemy inside the Core disappears, leaving Jason alone.

The Wordsmith lowers his helmet and gazes around, a mildly dissatisfied expression on his face.

"...That was easier than I expected."

"Hell has lost all of its Emperors and Dukes," Mind-Phoebe says. "Barons appear to be in short supply. That just leaves Lords and a bunch of bottom-feeders."

Jason nods. "Mmm. Mephisto's still alive. But, I guess one Duke won't be much of a threat... not compared to the Hidden Hells. Belial said we'd have to start worrying about them, since I've effectively wiped out the entire power structure of the First Hell. Hard to believe these life-threatening enemies we've been facing all this time weren't even the tip of the iceberg."

His mind-wife nods. "I talked to Belial a bunch, and my real-self, too. They told me all sorts of info about the Hidden Emperors. Honestly... I don't see why they have stayed off in their corners all this time when they had the power to overthrow the First Hell all along."

Jason slowly shakes his head. "Have you forgotten? Standing above all the Seven Hells is the First Emperor. The one demon publicly ranked above Belial. Nobody knows who he is or what he looks like. They only know that those who go to face him return with all memories of his face and features erased from their minds. I can't even restore Belial's memories with my Wordsmithing, that's how deep his magic cuts."

The Wordsmith chews his lower lip as he glances around the empty Core.

"What sort of demon is the First Emperor? Is he strong enough for even me to be worried?"

Gabriel stands beside Phoebe in Jason's Mind Realm. He remains silent, clearly knowing nothing about the so-called First Emperor's identity.

After Jason muses for a few more minutes, he turns and strides out of the Core, finding himself standing before the substantially larger ball of sludge-like energy. Having devoured a few hundred fleeing monsters, it has already grown to twice Jason's size. For a brief moment, he even thinks to himself that it might be equal to Kar's stature.

"**Halve. Reform. Disguise. Sentry.**"

With four words of power, he changes the shapeless, black hole-like blob into a pair of human-shaped figures identical to himself. They assume his appearance and stand at attention, awaiting his orders. With bodies far sturdier and denser than demonstone, they become a pair of nigh-unkillable sentries capable of defending the Core.

"**Sentience.** You two will stay out here. If demons come, kill them. If hostiles come, kill them. Anyone else? Tell me. I'll meet them myself."

The eerily human-shaped blobs nod their assent, then turn and stand at attention on either side of the former Great Doors to the Core, now both torn from their hinges and lost when the Core collapsed.

"There." Jason mutters. "I've taken back the Core. Soon, the rest of humanity's worlds will follow. The demons may have stolen them from us... but they cannot stop our inexorable march. Humanity's rise begins today."

"You sound awfully ominous." Phoebe mutters. "I'm glad you taking more proactive measures, Jason, but don't go too far."

"I'll go as far as I need to," Jason replies. "Whatever gets the job done. Now, we have a funeral to attend. I'm going to put some soldiers in the Core and head back to Tarus II."

His mind-wife shrugs. "Whatever works, love."

---

After returning to Tarus II, Jason shows everyone the reclaimed Core and continues explaining his future intentions. While a few people cheer, most simply nod, accepting that their Hero has finally stepped up to the plate. His victory over the Core gives them some inspiration, but the losses weighing on their mind continue to dampen their spirits.

Several hours later, Jason and a little over a dozen other people attend a private funeral.

The Wordsmith, Phoebe, Belial, Blinker, Makoto Ueda, and Hans Wagner all attend the funeral. Additionally, Chef Yamir, Salamander Ogie, Ogie's wife Jeera, General Chadwick, Corporal Hurent, Lieutenant Samuel, and Solomon's Nephew, Saul, also show up to attend.

Unlike the public ceremony for the millions of fallen citizens, this funeral only stands for two people: Sphinx King Kar, and the Wordsmith's daughter, Daisy.

Pictures of their faces rest upon two gravestones. Sadly, with neither of them having a body for anyone to bury, the funeral is mostly symbolic.

Blinker continues to sit on Belial's shoulder as they gaze at Kar's gravestone.

"Mephisto took my husband's body from me." She mutters. "I have to get it back. I can't let him desecrate Kar's legacy like that. He deserves to be buried with honor like he always wanted."

Belial and Phoebe stand next to each other. The Wordsmith's wife sighs. "You're pregnant, Blinker. You can't go chasing after Mephisto now. You owe it to Kar to have those kids first."

Belial nods. "Phoebe's right. Fairies only need a few months to give birth. Why don't you go back to the Fairy Queen's domain for a while and bear your children there? Raise them among the fairies, where the demons won't dare intrude."

Blinker shakes her head. "I never got along with those brainless idiots. They're always playing pranks and up to no good. If you haven't lived with the fairies, you won't understand how annoying they all are."

A few moments pass as a thoughtful glint appears in Blinker's eyes. "Well, there is _one_ place I could go. I should visit Kar's mother, Marie. I wouldn't mind raising our children with the lizards. There's no chance the demons would try anything under Miss Marie's nose. She was also very loving and protective of Kar... so I bet she'd be alright with helping raise our kids. Then, once we've settled in... I can leave to chase down Mephisto and beat the crap out of him!"

Blinker sighs. "I barely even know the woman... and now I want to ask for her help after getting Kar killed. I'm the worst."

"Don't say that." Phoebe interjects. "You aren't at fault for what happened to Kar. Mephisto is. He planned everything, like a Viper slithering in the grass. He's your enemy, and he's _my_ enemy, too."

While the three women talk, another group of people congregates together near Daisy's grave.

Jason, the Minotaur Chef Yamir, and Ogie all pay their respects to the Wordsmith's deceased daughter.

The old salamander sighs emotionally. "Poor lil' tyke. I'm gonna miss her. She always used to give me a big smile in the morning."

Chef Yamir echoes his sadness. "I loved cooking for the little one. She had an infectious energy about her."

Jason nods sagely, his gaze distant. "We have to work harder to make sure no other child suffers like Daisy did. As a father, I have to protect the children of humanity, as well as all the monster youngsters out there. I'm going to build a fortified location that not even a hundred Demon Emperors could penetrate without killing themselves. I will make sure something like Stormbringer never catches us offguard again."

Ogie's wife, Jeera, wanders over to her husband's side. Mister Hiro, as long as you do your best, I'm sure your little girl will smile at you from the Great Beyond."

Jason's expression darkens. "When people die, their souls end up in the River Styx. That means Mephisto might have Daisy's soul. Once I get everything sorted, I'm going to hunt that bastard down and take back all the souls of the people he's stolen. Daisy, Kar, and all the others, too."

Yamir blinks. "Are you serious? If he has Daisy's soul... then... couldn't you bring her back?"

"I don't know." Jason states. "But possibly, yes. I'm willing to risk everything if it means putting her soul back in a body. I can recreate Daisy's body with my Wordsmithing... I just can't bring her soul back from the Great Beyond. If it has somehow landed in Mephisto's grasp though... then there is a chance!"

A spark of light appears in the eyes of Yamir, Ogie, and Jeera. 

"Really?!" Ogie asks. "That's great news! You-"

Before the salamander can continue, Jason raises his palm. "Don't say anything else, and keep your voice down. Beelzebub blasted the entire area with some sort of toxic, corrosive 'chaos energy.' It's entirely possible Daisy's soul was... I'd rather not say. I don't want to get anyone's hopes up, least of all Phoebe's. She's suffered enough because of me."

The other three nod quietly.

"Yes, of course." Yamir says, before lowering his gaze. "We can only pray..."

After a few moments, two figures walk up behind the Wordsmith.

"Ahem." A man says, clearing his throat. "Jason Hiro, ja? I wish to speak to you about somezing."

Jason turns around to look at the speaker, who turns out to be Hans Wagner, flanked by Makoto Ueda.

"Hans. Good to see you," Jason says, offering a weak smile. "What's up?"

"It is in regards to mein friend, Neil," Hans says. "Many believe zat he perished during zee Core collapse, ven zat hateful boy, Henry, left him behind. However, according to trace sensor readings, it seems his suit vas not destroyed ven zee Core crumbled. I believe he may still be alive."

The Wordsmith widens his eyes. "Really? So that could mean the demons did capture him. However, I've tried using 'Locate' to detect him, Daisy, Hope, and several others, but without any luck. If any of them are alive, then..."

Jason trails off, his meaning clear.

Hans shakes his head. "Nein. I believe zee demons have found some way to hide Neil's soul from your Vordsmithing. I don't know zee details, but it seems likely. He might be someone zey have designated a valuable hostage."

Both men fall silent, while Jason ponders Hans' meaning.

"The demons must have placed Neil in some isolated location. Maybe they placed a barrier around him which prevents my Wordsmithing from detecting him. The same might be true of Hope, too, but I don't think Ose managed to capture Hope before, during, or after the Stormbringer Attack. Something else is going on, and I don't know what..."

"I have nothing else to report," Hans says, throwing his palms up in exasperation. "Neil is mein good friend. I do not wish to imagine him suffering under zee demons' claws. I pray you can save him through your Vordsmithing, or through some other means."

"I'll do what I can," Jason promises.

Hans nods, then turns and walks away. However, after he leaves, Makoto Ueda stays behind. The Japanese man smiles half-heartedly, more out of politeness than for any other reason.

"Hiro-san, is good to see you again. I wish we speak under better circumstances." Makoto says.

Jason shakes his head. "It's enough that you came to pay your respects. Daisy liked you a lot."

"And I liked her, too. Such a charming child," Makoto replies. "I came to offer a shoulder if Hiro-san needs emotional support. Sometimes, great man have many burdens he cannot share with those closest to him. If you wish, you may visit me any time you wish. I have moved in with Saul outside of the city."

"Solomon's nephew..." Jason mutters, as he remembers Saul's face. "I haven't spoken to him in a while. Bah, we're not that close anyway. Thanks for the offer, I guess, but I'm doing alright."

"Very presumptuous of me," Makoto says, as he bows slightly at the waist and cups his hands. "Hiro-san has many friends and advisors. My guidance is shallow, and my wisdom limited."

"That's not true at all," Jason says, his smile strengthening for a moment. "You offered me valuable spiritual advice once, and I appreciated the assistance. Sometimes, an outside perspective can greatly help in a moment of crisis."

"Mhm. Yes, many thanks for your kind words," Makoto replies, before turning to walk away. "We speak again soon, I hope."

"Of course."

Makoto leaves Jason to mull over his words while taking his leave. As the Wordsmith mentally replays his life's mistakes, another group of people walks over, this one consisting of General Chadwick, Lieutenant Samuel, and Corporal Hurent.

"Jason, my boy!" Chadwick says, his voice much louder than the other funeral-goers. "How good to see you."

Pushed back by such a surprising amount of enthusiasm, Jason can't help but smile at Chadwick. "General. Thank you for your service during the Stormbringer Attack. Your leadership saved countless lives, including my wife's."

"Hahaha! I barely did anything, there's no need to thank this old man for doing his duty!" Chadwick laughs.

While the Wordsmith talks to the group of people surrounding him, Makoto Ueda slowly walks away from the funeral proceedings. His eyes flick to the darkening sky, as the sun slowly begins to set on Tarus II's horizon.

_Life so fragile. So many die, but for what reason? Demons kill, humans kill, never does cycle of violence stop._

As the Japanese man walks, a flicker of light materializes behind him. Lasting for only an instant, nobody at the funeral notices as the firefly-sized speck rushes into Makoto's back, making him slow to a stop and freeze on the spot.

The Japanese man's eyes lose focus. He grinds to a halt and stares forward, his eyes still locked blankly on the sky.

After five minutes, he blinks wearily, a dazed look swallowing his face.

"...Where am I? What era is this?"

Makoto's voice takes on a curious cadence, different from how it normally sounds. He pauses to sweep his eyes from side to side, frowning as he takes in the unfamiliar sights.

"I do not recognize this sky. I do not recognize this location. The stars seem different. The smell in the air is foreign."

'Makoto' stretches his arms, then turns around to stare at the funeral proceedings with a look of confusion.

"These people... monsters standing alongside humans? It seems much has changed since I last returned. And that fellow there seems to have the aura of a Hero. How curious."

He nods slowly, while his thoughts turn inward, reflecting on the memories stored within his newly obtained body's vessel.

"...One hundred thousand years... demons have taken over Hell... Earth destroyed... so much has changed. I must reacclimate to this era if I hope to understand the proceedings of Karma."

'Makoto' turns away from the funeral and continues walking. This time, his steps appear more determined and full of conviction.

"I wonder how my protégé has fared."
