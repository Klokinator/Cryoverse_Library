Resurrection. Amelia brought up a good point. Bringing people back from the dead sounded impossible, but maybe it was more doable than he thought. Did he have power over life and death? Could a single word from him reshape the cosmos?

_Well, I don't have a dead body hanging around, but maybe something else would work._ Jason thought.

Jason took a few moments to ping the Crown and pull up several options. His eyes widened as one possibility came into focus.

Reaching down, he pulled out his sword. "**Boulder.**" A gigantic rock popped into view and crashed to the earth. Amelia flinched, not expecting him to Wordsmith all of a sudden. 

"What- what are you doing?"

Instead of answering, Jason swung his crappy sword at the rock. It banged loudly and bounced off, as he had expected.

"**Sharpen.**"

He swung again, and still, it bounced off.

"**Sharpen!**"

This time, the sword cut into the edge of the rock and jammed a few inches inside of it.

_It's working._ Jason thought.

He yanked the sword out and tried again. "**Sharpen!**"

When he swung for the fourth time, his blade passed through the boulder, carving a razor-thin slice through it. The instant it flew out the other side, the blade snapped, and a chunk of the sword flew out and embedded in the ground ten feet away.

_It's sharper, but still just a weak sword._ Jason thought.

Now for improvements. "**Reform. Sharpen. Sharpen. Sharpen. Reinforce. Reinforce.**"

He shook the sword around and smiled when the blade did not wobble at all. This time, when he sliced the rock, the sword passed through with no resistance, and the boulder flopped over and smashed into the ground.

_Now THIS is a weapon I can use against monsters and demons._ Jason thought.

Another idea appeared in his mind. Stupid, but worth a shot.

"**Sentience.**"

He aimed his mind at the sword and waited. 

A minute passed. 

Nothing happened.

"What are you doing, Jason?" Amelia slid up next to him and gazed at the sword.

"I'm trying to make this sword sentient. I wondered if I could make it talk or something."

She smirked. "That's silly. It's a sword. You have to have a soul to be sentient. Everyone knows that."

"The hell I do!" A shrill voice screamed out from the sword, making him drop it and hop back out of fright. "Ain't no dame shit-talkin' me, no sirree! Not on my watch!"

Amelia's eyes widened like dinner plates. "Jason. The sword spoke."

"Damn tootin' I did! I got a lot to say to a pretty lass like you! Name's Dellfingler, and what's yours, sweetcheeks?"

His odd accent was rough and irritating to listen to, but Amelia did not seem to mind. She reached down and picked the sword up. "I'm Amelia! How are you speaking? Do you have a mouth?"

"Course not! I'm a sword, little lady! Swords ain't got no mouths, honey-bear! I'm usin' voice projection! Can't see anything around me either, but I kin' feel the vibrations in the air!"

Amelia looked at Jason like a puppy. Her lip quivered. "Oh, Jason, he's wonderful! I'm not one for weapons, but can I have him, please?"

"Hell yeah, honey! That's what I'm talkin' about! You 'n me are gonna ride off into the sunset together and get all hitched up! Yeehaw!"

The sword's enthusiasm was quickly getting on Jason's nerves. He nodded. "Yeah, that's fine. I can make another one. Here, take the scabbard, too." He pulled the sheath off and handed it to her, and as he did, her hand touched his. Amelia stepped back quickly, and her cheeks flushed red. "Th-thank you, Jason. Nobody's ever given me a present before."

Jason's heart beat like conga drums. "Glad I could be your, err, first. Haha."

Jason suddenly realized what he had just said had a lot of innuendo, and he cursed his filthy mouth. Luckily she did not pick up on it. _Thank god._ Jason thought.

Kar grunted. "Hurgh. Stupid machine. Worthless!" He pounded his fist on its display. "It's broken! Busted! We're stuck here forever!"

"You don't have a key to access it?" Amelia walked over to him after she finished latching Dellfingler to her belt. "I thought you obtained something like that when you killed Bahamut."

Kar shook his head. "Nay. It's not a simple matter of opening the portal with a password. We also have to do a bunch of math and crap to open the portal inside the Labyrinth Core. I ain't smart enough for this crap. The other humans and monsters on this planet rely on the team at the Labyrinth Core to open and close the portals."

Dellfingler piped up. "Hey, I know! Why not try'n break it! Just punch it til' it works! That never fails!"

Jason walked over and gently nudged Kar out of the way. "Let's call that 'Option B,' okay, Dellfingler? I'll try messing around. If it's just a matter of math, maybe the Crown can help."

When Jason tapped the console, an array of numbers and letters appeared, along with hundreds of rapidly changing variables, equations, and other such things. The Crown activated, improving his mental faculties until he could understand what everything on the console did. There were variables like planet orbital speed, distance to one of seven hundred locations throughout the galaxy, atmospheric fluctuations, and more things that continued on and on.

Five minutes later, Amelia reached over and touched his arm. "Jason? Are you okay? You've been just standing and staring this whole time..."

Jason nodded absentmindedly. "I'm calculating. Won't be long now."

She pulled back, and he saw her shrug at Kar from the corner of his eye.

Finally, his mind had all the variables in play.

Jason tapped the connection numbers in a row. "Four deltas, sixteen alphas, four-two point two-three sequence breaks, then a parameter of eight and fifteen for the lightspeed calculations... engage." His finger reached over and mechanically tapped the initiate button. 

Eruptions of electricity arced from the gate's pylons to form a circular hole in space.

Amelia and Kar exhaled simultaneously. The Sphinx grinned. "Unbelievable. You truly are a Hero, Jason."

Jason shot a glance at Amelia, then back to Kar. "I'd prefer if you stopped calling me that, but thanks."

Before they started walking up the steps to the portal, Jason realized that without a weapon, if they met armed resistance inside, he might be a sitting duck. Several weapon ideas popped into his head, including some excellent plasma blaster concepts, but a single word took his breath away due to the sheer possibilities.

"**Lightsaber.**"
