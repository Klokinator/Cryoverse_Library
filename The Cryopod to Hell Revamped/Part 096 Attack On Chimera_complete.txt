"Holy shit. That was intense," Jason said.

Before he meant to say anything, a curse left his mouth. Jason opened his eyes and found himself before the bonfire once again, as the Archangels and Solomon gazed at him.

Michael stuck his chin out. His face contorted into disgust.

"Not a pleasant vision, was it? 'Twas a day I'll never forget. Satan, the leader of the demons, turned our brother against us. He forced Samael's hand, turning him into a living weapon," Michael said.

"Samael didn't deserve such misery," Raphael said, his voice low. "Sadly, that day was only the beginning of his torment."

Jason raised his hand. "Question. Samael used a power he called the Eight Vectors. When I battled Satan six years ago, he also had it. Given this vision showed Samael signing one of Satan's Blood Contracts..."

"Thy presumption is correct," Michael replied. "Satan eventually took Samael's powers for himself. Not only Samael's abilities, but those of other demons, heroes, and angels. Satan may have been the weakest of his people at first, but as time passed, he eclipsed his peers. In many ways, thy rise mirrors his."

Jason raised an eyebrow. "It does? I do?"

Solomon nodded. "I've taken my time training you, Jason. We've been lucky to have a lengthy reprieve from conflict with the demons. Wordsmithing holds more power than anything the demons and heroes could ever imagine, but it will require mental willpower, vast mana stores, and excessive creativity to master."

"The Knowledge Seeker speaks truth," Raphael said. "One of the reasons we're sharing our knowledge of the past is because we believe thou can'st repair the damage the demons have wrought upon the galaxy. Returning the lives of our brothers and sisters is impossible, but thou may yet restore a sense of balance to the galaxy, preventing the demons from tearing apart everything in their path."

"I don't think the demons intend to cause permanent damage," Jason said. "They lived stably for a hundred thousand years. I'm unconvinced they're genocidal monsters. To be honest, the way you treated them in the past seemed downright awful. It's no surprise they turned against you."

Michael's expression soured. "Dost thee believe the demons were right to attack us? That we deserved the horrors they unleashed? We made many mistakes, but never anything worthy of the violence they rained from the skies. Now that we know about Leviathan, 'tis reasonable to conclude he corrupted the minds of the demons and turned them down the path of evil."

"Leviathan certainly had a hand in the demonic insurrection," Jason conceded, "but you kicked and bullied the imps. You treated them like slaves for thousands of years. Millions, perhaps? I'm not surprised they went rogue. I would too."

"Blasphemy," Raphael said. "The angels conceived the imps to serve us. Thou art implying the imps had the _right_ to revolt, but given they owed their existence to us, we held control over their lives from the moment of their creation."

"I don't care why the angels created the imps. What matters is how they treated them. Kicking, beating, and insulting them was inhumane. I can't see the imps as the bad guys, considering the circumstances," Jason countered.

A moment of silence passed. Jason's words hung in the air as the three Archangels stared at him.

Michael waved his hand flippantly. "Give it time. Thy tune will change when thou dost witness what came next."

Solomon cleared his throat. "Ahem. Let's return to the past. This time, we'll see the aftereffects of Satan's Blood Contract and what it could do to a person's mind. It wasn't pretty."

Jason said nothing. Solomon motioned with his hands, and the world faded to black.

_I can't see the angels as the good guys, no matter how they try to spin the past,_ Jason thought.

.......................................................

"Uzziel! Sister! Art thou injured?!"

Uriel's panicked voice jolted Uzziel awake. She opened her eyes to see the waifish Archangel hovering above her. Uriel rested her hand on Uzziel's forehead.

"Where am I? What happened?" Uzziel asked.

In the distance, a dull _whoomph_ blasted outward, shaking the ground. Uzziel lifted her body to see what caused the noise and gasped as a chunk of rock whizzed past and missed her by mere inches, slamming into the ground beside her face.

Samael, the Archangel of Change, no longer resembled himself. Instead, he had transformed into a chimera, one with a cave bear's body, a lion's head, and a wolf's tail to boot. Jason's first thought was that it resembled Kar's Sphinx form. However, the thirty-foot-tall monster dwarfed Kar's transformation by a factor of three, so the comparison didn't seem apt.

"Samael! Why has he transformed?" Uzziel asked.

Uriel helped Uzziel to her feet. Her numb legs trembled as she dusted herself off. "Worry not, Uzziel. The foul demons attempted to capture thee from right under our noses, but I put a stop to their plans. They fled, but left Samael behind. He's been battling our brothers and Camael for thirty minutes now!"

The humongous monstrosity, Samael, unleashed a howl that blasted away the clouds above. "**GROOOOOAAARR!!**"

Despite his transformation, Samael didn't appear to have gone berserk. Instead, his eight vectors intelligently punched and thrashed the other Archangels as they tried to hem him in and cut his movements off. Gabriel, once the tallest angel in Heaven, now only stood at half Samael's height. He continuously fired powerful blasts of holy energy at his brother, but they dealt little to no damage. Samael concentrated his attention on Camael and Michael, ignoring Raphael and Gabriel for the most part.

Michael darted around, tossing rocks into the air and batting them with his sword at Samael's reinforced animal hide repeatedly. His incredible strength and speed allowed him to transform the stones into cannonballs, blasting Samael over and over.

Camael, meanwhile, wielded a tremendous greathammer, taller than her body with a head that must have weighed a ton. Her speed, while significantly slower than Michael's, was made up for by her strength. She leaped at the cave bear and slammed her hammer into his stomach, knocking Samael fifty feet to the side. He crashed into the ground and leaped to his feet instantly, appearing unharmed by her attack.

Meanwhile, Raphael weaved several magic signs that appeared to do nothing. Jason couldn't tell if he was helping or trying to distract the Archangel of Change with spooky finger movements. He hoped it wasn't the latter.

"Art thou capable of standing?" Uriel asked. "I have to help stop Samael. His transformation puts him well beyond a level of power our brothers can handle. At least, not if we hope to subdue him!"

Dizziness hit Uzziel for a moment, but she shook it off. "I'm fine. I'll help, too. Michael and Camael must be exhausted."

"Thou hath no idea," Uriel replied, worry in her voice. "We've expended valuable mana trying to stop Samael. I don't know what that little red rat did to him, but he won't listen to reason!"

Uzziel chewed her lower lip. "Satan summoned a strange, glowing scroll and forced Samael to sign it. I don't know what it did, but Samael lost control at that moment. There's evil magic at work, sister. Let us work together to subdue him. Don't blame Samael for his actions!"

"I banish such thoughts," Uriel replied, ice in her voice. "Conserve thy energy, Uzziel. Thy body lay unconscious for a frighteningly long time. Leave this battle to me!"

"I'll be fine. We both have to save our family!" Uzziel insisted.

Uriel nodded, but a grim expression spread across her face. "I won't lose a brother or sister. Not today."

Together, the two of them stood up straight and faced their opponent. Samael, well over a football field away, lunged his invisible limbs out. Camael dived to the side as the ground exploded behind her. The impacts of his vectors left bowling-ball-sized craters in their wake. Michael started to bat another rock at superspeed toward Samael, but a Vector caught him off guard and pounded Michael backward, stunning the Archangel. Michael rolled and spun as he tumbled a hundred feet away. When he came to a stop, it took several seconds for him to pull himself up. He was fatigued beyond belief.

"Dammit, Samael," Gabriel said, his voice listless. "The Creator would weep if he witnessed thee in this state."

Samael didn't reply. He only maintained his relentless onslaught and swiped at the ground, sending a hail of rocks at Gabriel. The Archangel of Power quickly raised his arms to shield his face, but in that instant, he revealed his chest as a blind spot. Samael charged at Gabriel and hit him five times at once, slamming his brother deep into the dirt. Gabriel cried out in pain, but a volley of blows silenced him.

Jason's heart sank. Only Camael, Raphael, Uriel, and Uzziel were left standing. Camael motioned with her hands, and a tiny metal orb, barely the size of a marble, materialized before her face. It hovered for a moment, then fell into her outstretched palm. When she touched it, it expanded and encased her gauntlet in iron, then transformed into a glowing shield.

Samael turned to look at her. The moment he made eye contact, the shield unleashed a ray of blinding light. The chimera screamed in pain and recoiled, clawing at his eyes in agony. Camael pounced toward him, greathammer raised, and drove it into his head, smashing him into the ground. All of Heaven quaked from the impact, nearly jarring Uzziel off her feet.

"Now!" Uriel yelled. "Assist Camael!"

Jason had no idea how Uzziel's powers worked, but she did. Uzziel weaved several signs, and after a few seconds, thick roots burst from the ground and wrapped around Samael, pinning the chimera in place. At the same time, Uriel summoned four servants. The first two resembled angels, while the others consisted of an orb and a cube levitating in the air.

The cube rapidly oscillated, and invisible forces pressed against Samael, holding him tightly. For some reason, instead of taking the opportunity to attack him while he was defenseless, Camael flashed her gaze toward Uriel, nodded, and leaped backward, flapping her wings to put as much distance between herself and the chimera as possible.

Jason found out why only a few seconds later. Uriel's third servant, the orb, began vibrating and spinning like the cube. A volley of rock and flame burst out of its top and raced toward the helpless chimera like a prehistoric artillery strike. Samael's _aaiiyee_ of agony when they struck unnerved Jason, reminding him of someone kicking a wounded dog.

Smoke billowed out from the impact point. Uzziel stared, wide-eyed. "By the Creator! Is... is he..."

Uriel tensed up. "He's still alive. And angry."

A sound like firecrackers exploded in the smoke. Samael clambered to his feet, tore apart the roots with his Vectors, and rushed toward them. "**GROOOOAARR!!**"

Jason's heart stopped. Seeing a thirty-foot-tall monster charging him scared him shitless. Luckily, Uzziel retained her senses. She motioned with her hands and a hundred-foot-tall tree sprouted between Samael and her. Its branches exploded outward, transforming into a spiderweb of green and brown, creating a wooden barrier between the two of them.

Jason almost breathed a sigh of relief. At least, until Samael _jumped_.

The tree presented no obstacle for him. The bear-lion-wolf hybrid animal leaped into the sky, cleared the obstacle, and dived toward Uzziel.

_Death,_ Jason thought.

The word flashed through his mind as the unstoppable killing machine speeded toward Uzziel's body. She stood, helpless, and waited for her end to come.

Then, unexpectedly, Samael's rapid descent came to a screeching halt. Like a bug hitting a windshield, the chimera awkwardly slammed against a faintly visible barrier above. His body contorted, twisted, and flipped as a look of confusion passed over his face.

Uriel grabbed Uzziel's shoulder. "Sister, do not hesitate! My barrier caught Samael off-guard! Grab him before he recovers!"

Nodding, Uzziel weaved more magic signs. This time, vines erupted from the ground, entangled Samael's limbs, and yanked him down the side of the magical barrier. When he hit the ground, more of them sprouted.

Uzziel dashed over to him and hopped into the air. She motioned again, causing a bed of purple and green flowers to form around Samael. Jason and Uzziel landed atop his hairy hide. A faint shimmer in the air revealed several vectors rushing toward them, but Uzziel pressed her palm against his chest.

Vines erupted from Uzziel's grasp and joined the others on Samael's body. His vectors grabbed her and tried to fling her away, nearly tearing her arm off, but still, she held steady, wincing from the pain.

_Wham! Bam!_

Over and over, invisible fists bashed Jason around, hitting him in the face, stomach, back, and every other part of his body. Samael couldn't pull Uzziel off, but he certainly tried. _I'm going to die!!_ Jason thought.

Uzziel coughed out something, but Jason couldn't make out what she said. A moment later, every flower around Samael exploded.

A cloud of gas rushed into the air. Jason's eyes began to droop. Sleep took him.

Oddly, as Jason's eyes grew heavy, Samael's attacks ceased. The chimera monster coughed and wheezed. Uzziel fell against his fur and gasped as the breath was knocked out of her.

But soon... after not many seconds, Jason began to drift off to sleep.

.......................................................

A dark void appeared. Jason reached out to touch it, only for someone standing beside him to do the same — a man.

Samael stood five feet away. Jason turned to look at him, blushing at his full-frontal nudity. His golden mask wasn't present, nor was anything else to protect his dignity.

"Thank Heaven that thou finally stopped me," Samael said quietly.

"I only did what I could, brother," Uzziel said, her voice soft.

Samael's eyes glistened. A tear ran down his cheek. "I... I apologize, sister. I couldn't... couldn't protect thee. The demon, Satan... he used thee as a hostage, and I failed to see through his ruse. I serve him now, unwillingly. I cannot refuse his orders."

"Thy soul is mighty. If thou should put effort toward resisting him—"

"No." Samael cut her off. "Satan's magic isn't like ours. I feel it festering in my blood. My mind. My soul. A monstrous claw clutches my heart. For every second that passes, it squeezes tighter and tighter. I cannot wrench free."

Uzziel stared longingly at Samael. "A curse, then? Surely, there must be a way to free thee from his clutches."

"Perhaps. Perhaps... not..."

Samael turned away. More tears began to flow. "Everything hurts. I stand at death's door, little sister. Satan's last order was to fight, and so I had no choice but to obey. I fought transformed for far longer than I should have. I haven't any mana left to spare."

"What?! No!" Uzziel stepped toward Samael and grabbed his hand, then pressed it against her heart. "I'm here! Take my mana, quickly! I won't allow thy spirit to fade! Thou must live on! Thy family—"

Samael reached over and placed a finger on her lips. He shook his head. "I can't go on like this. I failed thee, Uzziel. I hurt my brothers and sisters. Whether by accident or incompetence, I brought death and sorrow to Heaven. The imps are still out there. If I survive, they will surely turn me against thee again. I... I cannot allow that to happen."

Uzziel's eyes began to blur. Tears flowed freely. "D-don't say such things. Nobody blames thee for thy actions. We know 'twas Satan's doing."

Samael pulled his hand off her chest. He took a step back, putting distance between them. "'Tis my right to choose, Uzziel. 'Tis thine to try to stop me."

He looked away. "I won't trade thy life for mine. Live on, Uzziel. Forget not what the demons did to us. Punish them for their sins."

The Archangel's body wavered for a moment, like smoke spinning in the breeze. He lowered his gaze to his hand. It began to evaporate into dust, carried away by a silent wind.

"Samael! No! NO!!" Uzziel dashed toward him and tried to grab hold, but her arms passed through his body, scattering his remnants into the breeze.

"Farewell... sister..."

Samael's voice faded away. Uzziel swept her arms around, trying desperately to reform him through sheer willpower alone.

She failed.

"SAMAEL!! BROTHER!! UWAAAHH!!"

The heartbroken woman fell to her knees and wrapped her arms around herself, sobbing and choking with every breath.

"Creator, please... don't take him... don't take him from us..."

.....................................................................

A current of electricity coursed through Jason's body, jolting him awake. Uzziel sat upright and found herself in a shallow crater. Grass and flowers lined the edges. Samael's body lay motionless beside her.

Uzziel turned to stare at him. This time, no tears formed. The emptiness in her chest brought an ashen taste to Jason's mouth.

"Brother..."

A single word was all she managed.

Footsteps drew near. Uriel rushed into view at the crater's edge. "Uzziel! Uzziel! Art thou alright? Is Samael—"

She froze mid-sentence. Her eyes pivoted to her brother's motionless form. His angelic body, normally a softly glowing shade of yellow, lay cold and grey, lifeless in the dirt.

"No... it... it can't be..."

Uriel eased into the crater. She slumped to her knees beside Uzziel, and the two of them embraced.

Her cold, calculating nature melted away for a few minutes. Uriel tried desperately to suppress her anguish, but eventually, she succumbed to grief and wept in Uzziel's arms.

"Samael... I can't... I don't want to believe..."

Raphael and Camael appeared at the top of the crater, Michael suspended between them. The beaten and battered Archangel of Courage hung limp, barely lucid. Even so, all three of them spotted Samael's corpse. His dull, colorless body echoed those of the hundreds of cherubs scattered nearby.

Camael closed her eyes. She muttered something under her breath, possibly a prayer for Samael. When Camael opened her eyes, she and Raphael lowered Michael onto his back at the top of the crater, then they descended into the pit.

Raphael stood over Samael and stroked his beard. "Today is a dark day for our people," he muttered. "Another brother, gone. Unforgivable."

Camael crossed her arms. "Long have I seen visions of a murky future. My ominous premonitions were once little more than a vague warning in the back of my mind, but now they have coalesced into something more tangible. There is a threat out there... and it lives among us."

Raphael closed his eyes. "We must purge the imps. Immediately. Those in Heaven, as well as those outside it."

Uzziel's eyes bolted open. "Th-thou art... thou art going to... to kill—?"

Uriel pulled away from Uzziel. She sniffled and wiped her eyes. "Uzziel, p-please. Thy compassion goes too far. The imps... the imps killed Samael. We must wipe them out at once. At once!"

"'Tis as Uriel states," Camael said. "The imps are now our sworn enemies. We shan't go another day without avenging the loss of our brother."

Camael kneeled beside Samael. Her expression, usually stern and callous, softened for a moment. She reached out and touched his bear-like shoulder, then pulled away. Rage boiled in her eyes.

"Killing one of us would be terrible enough... but the imps made us fight our brother. They forced us to kill him ourselves. How cruel. They haven't a shred of empathy in their entire bodies. After we kill those in Heaven, we must set out and scour Earth for their lair," Camael said.

A voice boomed from outside the pit. "We cannot, sister. No matter how much thou dost wish to do so."

Gabriel appeared at the edge of the crater. His face, black and blue, was misshapen and deformed. Several patches of skin were missing entirely. His chest was covered in scars and bruises. The Archangel of Power flopped onto his back at the crater's edge and wheezes.

"Haah... the Titans hold jurisdiction over Earth. We must seek their permission before we can enter," Gabriel said.

"Their permission can lick the shiny side of my boot," Uriel growled. She wiped away the last remnants of tears from her eyes and staggered to her feet. "We have a blood debt to settle. If those Titans dare get in our way—"

"We will do nothing," Raphael said. "Calm thyself, Uriel. We barely held our own against Samael. We haven't any strength to spare against the Titans. Above all else, we must appear strong. If the Titans sense any weakness from us... I fear the actions they might take."

Raphael's words cast a pale gloom over those present.

Quietly, Uzziel asked, "If we kill the imps, how shall we regain our mana? I barely have the spiritual stores to sustain myself. Creating new life to give us mana will prove impossible."

Camael licked her lips. "Unfortunate. We'll have to think of something. In the meantime, I shall return soon."

She turned away, jumped into the air, and flew into the distance.

Raphael clicked his tongue. "If we are to search for the missing imps, then 'tis quite fortunate we captured two of them during the battle. We must interrogate them and learn where they live."

From the ridge above, Michael's voice croaked out. "Ha... haha... hahaha... l-leave it... to me. I shall... cut them open. No secrets shall... escape my grasp. They will pay for what they... what they did to Samael."

Uzziel clambered to her feet and shakily flew up to Michael. Despite the wounds covering his body, he slowly regenerated the worst-hit spots here and there.

"Michael, don't torture them. Please," Uzziel said.

Michael stared at her stonily. "Stay away when... when I speak to them... Uzziel. Their screams will haunt thy worst nightmares."

Until now, Uzziel had done everything in her power to protect the imps. However, Michael's threat hung in the air for only a moment before she turned away.

"A-alright. Do what ye must. Find the rest of their kind," Uzziel said.

Michael slowly raised a hand and caressed Uzziel's arm. "I shall, little... little sister."

He smiled at her.

Uzziel didn't smile back.

Uzziel's thoughts formed in Jason's mind.

_It's all my fault. I loosed the imps from Heaven. Samael died because he was protecting me. It's all... my fault,_ Uzziel thought.

_The poor girl,_ Jason thought.
