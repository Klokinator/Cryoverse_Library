The whirrs and clicks of machinery around them, while irritating at first, were easy enough to ignore once Jason tuned them out. Hans' laboratory served as the forefront of humanity's research and development, allowing the species to improve and iterate on their technological progress, thus elevating humanity by leaps and bounds. He was on a well-earned vacation today, allowing Phoebe and Jason to mess around with his newest creation.

Phoebe adjusted several leathery straps attached to the gizmo on her chest. "The exosuit's modulator needs some adjustments. It pushes against my breasts and hurts my ribs. A man could wear this comfortably, but women will need a differently-shaped model."

Jason took a step back and examined the new armor system. The device itself was simple in its design. It consisted of a circular metal chassis, small enough to fit in his palm, with two leather straps sticking out of its sides. The straps were designed to wrap across the wearer's chest in an 'X' shape, then loop behind the wearer's shoulders, where they connected at the spine.

Jason called it the Transforming Replicant Exosuit, or _T-REX_ for short. Phoebe thought it was a silly nickname, but Jason could be very persuasive when the situation was urgent.

"It'll have to do, for now, Phoebe. Test the nano-assemblers to see if they work properly."

Phoebe pressed the button on her T-REX three times. The device engaged, and immediately, liquid metal began pouring out of the chestpiece. It wrapped around the straps fastening the T-REX to her body, then flooded across her arms and down her legs. Moments later, the nanites hardened into an ultra-light compound resembling Kevlar but more durable than steel.

The device finished building her armor within six seconds. Phoebe flexed and spun around. Jason couldn't see her face behind the helmet covering her head, but he imagined she was grinning hugely. "Wow! I can barely even feel it. Hans is amazing."

"You helped him, didn't you?"

"Mhm. Still, Hans did most of the work. Even without the Crown, he's incredible."

"German engineering is impressive like that."

After a minute, Phoebe turned to face Jason directly. "Hit me."

"I beg your pardon?"

"Punch me. Right in the chest."

Jason raised an eyebrow. "Uhh, I'd rather not."

"Why not? Scared you'll break your fingers?"

"No. I'm worried I'll hurt you."

Phoebe waved her hand flippantly. "Jason, the point of this new armor is that it should allow us to fight on equal terms with demons. If one punch from a man can hurt me, the armor is useless."

Jason considered her point.

"Alright. Here goes. Brace yourself."

Jason reared back and paused for all of a second. Phoebe took a wide stance and centered herself. Then, Jason punched as hard as he could.

_CRACK!_

Jason shattered every bone in his hand.

"Ahhhh! Fuck, shit, ass, bitch-tits! GRAAAHHH!!"

Jason doubled over and whimpered like a baby for a few moments before quickly healing himself. He raised his head to look at Phoebe. She was unharmed.

"Are you okay, Jason?"

"Y-yeah. I'm fine. I healed the damage. Did that hurt you?"

"Nope. The armor has shock-absorbing technology. It can invert a certain amount of force applied to the nanites and expel an equal and opposite amount from the rear. Essentially, when you punched me, the kinetic energy passed through me and exited the rear of the suit."

"That was a lot of technical wording. I'm the one wearing the Crown right now, aren't I?"

"Haha! Ass!" Phoebe slugged Jason's arm lightly, but the suit enhanced her punch and turned it into a piledriver that sent him hurtling to the side.

Jason slammed onto his other arm and yelped in surprise. "Gahh! Dammit, Phoebe! Warn me!"

Phoebe pressed several invisible buttons in the air that only she could see, and the suit melted away. It retracted inside the shell after a few seconds. Phoebe smiled. "Sorry. I forget how it enhances my strength. I feel like a superhuman when I'm wearing it!"

Jason healed himself again and jumped to his feet. "That's because you _are_ a superhuman. You're like Supergirl or something."

"Who?"

"She's a character from a comic book."

Phoebe detached the armor piece and handed it to Jason. "I see. Alright, now it's your turn."

"Really? Don't you need to calibrate it or something?"

"Nope. The T-REX is a one-size-fits-all armor. That's part of the appeal."

"Nice."

Jason stuck his arms through the leather straps and adjusted the circular piece across the center of his chest, while Phoebe clipped the whole thing together behind him. Jason glanced down at the conspicuous red button on the T-REX. "I'm supposed to push this three times to activate it, right?"

"Mhm. Safety feature. We wouldn't want people activating it by accident."

"Wouldn't that make them extra safe if they did?"

"Oh, you."

Jason shrugged and pushed the button thrice with his thumb. A second passed. Suddenly a sensation like warm water poured over his body. The nanites rushed across him, making him gasp involuntarily. The first two seconds were uncomfortable, but the last three were terrifying. Once the nanobots started crawling up his face and covering his mouth and nose, claustrophobia took over. Jason stumbled to his knees and gasped like a madman.

The moment passed. After the suit hardened into its metal form, the pure darkness in front of his face vanished. The helmet turned transparent, allowing Jason to see a full 180 degrees in front of him. Based on what he saw when Phoebe used the armor, the helmet's outside looked like a solid metal plate, but on the inside, it was as if there was nothing there. Jason couldn't even tell he was wearing a helmet.

A male voice, filled with power, spoke to him. His timbre was deep and gruff. "**WELCOME, PILOT ZERO-ZERO-THREE. I AM CENTURION, YOUR ONBOARD NEOMECHANICAL ASSISTANT. STATE YOUR NAME.**"

"Jason Hiro."

"**HELLO, JASON HIRO. I CAN DEBRIEF YOU ON THE ADVANCED FEATURES OF THE TRANSFORMING REPLICANT EXOSUIT. DO YOU WISH TO HEAR THEM?**"

"No, not right now. Can Phoebe hear me?"

"**PILOT ZERO-ZERO-TWO, PHOEBE BERTHOLD, CANNOT DETECT YOUR VOICE. I WILL ENABLE AUDIO TRANSPARENCY. ENABLED. OMNIDIRECTIONAL AUDIO COMMUNICATION NOW AVAILABLE.**"

Phoebe waved. "How is it, Jason? I can't see your face."

"It's cool as fuck! This suit is the dopest thing I've ever seen in my life. It's like a hundred different movies I used to watch."

As Jason spoke, the suit began drawing circles across various parts of Phoebe's body. Her heartbeat appeared, along with a lie-detector algorithm and several other sensory readings.

Jason frowned. One of the readings concerned him.

"Hey, Phoebe, the suit shows that your estrogen levels are 250% higher than normal. That doesn't seem-"

Jason's voice caught. _No way._ Jason thought.

Jason stared at a bunch of other readings, and lowered his head to look toward her stomach.

"Jason? What's wrong?"

Jason's jaw dropped. "I... I don't believe it. Ph-Phoebe! You're pregnant! Holy shit!"

"What? I am?!" Phoebe's eyes shot open. She followed Jason's gaze to her womb and patted herself repeatedly. "That's fantastic! Wow!! We're going to have a child!"

Jason looked to the top right of the suit's visual indicators and spotted a series of buttons. Reaching up, he tapped on several of them and turned the suit off.

"**CENTURION REVERTING TO OFFLINE MODE. GOODBYE, PILOT ZERO-ZERO-THREE.**"

The suit melted away, returning Jason to normal. The moment it disappeared, he grabbed Phoebe and hugged her hard. "I'm so happy, Phoebe! Wow! And it only took two nights!"

Phoebe turned beet red. "I was hoping we could spend more time as a couple before a baby showed up, but the Creator works in mysterious ways. Do you think it'll be a boy or a girl?"

Jason smiled like an idiot. "I don't even care. If it's a boy, I'll make him a man. If it's a girl, she'll be as beautiful as you."

After a moment, Phoebe pulled away. Her smile lessened. "Jason, um, I have a question."

"What?"

"Well, our child. You have special powers. You're the Hero. Will the baby have powers too?"

"I don't believe he or she will. I'm not one hundred percent on how the Heroic Aura works, but I think I have to die before it transfers to someone else. That's what the Angels told me. They also said that when the angels went extinct, it eliminated the Heroic Aura's transference. That means I'm the last Hero, and will always be."

Phoebe wiggled her nose. "Well, you and Hope."

"Right, yeah. Him, too. I can always ask the angels for clarification if you like."

Phoebe smiled again. "Eh, whatever. It's wonderful news either way. Hey, speaking of Hope, why not tell him about the baby? We can also show Neil the prototype armor. He'll be glad to see how well it works."

Jason nodded. "Good idea. I'm interested to see what Neil and Hope have been up to over the last few days."

Phoebe looped her arm through Jason's, and they walked toward the door. "What should we name our child? We should think of names for both boys and girls."

"I could use my Wordsmithing to find out the gender."

"Jason! Nooo! That spoils the fun!"

"I know, I know. I'm kidding. How about something like Timothy or Sarah?"

Phoebe cocked her head. "Too plain. Something involving nature would be better."

"What, like _Treebeard_?"

"Jason!"

"Nah, I took that one already."

Phoebe nestled her head against Jason's shoulder. "Oh, you."

Two hours later, Phoebe and Jason drove into the Core, along with dozens of new blaster rifles in her truck's bed they replicated over the last few days. She waved at a few people standing nearby. "Special delivery! I bring gifts. New guns!"

The men nearby would usually smile at her, but today they didn't. They simply nodded. "Okay. Thanks, Phoebe."

Phoebe climbed out of her truck, as did Jason. "Mark? Henry? What's wrong?"

Henry kept his expression solemn. "Sorry, Miss Berthold. Maybe you haven't heard yet. Neil and Hope went to liberate Maiura, but there were fifty thousand orcs and a Demon Lord waiting in the capital city, Meldora. We managed to capture the Lord, but he later escaped and killed over a hundred soldiers."

"Oh my god." Phoebe covered her mouth. "That's... that's horrible."

Mark, a young man only three years older than Henry, scratched his scalp. "The demon killed Paimon, too. We're lucky the succubus showed up-"

"Paimon?" Jason interrupted him and stormed around the side of the truck. "No, no, you're joking! Isn't he the demon I spoke to after that batch of Lords and their minions assaulted the Core a few months ago?"

Mark and Henry glanced at each other. Henry shrugged. "Don't know, Mister Hiro, sir. I only met Paimon two days ago. Barely said five words to him."

"Shit." Jason looked away into the distance, remembering his talk with the demon. _It took over an hour to convince him that humans weren't the pathetic weaklings he thought we were. Now he's dead. What the hell?!_ Jason thought.

Phoebe patted Henry on the shoulder. "Thank you for letting me know. By the way, you mentioned a succubus...?"

"Mhm. Samantha, milady. The same demoness who healed us and saved the Core from Beelzebub."

Phoebe sighed audibly. "Oh, thank the Heavens. I was worried about her."

Jason spoke a word to the wind. "**Locate.**" His mind barely traveled a few hundred feet past all the rows of servers to spot Hope staring at a row of bodies. His expression was grim.

Jason returned to his body and grabbed Phoebe's hand. "Henry, Mark, thanks for the report. My fiancée and I need to speak to the Commander and my clone. Excuse us."

"No problem, sir. Have a good day."

...

A few minutes later, Phoebe and Jason walked up to Hope. He and Neil stood and stared at the rows of bodies.

Neil shook his head. "-a good job back there, Hope. Don't beat yourself up. As I said, some casualties are unavoidable."

Hope remained unconvinced about whatever the Commander was saying. "I could have done better. I did my best, but it wasn't good enough. I didn't-"

"Hope?" Phoebe's cautious voice made Jason's clone pause. He turned to look at her and Jason. "Oh. Phoebe, Jason. Haven't seen you two in a few days."

"Sorry," Jason said. "We've been busy. I, uh... I guess you were, too, huh?"

Neil jumped in before Hope could reply. "As I was telling your doppelganger, casualties are an expected part of the war effort. However, there's no need to worry. Hope managed to pacify fifty thousand orcs all by himself. Also, Phoebe, you were right about that demoness. She saved our skin, and mine specifically, a second time. I misjudged her."

Phoebe waved her hand. "It's no big deal, Neil. Don't worry about it. It's only natural you'd be worried about a Demon Emperor in our ranks."

Neil glanced at Hope for a moment, then back to Phoebe. "About that. I spoke with Samantha after she saved us. She was happy to provide me with Intel regarding the state of demonkind. Jason, we've been dealt a favorable hand. You won't believe this, but the Third Emperor, Diablo, was defeated by Mephisto. Additionally, the Duke of Reptiles, Agares, died during the battle against the Black Witch. Their ranks are crippled, and the demons are undergoing a power struggle to determine the new noble class."

Phoebe shook her head. "I don't understand. How is that favorable for us?"

"The demons won't attack the Core again until they repair their power structure," Hope replied. "They have a strict hierarchy, and they'll freeze their military operations until they solve it."

"Alright," Jason said. "Then you'll love what I'm going to show you today."

Jason pointed at the circular imprint underneath his shirt. Neil and Hope looked at it and raised their eyebrows. "What's that?"

"It's the new armor system Phoebe and Hans developed. It should allow ordinary humans to go toe-to-toe with demons. Well, at the Lord rank and below. I don't know how it will fare against a Baron, but when facing lower-ranked demons, it'll put us on equal footing."

Neil pursed his lips. "How unfortunate we didn't have it on our troops yesterday. We could have saved a hundred lives. Better late than never, I suppose. Care to demonstrate?"

"Sure. **Give.**"

With a single word, Jason made the T-REX on his chest disappear and reappear atop Neil's uniform. The Commander took a moment to notice the weight on his chest and gasped. "My god! That startled me!"

"Sorry. I figured it was faster. Press the button in the middle three times to activate the armor."

Hope raised an eyebrow. "What do you mean, 'to activate it'?"

"It's a nanite-based liquid metal compound. Think like Wolverine's Adamantium."

Hope nodded. "Or like Colossus?"

"Hell yeah." Jason smiled, glad that at least _someone_ around there could understand his references. He never noticed before, but he was starving for a fellow connoisseur of pop culture from his time. Did it count if the only person who got his jokes and nodded was his clone?

_Oh, god. Sometimes I realize how lame I am and it hurts._ Jason thought.

Hesitantly, Neil pressed the button thrice. His eyes bolted open as the metal began crawling across his body like a million angry bees. "W-what? What is this?! Ahh! It's all over me! It's going up my-!"

As it covered Neil's face, his voice cut off. The Commander scrabbled at his face and clawed furiously for a moment, before settling down. His body turned relaxed. A minute later, he spoke out loud. "Hello? Hello? Can you hear me?"

Hope nodded. "Yup. Can't see your face, though. Jason, what's the point of a solid metal helmet?"

"It's like a one-way mirror on the inside, Hope. It transfers oxygen, purifies contaminants in the air, and has a screen that displays the entire world outside the helmet."

Jason's clone crossed his arms. "Okay, sure, but it looks ugly as hell. He's wearing a tin can on his head. Can't you do something about the aesthetics?"

"We could," Phoebe said. "But look at it this way. The best method we have to keep demons at bay is to frighten them. When our faceless warriors start taking them down a peg, they'll start to associate us with fear. Demons respect power. If anything, the anonymous nature of the suit may allow us to recruit more demons and monsters who wish to live under us."

Neil turned away and held his hand up, examining the top of it. "Incredible. I can zoom to the molecular level. This suit has many practical applications outside of combat. It'll help our surgeons too."

Phoebe held up a hand. "Oh, about that. I couldn't bring it along, but Hans is developing a second prototype. It's a skin-patch that can detect when the user is under stress. The problem is, it's a single-use object, but it can heal most wounds, or at least stop the bleeding for a medic to help them. I'm looking into other practical applications for our battlefield medics as well."

Hope's jaw dropped. "Wow, Phoebe. You're incredible. I remember when you could barely understand concepts from my time, and now you sound like a scientist who's spent her whole life researching!"

"I'm nobody remarkable," Phoebe said, blushing from his praise. "I had the Crown for a while, and it helped me learn and understand concepts quickly. Without it, I'd be lost."

Hope raised his eyes to Solomon's Crown on Jason's head. "Err, speaking of which... Jason, I don't want to sound like a beggar or anything, but am I ever going to get to use it? I know I'm just a lowly clone, but-"

Jason's face paled. "Don't say that, Hope. You're more than a clone. I look at you like a twin brother, you know? The problem is, I tried, but I couldn't copy the Crown. We only have this one. I want to let Phoebe and Hans use it where possible, and I need it to visualize infrastructure while Wordsmithing in Hero City."

A few moments passed. Hope lowered his eyes. "I understand. I'll make do without it, I suppose."

"I'm sorry. Look, if you want to borrow it sometime-"

Hope raised his palm. "No, I'm sure you'll do fine. I was just being greedy, that's all. It doesn't matter who has the Crown, as long as they use it for good, right? That's what matters."

"Yeah. E-exactly."

An awkward silence followed. Neil coughed. "Ahem, Jason, how do I get this suit off? I'd rather someone else have it than me. It should go to our best fighter."

"We're going to mass-produce the suit, Neil. Everyone, including you, will have one. But uh, if you want to remove it..."

Jason spent a few minutes explaining to the Commander how to press buttons inside the display, and while it took him much longer than it had Phoebe or Jason, he eventually figured out the correct commands. Sometimes Jason forgot how few people in the Core understood technology.

Phoebe nudged Jason. "Jason, now's a good time to tell them."

"Tell them?" Jason blanked out for a moment. "Oh! Right!"

Neil unwrapped the T-REX straps from around his body and handed it to Hope. "Tell us what?"

While Hope started to coil the straps around his back, Phoebe smiled wide like the Euphrates River. "I'm pregnant! Jason and I are going to have a baby!"

Hope froze. Anxiety appeared on his face. He smiled, but Jason immediately sensed his distress. "Y-you are? That's... that's good! Great! I'm glad for you!"

Phoebe's excitement lessened. "It's everything I've wanted. Hope, you're not mad, are you?"

Jason's clone lowered his head. "Haah. I'm not mad. It's just... well, I don't know how to explain my feelings, here. I see Jason going around and doing things I used to do, but I can't be a part of those activities anymore. You and Jason love each other, but what about me? I still... I still have feelings for you, Phoebe."

Phoebe and Jason stared at Jason's clone for several moments. Her happiness vanished entirely. "I'm sorry, Hope. I didn't think about that. I should have been more considerate."

"Err, Phoebe..." Jason swallowed, realizing how awful what he was about to say would sound. "H-how about... why don't I clone you? Then Hope won't be alone. He's right. It isn't fair he had to give you up, but I didn't."

Hope raised his head. He met Phoebe's gaze. Both of them spoke to each other with only their eyes.

After a minute, Hope closed his eyes. "No. That isn't right. I know what was on your mind when you made me, Jason. You wanted another Wordsmith to help protect the Core. That's my job. I have to make sacrifices."

Phoebe pressed her palms together and twisted. "Nnn, I don't know how I'd feel about having another of me running around. Still, I can't be selfish. If you want to clone me, Hope, I'll understand."

Hope opened his eyes and smiled. "Really? You'd do that for me?"

Jason quickly interjected. "Hold on. Won't Phoebe's clone be pregnant if we do it now?"

Immediately, Hope's smile vanished. He looked at Neil. "Pregnant with Jason's child. That complicates things."

"Technically, we're more identical than twins," Jason replied. "It'll be your child too."

"I don't know how I feel about that. Never mind. Forget it." Hope waved his hand flippantly. "I'll get a new girlfriend eventually. Don't make any other clones if you don't have to."

Jason nodded. "Okay. That's for the best."

After a moment, Neil and Hope politely laughed. The Commander asked, "If I may be so bold, when is the wedding?"

Phoebe smiled. "Next week. It'll be a private meeting. I'm only inviting our close friends. I was thinking we could get married out near Hero City's forest."

Hope chuckled. "Haha. Sounds fun. I'll attend. You'd better make me the best man, though."

"Of course," Jason replied. "Who else?"

The four of them shared a laugh, and when Phoebe and Jason left, she pointed at someone speaking to Kar. "Look, Jason! Samantha's here."

Jason glanced back at Hope and Neil. They'd already returned to examining the bodies.

"Let's say hi, Phoebe. I'm sure _she'll_ be excited to hear the good news."
