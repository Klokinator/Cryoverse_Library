Part 749: I'm His Envoy

_Far-Future Era. Day 20, AJR. Chrona._

Timothy slept deeply for six hours. He woke up with a start, only to find that everyone else was awake and either meditating or sitting quietly. His heart momentarily racing, Timothy sat up and looked around.

"Welcome back to the world of the wakeful." Paimon said quietly.

He was lightly teasing Timothy, but Timothy didn't even register the joking tone. He patted his chest and looked around with trembling eyes.

"Hm? Timmy? Something wrong?" Marigold asked, picking up on his odd behavior.

"I fell asleep..." Timothy said.

"Yes. You were quite tired, after a long day running from enemies." Paimon said.

"No. I mean, I fell asleep _way_ too easily." Timothy said. He rested his back against the tree and shivered. "I always toss and turn for at least thirty minutes, and that's in a comfy bed. How the hell did I fall asleep so quickly in a situation like this, with danger surrounding us, and also being way up in a tree? I don't exactly have a fear of heights, but I'm not _not_ afraid of heights. I should have been at least a little nervous."

Marigold shrugged. "It sounds like you're really underestimating how tired you were to me."

"Yeah?" Timothy challenged. "And you fell asleep not long after we entered the forest even though you really shouldn't have been tired at all. Don't you find _that_ a bit strange?"

Paimon and Raavul looked over at the other three. Raavul slightly frowned.

[Now that you mention it, that does seem somewhat... suspicious.] Raavul said. She didn't elaborate.

"Marigold, did you sleep at all?" Timothy asked. "While I was out, I mean."

Marigold looked miffed. She felt as if Timothy were interrogating her again, bringing back her bad mood from earlier. "I wasn't sleepy! I stayed up and kept watch with the others! Jeez, no need to be such a jerk!"

"That's not... not what I'm saying..." Timothy said.

Timothy looked to the right. He looked to the left.

The forest was, as always, eerily silent. Their voices should have spread out quite far, but the Dark Forest seemed to consume noise, making it dissolve into the darkness within seconds.

Timothy shivered again. He rubbed his arms. "Something's not right. Raavul, Ferral, check the area! Are we alone? I feel like I'm being watched."

Raavul raised an eyebrow. Her many eyes and investigative psionics swept a 1000-meter zone around their tree. Her senses were more attuned than all of the others, and she could detect finer details at greater distances than even Ferral.

[Nothing. We're alone for a kilometer in every direction.] Raavul said.

Paimon activated his T-REX. He turned on its various scanning sensors and stood up, turning slowly to surveil every direction he could.

"Nothing." Paimon said. "Timothy... you're not going mad on us, are you?"

Timothy activated his T-REX. He tossed his bedding into his inventory and shakily stood up.

"I'm fucking serious, guys. I feel like I'm about to throw up here. There's something looking at me, and I don't know from what direction."

Timothy suddenly realized something. He activated his best skill, the Eye of Yredelemnul. He looked out into the distance and activated it once, but found nothing. Then he looked a little more to the right...

His heart stopped.

Five trees away, the Eye of Yredelemnul popped up outlines of three _somethings_ clinging stealthily to the sides of the tree. For a brief moment, Timothy locked gazes with an amber-eyed creature with vertical slit pupils.

"SHIT!" Timothy screamed inside his T-REX. "They're everywhere! We're surrounded! RUN!"

The others didn't immediately believe him, but the panic in Timothy's voice made Raavul and Ferral react instinctively. They summoned a shield of psionic energy around the group.

As if on cue, objects flew out of the trees from all directions. Javelins, rocks, other primitive projectiles slammed into the hastily formed shield with alarming power, rattling both Psion's bones and making them cry out in pain. It was as if thirty trolls had hammered down on their shield with giant clubs made of stone!

[Aaargh! What power!] Ferral cried.

Timothy continued to look around. The more he activated his Eye, the more he saw these unknown creatures surrounding them. Even at this moment, more were stealthily sneaking through the underbrush and climbing the trees. Their camouflage was so potent that the Psions still couldn't detect them, and neither could the T-REXes.

After the first barrage, a second round flew toward the group. Unable to determine exactly where the hidden creatures were, Raavul and Ferral had to blindly grab the others, yank them off the tree, and dive down toward the foliage below.

It was then that the Psions got their first look at the creatures, as they finally spotted a pair directly below the tree, looking up at the group of non-forest denizens falling toward them. They looked like goblins, but their skin was mottled and green. When they didn't move, they blended in perfectly with the foliage. The only way to spot them was when they moved, by following the trail of their ember-colored eyes.

Raavul and Ferral curved the fall of the group clumsily, but they succeeded in pulling up before they slammed everyone to their deaths against the forest floor. After pulling up, they raced forward, Paimon aiming his rifle around wildly, but unable to spot a single enemy.

Marigold also pulled out a rifle, one that was modified to better fit her larger claws. Her pupils, the eyes of a predator, narrowed to slits as she carefully looked around.

_KZAAT!_

A bright blue beam of electrical energy fired from the tip of her rifle. It was so bright it caused temporary blindness in anyone looking at Timothy's party from the direction Marigold was facing. Several of the goblin-like creatures screeched as their sensitive eyes conjured tears of pain.

The beam of blue energy struck one of the creatures and instantly lit it on fire, causing it to cry in a high-pitched voice. It leaped into the jungle foliage, spreading fire and flames as it ran.

At this point, Timothy had a brief moment to check the information he'd scanned from these creatures. He quickly looked at it.

...

**Dark Forest Stalker - Middle Mortal**

_A powerful species variant of the Goblins. These creatures possess potent camouflage, acute senses of sight, hearing, and smell, and can track their prey for tens of kilometers. Once they mass up in sufficient number, they will strike, leaving their enemy no time to react. Their strength and claws allow them to easily tear flesh from bone._

...

Timothy's pupils constricted. Middle Mortals, those were on the same level as Demon Dukes! Each one of these creatures was that strong?! His group didn't have a chance!

Even as they flew away, the Dark Forest Stalker Marigold had lit on fire hadn't actually died. It eventually put out the flames on its body and simply curled up on the ground, trembling in pain. Since it didn't die, she received no experience, gold or items.

She couldn't shoot it again, either. The Psions were flying the party away as fast as they could. With no other choice, Marigold simply took aim again and shot another bright blue beam of energy out, causing another Stalker to scream in agony. She shot two more times, but unfortunately missed, before shooting again and hitting a third target.

Frustratingly, no matter how many of the bastards she shot, the hardy little creatures didn't actually die. This caused her rage to build.

Timothy and Paimon weren't sitting on their laurels either. Timothy reached into his inventory and pulled out a couple of flashbang grenades. He threw them in different directions behind the party, and after several seconds, they exploded, stunning the Dark Forest Stalkers who were nearby. Their senses were far more acute than those of humans, so the feedback they received was also much more painful as well.

Paimon couldn't see the enemies at first, but his demonic vision, coupled with the T-REX night vision, eventually allowed him to spot the tiny glowing orbs of light that represented the eyes of the Dark Forest Stalkers. He started firing his rifle whenever he thought he saw movement. 

Surprisingly, Paimon's shots connected a few times. He wasn't able to kill the goblins, even with the astounding power of his AR-15, one of the most powerful weapons from Ancient Earth, but he was able to cause them a lot of pain and force them to retreat in a panic.

Still, the spears flung by the Stalkers continued to fly toward the party as they hovered forward at high speeds. The number of Stalkers in the area must have exceeded a thousand! Timothy had no idea how the group would have survived if they had waited on that branch any longer. It seemed the enemies had almost reached their target density and were planning to kill his party in one swift attack.

Minutes passed. A spear crashed into Marigold's body and rattled her bones, but her T-REX absorbed the worst of the impact. A deep gash was carved along her chest, making her roar in pain. The spear fell to the undergrowth below, and more nanites rushed in to fill up the gap. Her Spectre suit was not suited for defense, so the injury she suffered was worse than if she'd been wearing a Rhino or Thundercat variant.

Marigold gritted her teeth, her long jaw pressing together tightly as tears welled up in her eyes, along with anger. She fired more beams into the forest, lighting the area up behind the group as the Stalkers leaped from tree to tree to pursue Timothy's group.

Fires spread left and right. The forest started to burn again, and soon, even Marigold started to get some kills. Weaker creatures approached from the rear, attracted by the heat and light. Their dying screams produced EXP and gold for her, but she was too busy shooting enemies to notice.

Timothy opened his inventory. He blinked in surprise. "What the hell- where is it?!"

He scanned his inventory five times. The weapon he wanted... wasn't there! Had it been stolen?

"Where's the goddamn Sword of Justice??" Timothy cried out.

Had he stupidly forgotten it? Had he left it at home like a complete idiot?

An ominous premonition swallowed Timothy's mind. He checked his stat sheet, and his eyes turned bloodshot with rage.

All his stats had dropped by -1!

He hadn't even gotten to use the goddamn sword, and it had already broken, cursing him with -1 to all stats for 24 hours!

"Umi! What the hell, why did the sword break?! I didn't even kill any innocents with it!" Timothy complained.

Umi's tiny speck of light flickered into existence in front of Timothy's face.

"**This is why you are an inferior biological. The sword stated that if you killed an innocent, it would break. It did not say the sword would break if the sword itself killed an innocent. You haphazardly lit a forest on fire. Do you really think all the creatures that died were guilty of great sins?**"

Timothy's face fell. "Fuck! I don't know whether I'm more mad at you or more mad at myself for not realizing- goddammit! Looks like I have no choice!"

With the Sword of Justice broken, Timothy was a lot weaker now, and this meant his damage output had dropped considerably. With great reluctance, he pulled out the relatively ordinary Longsowrd he'd acquired from a Lootbox.

Timothy's best combat skill was Divine Blaze. He realized that in this particular situation, it would have terrific results in dealing with the enemies pursuing them.

Even though Timothy couldn't reach the Stalkers that were clearly out of melee range, he still shouted at the Psions. "Change the formation! Single file, wide-stance. Me in the middle, Paimon and Marigold on the sides!"

The Psions nodded. They didn't have a lot of attention to spare, what with their low Psionic levels, but they were able to change the formation from a diamond-shape to a line-shape. The Psions flew on the outside, with the three others lined up between them.

Like this, Timothy tapped into his mana. He could enchant his weapon with roaring flames at the cost of one mana per minute. He had enough mana to last 45 minutes if he cast no other spells.

And so, that was what he did. Timothy lit his sword on fire and started sweeping the forest below as they fled, deliberately lighting another massive blaze to slow down the pursuit of his enemies.

Screeches of rage came from behind. The Stalkers started to slow their speed as they continually came in contact with foliage and trees lit aflame. Their piercing shrieks, mixed with the scorching heat and bright light drew even more monsters toward the group.

Soon, Timothy heard a stomach curdling roar. A powerful creature, five meters tall, wielding a giant wooden club, rushed toward the Stalkers. It started grabbing the nearest ones up and stuffing them into its mouth while the Stalkers howled like terrified apes.

Whatever this creature was, it seemed to be the predator of the Stalkers. Not just one of them appeared, but five! The massive giants started roaring battle cries and grabbing at the Stalkers, spreading mass panic among their ranks. Before long, the Stalkers fled in all directions, no longer chasing Timothy's party.

[Douse your flames.] Raavul said. [Let us attempt to return to stealth.]

Timothy did as Raavul ordered, and Marigold also stopped firing her gun. Before long, the flames behind them turned into distant specks of light. The screams of Stalkers and other dying creatures faded away.

Timothy continued to accumulate kills, but the monsters that died were relatively weak and ordinary. They didn't give him much EXP.

Marigold was different. She also shot up in levels, eventually reaching Level 13! This was a huge increase, albeit not at Timothy's level. She clapped her claws excitedly and gave Timothy a toothy grin.

"It's not as fun as killing them myself, but leveling up is still leveling up!" Marigold crowed. She casually used a Full Heal to regenerate the injury she'd suffered from the spear.

The Psions tightened up the formation. They continued flying low to the ground, their expressions dark.

[That was close.] Ferral said. [If Timothy hadn't woken up, we might have all perished. Timothy, how did you spot those creatures when even Raavul could not?]

"Yredelemnul's Eye." Timothy said. "Those monsters were called Dark Forest Stalkers. They're a sort of super-goblin variant, or something. I don't know much about them, but apparently they were all as strong as Demon Dukes. That's why we basically couldn't kill them."

[How frightening.] Raavul said, her tone emotionless. [Demon Dukes are roughly as strong as 4th Level Psions. An army of a thousand... if they were to fall upon the Seven Hells, even the demons would have a difficult time killing them. And that group we faced was unlikely to be the full extent of their species.]

"If they only had a hundred thousand more among their ranks, I would be surprised." Paimon said, his tone deadpan. "There could be millions of those creatures crawling around this forest. We simply can't see them."

Timothy shuddered. He activated his Eye again and again, but he didn't spot any other Lurkers hiding in the darkness. Not for now, anyway.

As the group flew, Timothy looked at Marigold. Then he lowered his eyes and looked away.

"Hey... Marigold?"

"Yeah? What?" Marigold asked, crossing her arms. It was clear she was still angry.

"I owe you an apology." Timothy said. "It wasn't your fault you fell asleep. I realized it after I woke up. Those creatures... they must have cast a spell on us or something. After I set that fire the first time, I killed a lot of monsters. My stats were debuffed, and what happened to you happened to me. I fell asleep too. So... it wasn't your fault you passed out, and I shouldn't have jumped on you like that. I'm sorry."

Marigold looked at the side profile of Timothy's helmet. Her expression softened. "Oh... well, if it's like that... yeah. Um. I got pretty angry too. I'm sorry for lashing out at you too."

The wound wasn't healed, and trust would require time to rebuild, but it was a start. Timothy and Marigold both heaved sighs of relief. They felt a lot better after apologizing to one another, and a lot of the tension faded away.

The group continued to move.

Since the Psions were tireless, and since Timothy knew the Stalkers would be able to pursue them for tens of kilometers, this meant that laying down and sleeping wasn't really possible. Staying in one spot for too long could bring about another disaster. Enemies lurked everywhere, with the group frequently needing to change directions as they traveled.

"Hey, guys?" Timothy said, at one point on the second day of their expedition. "Which way are we going?"

[North.] Raavul answered.

"Right now? We're flying that way _right now_?" Timothy asked. "Because my T-REX's HUD says we're going west."

"Oh, huh, mine says north-west." Marigold added.

"...Mine says north." Paimon said, frowning.

[Mine says west.] Ferral said. [I hadn't actually looked at it in a while. I was following Raavul's lead.]

[Mine says north...] Raavul said. She glanced at Timothy. [Why do all our instruments show we are traveling different directions?]

"I... don't know..." Timothy answered. "Maybe the forest itself is messing with our sensors? Maybe it's enemy interference?"

A moment of silence followed.

[Timothy, don't you have an item that always points north?] Ferral asked.

Timothy blinked. "Oh, yeah! I almost forgot."

He reached into his inventory and retrieved an item.

**Tin Compass [Item] [Common] [Utility]**

_Points north. Always. Even underground, underwater, and in dimensions where north does not conventionally exist. In those cases, it points toward the nearest approximation of north, which is sometimes unsettling. Does not interact with any navigation skills. Cannot be improved further. It simply points north._

The group slowed their speed until they came to a stop. Timothy waited for a moment, and the needle eventually pointed in a certain direction.

"It's actually east??" Timothy asked. "We were all wrong! If the compass is correct, we're going almost entirely the wrong way!"

Paimon scowled. "If that's the case, we might have been going off-course for a long while. We still have a deadline to adhere to. We can only hope that if we reorient ourselves and get back on track, we'll reach the edge of the forest on time."

The group adjusted their T-REX's internal compasses, but Timothy didn't want to take a chance, so he kept the Tin Compass in his grip and periodically started checking it every few hours.

Time passed again. It was now the fourth day of the Dungeon expedition.

The group stumbled into many different encounters. They ended up having to fight a powerful beast similar to the ones that ate the Lurkers, and that fight took them an hour to win. Timothy ended up beaten and battered, and Marigold wasn't much better off. Ferral earned an entire level-up off the fight, bringing him to Level 8.

At one point, they accidentally found a massive city in their path, a city built in the treetops, glowing with divine radiant light. The creatures living there appeared to be members of the Ashenzari cult, so Timothy's party had to take a huge detour around the city so as to not be seen by potential enemies.

Timothy was almost tempted to see if they could enter the city. Didn't that Envoy recognize him as an ally? Maybe Ashenzari and Yredelemnul were actually quite close and Timothy could speed up the journey by passing through?

Naturally, Timothy did not follow up on this idea. There was no reason to potentially throw away everyone's lives for a nebulous reward.

Unfortunately, as the group circled around the great city, a bright light erupted from the darkness. It shone directly on Timothy's party, startling and blinding them. Even the Psions were not immune to the blinding effect. Raavul and Ferral both ground to a halt, lifting their hands to cover their eyes as a single figure strode forward, a brilliant staff held aloft in its hand. The figure pointed the staff of light toward the group.

"Eratti, vishtuul mata Yredelemnul." The bipedal creature said.

It lowered the staff, causing the light to dim. When Timothy's party regained their sight, they saw a powerful and imposing alien-looking creature with pale white skin, flared cheekbones, two pitch-black eyes, and a completely smooth head. The back of the creature's head was similar in shape to a squid, or perhaps an arrowhead. The creature's skin seemed slightly wet, as if it were native to an aquatic biome, not a forest. The creature wore a ceremonial dress, but the lack of armor did nothing to obscure the fact it was an extremely powerful life form.

When it spoke, Timothy immediately understood its words.

"Greetings, followers of Yredelemnul." The creature said, its voice somewhat feminine. "We have witnessed your arrival in the Dark Forest. We wish to invite you to our land."

Timothy's heart skipped a beat.

This was an idea he had already thought of, but one he had shaken off as irresponsible and dangerous.

Now, it seemed he had no choice but to go along with the creature's wishes.

Timothy shivered. Since he was the only one who could speak, he had to take the role of diplomat in this situation. He projected his voice outward, through the T-REX's exterior speakers.

"Ah, greetings. I am Timothy... Envoy of Yredelemnul." Timothy said, cringing as he spoke. He really hoped he hadn't just committed a grave faux pas by labeling himself an envoy. Maybe he should have said 'friend'? Ally? Servant? _Slave_??

"Envoy of Yredelemnul? Your power is lacking, but you carry a proper fragment of the Dark One inside you." The creature said. "My name is Sylphid. I am the 32nd Envoy of Ashenzari. What rank might you be?"

Timothy hesitated.

Sylphid could be lying. What if there were only one or two Envoys and this was a test to see if Timothy knew such basic information? What if there were hundreds, and claiming he were of a position too high might expose his ignorance?

Timothy had too little information to work with. In fact, he had basically nothing!

A fraction of a second passed. Timothy's eyes flashed as he quickly came to a decision. He puffed up his chest and snorted.

"You are quite presumptuous, Sylphid. I am a Secret Envoy of Yredelemnul. My status goes beyond the concept of 'ranks'."

His words came out fluently, in the exact same language as the one Sylphid spoke. Sylphid looked the armored young man up and down. It was clear even without seeing his face or body that Timothy was not a member of the species among the Dark Ones' home galaxy, but the Dark Ones had long extended their reach out to multiple galaxies. It was well within the realm of possibility that, among the thousands of species throughout all those galaxies, there was one which resembled Timothy.

The bigger question Sylphid faced was... who were the other unknown Sentients traveling along with the young man? Sylphid had not seen such a motley crew in her entire life. Especially the one with the extremely elongated skull. It looked wildly different from the others.

"A Secret Envoy? I have not heard of such a thing before." Sylphid said.

"Well. You have now." Timothy retorted, crossing his arms and assuming what he hoped was a heroic pose. "I possess a powerful fragment of my liege. It was given to me in preparation for his future plans. You are unworthy to know such secrets."

Sylphid did not cower away. Instead, her eyes lit up in interest. "Is that so? I'm growing more interested in you all the time. Won't you and your servants come along with me?"

Timothy blinked. _Servants_? Did she think Paimon and the other were his lackeys? That must be the case.

He wanted to refuse... but Sylphid was much more powerful than all of them put together. He used the Eye to check her description, and what he saw nearly made him break his 'powerful expert' illusion from pure fear.

...

**Sylphid, Envoy of Ashenzari - Dark Forest - Supreme Existence**

_A powerful adherent of the cult of Ashenzari. An elite capable of fighting ten other Sentients at the same time. One of the highest ranking Envoys among Ashenzari's Devoted. Ashenzari's Envoys possess the power to tap into nature itself, making them especially deadly in dense woodlands._

...

It wasn't the same description as the other Envoy. That told Timothy that Sylphid was not the gold-mask wearing Envoy he had seen previously.

Even so, if he went with her, there was a chance they might run into the gold-masked Envoy. Whether that would be good or bad news was anyone's guess.

"Allow me to deliberate with my party." Timothy eventually said.

Sylphid cocked her head. She levitated in the air, a long silvery tail swishing back and forth behind her. She made no motion to stop him, so Timothy took her silence as an 'okay'.

He turned around and switched to internal comms.

"So... this is the situation." Timothy said quietly.

He brought the others up to speed, and even though he couldn't see their expressions, he sensed from their body language that some of them were not happy about this development.

"You're playing with fire." Paimon warned. "We cannot delay. If a dungeon break occurs, all the monsters in the Dark Forest could break out into Chrona. Everyone we know would die."

"I don't disagree, and time is obviously of the essence, but do YOU want to tell the scary Envoy lady 'no'?" Timothy challenged.

Paimon flicked his eyes to look at Sylphid. She patiently levitated in the air, waiting to hear Timothy's verdict.

"I dunno. She seems nice!" Marigold chirped. "Maybe we can get a good night's sleep before we set out again?"

Timothy wanted to rebuke Marigold, but they'd been traveling for four days straight now, and they only had three more days before the quest failed. He wanted some beauty sleep too, but he didn't want to jeopardize the mission.

[I want to go inside.] Ferral suddenly said.

"Huh? You do?" Timothy asked, slightly stunned.

[Yes. This species interests me. I want to explore and see the wider world. If I shy away from every dangerous situation, then what wonders will I ever witness? I believe we might even find the exit to the forest faster if we probe for information from this female.]

Raavul lifted her chin slightly. [I am also interested in learning more about Ashenzari. We cannot overpower Sylphid, so we might as well accept her hospitality. Her gaze is warm, and I sense no malice. I am interested in learning more about her species.]

With that, there were three 'yes' votes, one 'no' from Paimon, and Timothy was 50/50 in either direction.

"Screw it. I guess I'll just say yes, then." Timothy said.

He turned back around, then opened up his helmet to reveal his face. Sylphid finally got a good look at him for the first time and was surprised. She didn't recognize Timothy's species at all.

"Sylphid. We thank you for your generous offer. My people and I are on an important mission, but I would very much like to experience the culture of Ashenzari's followers and compare it with that of my liege. This is my first time meeting your kind, so I hope you will forgive me if myself or my followers commit any minor errors."

Sylphid was pleased with the weaker Envoy's respectful tone. It was good that he wasn't too arrogant and knew his place. If he truly had a seed of a Dark One inside him, then he could become a formidable powerhouse someday. It would be a huge waste not to befriend him now, while he was still vulnerable!

"Ashenzari welcomes you. Please follow me into the Golden Glade." Sylphid said, smiling sweetly at Timothy.

Timothy's face flushed. It was only now, looking at Sylphid with his unfiltered eyes, that he realized she was actually... extremely beautiful!

Behind him, Marigold's gaze flickered with uncertainty.

_Drat, she's a threat!_ Marigold thought. _A huge threat!!_
