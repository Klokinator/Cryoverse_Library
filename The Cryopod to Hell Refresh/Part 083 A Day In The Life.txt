Part 083: A Day In The Life...

Dreams of clouds and rabbits and the taste of Snicker bars give way to the sensation of something touching my nose.

Groggily, I swipe at it. 

"Fuggin' flies. Go away..."

The thing touches my nose again. I swat at it.

"Fugginnn hell..."

Suddenly an anvil slams onto my stomach, blasting the wind from my body. I jerk up in bed, gasping for breath. The light of a supernova blasts my face thanks to Phoebe opening the goddamn blinds again.

"Wha-?! Kuhhh!"

I choke out a gasp and shield my eyes from the light.

"Wake up, daddy! It's morning! Morning morning morning!!"

The little blonde-haired girl flops off my belly and rolls to the side as I pull myself into a sitting position. I stare at her in annoyance. "Aw, man, can't you lemme nap?"

"Noooo! You promised me the bestest birthday ever!" My daughter reaches up and pokes my cheek. I summon a second wind and breathe a little easier.

The door to my room opens. Phoebe steps inside. "Daisy! What are you doing? I told you to let daddy sleep.  He's tired, honey."

Daisy rolls her eyes. "No, he _isn't_. He can wake up with _magic_."

I shrug. A smile escapes my lips. "Well, she's not wrong. **Awake. Alert.**"

One minute later, I slide out of bed, my mind as sharp as if I'd slept like Rip Van Winkle. Wordsmithing is life-changing in small ways one couldn't imagine. "**Coffee.**"

A cup of Joe pops into my hand, already the perfect temperature. I summon my clothes and stretch hard, popping my back in the process. Phoebe heads into the bathroom to get ready for her big event. 

I'm looking forward to a fun-filled day.

Daisy hops around on the bed. "Come on! What's taking you so long?"

It's my daughter's sixth birthday today. The terrible twos are a distant memory. Honestly, age four was the worst. That was when she started trying to snatch Solomon's Crown off my dresser. If someone wears his crown, I can't take it off without their permission, unless they die. If Daisy ever got ahold of it, I'd never get it back. She won't even share snacks with other kids, let alone a sparkly all-powerful crown of knowledge.

"**Unlock.**" With a single word, I pop open the bedside drawer and snatch his artifact out, then place it on my head. The soundproof dresser was one of Phoebe's ideas. Great for making sure that creepy guy can't listen in while Phoebe and I try our hardest to make another baby.

Solomon materializes beside me. "Good sleep?"

"Eh, can't complain."

Daisy grabs my arm. "Where's uncle Sally? I wanna see hiiim!"

Solomon flinches. "Please don't."

"Aww. But Daisy _loves_ you, Solomon. **Project.**" 

His ghostly body materializes beside me, and Daisy squeals in delight. "Eee! Uncle Sally! Uncle Sally! Do you wanna play hopscotch?!"

"No."

"Chess! Let's play chess!!"

"No."

Solomon glares at me. His voice speaks in my mind. _I hate it when you do that._

I was surprised when I learned a few years ago that Solomon detests children. I mean, the guy's had several kids of his own, but then again, in the Bible, he suggested cutting a baby apart and giving each half to two women. You'd have to be one hell of a sicko to imagine such a thing.

Daisy pounces on my back and wraps her arms and legs around me. "Piggyback! Piggyback!"

I nod and hold her legs up so she doesn't fall. Predictably, she starts tugging on Solomon's Crown, but it doesn't go anywhere. "I wanna wear Sally's crown! Make me a princess, daddy!"

"Technically, you already are," I reply.

"Yeah, but I want a crown..."

"I'll tell you what, sweetie. When you're ten years old, I'll let you wear Solomon's Crown."

"What?! Really?"

"Yup."

"Promise?"

"Promise."

That satisfies her. She returns to yapping at Solomon about something while he gives me a thousand-yard stare, but my eyes travel elsewhere.

Phoebe steps out of the bathroom dressed in a full track-suit. The skintight black fiber clings to every inch of her body, arousing me like it always does. She stops in the doorway and meets my gaze. "Enjoying the view?"

"Y-yeah. Uh-huh."

"Sometimes, I wonder if you or Solomon are the bigger pervert."

"Me. Definitely me. You look great."

Phoebe opens the door and gestures outside. "After you. Eyes up here, Jason."

Daisy yanks on my hair like a wild animal. "Mommy's big race! Zoom, zoom!"

---

_One hour later._

"Laaadiesss and gentlemen! Boys and girls! Kobolds and salamanders! Welcome to the Superstar Arena Megadrome Shooowdooown!!"

The racetrack announcer bellows into his loudspeaker, sending excitement and cheers through the crowd. Daisy hops around excitedly as the massive trucks in the arena below all begin revving their engines. The roar of a dozen twenty-ton monster trucks is somehow noisier than the loudspeaker above us.

A series of dirt hills and miniature lakes await the challengers. The circular racetrack extends around the entire border of the arena, which itself is three times the size of a football stadium.  In the center of the track, every driver has a team of medical experts on call. While I can't see her, I know Phoebe's best friend, Samantha, is watching in case Phoebe gets in a wreck. Of course, I'm also here and can heal Phoebe instantly, but it can't hurt to have a backup.

I rattle a bucket around. "Daisy. Want some popcorn?"

Hundreds of people around me scream and shout excitedly, distracting my daughter. She bounces up and down, trying to see over the head of a Harpie in front of us.

Daisy whines, "Where's mommy's truck? I can't seeeee it!"

Using my telekinesis, I levitate her a foot in the air. It's something I do often enough that she doesn't bat an eye. "She's over there. The third one. It has the lightning bolts on the side."

Daisy flails her finger toward Phoebe's truck. "I see it! I see it! It's the one with the zappers!"

"I just said that..."

Solomon sits beside me. He enjoys being around people, but Phoebe's races don't interest him much. "Children take forever to learn the value of listening."

I stuff popcorn in my mouth. "Yeah. You're telling me."

Phoebe and I conceived the idea of races several years ago. The demons suffered heavy losses thanks to Uriel's vicious attack, and realizing we weren't a threat, they retreated to the Labyrinth. Thanks to the unexpected peace, we've had six straight years without war. It afforded me the time to develop all sorts of defenses for our planet, but also recreational events.

Still, I try to be humble. I always tell people that I might be the Hero, but I'd prefer people didn't treat me like I'm someone overly important. I could have built a special booth in the Megadrome for Daisy, Solomon, and myself, but I don't want my daughter growing up with a royalty complex. We have our giant multi-story house and dozens of servants. That's good enough. And besides, in Hero City, everyone lives like kings. No beggars. No impoverished people. Only a few drunkards and loudmouths. It's all thanks to the power of Wordsmithing.

I'm surprised everything has worked out so well.

Solomon pokes me. His finger feels like a tingle of ice on my skin. "Jason. We have to visit the colony today. I know it's Daisy's birthday, but we need to improve its biodiversity dramatically."

"Right, right. It can wait a day, can't it?"

Daisy ignores us. She continues to screech and point at Phoebe's truck, yelling things like, "Go, mommy! Vroom, vroom!"

"I suppose. Why not frame it like a birthday present? You haven't shown Daisy the colony yet, have you? I bet she'd like it."

I roll my eyes. "Oh, sure. _Now_ you're the expert on children."

"Hey. I've had my share of ankle-biters. That's why I can't stand them!"

"You're just a grumpy, frumpy old man."

Solomon doesn't protest. In the distance, the engines suddenly roar to life. 

The announcer yells again. "Alright, everyone! Today, the undefeated twelve-time champion, Phoebe Hiro, is up against her arch-nemesis, the blood-bathed, demon-loving, baby-eating monster! That's right; she's facing off against none other than Ogie, the most despicable salamander to ever hit the dirt!!"

The crowd boos and howls. Daisy joins in. "Boo! Uncle Ogre is a big fat meanie!!"

I snicker. The racing events in Hero City remind me of wrestling from my time. There are tales of triumphs, losses, rivalries, and all sorts of things like that. It's all part of a series of running gags, and everyone's in on it — even a six-year-old girl.

A minotaur at the front line howls violently. "ON YOUR MARRRKS! THREEE! TWOOO! OOONE! GOOOOOO!"

His cry lights a fire under the tires of every truck on the line. The racers accelerate and immediately hit the first bump, sail ten feet into the air and slam the ground with enough force to rattle the racers' teeth. Naturally, all of the trucks have internal momentum compensators to assist the drivers, but Phoebe is such a rabid competitor that she turns hers off. She races for the thrills, and also because she's a bit over-zealous.

If I had known seven years ago what I know now, I'd never have been able to picture Phoebe's transformation into such a sexy badass. She used to be demure and polite, but I suppose looking back, there was always a sparkle of bloodthirst in her eyes.

The first lap goes mostly as planned. The lakes of water on the opposite side of the stadium explode outward when the racers hit them, showering the screaming fans with filthy mud. I usually position myself far away from the tracks, as do the older members of our community. I'm in no mood for a mud bath.

The last-place driver suddenly jerks to the right and slams against the concrete and metal wall. I inspect the damage with my Wordsmithing, which reveals a twisted axle. I quickly repair it and send him a telepathic message. _You're good to go, Sam. Don't fall further behind!_

_Thank you, Your Majesty!_

I hate it when people call me that.

Sam reverses and rushes back into the action, but he's already fallen pretty far behind. 

Another icy touch on my arm makes me turn to Solomon. "What?"

The king stares at me for a moment. "Have you spoken with Hope lately?"

"Ugh. I'm trying to watch the race."

"Jason..." Solomon trails off. He looks away. "You always dodge the question."

I shrug and ignore him. It doesn't matter if Solomon pesters me. Hope and I barely talk anymore. Hell, it's been three months since I last saw his face. He has a villa on Maiura, and so does Neil. Hope has his life, and I have mine. That's all there is to it.

I exhale slowly, releasing something that isn't quite a sigh. Where did I go wrong with Hope? You'd think a clone of myself would be the easiest person in the universe to get along with. Instead, he's the complete opposite of me. 

Eh. Perhaps I should drop by the Core later. It's been a while. I haven't seen Kar for at least a month. Couldn't hurt to say hello.

Phoebe's trucks zooms past, and Daisy cries out. "Mommy! That's mommy! I saw her, daddy!"

"Yes, you did, sweetheart."

Absentmindedly, I check my multi-purpose watch. The race will continue for another thirty minutes. I have a lot to do today, though. Daisy's birthday party... her human and monster friends will be over later... then I have to visit the colony... Wordsmithing training with Solomon...

Jeez. I can't believe I'm thinking about something other than Phoebe's race. 

There's so much to do and so little time in the day.

---

_An hour later._

In the back of my mind, a silent dread lurks. It's been there ever since I built Hero City. I'm from a different time, a different world. Earth. A planet inhabited primarily by humans. I come from a world where people once looked down on each other for minor differences — the color of their skin, political affiliations, religion, and a thousand other discrepancies. 

But... that isn't the case here, on Tarus II. Humans and monsters live side-by-side in all-but-perfect harmony.  Why judge a man for looking a little ugly compared to other humans when beautiful and disgusting-looking monsters surround you?

It's not that differences don't exist. They do. Humans and monsters living on Tarus II know we have a shared enemy living in the Labyrinth. In some form, I believe demons aren't all that bad. Yes, they devour the flesh of humans and absorb souls of the departed to gain power, but they aren't inhuman unthinking freaks.  Paimon demonstrated that fact, as does Samantha every day.

But... this dread never escapes me. I'm different. I try not to judge others based on how they look, but it happens unintentionally. What if I pass my biases onto Daisy? What if I let a joke slip that comes off as insensitive or offends people. I'm not some nobody on the street; I'm the figurehead of our society.

That dread hits me in waves as Daisy stares up at the minotaur standing beside us on the bus. The moment she opens her mouth, I know she's going to say something horribly embarrassing.

"Daddy, why do his horns bend all weird like that? He looks different from the other meenaturrs."

Horns, for minotaurs, are a sensitive subject. They have a whole religion about them and how their length and complexity determine the rightful successor to any given clan. 

This big fellow's horns bend slightly downward, which is very different from Yamir's, the proud cook at my house. I don't know a lot about what different horn-shapes mean, but taking the bus is usually reserved for those without a vehicle. Nobody in Hero City lives poorly, but those who take the bus tend to have fewer possessions and prestige than their peers.

Solomon's Crown instantly fills in the blanks in my knowledge regarding minotaur culture. Based on his horns, this one is probably an outcast. He's one of the few who came to Hero City freely, not someone who already lived here. He's from the Labyrinth.

The minotaur tilts his head down to stare at my daughter. Then, he looks at me. He must notice the look of panic in my eyes because he merely shrugs. "Human whelps should learn to keep such observations to themselves."

"S-sorry. She didn't mean anything by it. Kids, am I right?"

The half-bull half-man quietly reaches up and touches his horns. "My family cast me out for my deformity. I am six hundred years old. This world is the only place I've found acceptance in my entire life."

I nod. "I'm glad to hear that. Sorry for Daisy's, erm, rudeness."

He turns away and stares out the windows at the massive buildings as we drive toward the northern side of the city. "Were it not for my deformity, I might not have found such a wonderful city. I wonder if perhaps it is a curse that eventually became a blessing."

Solomon, standing across from me, raises his eyebrows. "Do such things exist?"

The other passengers ignore our conversation. Several of them look like they woke up only a few minutes ago.

"If they do, one may go to the grave without knowing."

Solomon nods, satisfied by the minotaur's reply. One common theme I've noticed among their species is their eloquence and intelligence, not only among monsters, but in general. Minotaurs appear brutish and powerful, but physical altercations between them are rarer than a blood-red moon. They prefer to draw, cook, and pursue their hobbies.

Still, their intelligence lacks in some other areas. The whole horn-superstition thing, for one. Oh well, nobody's perfect.

The bus slows to a stop. I breathe a sigh of relief and nod at the minotaur. "See you around."

"You as well, Your Majesty."

I always take the bus to try and keep Daisy as down-to-Tarus as possible. The last thing I want is for her to grow up a snob with communication issues. I don't want her to look down her nose at others. It isn't who I am, or who Phoebe is. It isn't the lifestyle meant for our child.

But, at the same time, I wonder if I'm doing exactly the opposite. Is it normal for kids to make such uncivilized comments? Did she somehow pick up the trait from me?

"Daddy? What's wrong?" The bus drives away, leaving us behind. 

I blink and stare at her. "Huh? What do you mean?"

"You look sad."

"Oh. It's nothing. I'm thinking about, uh, how boring your birthday party is going to be! I only invited fifty of your friends over. You probably won't have any fun at all."

Her eyes widen. "You did?! Even Kilva and Ga... Garu... Gaaaruu...?"

Monster names are hard for her.

"Yep, even G'arunth. You ready to see them?" I gesture ahead to the annex I created a few days ago. It has a whole bunch of carnival rides and other things she'll love.

"Oh, yay!! You're the bestest best daddy ever!!"

"I wish that were true."
