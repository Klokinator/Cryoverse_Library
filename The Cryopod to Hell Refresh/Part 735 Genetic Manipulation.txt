Part 735: Genetic Manipulation

_Far-Future Era. Day 21, AJR. The Garden of Eden._

Abby was in a miserable state, but hugging Bael improved her mood quite a bit. After five or ten minutes of him gently rubbing and patting her back in silence, she eventually pulled away and wiped her eyes.

"Th-thanks, Bael. I'm sorry I got so weepy." Abby mumbled.

"It's fine, honey." Bael said, smiling at her. "You wanna talk about it?"

Abby thought for a moment, then shook her head. "No... not really."

"Oh. Okay." Bael said.

After a minute, Bael scratched the back of his head.

"So, uh, did you do that thing Ose wanted?"

The Baron of Happy Thoughts blinked. She thought for a moment. "Oh... yeah. I got in touch with them."

She reached into a pocket on the side of her booty shorts, where she'd stuffed the datapad from earlier. She mumbled something to herself as she took a minute to login to the device. She wasn't good with technology at all, but in order to follow in Ose's footsteps, she had been putting in real effort to try and learn.

After running three different biometric scans to validate her identity, Abby started scrolling down the list on the datapad.

"Um, I contacted Laharl. She didn't seem too interested at first, but when I explained to her that becoming a Duke or an Emperor wasn't viable in the long term due to a soul shortage, she bit. She has the um... the skillset we need too."

Abby sniffled. She wiped her nose, trying to refocus her thoughts away from what Gressil told her before.

"Then I um... I looked for Vepar. It turns out she's already a Deity now, so... looks like our info is out of date."

"Vepar is? Man, guess I've been on this planet too long." Bael mumbled. "Shit, it's only been a couple of days since I last saw her."

"A little longer than that..." Abby said, before remembering Bael didn't know how to count. "Uh, who else? Oh, I found Orias! He's exactly the type of demon we want, but... he wasn't interested."

Bael nodded. "That's a shame."

"Several of the Dukes from the Hell of Lust seemed interested." Abby said. "But um... they said they were going to stay with the humans."

"Typical." Bael grunted. "Those sex-pests've always gotta stick around with humans."

Abby scrolled her eyes down the list of names. There weren't many left.

"So yeah... that's it." She said eventually. "Just Laharl. She was the only one who fit Ose's qualifications. I found plenty of Grunts and a few Lords, but thanks to Glinch's pills, most demons have been split between Emperors or stronger and Lords or weaker. The Seven Hells don't have a lot of Barons left, and not too many Dukes either."

Bael fell silent for a moment. He seemed to be listening to something.

"Mmm. Ose says that'll have to do. You did the best you could. Her gadgets would work better if we could throw 'em on Barons and Dukes who focus on physical combat, but Lords and Grunts can get boosted just fine, too."

It turned out the reason Abby had gone into the Labyrinth was because Ose was finally planning to equip her technological upgrades on other demons. She needed demons willing to experiment with this technology, but unfortunately, the number of demons these days who were most compatible with her equipment was painfully small.

In Ose's ideal world, her armor suits and weapons would augment the strengths of Demon Barons and Dukes better than any other class of demons. Demon Emperors were already strong enough that the equipment would provide a negligible boost, while Grunts and Lords were a little too weak, unable to use the armor to its fullest extent. 

Of course, anyone at or below the Baron level would get a tremendous boost to their combat capabilities, but Barons were the target audience since their strength could be directly raised to levels comparable to Demon Emperors. Lords would only become as strong as Dukes, and Grunts might barely reach the level of Barons.

For Demon Dukes, they would also be raised to the level of Emperors, but Emperors didn't have a lot of headroom for their own increase. Thus, the new technology would be wasted on them.

Unfortunately, due to Glinch's pills, countless Demon Barons and Dukes had been uplifted to the level of Emperor, and many Emperors had been raised to Deity by Diablo before his unfortunate death. This would normally be great news, but for Ose, it couldn't have come at a worse time.

No doubt, she was silently cursing her luck inside Bael's belt, but if she was, Abby couldn't hear it.

"I'm sorry." Abby said sullenly. "There's still plenty of strong Lords out there..."

Bael didn't even bother speaking Ose's next words. He just smiled and squeezed Abby's shoulder reassuringly. "Seriously, it ain't your fault, sister. We'll do fine with Lords 'n Grunts."

After a minute, Bael scratched his head. He looked off to the side.

"Huh. Really?" Bael asked.

Abby looked at him. It was obvious he was talking to Ose.

"What did she say?" Abby asked, looking wistfully at the belt that contained Ose's soul shard.

Bael looked back at Abby. "Uh, I don't really get it. Ose said she sensed a thing from you earlier. Wanted to test it out."

"A thing?" Abby repeated.

"Ye." Bael said, giving her a goofy thumbs up. "A soul thing. A soul... redderbence. Uh. Rememberance. Soul roadkill. No? That ain't it either? Well for cryin' out loud, don't use such a big word then!"

Bael suddenly started shouting at the air angrily.

"Lady, it's YOUR stupid word! Not my fault you can't say it right."

"Now now, don't you go pointing fingers at me, missy!"

"Fine, I'll try again! Soul... redshift!"

"Seriously?! Lucifer's tits, why can't you just use an easier word then? No I don't give a shit if she's your mom, she had a nice rack! I missed lookin' at them triple-titties!"

Abby stared, her jaw slightly ajar, as Bael launched into a one-sided argument with Ose, an argument Abby wished she could fully hear. It was off-putting hearing him shout at the air.

"Um.. Bael..." Abby said quietly. "Did you mean to say... Soul Resonance?"

Bael spun to look at Abby. "Exactly! Soul Retarding! You got it, sister!"

Abby ignored his mispronunciation. "So, Ose said she sensed a resonance earlier?"

"Mhm. Yeah." Bael said, giving her another thumbs up. "She said she wanted to run a test with you."

"Okay! Let's try it, then!" Abby said with a smile.

It wasn't the first time they had done this. In fact, weeks before, Abby had pestered Ose constantly, hoping they would be able to find a soul resonance so that Abby would be able to talk to Ose. Unfortunately, she had failed over and over again. Eventually, Abby gave up, feeling sad and disappointed in herself.

But now, Ose's words gave her a renewed hope! Abby practically dragged Bael along to a certain test chamber in the Garden of Eden, where ADAM met up with them shortly after.

"**Another resonance test?**" Adam queried.

"Yeah!" Abby exclaimed. "This time, Ose said she sensed something!"

ADAM glanced at Bael who nodded in response. "That's all she told me, Add-Dumb."

The Protectorate acknowledged their command without speaking any words. ADAM gestured to Abby, and Bael unbuckled Mjolnir's belt from his body. He passed it over to Abby, who quickly put it on, then walked over to a cylindrical glass tube placed on top of a machine of some sort. The tube lifted up, Abby walked underneath it, and it closed down on top of her, sealing her inside.

"Please, please, please..." Abby whispered, closing her eyes and crossing her fingers as a look of hope mixed with fear played upon her face. "Don't tease me. Let this be the one..."

A glowing blood-red demonic rune activated underneath Abby's feet. Soon, a tingling sensation began to activate within her body. She shivered, not from the cold, but from her nerves, as her toes and fingers started to hurt a little. Waves of electrical energy surged through her body over the next five minutes, making her feel as if her body had gone numb, and now she was standing on pins and needles.

Abby gritted her teeth even harder. She rubbed her arms and winced as the pain grew more intense than before. It was far from unbearable, but it wasn't pleasant, either.

Eventually, the feeling died down, and ADAM turned off the device. The robot's expressionless head turned to look at her.

"**I have detected a resonance.**" ADAM said without any fanfare. "**Previously, your Synchronization range fell between 0.35% and 1.11%; within the margin of error. Now, however, your Synchronization has risen to a range between 10.91% and 17.31%. This range is far higher than before, and it appears to be growing.**"

Abby looked at him, slightly dumbstruck, not quite able to comprehend the robot's words.

"So, that means..."

"**I have adjusted Master Ose's soul frequency window. You should be able to communicate with her now.**" ADAM said.

For a brief moment, Abby's heart nearly stopped. She felt a wave of disbelief sweep over her as she looked down at the belt.

"Os... Ose? Is that true? Can we speak to one another now?"

For a moment, nothing happened. But then, a faint phantasm appeared inside Abby's field of view. She instantly knew the person she was seeing was Ose, but Ose's physical features were extremely faint and difficult to make out.

[I believe so.] Ose said, her voice not exactly crisp and clear, but certainly audible. It sounded somewhat muffled, as if she were speaking through a tube made of cardboard, but to Abby, it was the most glorious sound she had heard in a long time. [Can you hear me, Abby?]

"Hear you... hear you..." Abby said, her mind in a daze. "Y-yes. YES! I can- I can HEAR YOU, Ose!! EEEEEEEE!!! Ohmygosh it's you! It's really you! Ohmygosh ohmygosh!!!"

Abby started babbling excitedly. Ose's expression turned to a grimace, but she could only sigh and massage her eyelids tiredly. [Well. At least it's better than being stuck talking to that other idiot.]

"Ose! Ohmygosh I can't believe it! You're back! I had my doubts sometimes but now I can see it's really you!!" Abby cheered, her spirits rising to the heavens. "We're gonna have so much fun now! We're gonna play games, and have girls nights out, and-"

[Abby!] Ose shouted, snapping Abby out of her babble-streak. [Focus. Calm down. It's nice to talk to you, too. Okay? But let us descend from the clouds to the real world.]

Abby snapped her mouth closed. She nodded up and down in big, broad movements, beaming the happiest smile of her life. She simply couldn't stop grinning now that she had the chance to talk to her idol, the woman she loved, once again.

With Abby finally shutting up, Ose managed to slow down the tempo of the conversation. She took a minute to collect herself, then pressed her palms together, resting her fingertips against her nose. Her expression became contemplative.

[Bael is an idiot. I've been trapped in his mind, unable to change the world because he doesn't know how to follow basic commands. But you're smart enough that you can understand technology at a basic level and properly relay my words to ADAM and the others. Isn't that right?]

Abby nodded. "I can do that!"

[Good. But there's another reason to be excited. Bael's soul is too powerful. I have no way of getting back into my old body. But _you_ are only a Baron. I can get out of this belt and share a mental space with you, and together we can swap control of your body once in a while. By doing this, I can directly affect the real world and start my work back up once more!]

Abby's eyes widened. "Is that... possible? I can have you in my head?"

[I'll need to increase the resonance further, but yes. It's definitely doable. I long ago made preparations to revive in the event of my body's death. Unfortunately, I did not anticipate Bael's soul being so powerful. But in essence, transferring my soul into your body so we can share a space isn't difficult now. We've already gotten past the hardest part.]

Ose elaborated further, hitting Abby with a barrage of technical information. Abby was a lot smarter than Bael, but that wasn't saying much. She wasn't a technomancer, and that meant many of Ose's theories flew over her head.

"Whatever." Abby said eventually. "I trust you, Ose. If you think it will work, then let's do it! You can control my body any time you want! I'm happy to share it with you! Really!"

[If you're truly okay with it, then we can initiate the transfer right here.] Ose said. [This unit is a multi-purpose Soul Resonance Alteration Device. I designed the SRAD to help me find a new host in the event of my death, and transfer my soul into it. Unfortunately, I ended up stuck with Bael for longer than intended. But... that's in the past now.]

At Ose's recommendation, Abby asked ADAM to initiate the transference. Unfortunately, the resonance rating was a little too low, so ADAM had to first alter Abby's body using the SRAD's genetic configuration settings.

Pulses of electricity surged through Abby's body, making her wince in pain. This time, the pain was much more acute. Reconfiguring her biology wasn't possible before, as she and Ose had zero compatibility, but now that ADAM could use the limited resonance frequencies to find a 'target lock' on certain genetic markers, the transition become much more doable.

Abby whimpered quietly. She fell to her knees and hugged her arms against her chest, sweating profusely as she rested her head against the inside of the SRAD's glass tube.

All the while, Bael watched, a frown stuck to his face.

"Hey, Add-Dumb." Bael grunted, shuffling closer to the robot. "This zappy thing looks like it hurts. Is it safe?"

ADAM responded in the affirmative. "**A certain amount of discomfort and pain is to be expected. I need to reconfigure Abby's genetic markers to ensure proper sequencing for Ose's soul transference.**"

Bael nodded. "Mmm, I see. Got it."

Bael leaned away from ADAM, nodding solemnly to himself. He had absolutely no idea what the Protectorate had said, but he wisely knew not to say too much, otherwise people might think he was an idiot or something.

Minutes passed. Soon, a full hour had come and gone. Abby was exhausted. The pain never seemed to end. She wavered in and out of consciousness, and had eventually slumped against the inside of the glass. The pain was severe, though nowhere near her limits. Her biggest issue was that it was a constant pain that changed all the time, affecting different parts of her body. Her DNA was being overwritten, which had no affect on her appearance, but did have all sorts of implications regarding her internal biology.

Soon, ADAM completed its work. The Protectorate dialed the system to a stop, and Abby nearly fainted from joy that the pain was finally over. She breathed heavily, her exhales coming in the form of ragged gasps.

"It's... ohh... it's done?" Abby wheezed.

ADAM bobbed its head. "**The genetic transformation is complete. According to my readings, your soul resonance has risen to a range between 77.89% and 97.44%. While imperfect, this level is within the acceptable range for a soul transference.**"

Abby's eyes lolled to the left. Kneeling on one leg beside her, she saw Ose's face for the first time. Not a vague apparition, but a genuine, physical-looking spirit-body of Ose where she could make out all the details of her favorite outfit, all the curves and lines of her face, and her yellow and red eyes.

[Abby.] Ose said, reaching out to touch Abby's face. Her hand failed to make contact. [Are you alright? I'm sorry you had to suffer for me.]

Ose's voice was warm and kind. It was unlike her usual self. But Abby could tell she was genuinely concerned for her friend's well-being.

The Baron of Happy Thoughts shuddered. She forced herself to turn around and sit on her butt with her back against the glass.

"I'm... I'm fine, Ose. Just a little bit tired. That's all."

Abby wanted to cry all over again. She was so happy to see Ose's face, she could barely contain her emotions. At the same time, she was exhausted, and even just crying would take too much out of her.

"Let's stop here." Ose said, her voice soft. "You've endured well. We can finish tomorrow."

Abby's breath caught in her throat. "What? No. No, we've waited long enough. Let's just... get it done now, Ose. Transfer into my mind. Then I can rest afterward."

Ose hesitated. "That would be... a little dangerous. You need time to rest."

Abby grunted. She raised her right hand and shook the feeling of numbness out of it. Then, through sheer force of will, she scooted backward, carefully standing up by leaning her entire body against the glass. Her legs were wobbly like Jello. Her consciousness wavered in and out, but eventually, she recovered thanks to her demonic physiology.

"I'm fine." Abby said, surprising both Ose and herself with the force behind her words. "How long will the transfer take?"

Ose paused. "Not long. A minute or less."

"That's it? Great. Great!" Abby said, the excitement of what was about to happen powering her psyche up even further. Her posture straightened further, and her footing became more steady. She grinned, then cracked her neck. "I'm ready! ADAM! Do it! Merge me and Ose together!"

"**A short rest first would be advisable.**" ADAM said.

"I don't need rest. I'm fine, now!" Abby protested. "I'm tougher than I look. Just do it."

The Protectorate gave her a long, hard stare. Eventually, it nodded, then began to press some buttons.

"**Very well. Beginning Soul Transference now.**"

Another round of tingling began to crackle inside Abby's body. This time, it was a lot gentler than before. She braced herself for pain, but the process felt more energizing than anything else.

Ten seconds passed. Twenty seconds. Thirty...

Abby looked around, slightly nervous, but the pain didn't seem to be intensifying.

"Is this actually doing anything?" Abby muttered. "I don't feel any diff-"

Suddenly, a massive rush of energy surged from Abby's waist, through her belly, and rushed up into her head. It was as if she had fallen face-first into a freezing cold lake. Abby's mind immediately blanked out. She fell forward and collapsed onto the ground, unconscious.

"Huh?!" Bael gasped. "Whoa, whoa! What just happened?! Abby! Hey Abby, you okay?? Add-Dumb, is she okay?!"

The robot looked at Bael. "**Soul Transference complete. All vitals stable. It was only a minor shock to Abby's system. Her life signs are within acceptable levels.**"

Abby's eyes fluttered open. Her head swam with dizziness. She wavered in and out of consciousness. Ahead of her, she could see Ose laying on the ground, her spiritual body also unconscious. At the same time as Abby started to wake up, so did Ose. The two of them met each other's gazes.

Abby was immediately starstruck. Ose now looked as clear as could be. She appeared ghostly, with a bit of a glowing aura to her figure, but she was so lifelike that Abby swore she could reach out and touch Ose's hand.

She tried, but failed. Her hand passed through Ose's hand without even the slightest sensation following.

"It... worked..." Abby said, smiling dreamily.

But Ose did not smile back. Her eyes fluttered open and shut.

[I'm... I'm sorry, Abby. I was... I was wrong.] Ose said, her voice hoarse.

"Didn't work?" Abby asked, her voice slurring. "What... didn't work?"

[My soul... it was too weak.] Ose said. [I overestimated myself. I'm sorry. I thought we could... be together. But I was wrong.]

Ose closed her eyes. Abby's heart jumped. She struggled to sit up, but she had no strength left in her body.

"O-Ose? Ose?? What's... ohh... what's wrong? What happened?"

Ose groaned. Her voice was fainter than before.

[Your soul. It's... devouring mine.] Ose mumbled. Her words were slurred. She was having difficulty speaking. [I can feel my... consciousness... wavering. I don't have long now.]

Ose smiled weakly.

[I'm sorry, Abby. I wasn't a good friend... to you. I used you. Now I'm paying the price. I wish... I wish I had been... kinder to you. This is going... to be... the last time we talk.]

Tears welled up in Abby's eyes. She reached out to touch Ose's hand again, but both their palms passed through one another.

"No... Ose..." Abby croaked. "There... has to be something. My soul... too strong? Maybe... I can weaken it?"

[You've done enough.] Ose said, her eyes now tightly closed. [The only way... if your soul was... depleted. But no. Don't. Don't do it. Not for me. I'm not worth it.]

Abby felt a flash of inspiration.

"You said... my soul... it's in the way, right? So if I... drain my soul away? You can live? You can take over my body?"

Ose didn't answer. Her spirit-body was slowly becoming more and more transparent. This caused Abby's heart to jump in alarm. She realized time was running out.

"Ose... Ose!! No, you can't die! Not again! Not again!"

Abby hesitated. She realized in this moment, she still had a chance to save Ose. But doing so would, undoubtedly, force her to pay the ultimate price.

Her hesitation barely lasted a single second. She scowled angrily as she hardened her heart.

"I won't let you die, Ose. _I won't_. It's fine if I die. I'm nobody special anyway. I just wish... I could have spent more time with you."

Abby stretched her arm out. She tried uselessly to touch Ose's cheek, but as before, she failed once again.

A tear slid down Abby's face.

"Ose... I love you. I love you so much..."

Abby closed her eyes. She focused on the sensation of her soul, where she sensed Ose's soul fragment, minute by comparison, floating not far away from it.

Abby gritted her teeth and scrunched her forehead muscles. With great pain, she began sending her soul energy into Ose's soul fragment. The effects were immediate and unmistakable.

Abby's sense of self began to diminish. She opened her eyes a fraction, watching as Ose's soul form became slightly more corporeal.

But at the same time, Abby's vision began to waver. The more soul energy she transferred, the weaker her grasp on reality became.

It was now, in these final moments, as death approached, that Abby's mind became remarkably clear.

She remembered her life. She remembered the first time she spoke to Ose. She remembered how they met, and the way she fell in love with Ose the moment she laid eyes on her.

It started out as a childish infatuation. But it continued for 100,000 years. It was a painful, one-sided love.

All along, she knew Ose never loved her, and probably never would.

But it didn't matter. Ose had said such kind words to her only moments earlier. Even if those words were sweet lies, they were the only thing Abby had ever wanted to hear.

Sometimes, sweet lies were far superior to painful truths.

Abby closed her eyes, one last time.

She remembered what Gressil had told her, just a few hours earlier.

_So that's what he meant._ Abby thought silently to herself. _He knew what was coming. Oh, Gressil... thank you... for being there for me._

_I'm glad we got to talk..._

_One... last..._

Abby's consciousness faded away. Eventually, it disappeared entirely.

A long minute passed.

Abby's eyes eventually opened. She stood up, no longer pained in any way. Abby looked around the room and cracked her back, seemingly unharmed by all the events that had just transpired.

"Finally. She really made me drag it out." _Abby_ said, her tone much more pointed than before. "ADAM, code Four-One-One-Seven-Six-Niner-Zeta-Alpha-March."

ADAM nodded. "**Welcome back, Master Ose. It appears the Soul Transference was a success.**"

The glass tube lifted, revealing the new owner of Abby's body.

Ose, the former Demon Emperor of Infiltration, had returned to the world of the living.

"Get me some proper clothes." Ose commanded. "I'll not be caught dead walking around in this awful getup."

"**Yes, Master Ose.**" ADAM replied.

Bael stared at Ose with a distant, haunted look.

"Hey... hey... what's going on? Where's Abby?"

Ose snorted. "Abby? Unfortunately, it seems she pushed herself too hard and passed away. Now I own her body."

"WHAT?!" Bael exclaimed, looking at Ose in horror. "No way! Not sweet little Abby! Ose, what the hell did you do?!"

The New Abby dramatically rolled her eyes. "Oh, don't get your panties in a twist, _body-thief_. Abby did all this willingly. I barely even had to manipulate her. She practically threw her life away at the drop of a hat for my sake. It's better this way. Sharing would have been _such_ a pain, especially with her constantly yammering at me."

Bael's pupils twitched. He found himself rooted in place, horrified and unable to move, as Ose casually strolled out of the Soul Transference facility.

Ose had returned. And only one other person in the entire galaxy knew this unprecedented event had transpired.
