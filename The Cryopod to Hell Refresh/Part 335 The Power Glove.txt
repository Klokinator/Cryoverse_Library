Part 335: The Power Glove

Dozens of troopers rush to Cassiel's residence. They swarm around the exterior and sweep the perimeter, but nobody finds any indication of the Emperor of Chaos's presence. As if having stepped through time and space, he vanishes, never appearing on anyone's sensors.

Phoebe holds Cassiel in her arms while the angel quietly shakes and trembles, too petrified to move a muscle. Phoebe hastily cleans up the disgusting saliva left on her face, all while rage-filled thoughts boil in her mind.

"I won't leave you. Don't worry, I'm here for you, Cassiel. Just stay calm, take deep breaths..."

As Phoebe consoles the broken woman, Jason storms outside and grabs the nearest trooper.

"Well?! Where is he?! Don't give me that shit! You find that cocksucker! Shut down the portal and trap the motherfucker here! I'll kill him myself if I have to!"

The Wordsmith exhales so hard that his nostrils flare up and his eyes turn bloodshot. Even as the trooper stammers out several apologies, Jason continues ranting.

"How did he get here unseen, anyway?! I fully upgraded and reinforced the Core's security and scanning protocols to ensure this wouldn't happen! What, did he fall out of the fucking sky?!"

"I-I'm sorry, sir," The troopers says. "We don't know how Gressil made it past our barriers. Since he can use illusion magic, and he has some sort of teleporting ability, we think he might have-"

"I don't want you to goddamn _think_ about what he did, I want you to make sure this doesn't happen again!" Jason roars. "What if he'd gone after Phoebe, or Hope, or even me?! Hell, if he can get past the Core, he could go anywhere in our defensive line! We can't just sit here and-"

Before Jason can continue any longer, a hand grabs onto his shoulder and forcefully spins him around. Jason scowls when he finds himself face-to-face with Belial.

"Alright, Jason. Simmer down. Stop blowing your top at this poor guy." Belial says, frowning at him. "It's not his fault."

Jason inhales and exhales several times before pushing Belial's hand away. He shoots a glare at the trooper, but then softens his expression.

"You're right. Sorry! Never mind. Keep looking for him, and let me know if you find anything."

The trooper nods hastily. "Y-yes, sir!"

He leaves Jason alone to rant at Belial.

"I didn't realize- I didn't think he could just wander in through the front fucking door." Jason growls. "This is absurd, absolutely god-damned absurd! How the hell are we supposed to deal with an illusory, teleporting, sadistic fuck-bastard like Gressil?!"

Belial glances at the open doorway leading to Cassiel's room.

"I don't know, Jason. Now that Gressil has shown he can come and go as he pleases, we'll have to figure out how to even detect him. Magical technology won't work; he'll shut it down when he gets close, or it won't even penetrate his anti-magic aura in the first place. And traditional technology probably won't work either, because he can hide himself in illusions."

"What, so we're just screwed, then?" Jason asks.

"I... I don't know." Belial says with a sigh. "I punch things, and I heal things. I also sneak around and stretch my limbs. None of my skills will be of any use in tracking Gressil down, not unless he wants to make a booty call."

"Hilarious." Jason mutters.

"You're missing the bigger picture." Belial says. "We don't know what Gressil said or did to Cassiel. What we do know is that he could have easily whisked her away from right under our noses, but he didn't. Why do you think he left her here?"

Jason frowns. "Are you implying..."

"I'm not implying anything," Belial says. "I'm postulating that Gressil _let us_ take Cassiel and Hope from his dungeon. Think about it. As hard of a task as our escape was, if Gressil didn't want us to rescue them, we'd have been powerless to stop him. He could have grabbed Cassiel and Hope both and teleported away. He must have some broader aim, some goal he's pursuing."

The Wordsmith's expression turns chilly.

"So, what? He only came here to traumatize Cassiel? Or do you think he did something else to her?"

"One thing is for sure." Belial replies. "Gressil isn't right in the head. He devoured his mother, and now she's become part of him. Looking at the facts I've uncovered, Gressil... seems to be able to consume others and merge them into himself. Since that's the case, it might explain why he captured Hope."

"You mean..." Jason says, while sucking in a shallow breath. "...He wanted to merge Hope into his body? He wanted to steal Hope's Wordsmithing powers?"

"Possibly." Belial answers. "But I'm only guessing. Then, of course, we have to ask why he didn't do that in the first place. Why rip off all of Hope's limbs and torture him until he fell into a coma? Maybe there's some... some other reason for his actions we aren't seeing? And Cassiel, he might have some awful, nefarious plan for her, too."

Jason falls silent. He drops to the ground and plunks on his butt as many heavy thoughts weigh on his mind.

Several minutes pass. While the troopers hustle and bustle around Cassiel's residence and swarm around the city, searching for life signs or magical technology failing to do its job, the Wordsmith merely closes his eyes and thinks.

Belial sits beside him. She waits for him to finish thinking, and to finish conferring with his mind-wife.

Eventually, Jason opens his eyes. He lifts his gaze upward, to the sky.

"...Tarus II isn't safe." Jason mutters. "Not in the least. It has a fixed location. It's easy to infiltrate. I was planning to remedy this in the future, but I thought I could rebuild and fortify here, at least for now. As it stands, we're sitting ducks."

Belial raises an eyebrow. "What do you mean? Should we move to another planet?"

"No." Jason says. "Not another planet. Camael's Cube. It has a vast living space inside its interior. It would be the ideal position for a mobile base. The demons wouldn't be able to touch us. The Volgrim would have a hard time even detecting us. We'd be so much safer than here, living on this blasted mud-ball. I already planned to move everyone into the Cube at some point, but I didn't expect to have to move so quickly. Gressil's abilities change things."

"How long would it take for you to make the Cube hospitable to human life?" Belial asks.

"I don't know." Jason answers. "Currently, the interior of the cube is rich with magical energy. But oxygen? An ecosystem of plants and animals? Doesn't exist. I'd have to Wordsmith all of that into existence. I'd have to terraform the Cube into something more livable. I could make it like a giant space station, but I'd rather give it seasons, functional day/night cycles on a 24-hour rotation, and so on. I haven't thought about how to imitate sunlight, though, or set up photosynthesis and all that other stuff. No offense, but Phoebe is only a substitute for Solomon's abilities. His Crown could make terraforming take minutes, or perhaps even seconds. It will take Phoebe a lot longer to compute all the variables, and that's while she's working on all our other projects."

"In the meantime..." Jason continues, slowing his words down while he continues to think, "I have to find some way to protect Cassiel and the others. Once Hope awakens, he should be able to protect himself. But right now, he's helpless."

"Should we leave him alone?" Belial asks, glancing at Hope's infirmary. "Gressil could grab him right this second."

"If he didn't capture Cassiel, I don't think he'll take Hope." Jason answers. "But that being said, I placed a dozen of my best soldiers in his room, and I shut down the portal to the Labyrinth. Gressil should be either stuck here, or stuck in the Labyrinth..."

While talking, a glint appears in Jason's eyes. He suddenly stands up and nods, as a look of surprise appears.

"Of course! I didn't even think about that."

"Think about what?" Belial asks, also jumping to her feet.

"My Black Hole Constructs." Jason explains. "For now, what Hope and Cassiel need are bodyguards, someone who can stay awake 24/7, maintain a vigilant watch, and never leave their side. My constructs are perfect for that job. Unlike normal sentients, they never get tired, and they're excellent at focusing on one task. Their bodies are damn-near indestructible, and they hit like firetrucks."

Jason glances at Belial. "You fought Gressil. How strong was he? Do you think he'd win if he and one of my constructs came to blows?"

Belial scrunches up her face. "Um. It's hard to say. His ability to teleport is really, really troublesome. His illusions screwed with my mind, making him able to trick me repeatedly. But physically? He's not even close to my match. I beat the crap out of him, with ease."

"So he's not invincible." Jason concludes. "That's fine. I can work with it. **Recall. Recall.**"

Jason utters two words of power, causing both of his Black Hole Constructs to appear before him. The eerie copies of his body stand at attention, their vacuous eyes gazing forward at nothing in particular.

"Is it alright to put the constructs into such minor roles?" Belial asks. "I thought you built them to protect the Core?"

"I did, but they're more of deterrents, or ultimate weapons. Using them as bodyguards for VIPs is perfectly fine. Maybe I'll even make more, to protect my wife and other high-profile people."

"Including yourself?" Belial asks. "Jason, if Gressil can get the jump on Hope, he can certainly do so to you. You definitely need protection. Maybe even me, too. If Gressil were to attack me at an inopportune time, I'd probably end up on the losing side of a fight."

"We need to catch that slippery cunt in some sort of trap." Jason says, while scrutinizing his constructs. "For now though, let's work on one thing at a time. **Appearance. Shift. Morph.**"

Jason mentally manipulates the face and body of one construct, changing it from an ugly facsimile of himself into a pretty, delicate, and gentle-looking angel, not dissimilar to Cassiel, but with red hair, a tiara on her forehead, and a cute red dress.

Belial glances at him. "Um."

"It's not what you're thinking," Jason says. "Cassiel is obviously afraid of men and demons. If I'm going to give her a bodyguard, they should look like an angel, you see? Then she'll feel more comfortable. So I'll give her a dainty, sweet-looking girl to protect her."

Jason smiles evilly and chuckles to himself. "And if Gressil reappears, maybe this little cutie will catch him offguard. He might not realize how terrifyingly strong she is, heh heh heh..."

"Are you sure you didn't hit your head recently?" Belial asks. "You worry me, sometimes."

Jason waves his hand. "I'm just messing around. Anyway, this isn't enough to stop Gressil. Let me add some new 'features' to this Construct."

Jason falls silent for several minutes while conversing with his mind wife. After some time, he speaks a word of power.

"**Transmitter.**"

Jason holds out his hand, and a tiny bean-sized gadget appears in his palm. He grabs the hand of the female construct and slaps the device into its grasp.

"Swallow this."

The Construct doesn't nod or acknowledge his words, but it does open its mouth and toss the gadget into the back of its throat. After swallowing, it returns to a neutral standing position.

"What was that?" Belial asks.

"I'm making the Construct better able to contact others for backup in the event that Gressil or some other enemy does appear." Jason explains. "The transmitter will work anywhere on Tarus II, and it has a triangulation beacon in the event the Construct ends up inside the Labyrinth. Even if Gressil's anti-magic field appears, the Construct will be able to send out a distress signal."

"Such a bland term." Belial comments. "Why not give her a name? Just calling her 'Construct' seems lame, right?"

"It's essentially a biological machine." Jason says. "Its appearance doesn't matter. It's not a real girl, you know?"

"Sure. But why not treat her like a real person?" Belial asks. "Let's call her... Sally!"

"That's terrible." Jason says, rolling his eyes. "If we're going to name the Construct, let's at least make the name something angelic. Uh..."

He pauses for a moment to think.

"How about... Soleil?"

"Bit of a mouthful." Belial says.

"Sure, but Cassiel, Muriel, Samael, etc. They all have names that end in I-E-L or A-E-L. Since she has red hair, that made me think of fire, the sun, Earth's former star, Sol... So, why not Soleil?"

Belial mulls the idea over in her head for nearly a full minute. She finally nods.

"Alright. Soleil. It's adequate."

"Right, and for the other construct..."

Jason snorts to himself and tries to suppress a smile. He Wordsmiths again, this time aiming to change the appearance of the other Construct.

"**Appearance. Shift. Morph.**"

This time, Belial crosses her arms, turns to look at Jason, and shoots him an expression which seems to ask, _are you an idiot?_

"What are you even doing, Jason? Stop messing around. That looks totally stupid."

"No it doesn't!" Jason says, defensively. "He looks awesome!"

"No. He doesn't. What's with the black, rubber suit? And the pointy horns on his head?"

"They're not horns. They're like, uh, animal ears."

"Animal ears? They look like horns! Also, a cape? Why would you give the suit a cape? It's completely impractical."

Jason scowls at Belial. "Come on, don't tell me you don't recognize _Batman!_ He's so cool! Hope will get a real kick out of waking up and seeing Batman protecting him. It might even make him laugh!"

Belial facepalms. "I don't know who 'Batman' is, but he looks utterly stupid. Do something else."

Jason rolls his eyes. "Fine. **Appearance. Shift. Morph.**"

Upon seeing the Construct's second change in appearance, Belial groans in displeasure. "Ughhh. Seriously? Why is this guy made of nothing but muscles? He looks like some dumb idiot roid-rager. Like Bael, but even more ridiculous."

"He's _Arnold Schwarzenheggar_!" Jason protests. "A super cool 80's action star! He can definitely intimidate Gressil if he shows up."

"He won't intimidate anyone! He looks like a lunkhead!" Belial says, cracking a smile in part due to the ridiculousness of the situation. "Are you messing with me? Get serious, Jason!"

"Fine, fine." Jason grumbles. "_Someone_ isn't any fun. Alright... lemme think."

Jason falls silent for another minute or so before his expression becomes more serious.

"Alright. This should unironically be pretty useful in a lot of ways. **Appearance. Shift. Morph.**"

This time, when the construct changes its shape, Belial nods along, silently examining it from all angles. She walks around the construct and flashes a look of surprise at Jason.

"King Arthur. That's an... interesting choice. Why make a doppelganger of the Hero King himself?"

"A few reasons." Jason explains. "First, he's intimidating. Second, he has one hell of a reputation with the demons. Just seeing him should spook anyone who remembers his face. And thirdly..."

Jason pauses.

"...Wouldn't it be incredible if I could mimic some of Arthur's powers and give them to this construct?"

"Can... can you even do that?" Belial asks. "I know you boosted Phoebe's body to give her super-strength, but... how do you mimic or recreate someone's powers?"

"My Wordsmithing is versatile." Jason says. "But its true power lays not in making myself a godly fighter, but in improving other people and creating artifacts and gadgets. I've boosted Phoebe's strength and durability to her biological limits, sure, but I've also given myself telepathy, and telekinesis, flight, and super-speed. Surely, with some effort and careful thought, I could grant other types of abilities."

"Have you tried doing that before?" Belial asks.

"Sort of..." Jason says, his tone slowing down. "The thing is, me doubling the gravity in an area through my Wordsmithing isn't the same as giving someone else the power to manipulate gravity. I've always hesitated to dole out powers, because if they were used for evil, turned against me, or mishandled by accident, someone could do a lot of damage. Even so, Phoebe and I have been talking. She's working on a... a new gadget."

The Wordsmith glances around at the troopers in the area. Without hesitation, he Wordsmiths a privacy field around himself and Belial, hiding them from view and turning their words inaudible to the outside world.

"I don't want to get anyone's hopes up." Jason says, keeping his voice low. "But... basically, I think it might be possible to mass-produce something that will benefit every single person that pledges themselves to humanity's cause. Not just humans, but monsters and demons alike."

"In what way?" Belial asks, her curiosity piqued.

"**Projection.**" Jason says, summoning a small ethereal image in the air above his palm. 

A mechanical glove-like device appears, one with all sorts of technical-looking pieces, a display screen, and a mechanical rotator dial built-in to the wrist.

"This is the next step in humanity's warfare doctrine." Jason says. "I call it the Power Glove."

Belial groans louder than before. "Your names _seriously_ suck."

"Nah. The Power Glove is so _bad_," Jason says, offering her a faint smirk before continuing. "But, true to its name, the Power Glove will give people the ability to shapeshift into other forms, or to switch between wielding different sorts of magical powers."

He pauses before adding, "As far as the Shapeshifting thing goes, I got the idea from you."

"From me?" Belial asks. "Wait, can this Power Glove turn people into... anyone else?!"

"Well. Sort of." Jason says. "It has some... technical limitations. Currently, I can only store a few blueprints in it at a time. After all, storing an entire entity's genetic makeup would require a lot of digital storage space. If you wore it, you could turn into a human, an angel, a monster, or whatever was programmed into your module. Your appearance would only be capable of becoming one type of thing, though. So it's not like you can select 'human woman' and then customize your hair, eye color, or anything else. You'd only have access to a few pre-programmed appearances. And the glove would have to stay on, so it would make identifying a transformed person pretty easy."

"I see." Belial says. "What's the functional reason for allowing people to swap species?"

"Humans have a serious problem." Jason explains. "Magically speaking, we're all utterly dull and useless at it. We don't have Psionics, magic, or any other sort of extraordinary abilities. Only I do, as a Hero. But a Power Glove, if it works, could upend all of that."

Jason becomes more excited as he talks. "I mean, think about it! If someone needs to move a bunch of debris, they could morph into a huge Troll! Or they could select the super-strength augment! Flight? Got that covered. Invisibility? Check. Teleportation? Yup."

"There must be a catch." Belial says. "Surely, a device this ridiculous would shatter the balance of power."

"Oh, it _will._" Jason says. "I promise that. But yeah, it's going to have some limitations. From what I've discovered, most people have 'affinities' for different types of power. Phoebe is pretty versatile. She can handle super strength, telekinesis, some other stuff, but her ability to summon and fling fireballs is complete crap. We'll have to build Power Gloves individually, customizing one for each soldier. It'll take a lot of time and effort to do all that."

"Plus," He adds, "There's one other limitation. Humans innately can't control magic very well. Not only are they useless at it, but their bodies can't store much of it at all. So, do you remember what I said during the meeting earlier? About making something like a mana battery to store humanity's collective magical energy?"

Belial nods. "I do. Will the gloves pull from that power source?"

"Kinda. If everything works out, people will be able to recharge their Power Gloves at the Mana Batteries. The gauntlets will store mana until needed, and then people will be able to use magical powers for a short time. But! There's one neat factor. Someone who possesses a lot of mana in their body already, such as a high-ranking demon, or a monster like Blinker... they'd be able to use the powers much more versatilely. They can run the abilities not only through the glove's internal batteries, but through their bodies' mana circuits."

The Wordsmith stares at Belial meaningfully. "You have a lot of mana, but not many ways to use it. With a Power Glove, you'd gain access to an entire arsenal of powers."

"Whoa." Belial mutters. She falls silent for half a minute, contemplating Jason's words. "This is... this is kind of huge, Jason. A genuine game-changer."

"Yeah. But right now, it's all theory. I need to do some other stuff first before the Power Gloves become viable. Phoebe hasn't even completed the designs yet. They might not be practical."

The Wordsmith shrugs. "Even if the Power Glove idea doesn't work, I have other concepts that will reshape our society. Ever since... ever since Stormbringer... I've been thinking more in the bigger picture."

Belial rests her hand on Jason's shoulder.

"You're doing a good job, Jason. You're trying your best. I'm sure, with Phoebe's help, this Power Glove thing will work out. She's a genius, you know. That's why I love her, among other reasons."

"Haha..." Jason chuckles. "There you go, getting all starry-eyed about my wife, again. Are you going to pull her aside one of these days when I'm not looking? Get a little smoochy-smooch going on?"

Belial's skin flushes with color. "No, no, don't be silly! I'd, I'd never do something like that! That would be a betrayal of our friendship!"

"I'm just kidding." Jason says, waving his hand. "And hey, if anything happens, I'll just look away for a bit."

He smiles at Belial, but a hint of sadness rests in his eyes.

"You've suffered a lot in your life, Samantha. I'd like for you to find happiness, too."

The former Second Emperor stares at the Wordsmith for a moment before averting her gaze.

"Thank you."

Jason nods. "Anytime."

He shifts his attention back to the two Black Hole Constructs.

"Ahem. Now then, returning to these two... how can I make them into Gressil-slayers, I wonder?"
