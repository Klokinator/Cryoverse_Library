Part 057: Her Country, Or Her Morality?

Phoebe's heart pounds like a thousand galloping stallions. The women quickly shuffle into her house, but only after Phoebe shuts the door does a faint feeling of relief wash over her.

A panic attack immediately follows.

Trembling violently, Phoebe clutches her chest, gasping for breath as sweat and terror drench her body. She shivers and mutters incomprehensibly, before falling to her knees. When she comes to, the demoness at the front of the line is straining to get closer to her, with the one behind her being as accommodating as possible, given the circumstances.

"Fleshbag? Are you okay? What's wrong?"

Phoebe blinks. "I... I'm... scared. I disobeyed the king. We're all going to die. They'll behead me. I'm evil. I betrayed my people."

Phoebe babbles rapidly, releasing her pent-up emotions upon the unwitting demoness. However, the succubus merely nods without passing judgment.

"You're not evil; you're brave. Never thought I'd see the day when a human would save us from other humans."

Phoebe pulls herself up and fumbles for the key she took from the jail cell. She unhooks each demon's bindings but stops short of removing their leg and arm shackles. "I have to keep these on until we leave the city. It won't do to have you all unbound in the back of the wagon. The guards will be suspicious."

The succubus nods. "A sound idea. I'd rather free myself and try to escape now, but I can't repay your kindness with selfishness. Not when those animals still have Merle and Hana locked down in those hellish dungeons."

Phoebe nods. She unlocks the little girl's restraints, then walks over to her closet and grabs a blouse. "I won't parade women nude through the city. We'd be begging for trouble. Everyone, come here and take my clothes. I'll fetch blankets from upstairs for the rest of you."

The small house, barely more than a few rooms, teems with life as the women pass clothing from one to the next, sighing when they can't get any of their arms through the sleeves. Phoebe returns to undo and redo each of the women's bindings one after the other, allowing them to put the shirts and pants on comfortably.

A drop of sweat rolls down her back. _Percival... it's been over half an hour. Where are you?_

The little girl, Ose, sniffles. "Big brother... they're both gone. I'm... I'm never gonna see them again."

Phoebe walks over and hugs the girl, trying to comfort her. "No. I'll do what I can to help the men. My life is already forfeit at this point. I won't let your brother die, little one."

A sudden pounding at the door makes every person in the cottage jump in fright. Phoebe draws her sword and holds it behind her back, nodding at the women to make them stay out of sight. Only when she cracks open the door and spots Percival standing in the doorway does she relax slightly.

Percival jerks his head from left to right. "Are they all here? Did anyone see you?"

Phoebe nods, then shakes her head. Yes, they are. No... I don't think anyone did. Thank you, Percival. Leave, before anyone sees you here. I don't want you getting involved beyond this. You're a wonderful knight."

"Shut up. Get the whores out here and loaded into the covered wagon. I'm going with you."

Phoebe shakes her head. "No! You can't! My life is already over! Don't throw yours away, too!"

"I'll throw away whatever I damn well please." Percival eyes Phoebe silently for a moment. "I told you. I'm not helping the bloody demons. I'm saving you, Phoebe. After we release these women, I want you to flee with me. I have a relative outside Camelot's borders. He'll take us in."

Phoebe shakes her head. "I won't. I'm going to submit to the crown for my punishment afterward. I can't live with my betrayal of His Majesty."

Percival grits his teeth. "God... damned... woman. Confound you. We don't have time to argue. Ladies! Get out here, on the double. The guard shifts will be changing at high-noon at the border gates. We have to leave before Sir Lorgon arrives. No way he'll let us pass."

Phoebe's face whitens. _Sir Lorgon?! I forgot about him. He hates demons more than just about anyone else. He lost his wife and daughter to them._ 

Phoebe quickly looks back and beckons at the demonesses peeking around the corner. "Come quickly."

The women nod and shuffle out as fast as their feet can move. Phoebe takes the little girl in her arms and hoists her into the wagon parked mere feet from her door, obscuring her house entrance from prying eyes.

Percival helps each woman up, barely giving a passing glance at their ill-fitting and often embarrassingly revealing clothes. Instead, he steals glances at Phoebe now and then.

Once everyone enters the wagon, Percival closes the front and back, hiding the cowering women from prying eyes. "If anyone looks inside, make yourselves look as pitiful and weak as possible. Pretend you've already suffered terribly in the dungeon."

They nod in unison. _Simple enough,_ their eyes say. _It wouldn't be a lie._

Percival and Phoebe climb into the wagon's front. Only now does she take the time to look around. Several people are playing horseshoes across the distance, but they don't seem to notice the covered wagon.

"Percival, please. Leave. You've done enough for me already."

"I won't," he says matter-of-factly. "If there are two Knights of the Round, that will legitimize our leaving the castle with thirty demon prisoners. If it's just you, a woman, they might question your sensibilities."

Phoebe blushes. She looks away, unable to meet his gaze. "Th-thank you... Sir Percival. You're too kind."

"No." The man takes the reins, and the horses start walking. "I'm selfish. I'm only doing this because I... toward you... I feel..."

"I must remain chaste," Phoebe says, cutting him off. "We can't. I vowed to the Mother of the Lord."

Percival clamps his mouth shut. He runs a hand through his windswept blonde hair. "So you say."

...

Both Knights of the Round ride in silence toward the city gate. Minutes pass. Phoebe's anxiety increases as they draw nearer and nearer. _It's a trap. It's a trap._

Finally, the city gate appears in the distance. The wall surrounding Camelot, made of stone and steel, stands a mere fifteen-feet tall, but with a wagon-full of chained women, it might as well be a mile high.

"Let me do the talking," Percival mutters. "You look like you're about to have a panic attack."

Phoebe clutches her hands into fists. "Last chance, Percival."

"Mhm." 

The man doesn't bat an eye.

The guards up ahead turn and spot the wagon coming. They hold up their spears to stop the horses. "Whoa, whoa! Hey there, Sir Percival, Lady Berthold! You headed somewhere?"

Percival nods, forming his face into steel. "We are. I'm taking a batch of the blood-skins we recently captured to a holding facility just outside of Camelot. Dungeon's too full for them right now."

"Zat so?" The black-haired guard swaggers over to the rear of the cart and flips it open. "Huh. They're all women. Why're you taking all the whores out of the city?"

Percival doesn't bat an eye. "I only do what the King and Sir Gallant ordered. He kept a few of the prettiest ones for himself. You might even get to have a taste if you pop over after your shift."

The guard sucks air through his teeth and glances at the sun. "Oooh, shiet! You're right! It's goddamn near high-noon! I might be able to break some backs and call in a few favors. If these babes are the ugly ones, I can't wait to see the cute ones!"

The succubus flinches as she realizes what his words mean. _My sisters..._

Closing the rear curtain, the guardsman saunters to the front. "Hokay then! Don't have too much fun on the way over! And for you, Lady Berthold, don't let this scoundrel take any extended breaks, hahaha! Lord only knows the moaning will drive you-"

Percival glares ice at the guardsman. "Are you done belching words yet?"

"Uhh..." The man nods dumbly at the imposing knight decked out in full platemail. "Y-yeah. Sorry." He shuffles awkwardly over to the other guards. "Alright, let 'em through."

The horses _clop_ across the ground as the guards swing open a gate for them. "Take care!"

Phoebe and Percival nod in unison, forcing themselves not to appear relieved.

Only once the gates slam shut behind them, and half a mile passes, do the two knights finally sag their shoulders.

"Heavens." Phoebe shivers. "That was terrifying. How did you keep your calm?"

Percival stares ahead blankly for a few moments. Then, he turns his head toward the white-haired woman. "I pissed myself."

"Y-you did?" Phoebe's eyes slowly lower to his lower region.

"Mhm. I'll need new trousers when we reach my relative's house."

Phoebe turns to gaze ahead, and a moment later, she chuckles. She starts with a quiet snicker but soon erupts into roaring laughter as she slaps her knee and guffaws at the top of her lungs. "Hahahaha!!!"

Percival joins in, and the two of them howl and poke each other cutely, ignoring the demons peeping through the front flaps to see what's so funny.

Two miles pass. Then three. Phoebe glances back and sighs in relief at the distant walls of Camelot. "We'll go on foot from here. It shouldn't take long for the dungeon keepers to alert the city guards."

Percival doesn't slow the horse. "Do you trust these demons? You'll have to remove their collars. You don't know how dangerous demons are, Lady Berthold. You have yet to see what they can do."

Phoebe twirls a few hairs around her finger. "I saved their lives. Do you think they'd stab me in the back now? Have you no faith?"

Percival's gaze lowers. "You're too trusting. Demons are evil. No matter how sweet or innocent they may appear, they are irredeemable."

"And humans are better?" A tinge of anger appears on Phoebe's face. "What I saw in those dungeons was a horror unlike anything else. Demon or not, they look like us, yet we treat them worse than animals. You act as if they're subhumans."

"Tch." Percival pulls on the reins, slowing the horses to a stop. "If you're resolute in your beliefs, release the she-devils. Do what you wish. My life is already forfeit, as is yours."

Phoebe doesn't reply. She pulls open the flap, then climbs inside. The demons look away, having heard the conversation the knights just shared. A sensation of shame and despair washes over the group.

The little girl stares up at Phoebe. "Do you think demons and humans can be friends?"

Phoebe nods, kissing the child on her forehead. "I do, precious one."

"But... but humans took my brothers..."

"Not all humans are like that." Phoebe sighs. "Most are, though. I'm sorry. I wish we could all get along."

The succubus nods. "As do I."

Phoebe unshackles everyone in the cart. They rub their sore wrists and ankles, relieved that their captivity is behind them. Phoebe pauses for a moment, then removes the first Cursed Necklace from the little girl. A small static shock makes her recoil, but she quickly moves on and continues releasing each of the remaining demons. Within a few minutes, she finishes freeing everyone.

Phoebe clambers past the demons to head for the front, but the Succubus grabs her wrist. "Wait. Human. Before we leave, kneel down."

Phoebe eyes the succubus quizzically but does as she's asked. To her surprise, the demoness pulls Phoebe's head in close and plants a passionate kiss on her mouth.

"Mgmm?!" Phoebe pushes away from the demon, a look of surprise on her face. "W-what?! Why? Th-that's forbidden!"

The succubus smiles and rubs her two little horns. "I don't care about your human laws. All I care about is you. You've saved us all, and awoken a passion in me I haven't felt before. I'll see to it that you're welcome among my sisters any time."

Phoebe nods dumbly. "I... I see. Thank you... I... suppose..."

She stumbles forward into the front cabin. Percival raises an eyebrow. "Are you okay? Did they hurt you?"

"No. I don't... think so." Phoebe's eyes lower. "Let's get a move-"

Behind them, in the distance, a horn blows from the city's watchtower.

_Bwaaaaaaah._

Phoebe's blood turns to ice. "We have to run! They're pursuing us!"

All the demons leap out of the rear of the cart at once. One of the women gathers the others around and makes a few motions with her hands. "I'm summoning a faint mirage around us. My illusion magic is weak, but it will throw off any hounds. Let's go!"

Phoebe watches as the demon women flee toward the forest a few miles away. Each demon glances at her for only a moment, then they all take off, fleeing for their lives.

Percival leaps into action, loosing two of the horses. "You take the mare. I'll take the stallion. Let's go!"

The white-haired woman doesn't move. Instead, she continues to gaze at the backs of the demon women as they flee for their lives. "No. I told you. I'll submit to my punishment honorably. No matter what His Majesty does to me, I will not walk the path of a coward. What I did was just. I will plead my case."

Percival blanches. "Lady Berthold! If you go back, they won't allow you to plead for anything! We committed treason! It's beheadings or the gallows for both of us!"

A rock forms in Phoebe's throat, one that won't dissolve no matter how hard she swallows. "King Arthur is a just man. He will spare me. I believe in him. He will force me to slave in the fields for the rest of my life, or throw me in the dungeon until I die... but he will not execute me."

Phoebe climbs off the cart and turns her back to Percival. "Farewell, Sir Percival. Leave and save yourself. This is my burden to bear."

"Phoebe..."

Percival's voice cracks. Tears roll down his face.

"Don't go. Please. _Please._ I beg you."

Phoebe ignores him and begins walking toward the city. Her boots crunch against the loose dirt and twigs lining the well-traveled road. Her eyes gaze ahead, imagining the future.

She smiles. _At the least, I succeeded. I released those poor women. God will look upon me favorably. Even if the king punishes me to a life in prison... even if the guards ravage me in place of the demons... I know my choice was correct._

Another voice in her head speaks. _Was it? You released demons! They are God's mortal enemy. Your countrymen will hang you... as you deserve._

Something grabs Phoebe's arm. She turns around in surprise to see Percival, tears in his eyes. 

"I love you, Phoebe. I won't leave you to die alone. I'll return with you. I'll be honorable and strong... just... just as you are. I won't let you face the gallows by yourself."

Phoebe inhales, seeing the passion in his eyes. "Percival..."

In the distance, horse hooves galloping toward them make the two knights stiffen. Phoebe and Percival turn to face their doom; their faces pale with terror.

"We could still flee," Percival offers, though he can see the riders approaching now.

"No. It's too late. We will accept our fates nobly, Sir Percival."

They stand in silence as the army of a hundred strong draws near, confusion on some of their faces. The cavalry eventually slows to a stop, their horses kicking up dust toward Phoebe and Percival.

A vicious-looking man with a full beard stares down at the two silent warriors. _Sir Lorgon._ The rage of his face ripples through the air. "Bald-faced traitors! Where are the demon whores?!"

Phoebe doesn't back down. "I released them. The women don't deserve the horrors of the dungeon. We shall return to the castle and allow His Majesty to sentence us for our crimes."

Lorgon's teeth grind together. "You _dare_ humiliate my brother, Sir Gallant the Brave, yet think I'll allow you to live?! Traitors die where they stand!"

He makes a quick motion with his fingers, and two riders raise their bows at Phoebe and Percival. 

Percival gasps. "What? No! The King-"

"There is no king here, foolish broad! I have the right to sentence two demon-lovers to death! FIRE!"

Phoebe's eyes widen as the arrows fly from their bows toward her. Time crawls in slow motion as she passively waits for her death to arrive. Instead, something massive shoves her out of the way. A choked wheeze erupts from Percival's lips as he jumps in front of her. An arrow lances into his chest, sentencing him to certain death.

The knight stumbles backward and lands on top of Phoebe, stunned, as his eyes lurch to the arrows in his gut. "G-guh! Muh-hurk!"

Sir Lorgon snarls. "Hah! The brave aristocrat wishes to die protecting his harlot. Give him what he wishes. Fire!"

"Nooo!" Phoebe screams in terror as Percival's body spasms. Two more arrows plunge into his chest. When the archers nock two more, something unexpected happens. All of the horses in the troop begin to buck and whinney in terror.

One of the Privates cries out in alarm. "What in god's name?! Something's got the mares spooked!"

Phoebe stares at the back of Percival's head as he lays on top of her, his arms spread out protectively. Warm blood trickles from his chest, wraps around his body, and spills onto her light, leathery armor. "Percival... Percival..."

"Ph... Phoeb... e..." 

From across the field, a blur of motion catches one cavalryman's attention, even as his horse kicks and bucks. "O-over there! In the grass! What is- GWAH!"

A flash of green leaps from the grass, tears the youth from his saddle, and jumps away, dragging the poor bastard into the dense grass on the opposite side of the road.

Lorgon's eyes widen. "To arms! Abandon these damned horses!" He dismounts and lands on the ground, smacking his steed's ass to send it running away before drawing his sword. The other men quickly follow suit.

The screams of their fellow soldier in the field, loud at first, suddenly go silent. A sensation of terror appears in their minds.

The creature, animal, monster, or _whatever_ it is, leaps from the tall grass again. 

"Look out!"

A man yells and raises his shield, but the monster, a terrifying human-shaped crocodile, smashes against it, shatters the man's arm in fifteen places, and slams him into the ground. The green-scaled monster whips its bladed tail in a horizontal motion and slices five other men in half before they can react.

"F-form a circle! Attack its weak spots!" Lorgon bellows orders quickly, having more battle experience than the troops from the city, soldiers that Phoebe herself trained not a summer before.

"What weak spots?! AAAUGH!" A man explodes into a puddle of gore as the lizard-like monster roars angrily. "Flee! Flee before the might of Agares!"

On the other side of the men, opposite the Duke of Reptiles, a wolf howls. From the distant forest, a four-legged creature with fifty eyes bolts from its cover, dashing across the fields at speeds one would be hard-pressed to follow with their eyes. The creature, a _Hellhound_, moves at thrice the speed of a cheetah, tearing up the ground during its mad dash. The flesh-rending razor-sharp teeth in its mouth, capable of tearing apart the finest steel armor in the world, shine menacingly as the horrific monster charges the poorly grouped-up defensive line.

Lorgon's eyes bulge. "Brace for impact!!!"

_BOOM_. The Hellhound batters the wall of troops, sending them flying. Bones snap, shatter, and twist. Men rain from the sky. Before they can hit the ground, the monster leaps into the air and tears them apart ruthlessly.

Agares laughs. "Excellent work, Lucifer!"

"Shut up! Kill! Tear!" The female hellhound unleashes her ravenous claws upon the defenseless troops, sending them rushing away in a panic. Her claws shred their steel armor like wet paper.

Phoebe stares at the two demons in terror as Percival's eyes flutter shut. She turns to look at him. "P-Percival... stay with me. Stay alive!"

The knight smiles. "Phoebe... won't you say... you love me? Just this once? Just... just this..."

Percival takes his last breath. His soul leaves him, and his body slumps against the woman he loves. Phoebe stares in silent horror. Then, her eyes return to the two demons. When they draw near, she whimpers.

Agares leans down. "White hair. Is she the one?"

The hellhound stalks over to Phoebe, its monstrous face and smoky-black skin sending terror through her body. All fifty of its eyes lock onto her.

_Sniff. Sniff._ "I smell the scent of our brothers. She is the one the child spoke of. She will take us to our comrades."

Phoebe's lip quivers. "Who...? What?"

"Devil's sake, Lucifer. She's petrified." Agares delivers a bone-crushing kick to a soldier trying to crawl away, flinging the man hundreds of feet. The distant _whump_ of his body impacting the ground signals his demise.

Lucifer's animal body begins to shift and morph. Within moments, she returns to her demonic form, and all Phoebe can do is stare with a mixture of trepidation and awe.

_These two are Demon Emperors?!_

Lucifer's muscular body and dark red skin contrast with the tight black leather she wears. Hundreds of razor-sharp teeth line her mouth, but Phoebe's eyes stare mostly at four massive breasts, each one the size of a cow's udders. Coupled with her wolverine claw-like hands, the demon woman appears to be a mixture of several species of animals.

When Lucifer turns to look at Phoebe, a third eye emerges on her forehead and locks onto the human. The eye, Phoebe notes, has several circular rings and is shaped like an archery target, with a red pupil smack in the center. "You're lucky, fleshbag. You saved my sisters. Were it not for that; I'd tear you apart like the rest of your kind. Get up!"

The demoness flings Percival to the side and yanks Phoebe up roughly by her arm. Phoebe whimpers at the demon's powerful grip. "Aaaoo! You're hurting me! Let me go! I need to bury-"

"What are you blabbering about?" Lucifer raises her claws to strike Phoebe. "Take us to the dungeon! I will see my brothers freed, or your life will be-"

"Lucifer!" Agares bellows, silencing the woman. "Calm yourself. Save your rage for those worth fighting. Use your nose and eye. What can this weak little girl do for us? We don't need her. We'll save our comrades ourselves."

Lucifer scowls at the reptile, but lowers her claw and releases Phoebe anyway. "Tch. I don't need a _Duke_ to give me orders."

"Got something to say?" Agares hisses, shooting her a look of disdain. "I can't wait to hear it."

Lucifer rolls her eyes. "You always play the sympathy card."

The Demon Emperor of Providence leans toward Phoebe and sniffs like a mad hound. Then, Lucifer grabs the top of Phoebe's head and forces the human to look into her third eye.

An impression of weightlessness envelops Phoebe for a moment, making her feel as though she's starting to float into the air. The sensation vanishes abruptly when Lucifer tosses the human aside like a ragdoll.

_Crack._

Phoebe lands fifteen feet away and nearly breaks her arm when she hits the dirt. "Aahhn..."

Lucifer doesn't give the human a passing glance. She turns to look at Agares. "Found them. Our brothers are trapped inside a dungeon adjacent to the castle. You'll have to slow down Arthur and his knights. I'll rescue our men."

Phoebe stares wordlessly as the two demons bicker, ignoring her entirely.

"You expect me to handle the Hero-King?! You're the Third Emperor! You fight him, and _I'll_ rescue the prisoners. Besides, your hellhounds will fare better against his knights."

Lucifer hisses at the lizard. "Fine, scared little frog. Send your critters into the catacombs and rescue our warriors. Time's running short. Based on what I saw, they've already killed at least a few of our kin. I won't let the filthy fleshbags embarrass us, not today!"

Before the two demons move to run away, Lucifer swivels her head and glares bloody daggers at Phoebe. "You've been spared. Should we ever meet again, expect death. I hate the stench of your kind."

Turning away, the demoness morphs once again into her fifty-eyed demonic-dog form and howls. From the forest, a chorus of other animals wail in return.

Phoebe shudders as thirty massive Hellhounds, all shaped like the Demon Emperor, come bursting out of the trees toward her. Lucifer growls at Agares. "Come on, then! Let's go." 

She and the lizard-demon dash away, tearing apart a few straggling soldiers in their path.

Many minutes pass.

Eventually, Phoebe turns to stare at Percival's cooling corpse. She crawls over to him and rests his head in her lap. The light in his eyes, now gone, make a chorus of memories wash over her. 

She remembers all the times he would rub her muscles after her training, or when he would offer excuses for her tardiness at the Round Table. She remembers the side-glances he gave her when they sat beside each other at the Round Table, as well as the time when he covertly left a rose beside her bed when she fell sick with the flu.

Phoebe weeps uncontrollably. _Percival. You always looked out for me. How did I repay you? How did I repay my king? I betrayed you and him both. Now a horde of demons descends on Camelot. What... what am I supposed to do? Everyone put their faith in me... but... but I..._

Phoebe buries her face in Percival's neck and sobs for several minutes as she presses him close. Distant screams from the city rattle her mind. So do the horrible howls of the Hellhounds tearing villagers apart.

She eventually pulls herself back together and cradles his head for a few moments longer. Standing up, she lifts his heavy body, exerting her toned muscles with all her strength. She walks a few hundred feet away and lays him on the ground, then draws her sword, hacks at the dirt, and slowly carves out a pit.

Her tears don't stop. She digs and digs and digs until finally she's torn up enough ground to make a shallow grave. Gently, she places her comrade's body in the dirt, staring at him for only a moment before piling the soil on top.

She jams her sword into the ground at the head of his grave, then gets up, and slowly walks over to the wagon and horses.

_I have nowhere to go. Nowhere to call home. I'm no knight. I'm a failure. I betrayed my people for the sake of demons._

She unhooks both horses, keeping only one for herself. 

_I will wander these lands. I will fast until I find death atop a mountain. Death by starvation. A fitting punishment for a sinner such as I._

She rattles the reins and nudges the horse to go forward, hardening herself to the cries behind her.

_Survive, Arthur. Never forgive me._

---

Phoebe doesn't meet my eyes. She stares at the ground for several long moments.

"So, you see, Jason... I failed everyone. That's all I am, nothing but a failure. I've never killed anyone in my entire life, but I stabbed my king and my nation in the back. I couldn't protect everyone from Beelzebub's attack. I'm nothing but a useless, pathetic, weak woman."

I swallow, unsure how to reply. Several moments pass. I decide to hug Phoebe. "You're not a failure. You made a hard decision. The way I see it, those demons would have attacked the city anyway. They were there to free their captured comrades. All you did was save thirty innocents."

Phoebe shakes her head, wiping her face against my chest. She pushes me away. "That's not true, Jason! Don't you see? Percival died because of me! All that happened thanks to my betrayal was death and suffering!"

"I don't think so." I ponder her story and its implications. "Look, the humans were torturing those demons. Your gut told you to save the women because you care about everyone equally. What if you had left them there to die? Would you feel better about respecting your king if it meant such acts of barbarism could continue? You sacrificed your standing with Arthur to save lives."

An odd feeling dawns on me. "Hell... you're better at self-sacrifice than I am. I couldn't surrender my morality to kill Amelia. Hoarhiim had to do it for me. You made an impossible decision. Besides, you don't know that Arthur would have punished you. Maybe he would have been lenient."

"He wouldn't." Phoebe's voice holds a note of finality. "You don't understand how much humans hated demons in those days. How much they still do."

I reach over and take her hand in mine. "Perhaps not. I didn't know the guy, so I can only guess at his thoughts."

Phoebe smiles faintly. "As I said, I saw him during the battle on Polaris. My heart nearly stopped. I wanted to ask him... ask him how he felt, and if he hated me."

"What if he did?" I counter. "Would you have killed yourself? Don't you think that after 100,000 years, you can forgive some of your past? Nobody's perfect, Phoebe. Not you, not Arthur... certainly not me. Compared to Amelia, you're a saint, and I still believe she could have repented for her wickedness..." 

My voice trails off as Amelia appears in my mind again. Phoebe notices, of course, and blushes.

"Sorry. I hate that we keep returning to Amelia," I say. "The sadness on your face hurts me as well."

"No, no, it's not your fault. It's mine. I'm telling you to forgive yourself, but here I am still tearing myself apart." Phoebe chuckles. "Haha... to be fair, my past is distant. Yours is recent. You've barely had time to heal. Perhaps we both have something to learn."

"Yeah."

We stare at each other for several moments.

I look away. "So, uh... that Percival guy. Blonde hair and blue eyes. Just like Arthur. Just like me."

I can almost hear the smoke coming from Phoebe's ears as her face turns crimson. "J-just a coincidence."

"Maybe. Someone told me about fate recently. Maybe there are no flukes in nature, you know? Perhaps everything happens for a reason."

When I turn back to Phoebe, her smile is gone. "Did Percival die for a reason, then? Did Arthur?"

Sighing, I pull away from her and aim my mind a short distance away. "I don't know, Phoebe. Sometimes death is senseless. It has no reason or rhyme. People die, and you can't save them, no matter how you try."

The Crown of Solomon activates as I form several mental images. "**Truck!**"

Before us, an exact copy of Phoebe's ten-ton monster-truck appears in midair, then slams into the ground. 

Phoebe smiles. "You remembered."

"Of course, I did. I love you too much to forget."

She sticks her tongue out. "I love you more."

"I know."

She smacks my arm. "That's not what you're supposed to say!"

"I know."

"Jason!"

"Haha, I'm joshing around. C'mon, let's go for a drive."

The two of us walk over and climb in her truck, but before she nudges the accelerator, Phoebe blushes and smiles at me.

"I love you, Jason."

I smile back.

"I love you too. Forever and always."
