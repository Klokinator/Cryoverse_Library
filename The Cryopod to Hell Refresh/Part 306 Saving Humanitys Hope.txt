Part 306: Saving Humanity's Hope

Ten hours later, Jason and Phoebe finish assembling a small but lethal army of humanity's remaining elite commandos, few as they are. After explaining the situation to them, the Hero ends up with General Chadwick, Lieutenant Samuel, Corporal Hurent, Belial, and even Yamir, the minotaur cook.

In addition to these leaders, five thousand human commandos come along, all outfitted in various types of lethal weaponry. A few of them wear basic T-REX exosuits, but the majority don specialized Spectre, Rhino, Hummer, and Clinician battle-armors.

Jason and the rest gather up at the top of the Fortress of Retribution's plateau, waiting for the final preparations before they all head to the Warpgate and head inside the Labyrinth. Already, the midday sun has set, and darkness covers the land.

"Is this everyone?" Jason asks.

Phoebe nods. "Pretty much. We just need to get the star of this little show."

"I don't trust her." Belial snaps. "How can either of you?! Jason, don't tell me you're letting your old feelings cloud your judgment! This is Amelia we're talking about, the bloody Black Queen! Let me remind you, she killed many millions of people! She's pure evil! She even killed my HUSBAND!"

Privately, Jason think to himself that killing the Devil was likely doing the universe a favor. However, he doesn't give voice to that thought.

"I know you hate Amelia, but she doesn't have a trace of magical power anymore," Jason says. "Whatever method Hope used to bring her back, it seems to have stripped away her powers. Either that, or he removed them after the fact."

"I don't care about that!" Belial hisses, barely able to keep her voice down. She shoots a sidelong glance at the commandos and commanders standing at attention, ready to move out as soon as the Hero gives the word. "Don't you think we're bringing too few soldiers? Five thousand isn't even worth sneezing at! If that girl is some pawn of some Emperor, and if she's lying to us, we could all die dog deaths! Don't you see how dangerous this whole situation is?"

"The fact stands that Hope hasn't returned, and I can't sense him with my Wordsmithing," Jason says, his voice calm and steady. "Or, more specifically, I _can_ sense him, but his presence is so weak and scattered that I can't get a lock on him, let alone a general location. He's still alive, and Amelia can lead us to him. I owe him this rescue, even if it puts my life at risk."

Phoebe gently rests a hand on Belial's shoulder, opening up her helmet so she can look the succubus in the eyes.

"I don't trust Amelia either, Sam. Not even a little. But her love for Hope is real, it's genuine. She definitely lied about certain parts of her identity, but not about Hope. We have to rescue him... we _have_ to."

Belial opens her mouth. "But-"

"No buts," Jason interrupts. "If you don't trust her, then keep your eye on her. I don't intend to let her out of my sight. Better yet, tell me everything you can about Gressil. I barely know anything about him, other than what I saw when I first awoke in the Labyrinth. Just thinking about his appearance gives me the shivers."

Realizing neither Jason nor Phoebe will back down, Belial relents. She takes a step back and sighs.

"Um... fine. Fine. Gressil is, uh... well, to be honest, I'm not entirely sure about him. I know he was Lucifer's adopted son, and Ose's older brother. I don't know anything about his abilities or rank, though. I never personally interacted with him, certainly not in the past fifty millennia. I've never been one to hang around other demons."

Jason frowns. "That doesn't give me any useful intel. He's the prison-warden, so he must be decently high ranking, right? Is he a Lord? Baron? Duke? Emperor?"

Belial holds up her hands helplessly. "I don't know. Frankly, the only thing I do know about Gressil is that nobody likes him, and many fear him. He's... reclusive. Hostile. Violent. He tortures prisoners to the point where rumors say you can hear their screams even outside of Faith's End."

She pauses for a moment, as a faint memory appears in her mind.

"Oh, I do remember one time I saw him. It was before the end of the Energy Wars. Demons and humans were right in the heat of their fighting, the most violent part of that conflict. I met with Lucifer out of obligation, not desire. Satan needed me to speak to her about some mundane matter, and he was too busy to talk to her himself."

"And you saw Gressil?" Phoebe prompts.

"Yes. Him and Ose both. They were... well, they weren't a happy family. Gressil wasn't a bad person at that time, at least that wasn't the impression I got. He actually seemed like a decent guy. He sat off in a corner while Lucifer and Ose grilled me on the information I was delivering. Something about tactical reports of soldier movements. Anyway, I glanced over and saw Gressil... playing with butterflies."

Jason blinks twice. "Um. Butterflies?"

"Yes. Glowing green butterflies. I remember thinking that they were quite eye-catching. From what I remember, they were somewhat fake-looking, as if they came from a dream. Far too beautiful to be from this world. I made a mental note that they must be illusions. If you hadn't prompted me, I'd probably not recall that moment... it's just..."

Belial lowers her eyes.

"...Gressil seemed so _sad_. The butterflies looked more like a ritual he used to calm down, and less like something he did for fun. He seemed like such a distant person, someone unaccustomed to contact with other demons."

"Like a coping mechanism?" Phoebe asks.

"Rumor has it that Lucifer was a somewhat unpleasant mother," Belial answers. "And from all the times I had to bite my tongue and suffer talking to the bitch, I can't see how that assessment was wrong. She hated males more than anything else in the world; a trait Ose inherited. I can't imagine Gressil had a pleasant upbringing."

"Fantastic." Jason snorts. "So he might have mommy issues. One of you two, be sure to give him a good slap if he acts up."

Neither Phoebe nor Belial laughs.

"That's all I can remember," Belial concludes. "He probably has some sort of illusion magic, and he's mentally unhinged. We'll have to act carefully around him."

"You're the Second Emperor, even if the other demons now refuse to accept that title," Phoebe says. "It's _him_ who should be afraid of you. Not to mention we have humanity's Hero right here. If Jason uses his Wordsmithing seriously, no demon will stand a chance against him. You saw what he did to Bael."

Belial hesitates for a moment before replying.

"...I suppose you're right. Although, you shouldn't speak so highly of me. After all, I never seem to win battles, these days. I'm not as good as I used to be. Even Ose... she beat me senseless."

"That was just a bad matchup," Jason comments. "She poked at the gaps in your armor, your emotional blind spots. If it was a contest of pure strength, you'd never lose."

"You can say that if you want," Belial replies. "But I don't believe it. Not anymore. Since Satan's death, I... I just don't have the capacity to fight with all my heart anymore. There's a gap in my chest, a void that I can't fill up with anything. It always weighs me down... every single day."

"Sam..." Phoebe whispers. "I'm always here if you need to talk."

"Thanks, Phoebe," Belial says, offering a weak smile. "That means a lot to me."

Belial takes a minute to compose herself, while Jason leaves the two women to walk over to General Chadwick and Lieutenant Samuel.

"Do you know anything about Amelia?" Jason asks. "You and Neil were close."

The portly Norwegian puffs up his chest. "We _are_ close, Jason. Neil is still alive. I know it in my heart."

"Right. But do you know anything about Amelia?"

"Afraid not," Chadwick says. "I know Neil kept many secrets close to his chest. Amelia is probably one of them."

"So you're telling me nobody knows if she's really one of ours or if her story checks out?" Jason asks. "Even if I assume she might be telling the truth, I cannot fathom what method Hope used to bring her back to life. It's beyond me."

Lieutenant Samuel lifts his chin. "You can't bring people back from the dead? I thought your Wordsmithing was practically omnipotent."

"It's not, I can assure you of that," Jason answers. "I tried a bunch of different methods for bringing people back. Nothing worked. Solomon even explained what the revival process would require... those who die end up having their souls pass on to what he called the 'Great Beyond'. I don't know exactly what it is, but it seems to be some sort of unbreakable barrier in the universe, or physics, itself. You need a tremendous amount of magical power just to pull a soul back. I doubt even my new Cube-tool would have the power necessary."

Chadwick and Samuel shoot glances at each other, unsure of what to think.

"Yeah, this bitch don't pass the smell test for me," Samuel says. "Somethin' ain't right about her. I think you ought to talk to Debra. Seems Debra knows something. With your Wordsmithing, you could make her talk."

Chadwick shakes his head. "I don't approve. Debra is Neil's most trusted confidante. She's on our side, and certainly wouldn't do anything to put humanity in harm's way."

"Yeah?" Jason asks. "Like that other kid? What was his name... Henry? You guys sang his praises, but he was the one who trapped Neil inside the Core. Trust seems to be given out a little too sparingly these days. Let's not forget the infiltrators who killed Elder Skarde, or the ones who took over the Atlas Cannon. Our people are still only human, General. You'd do well not to put too much faith in them."

"Henry was... an exception," Chadwick murmurs. "I deeply resent his actions. Stupid kid. Foolish brat. I don't know how Ose coerced him, but we'll be carrying out an investigation after I return from this mission. I have him locked up and under heavy surveillance."

Jason waves his hand flippantly. "Whatever gets the job done. Give me a minute. I'll go and pay Debra a visit. Amelia should be done chatting with her, by now. It's about time we all head out."

"Yes, Commander." Chadwick says, as he takes a step back.

"**Teleport.**" Jason says, vanishing with a flicker of movement.

...

A moment later, Jason reappears a quarter of a mile away, outside the makeshift stone house built for Neil's assistant, Debra. Like the several-hundred other buildings in the immediate vicinity, it contains only the barest amenities, hastily thrown together to provide a simple shelter from the elements. It lacks even a single window, looking more like a generic cube slapped into the middle of the barren wasteland around it.

Jason gives the house a once-over, then takes a step forward. Hardly has he begun his approach before the front door opens with some effort and a fully exosuited Amelia steps out, with Debra following behind her.

"I was about to go and join up with you and the others," Amelia says, her faceless helmet providing no window into her expression. "But I guess everyone must be ready sooner than I expected."

Debra remains silent, merely gazing at Jason from behind Amelia, her eyes giving no insight into her thoughts.

Jason faintly smiles, though his expression lacks any warmth. "Yeah, we're ready. Debra, I hope you two had a fun conversation."

Neil's assistant nods. "We did, yes. Thank you for giving us some privacy."

Jason's eye twitches. "Either of you mind finally telling me what's going on? Why the secrecy? If I wanted, I could have Wordsmithed to listen in on your conversation, but I didn't. I'd prefer to hear the truth from your mouths."

Debra flinches, only to immediately relax her posture.

"It's... it's better if you don't know, Jason. Not right now, anyway. You should focus on saving Hope first, and Neil, too. They'll tell you anything relevant once the time is right."

"So you _are_ hiding something from me," Jason growls. "Well, what is it? Spit it out!"

"If you want to know, you can always just read our thoughts," Amelia says, her tone flat. "Don't pretend like you've ever been good at keeping your curiosity satiated. I seem to recall you peeping on me during a particularly... ugly moment in my life. If you want to know, just look. It's not like I can stop you."

Jason's anger becomes mollified. He averts his eyes and shakes his head.

"No. Never mind. I... I won't do that. Not to you."

For a moment, neither Amelia nor Debra says anything.

Debra eventually takes a step back to return to her house. "Make sure you return safely," She says, before walking away.

After Debra steps inside her home and closes the door, several awkward seconds of silence pass before Jason raises his head.

"We should really be going." He mutters.

Amelia's helmet dissipates into nanite particles. The moment her face appears, she locks eyes with Jason.

"It wasn't your fault, you know."

Jason blinks. "Pardon?"

"Hoarhiim... he's the one who, um... who hurt me. You never wanted that. He forcibly took over your body and made you do... all sorts of awful things to me. It wasn't your fault."

The Wordsmith frowns deeply. "I never said it was."

"Yes, you did. Or rather, Hope did. Many times. The look of guilt on his face... you and him share all the same memories prior to his creation. I know you still feel a lot of guilt, just as he does."

Jason's frown turns ugly. "I don't know why you're bringing this up-"

"Because you need to hear it!" Amelia interjects. "Don't think I don't see the way you look at me, Jason. You aren't as unbreakable as you pretend. I understand just how much you've lost. Not only me, but Kar, as well. I didn't get to say goodbye to him. Then your daughter, too... your heart must be in immense pain, but you just keep all those emotions bottled up. Hope is no different. He also hides his suffering, not wanting to 'inconvenience' anybody."

Jason's expression softens. "You don't know me. I've changed a lot in the last six years."

"I know you have." Amelia replies. "I can see it in your eyes. But you're not the only one who's changed... so have I. Right now, you have the same eyes I did, all those years ago... when Satan took Levvy from me."

"Levvy..." Jason mutters. "Leviathan? The dark dragon?"

Amelia crosses her arms and looks away.

"Dark dragon... no. He was a good boy. A hurt soul. He lost all his brothers, his sisters, and his mother, too. He was lonely, just like me. All anyone else ever saw in him was a scary monster, or a beacon of power. He just wanted to be left alone. He gave up everything to save me. Not once, but twice. The day Satan killed him... I just wanted to rip the whole universe to shreds. And now, you have that same vicious hatred bubbling under your surface."

For once, the Wordsmith doesn't offer a snarky comment. He merely lifts his eyes to look out at the darkened horizon, and the stars in the sky.

"Well. I guess you're not wrong. I am still... utterly enraged. Heartbroken. I don't even know how I feel. It's just a tangled mess in my stomach."

"It will consume you." Amelia says. "Hatred begets hatred. That's what Hope taught me. I want to be a better person. The problem is... I still have that hatred in my heart. Only now... it conflicts with this other feeling..."

Amelia pauses, unsure how to phrase the next part. She gives up and shrugs.

"I can probably explain it better later. For now, let's just get going."

Jason nods. "Yeah. Let's do that."

After a few more moments of silence, Jason speaks again.

"**Teleport.**"

He and Amelia vanish from the spot, reappearing smack in the middle of his assembled army. Amelia sweeps her gaze across the five thousand soldiers and frowns.

"Only this many?"

"They'll be enough." Jason says. "I would like to rescue as many people from Gressil's dungeon as I can. Considering how narrow and cramped the Labyrinth corridors are, we'll have a good choke point if the demons try anything."

"Rescue people, huh?" Amelia mutters. "Well, I've seen the condition of his prisoners. There won't be many worth saving..."

"Even if we can only put them out of their suffering, that will be enough for me," Jason replies. He turns to see his wife and Belial walking up. "I brought Amelia. We're ready to go."

"Took you a few minutes," Belial says. "Hope you two had a good _chat_."

Jason shrugs. "We did."

While Belial maintains an air of relative hostility toward Amelia, Phoebe appears remarkably less so. Knowing she has a spiritual self inside of Jason mollifies any worries she might have about his feelings for the former Black Queen.

Belial puffs out her chest as she approaches Amelia.

"Listen here. I don't like you. In fact, I hate you. I don't want to speak to you, or even look at you. Understand?"

Amelia bites her lip. "Because of Satan."

"Yes!" Belial snaps. "I never liked you, not even one bit! Frankly, I should have killed you myself during that battle against the Archdemon! I tried my damnedest, but failed because of my own weakness. Now, there's nothing you can do to stop me. If you make even _one_ shifty sidelong glance at anyone here, I'll cut your head off!"

Amelia remains unmoved. "I don't think you will, Belial. You're not that kind of person."

Belial's right hand suddenly morphs into a gruesome-looking executioner's blade, a massive one-handed scimitar with a curved blade.

"TRY ME! I've already killed more demons in the last week than my entire existence before now, a thousand-fold! Adding a husband-killer to the list won't even make me bat an eye!"

Still, Amelia appears unmoved. She shoots a look at Jason, Phoebe, and Belial in equal measure.

"You've all changed in the past six years, and not for the better. That's... too bad. But understandable."

She stares Belial in the eyes.

"You hate me for killing Satan... but you weren't even there on that day. Do you even know what happened?"

"I know _enough._" Belial replies.

Amelia shakes her head. "After we save Hope... you and I should talk. There's... something you don't know."

"Is it about Lucifer?" Belial asks, a malicious smirk on her face. "I know she set Satan up. If she were still alive, I'd have a bone to pick with her, too."

"Lucifer? No." Amelia replies. "I didn't know she had anything to do with him showing up when he did. No, it's more about... I'll tell you later. Every minute we delay is another minute Hope suffers."

Belial snorts. "It must be nice. You still have a chance to rescue the man you love. If only the _rest of us_ were so lucky."

Phoebe steps in between Amelia and Belial. "Alright. I think you two have said enough. Jason, we're ready. Let's get moving."

The Wordsmith glances in the direction of the distant Warpgate, far to the west, now surrounded by heavily reinforced walls on all sides.

"Yeah. Let's go. I just pray we're not too late to save Hope..."

...

As the Wordsmith's army departs to leave the city's confines, a single trooper appears behind them, kitted out in a stealth-type Spectre exosuit.

This trooper watches for several minutes as the Wordsmith's rescue team departs Tarus II through its Warpgate and heads deep into the Labyrinth.

His helmet opens up, revealing the face of Makoto Ueda.

"Hmm. The Hero leaves to make a long journey. His path is uncertain. The ramifications of his actions make peering beyond the veil of Karma... difficult."

The man holds up his hand. A spark of spiritual energy appears for a moment, then vanishes, erased by the weakness of his veins.

"My abilities have weakened after so many reincarnations. It will not be long, now. I must find my successor. Orias... he alone holds the key to unraveling the Great Mystery."

Makoto Ueda's eyes glow pure white for a moment. His helmet closes up, sealing his head away inside his exosuit. Then, his body shimmers and turns invisible. 

He makes his way toward the Warpgate, heading for a dangerous destination.

He must locate his successor.

He must find the future Karmic King.
