Part 310: Little Lost Birdy

_Creeeeak._

The prison door marked 'Forbidden' slowly opens. Its ancient, rusted hinges squeal as if suffering from arthritis. Two commandos step into the room and swivel their guns toward a dark corner.

"What in the name of-?!"

The first trooper shouts in shock. His eyes fixate on the figure laying on her side, curling against the dark corner, terrified out of her wits. Multiple shackles and chains bind her ankles and wrists, tying her to the dungeon wall. Given her body's weakness, she stands no chance of breaking free.

Jason, Phoebe, Amelia, and Belial all enter a moment afterward. They follow the trooper's line of sight, and all four of them wince sympathetically.

"Oh my god," Phoebe whispers. "That... that poor girl."

"What's happened to her back?" Amelia asks. "Did... did Gressil experiment on her?"

"No." Belial says, as her expression calms down. "Those are wings. She's an angel."

A bolt of shock travels through everyone present.

"An angel?!" Jason asks. "But her wings! The feathers! Th-they're..."

"Stripped bare." Phoebe adds, her tone filled with heartbreak. "Oh, lord in Heaven... what horrors has this woman suffered?"

While the humans all hesitate, Belial quickly steps toward the angel on the ground. Her body, covered in hundreds of deep cuts, lacerations, burns, and scars, makes her look more dead than alive. Her dull blue eyes flicker weakly, yet she still tries to push away from Belial as the demoness leans over her.

"I won't hurt you," Belial whispers. "I'm here to help."

The angel's eyes widen in horror when Belial speaks. Without saying a word, her gaze flicks to the horns atop Belial's head, her blood-red eyes, and her crimson skin.

"Nnn... nnn..."

The angel's blood-matted white hair drags across the ground as she pushes herself into the corner with what little strength she has, shrinking back from Belial in terror.

"Sam, she's scared of you," Phoebe says. She quickly steps in front of Belial, blocking her from the angel's view. "Let me."

Belial flushes with embarrassment.

"Oh. Right, yes, I don't imagine she would... like my kind..."

The succubus retreats, while Jason and Phoebe kneel over the angel and pop open their helmets to reveal their faces. They activate dim lights on their suits to illuminate themselves so the angel can see them.

"You're an angel..." Phoebe says. "Poor girl. Poor, poor girl. Don't worry. We're here to help. You're safe now."

Jason bites his lip. "Her body's in bad condition. I can't Wordsmith to heal her. Belial's magic won't work, either. We definitely can't move her with her body this injured."

"I have a solution," Phoebe says, as she keys in a button on her exosuit. "I'll treat her with nanites. We can heal her once we reach the Core."

"Alright," Jason says. He waits for a few moments while his wife materializes a nanite-injection syringe. His eyes sweep across the naked and mutilated angel.

"I'm Jason Hiro," He says, trying to offer some comfort. "I-I'm a human. Not just any human, but a Hero, like King Arthur, or Joan of Arc. Do you know about Heroes? We're the good guys. I definitely won't let anyone hurt you, okay? Never again."

The angel looks at him, but says nothing. Her body language loses a small amount of tenseness, but otherwise, she still maintains a sense of vigilance.

"Her face might not be injured," Phoebe explains, "but her throat is far too scarred for her to speak."

"That's alright," Jason says. "She doesn't need to talk. We just need to heal her and get her the hell out of here. We must be close to finding Hope."

From behind Jason, Belial speaks.

"Have either of you noticed? There's a halo above that angel's head."

Jason turns to look at Belial in confusion, then back at the girl. He finally notices a weak, barely noticeable, circular ring of light hovering above her head.

"A halo? Oh, I guess that makes sense. Angels always have halos in fiction... but I don't remember seeing them in any of Raphael's visions..."

"Angels don't have halos." Belial says. "Not pureblooded angels, anyway. But Lazarites do."

Phoebe perks up her ears. "Lazarites? Wait, do you mean humans resurrected into angelic form? Like the ancient biblical story of Lazarus?"

"That's right," Belial says. "Through the power of the Lazarus Tower, the Archangels created a method for dead humans to be reborn as a pseudo-angel, a halfblooded creature known as a Lazarite. This girl isn't a pureblood; she was once a human who resurrected through the Lazarus Tower's magic."

Jason falls silent. In his head, he connects multiple pieces of a puzzle based on what Raphael told him during his flashbacks of the Ancient Era.

_The Lazarus Tower... Valac's Lantern.... Resurrecting humans into angelic bodies? That was the purpose of the tower? I see. That would have allowed the angels to rebuild their forces following the War in Heaven, giving them new angels capable of fighting the demons. I knew there had to be more to the story! And when Mephisto stole back Valac's Lantern..._

"I'm ready," Phoebe says, as she holds up a syringe. "Don't worry, 'miss angel.' This will heal you."

"Phoebe presses the needle into the angel's heart and pushes the plunger. A few seconds later, she extracts the needle, noting with a sad expression that the angel didn't even seem to notice the needle's presence, so terrible was her pain.

At a speed visible to the human eye, the angel's wounds begin to heal. While none of her scars disappear, the worst-festering wound seal up, and some of the rotted injuries manage to cleans themselves. Unfortunately, her right leg appears too far gone, with its entirety black from gangrene.

Phoebe chews her lip. "If she were a human, she'd have died countless ages ago. It's a miracle she's still alive."

"Or a curse," Jason snarls. "Gressil did this. That son of a bitch... I'll make him pay."

At the mention of Gressil's name, the angel's eyes widen in fear. She discovers a wellspring of strength and pushes away from Jason and Phoebe, curling back into the corner once again, even crushing her broken and mutilated wings by doing so.

"No, no," Phoebe says, shooting a dirty look at her husband. "Don't just say his name, Jason! Here, send me back to the Core along with the angel. I'll take care of her until everyone else returns."

"Are you sure?" Jason asks.

"Yes. We can't leave her here a moment longer."

Jason sticks his thumb over his shoulder. "Then take Belial with you. She can heal the angel."

"No." Belial says. "If Gressil truly is an Emperor, you're all in danger here. I'll stay with you until you return. The angel has survived this long without any medical care; she'll only do better with Phoebe in the Core."

Jason hesitates.

"...I can't go back until I find out what happened to Hope. Alright, Phoebe. You take the angel back to Doctor Fathy. Belial and I will continue with the rescue operation."

Phoebe nods, then turns to look behind her husband. "What about you, Amelia?"

"I'm staying here," Amelia says. "I'm not here for some angel girl. I'm here for Hope."

"Alright." Phoebe replies.

Jason's wife snaps the rusted shackles binding the angel's wrists and ankles. Removing them reveals disgusting, pus-filled scar-tissue. The angel's rail-thin body tells a tale of absolute starvation, torture, and barbarism worse than any horror movie could depict.

Phoebe places a teleportation beacon on the angel, then on herself. She offers Jason one last look.

"You'd better return. Don't you dare make me a widow."

Jason offers a weak smile. "I'll return. Promise."

Phoebe activates the beacons, teleporting herself and the angel out of Gressil's prison and back to the Core.

The Wordsmith rises to his feet. A strange look crosses his face.

"I'll have to ask Gabriel about that girl when we return. Can't contact him now. Belial, did you recognize the angel?"

The succubus shakes her head. "Nope. There were billions of Lazarites at one point. The angels used them as meatshields in the war against my people. In the final battle between angels, demons, humans, and the Volgrim, rivers of blood flowed through Heaven. I thought every last angel had died... but to think Gressil had secretly hidden one away beneath everyone's noses."

Amelia anxiously looks toward the doorway. "Whatever, she's just a Lazarite. Look, can we get back to searching for Hope? He's the reason we came here, you know!"

"Yes," Jason says, "But the fact an angel has survived past the Energy Wars is extremely important. There must be some reason Gressil kept her here. Aren't you even the slightest bit curious?"

Amelia lowers her helmet, revealing a cross expression. "No, Jason, I don't care about some stupid angel girl. Maybe if she were a pureblood, I'd care, and even then, that's a stretch. Hope is more important! Besides, something about her..."

The former Black Queen trails off, as if uncertain about a feeling in her heart.

"Fine, let's get back to looking for Hope," Jason says. "Who cares, anyway? She's just the only living angel we know about. No significance at all, no sir. Heaven forbid I might hold some curiosity over her identity... grumble grumble..."

The Wordsmith, Belial, and Amelia exit the cell, flanked by the two troopers from before. Jason informs General Chadwick of their discovery, as well as the other leaders with him.

Chadwick narrows his eyes. "A Lazarite, you say? I've heard of them. Did she have the signature halo above her head?"

"Yes," Jason says. "But how would you learn of Lazarites?"

"It was an open secret among the higher levels of human militaries," Chadwick explains. "Back in my day, I served under King Haakon as one of his generals. During the Great War, most soldiers and civilians had no idea that angels and demons truly existed, but the upper ranks knew well. I'll bet you aren't aware that the most pious and godly believers could even earn special favors from Heaven in the event of their death."

Jason blinks. "Special favors? Such as?"

Chadwick glances down the halls before continuing. "Those who prayed a lot and performed meritorious deeds for the angels would have the unique right to reincarnate as High Lazarites. Effectively the same as normal Lazarites, but they could lead legions of their own into battle. There were other privileges, but..."

"But now isn't the time to talk about these things," Jason says, picking up on Chadwick's meaning. "Tell me about that stuff later. In the meantime, let's keep looking for Hope!"

...

Half an hour passes. The humans continue sweeping through Faith's End, but all they find are more mundane prisoners, not Hope.

"We've searched almost everywhere," Corporal Hurent says, "There aren't many cells left to check. If Hope isn't in them..."

"We haven't run into Gressil either," Belial says. "I'll bet he took Hope and ran away! That devil-damned coward."

Hardly have the words left Belial's mouth before a shudder runs down her back. Purely out of instinct, she spins on her heel and glances down one of the side corridors, where, in the darkness, a little blonde girl stands, all by herself.

Belial's eyes shrink to pinpricks.

"No! Impossible!"

Jason follows her gaze.

"Daisy?!"

The sight of his daughter standing in a random Labyrinth corridor sets off alarm bells in the Hero's head, but those instantly collide with panicked thoughts of how his daughter might have somehow survived and ended up here.

Then, 'Daisy' speaks.

"Haha. Does this form surprise you?"

A deep, masculine voice speaks from the child's mouth. Belial snaps out of her shock first, then narrows her eyes.

"Gressil! You... how dare you summon this... illusion!"

The little girl smiles creepily.

"Illusion? Haha. Haha. Is that what I am? Daddy, look at me. I'm your little girl. You killed me, daddy. Haha. You won't even cry for me. You must not love me. Haha. Haha."

Gressil's deep, raspy voice violently conflicts with the image of a sweet little girl. Thanks to the disharmony of voice and appearance, Jason manages to shake himself awake, realizing that this sick illusion before him is not the daughter he lost.

"You piece of shit. How **dare** you toy with my emotions."

'Daisy' continues to grin, exposing her toothy teeth.

"Oh, but your pain gives me such joy, Wordsmith. Haha. Every human has a weakness. Every demon. Even the angels..."

The little girl cocks her head.

"You took something from me without asking. That is stealing, you know. Oh, but this weak demon does not stand a chance against the powerful weapons of humanity. I guess I'll have to meekly stand aside. You can have my beloved Birdy... but you'll have to treat her well. A very bad person hurt her several times, and now she doesn't scream like she used to."

"BASTARD!" Belial shrieks. Before anyone can stop her, the demoness lunges toward Gressil and rears her fist back.

**BOOM!!!**

Belial swings her fist down at Gressil's body, but her punch passes through him harmlessly and smashes into the floor, rattling the Labyrinth for a mile in every direction, even causing some of the human commandos to stumble and fall.

After the explosion of dust settles, Belial raises her head and involuntarily jerks in fright when she finds the Emperor of Chaos standing right in front of her, his figure having returned to its normal form. No longer does he assume the visage of Jason's deceased daughter, but instead, his own two-headed body covered in glowing red eyeballs.

Several commandos aim their guns at Gressil, but nobody pulls the trigger, as they wait for orders from their commanding officers.

"Such a brute." Gressil murmurs. "Demons like you are the worst, Belial. You always hurt those around you. You, especially, like to play the 'good girl.' You're so kind, so empathetic. You wouldn't hurt a fly. Isn't that right? You're not like me, a _monster._"

Belial retracts her fist and takes a step back, eying Gressil warily. The Emperor of Chaos remains rooted in place, seemingly unmovable, like a mountain of flesh. He slightly leans toward the succubus and sneers.

"Ah, but that isn't true, is it? Look at all the demons you've killed. It was only 'self-defense,' of course, but you did seem to enjoy ripping them to shreds with your claws. Hmm. Maybe you and me aren't so different after all."

"Shut your mouth!" Belial roars. She launches another mountain-shattering punch at Gressil, but her strike once again passes harmlessly through him as his body vaporizes into smoke.

Gressil's voice speaks again, but this time from behind the wall of human commandos, on the complete opposite side of the corridor from where he started.

"Like I said. We aren't so different."

Jason, Belial, and everyone else whirls around 180 degrees, shocked at the Emperor of Chaos's rapid teleportation.

"W-where is Hope?!" Amelia shouts. "I know you have him!"

Gressil's smirk falters. He shoots a look of disgust at the woman.

"**Quiet.** No false image may speak to me in that tone. It seems you haven't told the others who you really are. But I know. I know all too well..."

Jason's eyes flick to Amelia's armored figure. Her exosuit prevents him from seeing her expression and gleaning a clue as to her emotions.

"What do you mean, Gressil?" Jason asks cautiously, returning his eyes to the Emperor of Chaos. "And why should we trust anything you have to say?"

The prison warden eyes his heroic counterpart.

"I am many things, Wordsmith. A liar is not one of them. Believe what you like, or don't. By the way, I would like to congratulate you on your... evolution. You've filled out quite nicely since my sister's little operation. Already, you've killed a number of demons not inferior to the blessed Joan of Arc... but you've a ways to go before you reach Arthur's level."

"I don't keep a running tally," Jason says. "Every life lost is a tragedy. Pain and suffering is NOT commendable! I only kill because I must."

"Of course. _Of course._" Gressil replies, his tone creepily soothing. "Ah, but there are trillions of demons in the universe. You haven't even dripped a drop in the bucket yet. You _have_ succeeded in agitating the greed of the Hidden Emperors, I'll give you that much. They'll make a move soon to attack you. I will certainly enjoy the show, when it occurs."

Belial arrives at Jason's side. She eyes Gressil carefully, realizing she cannot sense whether the body before her is real or illusory. Without knowing, she doesn't dare to attack again.

Instead, her eyes drift to the second head on Gressil's body, the one hovering over his left shoulder.

"...Wait. That... that face."

A look of recognition appears in Belial's eyes.

"Lucifer?"

Gressil's expression darkens.

"You only just noticed? What a pitiful Second Emperor you are. Nobody questioned that woman's disappearance all those years ago. I didn't bother telling them, either. Not after the price I paid to finally... _silence_ her."

Belial's pupils shrink and dilate repeatedly.

"Oh, Seven Hells... you _ate_ her?! Your own mother?"

"I DID NOT!" Gressil suddenly roars, as he takes a menacing step toward Belial. The sounds of several guns readying to fire does nothing to stop him as he stomps toward the Wordsmith's battle lines. "I JUST WANTED HER TO SHUT UP! TO NEVER SPEAK AGAIN!!"

Seeing the advancing figure of an Emperor, Jason gives the order. "Open fire!"

Hundreds of bullets, plasma shots, and other assorted ordinances burst into existence and fly at Gressil, however, all of them pass through his body, dissipating him into the wind.

An instant later, as if stepping out of the air itself, Gressil reappears inches from Belial. Her heart skips a beat, but she fails to evade him as he lunges out his upper-right hand and grabs her by the neck.

"SATAN'S WHORE!" Gressil roars. "YOU SHOULD HAVE SAVED ME!!"

Several guns swivel to face Gressil's new position. They unload on him, but he vanishes once again, teleporting behind the army with Belial in hand.

**BOOM!**

Gressil smashes the Emperor of Passion against the wall. He raises his lower right fist to punch her, but Belial retaliates with a punch of her own!

**THUMP!**

Gressil instantly loses his grip and flies backward, crashing into the wall behind him just a few feet away. He plows through it, into an empty prison cell, and lands on the floor in a heap.

Belial's lips curl into a nasty snarl. "Don't think you stand a chance against me, Lucifer's spawn. If we come to blows, you'll always end up with the short stick."

Gressil lays on the floor in the cell, motionless, for a few brief moments. Then, he slowly raises his head.

"Don't say her name."

"Who? Lucifer?" Belial asks. "And why not?"

The moment she finishes asking that question, Gressil's otherwise dormant second head begins to stir. Both of its eyes open, along with the third one on its forehead.

"Hmm? What's all this racket? Brat! Are you trying to piss me off?!"

Gressil's left arm curls into a fist. Belial tenses up, expecting him to lunge at her. Instead, in a shocking twist, Gressil punches himself!

**Thunk!**

He punches his right head, the primary one he used before. In that moment, Belial makes a few mental connections.

_Lucifer is... alive? Inside of him? And she can possess his body? But how?!_

Gressil winces from the punch.

"Mother... mother, no-"

"Don't even speak to me!" Lucifer shrieks, her shrill voice grating on Belial's nerves. "You're worthless, just like Diablo! Pathetic little insect that he was!"

As she speaks, Lucifer happens to glance in Belial's direction. She pauses and blinks, while a sadistic smile crosses her face.

"Ohoho! Well, well, well, look who it is! Satan's Bitch, the queen slut herself!"

Lucifer jumps to her feet, taking full control of Gressil's body as her consciousness awakens more and more. A fervent look of excitement passes over her face as she greedily eyes the former Second Emperor up and down.

"You look as slutty as ever, succu-brat! I should have known you'd come here to seduce this ignorant boy of mine!"

"Seduce Gressil?" Belial asks, narrowing her eyes. "I wouldn't dream of it. I have standards."

"Bitch!" Lucifer shrieks. "He might be a pathetic little shell of a man, but he's ten times the demon you'll ever be! A thousand of you couldn't compare to my brat!"

Belial flicks her gaze down the hall, to Jason and the others. Realizing they won't be of much help if Belial and Lucifer come to blows, they hesitate on what to do.

Belial flicks her hair behind her ear. "I didn't come here to yap at you, Lucifer. I came here for Hope. Give him to me. _Now._"

"Hope? What hope?" Lucifer asks. "Hahaha! You've gone off your marbles, Belial! If you want 'hope,' I'll give it to you! I'll torture you so badly that you'll 'hope' I kill you!"

Lucifer lunges at Belial and raises all four of Gressil's fists to attack. At the same time, the third eye on her forehead glows red for an instant.

_Zot!_

A beam of concentrated mana fires from the Emperor of Providence's forehead, strikes Belial's chest, and slams her against the wall. Before Belial can retaliate, Lucifer strikes her with all four fists.

**Boom-boom-boom-boom!**

Like four hammers crashing against an anvil, Lucifer batters Belial hard enough to shatter the Labyrinth wall and knock her into the empty cell behind her. The two demonesses crash onto the floor, but Belial retaliates by shoving Lucifer back with all her strength.

Belial's power, shockingly, exceeds Lucifer's by several orders of magnitude.

Lucifer flies backward, caught off-guard by Belial's strength. She crashes into and drags across the ceiling, eventually arriving in the corridor outside where she falls down and slams into the floor.

"Unf!"

Lucifer gasps in pain, but quickly jumps back to her feet at the same time as Belial.

"Hahaha... you're as strong as ever..." Lucifer growls. "But I can't detect even a little magic from you. It seems in this battle I hold the advantage."

Lucifer smiles menacingly, while Belial doesn't.

"If you won't give me the Wordsmith willingly, I'll just have to _take_ him from you." Belial says.

Lucifer spreads out her arms and chuckles.

"I don't know what you're talking about, and I don't care. If you want something from me... you're free to try and take it. You don't have Satan here to protect you anymore. I _made sure_ of that."

Belial's eyes snap open.

"LUCIFERRR!!"
