Part 147: The Trojan Horse

**Author note: Part 73 takes place between this part and the last one. Just a heads up.**

---

Uriel, the Archangel of Protection, valiantly stands before Heaven's Gate. Using her vast arsenal of spells, she holds back the demonic hordes, preventing them from entering Heaven.

However, even as she drives the demons back, a sense of foreboding washes over the Archangel.

_Shik._

"Gah?!"

Uriel gasps in shock as her beloved brother, a man she's known for eons, thrusts his hand forward and plunges it into her chest. Michael jabs his knife-like fingers through her ribs with one hand and grabs her by the throat with the other. Too late, Uriel realizes her brother should only have one functional arm.

Uriel's body swoons. Before she can react, Michael spins her around and smashes her against the enchanted metal floor, knocking the wind out of her. The Archangel of Courage releases his grip on Uriel's throat and transforms his arm into a sword-like weapon. Uriel's eyes widen in horror as her brother dispatches all four of her constructs in the blink of an eye. His scyther-like limb blasts around the room at ten times the speed of sound, eviscerating them before they can react.

As Uriel's summoned beings transform into particles of light, Michael retracts his arm. His knife-hand, still stuck deep inside her chest, flicks around, cutting and severing other tendons. Tears form in Uriel's eyes as horrific, crippling pain combines with the shock of what's happening, rendering her paralyzed and helpless. 

Slowly, Michael's face shifts and melts, along with his robes and physique. Three seconds later, a black-haired succubus sits atop Uriel, transforming Uriel's shock over her brother's betrayal into terrified confusion.

_A demoness? Who... who is she?! How did she assume Michael's appearance?!_

Belial retracts her hand from Uriel's chest. She keeps her expression neutral, despite forcing down bile. "You left me no choice. I won't kill you, though. My husband has business with you."

Uriel chokes on blood as she manages a weak response. "Huh... huh... hus...band?"

"Satan. The leader of the demons. I apologize, but it didn't have to end this way."

Belial summons Artorias and has him stand guard over the Archangel of Protection. She warns him to keep Uriel alive, but not to let her heal herself.

After quickly finishing up with the others, Belial leaves Uriel in Artorias's capable hands, along with his annoying little brother, Barbatos. The young demon eyes Uriel greedily, but Belial pays him no mind. After all, he's only a Lord. Artorias should be able to handle him if he does anything stupid.

Turning around, the succubus once again transforms, but this time into Uriel. She reaches down to grab Gungnir and Gae Bolg, only to yelp as Camael's artifacts explode with holy energy in her grip. Belial bites her lip and curses, then kicks them off to the side.

"I don't need them, anyway."

Belial heads for the portal. The demons filing into the chamber watch her intently as she steps through.

...

Once on the other side, 'Uriel' quickly swivels her face around to gaze at the angels landing nearby. Thousands of angels levitate in the air, gently flapping their wings to stay aloft, while hundreds more surround the portal to Earth from every direction. Among them are the four Valkyries, Camael, and Raphael. They breathe a sigh of relief at seeing her face. 

_Michael isn't here yet. Neither is Gabriel or Uzziel. Thank goodness. They'd have figured me out in an instant,_ Belial thinks to herself. _Looks like the old man just got here. It won't take Raphael long to see through my disguise, but he's already too late._

Camael wipes his forehead. "Oh, Uriel. Thou art a sight for sore eyes! Where is Michael? Why didn't he come out with thee?"

Belial quickly scowls at Camael, forcing herself to act out Uriel's hardass nature. "He's relieving me for the moment! I left my summons to help him. Do you mind if I catch my breath? Is that too much to ask?!"

Camael flinches, surprised by her sister's reaction. "N-nay, of course not. Thou hath held off the demons for an hour by thyself. Still, Michael alone can't possibly..."

Before Camael finishes her sentence, she flicks her gaze to Raphael and trails off. The old man stares at 'Uriel' with a piercing gaze, like that of a hawk who has seen many field mice in his lifetime and knows one when he sees it.

"Uriel? Thy mana reserves dwarf ours by a wide margin. Never woulds't thee allow Michael to stand alone against the demon hordes when push came to shove, yet here thou standeth, while he reaps the glory."

Belial stares evenly at the aged Archangel.

_Raphael knows. He was there the day I used my illusion magic to help capture Samael. I bet he's already pieced everything together._

Belial's eyes flick to a tree perhaps a hundred yards behind the small army before her. Despite Heaven's light coming from all directions with enough intensity to prevent the formation of shadows in wide, open spaces, a single smudge of blackness sits at the tree's base like a panther awaiting the approach of prey. It shifts slightly when Belial's eyes fall upon it, and Belial nods as casually as possible.

_Berith._

The mightiest succubus returns her attention to Raphael. His eyes narrow as he rapidly evaluates the figure standing before him. With a start, Belial realizes why he hasn't ordered the others to attack.

_Ah! He doesn't know I'm a shapeshifter! He must suspect one of the demons are mind-controlling Uriel!_

Setting her jaw, Belial comes to a decision. Even as Sophia, Penelope, and the other Valkyries gaze at her with looks of relief, the succubus coils power into her legs and prepares to pounce.

Raphael gasps. "She's not-!"

_FWOOSH!_

'Uriel' leaps at the wall of several hundred angels standing in the plains. Her fists lash out like mighty sledgehammers and instantly batter two-dozen angels in their chests, sending them flying into the back lines. Chaos erupts as confusion sweeps through the angel's ranks.

"Ah! Uriel has gone mad!"

"Stop her!"

"Sh-she's too powerful!"

The angels above react much faster than those on the ground. Almost immediately, several thousand archers summon bows made from Holy Energy and begin firing at Uriel, though many of them pause, as they are afraid of accidentally striking their brothers and sisters.

Raphael motions with his hands, and a thick mist shrouds him. "Unleash all of thy powers, everyone! Don't hold back for even a moment!"

Belial swings her fist at him, but the old man vanishes, evading her attack at the last instant.

As 'Uriel' starts hammering angels from left to right, the others quickly realize her abilities don't match up to that of their sister. Never before has their sister held such monstrous physical strength, let alone the ability to bend and stretch her body like putty.

"That's not Uriel! She's an imposter! She stole Uriel's face!"

"Do not hold back! Use lethal force!"

While all the angels focus on Belial, a sudden flash of movement strikes them from behind. Berith, the Emperor of Backstabbing, leaps from beneath one angel's boot. The slight shadow formed between his foot and the ground is all she needs to transport from her tree to the middle of battle. Instantly, dozens of shadow-lances erupt from the boots of nearby angels and fly at their comrades. Horrified screams rise and fall as several cherub's bodies explode. 

Berith pounces at two angels and swings her palms sideways, judo-chop style, severing their heads from their shoulders. She hits the ground and flings both angel's heads into the portal, then spins around, grinning, as demons suddenly begin pouring into Heaven.

"We've been deceived!" Camael yells. "Summon the others!"

She's too late. A massive surge of grunts and Lords rushes forward, charging the frontline angel guard like lions hunting impala flesh.

Two grunts leap toward Sophia, but she swats them away with her staff without breaking a sweat. Tears well up in her eyes as she gazes at the face of her 'sister'. "What dids't thee do to Uriel? Where is my sister?!"

Penelope swings her hammer in a wide arc and sends three demons flying backward. "She's dead, Sophia! Don't let these bastards get away with murder! Avenge our sister!"

Catherine's jaw trembles as she hunkers down and prepares to bullrush the one wearing her sister's face. _I won't forgive thee._

Like two waterfalls crashing upon each other, the demons and angels collide with the fury of Mother Nature herself. Belial does her best to batter and smash nearby angels, but she always holds back enough to prevent herself from killing anyone. Berith rapidly fires shadow-balls and shadow-lances at any angel she sees, but unlike Belial, she has no qualms about killing everyone in her way.

"Ehehehe!" Berith cackles cruelly, as she summons a fist of darkness. "This is more fun than I expected! With Uriel out of the way, none of these weaklings can stop me!"

Her fist flies toward Hyacinth, but Penelope swoops in and destroys the shadow-projection with her greatmace before it can inflict any damage. "Hah! That only proves thou art a weakling! Any fighter worth their salt would cherish a challenge!"

Hyacinth jumps into the sky and begins releasing wind-arrows rapid-fire at Berith. This time, when the projectiles hit the ground, she twists her fingers at the last second to summon miniature tornados, which catch Berith by surprise and yank her off her feet. 

Berith hurtles upward into the sky, yelping as she tries to regain her footing. Suddenly, dozens of angelic archers begin firing holy-energy imbued arrows at her.

_Whiff whiff whiff! Shik shik shik!_

"Gaaugh!"

Berith howls in pain as glowing missiles lance through her shoulders, arms, legs, and half a dozen tear through her chest and gut. By the time the tornado dies down, she barely manages to teleport to a shadow on the ground and pop out near Belial.

"Gahk! B-Belial! Help me!"

Belial, now engaged in combat with Camael, winces as the Archangel of Divination swings Mjolnir with incredible strength. Belial dodges at the last second, and the mighty hammer strikes the ground with enough energy to send a minor earthquake throughout the nearby area. Dozens of angels and demons cry out in alarm as they stumble and lose their footing.

"I'm a little busy!" Belial yells as she ducks a horizontal swipe from Camael's hammer. "G-give me a few minutes! AHK-"

Before Belial can finish her sentence, Camael's hammer smashes into her left shoulder.

_FWUMP!_

Camael's downward attack crashes into Belial like a freight train. Belial screams in pain, only to vanish from where she was standing as the impact flings her several hundred feet down into the soft, uncompacted dirt of Heaven.

_Grrrrn!_

Belial grinds to a halt and gasps. "Huhk..."

Above her, a pinprick of light is the only thing she sees beyond the walls of dirt engulfing her.

_Aaooo... h-hurts..._

Belial grits her teeth as the pain from a shattered left arm finally breaks through her disorientation. Her limb rests behind her body, badly mangled from Mjolnir's power. Agonizing spasms, caused by holy energy, wrack her spiritual presence. The enchanted hammer, forged to combat dragons, proves just as deadly against demons.

Belial spends several seconds gathering her breath before motioning with her right arm to heal the left. Finally, she shifts her body and scowls.

_That hurt. A lot._

Her hands morph again, but this time into shovels. She dives into the ground and begins burrowing toward the enemy's flank.

...

Camael twirls her hammer in her grasp. She and Raphael quickly survey the battlefield as demons and angels dogpile each other, ripping their enemies to shreds.

"Michael will arrive soon," Raphael says. "We must hold out until he appears. What of Gabriel and Uzziel, though?"

"I haven't seen either of them today," Camael replies. She quickly spins on her heel and swings her hammer up, catching one demon grunt unawares as he battles a nearby cherub. His back explodes, spraying guts all over the female angel. She flinches at the sudden burst of gore, but quickly realizes Camael was her savior. With a hurried nod, she turns and jumps back into the battle.

Camael continues. "Uzziel has probably sensed the battle by now. As for Gabriel and Michael, well... they shall surely follow their sister."

"Indeed. I didn't expect the hellspawn to make it into Heaven. We were supposed to hold out at the entrance to Sinai and slowly whittle them down. Bah! What changed?"

Camael's expression darkens. "I know not, but we certainly underestimated the demons."

"That we did," Raphael murmurs while motioning with his hands. The old man fades into mist, and his disembodied voice speaks one last time. "We mustn't do so again. The fate of Heaven depends on us!"

A slight shimmer in the air is all the evidence remaining of Raphael's existence. The old man vanishes into the aether, leaving Camael to defend herself.

Suddenly, Camael shivers. From the portal to Heaven, several powerful demon presences enter the battlefield.

Bael, the Duke of Pain. He steps through the portal first and scowls. "Oh, look! Everyone's having fun already. Sheesh. _Couldn't put me at the front, eh, Satan?_"

The Duke of Pain looks around in annoyance, a chip on his shoulder. Behind him, an old-looking demon steps through the portal; The Duke of Reptiles. "Damn. It's a massacre out here. We should have shown up sooner."

Following Agares, a woman steps through. Vepar, the Baron of Toxicity. She hunkers partially behind Bael, a hint of worry on her face due to the beating she took from the Valkyries. "Tch. Two Archangels are already here. We'd better take them out before they cause any trouble."

Bael groans. "Humf. You two, go take a nap. Now that the big guy's here, this battle's pretty much over. I'll beat every Archangel's ass all by myself."

Vepar doesn't bother trying to dissuade him. Instead, she motions to Agares. "Remember Satan's plan. We have a job to do."

Agares nods. He motions with his hands and begins transforming into his powerful reptilian form. Green scales cover his skin, and a thick tail sprouts from his rump as he lowers his body into a nimbler fighting pose. "We need to try and take out one of the others, at least until Michael arrives."

Both demons stay low and dash into the throng of allies and enemies. Within seconds, Vepar's poison gas strikes nearby cherubs and seraphs, making them howl in pain, while Agares tears up their enchanted armor with his powerful reptilian claws. He turns his tail into a battering ram and slaps aside two angels at once, while tanking a sword slashing his back. The weapon glances off his scales, and the angelic attacker falls a moment later when Agares tears his throat out.

Camael shivers. _The demons are more powerful than I remember. My visions told me today would be a dreadful day for angelkind, but surely there must be a way to triumph. This battle is a test sent to us by the Creator!_

The Archangel nervously twirls her hammer once again and locks eyes with Bael. The big guy smirks as he eyeballs her from several hundred feet away.

"Heh. It looks like grandma wants a go at me. I don't think I've thrown down with her before. Now's as good a time as any."

Bael balls his meaty hands into fists. He strides confidently toward Camael, a giant grin plastered across his face. One angel swoops down from behind him and slashes at Bael with his sword.

_Fwap!_

The weapon snaps in half when it hits Bael's back.

Bael pauses. He turns around to stare at the cherub with a look of confusion.

"The hell? You got a death wish, punk?"

The male cherub stares at Bael with wide eyes, shakes his head slowly, and flies away, leaving Bael to scratch his head.

"What kinda moron picks a fight with someone who can stomp him like a bug? Sheesh. These flappy birds ain't got no common sense."

With a dismissive wave of his hand, Bael turns around and continues wading through the warring demons and angels as he heads toward Camael. If anyone ends up in his path, whether fighting for their lives or just out of bad luck, he lightly shoves them aside, flinging them hundreds of feet to his right and left.

"Outta the way, dumbasses. I've got a date with yer mom's mom."

Finally, Bael stops a dozen feet from Camael and crosses his arms. He smirks like a man who knows with absolute confidence that he can't lose. "Nice hammer y'got there. Do ya hit pretty hard with it?"

Sweat drips down Camael's forehead. _I recognize this demon. He's the one Hercules and Zeus warned us to avoid._

Camael grimaces. "If I'm not mistaken, thou art Bael, the infamous Balrog. Rumor has it that thou nearly slew the Titan King and his son all on thy own. Are the reports true?"

"Nah," Bael says, waving a hand dismissively. "It was a team effort. The others softened 'em up for me... a little. How about'chu? Think you can put up a fight with that little toy of yours?"

Camael narrows her eyes. "Some extra firepower wouldn't hurt."

The Archangel of Divination motions with her left hand. After forming several complex magical signs, she mutters several Latin words under her breath.

_Shoop!_

A massive tower shield materializes in front of her, then impales the ground a moment later. A helmet with Valkyrian wings appears atop her head, while Solomon's Crown tops it off. A golden belt wraps around her waist, and a pair of golden boots finish off her brilliant ensemble. It only takes me an instant to identify Hermes' Boots and Orion's Belt, but the rest of her armor appears unfamiliar to me.

Camael grabs the shield and takes a defensive stance. When she speaks, the helmet slightly muffles her voice. "I will bury thee as thou did my sister."

Bael's smile disappears. He uncrosses his arms and hunches down to ready himself for action. "Gimme a good fight, lady. I'm bored as hell."

Camael tilts her head. Then, she vanishes.

_WHAM!_

Mjolnir strikes Bael's chest so hard that a shockwave sends every nearby demon flying. Hermes Boots augment her speed in the same way they once did mine, transforming her into a bolt of lightning, while Orion's Belt gives her the strength of an atom bomb.

_BOOM!_

Hardly has Bael started to lose his footing before Camael jumps above him and brings her hammer down, smashing him into the ground the same way she did Belial.

Before Bael can plunge into the soft, supple soil of Heaven, Camael grabs him by his throat and flings him up into the sky. She pounces after him, springing atop invisible platforms as Hermes Boots provide a hardened surface for her to kick off, even in midair.

Jump, jump, swoop, _bang_! 

Camael's blinding speed allows her to batter Bael from left to right, up and down, and finally send him catapulting into the mountainside above Heaven's Portal.

_CRUUNCH!_

Bael's body breaks loose boulders and soil and sends a landslide tumbling to the mountain's base. Seconds later, hundreds of tons of debris crush the low-ranking demons flooding into Heaven while burying the portal.

Camael pauses. _Ah! I blocked the portal! Since Uriel didn't deactivate it, that might mean this is our best shot of preventing the demons from entering Heaven!_

...

Bael blinks. Horrible pain rings in his head from his brains being thrown around like jelly beans in a jar. The Duke of Pain tries to make sense of the dirt pressing against his face, but it takes several seconds before he can reorient himself.

"Guh... a-alright... that dame was tougher'n I thought... prolly shouldn'a looked down on her..."

After taking a moment to gather his strength, he yells out loud, "Hup!" and flings himself out of the collapsed mountain. Bael tears through dirt and stone in the blink of an eye and pounces onto the battlefield like an excited jaguar.

As he looks around for Camael, Bael raises his eyes to the sky and notices a massive contingent of angels flying toward the battlefield at alarming speeds. Leading them are Uzziel, Gabriel, and Michael.

A flash of anger appears in Bael's eyes. "Michael... tch. And here I thought this day couldn't get any better. I'ma pay you back for what you did to the boss 'n me, punk."

Bael spots Belial healing Berith, while also yanking enchanted arrows out of the shadow-walker's body. Occasionally, an angel will lunge down at them in an attempt to end the lives of two Emperors, but Belial easily dispatches them. After ten seconds, she finishes healing Berith and helps the Emperor of Backstabbing stumble to her feet.

"Don't thank me all at once," Belial quips, "but I just saved your life."

"Gratitude," Berith growls back. "Meanwhile, Camael blocked the portal to Heaven, and the other Archangels are nearly here! We're trapped! What should we do?"

Belial forces a smile. "No. We're not the cornered ones, Berith. The angels are. They're stuck here with us."

The two of them look around, and Belial raises an eyebrow as Bael jumps over and lands beside her. "You two done gabbing? Get ready. Michael's coming, and he's _pissed._"

A massive platoon of angels numbering in the millions covers the sky in winged demi-gods — their wing flap in unison, like a hive-mind hellbent on the demons' destruction. Behind them, two hundred massive Titans lumber through Uzziel's beautiful forest. They shove trees aside and howl bloodthirsty warcries.

Belial's blood chills at the sight of the approaching army. "Oh... dear. We're going to need a _lot_ more demons for this battle."

"Or a Balrog," Berith whispers, as she nudges Bael.

Bael ignores her. "Don't worry! Those chickens ain't got nothin' on me'n Satan!"

Hardly have the words left his mouth before a torrential downpour of winged beings descends from the skies. They swoop toward the demons below, notch their bows and arrows, and let a hailstorm of holy weaponry fall upon their prey.

_Whiff whiff whiff!_

Razor-sharp arrows formed from each angel's mana impale hundreds of demons in the blink of an eye. Several Lords fall, even with their guards raised. They, along with many grunts and imps, perish without knowing what struck them.

_Whamwhamwham!_

Thousands of angels smash into the dirt and grass all at the same time, kicking up clouds of dust that blow toward the battlefield. They draw their weapons and rush the demons, sending terror through Hell's ranks as the surviving warriors gasp in fright.

"R-reinforcements! We've gotta get outta- guwak!"

"Where's the portal?! Where is it?!"

"We're trapped! We're all going to die! Ahh!!"

Terror turns into panic as the demonic forces fall to pieces. Every demon begins fighting for himself as they rush toward the only way out of Heaven. However, their frenzied flight comes to a screeching halt as everyone realizes the angels took out the Burrowers in their targeted attack.

Suddenly, Gabriel, the Archangel of Power, stops mid-flight, closes his eyes for a moment, and begins to concentrate. A light like that of Earth's sun builds in his palm, dwarfing Heaven's Light with its incomparable brilliance. Many demons shriek and cover their eyes, but some are too slow. Their retinas sear to ash instantly, leaving them permanently blind.

Not that it matters.

Gabriel swings his palm down and aims it at the throng of demons trying their hardest to dig out mounds of dirt to flee Heaven.

"**Heavenly Beam of Holy Destruction.**"

A concentrated blast of holy energy fires from Gabriel's palm. It's the biggest, deadliest, most overwhelming holy attack ever seen by any demon. Not even the combined might of hundreds of cherubs unleashing a similar attack on Valac came close.

_KABOOM!!_

Gabriel's devastating energy wave plows into three thousand demons at once. They open their mouths to scream, but their bodies transform from flesh to ash before they can make a sound.

_FWOOM!_

Gabriel quickly shifts his aim to target the two Emperors and Bael. His unstoppable beam drags across the plains below, incinerating any demon caught in its path while sparing those born from holy energy, namely, other angels.

Berith's eyes bolt open. "Ah! Get down! It's coming!!"

She quickly motions toward the tree she first hid behind, several hundred feet away. In an instant, she teleports into the shadow of its roots, leaving Belial and Bael to fend for themselves.

Bael doesn't have any time to come up with an escape plan. Instead, he spins around, wraps his arms around Belial, and shields her with his body. "Don't let it touch you!"

Heaven trembles under Gabriel's nightmarish power. His colossal beam smashes into Bael with the divine equivalent of a hydrogen bomb, but Bael never lets go of Belial.

Even with the Duke of Pain enveloping her in his arms, Belial screams wordlessly, as waves of holy magic splash around Bael and buffet the ground like a solar storm of annihilation. Small trickles graze Belial's skin here and there, charring her body to ash wherever they hit, but still, Bael never releases her.

After ten seconds of concentrating with all his might, Gabriel gasps. He lowers his arm, and the incredible power within him slows to a stop.

Bael twitches as the onslaught of angelic energy finally ceases.

Seconds pass.

The Duke of Pain releases Belial. "Yo. You okay?"

Belial sags to her knees and flops on her back. Black scars cover massive portions of her arms, legs, and back. She stares at Bael, and a question forms in her eyes.

"How... how did you... survive..."

Bael bites his lip. He gently turns around and glances at his back. Smoke pours from his body, while charred black skin peels off his back.

"...I'm too tough for a weak little attack like that to kill me. But... damn... it _really_ fuckin hurt."

_Slam!_

An angel impacts the ground behind Bael, catching the Duke's attention. Bael raises his eyes to examine the new arrival.

"Here to finish me off after letting your big brother do all the heavy lifting, eh?"

Michael glares at Bael. "Bloodskin. To think one of thy ilk could survive Gabriel's mightiest attack... my condolences go out to thee, for now thou shalt face me instead. I will give thee a much slower and more agonizing death than Gabriel could."

Bael swivels around to face the Archangel of Courage. Camael and Gabriel land in the grass behind their brother and take up defensive positions, the somber looks on their faces betraying their disbelief that Bael survived such an overwhelming display of holy energy.

"Psh. You think I'm scared?" Bael asks, smirking. "You just royally pissed me off. I'm gonna rip all three of ya limb from limb."

The Duke of Pain carefully positions himself between Belial, who continues to writhe in pain on the ground, and the three Archangels, who won't hesitate to kill her if they have a clear line of attack.

Michael licks his lips. Ignoring his right arm, which hangs limply at his side, he uses his left to draw his sword and point it at Bael.

"Death comes for thee, bloodskin. Any last words?"

_BATOOM!_

Behind Bael and Belial, the rubble pile that buried Heaven's Portal suddenly explodes outward with incredible force, almost as if someone had detonated C4 inside of it. Despite only a couple of hundred demons remaining inside Heaven, all of them turn to gawk as three even more powerful beings step through the portal, followed by a swarm of lower-ranked demons ten times greater than the one before it.

Diablo, the Emperor of Annihilation. Lucifer, the Emperor of Providence. And, the mightiest of them all, Satan the Devil.

Satan pauses to rest both hands on his hips. He grins from ear to ear. "I'm sorry we're late. Lucy had to waste a few extra minutes on her makeup."

Lucifer rolls all three of her eyes. "Devils. Don't blame me, when it was _your_ idea to make a dramatic entrance."

Diablo's eyes sweep across the battlefield. He absorbs the sight of a million angels flooding the skies above, and the Titan army just now arriving on scene.

"I hope you know what you're doing, Satan. Can't say I like the idea of a suicide mission."

Satan doesn't even look at Diablo. "Have faith, chum. Everything is going to work out _just_ fine."

All of the Archangels flinch when they sense the overwhelming dark energy flowing from Satan's body. Uzziel's eyes widen as she lands between Gabriel and Camael. "Impossible... how could one being possess such limitless mana?"

Raphael materializes beside Gabriel. "'Tis as thou believeth, Uzziel. The last time I sensed such power, 'twas when we slew a dragon. Be on thy guard. The powers of soul manipulators mustn't be underestimated. Who knows what a journey the Devil has traveled to achieve this potential?"

Satan continues to smile as he stares at the frozen battlefield. However, the moment his eyes fall on Belial's shivering, beaten body, his expression turns somber.

"The fuck are we waiting for? Kill the angels, you beautiful bastards."

His command elicits a single response from all of the demons pouring into Heaven.

"**Yeahhh!!**"

With all the combatants on the battlefield, the War in Heaven reaches its second phase.
