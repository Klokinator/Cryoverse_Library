Part 277: Killer in the Shadows

_Hero City, one hour earlier._

Mara, the Baron of Games, darts around inside the human encampment, relying on her invisibility to evade the humans' detection systems. Thousands of heavily armored troopers patrol the streets, march quickly to reinforce command points, and otherwise keep on the alert, using every trick in the book to search for enemies in their midst.

Mara presses her back against an apartment building's wall. She motions with her hands and flicks a magical Tarot Card out of her sleeve, then whispers under her breath: "Ice Orc, **Imbue.**"

A layer of frost coats Mara's body, lowering her temperature to below the surrounding area's ambient levels. As an added bonus, her strength increases significantly, giving her a boost of fighting power.

_Okay. For the next ten minutes, I'll be able to keep my body temperature low enough that the humans won't detect me on their heat-scanners. Not bad for a Silver Aspect. Now, if only I can find the Hero's family quickly and without any fuss..._

Mara flicks out another card, then activates its effects. "Three-Eyed Gecko, **Enchant.**"

The Baron of Games enchants the Gecko's illusion-piercing eye onto a ring on her right hand. Then, she carefully pokes her fist around the corner and pivots it in several directions, using its all-seeing gaze to search for any traces of her prey.

_Over there? No. Maybe to the north? No. South? Drat! This city is much bigger than it appears! I must have at least ten kilometers of area to search, not to mention any hidden, underground facilities._

Mara stealthily slinks through Hero City's confines, her invisibility and low body temperature making it impossible for even the highly advanced exosuit troopers to spot her. Once in a while, she finds a soldier standing by themselves with their weapon at the ready. With a quick, sudden movement, she skitters over to them and snaps their neck, then burns the body and darts away.

Naturally, with troopers dying inside the city, humanity's forces go on the alert, but curiously, nobody manages to catch the assassin. As Mara spends ten minutes, twenty minutes, and even thirty minutes searching for Phoebe and Daisy, her expression becomes cross.

"Seven Hells! Where the devil are they? Don't tell me they already escaped! Ose will rip me apart if I fail here!"

Mara curses under her breath several times. She arrives at the eastern edge of the city, where powerful waves of mana emanate over the horizon, giving her an idea of the colossal-scale battle occuring between the Monster King, Kar, and his opponent, the Eldest Battle Brother, Zamiel.

Mara stops at the edge of the eastern cliffs and gazes into the distance. Her jaw drops as she observes the titanic battle unfolding within the valley.

"Z-Zamiel? Is that Zamiel?! When did he become a Duke? Why? How?! Was this part of Ose's plans? She didn't tell me anything!"

Mara's eyes grow as wide as saucers. She watches Zamiel trade blows with Kar, wincing whenever their fists and weapons connect.

"The Monster King is an Emperor-level powerhouse! How can a mere Duke stand up to him? There's something fishy going on here..."

Despite her misgivings, Mara still cheers for Zamiel in her heart.

_The Sphinx toyed with me. He embarrassed me, making me the fool during our clash. I won't forgive him. Kill him, Zamiel! You must succeed!_

However, she doesn't move to assist Zamiel, given that Ose's assignment has nothing to do with him. After observing the battle for a few minutes, Mara creeps away, slipping past a small battalion of human and monster reinforcements as they roll up to the battlefield.

"Just where exactly could the Hero's Wife be?" Mara muses. She sneaks atop the room of a four-story building in the middle of Hero City, assassinates two guards, then peers down on the city's center to get her bearings.

"Hmm. A group of ten soldiers over there, five to their north, and the hospital to the west... wow! The humans' hospital got annihilated! I guess the rumors about Belial were true."

Mara's gaze flicks to a lone figure wandering down the streets. An unarmored Salamander, a red-scaled monster holding a box of tools, shuffles to the west, toward the edge of the city.

"Aw, shucks. My back is killing me... but I can't just sit on my dad-gum rear while everyone else does all the fightin'. Gotta git out there and make m'self useful."

Mara aims her palm at the back of the monster's skull, reading herself to fire a burst of magic at him. However, after a moment, she pauses and shakes her head.

"Killing fleshbags is one thing. I won't hurt a sweet old lizard, though. Poor guy looks like he's had a bad enough day."

Mara resumes her search, scouring the city from top to bottom. After twenty minutes, she catches sight of a shimmer in the air to the north.

"Hmm? Wait, could it be? Ose mentioned something about... '_space-shits_.' Could the space-shits be over there?"

Mara slinks over to the northern side of Hero City. There, she spots a cordoned area with hundreds of troops patrolling, each one swiveling their heads from side to side to look for threats. However, past the cordoned area, the only thing Mara sees is a vast, empty field.

"Why are the humans protecting a whole bunch of _nothing_? They must think I'm stupid..." Mara mutters. She holds her fist up and shines the enchanted Gecko eye to the north. A visual imprint of some enormous, gigantic metal machine catches her eyes, making her gasp.

"Aha! I found it! The space-shit! ...Sheesh, what human came up with that name, anyway? It's idiotic beyond belief."

Having spotted one of the Covenant ships, Mara strokes her chin thoughtfully.

"I don't see the mother and daughter anywhere, but if these big flying machines can travel offworld, then I can't afford to let them leave. I had better destroy it before it's too late."

Mara flickers past the patrolling guards. She arrives not far from the Covenant vessel, then scans its interior with her Gecko eye. She observes more than thirty thousand humans already packed on board, but none of them resemble Phoebe or Daisy.

"Good. It seems the Hero's family hasn't arrived, yet. In that case, I will solve three problems with one move."

Mara grins devilishly. She flicks a card out of her Spiritual Deck, then places it back and draws another. For the next five minutes, she draws a card every ten seconds until finally pulling the one she needs.

"Hehehe. Farewell, fleshbags."

Mara coils strength into her legs, then leaps skyward. She jumps toward the top of the nearly-invisible Covenant ship, scaling its heights by adhering to its hull via magic. She rapidly climbs its half-mile-tall rocket-shaped body, then kicks off from its nose and pounces another three hundred feet into the air.

Wind whistles in Mara's ears. For a brief moment, she hangs in the sky like a fairy standing atop the apex of an invisible rainbow, then begins falling earthward. The moment her body starts to reverse direction, Mara holds her Tarot Card high and bellows in her loudest possible voice, "Thunder Giant, **Evoke!**"

Mara's rich mana pours into her sole Platinum Aspect, the strongest offensive card in her Spiritual Deck. As she starts to fall toward the Covenant ship below her, a tremendous fist made of thunder and lightning conjures into existence from the Tarot Card, then fires straight down at the planet's surface.

**BOOOOOM!!!**

The insanely powerful magic blast strikes the Covenant ship, compressing it like a tin can. Instantly, every human and monster aboard the vessel perishes, never knowing what hit them. The fist tears through the metal machine effortlessly and continues toward the ground below, where it crashes into Tarus II's soil and explodes, firing off thousands of lightning bolts in all directions.

Within two seconds, every exosuit-wearing trooper in the area screams and explodes, their bodies unable to withstand the power of Mara's magic. Her attack all but vaporizes the biological life within a half-mile radius, killing more than forty thousand humans in fewer than six seconds.

Mara plummets toward the soil below, then rotates in midair and lands deftly on her feet like a cat. She glances around for a moment, admiring her handiwork.

"See? Now _that's_ how an evocation of the Thunder Giant _should_ work! I can't believe it didn't even shake the Sphinx a little... just goes to show how durable that blasted crocodile is."

Mara shakes her head disdainfully, then turns to walk away. However, out of the corner of her eye, she spots a Felaris kitten lying amidst the crumpled spaceship's burning remains, half her body charred and scorched from Mara's evocation. The kitten lays on her back, coughing and gagging as blood gurgles and vomits from her mouth.

"Kuh... kuh... huh... hulp..."

The kitten's eyes pan from side to side frantically. Delirious and suffering from shock, her body starts to shake and tremble as the claws of death fall upon her.

Mara hesitates.

The Baron of Games turns to look at the sole survivor of her attack, someone who likely only endured thanks to a one in a million freak occurrence.

"Someone lived through that?" Mara mutters. She trots over to the kitten, but pauses as a painful sensation wells up in her chest.

"...That's right. I suppose there were children aboard the space-shit."

A hint of remorse appears in Mara's eyes. She glances around and even uses her Gecko eye to search the area, but doesn't find any other survivors.

Several seconds pass as she gazes at the dying child. The Felaris kitten raises a shaky paw toward Mara.

"Hull... hulp... muhhh..."

Guilt washes across Mara's face. Finally, she balls her hand into a fist.

"I'm sorry. I intended to kill everyone quickly and painlessly. Since I don't like to see people suffer, I'll put you out of-"

She rears her fist back to attack, only to pause mid-motion. Several expressions pass over her face as images flash through her mind.

_A human male._

_An olive branch._

_Faint, indistinct words._

Mara's eyes twitch. She tries to swing her fist down and kill the kitten, but once again, her body freezes up.

_If I manage to put an end to this stupid war, you should pop by the Core sometime._

_Pop by the Core sometime._

_If only I can put an end to this war..._

_Pop by the Core sometime._

Mara gasps for breath. She jumps back from the kitten, frightened by the uncanny feeling of déjà vu.

"What... what the hell is wrong with me?"

Unable to bring herself to deal the killing blow, Mara scrunches up her face and relents, digging into her Spiritual Deck to search for a card.

Somehow, on the first draw, she miraculously pulls the one she needs.

"Temporal Cricket, **Evoke!**"

She aims a spell at the Felaris kitten, bathing her in time-reversing magic. Moments later, the cat's wounds rapidly undo themselves, restoring the child to her state from five minutes before; well before Mara's bombastic attack.

The kitten freezes up and curls into a ball. She shivers uncontrollably as memories of the awful pain she just suffered linger in her head.

"Mommy... mommy... mommy, I want mommy..."

Mara looks away. Her eyes flicker with uncertainty. She takes several steps to the side, avoiding the kitten.

"Th-there! I saved you, okay? I saved you! That's all you get! Don't ever talk to me again!"

She quickly retreats from the Felaris child, leaving the kitten to fend for herself. Mara heads toward the south, where the sounds of approaching military troopers catch her attention.

Her expression dark, Mara rubs her eyes. She conjures another field of invisibility around herself, then darts toward a series of buildings, making herself scarce.

"Stupid. Stupid! Why did I do that? Why did I save that little girl? She won't have a mother to protect her anymore. There's no reason to... to help her! None at all!"

Self-doubt tugs at Mara's psyche, making her sweat bullets. It takes her five minutes of reflection before she manages to shake the feeling of existential dread weighing on her shoulders.

"I've never behaved this way, not in my whole life. If Ying-Ying were here, she'd definitely tell me I was acting strangely!"

Mara lifts her hand to touch a pair of earrings hanging at the sides of her head. She starts to inject mana into them, but pauses after a moment.

"...I don't have the time or luxury to sit around and chat with Ying-Ying! Forget it. I'll tell her later!"

Mara lowers her hands and shakes her head. Despite the muddled thoughts plaguing her mind, she forces herself to calm down and think rationally.

"The Hero's wife and daughter, Phoebe and Daisy Hiro. I have to find them. I've already scoured most of the city, so they can't be far!"

A minute later, Mara scurries out of her hiding spot into the open. With the Ice Orc's imbuement having run out many minutes before, she takes care not to stride directly into the path of any troopers, lest their heat sensors detect her. Even so, relying purely on her invisibility, she manages to evade detection, sticking to the rooftops wherever possible.

"These humans are formidable, but it seems that Barons can deal with them if we use our heads."

Having interacted with humans innumerable times during her life, Mara has always considered them inferior life-forms incapable of threatening even a modicum of her existence. However, now, having witnessed dozens of Lord and Barons perish to the humans' weapons and enhanced armor, she realizes they do have the means to stand up for themselves.

"Could it be?" Mara muses. "Did humans really once possess the means to rest their boots atop the necks of demonkind? Were those rumors and legends not as exaggerated as I once thought?"

Mara recalls several tales relayed to her by the great Duke of Reptiles, Agares, as well as by many other long-lived demons. Their words, often containing lingering memories of fear, hinted at the mighty planet-busting weapons humans once built. Even so, they never went into great detail about the humans' capabilities, leaving her to wonder just how exaggerated those tales really were.

"Bombs capable of destroying nations. Armored machines that could tank the attacks of even mighty Dukes like Bael. Soldiers with metal parts built into their bodies. If these errant humans hadn't risen up under the Hero's banner, I'd never have believed such tall tales in all my days."

Mara travels toward the southern edge of Hero City. She slows to a stop once the Pyramids come into view, great monoliths constructed by the former leader of Tarus II, the Sphinx, Bahamut.

"Hmm. I've never seen Tarus II's Pyramids in person before... they're surprisingly impressive! As I recall, Bahamut had them built in memory of her former home, a place with unique- hmm?"

Mara narrows her eyes. She leans forward to gaze off the top of her perch, a four-story nondescript apartment complex. She spots a large platoon of three hundred exosuit-wearing commandos, all of whom hustle southward toward the pyramids.

In the midst of the troopers, two figures stand out. A woman, laying motionless on a stretcher, and a small child running alongside her. One of the troopers picks up the girl and hugs her protectively to their chest while looking around worriedly.

"Ah!" Mara gasps. "That... that child! I sense a faint but unmistakable mana resonance! It must be her; the Hero's Daughter! And if that's the case, then the unconscious woman is most likely her mother, Phoebe Hiro!"

Immediately, Mara calms her frantic heart. She focuses her senses, draws a silver card from her Spiritual Deck, and holds it before herself.

"Howling Night Wolf, **Imbue!**"

Mara calls the power of the Tarot Card onto herself, forming a silver dot upon her forehead. Shaggy hair grows from her skin, her eyes shift into bestial forms, and a pair of dog-like ears sprouts from the top of her skull.

With her body's strength, speed, and senses enhanced, Mara jumps forward, launching herself into the air. She focuses her enhanced hearing on the group of commandos, easily penetrating the sound-dampening effects of their armor.

"...assets are in danger. Request immediate backup. Unknown demonic entity has destroyed the last Covenant ship. We are returning Bishop and Rook to the safe zone at once, over."

"Copy that, Gold Leader. I'm dispatching additional reinforcements to your position. How long before you arrive at the safe zone? Over."

"Not long now, Elder Skarde. Five minutes at most. We're about to reach- shit!"

The man howls in alarm as his exosuit blares a warning. He swivels his head upward, where he spots a figure falling toward him from above.

"Look out! Protect the Commander!"

He and twenty other troopers swivel to face their attacker. However, with so little time to react, they don't even get a shot off before Mara crashes upon them, bringing her werewolf-like strength to bear.

**Boom!**

Mara instantly kills two troopers, then swings her fists wildly, using the berserker-like strength of her wolf-imbuement to its maximum extent!

"Don't hold back!" Another trooper shouts, as he squeezes his gun's trigger. "Blast the motherfucker!"

A hail of bullets descends upon Mara. She flickers from side to side, evading the gunfire and causing the troopers to shoot their comrades. Each movement, already impressive due to her Baron-level might, becomes even more incredible when boosted by the effects of her Tarot Cards.

"Wind Blade, **Summon!**"

Mara activates a Bronze Aspect, one of her weakest cards. An ethereal blade materializes in midair, then lunges toward the nearest trooper. He screams in fright as it plunges through his armor, phasing past his defenses with ease. A second later, it releases a burst of wind magic inside his body, dicing him to pieces. He collapses into his own footprint, leaving little to connect one piece of his tissue to another.

The razor-sharp weapon begins rapidly dicing apart the troopers, improving Mara's killing speed dramatically. Within thirty seconds of her arrival, she slaughters fifty of the three hundred troopers, leaving the rest pale with fear.

Even so, they quickly form up a defensive line between Mara and her two targets. Bullets whizz toward the Baron of Games, some striking her and sending blood spraying, but never do any manage to reach her vitals.

Mara grits her teeth. "You devil-damned insects! Just perish, already! Polar Deathworm, **Enchant!**"

Mara summons frost energy onto her fists. She lunges toward a pair of nearby troopers, intending to flash-freeze and shatter their figures.

However at that moment, a surge of magic pushes against Mara and flings her backward!

"What?!"

Mara flies away from the troopers, dumbstruck at the unexpected development. She crashes against a nearby building, fragmenting its wall into chunks of brick and mortar. She recovers immediately thanks to her wolf-enhanced body, then jumps out of the wall's imprint, confusion on her face.

"Who dares?"

The troopers fire on Mara's position, but she dodges to the side and leaps skyward, still warily looking for her attacker.

"Finally, just what I needed! Matured Drake, **Transform!**"

Mara's body rapidly shifts in midair. One of her few Golden Aspects activates, empowering her with the might of half a dragon. Her body, still somewhat wolf-like, begins to change further, transforming her into a twenty-foot-tall and fifty-foot-long ancient monster.

Even stronger than when she fought Kar, Mara cackles evilly. "This is your end, fleshbags!"

She crashes into the ground below, flattening twenty troopers with her sheer bulk. No longer does she fear their bullets as her scaled armor deflects them with ease. In twos and threes, Mara swings her head from side to side, snapping and severing her enemies' bodies effortlessly.

It doesn't take long before she spots the figure of Daisy Hiro standing protectively in front of her mother's stretcher, a look of fright on her face.

"There you are!" Mara shouts, as she kills another five troopers desperately trying to protect the child. "All this trouble to catch one brat!"

The thirty remaining troopers put their lives on the line to protect Phoebe and Daisy. Even so, they fail to injure Mara in the slightest. After transforming into a half-drake, her strength reaches the bottom level of Demon Dukes, making her more than a match for humanity's conventional weapons.

"Grab the assets and run!" One of the troopers shouts to another. "Get them out of here!"

An instant later, Mara brings a claw down, crushing him into his shadow. The man perishes, as does the one he just yelled out to.

"Trying to run? You'll fail!" Mara laughs.

While the Baron of Games rampages, her primary target, the child known as Daisy Hiro, trembles fearfully.

"Mommy, mommy, wake up! There's a bad person here!"

Daisy grabs her mother and shakes her, but Phoebe doesn't stir. Daisy tries to touch the 'bad spots,' but even then, Phoebe doesn't awaken.

"Mommy, I'm scared!"

Mara finishes off several more troopers, leaving only a handful behind. Realizing they can't offer even a smidgen of resistance, the men and women freeze up in terror. Their bodies tremble and shake as they mutter their final words.

"D-demons... they're just... too frightening... too powerful!"

Mara raises her claw again. She swings it toward the five remaining soldiers, finally breathing a sigh of satisfaction.

"My work here... is done!"

At that moment, her claw slams against a barrier of light. Mara's gigantic claw, easily twice the size of her human opponents, grinds to a half and freezes in midair.

"Huh? What?"

Mara tries to pull her claw back, but as if stuck in a quagmire, it refuses to budge.

"What the devil?!"

Daisy turns to look at the Baron of Games.

"You... you are a very bad person! You hurt all my friends! You hurt my mommy!"

The child rears her palm back, then swings it toward Mara's face, some thirty feet in the air. A projection of psionic energy in the shape of her hand materializes, increases in size, and swings at Mara's draconic face.

**Slap!**

The might of an anvil crashes against Mara's face, jarring her senses. She stumbles to the left, momentarily dazed by the hit.

"Argh! What the devil?! That child... she is a Hero after all!"

More angered than injured, Mara turns her head to look at Daisy. A sneer of rage crosses her animalistic face, giving her the appearance of a predator.

"You... you little shit! How dare you slap me! If that's all you've got, then I think it's your naptime!"

The five troopers watch helplessly as the shield of light that protected them fades away. Their momentary relief changes to bloodcurdling fright as they see Mara rear back, sucking oxygen into her throat.

"Blaaaaaugh!"

Mara releases a torrent of flames at the troopers, angling her flames in such a way that they'll just barely miss Daisy and her mother.

"This is the end!" A female soldier whimpers, closing her eyes and waiting for death to arrive.

However, Daisy takes a step forward and raises her hand.

"...No! Bad dragon! Bad!"

Another psionic palm materializes beneath Mara's head. It mimics Daisy's motion, slapping upward toward the underside of Mara's fire-spewing maw.

_Glomp!_

The impact slaps her lower jaw shut, abruptly cutting off Mara's dragon-breath. With nowhere for the flames to go, they backfire in Mara's throat, making her cough and wheeze.

"Huk! Kah! You- _cough_! You little... _hack!_"

Mara chokes several times, while tears stream from her eyes. Even angrier than before, she turns to howl at Daisy, only to see the child woozily stumble to the side, exhausted from expending too much mana. Daisy flops to the ground and falls unconscious, leaving herself completely defenseless.

A moment of silence follows.

Mara and the troopers both look at Daisy, then at each other, then at Mara.

One trooper wets himself in fright. "...Oh no."

Mara furrows her brow. "Tsk. Stupid child. It seems all of your luck has just run out. You might be a Hero now, but your strength is far too lacking to use such powerful magic without restraint."

After a moment of debate, Mara shrugs her gigantic shoulders.

"Too bad for you lot! You may die, now!"

Finally able to capture her targets, Mara wastes no more time. She swings her claws at the troopers and splatters them into meat-paste.

"About time. Now, to grab the little girl and her mother."

Mara deactivates her transformation. She returns the Matured Drake to her Spiritual Deck, where it won't be useable for many more hours, then walks over and bends down to grab the back of Daisy's shirt.

Suddenly, Mara pauses. A strange look crosses over her face as a sense of danger seizes her.

"What's this? This mana! Something powerful... underground!"

Alarmed, Mara jerks her eyes toward her feet. There she sees an unbelieveable sight as a gigantic blob of mana begins rushing toward the surface.

"Spawn of a broodmother! That's-?!"

Mara immediately retreats, saving herself by only a fraction of a second. A gigantic head filled with razor-sharp teeth erupts from Tarus II's depths, snapping onto her previous position with deadly intent.

"**SKREEEEEE!!**"

A Hydra, one of many legendary creatures from ancient myth, claws its way out of the planet's bowels. Mara retreats further and further as she gazes upon the monster with shock-filled eyes.

"This can't be! Another Hydra?! Since when did ancient monsters from the olden antiquity era become so commonplace? And what of the mother and child? Surely, they'll be crushed to death!"

Hardly have the words left Mara's mouth before she spots an even more incredible sight. Daisy's unconscious body, as well as Phoebe's, somehow cling to the Hydra's back, as if held there by a magical magnet.

Astounded, Mara quickly flips through many possibilities in her mind. "The Hydra... it's protecting the Hero's wife and child? Could this be a secret weapon he left behind to shield his family?"

The Hydra screeches again, only to begin rampaging wildly. It thrashes about, swinging its four heads in all directions. Houses and apartment buildings crumble into dust, while people from all walks of life flee in terror.

"Why would the Hero create a monster to protect his family, yet allow it to rampage uncontrollably? There's no way he'd do something so idiotic! That must mean... he didn't make it. Perhaps... his daughter?"

Mara continues putting distance between herself and the Hydra. She glances backward at Daisy, slumbering atop the Hydra's back, and frowns.

"How could a child cast magic while asleep? That shouldn't be possible. Even so, who else could achieve such a feat? It must be her or her father!"

Gritting her teeth, Mara shakes her head.

"Forget this! Ose can do what she wants to me! I'm not fighting a devil-damned Hydra! I'm leaving this shithole before it kills me!"

Mara turns to leave. Just as she starts to head westward, she freezes in her tracks, a look of sheer terror on her face.

There, before her, three giants slowly begin to materialize. Their figures, vaguely humanoid, yet otherworldly and rippling with power, scare the devil out of her. At thirty, fifty, and seventy feet tall, each giant possesses an awe-inspiring aura and level of mana far beyond anything Mara has ever sensed in anything beyond Diablo himself.

"Wha-wha-what?! **WHAT?!** Giant humans! How are they appearing out of thin air?! It's... it's as if someone has summoned them from another realm!!"

Several seconds later, the skin, bones, and muscles of the giants fully coagulate. They land deftly on their feet, the mere impact of which causes a series of micro-quakes throughout the region. Then, they slowly lower their gazes to Mara's tiny, pitiful, insignificant form.

All thoughts of retaliation flee from Mara's mind.

"...Oh... _to hell with this_."
