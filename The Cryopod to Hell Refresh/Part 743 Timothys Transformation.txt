Part 743: Timothy's Transformation

_Far-Future Era. Day 20, AJR. Chrona._

On the way over to the southern Psion Enclave, Timothy and the others paused their walking to turn and look to the side. There, they found the only demon presently inside Chrona walking over to them, each hand holding a bag with a cardboard box inside it.

"Master Timothy, I finished preparing today's meal." The demon said.

"Awesome! Thanks a bunch, Paimon!" Timothy said, grinning at the well-dressed demon as he approached.

Paimon had been summoned by Timothy's Pawn Summoning system, but unfortunately, as he was a Pawn, he could not level up or grow any stronger like the other four. Thus, he was unable to train and improve his strength over time.

He wasn't exactly weak, but Paimon was only a Demon Grunt, a Burrower with the ability to manipulate rocks and soil. This meant in the future, he would inevitably fall behind the core three Players, and that caused Paimon to worry he would become irrelevant.

As such, Paimon revealed one of his non-combat skills. It turned out he was a decent chef who had served other higher-ranking demons! His culinary skills weren't on the level of someone like Chef Yamir, but he could whip up decently tasty food sufficient to meet Timothy's daily quest requirements.

At the same time, he also took the initiative to make food for Marigold too, and she was extremely appreciative! He even took her preference for meat into account. Paimon couldn't make food for Ferral, since he didn't need to consume anything, but at least he felt he had a purpose now.

With Paimon's arrival, Timothy and Marigold decided to sit down and have a quick lunch with a simple picnic spread. Since Ferral couldn't eat food, he decided to move on to the Enclave to fetch his teacher and bring her back. Thus, Ferral left the human, crocodile, and demon together.

Paimon sat on Timothy's right side, while Marigold sat across from Timothy. Before long, the three of them started talking and laughing as they recounted their day.

"Master Timothy," Paimon said, "I was speaking to one of the crocodiles earlier and suggested that I could remodel the surroundings of the Swimming Hole. It's quite bland, don't you think? Some more amenities to pass the time, such as slides, could give it a more fun-filled air."

"That's a great idea!" Marigold exclaimed. "It's just a big hole filled with water right now. Could you maybe add lots of little cavities underwater and other stuff like that for hide and seek?"

"Of course." Paimon said, pressing his palm over his heart. "It would be my pleasure."

Timothy took a big bite of a sandwich filled with peppers and steak. Part of the sandwich dripped out the back and landed on the napkins in front of his crossed legs.

"Damn, that's a good sandwich." Timothy mumbled between bites. "Ah right, Paimon, the three of us are thinking of going into a Dungeon for the first time."

"A Dungeon?" Paimon asked. "Don't you think it's a little early for that? It sounds quite dangerous, considering your current strength."

"We won't know until we try." Timothy said. "But um, I was debating whether or not we should take you. I figured I'd ask if you wanted to go."

"Of course I do. Your safety is my primary concern." Paimon said. "As of right now, I'm still at least a little stronger than you. I'm not a pushover, you know."

"Yeah, true, but... I don't have any way to make you stronger..." Timothy said hesitantly. "It would be great if you could level up, but that doesn't seem possible."

"Mmm. Indeed, that is a problem." Paimon muttered. He rubbed his palm beneath his chin. "Master, I have considered another possibility. Can I don equipment from your Inventory?"

Timothy blinked. "I... I don't know. That's a good question. Here, take this."

The young man opened his Inventory. Inside, he saw various good he'd acquired over the last few weeks. He still had the Octopus Armor which he wasn't wearing at the moment, and the common Longsword. He pulled the sword out and handed it over to Paimon.

Paimon took the sword. It didn't dissipate in his hands, so he stood up, backed away a little, and swung it around.

"It seems my conjecture was correct. Master, if you can acquire gear for me, I can become at least a little stronger and join you in Dungeons."

"I don't have much right now though..." Timothy said hesitantly. "All the gear I have is meant for me."

"I should still come along anyway." Paimon said, as he sat down and handed the sword back to Timothy. "Low level earth manipulation might not sound impressive, but I can bury an enemy's foot underground to trip them up, or fling rocks at them with extreme force. I can bring a ceiling down if needed. I won't be much use fighting at the front, especially when Miss Marigold is the sturdiest member of our group, but I can be of great assistance in the rear."

Timothy nodded. "We'll bring you along, then. I can probably get my mom to make us some armor first. Something basic to meet our needs. It's not like the King Network rejects real items."

As Timothy said that, his eyes momentarily glazed over. He blinked twice. "Wait... isn't that the solution? My dad made all sorts of artifacts, and my mom is pretty busy now but we still have machines that can forge stuff. Maybe we can outfit ourselves with proper equipment before we go into a Dungeon to ensure our safety!"

Marigold cocked her head. "Huh! Now that you mention it, that's a great idea. Kinda obvious in hindsight. Good thing you thought of it before we actually went in!"

The trio burst into laughter, realizing they hadn't thought of something so obvious already. In truth, while Timothy did think of the full idea, he wouldn't have done so without Paimon's previous question. This just went to show how they were a lot better off as a group than they were individually.

Some times later, once the food was almost fully consumed, Timothy looked at his Daily Quest. He'd already done all the workout parts of the quest, and with Paimon's big meal, the food part of the quest was also finished too.

...

**[Side Quest] [Repeatable] Train Your Body!**

_Small gains compound over time. Perform a series of exercises, with increasing rewards depending on how many repetitions you can complete. To complete this Quest properly, you must complete each type of exercise in one session each. You may not space them out across the day, or across multiple days._

_[Complete Pushups 100/100] Rewards: 1x Tier 1 Lootbox._

_[Complete Pullups: 100/100] Rewards: 1x EXP per 3 Pullups. 100th Pullup grants 5 EXP._

_[Complete Situps: 100/100] Rewards: +2.5% END, +2.5% CON._

_[Complete Squats: 100/100] Rewards: 1x Mundane Skill Lootbox._

_[Complete Long Jog: 10/10 Km] Rewards: +5% maximum movement speed._

_[Swim Session: 10/10 Km] Rewards: +2.5% swimming speed._

_[Eat Cooked Meat: 1.5/1.5 Kgs] Rewards: +2.5% Gut Digestion._

_[Eat Vegetables: 1.5/1.5 Kgs] Rewards: +2.5% Eyesight Improvement._

_Note: If the Player completes all Quest objectives within 24 hours, all rewards will be doubled. This Quest may be completed once per 24 hour period._

...

Each time Timothy did his Daily Quest, he would save up the lootboxes for later, while accepting all the other rewards. As such, his ability to digest food had grown around two times stronger than before he obtained the King Network. He could power through the daily quest's food requirements with great ease now, and while he couldn't really tell how much his eyesight had improved, he was able to see the individual leaves on trees a full block away.

He was also a much faster swimmer now, though Marigold's swimming prowess had started at a higher point and she was still far faster than him.

Timothy looked inside his inventory. After acquiring today's daily rewards, there was a stack of 30x Mundane Skill Lootboxes he hadn't opened yet, and a stack of 30x Tier 1 Lootboxes he also hadn't opened.

There was a good reason he hadn't opened them. It was because Umi had told him if he took three identical Lootboxes, he could merge them together into a Lootbox of one tier higher.

He wasn't sure if he wanted 30 common skills or 10 uncommon skills, or 3 rare skills and 1 uncommon skill, or maybe even a single Legendary skill. He had a hard time making that decision.

Marigold was the same as him. Players couldn't exchange their Lootboxes, or else Timothy would immediately hand them all over to his mom to open. Her passive granted +1 item when opening any lootbox, so that was a huge benefit Timothy couldn't reap the benefits for.

"I think it's time to open my lootboxes." Timothy said quietly. "After thinking about it, I've decided to merge all my skill lootboxes, but just use the Tier 1 lootboxes as-is."

Marigold shrugged. "If you think that's a good idea, then go ahead! Kyargh! I kinda just wanna open all mine as-is anyway."

Timothy nodded. Since they were still waiting for Ferral to return, Timothy decided to start cracking the lootboxes open.

When the first Rare Skill Lootbox opened, Timothy was not surprised by what he saw. He had, after all, locked two skills in place from his previous level up options. But in addition to those, there was another...

...

**Divine Blaze [Active Skill] [Rare] [Enchantment] [Mana Cost 1/min (Sustained)] [Cooldown 0]**

_Enables the Player to temporarily enchant a melee weapon with a light and flame effect that deals a base 50% additional damage to most enemies, and up to 200% extra damage against enemies weak to Fire or Divine attacks. Enemies resistant to both effects will take reduced damage. Remains active on the enchanted weapon until deactivated or until mana is depleted._

**Flow State [Passive Skill] [Rare] [Stats] [Toggle]**

_A Toggle ability that allows the Player to choose from one of three passive bonuses at any given time. These stats can only be swapped if Player is not currently in battle._

_Option 1 [Warrior's Physique]: +1 to STR and DEF._

_Option 2 [Wizard's Mind]: +1 to INT and WIS._

_Option 3 [Wanderer's Fortitude]: +1 to DEX and END._

**Transformaster [Active Skill] [Rare] [Toggle] [HP Reduced by 25%]**

_When this skill is toggled, any skills that transform a part or the entirety of the user's body have 50% reduced activation requirements. This skill will remain active until toggled off. The user will lose 25% of their max HP, which will remain reserved until the skill is toggled off. If Transformaster is toggled off, any skills that do not have their requirements met as a result will also toggle off._

...

Timothy's eyes nearly popped out of his head. His jaw fell open as he saw not only the previous two Rare skills he had intended to choose, but a new one as well!

"Holy crap!" Timothy exclaimed. "I hit the jackpot! This is exactly the skill I need!!"

He still wanted to choose Flow State, and Divine Blaze was definitely a good skill as well, but this new passive immediately solved a huge problem for him! With it, he could activate Crocodile Form! It also meant if he had a chance to obtain other transformations in the future, he could wield them immediately as well!

"What is it? What'd you get?" Marigold asked.

Timothy grinned. He didn't hesitate and chose the Transformaster skill. A moment later, Timothy activated it, causing his body to feel a little weaker. His maximum HP fell from 111 to 83, but so did the costs for activating Crocodile Form!

...

**[Active Skill] [Rare] [Transformation] [Holistic] [35(70) Mana (Sustained) OR 3(7) AP (Activated)] [No Cooldown]**

_The Player gains the ability to transform into a half-crocodile, gaining all the upsides and downsides that come along with such a transformation. Their skin will become hardened and scaled, granting bolstered defense. Their muscles will increase in density. They become incredibly adept in water. However, their mind will slow down somewhat. The player can revert back to their original form for free at any time._

_Grants +80% STR, DEX, CON, and HP, with doubled improvements when in water. Grants -35% INT and WIS._

...

With his MP now at 45, he was within the range to activate the skill. As such, instead of answering Marigold, Timothy chose to stand up and enable Crocodile Form.

Timothy's body shone with a brilliant light, causing Marigold and Paimon to wince. Within two seconds, Timothy's figure started changing. His head shifted and deformed. His mouth elongated dramatically. His arms and legs thickened, and scales grew over his body.

He went from a pale-skinned human to a grey-scaled Crocodile, rising another foot in height!

But, just as soon as the transformation finished, Timothy lost his balance.

"Guhh, guhh!!"

Timothy tried to cry out in warning, but his face felt so heavy that he could barely open his mouth. At the same, time, the way his snout 'felt' was wrong, and made him start choking. All that weight at the front of his body made him lose his balance and he fell forward onto his stomach, causing his snout and the front-most part of his jaw to painfully _clap_ against the ground.

"Aaaugh! Aaah, huugu, guuh!"

Timothy growled out some sounds that did not come out as words. He had narrowly avoided falling on top of his girlfriend, but suffice it to say, his first transformation into a Crocodile was... anything but elegant.

Marigold got up, then walked over to try and help Timothy up. "Timmy, are you okay? Did you bite your tongue? Here, help me get him up, Paimon!"

The two of them worked together, demon and Crocodile, and helped Timothy stand up.

Timothy tried to speak, but he could not properly make his huge, unwieldy mouth work properly. "Guhh, shlaaaw waaah..."

Timothy felt so embarrassed that he wanted to crawl in a hole and die. He'd finally been able to transform into the same species as his girlfriend, but he felt like a complete loser in front of her too. He wished he'd transformed in private first so he hadn't embarrassed himself.

Luckily, Marigold seemed to understand what the problem was. "I don't know how it is for you humans, but we crocs don't talk with lips and tongue. We talk with the back of our throats! You'll probably need to practice a bit to talk right, I guess..."

Marigold had never really thought about what a huge difference it would be to transform into a completely different species. Now that she pondered the matter, would she look like a dummy if she obtained a human transformation skill? Would she drool all over herself, trying to get her lips working properly?

Timothy opened and closed his mouth with great effort. To him, it felt as if he had to bend his jawbone in half just to open his mouth a few inches. It wasn't _exactly_ painful, but it was extremely uncomfortable, like nails on a chalkboard. It was almost as if he could feel the bones in his jaw scraping against each other. On top of that, his mouth felt extremely moist, as if he had much more saliva sloshing around than usual.

And his tongue! Timothy's long tongue felt completely alien in his mouth. It lacked the sensitivity and the sense of taste he was used to. His eyesight felt duller, and even his mind seemed to be working much more slowly than usual. That was likely due to the reduction to his INT and WIS stats.

Timothy blinked his eyes heavily. It was only now, after he obtained the ability he'd been hoping to get for such a long time, that he realized transformations were not what they were cracked up to be.

Even though Marigold didn't seem thrilled about his clumsiness, it was clear she really liked his new transformation!

But Timothy didn't. He'd only been in his new body for one minute and he already _hated_ it.

How could he tell Marigold he didn't like being a crocodile? Simply looking at her face, she was clearly excited to see him in this new form. Timothy also felt that after working so hard and putting so many levels into becoming a Crocodile, it wouldn't make any sense for him to immediately shelve it. He's already leveled it up three times!

If only he knew how much he'd hate being a crocodile, he would have avoided it from the very start. To him, this was body horror, plain and simple, like waking up in the body of a monster. It was utterly dreadful.

"Well? How is it?" Marigold asked. "How do you like being a crocodile?"

Timothy's eyes felt heavy.

"Muuhh, mulnn phlahhh..."

His tongue fell out of his mouth, and he had the damnedest time trying to get it back in. No matter how Timothy tried, the stupid tongue would not listen to his commands. He eventually had to grab it with his claws and push it back inside.

After seeing Timothy struggle for five long minutes, the excitement dimmed inside Marigold's eyes. She wasn't blind, and she wasn't stupid. She could tell he was really struggling.

"Hey... you can turn back into a human if you want..." Marigold said hesitantly. "It's okay if becoming a crocodile isn't working out for you. I already told you I like you just the way you are."

Timothy heaved a sigh. He had tried to sound enthusiastic, but even Marigold could tell he was absolutely awful at being a crocodile.

After another minute of deliberation, Timothy finally deactivated the skill. He transformed back into his original self and sagged to his knees. He felt exhausted. The pressure on the base of his skull from how huge his snout was had left him with phantom pain and a neck kink he couldn't get out.

"Uhh... I'm sorry... Marigold." Timothy said tiredly. "That felt... really weird. I'll try practicing some more in my free time."

"You don't have to force yourself." Marigold said, her tone quiet. "It's fine."

She sounded a little defeated, which made Timothy feel awful. He felt as if he had tried to engage with someone regarding their favorite hobby, only to fumble the interaction so badly that both participants lost all their enthusiasm. It was a feeling one could only understand if they had ever screwed up a social interaction in such a broadly pathetic manner.

Marigold sat back down to finish the last bit of her food. She smiled at Paimon. "Thanks Paimon. The meal was really delicious."

Paimon smiled back. "Anything to make you happy, Miss Marigold."

Timothy dazedly stared at his inventory. The newest Rare skill he'd acquired, along with the Crocodile Form L4 skill, seemed to be looking back at him mockingly. He'd spent quite a lot of effort to acquire and upgrade these skills, but now he felt he'd wasted his time. He had plenty of other skills he could have leveled up in that same time, but he'd passed on them to focus on the skill he thought would be most badass and make his girlfriend happy. 

Now he felt like an idiot.

The worst part wasn't even the disappointment or the lost time and energy. It was that he really did not enjoy the _feeling_ of being a Crocodile. Every part of the experience felt like an alien had infected his body and taken over his consciousness. He simply didn't want to become a Crocodile ever again, not even to practice and try to make it work.

If this was how bad the Crocodile Form made him feel, then what about the Octopus Form?

Timothy looked up the Octopus Armor.

...

**Octopus Armor [Item] [Uncommon] [Torso] [Balanced]**

_A versatile set of armor that grants the Player the ability to camouflage themselves when underwater, blending in with their surroundings. When camouflaged, the Player effectively doubles their remaining oxygen, but cannot move. The Player also passively heals 1% of their Max HP every minute underwater when wearing this armor._

**Comes with Active Skill: Octopus Form [Active Skill] [Uncommon] [Transformation] [Offense] [5(10) Mana (Sustained) OR 1(1) AP (Activated)] [No Cooldown]**

_Octopus Form sprouts six tentacles out of the Player's back. It allows the user to wield multiple additional weapons, up to six 1H weapons or three 2H weapons, in addition to the Player's base two limbs. The Octopus Tentacles cannot be controlled by the player and are fully automated. The player can revert back to their original form for free at any time._

...

Timothy thought for a moment. He realized the Octopus Form was nowhere near as body-changing as the Crocodile Form. With that in mind, and since he still had 10 MP left, Timothy gritted his teeth and decided to test the form out.

A second later, Timothy's body shone with another brilliant light. All of a sudden, six long octopus tentacles emerged from his back.

Even though Timothy had already been able to activate the Octopus Form for the last couple of weeks, ever since his near-death scare at the swimming hole, he'd avoided touching the armor. Now that he was trying out the transformation, he realized it actually... felt pretty good!

It wasn't bad at all!

The octopus limbs certainly felt a little weird, and the idea that six new 'arms' were sticking out of his back made him a little uncomfortable, but compared to the Crocodile Form, the Octopus Form was way more manageable!

Timothy stood up. He nearly fell backward from the extra weight, but two of the tentacles helpfully pressed against the ground to stabilize his movements. Timothy started walking around, threw a few punches, and experimented with lashing out with one of the tentacles at a time, then two, then three. He wasn't able to control them the same way he would his actual arms, but if he wanted to do something, the tentacles would follow his orders as if he were a movie director commanding his subordinates to execute tasks.

By the time Ferral was on his way back, Timothy had already started adapting to the Octopus Form. He felt that, in hindsight, this transformation was insanely strong. It basically had no drawbacks outside of looking a little weird, and he felt like his threat level had increased substantially!

"Wow, you're pretty good with those!" Marigold said encouragingly.

Even though she was saddened by Timothy's obvious rejection of the Crocodile Form, the fact he had adapted so easily to the Octopus Form hinted that maybe he'd be able to try again someday and actually do really well as a crocodile! It gave her hope, and she latched onto that with fervent desire.

While Timothy experimented, Ferral levitated toward them, his teacher Raavul following closely behind. Seeing Timothy in his transformation, Ferral glanced at him.

[Are you having fun?] Ferral questioned.

"More than I was just a bit ago." Timothy said. "Ah, Raavul. You're here. Did Ferral explain why he wanted you to come over here?"

Raavul bowed her head and blinked all of her eyes in unison.

[Yes. It seems that you wish to make me a Player in your King Network. This was not what I had in mind when I asked you before... but Ferral managed to convince me this was the right way to do things.]

[A teacher's dignity must not be infringed.] Ferral said, repeating the same words he'd said to Timothy previously. [If things had shaken out properly, you should have been the first Volgrim Timothy invited. As things are now, being the second is not such a bad result. Hopefully, we will be able to add the others as Players next, once Timothy earns more slots.]

Timothy nodded. "That's the plan. So, Raavul, are you ready to join us?"

The teacher of the Psions slowly nodded her head.

[In truth, I have little interest in this system of yours. However, I cannot deny the power and benefits it has brought Ferral. If we are to prevent the collapse of Chrona someday, spatial Psionic power may end up becoming a core component of stabilizing our home.]

She didn't seem excited to become a Player, but Timothy sensed she would at least take the system seriously. As such, he opened his player sheet and looked at the last available Slot before tapping it with his finger.

"Welcome to the King Network, Raavul."

[AN ADMINISTRATOR IS INVITING YOU TO THE KING NETWORK.]

[IF YOU ACCEPT THIS REQUEST, YOU WILL BECOME A **PLAYER**.]

[WILL YOU ACCEPT THE FRIEND REQUEST AND JOIN TIMOTHY HIRO'S PARTY?]

[YES | NO]

After a moment's hesitation, Raavul pressed her finger against the Yes option.

With that, she became the fifth member of the King Network.
