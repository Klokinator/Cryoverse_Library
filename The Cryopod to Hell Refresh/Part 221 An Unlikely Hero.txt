Part 221: An Unlikely Hero

_The world of Maiura, at the Wordsmith's residence._

A woman with the appearance of someone in her late twenties sits on a hand-carved bench in a large courtyard. The dwindling sunlight hints at the arrival of night-time, as the shadows cast from nearby trees stretch and lengthen. Outside the walls of the Wordsmith's manor, terrible and fearsome beasts roar and howl at the moonless sky.

However, the woman does not flinch. She has seen scarier creatures in her lifetime. The small fry aching for her flesh barely register in her eyes as a threat.

Amelia, the Black Queen, once the most feared of all who roamed the Labyrinth, stares blankly at the gate leading out of the Wordsmith's Manor. She leans forward on the bench and rests both of her arms on her bare legs. Wearing only a black T-shirt and skirt, her appearance is far plainer and less elegant than years before, when she brought forth terror to all who opposed her.

Now, she resembles a normal human, not a person who has consumed millions of terrified souls.

Amelia scrunches her toes in the grass anxiously. After a while, she sighs.

"Day sixteen, and Jason still isn't back. What happened to him? Why hasn't he contacted me?"

The young woman glances at a pretty little red ruby ring on her finger. She rubs it three times, but it doesn't glow, even while she wishes it would.

"Jason can't be dead. He can't! He promised me he just needed to visit the Labyrinth for a few days. Oh no... what if the demons ambushed him? What if they captured him, or worse?! No... no! I can't imagine such awful things. Jason is smart. He wouldn't allow himself to get caught, especially after they almost got him last time."

A shiver runs down Amelia's back.

"...After they nearly caught him last time? Oh, devils! What if I'm right? What if Jason didn't tell anyone but me that he was heading into the Labyrinth? I can't just sit here! He needs me!"

Amelia jumps to her feet. She takes a hesitant step toward the walls of the manor, but pauses.

"Jason told me not to leave. If anyone finds out I'm alive, it will cause him problems. If he is alright and I expose my existence, I'll have hurt him for no reason. Ohhh! What's a woman supposed to do at a time like this?!"

Amelia begins pacing back and forth. She runs her fingers through her long, black hair over and over, trying to calm herself down.

"Should I leave? Should I stay? Oh, gosh! I don't know! If Jason is in dire straits, it's worth the risk! If he isn't..."

Suddenly, Amelia stops. She gazes at the waning sun and narrows her eyes. "...If he isn't in trouble, then so be it! I'd rather get him in hot water for reviving me than have him tortured at the hands of those jerkoff demons!"

Amelia turns away from the gate and heads inside. With her mind made up, she steadies her shaking hands and begins to think.

"Plan. I need a plan. I can't just barrel into the Labyrinth. With the abilities Jason Wordsmithed for me, I can't take on any demon stronger than a Baron, and even then, most Barons will push my body to its limits."

Amelia walks inside the manor. She heads up the lobby's main spiral staircase to the second floor, where she walks past more than a dozen empty rooms. Eventually, she arrives at the master bedroom.

"That's right! Jason gave me that special armor-thingy. He told me I could use it in an emergency. Well, if now doesn't count, then I don't know what does!"

Amelia trots over to a small chest on her dresser. She raps her knuckles on its sides and top eleven times, striking several spots with practiced precision. A hidden compartment pops open, revealing a circular device, one with a dinosaur's head etched into it.

"My hidden T-REX," Amelia mutters. "Jason showed me how to use it once. I hope I haven't forgotten how it works. It's been almost four years..."

The young woman plucks the T-REX out of its hidden compartment, loops its straps around her arms, and fastens them together behind her back. The device's main activator rests against her chest, directly above her bosom, where it fits snug like a glove.

"Next step..." Amelia mutters. "...I have to press the activator button three times."

She takes a few deep breaths to prepare herself, then mashes the T-REX's big red activator button on its front, thrice.

_Press. Press. Press._

Suddenly, an explosion of nanites erupts from the T-REX. The nanobots swarm up and down her chest, cover her legs, and jump to her arms. A horrible sense of dread and claustrophobia makes Amelia scream in panic as the microscopic robots weave their way around her body and inevitably reach her face.

"No! Nooo!!"

Suddenly, the world goes dark. The nanites finish devouring their host and settle in together, transforming into a seamless alloy ten times tougher than steel. Just as Amelia's claustrophobia reaches its peak, the nanites engulfing her face become transparent, allowing her to see the world outside.

"**CENTURION BATTLESUIT, PROTOTYPE SPECTRE MARK II: ONLINE! HELLO, PILOT CODENAMED 'SEXYGIRL69.' WHAT ARE YOUR ORDERS?**"

Centurion's deep, robotic timbre rumbles in Amelia's ear, only adding to her panic. However, before she can take the suit off, Centurion releases a small burst of gas directly into her nostrils.

"**ELEVATED ADRENALINE LEVELS DETECTED! PILOT SEXYGIRL69 APPEARS TO BE SUFFERING A PANIC ATTACK. RELEASING NEURAL INHIBITORS TO DECREASE THE PILOT'S AGITATION. PILOT SEXYGIRL69, ARE YOU FIT FOR DUTY? THIS EXOSUIT REQUIRES AN ANSWER WITHIN SEVEN POINT THREE SECONDS.**"

Amelia quickly sets on the bed. Centurion's neural inhibitors cause a drastic decrease in her claustrophobia, allowing her to catch her breath. "Y-yes. Sorry, Centurion. I got scared for a moment. I'm... I'm not used to wearing these armored suit-things."

"**UNDERSTOOD. THIS PROTOTYPE BATTLESUIT IS SPECIALLY ADAPTED FOR INFILTRATION AND ESPIONAGE. PILOT SEXYGIRL69, PLEASE SET ASIDE ANY WORRIES ABOUT THIS EXOSUIT'S DEFENSIVE CAPABILITIES. IN THE CASE OF AN EMERGENCY, THIS BATTLESUIT IS EQUIPPED WITH AN ADVANCED AUTOPILOT FEATURE. THIS SUIT WILL ENSURE YOUR SURVIVAL, NO MATTER THE COST.**"

Amelia nods. However, her expression turns dim. "Why... why do you keep calling me... _that_? My name is Amelia. Please stop calling me 'sexy girl.' It's weird."

"**UNDERSTOOD. PILOT CONDENAMED 'SEXYGIRL69' WISHES TO CHANGE HER IDENTIFER CODE. IS THIS COMMAND CORRECT?**"

"Yeah," Amelia says. "Just call me Amelia, okay? Don't call me 'sexy girl.' Who the heck told you to call me that, anyway?"

"**IDENTIFIER CODENAME ALTERED! THIS BATTLESUIT WILL NOW REFER TO YOU AS PILOT 'AMELIA-OKAY.' REGARDING PILOT AMELIA-OKAY'S QUERY: THIS BATTLESUIT CHANGED YOUR IDENTIFIER AS PER THE ORDERS OF PILOT ZERO-ZERO-FOUR.**"

Amelia rolls her eyes. "Ugh. That's **so** like Jason. Anyway. Centurion, Jason told me your suit has a bunch of special powers, right? Can you give me a rundown? I need to get past the guard post on Maiura and enter the Labyrinth without anyone seeing me."

"**AFFIRMATIVE. ORDERS ACKNOWLEDGED, PILOT AMELIA-OKAY. THIS BATTLESUIT POSSESSES NUMEROUS TECHNICAL UPGRADES OVER THE PREVIOUS SPECTRE-CLASS MK. I EXOSUITS. IN ADDITION TO FIVE MINUTES OF LEVEL THREE STEALTH, THIS UNIT CAN MAINTAIN NEARLY INDEFINITE LENGTHS OF LEVEL TWO STEALTH SO LONG AS THE PILOT DOES NOT CHANGE THEIR POSITION. ADDITIONALLY...**"

Centurion begins rattling off a long list of upgrades, which takes the better part of ten minutes. Amelia listens intently, only interrupting Centurion to ask for details regarding certain upgrades.

Finally, Centurion finishes its lecture, allowing Amelia to fall into deep contemplation.

"Paralysis gas dispensers. Poisoned needle launchers. This suit can even adhere to the ceiling and walls. I guess if I need to assassinate someone, the Spectre will do the trick. Still, it feels weird that Jason would create a device with such lethal weaponry. If I'm going to find him, I'll need all the advantages I can get, but this just doesn't sit right with me."

Amelia lowers her head.

"I promised Jason I wouldn't kill anymore. Centurion, please deactivate any weaponry or devices that can inflict lethal damage. If I try to use anything that can kill someone, warn me in advance."

"**AFFIRMATIVE. ORDER ACCEPTED, PILOT AMELIA-OKAY. WARNING! DEACTIVATING LEVEL TWO AND THREE ARMAMENTS WILL DECREASE THIS SUIT'S COMBAT CAPABILITIES BY 67.3%. DO YOU STILL INTEND TO CONTINUE WITH THIS OPERATION?**"

Amelia nods. "I do."

"**AFFIRMATIVE. ORDERS ACCEPTED. USAGE OF CLASSES TWO AND THREE ARMAMENTS WILL REQUIRE A SECONDARY CONFIRMATION CODE. WHAT PASSPHRASE WOULD PILOT AMELIA-OKAY LIKE TO SET AS THE CONFIRMATION CODE?**"

"Ugh. How about... I don't know... 'Betrayal?' If I kill anyone, I'll be betraying my promise to Jason. Seems fitting."

"**AFFIRMATIVE. ORDERS ACCEPTED. THE NEW PASSPHRASE TO UNLOCK LEVEL TWO AND THREE ARMAMENTS WILL BE 'BETRAYAL.' WHAT ARE YOUR NEXT ORDERS, PILOT AMELIA-OKAY?**"

Amelia gently slides off her bed and takes a step toward the door. She pauses in front of a full-body mirror, taking note of her golden-armored appearance. Her faceless helmet gives her an intimidating aura, capable of cowing the meek and frightening the strong.

"My next order? Let's start with my name. Your programming sucks, Centurion. Call me 'Amelia.' Just 'Amelia.' Alright?"

"**AFFIRMATIVE. ORDERS ACCEPTED. THIS EXOSUIT WILL ADDRESS YOU AS PILOT AMELIA-JUST-AMELIA-ALRIGHT. IS THIS IDENTIFICATION CODE SATISFACTORY?**'

Amelia groans, long and hard. "Ughhh... _fine_. Let's go."

---

Amelia exits the manor and begins running down the main road leading toward a distant series of cliffs. The path cuts through a dense forest, with fifty-foot trees on both sides of the road. However, thanks to its relatively straight-line, the young woman is still capable of gazing into the distance, where her destination awaits.

With her Wordsmithed body, which puts her near the strength of a typical Demon Baron, coupled with her Spectre exosuit, Amelia achieves a casual running speed of sixty miles an hour. The young woman dashes past countless animals, startling them as they spot the freakish, golden-colored biped stomping through their woodland. More than a few predators shrink into the shadows, avoiding the deadly foe rushing past them. Even the 'stupid' animals know better than to punch above their weight class.

"I swear, Jason must have made this suit's AI as dumb as a rock just to annoy me," Amelia grumbles. Her feet leave deep impressions in the dirt road as she runs due to her armor's weight.

Even as she travels, Amelia's thoughts begin to wander.

_I've started to feel incredibly lonely over the past few years. Jason isn't home all that often. We barely get to talk. Without Blaarjiim in my head, the world has become eerily silent. At least I can talk to this Centurion thing. It's not much for conversation, but it's better than nothing._

A strange feeling of emptiness builds in Amelia's chest as she runs. Her thoughts darken further, as ancient, repressed memories worm their way up from her subconscious.

_Levvy. I miss you. Life hasn't been the same ever since you left me alone. Why did you have to die?_

Tears well up in Amelia's eyes. However, she doesn't slow her pace to wipe them. Instead, she allows them to trickle down her face unhindered.

_You told me you would always be there for me. You told me you were too powerful for anyone to separate us. Why did you lie to me? How could you leave me all by myself?! Didn't you know...? Didn't you understand you were the only person I ever truly loved?_

Finally, Amelia slows her running, as the sheer cliff leading to the Maiura warpgate appears before her. A massive military base with thirty-foot steel walls looms ahead, serving as an impenetrable obstruction to Maiura's native fauna. Instinctively, even the most fearsome beasts know better than to approach humanity's stronghold.

Amelia finally takes a moment to deactivate her T-REX and wipe her eyes. She sniffles quietly, then hardens her heart.

_It doesn't matter anymore. Levvy died. He made me promise never to give into my sorrow. I have to live, even if only to preserve his legacy. I may be a monster, and an irredeemable witch... but I'll never break my promises._

Amelia reactivates her T-REX, then stands up a little straighter. "Centurion. Scan the base. Point out all the life readings you can find."

"**AFFIRMATIVE. ORDERS ACCEPTED, PILOT AMELIA-JUST-AMELIA-ALRIGHT. SCANNING BASE NOW. IMPORTANT! CENTURION WOULD LIKE TO SUGGEST TAPPING INTO THE LOCAL TRANSMISSION NETWORK TO REVEAL THE POSITIONS OF ALL SOLDIERS AT THE MAIURAN FORWARD COMMAND POST. WOULD YOU LIKE TO INITIATE THIS PLAN?**"

Amelia frowns. "If we do that, you'll have to reveal your position to the others here. That's extremely dangerous. I'm trying to take a stealthy approach."

"**NEGATIVE. THIS EXOSUIT WAS SPECIALLY MODIFIED BY PILOT ZERO-ZERO-FOUR TO NOT APPEAR ON THE CENTURION MAINFRAME. AS SUCH, THIS EXOSUIT CAN INTERFACE DIRECTLY WITH THE MAIN COMMAND HUB WITHOUT RAISING ANY ALARMS.**"

Amelia blinks in surprise. "What? Jason... he thought that far ahead? He must have known I might need to escape the manor in the future. Wow, he's smarter than I give him credit for. Alright, Centurion. Tap into the listening post."

"**AFFIRMATIVE. ORDERS ACCEPTED.**"

Centurion falls silent. After a few moments, Amelia glances down at her suits' shiny, golden exterior. "By the way. Don't you think this color is a little bit attention-grabbing? If we're going for a stealthy approach, I'd rather not look like a miniature sun in the middle of the night."

"**AFFIRMATIVE. RECOMMENDATION NOTED, PILOT AMELIA-JUST-AMELIA-ALRIGHT. WOULD YOU LIKE FOR THIS EXOSUIT TO ACTIVATE 'CHAMELEON COLOR COORDINATION MODE?'**"

Amelia shrugs. "Sure. If it means people won't be able to see me from space, then that would be a big improvement."

"**COMMAND ACCEPTED! ACTIVATING LEVEL 2 STEALTH... NOW.**"

Suddenly, Amelia's armor loses its shimmering appearance. Instead of becoming pure black, or grey, or any other darkened color, it becomes a mirror of the environment around it, allowing the former Black Witch to become almost invisible amongst her surroundings.

"Oh, wow. Not bad," Amelia murmurs. "I can definitely sneak closer like this. How about that wire-tapping, Centurion? Do you have anything for me?"

The moment Amelia asks, a series of images appears in her helmet's readouts. The layout of Maiura's Forward Command post, along with a couple dozen red dots, reveal the positions of multiple sentries standing guard to watch for intruders.

However, Amelia frowns. "That can't be right. Centurion, I want to know about the positions of everyone in the command post, not just the sentries. Once I scale those walls, I'll have to dodge people on the inside, too."

"**AFFIRMATIVE, PILOT AMELIA-JUST-AMELIA-ALRIGHT. THIS EXOSUIT HAS NOTED THE POSITIONS OF ALL PERSONNEL CURRENTLY INSIDE THE FORWARD COMMAND POST'S GROUNDS. THERE ARE ONLY TWENTY-EIGHT LIFEFORMS WITHIN THE DESIGNATED BOUNDARIES. WOULD YOU LIKE CENTURION TO EXPAND ITS SCANS OUTSIDE THE COMMAND POST'S WALLS?**"

Amelia's frown deepens. "Only twenty-eight people, all of them mere sentries? That can't be right. Jason told me there are never fewer than four-hundred soldiers inside this base. Where did everyone go?"

"**PILOT AMELIA-JUST-AMELIA-ALRIGHT, ACCORDING TO MULTIPLE TRANSMISSIONS THIS EXOSUIT HAS INTERCEPTED, THERE APPEARS TO BE A MILITARY CONFERENCE OF UNKNOWN ORIGINS BEING HELD INSIDE THE LABYRINTH CORE. THE SOLDIERS AT THIS STATION HAVE LEFT ON OFFICIAL ORDERS FROM MILITARY COMMANDER NEIL ADAMS. THIS EXOSUIT HAS A 91% CONFIDENCE THRESHOLD FOR THIS ASSESSMENT.**"

Amelia's heart skips a beat. "Devils. Neil once told me that only in an emergency would he summon all the troops. It must have something to do with Jason's disappearance!"

Several seconds pass.

The young woman stares silently through her visor's display. She ponders her next move with the utmost caution.

Eventually, Amelia nods her head.

"I'd love to ask Neil if something has happened to Jason. However, I can't appear before anyone else or it will raise all sorts of alarms. Instead, I'll use this chance to slip inside the Core while there aren't any guards around. Centurion, will your invisibility feature work once we pass through the warpgate to the Labyrinth Core?"

Centurion offers an immediate reply. "**AFFIRMATIVE. THE SPECTRE MK. II EXOSUIT POSSESSES HIGHLY ADAPTABLE STEALTH TECHNOLOGY FAR SURPASSING THE MK. I! THIS EXOSUIT WILL NOT SUFFER ANY DISRUPTIONS AS A RESULT OF PASSING THROUGH A QUANTUM TUNNEL.**"

"Alright. I'll be counting on you, then. Let's get the hell out of here and find Jason!"

...

Amelia darts forward, using the suit's innate chameleon powers to merge into the encrouching darkness like a jaguar stalking prey. She weaves through a field of tall grass, slithering through the gaps with the grace of a jungle snake. Eventually, she arrives at the base of the encampment's thirty-foot walls.

"Centurion. Activate your invisibility."

"**COMMAND ACCEPTED, PILOT AMELIA-JUST-AMELIA-ALRIGHT. IMPORTANT! THIS EXOSUIT WILL ONLY BE ABLE TO MAINTAIN ITS PEAK STEALTH CAPABILITIES FOR FIVE MINUTES. INITIATING LEVEL 3 STEALTH... NOW!**"

With a slight hum, Amelia's figure disappears from the view of even the most eagle-eyed observer. Her nanites begin to vibrate at speeds beyond human comprehension while constantly shifting their color to match the ambient environment. Amelia seemingly vanishes into thin air, allowing her to leapfrog the wall without being seen.

As Amelia jumps the man-made barrier, she plummets over the other side and lands in a pile of tossed-aside paper, but her impact doesn't make a noise.

"Sound dampening?" Amelia mutters. "Nice."

The young woman darts across the field. She rushes toward the portal, only for her heart to skip a beat as a Harpie in a doctor's uniform steps through.

_Shit!_

Amelia freezes in place. She stares, wide-eyed, as the bird-like woman casually strolls toward her. However, even when the harpie passes within three feet of Amelia's frozen form, she doesn't bat an eye.

_Holy hell!_ Amelia screams internally. _Harpies have excellent vision and smell! If that lady didn't see me, I doubt any mere human will!_

After swallowing a few frantic breaths, Amelia darts forward, wincing when Centurion gives her a four-minute countdown timer.

Without hesitation, the young woman steps through Maiura's warpgate, directly into the heart of humanity's military. She arrives inside the Labyrinth Core, where, much like on Maiura, hardly any souls stir. A few guards stand at attention near each of the Warpgates, the entrance to Neil's office, and the giant double-doors leading into the Labyrinth. However, aside from a few scattered monster technicians, the Core appears eerily empty.

Amelia narrows her eyes. _Something terrible must have happened. Neil wouldn't call all of the Core and Maiuran personnel for any ordinary meeting. Jason needs me. I know he does!_

The raven-haired woman hesitates for a moment. She darts toward Neil's office and slithers past the two guards standing outside its entrance. Luckily, thanks to the Spectre's stealth capabilities, they don't notice even the slightest shimmer in the air.

When Amelia enters Neil's office, she grabs a feather and parchment and quickly writes a note for the Commander. It takes her almost two minutes to formulate and jot her thoughts down, but still, she finishes with over a minute left on the Spectre's cloaking timer.

_That should do it. If Jason returns and finds out I'm not at the manor, Neil will know where I went. I can trust Neil. Jason told me so._

Amelia shivers slightly, realizing what she's about to do.

_I'm going to head into the Labyrinth, now. I haven't set foot there since my death six years ago. If I didn't think Jason was in trouble, I wouldn't dare to do so. Should the Demon Emperors learn of my existence, they'll stop at nothing to kill me._

Amelia sighs. _If an Emperor comes for me, I won't be able to protect myself. My best chance will be to rely on this exosuit and flee. Without it, I'm nothing._

"**PILOT AMELIA-JUST-AMELIA-ALRIGHT, THIS EXOSUIT CAN ONLY MAINTAIN MAXIMUM STEALTH FOR ANOTHER THIRTY SECONDS.**"

"Right. Let's get a move on, then."

Amelia slips her letter to Neil inside an unmarked envelope, then she sets it under a book on his desk. As she darts away, Amelia smiles.

_Even with that pile of paperwork on Neil's desk, there's no way he won't notice someone has entered his room. That man has a nearly eidetic memory._

Amelia darts toward the two giant double doors leading into the Labyrinth. Luckily, they both remain open by a mere three feet, allowing people to enter and exit single-file.

With a hop, skip, and a jump, Amelia leaps through the doorway and lands silently on the other side. She rushes deeper into the Labyrinth, leaving the range of the deadly laser cannons mere seconds before her invisibility timer runs out.

"**PILOT AMELIA-JUST-AMELIA-ALRIGHT, THIS EXOSUIT WILL REQUIRE ANOTHER SEVEN POINT THREE HOURS TO RECHARGE ITS STEALTH CAPACITORS TO FULL. YOU MAY ACCELERATE THIS TIMER BY PLACING THIS EXOSUIT'S CAPACITOR ON A CHARGING TERMINAL AT YOUR EARLIEST CONVENIENCE.**"

Amelia shakes her head. "Sorry, but we'll have to make do without maximum stealth for a while, Centurion. Let's get a move on."

As the young woman disappears into the Labyrinth's clutches, she groans audibly. "And would you stop with that Amelia-Not-Amelia nonsense! Devils! Get my name right, you stupid hunk of junk!"
