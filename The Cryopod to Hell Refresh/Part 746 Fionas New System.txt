Part 746: Fiona's New System

_Far-Future Era. Day 20, AJR. Chrona._

After receiving their T-REX's, Timothy's group immediately launched into another grueling month of boot camp. While Blinker was not a formally trained military member, she did have plenty of battle experience and was suitably blooded, having killed many foes, weak and powerful alike, especially during Stormbringer.

While Blinker didn't have a handbook on how to train recruits, what she did have was her projection powers, and her fairy formations. This meant, unlike the humanity of ancient Earth, she could simulate extremely realistic combat for her recruits, and they could truly learn how to fire and swing their weapons. Additionally, if anyone was badly hurt, they could be healed up in hours underneath the dedicated Medical Formation, which activated the body's natural healing abilities at an absurd level.

On ancient Earth, recruits would often train by reloading their weapons, shooting and throwing grenades at static targets, and other such things, but it would be difficult or at least morally questionable to acquire real living targets to kill. Thus, Blinker's training was a lot more effective in getting Timothy's group used to cutting down hordes of enemies without feeling squeamish.

For the second month of training, Blinker focused on increasing the difficulty of combat each day, little by little. She pitted the group of five against minotaurs, orcs, enemies at the level of Demon Baron, and even did a simulation against Beelzebub as a Duke. In fact, Blinker trained them against several demons she was relatively familiar with, including a simulation fighting a weakened version of Ose, with some of her stats dialed down a little, but still nevertheless a terrifying foe to face while in basic training.

A good portion of the training was focused on making Paimon the commander of the group. It was a little strange making a non-Player the leader, but the more they fought, the more Blinker realized he had a keen strategic mind and was best suited for the role. Timothy was too young and inexperienced, Marigold was too hot-headed, Ferral was probably the best runner-up, and Raavul was leery of making 'gut decisions'. She preferred to only make moves based on known intel, and wasn't good at weighing and taking risks.

Thus, Paimon ended up being the leader. Blinker wanted to come along, but she was not a Player, and neither was she an entity summoned by a Player, like Paimon was, so she simply couldn't. Timothy had already debated whether he should make her a Player, but after seeing the nerfs the system gave to Raavul, he decided that even if he had another Player slot available, it wouldn't be a good idea.

Thus, four weeks passed by. Timothy and the rest didn't have time to do their daily quests, but surprisingly, the extensive combat training actually increased a few people's stats by a few points.

Timothy gained 2 STR and 2 END, as well as 2 DEX.

Marigold gained 1 STR, 2 END, and 1 DEF.

Ferral gained 1 DEF and 2 RES.

Raavul gained nothing, and neither did Paimon.

Timothy was the weakest, so Blinker's training benefited him the most. Marigold, as the party's tank, took the most abuse, so she gained quite a bit herself. Ferral was the secondary tank, so he also improved his defenses a little, but the other two were either too powerful or couldn't benefit from the King Network's reward functions.

Thus, in the end, the teamwork of the group improved further beyond their starting point, and with Blinker's help, they felt ready for the journey ahead.

"I can't wait to start!" Marigold crowed. "I'm so tired of fighting fake weaklings and not getting any cool items or EXP!"

"I wouldn't call Ose a weakling..." Timothy said with a shudder. He was still slightly traumatized from the simulation a few days earlier against her.

"You guys can enter the Dungeon whenever you want, but I recommend resting for a couple of days first to condition your minds and bodies back to their optimal state." Blinker suggested. "A few days off will do wonders to get you in a better head-space. Also, Timothy, your mother wanted to see you. She told me to wait until your training was done. She said she had a question for you."

Timothy nodded. "I need to take a shower first, but I'll go and see her in a bit."

Everyone said their goodbyes, and Timothy trudged home, his body sore and aching from the training. He took a shower and ate some food that Paimon had whipped up now that they shared a house, then Timothy headed over to meet his mom at the Spynet.

When Timothy arrived, the door was unlocked. It had already been a couple of months since his 'peeping' incident, and given he had a girlfriend now and had been a good boy, Fiona had eventually stopped locking him out and started showing some trust toward him again.

When Timothy entered the Spynet and looked around, he frowned. It had only been two months since he last had time to come over, but already three dozen more monitors had burned out. Now, less than a third of the Spynet's monitors were spying on the various Sentients of the Milky Way, and all the monitors that had once focused on the Volgrim were completely blank.

There were still plenty of monitors pointed at the humans on Sharmur as it hadn't yet been absorbed into the Cube, and there were monitors that displayed some parts of various monster and demon worlds, but Fiona no longer had even a scrap of intel regarding what the Volgrim were up to.

Timothy looked at the monitors for a moment, then he knocked loudly on the door to catch his mother's attention.

"Mom! You in here?"

A moment later, Fiona popped out from behind a pile of monitors laying on the floor in a darker part of the room.

"Oh, Timothy, there you are. Good timing."

She stepped out of the pile of monitors, then stretched while gritting her teeth. Her back audibly popped, and she cracked her neck afterward. It seemed her body was becoming less phantasmal and more corporeal as time went on, though she would never 'truly' become a genuine human like Phoebe was.

Fiona walked over to a desk, then swiped a piece of paper and handed it to her son.

"What's this?" Timothy asked as he looked down at the paper.

"I hit level 10 several weeks ago while you were in training. I'm level 13 now." Fiona said. "I obtained these options for my Level 10 System unlock, but I'm not sure what to choose..."

Timothy's eyes widened. He quickly looked at the paper.

...

**[Pawn Summoning]**

The Player may summon a Pawn from across time and space to accompany them on their journey. The Pawn is random and cannot be chosen, but unwanted pawns can be discarded and re-summoned every set period of time. The period of time can be influenced by many factors. Pawns are NPCs that can either be mindless robotic companions or full-fledged characters with their own journeys and desires.

**[Real Time Strategy Mode]**

The Player obtains the ability to switch their perspective to an omniscient top-down perspective of the area they currently reside upon. When in RTS Mode, the player can execute broad management strategies for the 'units' they control as well as the Buildings inside their region. The Player can only alter or command forces under their control and will be unable to alter the forces of enemy or allied factions.

**[Automated Factory]**

_Crafting System required to access this sub-system._

The Player gains access to a new system which allows them to place down automated crafting devices which can feed into one another to make weapons, armor, devices, and just about anything else on demand. This system can quickly convert raw materials into functional tools, provided the Player has taken the time to construct their automated factory. This system requires a lot of time and resource investment in order to produce results, but those results can turn out far greater than the base Crafting system by itself.

...

Timothy read the paper his mother gave him and his heart nearly stopped.

Seeing the look on her son's face, Fiona nodded. "That's about the reaction I expected. I'm at a loss of what to choose. I know doubling up on these systems is probably a bad idea, but Paimon has been a great help to you and your friends, so I was thinking being able to summon an assistant, or maybe an army of assistants would be useful."

"But," Fiona continued, "I then thought that this 'RTS' mode sounded like it could be exceptionally useful too. I thought you might know better than me."

"It's good." Timothy said slowly, feeling a great sense of disbelief at his mother's incredible options. "It's really good. It's like an Empire Building system, basically. It's perfect for you. It's exactly what you're good at."

"Right, but that Automated Factory sounds great as well..." Fiona said, her tone hesitant. "I worry we don't have enough raw materials on hand to make it useful. What's most important is that nobody else has Crafting but me, so if I don't pick it, someone else will need to choose the Crafting system to get access to Automation. I feel like it's tailor-made for my needs."

Timothy massaged his face furiously. "Gah! If I were to pick, I'd pick the RTS Mode in a heartbeat. But you're right, this Automation option sounds awesome. I definitely don't think you should pick Pawn Summoning, mom. Maybe I can level my Pawn Summoning up and summon you some assistants. Doubling down on my System is a bad idea."

It didn't take long before the two of them came to a decision.

"I'll choose Automated Factory then." Fiona said. "I've barely touched the Crafting System except a little fiddling with it here and there. It seems useful, but I've been too busy using my basic abilities and leveling up to bother with it. Maybe if I invest in this Factory option, I'll have to bring myself use the Crafting skill a lot more."

With that, Fiona made her choice. There was little fanfare, and certainly nothing Timothy could see, but his mother's eyes lit up after a few moments.

"Oh! Ohh! How intriguing!" Fiona exclaimed. "Wow, this is more interesting than I thought! Ohh!"

"What is it?" Timothy asked. "Did something happen??"

Unfortunately, his mother didn't immediately elaborate. She kept staring off into space, or rather, her invisible inventory, oohing and ahhing at something Timothy couldn't see.

Suddenly, Timothy realized this must be how ridiculous he looked when he first showed off the power of the King Network. He must have looked like a complete idiot, staring at absolutely nothing while everyone else waited. It was his just desserts that he had to suffer and wait for his mom to snap out of her daze. What went around came back around...

Five long, excruciating minutes passed. It almost seemed as if Fiona had completely forgotten her son was there, but eventually she snapped out of her daze, blinked her eyes, then turned to Timothy with a giddy expression.

"The King Network just gave me several items, and they're all Soulbound Legendaries!"

"What? Legendary items?!" Timothy exclaimed. "I wanna see, mom!"

"I can't exactly show them to you. I'm not allowed to trade them to other players. I have to summon them with my new ability 'Amateur Factory Worker'." Fiona explained. "Basically, using these items, I can pick up and move objects and even buildings for... basically no cost!"

Timothy squinted. "I don't get it. You can already pick stuff up and move it around."

"No, silly. Didn't you hear what I said?" Fiona quipped. "I said items AND buildings! BUILDINGS, Timothy! I can move buildings!"

Timothy's eyes widened. "Wait, seriously? How does that even work?"

"I don't know! Let's go test it out." Fiona said.

And so they did. The mother and son went outside and walked over to one of the ordinary residences that wasn't currently being used. Fiona poked the air with her finger, and all of a sudden, a strange gun that looked like a prop from a 1960's sci-fi movie appeared in her hand. It was yellow with blue rings around what Timothy assumed was the 'barrel' of the gun, except it had some sort of a Tesla-style orb poking out of the end. It looked like it was designed to shoot ray beams or lasers or something.

Frankly, Timothy thought it looked silly and cartoonish.

Fiona held the small gun in one hand. Then, she pointed it at the building.

A few seconds later, she frowned and pulled it away. "That's weird. I can't activate the tool. It says there are 7,044 life forms inside the structure, preventing it from being moved."

"Seven thousand??" Timothy asked in bewilderment.

"Yes. Umi, nobody lives inside the building. Why would it give me that warning?" Fiona asked.

Once again, Fiona spoke to someone Timothy couldn't hear, and while he knew it was obviously Umi, he once again realized how silly it looked when a Player seemingly talked to the air itself. He made a silent vow not to do that when non-Players were present.

"Oh." Fiona said after a moment. She scratched her head. "It seems, um, there are bugs inside the building. Lots of them. The tool can't move a structure that has any lifeforms inside of it."

"That's an odd restriction." Timothy muttered.

"Well, there's another restriction too, which is that the structure I want to move has to have been 'mostly' constructed by me personally, or by my powers. So I have to have been responsible for at least 51% of the building process. And, well, it was your father who made all the buildings in Chrona, so I can't actually use the tool on them."

"That's unfortunate." Timothy said, shrugging his shoulders. "Maybe it'll be useful in the future?"

"Definitely!" Fiona chirped. "Because the restriction on buildings doesn't apply to smaller objects. Check this out!"

She pointed at a shovel leaning against a wall. Then she aimed the fake-toy-looking gun at the shovel and pulled the trigger. A split second later, the shovel abruptly turned into a speck of light, then flew toward Fiona's body with a blur of movement before disappearing right before it was about to strike her.

"Huh?!" Timothy guffawed. "The heck was that?"

"I can move items directly into my inventory with this gun." Fiona said smugly. "And I can place them down remotely too, even controlling the exact positioning."

"But... all you need to do is touch an item to add it to your inventory." Timothy said hesitantly. "That gun doesn't seem all too useful."

"Usefulness is in the eye of the beholder. I can instantly take a monitor off the ceiling now, then put it on the ground, repair it, and stick it back on the ceiling where it originally was." Fiona explained. "And that's just one thing this gun will let me do now! Furthermore, this isn't even the only new tool I acquired."

With a wave of her hand, Fiona made the gun vanish with a flash of light. Then, a new device appeared in her palm. It looked like an equally cartoony and deeply unserious device, colored gray with a red antenna doohickey sticking up out of the top right corner. It had some buttons on the front, and a black screen with green text on it. When Timothy looked at the green text, it looked like random letters and numbers, completely gibberish placeholder text.

"With THIS device, I can scan any inanimate object and see a listing of its faults, its wear and tear status, and any structural issues. Then if I spend resources, I can automatically repair the object back to working order using a third device. This is really going to speed up my constant repair work around Chrona!"

Timothy was completely bewildered. "I mean, that one sounds a lot more useful, but why does it look so... silly? It's made of cheap plastic."

"No idea." Fiona said, shrugging the question off. "All I care about is that it works. I also have a Constructor and Recycler tool. They work a lot like my active skills, except they retrieve 100% of the materials used to build things, minus any degraded or damaged components. So I can deconstruct buildings and large objects, then use their parts to rebuild the same objects or different ones."

Timothy raised an eyebrow. Suddenly, his mom's newest upgrade didn't sound so pitiful anymore. "It sounds like you've acquired all the tools you need to completely tear down and rebuild Chrona from scratch..."

"That's exactly it!" Fiona exclaimed. She beamed a bright smile at her son. "I'm really starting to like these powers, Timothy. We might have a real shot at repairing Chrona's foundations. When you enter the Dungeon, I want you to be safe, but also try to see if you can acquire lots of raw materials for me. That half-ton of steel, for example. The more stuff like that you can get me, the better."

"Sure, mom. I can do that." Timothy agreed wearily. "I'm glad your new ability is exactly what you needed. I'm gonna go home and get some sleep. I'm still pretty beat from Aunt Blinker's training."

"Are you hurt?" Fiona asked, as a hint of concern played on her face. "Need me to use my Mother's Intuition skill to heal you?"

"Cough cough!! I'm fine!" Timothy said awkwardly. "I'm gonna go now. Bye mom!"

Even though he was a little embarrassed, Timothy didn't neglect to give his mother a hug and a kiss goodbye. Afterward, he headed home and plopped down face-first on his bed to sleep.

"Two more days." Timothy muttered. "Two more days and we can finally do our first dungeon raid."

He smushed his face into his pillow and grinned like an idiot.

"I'm so excited! I can't wait!"
