Part 748: The Dark Forest

_Far-Future Era. Day 20, AJR. Chrona._

Time passed quickly inside the Dark Forest Dungeon. Before long, three hours had come and gone. The Dungeon stopped looking like an endless sea of trees and shrubbery. At some point, the party of five started encountering more diverse terrain. They saw a pitch black pool of water in the distance, where there was a notable void of trees around it. They had to take a detour to get around it, and they slowed their pace a little, looking at the water with fear in their eyes.

The pool had creatures near it! Faint torches were held aloft by powerful warriors armed with primitive-looking sharpened spears. For the first time, the party spotted life lurking at the edge of the small lake, each individual looking around warily. A few creatures carefully scooped water out of the lake with that looked like five gallon buckets. They looked hesitant about disturbing the water.

The creatures were bipedal. They had fur all over their bodies, and wore mean-looking necklaces and clothing made of bone. It was clear at a glance that they wore their garb as a warning to others not to mess with them. They didn't look human, more like purple-skinned apes with deformed faces and huge noses. Their eyes were especially large and pitch-black. 

[Slow our pace...] Ferral said warily. [Those creatures likely have excellent night vision.]

[A sensible idea.] Raavul replied.

The party carefully detoured the pool. As they did, Timothy chuckled nervously into his microphone.

"Careful now, Marigold. I'm sure you want to go for a dip, but we'll have to sit this one out, hehe."

He laughed to himself, but he didn't get a response from his girlfriend. After a moment, Timothy frowned. He carefully looked backward, but Marigold was still in the rear, like before. He looked at her for another few seconds.

"Marigold? Hello? Chrona to Marigold! Are you there?"

Marigold lifted her head. "...Huh?"

"Marigold!" Timothy snapped. "Are you _sleeping?!_"

Marigold became a little more alert. "Oh! Um... I just... was resting my eyes..."

Ferral glanced back at Marigold. He hadn't noticed her drowsiness, because he had been focusing on levitating the others while trying to perceive the area around their party. It took all his mental effort to do just this, so he didn't have the energy to spare for other things.

[If you are tired, you should have told us.] Ferral said, his tone one of light anger.

[Yes.] Raavul said, not bothering to look back. [You have endangered the party. What if we were being pursued and your inattentiveness caused us to overlook the presence of possible enemies?]

"How could you be sleepy anyway?" Timothy asked, furrowing his brow. "Didn't you sleep last night? I told you to get a good night's sleep before we left!"

Marigold lowered her head, slightly ashamed. "Kyargh... I'm sorry. I don't know what came over me. I did sleep, yeah. I don't usually get drowsy like this..."

The Psions levitated to a stop. They gently set the others down, then used their methods to sweep the area around them.

Raavul stiffened. [Damn. We've been followed.]

Timothy's blood ran cold. He looked at her helmet, then followed her general gaze, which seemed to be focused somewhere on the path behind them.

[Fifty potential hostiles sneaking in the underbrush behind us.] Ferral said, following his teacher's lead. [We'll need to pick up the pace if we hope to lose them.]

[I cannot sense their visual appearance.] Raavul said, her tone tinged with worry. [I can only sense the movements of the glade. The stealth capabilities of our pursuers exceed the scope of my understanding. We must flee!]

The good news was, the party hadn't been surrounded. If they drastically picked up the pace, Timothy's group might be able to shake their pursuers. The bad news was, they were no longer as incognito as they had previously hoped.

Timothy glowered at his girlfriend underneath his helmet, where she couldn't see. He was rightfully annoyed, and even a bit pissed off. It was one thing if she was tired and had said something, but she'd put the whole group at serious risk, and they were only a few hours into a possible seven-day mission!

How could she be so irresponsible as to literally fall asleep on the job?!

Timothy exhaled quietly. He kept his thoughts to himself, then turned to Paimon.

"You think we should stand and fight or make a break for it?"

Paimon shook his head. "If we fight, we risk drawing the attention of the watering hole group. Better to pick up the pace, even if it does make us more conspicuous. Let's get a move on."

Raavul and Ferral nodded. Since Paimon was the designated leader, they chose to do as he ordered. A moment later, the trio was lifted up again, but this time, Marigold was made to 'stand' in the middle and Paimon was moved to laying down in the back. 

Paimon took out the rifle Fiona had given him. He decided to cradle it in his arms so that if the enemies suddenly attacked from behind, he could hold them off and slow them down, though doing so would definitely alert the denizens of the Dark Forest.

With the new formation prepared, the group set off, deliberately increasing their speed. Paimon watched warily as the enemies behind them fell behind for a while, then picked up their speed when they realized Timothy's party had noticed their presence.

An hour passed. Slowly but surely, the enemies in the rear faded into the background. Paimon eventually lost sight of their heat signatures, and he also stopped being able to see them at all.

"Movement up ahead." Timothy said, causing the others to become more alert. "It's something... big."

Paimon cursed under his breath. The group slowed to a stop and carefully adhered to a tree, with the Psions levitating in place, and the other three grabbing onto the tree with both feet and one hand. Paimon squinted as he looked up ahead into the darkness, using his free hand to hold the rifle defensively.

Demons had incredible night-time eyesight, but their species advantage was dramatically lessened at the lower levels. Belial could see clearly even in pitch-black darkness, but Paimon's vision was much closer to that of a 'peak human', since Paimon was merely a Grunt. He could distinguish movement in the Dark Forest, but he couldn't make out much in the way of details.

As such, even Paimon had to rely on the T-REX for visual clarity. The night-vision feature, coupled with his superior vision, helped make up for his deficits.

Up in the distance, a lumbering behemoth was slowly moving forward, pushing the trees aside as it walked. It was a quadruped covered in leaves and moss. Its exact shape was impossible to make out. Two yellowed tusks stuck out of whatever represented its mouth, even protruding through the leaves hiding its body from view. It was huge, but it made surprisingly little noise, as if it had adapted its biology for stealth rather than power or defense.

[What a strange creature...] Ferral said, his voice low. [Is it an exobeast? Or a Sentient?]

Nobody knew the answer. They merely watched as the creature lumbered past. It took 15 minutes, but eventually the behemoth traveled out of range.

"The ones from before have caught up." Timothy said, gesturing with his free hand in the direction the party just traveled from.

Everyone grimaced in unison.

[Our pursuers are persistent.] Raavul said.

"You think we should make our stand and take them out? Nip the problem in the bud?" Timothy asked.

Raavul hesitated. She looked at Paimon, as did the others.

"Even though there are only five of us, and the enemy numbers around fifty, at least as far as we can determine, we likely have some real advantages." Paimon said. "The enemy is staying low to the ground, while we can take to the tree branches. We have ranged weapons. We can kill several of them if we strike hard."

Paimon shook his head. "But this is a foolish decision. The sound of our attacks will likely draw a lot of attention. We should only fight if we absolutely must. Killing off these foes will save us for now, only to call down a guillotine upon our necks later."

Everyone nodded. Paimon's words were reasonable.

"However," Paimon said, "just because fighting these enemies is a bad idea, that does not mean we cannot use other means."

Once he spoke, everyone realized what he was thinking. The group once again took off, levitated by the Psions. As they flew, Timothy reached into his inventory and took out some materials his mother had provided him. Among them was a ten-gallon tank of gasoline. Intended to provide fuel for fires in survival situations, the gasoline served a different purpose now as Timothy began lightly pouring it behind the group as they traveled.

It didn't take long before Timothy had drawn a 500 meter line of flammable liquid. After that, he lit a match and tossed it into the foliage, causing a fire to spring up behind the party.

With this, the Psions quickened their speed. They raced forward, leaving the newly awakened wall of fire to light up the darkness, causing mayhem as countless screeches and roars erupted from all angles.

Fearful of the consequences of their actions, the group fled as fast as they could. In the distance, they saw huge shadowy shapes descending upon the fire, and those shapes began to violently battle one another. The impact of their attacks sent shockwaves outward, which caused the fire to spread even more quickly than before.

"The creatures here will fight each other?" Timothy asked doubtfully. "I didn't realize that. We could use this to our advantage in the future."

Right after he spoke, Timothy flinched. A window popped up in front of him.

...

**Deep Forest Marauder defeated.**

_Acquired: 140 exp. 240 Gold. 1x Deep Forest Marauder remains. Deep Forest Marauder tooth x3._

...

Timothy blinked. "Huh?"

It didn't take a rocket scientist to figure out what had happened. He looked backward in shock. "I just got a kill message! The forest fire is counted as me killing the monsters!"

Then he got another message. And another one.

...

**Deep Forest Marauder x3 defeated.**

_Acquired: 420 exp. 900 Gold. 3x Deep Forest Marauder remains. Deep Forest Marauder Pelt x1. Deep Forest Marauder Claws x2. Deep Forest Marauder Eyeballs x5._

**Forest Crawler Defeated.**

_Acquired: 90 EXP. 580 Gold. 1x Flint Knife (Common). 4x Darkstones._

**Lurking Eye Defeated.**

_Acquired: 501 EXP. 1400 Gold. Darkvision Skillbook (Common)._

...

Timothy felt deep shock at first, but gradually, as the kills started to rack up, his emotions turned to excitement, then numbness.

Monsters were dying at an inconceivable rate! Every minute, five or more perished to the flames, and those flames attracted even more monsters who died afterward.

Timothy stared at his Character sheet. He was Level 9 when he entered the Dark Forest, but now, he had reached Level 12. And his EXP continued to climb...

[You have leveled up.]

[You have leveled up.]

[You have leveled up.]

[You have leveled up.]

Timothy stopped focusing on his levels. He simply didn't have time to look through all the awards, so he put them out of mind.

Eventually, the flames either burned out or enemies stopped running into them, or maybe Timothy left the range of the fire and the King Network stopped counting the deaths of the forest denizens as Timothy's credit. Timothy's radical upward growth slowed down until he reached Level 21.

He had gained 12 levels in less than two hours. This was a shocking pace of development, to the point that he was shaking with anxiety. He was happy, no doubt, but the thought that he had leveled up off the deaths of other creatures felt somewhat... morbid.

Timothy made sure to let everyone else know. All of them were in awe, and definitely jealous.

"Kyargh! You think if we all burn this whole forest down, we'll hit level 100??" Marigold asked excitedly.

[Regardless of whether the creatures here are 'real' or not, that seems a vile way to acquire levels.] Raavul said disapprovingly. [Timothy obtained those levels by accident. He cannot be blamed for the creatures throwing themselves into the fire, but if he were to do so again, he would definitely be to blame.]

[I do not believe the creatures here are 'false' or 'fake' lifeforms.] Ferral added. [The fact a Dungeon Break event can cause them to spill into our universe indicates they must truly exist somewhere, somehow. Thus, killing them en-masse for sport or gains can be seen as an act of genocide.]

That made Timothy's mood even worse. "Even if it was an accident, you're right. I didn't want to kill all of them. I thought I'd just scare them away. Now I feel like a monster..."

Timothy looked at his Character page. After killing all the Dark Forest creatures, he had acquired more than 75,000 gold. He didn't have a clear barometer for the value of the gold, but 75,000 sure seemed like a lot!

_What's done is done._ Timothy thought. _I can't take back all those deaths. I'll just have to make sure I don't do it again. In the meantime, maybe I should visit the Shop to see what I can buy?_

But then, he woke up from his thoughts when his T-REX beeped a warning. "Guys! Movement up ahead. Multiple bogeys."

The party slowed down. Their expressions turned ugly. There appeared to be a massive caravan crossing the forest up ahead. Unlike all the other enemies inside the Dark Forest, these creatures were holding torches, dressed ostentatiously, and wearing armor that looked extremely well-maintained.

One of the creatures sat atop a giant lizard-like steed, while all the others surrounded the noble figure in a protective formation, yet there was no trace of fear in their movements. It was as if they were openly taunting the forest, daring others to come attack them. None took the bait.

Timothy activated his Eye of Yredelemnul skill. He scanned a few of the creatures, but their stat sheets didn't pop up.

...

**Emissary of Ashenzari - Dark Forest - Supreme Existence**

_A powerful adherent of the cult of Ashenzari. An elite capable of fighting ten other Sentients at the same time. Fast and strong, their lacking defense is improved by the quality of their armor. Ashenzari's Emissarys possess the power to tap into nature itself, making them especially deadly in dense woodlands._

**Warrior of Ashenzari - Dark Forest - Guardian**

_A high-ranking adherent of the cult of Ashenzari. A powerful warrior who fights to protect their designated Emissary. Capable of entering a Battle Rage, they can rip through the defenses of their enemies, tearing their flesh and bones apart with great ease._

**Priest of Ashenzari - Dark Forest - Devotee**

_A faithful worshiper of Ashenzari. Capable of healing their allies swiftly through Ashenzari's Blessing while also wielding great power through their curse magic. Ashenzari's Priests are capable of peering into the darkness within the hearts of others and snuffing their life out, should that darkness prove oppressive enough._

...

"These are strong enemies." Timothy said quietly. "We don't want to fight them. They might be the apex predators of the Dark Forest."

Just as Timothy spoke, the lone Emissary atop the giant lizard suddenly turned its head. The Emissary was wearing a golden mask made in the style of a giant diamond, obscuring its face. But even so, Timothy felt as if he had been spotted.

[A powerful entity has observed your location.]

A warning appeared in front of him, generated by Yredelemnul's Eye. Timothy froze, and his heart nearly stopped.

Another notification appeared.

[A powerful entity has recognized that you are a devotee of a fellow Dark One. The powerful entity has lost interest in you.]

The Emissary looked at Timothy for all of five seconds, then looked away. The procession continued, leaving Timothy sweating nervously until they had passed far, far out of range.

"Was it just me, or did that strong guy look in our direction?" Marigold asked, levitating behind Timothy. "He was really scary."

[It seems the creature sensed something amiss, but then it lost interest.] Ferral replied.

[We're fortunate they passed us by.] Raavul added. [If we were to fight those monsters, we would have been annihilated. They were all at the peak of mortal strength. Each was stronger than a Demon Emperor.]

Timothy's expression distorted. "What about the one on top of the mount?"

[You would experience great fear if I told you.] Raavul said simply.

Timothy wasn't sure why his Eye couldn't detect the creature's stats. Maybe it was because Ashenzari was one of the 'dark ones'? He didn't know who they were, but they sounded scary. It seemed they also mistook him for a fellow devotee, but he knew he wasn't one.

Timothy thought for a moment, then he remembered that Yredelemnul's Eye stated it once belonged to a 'Dark One.' Could that have been what the Emissary detected?

_I'm lucky I had this skill. It caused a misunderstanding that helped us out!_ Timothy thought.

Obviously, he wasn't a devotee of a 'Dark One', but he did possess a fragment of one individual's power. If it caused strong enemies to avoid him, then the effect was good in his eyes.

Once Ashenzari's procession passed, the group continued onward. They traveled for six more hours without incident, and no enemies pursued them either.

Eventually, everyone came to a stop. They spotted a tree with particularly dense branches, and after making sure no other life forms were nearby, the tired group stopped to make camp for the night.

"Do either of you need to sleep? You've been doing all the work, levitating us around and such." Paimon said to the Psions.

[Psions do not require sleep.] Ferral said. [I am indeed somewhat drained, but a few hours of meditation will restore my mind back to its full capacity.]

[I will take the first watch.] Raavul added. [Ferral, you will meditate, and once you have rested, I will meditate next. I have more energy than you. As your senior, taking the first watch is only natural.]

Timothy was exhausted. He looked at Marigold. She seemed to be full of energy.

"Did you get lots of sleep on the way over here?" Timothy asked, his words sarcastic but also tinged with bitterness.

"I already said I was sorry!" Marigold snapped back. "I don't know why I dozed off. I got plenty of sleep last night, I just felt like my eyelids were really heavy."

Timothy shook his head. "Whatever. Just try not to let it happen again. If you're feeling sleepy, just tell us. If you don't, someone could sneak up on us."

"Sure." Marigold said, but it was clear she was angry about being interrogated like this.

Timothy reached into his inventory and pulled out some sleeping bags and pillows. He offered them to Marigold and Paimon, but Marigold didn't seem too sleepy, and Paimon was a Demon Grunt, so he could probably go another 2 or 3 days without sleep.

Sadly, Timothy, even though he was a Player, was still only a human in the end. He needed his beauty sleep.

As such, he laid his head down and fell fast asleep within minutes.

Half an hour later, Marigold slid over to Paimon's side. He was meditating to rest his mind a little without actually needing to sleep. When he sensed Marigold's presence, Paimon turned to look at her.

"Do you need something?" Paimon asked.

Marigold kept her voice low. "I'm so sorry I fell asleep earlier. I didn't mean to!"

"I know you didn't. Timothy does too. Perhaps the darkness of the forest tricked your senses into thinking it was night-time. Your body reacted accordingly."

"Maybe that's it." Marigold mumbled. "Or maybe I'm just not dependable..."

Marigold sighed. She felt terrible, but she didn't know what to do. She thought the Dungeon would be exciting and fun, but so far all the party had done was run and hide from monsters she couldn't even see properly. It was more boring and irritating than anything else.

"I hope something fun happens soon." Marigold grumbled. "Then I'll be able to show off my worth."

Paimon shook his head.

"Be careful what you wish for."
