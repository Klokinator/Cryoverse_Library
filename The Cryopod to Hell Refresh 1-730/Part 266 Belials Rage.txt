Part 266: Belial's Rage

_First Day, Eighteenth Hour._

Ose, the Emperor of Infiltration, soars around Tarus II at blinding speeds, unconstrained by wind resistance, friction, or most other limitations placed upon mortal bodies. Only gravity exerts any pull on her invisible and immaterial ghost-body, preventing her from soaring to the heavens and leaving the planet behind.

She flickers from building to building inside Hero City's environmental shield, steadily growing more impatient and antsy as she scours every nook and cranny for the thing she needs most.

"Damn!" Ose shouts, after searching every inch of Jason Hiro's home, the giant building once owned by Bahamut. "It's not here, either! Where did Neil hide the devil-damned Trifrancium?!"

She pauses momentarily to hover just outside the house, scowling as she watches dozens of human commandos running around, traveling through the city to reinforce weakening positions on the border's defenses.

"I must have scoured every godforsaken building in this wretched shithole ten times! I've searched the prisons, the factories, even the stupid little huts on the city's outskirts... I've searched everywhere! Neil, you bloody bastard! Where did you hide it?!"

She paces back and forth in midair, hover-walking to make herself think more strategically.

"...It wasn't in the Core. If it was, then when Neil blew up the core, the Trifrancium would have detonated and left a planet-sized hole in the Labyrinth's center. A kilogram of Trifrancium has almost 50 gigatons of explosive yield; more than enough to irradiate the entirety of the Labyrinth and make it inhospitable to life for a million years. I cannot allow the humans to possess it any longer."

After a moment, Ose flicks her eyes upward.

"Perhaps Moonbase Kelkin? My Astral Body is constrained by this planet's gravity, so I can't travel there myself. What are the odds Neil would know that fact and plan accordingly? Bah! He could have hid the Trifrancium there for any number of reasons. If so, then it will remain out of my grasp until after I conquer these humans. I'll have to fly a ship up there afterward and take it back by force. Drat!"

She frowns. "Oh, devils. What if Neil hid the Trifrancium on one of humanity's other planets? He could have thrown it under some random rock and I'd never be able to find it. It's like looking for a needle in a hundred planet-sized haystacks! Utter insanity."

Ose's shoulders slump in defeat. "I need a drink."

The Emperor of Infiltration falls silent. She continues to hover in midair, thinking slowly and methodically about a great many things.

_Jason hasn't made any moves since I last heard from him. It seems my Wordsmith-crippling device successfully bombarded him with chaotic energy. His magical conduits will need weeks to heal; more than enough time to uncover his hiding spot. Where the devil has he gone, anyway? He only showed up to the battlefield remotely, but also seems to have discovered an inexhaustible source of mana. There's no way the Jason I've observed could have continuously closed hundreds of portals for hours on end. Not even Hope could pull off such a feat._

_And speaking of Hope; where has HE gone? Both Wordsmiths mysteriously vanished right as I initiated Stormbringer. Coincidence? Unlikely! They must be planning something. I can't just sit back and smile, thinking I've won this war. If I only capture one Wordsmith, I'll have to give him to Yama. That was the deal I agreed to, in exchange for the Shadow Emperor's help._

_Hmph! On second thought, why should I abide by those terms? Yama's worthless Shades failed! They didn't capture Jason's daughter or wife! They died like useless, dried up bags of skarn-droppings. Forget Yama! If I only catch one Wordsmith, he'll be mine! What can Yama do anyway? He'll be going up against Hell's most intelligent Emperor and her Wordsmithing slave! He'd only end up guaranteeing his death! Yes, I like that idea much more._

Ose nods to herself. "An excellent plan. Jason will be out of commission for weeks while his magical circuits heal, leaving me plenty of time to take out humanity, capture his wife and daughter, and turn them into bargaining chips. I can interrogate Neil to uncover where he hid the Trifrancium, deliver it to the Overlords, and avert a catastrophe."

The Third Emperor smoothly flicks her eyes in all directions. She expands her senses, gathering intel from the battlefield, then turns her head to the east.

"What's that? A tremendous surge of mana? Ah, it's the Monster King. Looks like he's engaging with the Battle Brothers. Bah, they're durable. They can keep his attention for a while. Once Bael and I recover, we'll join with Artorias and Mephisto to kill that Sphinx once and for all. Oooh! Perhaps, if I can uncover the secret to his riddle magic, I might even take his powers for myself! Wouldn't that be the ultimate payoff? With a Wordsmith as my slave, and the Sphinx's body transformation powers to elevate my status, no other Emperor will dare to challenge my authority!"

Ose turns toward the west, then zips forward at hyperspeed, traveling several miles almost instantaneously. She arrives above the western front, between the Warpgate Encampment and the Horned forest, where humanity's defenses barely manage to hold the line. Every second, the humans and their monster allies battle to the death, killing ten times more demons than their forces lose as casualties, but even so, Ose's army slowly pushes forward with its unstoppable numerical advantage.

"Hehehe. It seems all is going well over- huh?"

Ose's eyes narrow to slits. She focuses her vision at the ground below her, where Artorias, the highest ranked demon among the western front's forces, engages in battle with an Emperor-level mana signature.

"What! Belial? Satan's whore?! What is she doing here?"

Ose gnashes her teeth. She flickers to a spot only a few hundred feet above the ground, where she watches in fascination as Belial punches away demons by the dozen. Every swing of her fist, capable of pulverizing mountains, blasts away Grunts and Lords like leaves in the wind. Only a few Barons manage to hold their ground, while Artorias suffers the most under Belial's attacks.

"Blast!" Ose curses. "Last time I checked, Belial had no mana left and was utterly exhausted! Why is she so full of energy now? Oh, let me guess! I'll bet Jason's last defiant act was to reinvigorate her with his devil-damned Wordsmithing! Well, isn't that just _delightful_?!"

It takes a few minutes of screaming and cursing the elder gods before Ose regains control of herself. She focuses her mind and turns her thoughts inward.

_Belial and the Monster Royals are the only dangers left to successfully completing this operation. Unfortunately, it seems the Sphinx empowered himself at some point in the recent past, elevating his strength to that of a Duke, or perhaps even an Emperor! Not only that, but his wife possesses incredible strength, as well. Even if I assume the best, it's one Emperor and two Dukes battling one Duke and three Barons. Belial, Kar, and Blinker against Artorias, Mara, Duriel, and Zamiel. Weaker Barons won't matter in an engagement this lopsided._

_Worse yet, if both Royals are indeed at Emperor-level strength, then I have no counterplay at all! Even if I show up with Bael, it will be three Emperors vs one Emperor, two Dukes, and three Barons._

Once again, Ose expands her senses, searching the battlefield for mana blips.

_I don't understand. Where did Beelzebub go? Did he leave the battlefield without telling anyone? The last I heard, he left Mara and Artorias behind in the prisons, and then he disappeared off the face of the planet. Did the Wordsmith interfere? Did Jason kill him?_

She shakes her head. _No. Jason isn't a killer. He's too soft. It might have been Hope. Ah! That must be it! Hope probably turned himself invisible and decided to stalk the demon leaders from the shadows! That's exactly the sort of thing he'd do. I'll bet he attacked Beelzebub and... no, that still doesn't make any sense. Wordsmithing is powerful, but it's far from omnipotent. If Hope had fought Beelzebub, their encounter would have caused quite the uproar. I'd have noticed, for sure! Beelzebub must still be alive..._

Ose's eyes twitch. Confusion appears on her face, along with hesitation.

_I don't have enough information. I'm missing pieces. There's something off about this whole operation. Why haven't the devil-damned Wordsmiths appeared on the battlefield? Why haven't I heard a single thing from Hope? Why did Jason come here in soul-form? Could he be trapped somewhere? Did someone capture him after he left my palace? What is going on?! It's infuriating!_

Her eyes turn cold.

_I don't have a choice. I'll have to leave the battlefield for now. Vepar and Laharl have likely recovered, so I'll send them back to reinforce Artorias. Then, I'll return to the Blood Pits, interrogate Neil, and find out where he hid the Trifrancium._

Ose turns to the west again. She flies toward the now-open warpgate and flies through, smiling faintly.

_That's right. I should focus on killing Belial first. It's unfortunate, but the Battle Brothers will have to act as sacrificial pawns. The Monster King has an old grievance with them. Therefore, he and his wife won't leave until they've killed both brothers. In the meantime, perhaps I can amass a force large enough to kill Belial. With her gone, the numbers will tilt significantly in my favor! Both Monster Royals against all of my forces? They won't stand a chance._

Ose disappears into the Labyrinth, laughing wickedly all the way.

"Hahaha! My hour will soon arrive! Total control of demonkind! My ascension to Second Emperor, and perhaps even First, once I find and eliminate the worthless bastard sitting uselessly on his fancy throne! I'll enslave the Wordsmiths and make the galaxy mine!"

"A new era of demonkind has all but arrived!"

---

_Tarus II, Western Front._

Belial clashes with Artorias, the Duke of Steel. As the Second Emperor of demonkind, her bodily strength stands an entire tier above other Emperors, placing her at the absolute height of power any Demon will ever hope to possess.

Having assumed a terrifying form, Belial's beach-ball-sized head appears like a disfigured monster with dozens of eyes and a mouth filled with multiple rows of teeth. Her lower body is no less terrifying, sporting four arms, all of which possess five knife-shaped claws capable of turning flesh into sashimi. Her two legs are as thick as tree trunks, giving her the strength to kick her foes into the stratosphere or stomp them into meat-paste.

An apex predator, Belial not only battles the Duke of Steel, but even takes 'breaks' to eviscerate other demons nearby, ripping them limb from limb. Their horrified cries of pain disappear in an instant as the Emperor of Passion slaughters her kin wholesale.

"Belial!" Artorias shouts, after raising his arms to block one of her mountain-flattening kicks, "Argh! Stop! Have you gone insane?! You're killing your own people!"

Belial whips her freakishly large head toward him. The blood of chewed-up demons drips from her lips, while their entrails sit wedged between the gaps in her teeth. "Shut up, Artorias! You don't get to judge me! You attacked humanity, and now all who oppose me will perish! You made your bed... NOW LIE IN IT!"

Her voice, no longer soft, cute, and demure, sounds hoarse and vicious, like an eldritch monster fresh out of the deep sea. She lunges toward Artorias and headbutts him, knocking him on his ass and flinging him fifty feet away. Before Artorias can reorient himself, Belial rushes toward him and rears her leg back.

_WHUMP!_

Belial kicks Artorias like a football, blasting him into the air. Artorias goes flying. He spins and careens helplessly while hurtling up into the sky at unthinkable speeds.

Before he makes it a thousand yards, a portal appears in the sky before him.

_Foop!_

Artorias flies into the portal and exits out another one aimed at the ground. He only gets a second to register Tarus II's soil rushing at him before he crashes into the horde of demons below, instantly killing several dozen Scythers and Brutes. Artorias grinds against the dirt while riding a mushy meat-toboggan made of his now-deceased comrades. Several seconds later, he finally grinds to a stop and gasps for breath.

"Huk! Huk! Damn. Damn! Belial is... she's too much for me! She's monstrously powerful, like an unstoppable force of nature. Where is Ose at a time like this? We need her to deal with the Second Emperor!"

Artorias climbs to his feet while grimacing at the sensation of mashed demon parts squishing beneath his shoes.

After steadying himself, he turns his attention toward Belial's Emperor-level mana signature. With Barbatos's tall body, Artorias gazes over the waves of demons and undead, clearly able to pick out Belial's frenzying figure without any trouble.

"Seven Hells..." Artorias mutters. "I'm only one Duke. I can't even stall Belial on my own. All I have is my earth manipulation. Barbatos... if he were awake, he could use both our powers."

The Duke of Steel hesitates. He searches his mana conduits with his spiritual sense, only to find nothing.

"Uriel and Barbatos are both sleeping. I can tap into Uriel's magic for now. Having access to all four elements, plus light and dark manipulation, that would allow me to fight somewhat evenly with Belial. However, if I do that, there's a chance I might awaken Uriel. I cannot allow her to emerge!"

Grunts and Lords sweep around Artorias's motionless figure, avoiding bumping into him instinctively due to his Duke-level mana signature. He, in turn, ignores the flood of comrades surrounding him and grits his teeth.

"I cannot resist Uriel on my own. Only by combining my soul with Barbatos's can we prevent her from controlling us. In turn, I can only manipulate my brother's body when he and Uriel both fall dormant. Tch. If only I could tear the Archangel's soul out, my brother and I would become the strongest Duke in all the seven Hells, even beyond Bael. The power of two demons unified in one body would make us unstoppable among our peers."

Artorias shakes his head. "But even if that were the case... we still wouldn't match up to the Second Emperor. She isn't only an Emperor, but the strongest of our species. Only Satan himself could defeat her in battle, and that's only assuming a theoretical matchup."

The Duke of Steel crouches down to build up strength in his legs. He aims his gaze in Belial's direction and sighs.

_If I can't beat her... I'll have to convince her to stop fighting!_

A moment later, Artorias jumps skyward. His superior Duke strength allows him to jump half a mile with ease, leaping over the heads of all the grunts beneath him while gazing fixedly at Belial's rampaging form.

The Second Emperor roars like a mad lion, then pounces at a group of Burner demons. They panic and throw fireballs at her, but she ignores their pitiful attacks as if they were gnats timidly bumping against her nose. She opens her mouth and bites down, chomping three of the Burners in half, before swinging out her bladed arms to eviscerate those who remain.

_Whump!_

Artorias stomps into the dirt behind Belial and slides to a stop. He assumes a defensive position and shouts. "Belial! Stop fighting! We can talk about this! I understand that you're angry, but-"

"SHUT UP!!" Belial roars. She spins around to face Artorias with a crazed look in her eyes. "I ALREADY BROKE A SOLEMN VOW! NOW, I'LL BREAK YOU!"

Artorias sucks in a quick breath before Belial lunges at him. "Oh, spawn of a..."

He doesn't even see Belial's attack coming. She skitters along the ground like a lizard, clearing the short gap with lightning speed, then jumps into the air.

_BOOM!_

Belial's muscular legs stomp Artorias into the dirt, crushing the Duke with brute force. In one instant, he finds himself crouched and ready for anything, but in the next, the world turns upside down while stars dance in his eyes. 

Artorias's helmet strikes a rock, pulverizing it into earthen powder. Belial pauses for a split second to spread out her bladed-fingers, then stabs them toward the Duke's chest.

_Tink-tink-tink-CLANG!_

Most of Belial's makeshift knives bend when they strike Artorias's sturdy demonstone armor, but a few find their way between the gaps in his armor and impale his vital organs.

"Argh!" Artorias shouts. He tries to push Belial away, but his strength proves wildly inferior to hers.

"It's too bad, Artorias!" Belial shouts. "I never disliked Barbatos! He wouldn't have chosen to come here! But you did! Now, you both will die for that decision, along with Uriel, too! At least I can finally give her the burial she deserves!"

"Wait, Belial! Stop! You're not a killer!" Artorias yells. "This isn't like you!"

A twisted grin plays across Belial's monstrous face. "I didn't choose this path, Artorias. You forced it on me. OSE forced it on me!"

Belial lifts her foot up.

_BOOM!!_

It crashes upon Artorias's chest, smashing him into a shallow crater in the dirt.

_Boom! Boom! Boom!_

Again and again, Belial stomps Artorias, overpowering him to the point he can't even offer even the feeblest resistance. His limbs hang off to the side uselessly, while he gasps for breath.

After bringing her heel down upon Artorias ten times, Belial hesitates. She takes a step back and scowls, the expression looking even more hideous upon her shapeshifted face.

"Barbatos saved Phoebe's life once. Out of respect for your little brother, I'll return the favor. Cross me again, and you will not be so lucky."

Artorias coughs. "Kuhuk... Ph-Phoebe? The... the Hero's wife?"

"That's right. I'm sure you remember it. Even if you don't, I couldn't care less. I'm only going to give you one warning, Artorias. **STAY OUT OF MY WAY!**"

Without another word, Belial departs. She jumps out of the six-foot-deep crater and disappears from Artorias's view, leaving him to cough out several wads of blood.

"Kuh! Kuh! Shit, I... oh, devils... she buh-... broke all my ribs..."

Suddenly, a demon flickers into existence inside the crater. Mara, the Baron of Games, dissipates a veil of invisibility surrounding her body and kneels beside Artorias to examine his condition.

"Stupid bastard," Mara mutters. "Do you have a death wish? Let Belial rampage for a while! So what if she kills a bunch of Grunts and Lords? We have trillions more lined up to die! You're lucky she spared you."

Artorias groans. "You... you were here... the whole time?"

"Of course I was. Unlike you, I'm not stupid enough to fight Belial directly. I just tried to slow her down from a distance, so as to not draw attention to myself. I stayed invisible, out of her line of sight. Like I said, you're a stupid bastard."

Mara motions with her hands. She casts a spell, summoning a magical card from her spiritual deck.

"...Wrong one. Sorry, give me a sec. I'll fix you up. I just need to draw the right card."

Artorias lays motionless, listening to the screams of other demons outside his crater. Many times, their cries of alarm instantly fall silent as Belial cuts them down.

"You..." Artorias coughs. "Did you... ever meet Satan?"

"Stop talking," Mara mutters. "It won't help your condition. And no, I didn't. He died before my broodmother whelped me."

Artorias's expression remains hidden beneath his helmet. Only a faint dimming of his glowing red eyes reveal the pain on his face.

"Belial... was the nicest... of all of us. Satan conquered. He killed. He slaughtered... but she never hated him. She always stood by his side... yet she also never partook in his violence."

Mara summons another card, then frowns. "Blast. Another dud. Thanks for the history lesson, but I'm trying to concentrate."

Artorias ignores her. "Were we wrong? We turned Belial... into a killer. Cough, cough. Somehow, all of this feels... so evil."

"Our enemies are fleshbags," Mara replies, her voice monotone. "They're inferior to demons. We will kill them, because that is our right. The weak should fear the strong."

She motions with her hands again to summon another card. When it appears, she breathes a sigh of relief. "Oh, thank the devils. Temporal Cricket, **Evoke!**"

A flash of golden light ignites in Mara's hands. It leaps upward, then falls down upon Artorias's body and envelops him in golden light.

Immediately, Artorias regenerates from all of the injuries he sustained. His ribs pop back into place, his concussion disappears, and all his lost blood flows back into his veins.

Ten seconds later, the golden light disappears, allowing Artorias to stand up. He stares at Mara incredulously.

"Your healing magic... it's incredible! That was just as potent as any spell Belial has ever cast!"

Mara rolls her eyes. "It wasn't healing magic. I merely reversed the flow of time on your body, returning it to a state from several minutes ago. It would have been useless if the injuries were more than ten or so minutes old."

"Oh," Artorias mutters. He glances toward the top of the crater and sighs. "Well, I owe you my thanks. And now, I must return."

Mara's expression falls. "Are you kidding me?! You're going to go back and fight Belial again?! You almost died! Next time, she'll kill you for sure!"

Artorias lowers his head to stare at Mara.

"Yes, that is true. However, a friend recently gave me an idea. Perhaps, instead of fighting Belial head-on, I should try slowing her down from a distance. I will travel underground and use my earth manipulation to hamper Belial's killing speed, at least until Ose, Bael, and the other reinforcements appear."

Mara sighs in relief. "Oh! Well, great, yes. Do that. Please. You're not Beelzebub; I'd hate to lose my second favorite Duke."

Artorias chuckles. "And the first is...?"

"Bael."

"Of course."

The Duke of Steel shakes his head and laughs. A moment later, he kicks the ground and sucks into the dirt, disappearing into the planet's bowels.

Mara taps her foot, then draws a card.

"Dammit. Another dud. Oh, well. The next one will be what I need, for sure! I have the heart of the cards!"
