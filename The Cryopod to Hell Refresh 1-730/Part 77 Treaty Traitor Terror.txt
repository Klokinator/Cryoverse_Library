Part 77: Treaty, Traitor, Terror

I frown in annoyance. _The heater in Neil's SUV isn't working. It's freezing cold outside._ 

Neil and I drive toward Meldora, the city on Maiura I liberated not even a week ago, and the harsh bite of a snowstorm chews through the rig's steel-clad exterior, chilling the inside of the car. Maiura's weather cycle can be described as chaotic at best, and already we're experiencing its worst days.

I rub my arm as I drive. Centurion speaks to me inside my mind. _PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR, YOUR BODILY TEMPERATURE IS FOUR DEGREES BELOW OPTIMAL LEVELS. WOULD YOU LIKE ME TO REDUCE MY THERMAL DAMPENING?_

I speak out loud. "You can do that?"

_AFFIRMATIVE._

"Don't bother. **Warm.**"

The temperature in the car increases noticeably. Neil glances at me and shivers. "Who are you talking to?"

"It's a long story, Neil. You interrogated Samantha upon her return, right?"

"Mhm."

"Well, I was wearing Phoebe's new nanosuit when Beelzebub detonated his body. I don't know exactly what happened, but the radiation must have embedded the suit's nanites into my bloodstream. I can hear Centurion in my mind."

Neil purses his lips. "I see. So the reason your shoulder felt like steel was..."

"The nanites hardened my skin. It's as if I'm always wearing the suit. It gave me a bunch of other perks too. The downside is that I had to suffer radiation poisoning to wear it."

Centurion replies. _NEGATIVE. THIS CENTURION UNIT HAS SCRUBBED ALL CANCEROUS CELLS FROM PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR'S BODY. PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR IS RUNNING AT 100% EFFICIENCY._

I blink. "Uh. Never mind."

"Never mind, what?"

"Centurion healed me. I thought I'd need Jason and the Crown to purge the radiation from my body. It's my lucky day."

Neil loosens his suit collar. "So you say. How are you holding up? I mean, you fought an Archangel. That couldn't have been easy."

"It wasn't. Uriel nearly beat me several times. I stripped her powers away with my Wordsmithing, and even then, her melee combat skills were ridiculous."

"You can do that? Remove someone's magic?"

"Apparently."

A moment passes. Neil glances out the window at the sea of snow surrounding us. The capital city of Meldora looms in the distance. It's a tiny village compared to Hero City, but still the most populated settlement on Maiura. The torches at its gates signal the watchful eye of its few remaining soldiers.

"Have you told Jason about this newfound aspect of Wordsmithing?"

"Nope. I wanted to talk to you first."

"Good. That's very good." Neil says. He eases back in his seat and smiles. "You're advancing quickly. It wasn't long ago that Jason barely held his own against the First Emperor of the demons and the Black Queen, and now you're beating an Archangel without any help."

"Technically, Samantha was there. She did a bit of damage."

"Sure, but you sent her away to face the enemy alone. That was brave of you."

I hesitate. A few thoughts rustle under the surface, making me wonder how I should respond.

"I only wanted to protect Belial. She might be an Emperor, but the matchup against Uriel's powers was uneven."

"Protecting those weaker than you is admirable as well."

"Thanks."

"Mhm."

Meldora draws ever closer. Soon it's only half a mile away. I can faintly make out the torches of men standing in the snow, peering at our vehicle.

"I let Uriel go."

"Oh? Why?"

"She showed me a vision. An image of the past. Something revolving around the demons and how they attacked Heaven. I believe her grievances were... legitimate."

Neil stares ahead stonily. "Do you think you did the right thing by walking away?"

"Kinda. I don't know. I even opened a portal to Hell Harbor to give her access to the demon homeworld. I didn't know that was even a place until she told me."

"Why didn't you go with her if you thought she was doing the right thing?"

The city gate comes into view, and Meldora's soldiers barely give us a once-over before letting us through. The poor men are freezing alive. As we drive past, I speak a word of power to warm their bones and dry their clothes. The feeling might not last long, but it's the least I can do.

I drive further into the city and slow to a crawl as I scan for people walking in the road. Given the hellish sleet falling from the sky, I'm not surprised by the empty streets as far as the eye can see.

"Like I said. Uriel's grievance seems legitimate... but it isn't mine. The demons didn't do those awful things to me."

Neil's voice softens. "But they did. By annihilating the angels and gods, the demons won the war and enslaved humanity. I think you should have helped her kill as many of the bastards as humanly possible. It's only my opinion, though."

"I don't like killing, Neil. Not unless I have to."

"So you say. Ah, right there. That building." Neil gestures toward a wooden structure covered in snow. Smoke belches from a chimney above, while light shines through the windows. "That's the place."

"Doesn't look like much. Pretty small."

"Yeah, you're not wrong. However, after all the killing those orcs did, Meldora is a bit of a ghost town. We'll have lots of privacy inside."

Neil and I hop out, and the bitter cold plunges through my veins like a hundred daggers. Wind beats my face, and snow batters our car. 

I shout over the wind. "Christ! Why couldn't you have picked a pub in a nice, warm, tropical location?!"

"Haha! Because everyone would be there!" Neil replies.

I ignore Centurion's second offer to warm my body. The ten feet to the door takes longer then I'd like, and by the time we step inside, my boots are already waterlogged.

Neil slams the door shut behind us. The pub is empty, save one man sitting at the counter, his legs propped up on the stool beside him. He looks at us as we enter. "Oh? Does ye need a drink in this storm? Be ye humans or demons, laddies?"

"Human. Very, very human," Neil says.

"Take a seat then. I'll have the missus pop over in a jiffy."

"Sure."

Neil gestures to a spot off in the corner. The warmth of the fireplace next to the booth feels amazing. As soon as we sit down, I Wordsmith for Neil and I both. "**Dry. Warm. Clean.**"

Our clothes and bodies flash-clean themselves. Neil glances at his boots, which are now in mint condition. "Damn. Not bad. I could get used to this."

"I find my magic hard to use, sometimes. Too many possibilities. The thought of all the effects my lips can unleash makes my head spin."

"Too many choices?"

"Exactly. I suffer from choice paralysis. There are so many ways I can Wordsmith that I can't wrap my mind around _what_ to say."

"You thought of taking Uriel's powers when you fought her. That was clever."

My gaze wanders to a window behind Neil. From inside the pub, the snow looks almost pleasant, like something I'd see on a Christmas card back home.

"Sure, but I feel stupid for not thinking of it sooner. Or at least, for Jason not thinking of such a thing. It would have made battling Satan and his minions much easier."

"To say the least," Neil replies. "If you remove a demon's magic, they should become weak enough that even a slightly enhanced human can fight them on equal terms. Still, you shouldn't beat yourself up over it. Divorce yourself from Jason's actions and yours. You're two different people who do things uniquely."

"I keep telling myself that."

Both of us sit in silence for a few moments. Eventually, I break the ice. "Neil. Do you remember how I told you I let Uriel go? How she showed me a vision of the past?"

Neil barely reacts. "Sure. What about it?"

"Have you ever heard of the War in Heaven?"

"I have not."

"Me neither. It seems to have been a huge event." I rub my chin thoughtfully. "The demons launched an assault on the angels and gods. Uriel was the frontline soldier. She slew tens of thousands of demons and prevented them from entering a gate to Heaven, one similar to our warp-gates in the Core."

Neil smiles. "She sounds like a brave warrior."

"From what I saw, she was. Uriel stood against the horde all by herself. However... someone attacked her from behind. They, uh, stabbed her in the back and allowed the demons to pour through."

"Who?"

I don't hesitate. "Samantha. She's the one responsible for the demons killing off the angels."

Neil stares at me in surprise. "I expected you to say many names... but now I don't know what to think. That succubus was the backstabber?"

"Yeah. She's a shape-shifter. She can mimic anyone's appearance. I never realized. How can we trust someone like that?"

Before Neil can reply, the bar owner's wife comes over and plunks down two fat mugs of beer. She smiles sweetly at Neil, but he ignores her and continues to stare at me incredulously. The lady rolls her eyes and walks away.

The commander shoves one of the beers at me. "Here. You need one of these."

"Yeah. This time, at least. Christ. How am I going to tell Jason? She stuck the knife in Uriel's back and twisted. Trusting a demon is hard enough, but a shape-shifting succubus?"

Neil sips from his mug and averts his eyes from mine. "Succubi can manipulate the emotions and feelings of the opposite sex. It's like I warned you, Hope. She's twice as dangerous now that you've told me about her past. On top of manipulating my men, she could be plotting to kill me and take over my position. Or you. Or Jason. Hell, even Phoebe."

"I don't know if I'd go that far."

Neil's eyes lower to stare at the table. "I know what you're thinking. People can change. Hell, even demons can change. Look at Paimon. Until that bastard, Lorvos, gutted him, Paimon was our guy. I wouldn't want to suspect the demon female just because of some vision from an Archangel."

"Right. That's my point. Think of how devastated Phoebe and Jason would feel if we just walked up and said, 'Yo, we think your succubus friend is plotting to take over the Core.' They'd call us conspiracy theorists."

"Mmm." Neil and I pause to take a drink. The moment the liquid hits my tongue, a gagging reflex starts to take hold. Centurion yells in my mind. _WARNING! INVOLUNTARY EXPULSION REFLEX DETECTED! LIMITING THE EFFECTS OF TOXIC SUBSTANCE K3-11248. TOXIN NEUTRALIZED! NO ILL EFFECTS INCURRED!_

I stare at the beer silently. It tastes like water now. _Thanks, Centurion._

Neil sets his glass down and smiles at my half-empty cup. "By the way, I know you were a little depressed at the wedding. It was all over your face."

I wave his concerns away. "Aw, Neil. It's no big deal. I'm happy for Jason and Phoebe. They deserve each other."

"Sure, sure. But you need to think about yourself. Did I ever introduce you to my assistant, Debra? She sometimes pops over at the Rust Bucket."

"Uh, I dunno. Maybe once or twice."

"Great! Well, she has this cute sister. Single. Why don't you let me introduce you to her sometime? I think you'll like her. Looks a bit like Amelia. Might help you get over your loss."

I release a long-held breath. "I'm fine, Neil. Thanks. I don't need a woman in my life right now."

"Shut up, you terrible liar, you." Neil laughs, shaking his head. "We ought to play poker sometime. I'd never lose to someone with such a poor poker-face."

"Don't be so sure," I counter. "I can always give myself a royal flush with Wordsmithing."

"Heh, _right_. If cheating is allowed, I'll have a look at your cards to even the playing field."

We both laugh at his little joke. Afterward, I finish off my beer in one big gulp. Centurion tells me it's nullified the alcohol content, so it appears I can't get drunk anymore. That's either good or bad, but I don't know which.

Neil sets his empty mug down. "Hope, I have to ask you... where do you think we should go from here?"

"I don't follow."

"The demons. They run everything. Holing up in the Core won't last us forever. If your suspicions about the succubus are right, then for all we know, the demons could be plotting to take us out in our sleep. We won't know what hit us."

I rub my chin thoughtfully. "If that was their intention, why wouldn't Samantha have finished us off after Beelzebub's attack?"

"I considered that very thought. But remember, the goal of the demons isn't to kill humans. They need to make an example out of us. We're supposed to be their food and their source of soul energy. If Samantha- well, if Belial decided to kill us, it wouldn't prevent more humans from eventually rising to fight again."

"Sure. But the only reason humans can combat the demons is that Jason freed the best and brightest from Bahamut. He made the weaponry we use to stand half a chance against our oppressors. If she kills him and me, then..."

Suddenly something clicks in my mind.

"Holy shit, Neil. I just realized. If Jason and I die, humanity is doomed. That's not conceit speaking. Without our Wordsmithing, humans can't fight back against the demons."

Neil waggles a finger. "Not true. We can reach a point where humans have the technological prowess to stand against our enemies without assistance. The problem is that while the demons have gained power over a hundred thousand years, we've lost it. It will take a great deal of time to restore a sense of balance between the sides."

"I guess so. I'm not trying to make myself sound ultra-important, but I feel like the demons are going to target us, the Wordsmiths, eventually. If that happens, humanity loses."

"You're not wrong. That will certainly be the case for the forseeable-"

Neil pauses. He frowns and reaches into his pocket to pull out a palm-sized square gadget. The little black box has a screen on it, and reminds me of something from Earth...

Ah. It's a pager. Err, why would Neil have one of those?

The commander glances at the small display on the front. "Son of a bitch. We finally sit down to talk, and someone messages me from camp. I guess we have to go."

"Is that a pager?"

"No clue. I asked Jason to make a device that would allow people to reach me anywhere. He created this. It uses magic to receive short messages."

"So... it's a magical pager."

"I suppose."

Sighing, I climb out of the booth. "Well, okay, then. Nothing to do about it. Let's go see what they want."

---

Henry and I lean forward and stare at the screen. A video begins to play. The quality is abysmal, with artifacts and glitches present. It's in color, but file degradation has rendered a lot of the information difficult to see.

"**Repair.**"

I speak a word, and instantly the screen clears up.

The scene appears to be a barren, rocky planet. Several massive skyscrapers loom in the distance, but their designs are unlike anything I've seen before, made mostly of glass and metal. They twist into the sky and form several ornate shapes. Very different from the tall rectangles on Earth.

A thousand demons stand, weapons and claws at the ready. Scythers, shadow-walkers, and a dozen other types all stand in a military formation and prepare to attack.

One demon I don't recognize stands in the front. He's a fellow with some facial hair and a nasty scar across his eye, and judging by his horns, he must be a Baron, possibly a Duke. He points toward a solitary hooded figure. "I've heard enough from you, paleskin. I won't allow my people to stay and cower in the Labyrinth for all eternity! I won't allow you to banish us so that you can take over the galaxy for yourselves!"

The figure's hooded face is impossible to make out. However, beneath his hood, I spot six faintly luminous eyes, arranged vertically in two rows across what must be his head. 

The hooded figure 'speaks,' but his voice seems to come from everywhere, and not from the place I'd expect his mouth to be.

[Yuia knugh sha 'iaftat, ganun. Wa ha'a a s'aasx.]

The crown translates for me.

_You know the rules, demon. We have a treaty._

The young demon takes a menacing step toward the mysterious figure. "I don't give a shit about your treaty. You lied to us! You gave us a few planets as a pittance, and kept the rest of the galaxy to yourselves! The demons and Volgrim were supposed to be allies! Equals!"

[Duat Duaftftu knugh aftuias shut? Aftuias xuia'... untiaftu'gunasuun?]

_Does Diablo know about this? About your... insubordination?_

Another demon steps out of the crowd. She wears a sultry black robe that presses against her flesh. She slides up to the leader and wraps her arm around his. "We don't care about Diablo, do we, Lempo? Besides. Diablo agreed to the treaty without consulting the rest of our people. We want to rewrite the terms!"

The hooded figure's tone maintains its neutrality. [Tha sa'nt gha'a rau'. Yuia ha'a nu tax un sha nassa'. I ghuftft ru'gu'a shut ft'aathh ur ashutht... ftias unftx untha. Laa'a.]

_The terms were fair. You have no say in the matter. I will forgive this breach of ethics... but only once. Leave._

The shrouded one turns his back on the demons and struts away. Lempo, the demon leader, grows noticeably angrier. "Hey!! I didn't say we were finished, you Volgrim filth! If you walk away, I'll consider this a declaration of WAR!!"

The figure pauses. He turns around and raises a hand. His skin is grey and mottled, and the six fingers tell me it doesn't belong to a human.

"Yuia ha'a nu aiashu'usx su gathfta'a gha'. Ir xuia a'a ft'a'a anuiagh su unthia' sha hutsuftusx ur nx laulfta, gha ghuftft 'aun ru'a ialun xuia."

_You have no authority to declare war. If you are brave enough to incur the hostility of my people, we will rain fire upon you._

"So that's how it is, then?"

Lempo begins to weave signs with his hand. Before he can finish, a voice yells from the demon force's flank. "Lempo! Stop! What are you doing?"

I recognize the voice as Diablo's. The Emperor of Annihilation strides up, his black and red robes hanging off his body and trailing behind him all the while. Lempo ceases his incantation and quickly bows. "Emperor Diablo! I apologize. I did not mean for you to-"

"To get involved? To learn of your treachery?!" Diablo grabs Lempo by the shirt and slams him into the ground. "You little shit! How dare you stand with claws at the ready against our allies! I would kill you on the spot if it weren't for your rank!"

The younger demon growls. "I'm... I'm only doing what you refuse to! Stupid old man! The Volgrim are taking us for a ride! They scarcely give us a pittance while they take the lion's share of the galaxy! It isn't fair!"

Diablo freezes. He glances at the Volgrim standing thirty feet away. "I apologize for my subordinate's behavior. He doesn't know his place."

The Volgrim slowly places his hands together. [I an nus tu tha'saun. It shut sha ulunuun anung xuia' laulfta? Thas gha ha'a ftaan... ianrau'?]

_I am not so certain. Is this the opinion among your people? That we have been... unfair?_

"Of course not. The humans have worlds to live on. We have the ever-expanding Labyrinth. We live comfortably."

"Lies!" Lempo struggles in Diablo's grasp. "We live like rats in the darkness, while the Volgrim live in towers of steel and glass! We helped them defeat the fleshbags! We should reap the rewar-"

_WHAM._

Diablo slaps the youth's face so hard the ground shakes. He instantly goes limp in Diablo's grip and offers no more resistance.

The Volgrim, however, doesn't miss the exchange. [I taa. Su shut ut sha ulunuun ur nanx un xuia' tuthuasx.]

_I see. So this is the opinion of many in your society._

Diablo stands up straight. "No. A vocal minority. I'll take care of them. I'm... I'm sorry for the inconvenience."

The Volgrim bows his head. [An aluftugx utn's anuiagh. Thut ut sha tathung g'uial su thugh ial un sha ftats ru'a xaa't. Ir anusha' allaa't, sha Fuianga't ghuftft saka g'aas urranta.]

_An apology isn't enough. This is the second group to show up in the last five years. If another appears, the Founders will take great offense._

A moment later, the female demon who grabbed Lempo earlier clutches her throat. All the demons behind her do as well. Every single one of the two-thousand demons, including Lempo, begin to wheeze and gasp, as though something was choking them with two thousand invisible hands.

Then... their heads explode.

The sounds of two-thousand fleshy balloons detonating makes my stomach churn. Henry grabs his mouth beside me and recoils from the screen in horror.

Their bodies flop to the ground. Only Diablo remains.

The Third Emperor stares silently at the Volgrim. "Damn you. I could have stopped them. You didn't have to use force."

[Anung nx laulfta, guttans ut lianuthag ftx gaash. Onftx shuta ghhu ag'aa ghush sha Fuianga't a'a aftftughag su ftu'a. Ir xuia ghuth su taa ragha' ur xuia' ruftsh gua ftuka shata sugax, shan xuia thuiaftg thuna ial ghush a tuftiasuun.]

_Among my people, dissent is punished by death. Only those who agree with the Founders are allowed to live. If you wish to see fewer of your filth die like these today, then you should come up with a solution._

A bead of sweat rolls down Diablo's face. "Of... of course. I apologize for the trouble, Executor Nufaris. It won't happen again. I promise."

...

The video cuts off.

Henry stares in disgust at the screen. "What the hell was that? I've never seen two thousand heads explode at the same time."

"Can't say I have either. Christ. Whatever these Volgrim are, they're dangerous. Even Diablo looked spooked."

The young man wipes his face with his sleeve. "What was that one guy saying? His words made my head hurt."

"Nothing important. He threatened the demons and said some other stuff. More importantly, one of my biggest questions was answered today."

"Yeah? What's that?"

I tap my lip and turn to look at each of the warp-gates in the Core. "We've scoured the database of known planets. Every single one had humans living on it. If humans are the cattle for demons, then why do demons live inside the Labyrinth? Why don't they take the lush worlds and relegate humans to the dark corridors of Hell?"

"I don't know. Why not?"

"Because of some ancient treaty. The demons must have agreed to something along with these 'Volgrim.' That's the obvious answer. I feel like I'm missing puzzle pieces, though."

Behind me, the screen with the Volgrim video makes a loud _BEEP._

I turn to look at it. A message appears onscreen.

UNAIASHU'UCTHAG ATHTHATT GASATHSAG. INRU'NUNG P'ATUGANS OTA.

The Crown translates for me.

UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS DETECTED. INFORMING PRESIDENT OSE.

I blink in surprise. "Err, it says it's contacting... 'President Ose'. Who's that?"

Henry looks at me worriedly. "I don't know. Never heard of him. Maybe we should turn this off. It sounds ominous."

I reach over to turn the computer terminal off. "Yeah. You're probably right. I need to speak to Samantha anyway. Maybe she can tell me what-"

The moment before my finger makes contact with the screen; something leaps out of the monitor and flies into my eyeball. There's no pain for a moment, but a split-second later, I feel a burning, festering agony inside my brain.

"Ahh!! Shit! Something... in my... head!!"

A blur of movement. The floor rushes at me.

"Mister Hiro?! Holy sh-"

Henry's voice fades away. A woman's voice replaces his.

"Ah. I see. So you're the one who opened the forbidden files. I should have known."
