Part 209: Olive Branch

As the Monster King and his Baron-level sidekick slow to a stop, Mara's eyes immediately snap to the newcomer. Her gaze hardens as she scrutinizes him from head to toe.

The young Baron wears a simple black cloak, one mirroring Mara's. A hood rests over his face, concealing his thin and wispy blond hair, further echoing Mara's appearance. Were one to see the two of them side by side, they might believe the newcomer was a member of Mara's family.

Mara purses her lips. _Hm. Now here's a Baron I've never seen before, and I've met them all._

"You there," Mara says, gesturing to the handsome devil standing across from her. "Who are you? What is your name and title? I know every Baron in the Labyrinth, so don't even try to lie to me."

The young Baron smooths his hair back. He slowly blinks at Mara, almost as if seducing her.

"Now, now. There's no need to become hostile, Mara, Baron of Games. I'm just a simple Baron, newly risen thanks to His Eminence's magic. The grand and magnificent Kar, King of all Monsters, has blessed me with a small portion of his might, allowing me to ascend from a lowly Lord to the prestigious rank of Baron."

Mara's gaze hardens even further. She glances at Kar while raising an eyebrow. "Is this true, Monster King? Did you raise a Lord to the rank of Baron with your magic?"

Kar crosses his arms. "Hurgh. I did. Why? Did I break one of your laws?"

"No," Mara replies. She glances at Ying-Ying, noting the stars in her sister's eyes. "It's not that. There are no rules stating monsters and demons cannot trade mana. However... I have always been under the impression that monster and demon mana was incompatible."

A flicker of movement flies off Kar's shoulder. Only now does Mara notice the tiny little fairy woman perched atop the Crocodile's arm. Blinker flies over and waves at Mara cheerfully. "Hi, hi! That's where you're wrong, silly! Anything is possible with fairy magic!"

"Fairy magic? Who are you?" Mara asks, her confusion apparent.

As Blinker speaks, she makes a hand flapping motion at the Baron of Games, irritating Mara further. "Don't you know? I'm the Monster Queen, wife of the Last Crocodile! My name is Blinker, and don't you forget it, sister!"

"And my name is Smithy, the Baron of Prediction," the young Demon Baron beside Kar says. He bows slightly, showing deference to his senior. "I am but a humble servant of the great and mighty Kar, heir of the Sphinx's power. I have sworn a vow to serve him until the day I die."

Before Mara can reply, her sister steps forward and jerks out her hand. "Nice to meet you, Mister Smithy! I'm Ying-Ying! Are you single? I'm single! I like to blow bubbles and dance under the sun! How about you? What sorts of hobbies are you into?!"

Ying-Ying babbles wildly, her mouth racing at a hundred miles an hour. However, Smithy merely smiles politely, showing little reaction to her eager and over-the-top greeting. "Haha, how wonderful. I am with mate, as it happens. She has expressed openness to other relationships under certain conditions, but we mustn't rush. You and I barely know each other, and I only exist to serve my Master."

"Psh, no kidding! I should've guessed you had a mate! I mean, just look at you! I bet you beat the ladies off with a stick! I have the same problem, given my stunning good looks! The males line up all day trying to pound down my door, but like I always say, I must wait for the perfect guy!"

The more Ying-Ying talks, the more Mara's eyes begin to twitch. _What nonsense are you blabbering about now, sister?! You fornicate with a new boy-toy every week! Don't spit all that garbage about chastity and- bah! Never mind. Let her keep that Baron occupied. I've other business to worry about._

Mara casts aside further thoughts of the Baron of Prediction, opting to turn her sights on the Monster King. "Forgive my rudeness, as well as my sister's, Monster King-"

"It's just Kar," Kar says. "Hurgh. There is no need to be so formal."

Mara shakes her head. "I disagree. Given how you've followed my sister and me through the Labyrinth for the last few weeks, I must presume you intend to enter Hell Harbor. Doing so requires specific authorizations for one who has never traveled there before. Isn't that the long and short of it?"

Kar glances at his wife as she continues to flutter around Mara, peering at the blonde-haired demoness's long, flowing locks of hair, only a few of which spill out from her hooded face. "Hurgh. You have guessed well. My slave- I mean, my _assistant_ and I, must travel to meet your current reigning Emperor, Ose. I come on behalf of the Hero, who sent me to discuss a variety of topics with your leader."

Mara's scowl fades somewhat. "The Hero. I sensed his foul magic trailing after you for the past several days. Why hasn't he come along? Why send you and this little upstart Baron over here?"

Kar spreads out his arms while baring a toothy grin. "Hurgh. Every time we drew near, you fled, along with the Keymaster. The Hero is a kind person, someone who does not harbor any ill intent for your people. He decided it would be best if he allowed my assistant and me to go on by ourselves. We don't want to startle you, after all."

Mara taps on her lips thoughtfully. She meets Kar's gaze for a moment before narrowing her eyes to slits.

"Jason Hiro is the current leader of humanity. His ability is called Wordsmithing, is it not?"

Kar nods. "Hurgh. You have heard correctly."

"Wordsmithing. Hmm."

Slowly, Mara's eyes travel to Kar's right, where Ying-Ying continues yapping the ear off her newfound Baron crush.

"-pink! It's such a great color, so full of vibrancy and love! Of course, I like red more, because demons are red, and so is blood! Nothing like a delicious Blood Soup to wake me up in the morning! Don't you agree?"

Smithy winces. "...Yes. I suppose some... some Blood Soup can hit the spot on occasion... haha..."

Gears turn in Mara's head as she stares at the distracted Baron of Prediction.

_Wordsmithing, eh? And this demon's name is Smithy. Hehe. Do I look like I was born yesterday?_

Mara's eyes bolt open. She snarls at the young Baron, ignoring the Monster King. "Smithy! Who do you think you're fooling? Do you honestly expect me to believe you're some no-name demon granted the rank of Baron by a monster?! Preposterous!"

Blinker, hovering by Mara's right side, stares with wide-eyes at the Baron of Games. "W-what do you mean? Smithy didn't lie!"

Ying-Ying turns to scowl at Mara. "Jeez, sis! What are you on about this time? Can't you see I'm trying to make some moves over here? Stop throwing off my mojo!"

"No. I won't," Mara says, her voice deadly calm. "I see now that this Smithy is a pretender. He's no servant of the Monster King."

Smithy gently pushes aside Ying-Ying. "I beg your pardon? Would you mind explaining?"

"It's simple," Mara says, the corner of her lips curling into a tight smile. "The Hero wields a power known as Wordsmithing, and you call yourself Smithy. I've never heard such a ridiculous name for a Baron in all my life! What else are you if not a liar?"

Mara swivels her head toward Kar. "You did not boost this demon with your magic. The Hero did! He used his Wordsmithing to turn some Lord into a Baron! You intend to take this 'Smithy' behind our lines and gather intel for your human leader! Admit it!"

As Mara speaks, the Baron of Prediction glances at Kar, and Kar back at him. Both men share a knowing look, as if speaking to each other with their eyes.

Kar rests his hands on his hips. "Hurgh. You've guessed well, Baron of Games. I should have expected as much from the leader of the Tarot Sect."

"Yes, very well done," Smithy replies, his voice low. "But you're wrong about two things. I'm not a spy, and my power _does_ come from the Monster King."

Mara gnashes her teeth. "Bah! You cannot expect us to grant you entry to Hell Harbor while spouting off such feeble lies!"

"I am no liar," Smithy says, gently placing his hand on his chest. "Jason Hiro merely helped alter the mana from His Eminence's aperture into mine, then converted it to something my demonic body could handle. You might not know this, but the Hero's mana reserves are quite low. He does not possess nearly enough energy to compete with the average Demon Lord, let alone Barons, Dukes, and Emperors. Only someone with deep reserves, such as King Kar, could grant me my current title."

Kar nods sagely. He closes his eyes and sighs. "Hurgh. I can grant a Lord the power of a Baron, but I cannot grant a Baron the power of a Duke. To attempt such a feat would drain me of all my mana. I might perish if I were to even try."

Mara's steadfast conviction falters. She chews her lower lip for a moment while glancing between the Baron and Crocodile. "Is... is that so? Hm. Your words make some modicum of sense, but how am I to know if you are telling the truth? I cannot allow a demon tainted by Holy Magic to enter my people's capitol."

While Mara voices her concerns, Ying-Ying slithers over to her sister's side and whispers something in her ear.

Mara nods.

"Hm. Yes, that is a fair point. A conductive test would expose any lingering heroic energy contained within Smithy's body."

Smithy raises his eyebrows. "Hm? What's this about a test? Do you need to take my temperature?"

Mara chuckles. "Haha. No, don't be absurd. Unless I determine you and the Monster King lack all traces of Holy Energy, I won't allow you to enter Hell Harbor. It's as simple as that. If you're still dead-set on arranging an audience with Emperor Ose, you'll have to submit to a few magical tests to clear your names."

"Now, hold on just a minute!" Blinker yells, as she flies over and hovers next to ara's face. "Who are you to treat us like criminals?! We're famous people, you know! Way more than you! Kar could snap you like a twig!"

Mara rolls her eyes. "If the Monster King attacks my sister or me, he'll be lucky to make it back to the Core in one piece, let alone travel to Hell Harbor. You need my sister to open the gate, and you need a high-ranking demon, such as myself, to authorize your travel. Piss off one of us, and you can kiss your audience with our Emperor _buh-bye._"

The Baron of Games exposes a sinister smirk. She spreads her arms and leans forward. "Attack me if you dare. I may not have enough strength to defeat you, but you'll find my powers more than adequate for ensuring my survival while punishing your impudence."

Kar holds up one of his claws. "Hurgh. There is no need for us to resort to violence. My sla- err, _assistant_, will submit to whatever tests you like, as will I. However, don't break his arms. If he can't deliver my coffee in the morning, I'll be awfully sad."

Smithy's right eye twitches. "Truly, my master's benevolence knows no limits."

Mara nods. "This won't take long. Smithy, I want you to start by demonstrating your powers for me."

Smithy sighs. "Fine, but only because Master Kar ordered me to do so."

Smithy closes his eyes for a moment, then holds his palm out, upright.

_Foop!_

A ballpoint pen appears in midair mere inches above the Baron of Prediction's hand. It lands in his palm without making a sound.

"If you wish to see my powers in action, I'll need this."

Mara crosses her arms. "Oh? Is that some sort of magical implement?"

Smithy shakes his head. "It's a pen."

Ying-Ying raises her hand. "Ooh! Ooh! It's a magical pen though, right?"

"No," Smithy sighs. "It's _just_ a pen. An ordinary one."

Mara cocks her head slightly. "Alright. You have piqued my curiosity. Continue your exhibition."

The Baron of Prediction nods. He turns to Ying-Ying and smiles. "If you don't mind, I'd like your assistance to verify my powers."

"Huh? Me?" Ying-Ying asks, confusion trickling into her voice. "Why do you need my help?"

Instead of replying, Smithy holds out his palm again. This time, a five-inch notepad pops into existence above his hand and lands in his palm. He stays silent and begins furiously scribbling a series of sentences in the notepad. After five minutes, he nods. "That will do. Miss Ying-Ying, if you would be so kind, please take this notepad and give it a read. Don't tell your sister about its contents; just keep them to yourself for now."

Ying-Ying's confusion doubles as she nods, takes the notepad, and flicks her eyes across its contents. "Huh...? But... how are you going to get Mara-"

"Now, now," Smithy says. He presses a finger against Ying-Ying's lips, shushing her mid-sentence. His touch causes Ying-Ying to blush and stammer, but she quickly falls silent.

After a few moments, Smithy turns to Mara.

"Baron of Games. Let's make this a little more interesting. You tell me about your powers, and I'll tell you about mine."

Mara frowns. "That wasn't part of our agreement."

"I know, but why not humor me? Come, now, we're both Barons. There's no need to be embarrassed if your abilities don't match up to mine."

Mara immediately uncrosses her arms. She takes a step toward Smithy, fury in her eyes. "What was that? Do you honestly think you can say something so insulting and get away with it?!"

"Whoa, now," Smithy says, holding up both hands in a feigned surrender. "It seems I hit a little too close to home. How about this: Why not tell me something personal about yourself? For example, how many moles do you have on your back?"

Mara's eyes grow as wide as saucers. She stares at the Baron of Prediction with open-mouthed outrage. "H-h-how many m-moles I have...?! What kind of absurd-"

"Seven, correct?" Smithy asks.

The Baron of Games averts her eyes. Her cheeks flush deep-red as waves of embarrassment wash over her. "No!! How would you even know something like that? Have you been spying on me?!"

Smithy sighs. "Of course not. Haven't you tried to hazard any guesses about my powers? Are my abilities not given away by my title? Take a look at your sister's face. She'll confirm their validity for you."

In between flashes of rage and humiliation, Mara glances at Ying-Ying, only to see that her sister has even wider eyes than herself!

"It's all on here!" Ying-Ying says, as she scans Smithy's writing. "Every word you and Smithy spoke- he put it all in this notepad!"

"What? How?" Mara asks. Without hesitation, she walks over and snatches the notepad from her sister's grasp. "That's impossible. Not even the best Oracles could do such a-"

Mara stares at the notepad for several long, drawn-out seconds. Her pupils slowly dilate as waves of adrenaline surge through her body.

_I will turn to Mara and say: "Baron of Games. Let's make this a little more interesting. You tell me about your powers, and I'll tell you about mine."_

_She will reply: "That wasn't part of our agreement."_

_Then, I will say: "I know, but why not humor me? Come, now, we're both Barons. There's no need to be embarrassed if your abilities don't match up to mine."_

A dozen or so sentences detail Mara and Smithy's entire conversation. The more she reads, the more her heart palpitates.

_Mara will start to get frustrated. She will say: "What? How?" And then she will take the notebook from you. Immediately following, she will say: "That's impossible. Not even the best Oracles could do such a-" and fall silent mid-sentence. By then, she will have begun reading this notepad._

The notebook ends on that line.

Sweat drips down Mara's face as she stares at the impossibly precise prediction, with thousands of thoughts running through her head.

_Seven Hells! I thought this Baron of Prediction might have a few interesting abilities, but to be able to gaze into the future is not something I ever thought possible! Not even the Oracles can peer behind the curtain of time!_

Mara swallows her saliva to taste the dryness of her mouth.

"You... you predict the future. That's why your title is the Baron of Prediction. Smithy, how did you manage to evolve such a powerful ability?!"

Mara raises her eyes from the notepad, only to see Smithy awkwardly shuffling his feet.

"W-well, ahem, I don't actually see the future, Mara. That's not how my magic works."

"What nonsense are you speaking?" Mara asks, her jumbled thoughts becoming even more chaotic every second. "What is your power, if not the gift of divination?!"

"I do predict _things_," Smithy says, waggling a finger. "But my power revolves around deductions and logic, not foresight in the sense you imagine. After speaking to you for several minutes, I gathered information about your personality and mannerisms, and then I deduced how you would respond to anything I asked."

Smithy sighs. "For me, deductions are as easy as opening and closing my eyes. If you and I were to fight, you would find out how terrifying my ability can be."

Mara slowly drops her arms to her sides. She nods along to Smithy's words, not entirely understanding his meaning. "But... even if you predicted my responses, how could you guess the number of moles on my back? I don't even know how many I have!"

Smithy smiles. "I don't know either. I simply guessed the number seven because it sounded good. I knew you would deny any number I invented, so it didn't matter which one I picked. You not knowing the number either was a lucky bonus!"

"Wow! Wowee!!" Ying-Ying yells. "Ohmygosh, you're so cool! Your power is amazing! Can you predict things about me, too?!"

Smithy shrugs. "Sure."

"Great! What's my favorite color?"

Smithy raises his eyebrow. "...Red."

"Wow!! You're amazing! You didn't even bat an eye! Wait... wait a minute! You cheated! I told you my favorite color already!!"

"Ying-Ying, please," Mara mutters, exasperation in her voice. "Now isn't the time for this conversation. I need to continue my test."

Ying-Ying groans. "But whyyy? Smithy already passed! What more do you want from him?"

"No, he didn't," Mara says. She regains her bearings and takes a few steps back, keeping a lingering eye on the Baron of Prediction. "My test was supposed to detect the presence of holy energy in his body. However, Smithy's predictive ability doesn't release any mana whatsoever. I have yet to confirm his demonic purity."

Smithy claps his hands. "Oh, right! Well, if all you wanted was to sense my demonic energy, you should have said so! I can bring it out for you right now."

Without waiting for Mara's reply, Smithy extends his hand toward the notepad still clutched in Mara's palm. Despite the ten-foot gap separating both Baron's, Mara's heart nearly jumps out of her chest when the notepad vanishes from her grasp and reappears in Smithy's, all without him physically taking it from her.

"Yoink, haha!" Smithy laughs. "See? How about that? Did you sense my mana?"

Once again, a dazed expression appears on Mara's face. She shakes her head slowly, unable to process the notepad's disappearance. "Ah... yes. But what power did you use just now?"

"I call it the 'Sneaky Yoink'!" Smithy says. "I can move things to and from my hands. Pretty neat, right?"

Mara glances at her sister, then back to Smithy. "Isn't that teleportation? Like what the Warpers use?"

Smithy shrugs. "Probably?"

"You're quite the jack-of-all-trades," Mara murmurs. "Any other abilities?"

"A few," Smithy says. "But a magician never reveals his secrets. I've held up my end of the bargain. Go ahead and test my Master now."

Mara starts to protest. "But, I still have questions-"

"And we're still on a tight timetable," Smithy replies, dismissing her concerns. "After we speak to Ose, I'd be open to goofing around later. Right now, a hammer hangs in the air between the humans and demons. Should it fall, millions, billions, perhaps even trillions will die. Your curiosity comes second to saving lives."

Crestfallen, Mara merely nods and sighs. Her shoulders slump as she turns to face Kar and Blinker.

"Uhm, well, Smithy isn't wrong. Would both of you kindly demonstrate your magic for me? We might as well get this over with."

Kar scowls at the Baron of Games. "Hurgh! If I didn't know any better, I might think my presence _bored_ you! Do you not understand who I am? I am the mightiest monster in the Labyrinth! How many Duke-level monsters have you ever met, little Peaches?"

"Not many," Mara admits. "Perhaps three. Bahamut was among them, but her physical strength surely paled in comparison to yours. Were it not for her adept enslavement of other beings; she would not have held much clout among Hell's denizens."

"Exactly," Kar replies, as his scowl shifts to a braggadocious grin. "I'm the top gator around here. You'd better open your eyes and pay attention, 'cause I'm about to put on a show like nothin' you've seen before!"

Blinker lands on Kar's shoulder and crosses her arms. "Sweetie, please don't embarrass me like you did last time."

"I'd never do such a thing," Kar replies.

"Yeah. You _would._"

Blinker sulks in silence while Kar stretches his arms. The giant crocodile ignores his wife's concerns as he begins weaving a series of magical signs.

Suddenly, he vanishes.

Kar launches himself toward the far wall at sub-sonic speeds. His instant acceleration startles the monsters in the room, who have all but ignored the 'demon business' happening in the far corner. Many of them jerk their heads to the side as a deafening explosion blasts throughout the small, makeshift arena, the entire sector shudders, and a shockwave flings everyone off their feet. Even Mara and her sister stumble and trip, landing on their asses with looks of astonishment on their faces.

Kar follows-up by leaping into the air and rapid-punching toward the floor. Each swing of his arms sends mana rushing forward, which erupts in dazzling explosions of dirt and rock. The Labyrinth's firm ground, made from magically compacted soil, crumbles under his relentless power.

_Boom! Boom!_

Hundreds, even thousands of pounds of the Labyrinth's foundation, go flying in every direction, burying the monsters and demons present under an avalanche of debris. Some monsters take cover to protect themselves from Kar's hailstorm, but many do not.

After a full minute of ceaselessly hovering in the sky and punching the air, Kar finishes his demonstration and abruptly stops. The cacaphony of thunder disappears as quickly as it began, and Kar falls to the ripped-up floor below, landing on his feet with a hard _thud._

The room falls silent. Several hundred monsters begin clawing their way out of Kar's landslide, but many others need assistance from their friends to escape.

The Monster King grins like an idiot as he surveys the chaos surrounding him. He rests one claw on his hip while turning to the Baron of Games. "Hurgh. How was that?"

Mara shakily pulls herself to her feet. She helps her sister up as well, and both of them dust off their clothes. "You... you didn't have to unleash such raw, destructive power! Look at the mess you've created!"

Kar shrugs. "That's why you have goblins; to clean up in my wake."

Mara starts to retort, only to glance to her left, where Smithy stands, a circle of perfection surrounding him. Unlike the rest of the room, which was swallowed up by Kar's destruction, the Baron of Prediction stands in the same spot as before, his immediate surroundings untouched. He leans against the wall with a bored expression on his face, as if he hadn't just witnessed an incredible display of Duke-level strength.

"You!" Mara balks. "You didn't get knocked around by the Monster King's attacks?"

"Nope."

"...Why not?"

Smithy sticks his pinky in his ear. "I've seen more impressive feats. If you think Kar's strength is wild, you should see what the Hero can do. It would blow your mind."

Ying-Ying walks over and pokes Smithy's arm. "Wow. You aren't faking confidence or anything! Did you summon a barrier to protect yourself?"

"Something like that," Smithy replies, keeping his answer neutral. "Anyway, did we pass? Are we cleared to enter Hell Harbor?"

Mara stares at the Baron of Prediction for several moments. Her laser-like gaze threatens to bore a hole in the side of Smithy's head, but he doesn't react at all.

Finally, Mara sighs. "Whatever. I've done my part. Unless the Monster Queen is hiding holy energy in her tiny little body, you three can enter Hell Harbor. _Ugh._ Cleaning up this mess is going to cost me dearly."

Smithy cocks his head slightly. "In what way?"

"You don't know?" Mara asks. "I oversaw today's tests, and failed to provide a suitable chamber for testing the Monster King. As such, I'll need to pay for the Labyrinth's repairs with Soul tokens. How long have you lived among fleshbags to forget such basic information?"

"Oh, Soul Tokens," Smithy says, nodding. "I misunderstood your meaning. I was only a Lord until recently, so I didn't have much use for, uh, those."

Ying-Ying glances at Mara in confusion. "What does he mean, sister? I thought all demons needed Soul Tokens to pay for things. Isn't that how we earn the right to reach higher ranks?"

Mara frowns. "It is. Smithy, how could you become a Lord without using Soul Tokens? All demons need them. You need one thousand souls to become a Lord in the first place, so how could you not know of their existence?"

Smithy's expression twists noticeably. For the first time since his arrival, he appears at a loss for words. He pulls away from the wall and starts rubbing his chin.

"Ah, well, you see... well, it's like this..."

The Baron of Prediction scrunches up his forehead and begins to sweat profusely. As he does, Mara's suspicions deepen.

"Smithy? You're starting to worry me. Answer my question. All demons need Soul Tokens, so how could you not know of their existence?"

Before Smithy can answer, Blinker flies over and interrupts him. "Oh, geez! It's fine; you can tell the lady! There's no reason to hide your powers."

Smithy blinks twice. A look of surprise appears on his face. "Huh? Tell her what? I don't follow."

Blinker lands on Smithy's shoulder and whispers something in his ear. As she does, a look of relief passes over the Baron's face.

"Oh. Oh! Right, yes. How could I forget? Haha. I'm such an airhead sometimes."

The young Baron blushes in embarrassment as he returns his attention to Mara. "Right, so, I never needed Soul Tokens in the first place. I can, uh, take human souls without using the River Styx."

Mara's frown intensifies. "Oh? You're just one big surprise after another, aren't you? How can you obtain souls on your own? Tell me."

Smithy shrugs. "Well, have you heard of Soulfire?"

Mara nearly jumps out of her skin. "Soulfire?! How could you possibly possess such a unique ability, in addition to all the others you've demonstrated today?! Few demons know this, but the fact Beelzebub uses it was considered a high-level secret among the upper ranks! For you to wield it as well is simply inconceivable!"

Smithy nods. "Yes. You're right. I don't possess Soulfire."

Mara's heartrate quickly normalizes as she breathes a sigh of relief. "You don't? Devils. Don't scare me like that. Forget entering Hell Harbor; I'd have to report you to Emperor Ose post-haste if you-"

"I have a similar ability, though," Smithy says, interrupting Mara. "I can take the souls of other beings under, erm, specific conditions."

Mara swallows her words. A flash of anger washes over her face. "Damn you! Stop toying with me! This is no time for jokes, Smithy! Bypassing the River of Souls is a high crime among our people! If you don't explain yourself right this instant..."

Smithy glances at Blinker as she sits on his shoulder. A look of annoyance passes over his face. "A high crime, you say?"

"Yes," Mara replies. "The foundation of demon society rests on doling out a limited number of souls to the most deserving and promising talents. We can't allow demons to reap souls on their own, lest they kill too many fleshbags and stifle the flow of new arrivals into the River Styx. The only reason Beelzebub got away with using Soulfire was because of his status as Agares' pupil, plus a healthy dose of nepotism. For a young, unknown figure like yourself, the punishment will be quite severe."

As Mara speaks, Ying-Ying wrings her hands while looking both confused and disoriented. "Uwah! Smithy, darling! You can't be a criminal, right? Tell me it's not true!"

Exasperated, Smithy shrugs his shoulders. "I didn't, uh, realize taking souls without permission was a crime. Damn. This complicates things. That's what I get for running my mouth."

Blinker facepalms. "It's my fault. I should have remembered before telling you."

"No, don't blame yourself. You're a monster, not a demon. You probably aren't aware of the intricacies of their society."

Mara stares at Smithy as he converses with the fairy on his shoulder. As he speaks, a soft tingling appears in the back of her mind.

_Before Kar and Smithy came here, I placed three Tarot Cards in anticipation of their arrival. The cards warned me of a great danger, a terrible lie, and an extension of good faith._

_However, what lie, and what danger did they refer to?_

_So far, while he has been somewhat evasive, Smithy's fibs have been little more than little white lies. Hardly worth mentioning. Kar's demonstration was only a bit dangerous, nothing that would have endangered my life._

_What crucial detail am I missing? Why can't I shake the feeling that I shouldn't trust these three with the keys to Hell Harbor?_

As Mara thinks, her sister, Ying-Ying, pipes up. "Smithy? What do you mean by 'their society?' Don't you mean 'our society?' You're a demon too, silly!"

Smithy pauses his conversation with Blinker. A strange, distant look appears on his face, as if he's just realized a terrible mistake. "Oh. Hm, yeah. You're right. A slip of the tongue on my part."

Mara's heart skips a beat.

_How could Smithy make such a silly mistake? What self-respecting demon would refer to his people in the third-person?_

_Unless..._

_Wait. A crucial detail._

_Have I overlooked something, after all?_

Puzzle pieces click into place in Mara's mind.

_Spawn of a broodmother. I have. How could I be so stupid? Jason Hiro is a Wordsmith. He can make anything happen by speaking a single word. Why would he boost a demon, even someone he considered an ally, to take his place, when he could change his appearance via Wordsmithing instead?_

_Devils! Have I uncovered his scheme?! The damned Wordsmith didn't leave! He must have disguised himself as a demon! It's no wonder this 'Smithy' doesn't know about Soul Tokens! It's no wonder he refers to demons as if he isn't one of them, because he's not!_

_Smithy is Jason Hiro, the Wordsmith, in disguise! My sister and I are in great danger after all!! The filthy bastard has been lying to us the whole time! He's-_

As Mara finishes assembling the puzzle and takes a step back to re-evaluate it, Smithy suddenly turns to look at her.

A cold, icy dread washes over the demoness.

Inside her mind, a second voice appears.

[Damn. You figured it out.]

_Smithy? No! Jason Hiro?!_

[That's right.]

_You're reading my thoughts! You're a devil-damned telepath!_

Mara opens her mouth to warn Ying-Ying. However, before she can utter a single syllable, Smithy speaks.

"**Silence. Suppress. Bind.**"

In an instant, all of Mara's instincts fail her. Before she has a chance to defend herself, an invisible clamp grabs her throat and squeezes it, preventing her from speaking. Her body seizes up, preventing her from moving, while chains formed from mana grab her arms and bind them behind her. Unable to perform magical signs, the Baron stands motionless, unable to speak.

Jason Hiro, the Wordsmith posed as a Demon Baron, repeats the same three words, but aimed at Ying-Ying. She, too, turns as stiff as a statue, powerless to resist his magic.

Both women stare at the Demon Baron, wide-eyed with fear. Unable to speak, they can't do anything but look at each other and back at the Wordsmith.

Jason Hiro maintains his demonic appearance. He glances behind the girls at the still-disoriented monsters pulling themselves from the rubble on the opposite side of the room.

"**Mirage. Distraction.** There. Now nobody will bother us."

Mara's heartrate triples. It bounces around in her chest like an angry gorilla, trying desperately to force her body to move. However, no matter how she tries, Mara can't escape the Wordsmith's imprisonment.

_Bastard! You... you disgusting, wretched fleshbag! I should have known! You won't get away with this! When the Council hears of our deaths, all your claims of coming in peace will fall apart!!_

Jason cocks his head as a look of confusion passes over his face. "Huh? No, no. I'm not going to kill or hurt you. I'm only temporarily restraining you, nothing else. I wouldn't go this far under normal circumstances, but if I'd let you go on any longer, you'd have run away or brought the whole Labyrinth down on me."

The Wordsmith walks closer to Mara and smiles. However, contrary to her expectations, his expression is anything but smug. His smile radiates warmth, making her doubt, even if only for a moment, the idea that he is her enemy.

"I didn't lie about my desire to seek peace, Mara. You and I are currently enemies, but I had no say in that. Maybe I'm just a stupid idealist, but I believe peace between our people is possible. However, I know you don't believe me, and possibly never will. Once I get the location of Hell Harbor and erase your knowledge of my identity from your memories, I'll let you go."

Mara's eye twitches. _Liar. If you could forcefully take Hell Harbor's location from me, you would have done so sooner._

"I wanted you to take me there of your own volition," Jason says. "Forcefully taking the information is a backup option, one I regret having to use. Look, I know you don't like me... but... well, maybe just give me a chance, alright? You seem like a good person. We could have been friends in another life. Hell, maybe we still can."

With a sigh, Jason turns to face Ying-Ying. "Um, for the record... I'm married. You seem like a nice girl. Keep that positive attitude going, and I'm sure you'll find a nice guy, someday."

Mara glances at her sister. To her surprise, Ying-Ying seems far less alarmed than she should be. She stares at Jason Hiro with a look of acceptance.

"Yeah," Jason says to Ying-Ying, as if answering an unspoken question. "I'm the Hero, disguised as a demon. Sorry for lying to you. You didn't do anything wrong. Don't blame yourself. If anything, it's Mara's fault. She was clever enough to figure out my identity. I've gotta give her props for that."

With a shrug, Jason speaks two words of power.

"**Extract. Erase.**"

Ying-Ying's eyes flutter for a moment, before eventually closing as she falls into a deep sleep.

Jason turns to face Mara. "Don't worry. Your sister will be fine, and so will you. If you happen to regain your memories, just know that I didn't come here to hurt anyone."

Mara's stomach clenches as tears begin to bubble in her eyes. _I don't believe you. You're... you're a damned, filthy fleshbag._

"Hey, we fleshbags aren't that bad," Jason says, laughing wryly. "If I manage to put an end to this stupid war, you should pop by the Core sometime. My wife makes dumplings I'd die to protect."

After a moment of silence, Jason closes his eyes.

"You predicted a great lie, a great danger, and an olive branch. In terms of foresight, your abilities eclipse mine. Consider this my olive branch to you, Mara."

"**Erase.**"

Jason Hiro speaks a word of power.

Drowsiness takes Mara, as her eyes begin to flutter and close.

_An... olive... branch..._
