Part 308: Approaching Faith's End

A small army, 5,000 strong, consisting of male and female humans, monsters, and even a few scattered demons, makes their way into the Labyrinth. Led by humanity's Hero, the Wordsmith, they begin a tactical assault on the unsuspecting Labyrinthian denizens while slowly and steadily advancing toward the halls of Faith's End, Hell's primary prison.

Jason Hiro stomps forward, leading the charge. His exosuit allows him to brazenly walk forward without fear of any demon's attacks. His glowing eyes flick from side to side as he uses a familiar set of Words of Power he first created back when he lost his vision.

"**Perception. Infrared. Sonar. Detection. Tracking. Targeting.**"

A wireframe-like view of the world spreads throughout the Wordsmith's vision. His head swivels around with mechanical efficiency as he points at upcoming corners and calls out the positions of unseen enemies.

"Thirty-four meters ahead. One Demon Lord. Two minotaurs. Three goblins and a half dozen orcs."

Without missing a beat, he adds in a Word of Power.

"**Nausea.**"

His magic directly strikes the soul of that group of enemies, instantly forcing them to their knees. They all heave up their guts, making them helpless against the human army as they turn the corner and gun them down.

"Up ahead, another group," Jason says, as he outlines another set of enemies.

This time, before the humans can get close, those monsters turn tail and flee.

"Never mind." Jason says.

As the Wordsmith's metal boots pound against the floor, his wife takes up a position at his right, while Amelia stays on his left, and Belial lingers behind all three. Unlike the humans, Belial doesn't bother wearing an exosuit, as it would only hamper her powers.

"These hallways look different from how I remember," Amelia says. "Do you know the way to Faith's End?"

The Wordsmith quietly nods. "I know it well. It's where I first woke up in Hell."

After a momentary pause, he adds, "It hasn't all been bad, though. I've made a lot of friends. Even married a hot lady."

Phoebe snorts. "_So_ romantic."

As the Wordsmith's army continues gunning down monsters and demons, the word gets out that an elite human killing force has entered Hell's halls. Despite Jason's anticipation, not a single powerful enemy bars their way.

The Wordsmith frowns.

"I expected to see a Hidden Emperor, by now. A Duke, at the least. Certainly a Baron. What's going on, Samantha?"

The Emperor of Passion shrugs, a motion he picks up with his 360-degree omnivision.

"I don't know," Belial replies. "Certainly, the Hidden Emperors won't like to get involved with the First Hell's matters, but you have practically dismantled their entire leadership. I can't imagine Fenrir or the others would let a chance to assert their dominance slip past."

"Maybe they're all afraid of Jason." Phoebe suggests.

"Unlikely. You don't know how _arrogant_ some of the Hidden Emperors are. Take Gorn, for instance. He is one of the mightiest Emperors I know. If I had to fight him, I don't know if I would come out on top. He has equal ambition to rival his strength, too, which is what makes him so fearsome."

"But you're the Second Emperor," Phoebe says. "Even if that title was taken away from you, it implies you're the strongest Emperor in existence, aside from the First."

"That title was given to me during the Ancient Era," Belial says. "I was absolutely the strongest aside from Satan, back then. These days... I'm not so sure."

Jason listens to her concerns and nods. "I'll have to keep an eye out for him, then. Gorn, huh? The Emperor of Many Faces. Sounds like a scary guy."

"I try not to talk to men like him," Belial says. "Or most other powerful demons. They're all fairly stuck-up and snotty. Fenrir especially gives me the shivers."

"Sounds like a good choice to me," Amelia mutters. "I'd do the same."

...

In the rear of the human army, Lieutenant Samuel wears his Rhino-type T-REX. His heavily armored figure intimidates any enemies who might be foolish enough to strike at their flanks.

Beside the Lieutenant stands one of fewer than a hundred demons living among the humans, and one of only two Lords in their ranks, a female demoness named Kiari, the Lord of Gnats. Her long brown hair is somewhat stringy and sticky, as bits of mucus adheres it to her skin. She wears simple woven clothes, while thousands of tiny bugs crawl around on her skin, giving her a rather nasty-looking appearance. Her face, somewhat plain, doesn't make her look particularly ugly or pretty, but the gnats nibbling at her skin would cause most men to avert their eyes in disgust.

Kiari periodically turns around to look behind the army. Her gnats swarm in the distant hallways, transmitting information to her telepathically.

"We have a few more followers," She says to Samuel. "Couple of orcs. Both undead. They might be Mephisto's."

Samuel doesn't reply for a moment. He merely turns his head to look at her, then returns to facing forward.

"...Sir?" She asks.

"I may have been assigned to walk with you," Samuel says, "but I don't have to talk with you. Cut the chit-chat. Only warn me if someone dangerous shows up."

"Have I... done something to offend you?" Kiari asks.

"Something like that. You're a demon. Demons killed my family. I'll never trust your kind."

The Lord of Gnats winces. "I'm sorry to hear that. I didn't know. I've always sympathized with how humans were treated. I've just been too weak to change anything..."

"Tell it to someone who cares." Samuel growls. "I don't want to talk to you or look at you. Once this mission's over, we'll never speak again."

A few soldiers walking ahead of Samuel turn their heads back to observe the conversation, then return to facing forward, thinking better of getting involved. After Samuel's heroics in the Stormbringer Attack, the vast majority of people deeply respect his opinion.

Kiari lowers her head. "I'm sorry, sir. I didn't mean to offend you. I'll be quiet, now."

Moments later, a trooper in front of Kiari turns around and lowers his helmet to look at her.

"With all due respect to the Lieutenant, I have a different opinion. I heard about the good you did after Beelzebub's... detonation. Your gnats helped us find several people buried under rubble, including my daughter. I want to sincerely express my thanks to you, miss Kiari."

The Lord of Gnats lifts her head and blushes. "R-really? I'm glad to hear that. Was she that little girl in the raggedy white dress? She was very sweet."

"She had some burns, but was otherwise alright, and she made a full recovery," The soldier says. "A lot of demons are awful monsters... but anyone who stands on our side deserves my respect. It's brave of you to fall in with humanity when we're so weak, especially when the so-called 'Hidden Emperors' might appear at any time and attack us. Good things will definitely come for you in the future."

"I don't know if humanity will make it," Kiari says, "but I just want to make a difference, like Miss Belial. I've always admired her kind heart. I just wish my people weren't so... brutal."

The soldier reactivates his helmet, then turns to face forward again.

"We can only pray the Hero manages to rearrange the natural order. Demons like you give me hope for a better future... one where we can all live in harmony."

Samuel snorts. "If we ever get there, and I _doubt_ we will, it will only be after our boots stomp the skulls of countless bloodskin oppressors. Call me skeptical, but I have little hope for the future."

Kiari sighs. "Me too, honestly. It's all so... bleak."

...

The human army marches inexorably toward Faith's End. The closer they draw and the more enemies they crush, the fewer that oppose their way. The hallways soon become utterly barren as every remotely intelligent sentient steers clear of the small but frightening force walking into their land.

"We're getting close," Belial says. She furrows her eyebrows immediately after. "But something feels... off."

"In what way?" Phoebe asks.

The Emperor of Passion pauses for a moment. She stares down an upcoming corridor and frowns deeply.

"I... can't sense anything up ahead."

Jason nods slowly. "My omnivision can't see forward at all. It's as if a huge, bubble-like void is blocking my vision. Some sort of... anti-magic energy."

For a brief moment, the Hero and Second Emperor exchange concerned glances. Jason's faceless helmet meets Belial's gaze, and they nod.

"We'll move forward slowly," Jason says. "This might be a trap. Could the Hidden Emperors have anticipated my coming here?"

Amelia pipes up. "When I first showed up here, Dawn told me she couldn't come along with me. I didn't think much of it at the time, but now I'm wondering if this wasn't the reason why..."

"Dawn?" Jason asks. "Who is that?"

"Um. She was a Demon Lord," Amelia explains. "The Lord of Caves, to be exact. I hadn't met her before. She told me Fenrir knew Hope was here..."

"Why didn't you mention here before?" Belial asks, turning to frown at the former Black Queen. "You're telling me _Fenrir_ had one of her underlings lead you here? This reeks of a trap! What else aren't you telling us?!"

Amelia winces, though her expression remains hidden behind her helmet.

"I... well, now that you mention it, there is one other thing I've just recalled... I really ought to have mentioned it sooner."

"And that would be?" Belial asks.

"...Gressil. Um. Dawn told me that he is a Hidden Emperor. Specifically, the Emperor of Chaos."

Jason and Phoebe both whirl around to look at her. The Wordsmith's tone turns harsh. "What?! How could you leave out such an important detail, Amelia? Are you KIDDING me?! Don't you think we'd want to know we're about to walk into the lair of a god-fucking-damned EMPEROR?"

"Seriously!" Phoebe shouts, her anger just as pointed. "Is this a game to you?! We only brought five thousand soldiers! We could die here!"

Amelia lowers her head. "Sorry. When Gressil attacked me, I... I had a lot of things on my mind. Some details kind of slipped away. And I was scared for Hope, so... I didn't think to mention a few things."

The Wordsmith audibly groans, but doesn't maintain his anger. "Gah! Alright, fine. We know now, so Gressil won't catch us by surprise. Belial, can you tell anything about this strange anti-magic field? What's causing it?"

The Emperor of Passion shakes her head. "I can't tell much of anything. It doesn't seem corrosive to our souls or something terrible like that, but I won't be able to activate any magical abilities if we enter. Neither will you."

Jason hesitates.

"So, no Wordsmithing?"

"Probably not." Belial answers.

The Wordsmith pauses for a full minute, weighing the pros and cons.

"Emperors cannot be defined by common sense. A casual slap can kill ten humans, even if they wear exosuits. If neither you nor I can use our magic, we'll be sitting ducks."

"Not quite." Belial says, her expression turning serious. "You forget. While I do possess potent healing and transformation magic, my true power comes from my physical strength. Among all the Emperors, I am unmatched. Even when lacking my magic, I can go toe to toe with any other Emperor in a battle of fists."

"Maybe so, but Gressil seems to have at least some abilities revolving around illusions," Jason says. "And if he's an Emperor, those illusions will be even stronger than when you saw him as a young Lord. I've seen the illusions Raphael could unleash, and they were quite frightening."

"But what about the anti-magic field?" Phoebe asks. "Won't that prevent Gressil from using his illusions?"

"Oh, yeah..." Jason mumbles. "That's a good point. But why would he deliberately cripple himself?"

"Maybe the anti-magic field is what has prevented you from locating Hope," Belial suggests. "And Neil, too, I'd wager. They're both probably imprisoned here where your Wordsmithing can't detect them. I'll bet that's why Ose wanted to capture Phoebe and Daisy... she would have put them here and issued demands, while you wouldn't be able to sense their presence."

"Ose and Gressil are brother and sister," Jason mutters. "The facts add up."

Neither Jason nor Phoebe says anything about Belial's casual mention of their daughter. Just the sound of her name pains their hearts terribly.

"Gressil might be weakened in this anti-magic bubble," Phoebe observes. "But then again, he might not. We should prepare for the worst. Jason, can my spiritual self construct a device for you? We'll want a last-ditch item in case the worst happens."

Jason nods. "**Projection.**"

His mind-wife materializes next to Physical Phoebe. The two of them chat for a few minutes while the army takes up defensive positions, keeping their eyes peeled for any enemy ambushes. Despite their relatively low numbers, not a single demon or monster approaches them.

Ten minutes later, Mental Phoebe pulls back into Jason's brain with a blueprint held in her hands.

"It's not a magical device, but a technological one. Considering the short amount of time we have, we can maybe crank out a couple hundred of these; enough for our most important soldiers and VIPs."

Jason looks into his Mind Realm at the blueprint his mind-wife has conjured onto one of the 'servers' in his brain.

"A teleportation device?"

"Yes," Mental Phoebe says. "It's a one-time use beacon that will teleport anyone it's attached to back to the Core. We can use it to escape if Gressil somehow traps us."

"I see," Jason says. "I can't connect to the Cube from inside the Labyrinth, but my internal magic reserves should be enough for two hundred of these. Give me, like, twenty minutes."

Jason immediately begins Wordsmithing, using the blueprint in his brain as the scaffold for its construction.

"**Synthesize. Synthesize. Synthesize. Synthesize.**"

Each Word of Power lowers his mana drastically, forcing him to dip into the Mana Beads placed inside his body. After consuming twenty-five of them to make one hundred and seventy-three micro-teleporters, he stops with only five mana beads left to spare.

"I'll keep a few in reserve. If we use these teleporters, we'll have to leave thousands of soldiers behind... so let's try to avoid that choice if possible. I don't even want to imagine what Gressil would do to our men if we left them for him as prisoners."

Amelia shudders. "No. You don't."

With that reality settled, Jason spends another few minutes transmitting orders to all of the soldiers behind him.

...

At the rear of the army, Kiari listens intently as Samuel relays the orders to her.

"An anti-magic field? That's strange," Kiari says. "I haven't heard about anything like that at Faith's End. I don't think it will prevent me from speaking to my gnats, but I won't be able to control them."

Samuel shrugs. "Then stay behind and head back to the Core. You'll only get in our way if you can't use magic. That's why we brought you, after all."

"I can still be useful!" Kiari protests. "Won't you just give me a chance? I want to help Hope and the rest of the prisoners, too! I'm doing my best, here."

Samuel pauses when the male trooper from earlier turns to look at him.

"...Alright. You can come, but don't be shocked if the situation goes south. If fires start breaking out, I'm getting my fellow _humans_ out alive first. Understand?"

Kiari's shoulders sag. "Yeah. I understand."

"Good. Let's move out, then," Samuel says. "Everyone, get ready to march on the Wordsmith's mark!"

---

Elsewhere in the Labyrinth, a single human wearing a stealth-type Spectre exosuit walks down the halls with poise and grace. The man's footsteps make no sound, as the soles in his boots dampen the vibrations to a nearly undetectable level.

This man, Makoto Ueda, now possessed by the soul of Siddhartha Gautama, the Hero known as Buddha, makes his way toward a destination called forth by his mind: A junction far off at the remote edges of the Labyrinth, where one of only two Warpgates still controlled by the demons remains open.

As Buddha walks, his eyes sweep from side to side.

He does not speak. He only observes and contemplates.

Silently, the reincarnated Hero walks directly toward a pair of female Demon Lords guarding the long hallway leading to that specific Warpgate. When the two demons see a human approaching them so brazenly, without any backup, yet wearing one of the feared 'demon-killing suits' from the Core, they jump into action.

"Intruder! It's a fleshbag!"

Buddha walks without fear. Each step he makes appears as if performed by an illusory dancer, one who has mastered the art of walking on water and swimming with the wind.

A halberd of fire materializes in one of the Lord's hands. She charges toward him with a cry and swings her blade down.

Buddhe casually steps to the side, evading the blow with ease. As if having fought this female a thousand times, he gently dances along with her movements, ducks her side-slashes, and spins around behind her to deliver one precise palm-strike to her back.

_THUMP!!_

Buddha's palm hits the middle of the Lord's spine, paralyzing her body for a moment and driving the wind from her lungs. She collapses to the floor, sputtering painfully and choking on her own phlegm.

At the same time, the other Lord advances toward him while spinning a pair of rapidly revolving wind-whips all around her body like a hurricane. The sheer speed of them dazzles the eyes of any who happen to spectate her. Dozens of goblins, orcs, and other demon grunts wait for her to engage the human, some of them visibly shocked by the fall of the first Lord.

The second Lord's whips tear apart the ground and walls surrounding her body. Their tips lash the Labyrinth's earthen surface, spraying debris in all directions and whipping up a cloud of dust to spray upon herself and the unknown human.

Buddha does not flinch. He calmly advances toward the demoness, as if ignorant of the terrifying speed and cutting power of her whips.

"Stupid bastard!" The demoness roars. "My whips will rip you to shreds!"

She whirls her weapons twice as fast, making them move far too fast for a mere human's eye to follow.

However, Buddha closes his eyes as he walks. He only listens to the sound of the world around him and its reverberations. He steps directly into the sphere of destruction and moves his body several times in imperceptible ways. With each movement, the whip-tips pass him with less than a millimeter to spare, not even nicking his armor.

Silently, like a snake diving upon a helpless hatchling, Buddha terrifies the Lord out of her wits by sidestepping her furious assault, dodging every conceivable attack, and bending around behind her to deliver yet another palm-strike to her spine as well.

_THUMP!!_

The Lord coughs violently, then falls to her knees and collapses on the ground a moment later. The magic in her body sputters and bubbles as an intense, unseen reaction assaults it, making her unable to conjure her demonic power.

As Buddha continues forward, he opens his eyes once again.

"I pray that you will find the light, young one. This servant does not kill, and so, your life will be spared."

Alarmed by the fall of both Lords on the scene, the other demon grunts hastily and clumsily lob fireballs, hurl rocks, and shoot arrows at Siddhartha Gautama. However, the reincarnated Hero easily sidesteps these attacks, walking forward with only slightly jittery movements as if he were casually dodging raindrops falling from the sky. Countless deadly projectiles with enough force to crush his chest and armored figure into mush harmlessly whizz past him. His steady and unstoppable advance causes the demons attacking him to feel a nameless fear in their hearts.

"Who the hell is this?! Is he the Wordsmith?!"

"Fuck if I know, but I don't get paid enough! I'm not sticking around for even one second longer!"

Many of the cowardly grunts turn tail and flee, leaving only the orcs and monsters to stop Buddha's advance.

They fail.

Buddha easily dodges the clumsy attacks of the orcs, slaps aside the goblins, and spine-palms a few snake-like Nagas who show up near the end.

Before long, he arrives at an open warp-gate, one which displays an image of a dark underground tunnel not dissimilar to the Labyrinth itself.

Buddha pauses for a moment. His expressionless face turns bleak for a moment as he shakes his head.

"Earth, gone. All its life. All its karma. The day the Volgrim destroyed it, my avatar perished as well. I could not undo the damage. How unfortunate... now, too much time has passed. Even so, my successor still remains alive. I must search for the spark necessary to reignite the Akashic Sequence. Only then can the Karmic Cycle begin once again."

After pausing for a moment longer, Buddha strides through the Warpgate, leaving the Labyrinth behind.

The two Lords from earlier weakly crawl back to their feet, looks of pain on both their faces.

"...Who was that? I can't even control my magic right now."

"My back, ugh... my _back_," The other moans. "In just one hit... that fellow wasn't normal. We have to inform the Demon Council of this perimeter breach."

The first one nods, then slumps back to the ground. "In a few minutes. Gotta catch my breath, first."

"Yeah. Same."
