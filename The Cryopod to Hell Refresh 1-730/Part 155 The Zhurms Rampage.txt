Part 155: The Zhurm's Rampage

Many miles from the battlefield, far from the sounds of angelic steel and demonic magic clashing, the recently vacated city of Nirvana stands like a jewel cast among the pearls of Heaven's landscape. Like many other cities in Heaven, lavishly designed stone houses sit along a carefully developed road, one formed from flat stones placed together in simple geometric shapes. Despite the simple design of all the houses, each one has an elegant air to it.

With curved, oval-shaped roofs, and doors big enough for Gabriel to step through without stooping, each house seems designed for the Archangel of Power enter, even though no other angels come anywhere close to his stature.

A foreboding wind blows through the vacated city. All of its residents, now engaged in battle with demonkind, have left their possessions and belongings behind, unaware of the secret strike force dispatched to destroy their homes.

The ground explodes! A Burrower-type demon jumps out of a freshly-dug hole in the dirt and lands in a crouch. He quickly scans the area, turning a wary eye toward the nearby windows.

"Chee-chee! I don't see anyone..."

Behind him, several orcs emerge from his tunnel. All of them ride atop Hellhounds, but after a dozen pop out, one particularly nasty-looking fellow riding atop the biggest hound of all makes his appearance. Without the slightest hint of fear, Sheol, the first Orc Zhurm, king of his people, crosses his arms and sits proudly atop his mighty steed — a three-headed Hellhound that puts all the others to shame.

"How pathetic. Look at you lot, sneaking around like thieves in the night. Disgraceful. Raise your chins. Stop pissing your pants and get ready to work."

The Orc Zhurm gazes upon the Burrower-demon with disgust. Unlike the Demon Lord cowering in fear, Sheol sits atop his hound like a conquering leader, who's come to visit his subjugated land. His clothing, formed from the pelts of various predator animals, is also adorned with hundreds of bones from the angels and humans he's killed over the centuries. Compared to the lesser beings surrounding him, Sheol stands atop a mountain of bodies; all of which have earned him his aura of rulership.

"I-it can't hurt to be careful," The Burrower replies. He glances up at Sheol as if to search for a hint of understanding. Despite his rank of Demon Lord, the Burrower appears quite frightened of the Zhurm. "What if one of the pigeons jumps out and-"

"Quiet," Sheol growls.

His words instantly silence the Burrower. Afraid of angering the Zhurm, the Demon Lord quietly backs away and dives into his burrow. He disappears without saying another word.

"Coward," Sheol mutters.

Several seconds pass. Sheol and his orcs stand silently and survey the surroundings, taking a few moments to get a feel for a city they've never stepped foot inside.

Sheol sniffs the air. "Mmm. Smell that? That's the stink of loot. There's treasure hiding around these parts."

"Oooh, _treasure!_" 

A goblin jumps out of the Burrower's tunnel. Within seconds, dozens of others follow him and spread out like a plague of locusts. They run to the nearest houses and peer inside or crouch at the doors, readying themselves to break inside.

One goblin licks his lips. "Oy! Some'un say tree-sure?"

"That's-hut I heard!" A drunken-sounding goblin gurgles. He stumbles over to the nearest house and peeps in the window. "Oooh! This'uns got sum nice-ies!"

Ten other goblins follow his lead. They run over and throw the door open, then rush inside. After a few seconds, one of them cries out in frustration. "Grrrgh! Damn you, Mogin! There ain't nothin' in this dump but cups and crockery! Yer a damn shame to your kin! You got the eyes of a slug!"

The drunken goblin shrugs. "Pft. They looked pretty nice t'me."

"Skeee! Stinkin' codge-fondler! We'll let Jobi take the lead! He always sniffs out the best shrakh!"

Sheol watches silently as the goblins do what they do best —appropriate loot. 

After the goblins fan out and leave the orcs in their dust, Sheol grunts to his comrades nearest him. "Hmph. Those gobby's aren't worth five pinches. Follow me. Let the runts grab that worthless shrakh; we're here for twinkle-metal."

Unlike the wild and seemingly carefree goblins, the Orcs wait for their leader's orders before moving to action. His First Commander, Krugna, pumps his fist. "Aye! We follow _Zhurm_ Sheol to riches and glory!"

One of the lesser orcs, a low-ranker wearing only a few bones on his tribal necklace, grunts questioningly. "Any good meat here?"

Immediately, a higher-ranked orc beside him slaps his junior's head. "Stupid _kupa_! Forget meat! We here for twinkle-metal! Need move fast before pigeons come flapping home!" 

Sheol flicks his gaze toward the direction they left. A small mountain blocks his vision, preventing him from seeing how the War in Heaven is unfolding. With a shrug, Sheol uncrosses his arms and pets the hairy body of his freakish, mutated Hellhound. The monstrous animal growls softly as its master strokes it.

"Now now, there's no need to get excited, Cerberus. Ain't nothin' here but a breeze."

Cerberus's middle head jerks to the left, knocking aside the hound's leftmost head. Neither one seems to care, because they both lock eyes on a humongous cathedral in the distance.

"Grrrgh... graarrr! HAROOO!"

The vicious animal's eyes turn bloodshot as its keen sense of smell detects something inside.

"Somethin's got Cerberus spooked," Krugna says. He walks his hellhound ahead a few steps to peer in the direction Cerberus alerted. "Think some'uns over there?"

Sheol hesitates. "Hmm. It's probably nothing... but just in case, let's take a peek. Cerberus ain't the type to mess around. Besides, that _heza_ looks a little off from the others. I bet there's loot hidin' inside."

...

The pack of Hellhound riders rushes through the town of Nirvana. They move like the wind as they scale the two-story stone houses with ease and pounce from rooftop to rooftop. Despite the immense bulk of the Hellhounds and their riders, the monsters' talons produce frighteningly little sound as they leap toward the Holy Haven.

The riders slow to a stop before the massive monastery. Their eyes widen as they take in the sight of the ten-story building towering over them.

"Bloody hell!" One of the higher-ranked orcs gasps. "It didn't look this big from back there!"

Even Sheol gapes at the massive stone structure. "How'd the angels make something like that? It better have twinkle-metal inside, or my name ain't Sheol."

The lowest-ranked orc raises an eyebrow. "Huh? Your name not Sheol? When it change?"

His superior officer slugs him in the back of the head again. "No, stupid! Sheol make funny! Why you so dumb?!"

The junior rubs his head and whimpers. "Oww... you so mean to me..."

"Only 'cause you stupid like chicken!"

While the juniors grumble at each other about minor things, Sheol narrows his eyes. "You're too loud. I smell some pigeons inside."

That shuts his lackeys up. The youngest orc's eyes widen as he jerks his head toward the cathedral. A look of hunger appears on his face, and he licks his lips. "Oooh, we get fight?"

Sheol smirks. "Yeah. You go first — time to earn your bones, _kupa_."

Krugna, the First Commander, flinches. He doesn't say anything out loud, but a look of worry passes over his face. _Sheol wants a kupa to go in first? That means he expects a trap._

Despite the First Commander's wariness, the youngest orc doesn't realize the danger awaiting him. He smiles greedily and pulls out his weapons — a pair of sharpened bone-claws that fit over his hands. He adjusts their fit in the blink of an eye, then guides his Hellhound to take a few steps forward. "Me honored, Zhurm Sheol! Me go first and leave only blood behind!"

Despite his concerns, Sheol says nothing to dampen the youngest orc's enthusiasm. _Every warrior must earn their bones,_ he thinks to himself. _Without facing danger, no orc can obtain glory! True warriors face their enemies head-on, without any fear!_

The youngest of his troops leads the charge. With his comrades behind him, the _kupa_ charges into the Holy Haven with his claws at the ready. After the small group of elites arrives inside, the orc's sense of smell, far keener than any human's, finally picks up the scent Cerberus noticed from earlier.

"Shrakh..." Krugna growls. "There must be a hundred chickens in this coop."

Sheol nods, but his worries fade away. "Aye, but guess what I smell? Blood. These 'uns are weak. They ain't fit for battle."

The Zhurm's eyes turn ruthless as he finally indulges in his primal instincts.

"**Kill 'em all. Don't leave a scrap o' flesh behind.**"

"Aye!" All of the orcs roar! Their hellhounds howl in rage and quickly fan out to attack as many places at once as they possibly can. Throwing caution to the wind, they smash down doors and burst into rooms where sickly, injured angels lie on their beds, recuperating from various illnesses. Several cherubs and seraphs, having heard the Hellhound's howls only seconds prior, scream in fright as the terrifying monsters rake them with claws and teeth, ending their existences before they have a chance to fight back.

"Hahahaha!" The youngest orc cackles in delight. "Me get many bones today! Me become full-blood!!"

Unsatisfied with his Hellhound taking his kills, the youngest orc leaps from his mount's back and lands atop one female angel trying to run away. Because of her injuries, she can't make it to a window in time, and dies silently, unable to scream, as the _kupa_ rips out her throat with his claws. He crushes her brains and quickly shoves a meaty handful of angelic blood and bone into his gullet. Unlike demons, who suffer immensely when they come into contact with the holy energy inside angel bodies, the youth feels little more than a warm tickle, further increasing his battle rage.

Suddenly, from out in the halls, five seraphs burst out of an inner chamber. These warriors, a select few who stayed behind to protect their injured brothers and sisters, activate multiple spells and begin lobbing fire and fury upon a pair of unlucky orc elites who didn't notice them in time.

"Foul monsters! Perish in flames!"

The lead cherub, a male named Peter, casts a wide range of powerful amplification spells to boost his allies, while also unleashing chaos magic upon the orcs to weaken and debilitate them. Both orcs start violently wretching and lose their focus. With their minds spinning and their vision wavering, they nudge their Hellhounds off-course and cause the beasts to flounder. The four-legged creatures quickly realize their riders aren't fighting correctly and go berserk. Using their animal cunning, they target Peter and pounce at him, but that proves to be exactly what he wants!

The seraphs behind Peter finish their hastily concocted trap magic and form a magical circle in front of their leader. The Hellhounds, while intelligent, are unable to comprehend the glowing runes etched into the ground and charge directly into it.

_Shik, shik, shik!_

Hundreds of knives, formed from holy energy, fire out of the circle toward the ceiling. In the blink of an eye, both warriors and their mounts perish before they can react. The monsters fall to the ground and slide to a stop only inches from Peter's feet.

Peter flicks his eyes to the left and right. The sounds of other Hellhound riders scrambling toward his position drenches him in sweat. "Watch out! They're coming from the right!"

The Holy Haven's hallways, once beautifully etched with gems and rhinestones, now sizzle with green orc blood. Peter and his group put their backs to a window and quickly summon additional spells to entrap the incoming orcs.

_Crash!_

Before the seraphs can react, the window behind them explodes! Sheol and his First Commander leap through the stained-glass and land atop three seraphs in the back. Peter and his second-in-command whirl to face the threat, but they're too late. Sheol and Krugna rip apart their three brothers and immediately prepare to attack the remaining cherubs.

However, Peter and his final-remaining comrade prove much swifter of mind. They work together to activate a dual-spell and fire a hail of holy daggers at Sheol's First Commander. Krugna screams in pain as the rain of blades rips him off his steed and flings him against the wall, where the magical weapons impale him a hundred times in the blink of an eye. He slumps forward, dead.

"Krugna!" Sheol howls. Animalistic rage engulfs him. He leaps off Cerberus and jumps at the seraph beside Peter, impaling him with a small bone-dagger holstered at his side. Peter gasps and summons a confusion spell to addle Sheol's senses. The Orc Zhurm growls and swipes at Peter, but his vision blurs, and he misses his target.

Sheol screams at his angelic opponent. "Stand still! I'm gonna gut ya like a RAT!"

Peter doesn't reply. He summons a spear of holy energy and rears back to impale Sheol's fumbling form. Even as he does, the Seraph flicks his gaze to both of the Hellhounds.

_I cannot slay these beasts! But, the least I can do is kill the orc's leader!_

_Slash!_

The young orc _kupa_ appears behind Peter and gashes out the back of his neck, catching the Seraph off-guard. 

_Ahh! How... where did he...?!_

Peter falls forward, the life in his body having fled to the Great Beyond. A moment later, Cerberus and the other Hellhound pounce atop him and rip the Seraph leader to shreds.

Sheol grunts. He clutches his face and shakes off the dizziness plaguing him. It only takes a few seconds for Sheol to regain his senses, and when he does, he eyes the youngest orc with great reverence.

"Keegol. You saved your Zhurm's life. Today, you no longer call yourself _kupa!_ You took revenge for Krugna's death! That makes you a full-blooded warrior!"

The adrenaline in the youngest warrior's body threatens to overwhelm him, but he forces himself to nod along to the Orc Zhurm's words. "Me big thanks give to Zhurm Sheol! Zhurm guide me! Zhurm honor me!"

Sheol nods along to Keegol's enthusiastic words. However, after a moment, he flicks a regretful eye toward his deceased First Commander.

"Krugna died honorably. He fought hard and earned great glory. Keegol, you give the Rite of Blood."

Keegol's excitement dampens. He stares at the body of his fallen comrade, and finally cools the blood boiling in his veins. "Rite of Blood. Yes. Me understand."

With a heavy sigh, the young orc walks toward his ally and pulls him off the wall. He strips the armor off Krugna's body and begins systematically ripping chunks of flesh out of the orc, devouring him with very little enthusiasm. The mood turns somber as Sheol watches his former First Commander disappear into the maw of his youngest elite warrior.

By the time Keegol finishes, the other orcs arrive. Blood covers them and their mounts, and they watch without judgment as the youngest orc devours their comrade, not even leaving the bones behind.

"What happen Krugna? How he die?" A mid-grade elite asks.

"Killed by chicken," Another orc replies. "See? Bodies there and there. _Kupa_ avenged him."

"Not kupa anymore," A different orc mutters. "See? He perform Rite of Blood. Now he mighty."

Once Keegol consumes every last piece of the First Commander, a shocking change occurs! The young orc leans back and howls in pain as his body begins to glow with light. He falls to his back and writhes on the ground as a single magical rune etches itself into his scalp, then heals over, leaving no traces behind.

Several moments pass.

Keegol, now a full-blooded warrior, staggers to his feet and kneels before Sheol. He places one fist on the ground and bows his head respectfully.

"This warrior bows before _Zhurm_ Sheol. It is this orc's honor to serve as Zhurm's First Commander."

With his speech having improved dramatically in the blink of an eye, Keegol cuts a different figure from before. His muscles ripple with strength, and his eyes possess a new vitality from only minutes earlier. After completing the Rite of Blood, he ascends to the next plane of orc power and awaits his Zhurm's instructions.

"Good, good," Sheol replies. He turns to look at one of the other orcs. "Are all the chickens dead?"

"Aye."

"We shouldn't stay long. Take the chickens' twinkle-metal and keep moving. Keegol will lead."

"Aye, Zhurm!"

"Aye, Zhurm!"

All of the orcs nod in agreement and fall in line to allow Sheol to bark orders from the rear. The group of orcs loads the loot from their deceased enemies onto the Hellhound's backs, but the monstrous wolves barely notice the hundreds of pounds of extra weight tied to their muscular bodies. 

...

Half an hour later, Sheol and Keegol lead their warriors down a long pathway toward the edges of Nirvana. Having scouted dozens of houses along the way, they meet up with the goblins to plan their next moves.

"How are the demons doing against the chickens?" Sheol asks. "Are they winning? Losing?"

A mangy-looking goblin scurries over and rubs his palms together. "Nyehehe! Me thinks it's a bit of a back 'n forth, oh, mighty Zhurm! Them chickens're puttin' up a good fight, but the demons ain't doin' bad either!"

The little goblin, scarcely two feet tall, gestures toward ten other goblins dragging giant sacks filled with valuables. Despite their skinny arms and legs, the goblins possess incredible strength proportionate to their sizes. 

"Look at all the goods we've stolen! Them stinkin' pigeons ain't gonna have nothin' left by the time we've picked this place clean!"

Sheol appears unimpressed. "Fancy cutlery and pretty gold won't win us a war, stupid gobbys. Where's all the twinkle-metal? Where're the weapons? Armor? We need better equipment to fight against the chickens, not fancy cups 'n plates!"

The goblin cowers slightly as the wrath-filled eyes of the Zhurm fall upon him. "W-we looked everywhere, your nastiness! There ain't nothin' good here! The chickens took all their swords 'n shields with 'em when they went to fight!"

"As if!" Sheol snorts. He flicks his eyes to the left and right as he scans the city. "What group of proud warriors would only have one set of weapons and armor? There's bound to be more here!"

From the rear of the goblins, a little runty fellow slinks forward. Standing barely a foot tall, the little guy seems like a child compared to his peers. He fiddles with his pointy ears and clears his throat. "Ahm... ahem... y-your nastiness? This... this _lowly_ creature sawed somethin' scary... it no far from here... but me no wanted go there!"

Sheol's eyes snap to the newcomer. "You found something? Where? Out with it!"

Not only Sheol, but his hellhound, Cerberus, snarls viciously at the goblin, startling the little fellow and turning his dark-green skin into a pale, moldy color. 

"B-big place! Fire! Hot! It s-stinked of chickens! R-right up that hill!"

He extends a trembling finger toward the lightly forested mountain blocking their view from the war unfolding several miles away. Sheol follows the goblin's finger and gazes at the trees, only to grin evilly as he spots a single building, barely visible among them.

"Ohoho, that stone-hut looks different from the rest. I bet it's got some good shrakh hiding inside! Let's get a move on, you little runts! We've other cities to plunder after this'un!"

All of the orcs and goblins nod and raise their fists. "Aye! We follow Zhurm!"

Like a horde of rats, the orcs and goblins begin rushing out of Nirvana toward the circular building in the distance. It doesn't take long for them to travel half a mile and arrive at the strangely-shaped forge, but when they do, all of the monsters slow to a halt to gaze at it.

"You no kidding," One of the orc elites mutters to the little goblin who mentioned the building. "It hot like lava, here. Why so hot? Where heat come from?"

For once, Sheol doesn't charge toward the stone structure. He gazes at its top and spots thin trails of smoke streaming from its top. "There's a fire cookin'. Why light the inside on fire? Maybe there's a chicken at home, eh?"

Immediately, Sheol jabs a finger at the wimpy goblin who brought him here. "You; gobby! Go'n take a look! Open the door'n  see if there's anyone inside!"

The goblin turns pale. "Eee! It scary! Me no wanna go in there! Big danger!"

"My foot up yer arse is the real danger!" Sheol snarls. He reaches for a club at his waist and grins evilly. "Either I kill ya here, or you get yer lazy arse over there and hope there ain't no chicken inside! You got no choice, gobby!"

Terrified out of his mind, the goblin simply nods and scurries forward. He runs past the orcs and turns to face them, keeping one wary eye on Sheol as he shambles backward toward the stone hut.

As the goblin shuffles backward toward the heated forge, he periodically jerks his head back to see if any angels are about to jump him, but not even a fly stirs. The heat increases to sweltering levels, causing the goblin to sweat like crazy.

"Oh geez, oh no, oh geez..."

His teeth chatter. His elbows click. Finally, the goblin arrives at the entrance. Since the forge lacks any windows, the goblin can only stare nervously at the door. He wrings his hands as incredible streams of heat pour from a crack beneath it.

"Get a move on, gobby!" Sheol bellows. "I ain't got all day, ya little rat!"

Prodded by the Zhurm's orders, the little goblin reaches up to the wooden door handle, only to frown as he realizes it's well beyond his reach. He glances around, and spots a long, straight branch on the ground. The goblin quickly takes it and sticks it through the door's handle, then pulls with all his strength.

"Hrrrgh!"

With a mighty heave, the goblin's heart skips a beat as it swings wide open. A ghastly wave of heat spills out of the forge and washes over him, turning the goblin's thoughts to mush.

"Nnn! H-hah... hot!!"

He quickly jumps back and scurries away, leaving the forge behind him as he runs for the safety of his fellow goblins.

"You little shrakh-head! Coward!" Sheol howls. He readies himself to kill the goblin, but pauses as Heaven's Light illuminates the interior of the forge. "Ohh, wait a minute! Keegol, d'you see what I see?!"

"Aye!" The First Commander replies. His eyes brighten with excitement as various weapons and armor hang from the walls inside the forge. "Twinkle-metal! Lots of it!"

Sheol's anger at the goblin disappears. "Hoho! We've done it now, boys! Ain't no chickens inside, and lots o' loot for the plunderin'!"

Greed swallows the goblins and orcs. The orcs dismount their Hellhounds and leave them behind, then hurry over to the open door. As they draw nearer, all of them wince under the immense heat.

"Bah! It's hotter than Satan's anus in there!" Keegol cries. "Why's it so stinkin' hot inside?!"

"Damned if I know," Sheol replies. A familiar sensation of danger creeps into his mind, but the Zhurm's greed overwhelms and suppresses it. "Tch, tell the hounds to knock down some o' the walls! That'll let the heat out!"

"Smart thinkin' boss!" Keegol says. He grins at his leader, proud that his Zhurm is so wise and all-knowing. "Get on it, warriors! You heard the Zhurm!"

Five of the orc elites hop back on their mounts and run to the side of the building. While the heat does intensify the closer they draw, it's nowhere near as unbearable as at the front door.

"Ready! Hup!"

The highest-ranked among the five barks out a command. All five hellhounds charge at the reinforced stone walls, and their massive paws tear out the rocks sealed together with primitive angelic mortar. In the blink of an eye, five gaping holes erupt in the building's structure, sending heat pouring out from all sides.

The hellhounds flinch at the hundreds of degrees of hot air blasting them in the face. "Arrrooo! Eeyii?!"

Instinctively, the monstrous animals jump backward and paw at their faces, nearly sending their riders tumbling from their backs. The orc elites scowl as their mounts whimper like beaten dogs. "Stop whining; ya damn pups!"

Sheol flicks his gaze toward Cerberus, and the three-headed hound nonchalantly averts its gaze, as if to say, _Well, **I** wouldn't mind the heat. Don't lump me in with weaklings._

Despite the frantic cries of the hellhounds, they eventually recover from their shock and retreat to a safe distance. Sheol stares at the forge and waits. Sure enough, after ten minutes, the heat inside dies down noticeably.

"Alright. Let's sneak a peek. Grab anything you can stuff in a sack 'n pick the place clean!"

...

The orcs and goblins slink toward the forge. Even with most of its heat diminished, their discomfort intensifies once they arrive at the doorway.

"I might last five minutes 'fore I pass out," The third-highest ranked orc says. "Let's make dis quick. In an' out."

The moment the orcs step inside the forge, their desire to flee the heat begins to battle with their greed. Dozens and dozens of priceless, powerful treasures line the walls. Swords, clubs, shields, gems, bows, and other assorted valuables catch their eyes one after the other. The elites quickly get to work, grabbing everything in sight and throwing them out the door, where the goblins, with their much weaker constitution, snatch the items and throw them in knapsacks.

Sheol's entire body covers itself in sweat, giving his skin a slug-like appearance. The sweat dries up rapidly from all the heat in the air, only to repeat the cycle, sucking all the moisture from his body.

_Damn!_ Sheol growls inwardly. _Am I gonna die, while looking for twinkle-metal?! What a shit way to-_

Suddenly, his thoughts go silent. Sheol's eyes flick to a single sword laying on an anvil. The weapon, a blackened blade coated in coal-dust, appears pathetic compared to the other artisanry on display.

However...

As Sheol gazes at it, his eyes waver, unable to look at anything else.

_Somethin' about this sword... I sense magic in it... strong magic, too..._

The Zhurm licks his dry, chapped licks. The heat in the forge sucks the moisture from his tongue, but he barely notices.

"Oy, Zhurm! We've picked the place clean! Let's git outta here!"

One of his elites grabs him by the shoulder, but Sheol swats his hand away. "No, no... I want... I want this 'un, Georg. Lemme just..."

Sheol's heart palpitates. His hands tremble as he reaches toward the blackened blade. His eyes absorb more of its qualities, causing him to notice its razor-sharp edges, faded gold hilt, and an inscription going down the blade. 

_Excalibur._

Unable to read the language of the angels, Sheol hasn't a clue about the might of the weapon before him. His elites stumble deliriously toward him and try to pull him out the door, but he shoves them away and grabs for the blade.

"It's mine! MINE! You can't have it!!"

Confused, his soldiers stare at the ordinary-looking weapon with disinterest. They cannot begin to fathom the power contained in the weapon, nor their Zhurm's ability to detect its aura.

Sheol grabs Excalibur's hilt. He raises the weapon above his head, and cackles wildly.

"Mine! My sword!! With this weapon, I can-"

Suddenly, the Zhurm's voice catches. His chest shudders as an incredible amount of holy energy floods into his bloodstream. His neurological pathways seize up, causing him to freeze in place and tighten his grip on the weapon. Terror engulfs him! He tries to unclench his hand, but he can't let go!

"H-hnn! Grrrgh!"

Sheol's voice trembles. He fights against Excalibur's power with all of his strength, but nothing he tries saves him from the horrible agony tearing through his body.

One of his elites balks as their leader's body begins to glow with a brilliant light. "Zhurm! What happening?!"

"Ahhhh!!!"

Sheol screams as waves of agony and ecstasy collide, threatening to detonate his heart like a bomb! "HELP MEEEE!!!"

The agonized cries of their leader freeze the other orcs in place. Their eyes widen as his skin and muscles start to bulge. Sheol's unending screams draw the attention of the goblins outside, who quickly peep into the forge's openings, curious about the commotion.

**FWOOOOOOM!**

The holy energy inside Sheol's body finally reaches its limit. He explodes violently, sending beams of light firing in a million directions. Each one rips through the orcs and goblins nearby, turns their skin and bones into mist, and kills them instantly. The light continues to grow, obliterating the forge and everything around it.

In the blink of an eye, every orc and goblin perishes, unable to withstand Excalibur's incredible power.

Where Heaven's Forge once stood, now only a fifty-foot-deep crater rests. Half a dozen Hellhound bodies lay scattered around, with large chunks of their bodies vaporized. The rest slowly bleed out from their injuries.

However.

One Hellhound, the meanest, baddest, most sharp-witted of its kin, stands atop the crater. Cerberus sniffs the air in search of its master, but finds nothing.

"Rooo..."

Cerberus lets out a pitiful whimper. It descends into the pit and sniffs at the scraps of blood and skin scattered here and there, but despite its best efforts, the monstrous creature finds no traces of Sheol, nor his elites. It paws the ground and tries to dig for bodies, but after several minutes, the three-headed hellhound finds nothing of note.

Eventually, Cerberus spins in a circle and plops down in the center of the crater. It stares at the bodies of its dying and dead brothers and sisters, as a sense of regret engulfs its heart.

Cerberus has nobody left.

As for the ultimate weapon, the god-slaying blade known as Excalibur, no traces of it remain behind.

All that remains is a crater, the lone evidence that a terrible calamity unfolded here.

Cerberus... is alone.
