Part 290: Advent of Chaos

Small fires burn throughout the ruins of Hero City. The once-brilliant sights of the Pyramids no longer line the horizon. In their place, only shredded metal and molten bricks remain.

A smell of death pervades the air. Jason Hiro consoles Blinker as she continues choking out sobs. The emotional wounds they've both suffered have completely rent them to their cores.

"We... we should find Phoebe and the others." Jason says.

The Hero's impassive expression mirrors the deadened nerves spread throughout his body. The more Blinker cries, the less he allows himself to feel. Like a wall erected between his mind and his soul, the Hero demonstrates zero outward emotions.

Blinker shudders for a moment before pulling away. She wipes her eyes several times, smearing ash and dirt against their peripheries.

"Y-yes. I'm sorry. I just... I can't stop... uuu..."

Jason wraps his arm around Blinker's shoulder, then starts pulling her to the west. They slowly begin to walk in the direction of the ramp leading down from the plateau to the lower wildlands. 

From inside the Hero's mind, his wife, Phoebe, speaks. "Jason..."

_What?_ Jason asks.

Phoebe sits on a bench inside a small park. Her expression appears just as miserable as Blinker's, though she forces herself to hold her emotions together for her husband's sake.

"It's... it's not your fault."

_It is._ Jason replies. _All of this happened because of me._

"No. Ose did this..." Phoebe mutters. "Ose k-killed... she killed our little girl."

_Yes. But she never would have succeeded without my weakness._

"It's not your fault!" Phoebe repeats. "You can't just... lock your emotions away!"

_I don't deserve to cry. I don't deserve the luxury of emotions when all of this could have been prevented. I failed everyone, and now their deaths hang on my shoulders._

Phoebe hesitates. She presses her hands against her eyes as she tries to suppress the anguish in her heart. Seeing her husband stoically enduring the most crushing moment of his life only makes her pain worse.

"That doesn't matter, Jason. It doesn't matter one bit! Daisy is dead. She's dead! As her father, it's only right for you to... to shed tears!"

Jason and Blinker pause near the rubble of a devastated building. Unlike many of the others, a burned up corpse rests outside, one of an unknown gender and age. The body's burned skin and bones barely give the impression of a humanoid shape, indicating they were either human, demon, or some similar variant during their life.

_I had one minute and forty five seconds to react to Beelzebub's detonation,_ Jason thinks. _I could have done so much more to stop him. I could have fired a beam of holy energy at him. Perhaps by vaporizing his body, I'd have prevented his explosion. I might have saved a million lives._

"Yes, maybe! Maybe, maybe, maybe!" Phoebe replies. "But perhaps destroying his body would have ignited him sooner! Perhaps the holy energy would have increased his power and made his detonation more destructive! You don't know!"

_I don't,_ Jason concedes. _But I should have known. I should have prepared contingencies. Beelzebub already exploded once. He nearly killed Hope and Samantha. My failure to anticipate his movements led to this disaster. Him, and all the others._

Jason resumes walking. Neither he nor Blinker say anything out loud. For her part, the Fairy stays silent, merely swallowing all the death with her eyes while silently grieving for the fallen. As for Jason, he barely takes note of the world around him, instead staring vacantly into the distance.

_I knew Ose was a threat, yet I tried to play nice. I knew that all of the demons who took part in this genocide existed, yet I prepared no countermeasures. If I am not at fault, then who is?_

Tears well up in Phoebe's eyes.

"The demons," She whispers. "They are."

_Demons are demons, and humans are humans,_ Jason replies. _A hundred thousand years of history have proven as much. Many of the world's greatest minds warned me of the realities of our universe. But did I listen? No. I tried to change the unchangeable. I tried to turn monsters into bunnies and birds. Now that I know better, it's too late to undo the past. I can't bring Daisy back. I've already tried to resurrect the dead, and failed. There is no simple fix. There is no easy redemption. I have to accept my failings and change my future path._

Phoebe's soul momentarily jolts in surprise. "What are you saying?"

_Don't act surprised. You always told me to toughen up. You told me to stop treating the demons with kid gloves. Most of the defenses around Hero City came from your real body. It was you who prepared for the worst, while I focused on building a stupid moonbase. Oh. That reminds me._

Jason pauses his walking. He raises his eyes skyward, toward the slowly darkening sky. 

"**Locate.**"

Jason speaks a word of power, aiming it toward a tiny dot circling the planet. After a few seconds, his mind makes contact with the few remaining humans aboard the Atlas Cannon.

"Ose somehow wormed her way into our ranks," Jason says. "I think I'll start by purging all of the traitors who followed in her path. **Poison.**"

Jason conjures a lethal, but incredibly slow acting airborne toxin. He disperses it into the hallways of the Atlas Cannon, allowing it to permeate within the bodies of the dozen or so traitors left alive.

The noxious gas immediately paralyzes all of the traitors aboard the Cannon. Then, it slowly cooks them alive, causing them immense pain, yet preventing them from crying out or screaming. The agony of having their blood boiled from the inside-out proves more horrifying than the worst tales of Hell cooked up by ancient superstitious humans. It will take more than five hours for all of them to die, and in that time, they will suffer fates worse than death.

"Jason...?" Blinker asks, glancing at him in confusion.

"It's nothing," Jason says. "Just cleaning up a few rats."

...

Minutes later, Jason arrives at the base of Hero City, below the mountain plateau, near where the mines leading deeper into the planet reside.

Blinker winces as they pass yet another brutally burned, scarred, and malformed body missing half its limbs. The further they travel from Beelzebub's detonation, the more 'intact' corpses they stumble across.

Jason only looks at them for a moment before averting his eyes.

"...Another one I've failed."

He and Blinker walk separately now. The Monster Queen's tear-stained face reveals the extent to which she's wept, yet no longer does she cry. Having exhausted all of her emotional energy, her face becomes somewhat blank, mirroring Jason's.

"Are you doing okay, Jason?" Blinker asks. "You... you haven't cried at all."

Jason shakes his head. "I don't deserve to weep. Sorrow comes from the powerless, from those who lacked the strength to save someone, yet fervently wish the opposite were true. I had the ability to save everyone here, but failed to use it properly."

"That's not true. You don't deserve to suffer!" Blinker cries.

What follows is another back and forth mirroring the one Jason had with his mind-wife. Blinker fails to change his mind any more than Phoebe did.

"War sucks..." Blinker mumbles. "I hate it. I hate the harm it causes. I hate the destruction of loving bonds."

Jason nods sagely. "Yet those who refuse to engage only make themselves easy pickings for those who don't."

His words echo those spoken by Solomon and Raphael. Having finally suffered a debilitating blow on par with the betrayal Solomon endured, as well as material losses equivalent to Raphael during the War in Heaven, Jason finally understands what it means to permanently suffer an irreversible wound.

The two magicians walk into the mines beneath Hero City. Several bodies lay just outside the doorway, likely the last people who barely didn't make it inside the safety of the underground tunnels. Countless similar tunnels exist around Hero City's peripheries, though few of them are as easy to reach as the one at the base of the city's primary ramp.

Within thirty seconds, Blinker and Jason reach a small group of one hundred soldiers, all of them holding their guns with trembling hands.

"Commander?" One of the soldiers says. "Is that you?"

"It's me." Jason replies. "Where is Phoebe? Is she okay?"

"She isn't here," the man says. "The Second Emperor took her and your daughter somewhere safer. We... we panicked, and didn't know how to stop her."

"My daughter..." Jason mutters. A forlorn expression appears in his eyes. "Don't worry. Belial will protect Phoebe. Of that much, you can rest assured."

"We do have many of the other high-ranking leaders further in the mines, sir," The soldier says. "General Chadwick, Lieutenant Samuel, Corporal Hurent, and so on. Other than Elder Skarde, everyone is accounted for."

After questioning the soldier about a few mundanities, Jason pats Blinker's back. "You stay here. I need to get an eye on the damage."

She nods listlessly. "Okay."

Jason teleports away, flickering half a mile toward a dug-out hole in the cliff. Located somewhere vaguely in the northwest of the city's plateau, Jason arrives to find Belial hunkering over Phoebe protectively, shielding his wife's body with her own.

"**Heal,**" Jason says, directing his word of power toward the succubus. He quickly fixes her internal injuries and wipes out the radiation wracking her body, along with Phoebe's, too.

A minute later, Jason carefully pulls out Phoebe and Belial from the hole. He leans both of them back against his arms, with Phoebe in his right, and Belial in his left.

"Cough... cough..." Belial gasps. "Uhh. Head hurts. Whud habbened?"

Jason purses his lips. "Beelzebub blew himself up."

"He did? Oh. Oh! He did!"

Belial's eyes flicker quickly as she forces herself back to full alertness. She pulls herself together and sits up straight, drawing away from Jason.

"Phoebe! Is she okay?"

Jason nods. "She's fine. Still exhausted, though. She'll need at least another day of sleep before she wakes up."

Belial nods. "Right. Good, that's good."

As the succubus breathes a sigh of relief, several seconds of silence follow.

Suddenly, Belial begins to tremble. Her eyes flick around the nearby area, then scan Jason, causing her to notice his weirdly calm facial expressions.

"D-Daisy! Where is Daisy?! Is she okay? I... I had her with me, but then...!"

Jason doesn't answer. He merely stares into the succubi's eyes, his expression unmoving and hollow.

A horrible sense of dread engulfs Belial. "No... no, she... she has to be okay! Daisy's fine, right, Jason? She's fine! You wouldn't p-play a prank on me at a time like this... would you?"

Jason lowers his head. He strokes his wife's hair absentmindedly as she sleeps in his arms.

"Daisy's dead, Sam. She's dead."

"Nooo! No, devils, no!" Belial shrieks. She claws at her eyes as waves of nausea strike her stomach. "No, it's not true! It can't be true! Uuu..."

Every second that passes, Belial sinks deeper and deeper into guilt and self-doubt. Tears burst seemingly from her soul as the surrounding air trembles under her demonic mana.

"_Guuhuu_... Daisy... little Daisy, no... not my niece..."

Jason watches silently as Belial breaks down further and further.

"I should have held her! I should have protected her!" Belial cries. "Useless! I'm so devil-damned useless! A pathetic bitch, a stupid whore!!"

Jason sighs. "It's not your fault, Sam. I should have protected Daisy. She was my daughter. My responsibility."

His words do nothing to ease Belial's guilt. Within seconds, her heart-wrenching grief turns into maddened bloodlust and soul-consuming rage.

"I'LL KILL THEM!! I'LL FUCKING KILL THEM ALL! I'LL RIP OSE TO PIECES, AND BEELZEBUB AND ALL THE OTHER **FUCKING BASTARDS** WHO DID THIS!!"

Her mood swings, similarly, don't affect Jason in the slightest.

"Ose's dead, Sam. So is Beelzebub, Bael, and many of the others. You can't kill them again."

Belial pants like a maddened dog. "Is that what you think?! You're wrong! YOU'RE WRONG! Mephisto's still alive! That worthless little rat! I bet he'll bring them back! Even if he doesn't, he's still on MY kill-list! I'll show him who's the true master of the dead! I'll grind his bones into... into POWDER! I'll smash him, pulverize him!!"

The more Belial rages, the more emotionally fatigued she becomes.

After twenty minutes of screaming and crying, the Second Emperor finally releases most of her pent-up rage. She sags to the ground, staring listlessly at Phoebe's sleeping face.

"I'm sorry. I'm sorry. I... I shouldn't have..."

Jason shakes his head. "Don't apologize. You deserve to feel angry."

Belial nods, but her eyes fall on Jason immediately after.

"I suppose. But what about you? Are you... are you okay, Jason? You've barely spoken. I'm starting to feel guilty over my outburst. You must be devastated."

Jason doesn't immediately reply. Instead, it takes him several seconds to unravel his thoughts.

"I don't know how I feel. I still haven't come to terms with... all of this. I know it's my fault. I know I failed. But in the end, I just feel this... this crushing weight of emptiness. It doesn't matter what I do, or what regrets I have. I can never bring my little girl back. I can never hear her laughter, or ruffle her hair, or watch her grow up into a beautiful woman. I can't change anything, and so, I just feel... hopeless."

Belial nods slowly. She slides closer to Jason and wraps her arm around his back.

"I've lost someone I loved deeply, too. I lost Satan. To you, he may have been a tyrant demon Emperor, but to me, he was the man I loved through thick and thin. His death... changed me."

Jason says nothing, so Belial keeps talking.

"I always blamed myself for his death, just like I blame myself for Daisy's. I'm an Emperor, you know? I'm supposed to be strong, practically unstoppable. In the end, for all my powers, I always feel so... helpless."

She sighs. "I couldn't do much of anything during this war. I fought against Ose... and lost. I tried to protect Daisy from Yama's Shades, but Daisy ended up saving herself. I tried to protect the hospital, but everyone died under my watch. I've never felt as useless as I did today."

Jason nods lethargically.

"I've always put myself above others. I arrogantly thought my Wordsmithing made me untouchable. I spent the last six years working with Solomon to enhance my usage of it. Even so, when the die fell, I crashed and burned. I didn't pay the price for my actions, though. Everyone else did."

"That's not true," Belial replies. "You did pay the price. You paid a _terrible_ price, Jason, and so did I."

"Oh. I suppose you're right."

The two of them fall silent. The collapsed forest before them, filled with the bodies of countless dead woodland monsters, crackles and snaps as small fires consume what foliage remains.

After a minute, Belial rubs her forehead.

"What do we do now, Jason?"

"I don't know," The Hero replies. "But I'm sure it will involve a lot of dead demons."

Belial merely nods.

"Mmm."

_Elsewhere, deep within the Labyrinth's prison complex._

Gressil, the master of Faith's End, gazes silently at the wall. His eyes bore into it as if it contains the secrets of life, yet he seemingly finds nothing.

"Little sister Ose. You've finally fallen victim to your arrogance."

The prison warden inhales and exhales. The slow movements of his chest betray no emotion.

"I'll miss you, sister. You were the only one who truly knew me."

Slowly, Gressil turns around. He lowers his gaze to the mutilated, limbless body of Hope, the barely conscious Wordsmith. Blood soaks the table where his prisoner lays, while multiple festering, rotting wounds line the human's torso.

Hope wheezes painfully. Both of his eyes, now bloodshot from burst blood vessels, gaze at the prison master with a mixture of hatred and terror.

"Humanity survived," Gressil says. "Somehow. It was within my predictions. Your original body managed to turn the tables on his opponents by procuring... _something interesting._ Ah, but what a pity. He 'lost' his little girl."

Gressil chuckles, but then sighs.

"Life is a series of ups and downs. Some people seek to control the spontaneity of existence. They develop systems, dynasties, lineages, and all sorts of techniques, hoping to manage the universe around them. They always fail. Do you know why?"

The Warden slowly sits on a nearby stool. He gazes at the Wordsmith icily.

"Because life is chaos. Those who seek to control chaos always fall to its whims. It is only those who embrace chaos that manage to become whole."

Hope coughs up a wad of blood. Despite the trauma he's suffered, he still retains an inkling of sanity.

"Kill... kill... me..."

"You know I can't do that," Gressil says. "You're far too precious to me. Once you succumb, I can make you into one of my eyes. Even if I fail... that is merely the embodiment of chaos at work."

The Wordsmith grits his teeth, but says nothing else.

Seeing his prisoner fall silent, Gressil raises his head. A contemplative look enters his eyes.

"You think I'm a monster. They all do. But... I didn't always use to be this way."

Gressil raises his palm upward, casually aiming it toward the ceiling.

A flash of light appears in his grasp! It quickly changes shape, morphing into the appearance of a beautiful, shimmering butterfly.

"A long time ago, I was a Demon Lord just like any other. Quieter than most. All I wanted to do was sit around and play with my butterflies."

Gressil slightly lifts his hand, causing the butterfly to jump into the air and take wing. It slowly flies around the room a few times before phasing into the wall and disappearing.

"I didn't want to bother anyone. I didn't want to hurt anyone. I just wanted to play with my butterflies. That was all. A simple dream. A simpler time."

Hope's expression remains unchanged, appearing just as filled with agony as ever. Still, with nothing better to do, the Wordsmith spits out a couple of words through gritted teeth.

"_Kuh_. What changed?"

Gressil's eyes momentarily become vacant. He glances to the side, at his second head, which continues to lay dormant.

"_She_ tormented me. She didn't like my butterflies. She told me to grow up. To act like a proper demon. Every time little sister Ose pulled off some stunt, _she_ would poke and prod at me. Mock me. Insult me. I was never good enough. I was never strong enough, brave enough, or adventurous enough. _She_ wouldn't stop. Every day... she tormented me."

Gressil snorts air through his nose.

"And then. One day. I snapped. I surprised her. I tore her to pieces. I ate her flesh. She screamed at me. Told me to stop. Told me to be a good boy. But I didn't stop. I kept hurting her... hurting her like she hurt me."

The prison-master glances at Hope, only to see a familiar look in the Wordsmith's eyes.

"Hah hah. Don't you dare pity me. I suffered to become strong. Now I understand the cruelty of the world. I understand its inherent... _chaos_."

Even as his prisoner remains silent, Gressil looks away.

"I am not an exception, but the rule. All demons suffer. The leaders at the top have ruled us for thousands of years. We fall privy to their whims. They enforce limits on us. They try to curtail our innate desires."

The warden's eyes glow as he balls his palm into a fist. 

"They try to _control_ us. But that's the thing about chaos, Wordsmith. It cannot be controlled forever. Eventually, the systems break down. Eventually, a fluctuation appears. It shatters the limits, allowing the oppressed to break free."

"Heh. Hehe... cough... and that... that's you? The 'fluctuation'?" Hope finally asks. "You're... you're fucking... delusional."

Gressil doesn't take offense to Hope's insult. Instead, he calmly lowers his fist.

"Am I? Why do you think I released Jason? He is my incarnation. I need not prod him to action. Everywhere he goes, he spreads chaos. As for you? You are merely an experiment. If you fail me, I will move on to something else. I am not like the other Emperors. I do not care for power. I do not care for women. Money. I only want to break the shackles imposed by those at the top. I wish to free all beings from the systems foisted upon them by their births."

"Systems breed stability..." Hope coughs. "Without it... without it...! We devolve... into... barbarity!"

"Humans are inherently chaotic," Gressil replies. "It is only when a few elites group together to manipulate the masses that social orders come into play. The same is true of all sentients. Demons. Titans. Angels. There is no difference. What you call 'barbarity,' I call 'the natural order.' All I want is to restore that which has been lost to false machinations and silver-tongues. Nothing more."

Hope doesn't reply. His pain, unending, only becomes more torturous when he speaks.

Seeing his prisoner's lack of interest, Gressil stands up once again. He wanders over to a corner of the cell and gazes at the wall, transfixing his eyes upon an illusory mirror.

"Heh heh. Things have changed. Not only now and in the future... but in the past, as well. The only way chaos can thrive is if it affects all aspects of reality."

Gressil replays one particular moment of the Stormbringer war, the moment Beelzebub launched out of the soil and began to agitate the nuclear energy in his body.

"First, Ose took something which belonged to me. Then, Beelzebub joined in on the fun. Tsk tsk. These two hatchlings play with the machinations of chaos, yet they've no idea how it works. Their rudimentary imitations of my powers are, if nothing else, insulting. When Ose returns, I'll have to teach her a lesson. Heh heh..."

After laughing to himself for a moment, Gressil waves away his mirror and turns to leave the cell.

"I'll return in a bit, Wordsmith. For now, a cute little birdy requires my attention."

After the prison master leaves, Hope's eyes begin to droop.

"Good... fucking... riddance."
