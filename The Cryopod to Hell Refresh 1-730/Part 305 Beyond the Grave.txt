Part 305: Beyond the Grave

_Elsewhere in the cosmos, on the planet Tarus II._

A midday sun hangs atop the horizon, warming the planet to a comfortable spring-time temperature. Humans, monsters, and a few scattered demons work together to rebuild the ruins of the former Hero City, now named the Fortress of Retribution. While some haul away broken rubble, others start mapping out construction grids for new buildings, ones which the Hero will create through his enhanced Wordsmithing capabilities, once he has the time to spare.

Jason Hiro sits on a bench with his wife. His mind-wife's body projects spiritually before the two of them through his Wordsmithing. Not only does his mind-wife stand before them, but three other people as well.

Gabriel, the Archangel of Power, towers over everyone present. Standing almost ten feet tall, his spiritual self somehow seems more imposing than his physical body ever was. He crosses his arms and gazes into the distance, taking in the sights of a world he can walk around in for the first time in eons.

Shana, the little girl Jason rescued from a necklace artifact inside Marie's underground bunker. The little blonde-haired girl gazes at Phoebe's physical body, momentarily confused by the existence of both her spiritual and physical selves.

And finally, Sir Lorent, Phoebe's nephew. Mordred's Butcher hooks his arm around Spirit Phoebe's shoulder, while bowing his head politely toward her physical self.

"...And that is where his story ends." Sir Lorent finishes explaining. "After your disappearance, King Arthur wept profusely. He died during a terrible battle against the demon armies, killing several of their Emperors in one last glorious strike. I was born shortly after his death."

Despite having already heard the story, spiritual Phoebe still listens intently to Sir Lorents' depictions of past events, while her physical self appears absolutely enthralled.

"To think my king would fall in such a dramatic way..." Phoebe says, her tone wistful. "I wish I had been there for him."

"King Arthur never once begrudged you for your choice." Lorent Berthold says. "Indeed, after your disappearance, it is said that he beat his chest in grief, wishing he had listened and set the innocent demons free. He created many reforms aimed at identifying demons who bore no ill will toward human society, but with his reputation, he never succeeded in enticing any of them to live within his border walls. Compared to the demons I see inside this city's walls now, Arthur lived and died without ever affecting any particular change between the species. I am sure my king's father would be proud to see the ground you have broken with our enemies."

Phoebe sighs. "It wasn't me, it was Jason. I will always try to give demons the benefit of the doubt, but I have to be pragmatic."

Jason nods along to his wife's words, though his eyes carry a hard edge. "I tried with all my might to spread olive branches to the demons. Then they repaid me by murdering my daughter. Perhaps Arthur had the right idea after all."

Sir Lorent shrugs. "Whether demons are good or bad, I do not care. If they treat me well, so too will I show them mercy. And if not, my wrath will call forth blood to wash the land. Your anger is perfectly understandable, given the circumstances."

Phoebe smiles at the little girl hugging her spirit self's leg. "Shana! You're so cute! I can't believe you got stuck in that artifact for all those years. You remind me of..."

She pauses for a moment, then clears her throat.

"Well. That... I shouldn't say things like that. But look at you, such a cutie! I bet Samantha will love you to heck and back!"

Shana turns her head away and blushes shyly. "Two mommies. Very strange."

Mind-wife Phoebe shrugs helplessly. "I sort of adopted Shana, since we're both spirits. She's just confused that you're me and I'm you. She'll warm up to you, in time."

Jason summons a spiritual bench for his mind-wife and the rest to sit down, that way they don't have to stand awkwardly.

Once everyone plunks down, Shana sits in spiritual Phoebe's lap and kicks her legs slowly. "Mama. Why people working?"

"They have to rebuild the city," Spiritual Phoebe explains. "There was a big explosion and it broke a lot of houses."

"Oh."

Physical Phoebe leans her head against Jason's shoulder. "Do you have a grand plan formulated, yet?"

"Not quite, but close," Jason answers. "First, I'm going to build up and fortify this world to become untouchable. The demons won't dare poke their heads in here once I'm done. We won't be relying on a few piecemeal embattlements, but wartime fortifications to ensure our survival. After that, I'll make the Core utterly impenetrable as well. Finally, we'll start training assault teams with guerilla and hit and run tactics. We need to bleed the demons bit by bit, since they so drastically outnumber us. Meanwhile, I'd like to fortify Maiura and some other worlds, giving us fallback locations, just in case."

Gabriel's eyes glimmer with surprise. "Thou seemeth to have thought this matter through, Hero."

"What can I say?" Jason asks, rhetorically. "I won't screw up again, not like I did before. Hope had the right idea. He was out there hitting the demons where it hurt, rescuing human slaves from their clutches. If I could Wordsmith all the slaves out of their demon oppressor's hands, I'd do so in a heartbeat."

Lorent cocks his head. "Then why don't you? This matter should be well within your capabilities."

"Wordsmithing isn't omnipotent. It has a lot of flaws. Now that I better understand the nature of souls, I also understand my limitations," Jason explains. "Much like how I can't target someone with my Wordsmithing without locating their soul first via 'Locate,' I can't just target an area of people if they aren't directly in my line of sight, or if I haven't already figured out their exact position beforehand. I could locate humans individually, one by one, and teleport them back to the Core, but that would take forever. Better to build up an unstoppable army which can do so in my stead."

"These new measures are quite sensible." Gabriel says. "Based on talks I have had with thy wife, I believe automating thy powers might prove effective. Unlike Camael's artifact crafting abilities, thou cans't use thy Wordsmithing in exceptionally creative ways by creating constructs that perform automated tasks in thy stead."

Spiritual Phoebe blinks her eyes slowly. "Jason has already been thinking about making a machine that converts energy of all types into energy compatible with demons. We'll be able to promote the few demons living on Tarus II to Lords, Barons, Dukes, and maybe even Emperors, assuming his plan works out. Making other types of machines isn't a stretch... but it'll take me some time to work out the schematics."

Physical Phoebe smiles. "I'll help you with that."

The group continues chatting for a while, discussing all sorts of things.

At one point, Physical Phoebe widens her eyes in surprise. "Really? Ose was _that_ little girl? The one I rescued from Camelot's dungeon, all those years ago?"

Her spiritual self nods. "Yes. I honestly couldn't believe it at the time, but it's true. For her to have lived all these years with hatred for humanity... I feel as if I didn't do enough to help her when she was a child. She lost her brother in that dungeon, along with other friends and family. Camelot is where her innocence went to die, and where her hatred of humanity stems from, most directly."

Physical Phoebe sighs deeply. "It's too bad, but she's dead, now. I suppose it's better this way. There's no way I could help her, now."

"No. She isn't dead." Jason states. "Mephisto took her body, and her soul. She will return as one of his necromantic creations. Whether he gives her any autonomy or not will depend on how the Duke of Mist intends to use her. However, based on everything we've observed-"

Jason pauses mid-sentence. For a moment, his eyes glaze over. He turns his head to the west, in the general direction of the warpgate.

"...That's strange."

"What is it?" Physical Phoebe asks.

"The two sentries I placed outside of the Core yesterday have sent me a telepathic transmission. Apparently, a soldier wearing an exosuit has just arrived from outside the Core."

"What? Is it Hope?" Phoebe asks, her voice rising an octave. "Or maybe Neil?!"

"I... don't think so." Jason replies. "They're wearing a Spectre suit. Hope and Neil like to wear more... combat oriented exosuits. Uh, give me a minute, Phoebe. Me and the spirits will go and check this out."

"Hey! Don't leave me here," His wife pouts. "You can't just take my soul along for all the fun while I stay behind."

"Oh, fair enough. Let's both go, then." Jason says. "**Return.**"

Jason's four spiritual companions disappear and return to his mind, while only he and his physical wife teleport directly to the Core, leaving behind the Fortress of Retribution in their wake. On arrival, they find Jason's two lookalikes standing before a soldier in a nondescript Spectre exosuit.

The newcomer flinches when Jason and Phoebe arrive, but otherwise doesn't react.

"...Where is Neil?" The soldier asks, their voice somewhat robotic and lacking any unique vocal identifiers. Neither Jason nor Phoebe can tell anything about them, including their gender.

The Wordsmith raises an eyebrow. "Commander Neil is currently presumed dead, though I have my suspicions as to whether he survived or not. Who are you? Why are you using a voice-encrypter? State your name and rank."

"I can't do that." The soldier says. "I need to speak to Neil. Or... failing that, his assistant, Debra."

"I'm the commander of humanity's forces." Jason says. "State your name and rank, and take off that armor. If you refuse... you do know I can forcibly strip it off with my Wordsmithing, don't you?"

Phoebe takes a step forward. "You're wearing a Mk II. Spectre exosuit. Who gave that to you? I only handed them out after the Core fell to the demons, so there's no way someone could have slipped through the warpgate and evaded the demons."

The soldier's head slightly turns to face Phoebe, but still, they don't share any reaction.

"I... I cannot say. Please do not make me reveal my identity. I need to speak to Debra."

"What you _need_ to do is start talking. _To me._" Jason says, his tone growing noticeably colder. "I'll give you one last chance to tell me who you are. Don't make me take action myself."

The soldier becomes noticeably flustered. "You... you don't understand. I can't reveal my identity. I-I shouldn't. It's not... well, I just _can't._ Okay?"

Jason's eyes sweep over the soldier a second time, taking in every detail about their exosuit.

_Slim build. Could be a young man, maybe a woman. Why would they be so insistent on not telling me who they are? Maybe Neil was working on some secret experiment? Something he didn't want me to see?_

The Wordsmith shakes his head. "I don't trust people who try to keep secrets from me. You leave me no choice. **Disengage.**"

The Trooper doesn't even get a chance to protest. Before they can vocalize a rebuttal, their T-REX rapidly disintegrates, sucking itself back into its chest containment module.

The Wordsmith and his wife stand for several seconds, completely stunned by the figure in front of them. Jason's pupils expand and contract, as he involuntarily takes a step back.

"That's... that's impossible. You... you died. Amelia? No, how could it be you?!"

Phoebe covers her mouth with her palm. Her chest heaves as several completely wild and fantastical thoughts run through her head.

_Hoarhiim destroyed the world Amelia was on. Jason confirmed her death with Locate. Was she necromantically revived by Mephisto? But why does she not have any of the markers of necromancy?! And why is she here, at the Core, wearing one of my exosuits? Mephisto couldn't have obtained one of those... could he?_

Amelia Greyheart, the Black Queen, stands before Phoebe and Jason with a look of guilt on her face. She averts her eyes as the two people she wanted to see least look at her with expressions of shock and horror.

"It's... it's not what you think." Amelia mutters. "I'm not... no, that doesn't matter, right now. Listen to me, please!"

Amelia's eyes brighten as she takes a step toward Jason.

"Hope is in trouble! Gressil captured him, and he's holding him prisoner! He's torturing Hope right now, even as we speak!"

Jason, still unable to fully come to terms with the woman before him, hesitantly reaches out a hand to touch her face, before quickly pulling it back.

"You died! Don't just- don't just brush this aside! How are you here?! What the hell happened, Amelia?"

"I want to know, too." Phoebe says, her voice turning noticeably colder. "Lest we forget, you tried to kill Jason... twice. Why should we trust anything you have to say?"

Amelia bites her lip. "It's not what you think. I... Hope... Hope brought me back to life. He resurrected me from the dead. I've been living with him for the past six years."

Her words cause Phoebe and Jasaon both to experience another shock. 

"That's unbelievable!" Jason shouts. "Where? Maiura? Of course, I never went to visit Hope there. It makes sense he'd do something like- no, but hold on! I have personally confirmed that Wordsmithing can't resurrect the dead! You're lying to me!"

Amelia shrugs helplessly. "Am I? The proof stands before you, Jason. I'm right here."

The Wordsmith's eyes become unfocused. He looks away from Amelia, unable to maintain his gaze on her while so many confusing thoughts rebound inside his head.

_Amelia died. She died. I know she did! I didn't even want to believe it, but I saw the proof myself! I tried several Words of Power. I looked into my memories and saw what Hoarhiim did to her. I saw her last moments of pain as Joan of Arc pinned her to the ground..._

A faint pressure builds up in Jason's chest. For the first time in ages, a gash rips across his heart, making him feel a sensation which is somewhat alien, and yet comforting as well.

_I thought she was dead. I thought she couldn't come back. How did Hope do it? Does that mean... there's some way I could bring Daisy back, too?! Is there a way I can revive others who have fallen?!_

While Jason's thoughts race, the denizens in his Mind Realm sit silently and listen. In his moment of panic, Jason accidentally projects his thoughts to all of them.

His mind-wife lowers her head and sighs.

_Beneath all of these thoughts, it seems... Jason still cares deeply for Amelia. He hasn't forgotten about her, even after all this time. Maybe that's why Hope brought her back. He did lose me to Jason, after all._

In the real world, Physical Phoebe doesn't miss the changes in her husband's expression. She momentarily falls silent before turning to Amelia.

"You say Hope was captured by Gressil. That is ugly news, indeed, but how do you know this? And more importantly, I want to know why we should trust you. What's to stop you from going on another planet-slaughtering spree? Frankly, I'm sickened by your presence... especially after remembering the atrocities you've committed."

Amelia lowers her head.

"I... I'm not the same as in the past. I don't have magic anymore. It's all gone, and so is... Blaarjiim. Hope brought me back, but he either couldn't, or wouldn't, bring back Blaarjiim."

Amelia bobs her head slowly, as if she's having trouble believing her own words.

"It's better this way."

Jason, after assembling his thoughts, groggily returns his attention back to Amelia.

"So, Gressil has captured Hope, you say? And we have to... rescue him?"

Amelia's eyes immediately brighten. "Yes. Please! I don't know if you and Hope have any issues between each other, but you cannot let Gressil take over Hope's body. He's going to use the power of Wordsmithing if he succeeds!"

Jason nods slowly. "Yes. I... you're right. I have to save Hope. It won't be good to let him fall into the claws of- but _how_ did he resurrect you? You have to tell me at least that much, Amelia."

The Wordsmith's mind pivots back to his original thoughts, making him once again see a faint light at the end of a tunnel: A chance at resurrecting his daughter.

Amelia's reply doesn't give him much inspiration. "I don't know the exact details. You'll have to ask Hope after we rescue him."

"No. You're lying!" Phoebe abruptly shouts, catching Amelia and Jason both by surprise. "Don't lie to us, _Black Queen._ You know how Hope revived you, so spit it out! Tell the truth, for once in your godforsaken life!"

Amelia winces under Phoebe's barrage, but doesn't relent. "I don't know, okay? I don't know! He just... did. He never told me how he did it. Listen, I've given you what you need, okay? Every second you delay is another second Gressil draws closer to stealing Hope's power. You cannot allow that to happen! I NEED you to save him!!"

The sheer emotions in her response make Phoebe widen her eyes.

"Wait... this isn't just about protecting Hope from Gressil. You... lived with him for six years. Were you and him-"

"We were intimate!" Amelia shouts, as tears well up in her eyes. "There! Is that what you wanted to hear?! While Jason got to ride off into the sunset with you, what did Hope get? NOTHING! Jason just cloned him and threw him away, like trash! Neither of you gave one god-damn about him! When I met him, he was broken-hearted, feeling nothing but anger and emptiness inside! Don't preach at me when you don't know the least bit about empathy, _Phoebe!_"

Amelia bites off the last word, making the Wordsmith's wife wince. For a brief moment, a powerful sense of shame wells up not only in her heart, but Jason's, too.

Several seconds pass. Phoebe doesn't reply, and neither does Jason.

The two of them reflect on Amelia's words.

Jason remembers the original reason he made Hope; to assist Neil in the Core and take care of the duties he himself didn't want to bother with.

Phoebe recalls the moment she had to pick between Jason and Hope, shortly after Jason cloned himself. She even remembers seeing a faint look of betrayal in Hope's eyes, though she brushed it off as unimportant at the time.

After a period of contemplation, Jason breaks the silence.

"I... you're right. I treated him terribly. He was just a tool to me. Maybe that's why he hates me so much. No... it's not a 'maybe.' That's definitely the reason. I was so stuck up, arrogant, and full of myself."

Jason sighs.

"While Hope worked on taking out the demons and rescuing humans, I bunkered up on Tarus II and lived life care-free. I just did whatever I wanted, taking only the minimum steps to prepare for a possible demon invasion. Because of my lack of discipline, my spoiled attitude... Daisy had to pay the price."

Amelia's eyes flicker. "Daisy? Who's that?"

The Wordsmith purses his lips.

"My daughter. She was my... my daughter. And now she's dead."

Realization swells within Amelia's thoughts.

"That's why you want to learn how Hope brought me back. So you can revive your daughter."

Jason nods.

"Yes."

"Then... I don't think..." Amelia starts to say, before correcting herself. "...I pray that Hope will be able to give you the answers you need."

"Me too." Jason mutters.

The three of them fall silent for half a minute.

Phoebe eventually speaks. "Well, time isn't on our side. Let me gather the others. We'll need to assemble a team to rescue Hope. I want Samantha in on this, too."

Jason nods absentmindedly. "Yeah. Let's move quickly."

"And Debra?" Amelia asks. "Do you know where she is? Is she still...?"

"She's alive," Phoebe says. "Why do you need to speak to her?"

"Oh, um, it's not her specifically," Amelia says, as she brushes a strand of long black hair behind her ears. "It's more that she's a proxy for Neil. Neil and Hope were close, you know."

Phoebe gazes at Amelia with suspicion for a moment.

_Another lie. What is she hiding?_

But she doesn't vocalize her thoughts.

"I see. Well, right now she's back inside the Fortress of Retribution, inside her private office. We'll drop you off there, then pick you back up when we're ready to head out and grab Hope."

Amelia cocks her head. "The Fortress of _Retribution_? That sounds ominous."

Phoebe chuckles wryly.

"Yes. Well, a lot has changed in the last few days, Amelia. A lot has changed..."
