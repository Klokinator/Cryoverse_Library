Part 436: Fate of the Galaxy

Hope Hiro levitates just a few feet away from the high-ranking Volgrim covered in writhing, wriggling human skin. Her calm words violently clash with her hideous appearance. This does not prove easy for him to ignore. As such, he slightly narrows his eyes.

"You speak of offering me an olive branch. That's a line taken from a human parable. Are you an expert in human history?"

[Far from it.] Vulpanix says, her voice quietly transmitting into Hope's mind. [I spent a few dozen years casually browsing human historical texts. When you live as long as me, you will have plenty of time to learn little details about these sorts of things.]

"You think I will live to be 100,000 years old?" Hope asks.

[I would be surprised if your Wordsmithing cannot grant you immortality. It is already within Founder Unarin's expectations.]

"Mmm."

Hope lowers his arms to his side. He quietly stares at Vulpanix for a short while. She meets his gaze unflinchingly.

"Before I agree to your proposal," Hope says, "I have an important question."

[Ask it.] Vulpanix says.

"How can we ensure that we will keep to our agreement?" Hope asks. "Suppose you hand me Excalibur, the most powerful artifact Archangel Camael ever created. Suppose I then struck you down, kept the hostages, and all of the artifacts. What would your reprisal be?"

He pauses for half a breath.

"And furthermore, suppose the opposite were true. Suppose I agreed to work with you, but you plotted behind my back. Suppose you initiated some secret measure to quietly execute all of humanity in an instant, along with Jason, and even myself. It could take a while. Years. Decades. But a single instant in the scale of Volgrim life. How could we each ensure the other remains honest?"

Vulpanix slowly blinks her eyes. She nods.

[I have given this matter some thought. So, too, has Founder Unarin. The way my people see it, there are two options, two reasons, two methods we might use to restrain one another.]

[The first method is simple.] Vulpanix continues. [We rely on benefits. This is a simple but effective system. Do you think your human artifacts are all the Volgrim can offer you? Suppose we were to continue exchanging insights. Our species is much more advanced than yours. Humanity will, no doubt, catch up to the Volgrim eventually. Especially with a Wordsmith or two at the helm.]

[But,] She adds, [would it not be beneficial to accept our hospitality? To trade with us the things we want for those your people want? Let us not mince words. Your spiritual engineering alone is incredibly valuable. We can also likewise greatly speed up your knowledge of those heroic artifacts you've... _acquired_. Would you rather fumble around learning their properties, their origins, and the abilities of their resident souls? Or would you rather receive that information quickly and efficiently?]

"Those are good points." Hope says. "But not necessarily good enough."

He crosses his arms.

"Time is a limiting factor. But it's not much of a limiting factor for a Wordsmith. We have plenty of methods to speed up our perception and flow of time. Perhaps I could learn all about these artifacts quite quickly, without your help. Perhaps humanity will advance far faster than you can possibly imagine. Forget Excalibur for a moment. I have once again acquired Solomon's Crown. Is your knowledge all that important to me when the Knowledge-Seeker himself is at my disposal?"

Vulpanix slightly tilts her chin up. [I presume your main body is already in contact with Solomon?]

"I am. And he's helping me ask... important questions."

[I expected as much.] Vulpanix says. [To answer your query, perhaps you are correct. Perhaps you do not need the Volgrim's help at all. Maybe we have already passed the point where we are capable of stopping humanity. Maybe now, the best we can wish for is to cause a great deal of damage before you wipe us out.]

She shrugs.

[But is that truly the way you think? I have seen the way Jason Hiro acts. He does not like violence. He does not like killing. In a fit of rage, he slew a million demons. This action caused him a great deal of mental trauma. Would you rather engage in a violent war where billions, and even _trillions_ will die? Is coexistence not more preferable as an alternative?]

"Coexistence would be nice, yes." Hope admits. "War is brutal. Once begun, it is difficult to end. I've already lost my good friend Kar in this war against the demons."

He pauses.

"But don't compare me with Jason. I am not him. We are separate individuals."

[Separate, yes. But you both descended from the same soul.] Vulpanix points out. [You are bound to share more traits than you may care to admit.]

Hope doesn't offer a retort. He merely blinks.

"What about the second option you mentioned? If relying on benefits is not guaranteed to work, then..."

[The second option is more metaphysical in nature.] Vulpanix says. [And also unreliable. We could simply resort to a magical or psionic contract. The Psions do have one Executor capable of creating such a thing. You could place a magically binding contract on my soul. If I take actions to harm humanity, I will suffer a True Death. At the same time, we could place a psionic marker on your soul. It would do the same to you if you broke the terms.]

Hope's left eye twitches. "Unreliable indeed. Personally, I'd call it functionally useless."

He shakes his head, then closes his eyes and tilts his head downward.

"Suppose you put a psionic marker on my soul. Do you believe I couldn't undo it with a Word of Power? And if I couldn't, how would I know what that marker actually does? What if it holds 'malicious code' in its intent? What if I go to take a shit and that triggers a kill command?"

"Furthermore," He adds, raising his head once more, "My restrictions on you would be even dumber. You said I could limit YOU with a Word of Power? What use would that be? One Psion, out of thousands of other High Psions? Unarin could just order a different Psion to kill me. I'd need to mark the entire upper echelon of the Volgrim Empire. What is that, like, a gazillion people? And none of this mentions the fact Jason would not be bound by our terms, so even if we were both restricted, Jason wouldn't be. You and him already have your own little agreement."

[Indeed. It is unreliable. Useless, even.] Vulpanix affirms. [But it is the only other option. Given its forceful and coercive nature, Founder Unarin would rather not bother with it anyway. Look at how he leads. Founder Unarin is relatively easy to kill, all things considered. His body is far stronger than average, his mind much sharper, most certainly, but compared to killing a High Psion, it would be trivial to kill him and take his place. Even so, no Executor has done so. Why do you suppose that is?]

"Before I answer, don't you think putting the idea of killing Unarin in my head is a morbidly stupid idea?" Hope asks sarcastically.

[The moment you do, your civilization will crumble.] Vulpanix states factually. [That is not a threat. It is merely an observation. It will take time to replace Unarin. Valuable time. Worlds will fall to the Plague far too quickly for us to bounce back or plug the gaps. Before long, our whole defensive front will collapse. If you think my people are a threat, you haven't the faintest clue of the horrors out there in the Void. Killing Unarin would result in humanity's complete extinction.]

"Right. Well, to answer your previous question," Hope says, "I suppose the reason no Psions have killed Unarin is because Dosena protects him."

Vulpanix snorts. [You are playing the fool. Why does Dosena not simply kill Unarin, then? Surely, she could rule the Empire well enough. She is more powerful. She is even swifter of mind than him thanks to her Brain Enhancing superiority. And yet she does not...]

Hope frowns.

Unlike Jason, who has had this conversation with Unarin personally, Hope has not had any chances to interact with the First Founder. He hasn't much of an idea of his disposition, personality, or way of doing things. And as little as he knows about Unarin, he knows even less about Dosena...

"Enlighten me."

Vulpanix cracks her neck. [The Second Founder could crush Unarin mercilessly. She is infinitely mightier than him. Even if she has no aspirations for power, one of the Executors could just as competently lead or make a strong case to her to make her 'step aside' while they do the deed. But in the end, nobody does. Even with all the flaws our Empire has, none question Unarin's place as our rightful leader.]

[This is not merely because of how long Unarin has reigned,] Vulpanix continues, [though countless generations of Volgrim have been formed from the dust of the cosmos, never knowing any history that does not include Unarin's face at the top of our hierarchy.]

A hint of pride appears on her face.

[It is because our First Founder has earned our undying respect. He is kind. He is compassionate. He is cruel. He is ruthless. He is just. He is wise. Founder Unarin is not like the fragile rulers you imagine when you think of historical human kings and queens. He has united five warring subspecies under his banner without resorting to violence, because he understands the souls of others innately. You may have your Wordsmithing... but Founder Unarin...]

Her tone turns wistful.

[...he has his own 'power.' His ability to understand other life-forms, from the superior Volgrim to the inferior mud-dwellers, borders on the fantastical. If you follow him, or even if you collaborate with him, he will not cause you harm. He has already united five Volgrim subspecies. What harm is there in adding humanity to his umbrella, or the demons, or the monsters?]

She lowers Excalibur to her side and extends her free hand in a diplomatic gesture.

[Founder Unarin already has shown his sincerity. He will not pursue you for what you have taken. He merely wishes to deepen his friendship with you. Why not work with him? Would you not rather walk toward a peaceful future? One ruled by respect for all?]

Finally, she concludes her speech.

[If Jason Hiro were in your position, he would understand the benefits we are offering. Surely, you are not so obstinate as to stumble where he would walk?]

Hope's frown deepens.

"You know, Vulpanix... if Unarin is as incredible of a people-reader as you claim, it really should be him here negotiating with me. Because you just can't seem to understand one important fact."

He slightly leans his body forward.

"I am not Jason. I am Hope. And every time you compare us, it pisses me off. Every time you call humanity 'mud-dwellers,' it pisses me off even more. Frankly, if your smug, condescending attitude is what I have to look forward to, then there will never be any possibility for us to collaborate moving forward."

Vulpanix blinks. [I have offended you. My apologies. I forget that... other beings... can sometimes allow their emotions to cloud their judgment.]

"Fucking garbage-ass apology." Hope snaps. "Christ's sake. You even manage to insult me when you're trying to act polite."

He leans back and sneers at Vulpanix.

"I reject your terms. I will return to _my_ previous offer. If you give me Excalibur, I will return the hostages I've captured and leave this world untouched. That is my final concession. We will part on ambivalent terms. If in the future I decide to trade the use of my abilities for something you have, that will be my _courteous gesture_ of respect for your 'mighty' Empire. But you will have to accept my offer right here and now. There will be no second chances."

Vulpanix's eyes narrow. She visibly struggles not to react violently to Hope's words, but it's clear his sudden whiplash has caused her a mental shock.

[You fool! If I refuse to give you Excalibur, you will have to kill those hostages and make the Volgrim your eternal enemies! And I will not give up this artifact without some concessions! Why would you turn down such an excellent offer?! You are letting your primitive greed cloud your judgment!]

"Heh heh heh..." Hope chuckles emotionlessly. "So you reject my generous offer?"

[Generous?!] Vulpanix barks. [You gain everything, and I gain nothing! How is that generous?!]

"That's not true at all." Hope retorts. "I will gain all of humanity's stolen artifacts. They will be where they belong, with me, a Hero. You will get your people back, safe and sound. Serris will remain in pristine condition. You see? I am only taking back that which you stole from my people. By not retaliating for your millennia of theft and oppression of humanity, I am being far more merciful than you deserve."

He pauses to eye her body with disgust.

"To say nothing of that hideous mass of flesh writhing on your body. If Unarin were here, he'd have known that bargaining while wearing that thing was an imbecilic move right from the start. Hell, I bet even an 'inferior mud-dweller' could figure that out during a drunken stupor. Maybe your brain enhancements have actually dulled your mental acuity..."

Vulpanix does not explode in rage. Instead, she becomes eerily calm.

[I will not give you Excalibur.]

"You sure?" Hope asks. "Last chance. I'm being _really_ nice here. Don't you want to save the hostages?"

Vulpanix hesitates. Her face twitches. She seems to struggle with something, then finally starts to levitate away from Hope.

[The Executors will arrive soon. Do as you wish. If you know what is good for you, you will return those hostages now. Otherwise, we will hunt you down unti-AAAAAAAAA!!]

Vulpanix suddenly screams in pain. The human flesh on her body begins shifting and writhing, as if suffering an unbearable torment.

Hope remains unmoved. He merely watches calmly with his arms crossed while Vulpanix shrieks like a banshee, confused and frightened by the agony wracking her every cell.

"You stupid bitch." Hope mutters. "I hate smug little shits like you. Always acting so superior to everyone else. I gave you so many chances. Chances you didn't deserve. But you just _had_ to get greedy."

Vulpanix continues to scream, she loses some of her levitation power and begins to float downward, losing altitude as her psionic abilities go berserk and become nearly uncontrollable.

[STOP-AAAAA! MAKE IT STOP!!]

"I don't think I will." Hope responds. "You never had a chance. I was toying with you from the very beginning. Haven't you realized? Excalibur only follows your will because you wear the skin of humans. But what if I can bypass all that mana shrouding you? What if I can specifically target the flesh enveloping your body?"

Slowly, deliberately, Hope holds out his hand.

_Foop!_

A shepherd's staff with a jewel inside its central spiral appears in his grasp. At the sight of this ancient relic, Vulpanix's eyes widen in horror.

[THE DOMINION RO-AAAAA!!!]

"The Dominion Rod." Hope affirms. "It possesses the ability to command humans. That human flesh covering you... well. You can see for yourself how useful this artifact turned out to be. I'll admit though, it wasn't me who thought up this little trick. Solomon helped me."

He shrugs.

"Ah, but what can I say? Your greed was your undoing. For 100,000 years, you've beaten and bullied humanity, and now you want to make a deal once you've run out of steam where you can reap more benefits. By not demanding a massive amount of backpayment, reparations, I've been _so_ very generous, but in your blind arrogance you just can't see that, can you?"

Suddenly, Hope's body flickers. He vanishes from the spot and reappears before Vulpanix with the fake Excalibur raised above her head.

Vulpanis's pupils shrink to pinpricks. She can only watch helplessly as, in the span of a single instant, Hope swings the sword toward the wrist of her sword-wielding hand...

_SLASH!_

Hope severs Excalibur from Vulpanix's grasp. An instant later, the blade teleports into his palm, and he casually tosses aside the fake Excalibur.

With Excalibur no longer flooding Vulpanix's body, she regains access to her psionic abilities! But too bad. A sudden overwhelming force latches onto all of Vulpanix's clones, along with herself, freezing them in place, slowing down their thoughts, and making it impossible for them to escape.

Hope calmly evaluates the divine blade, the weapon that will allow his future ascension to godhood. He gazes at his reflection in its pristine surface, quietly marveling at how such a vicious battle left no blood on its edge, nor any chipped edges.

He returns his attention to the frozen and terrified Vulpanix. She struggles mightily to break free, but like a tiny fairy held in the grasp of a giant, she can't exert the slightest pressure.

"You Volgrim really aren't much after all." Hope says blandly. "Just a civilization of bullies who lucked out on the temporal lottery. You think just because you had a few million years of extra evolution time, that makes you superior to humanity? Think again."

"Humanity will match you. We will surpass you. And our rise begins now. But too bad, you missed the boat. All you had to do was politely give me my rightful possession and I'd have let you off the hook. At least for a little while. Oh, there would be a reckoning. Reparations, at the least. But now?"

He laughs.

"Now there's no _hope_ for you. You've shown me that you cannot be trusted. Your extinction is guaranteed. Go ahead, if you want. Lash out at humanity. Blow up Tarus II. But know that if you do, your extinction will come a thousand times sooner than if you simply tucked your tails and ran. I'll even generously give you a headstart."

[You foolish human!] Vulpanix barks, no longer bothering to hold back now that she's utterly lost to Hope. [You don't know what you're doing! If we leave-]

"The Plague, right. I don't fear it." Hope interrupts. He curls his hand into a claw-shape and casually glances at his nails. "Oh, it's terrifying, for sure. But it's not infallible. I'll simply create a place where humanity can build up our strength in secret. A folded space, like Heaven, or the Cosmic Realm. Ah, that's right, I forgot to mention... how silly of me!"

Hope laughs.

"I already made it! Yes, somewhere in this universe, there now exists a place only I know about. That's where I'm keeping your hostages."

[You mustn't kill them!] Vulpanix exclaims, her voice containing a tinge of panic. [Please! Psymin Miralax, she is a precious asset! Let me at least negotiate for her release!]

"Oh. Hmm, yeah." Hope mutters. "I suppose I don't have to kill the hostages. I do already have Excalibur, after all..."

Vulpanix's heart skips a beat. [You mean...?]

"Yes. I think I'll let them live." Hope says, smiling wickedly. He leans toward Vulpanix, his mouth mere inches from her ear, and quietly whispers.

"...Say. What do you think would _happen_ if I put Solomon's Crown on a Celestial Designer's head?"

Vulpanix's blood turns to ice. She freezes up, a look of horror washing across her face.

Hope pulls back, his evil smile widening further.

"You've finally realized? I bet Unarin already thought of the possibility, but you're nowhere as quick-witted as him. Yeah, all those offers you made of trading technology sure ring hollow now, don't they? I wonder what kinds of crazy bullshit I can cook up now that I have the knowledge of dozens of black-level Technopath researchers? In fact...!"

Hope makes a dramatic flourishing motion with his hand. To Vulpanix's absolute terror, a golden crown appears in his grasp.

"I'm such an idiot!" Hope laughs. "It just occurred to me! Why don't I take all the knowledge of a top-level Psion? I've found my knowledge of your sub-species to be quite lacking..."

[NOOO!] Vulpanix shrieks, writhing harder than ever. All six of her clones suddenly burst apart, collapsing as her psionic energy goes berserk. [THE MIND THIEF! NEVER! NEVERR!!!]

The air in the facility suddenly 'thickens' dramatically as a massive amount of Psionic power begins swirling around Vulpanic's body. Holding nothing back, she summons every last drop of her full power while shrieking like a terrified jackrabbit.

[NEVER! NEVER! NEVEEE-]

Abruptly, Vulpanix stops screaming. Her body freezes up, then slumps slightly.

The light goes out in her eyes.

A grim silence descends on the room...

Hope gazes at her figure.

"**Evaluate.**"

He hits her with a Word of Power, and his expression sinks. He quickly places the crown on Vulpanix's head, waits a few seconds, then pulls it off. He teleports it back to his main body, then his expression sinks even further.

...

Inside a secret pocket dimension somewhere in the Milky Way's confines, Hope stares at the ground with an ugly expression while Solomon belly-laughs inside his Mind Realm.

"Not one word from you." Hope growls.

"Bahaha! Was that your inner Jason acting up?" Solomon laughs. "Hahaha! STUPID! You should have put the crown on her head before you started gloating! You're still green, boy!"

Hope's expression sags further. He glances around at the incapacitated prisoners laying in piles here and there, then deeply sighs.

"Oh well. There will be other Psions. We've made incalculable gains already. Losing Vulpanix was only a drop in the bucket. Why didn't you tell me she could self-destruct her soul like that?"

Solomon stops laughing. He wipes away his tears, then positions himself uprightly, returning to a serious demeanor.

"Heh... well, I wasn't aware they could do that. I've never actually seen a Psion lose in combat, let alone be driven to a point where self-destructing was their only remaining option. Good knowledge for the future. But don't get complacent, Hope. Those Executors are still coming for you. Even with Excalibur, such a battle will be..."

"No need to remind me." Hope remarks. "I've already made my preparations. I anchored what remains of Heaven around that planet we discussed, and all the rest is taken care of. Now only one thing remains."

"**Return.**"

Hope utters a Word of Power and vanishes, reappearing in the void above Serris. His clone and Excalibur appear not far away. He examines them and nods.

"You did good work, Arthur. I'll be giving you a new soul soon. One that better matches your disposition."

The construct says nothing. It merely gazes at Hope, waiting for his orders with vacant eyes.

Hope teleports the construct into his secret dimension, then gazes at his motionless 'clone' floating nearby.

However, this 'clone' is not actually a clone at all. Instead, it is merely a corpse, a fake body made to perfectly replicate Hope's likeness.

Indeed, Hope never fought Vulpanix. Instead, he remotely 'piloted' this second body like a drone, using it to assault Vulpanix while keeping his actual self safe from harm.

But now, the stiff, corpse-like body lingers in front of him, its dead eyes even more vacant than Arthur's...

"I'd like not to have to ever do that again." Hope mutters. "But it's a trick that works well. It felt like I couldn't properly use Excalibur through this 'drone' though. Why is that?"

"Is it not obvious?" Solomon asks. "It is not your real body. You only piloted the 'drone clone' with a fraction of your soul's true essence. Therefore, you can only harness Excalibur's true power when using it personally."

"So if I ever intend to fight Jason..." Hope mutters.

"You'll be giving him Excalibur for free if you send a drone into battle." Solomon warns. "If the 'Cube' is as powerful as it seems, then Jason must not be underestimated. He may yet prove your match even when you possess Excalibur."

"I have over a thousand artifacts now." Hope laughs. "I'm sure I can overpower him. It's just a matter of finding the right ones... though Psymin's memories will surely help to speed that process up."

Hope casts a casual glance at Serris. The barren moon looks positively hideous, except for the light of the distant star faintly illuminating its distant horizon.

For a few moments, Hope stares at the planet as if wrestling with himself over what choice to make. Eventually, he shrugs.

Then, he raises Excalibur overhead and begins to call upon its power.

"Goodbye, Serris." Hope says wistfully. "With this, I declare myself the eternal enemy of the Volgrim."

"All of you can burn in hell."

_Then, he swings the divine blade down._
