Part 349: Hope Awakens

Annette sits beside Hope's bed, quietly watching over the Wordsmith while he sleeps. The hours stretch into days, and visitors come and go, but aside from eating food delivered to her by various medical staff and occasionally saying hello to her sister when Debra drops by, she remains content to sit beside the man she loves.

Eventually, a week after his return, the Wordsmith finally awakens from his coma.

Hope's eyes flutter open. He remains motionless, dazed and confused by his surroundings. Jason's clone stares at the dim ceiling-mounted lights, then slowly moves his eyes around as he tries to gather his wits.

"..."

Hope swallows heavily, but fails to make a sound, due to his dry, parched throat. An intense thirst bubbles up in his brain, making him feel like a wild animal that has choked on desert dirt for months.

He lolls his head to the right, then to the left, where his heart skips a beat. He finds 'Amelia' sitting beside his bed, reading a book. When she notices Hope's head moving ever so slightly, Amelia nearly jumps out of her skin. She drops the book, stands up, and turns to face him.

"Hope! Hope... you're awake! How are you feeling?"

The Wordsmith blinks a few times. He opens his mouth and licks his dried lips.

"..."

"Oh! You must be thirsty. Here, one second..."

Amelia grabs a plastic water bottle from a set of ten beside her chair. She props Hope's head up, and helps him greedily gulp and choke the water down until it sufficiently wets his palate.

"Cough... cough... where am... I?"

Amelia lays Hope's head back against his pillow, then pulls up her chair so she can sit facing his bed.

"Well. A lot happened, Hope. A lot. Um. We rescued you... from... from Gressil's prison..."

The mere mention of Gressil's name makes terror explode in the Wordsmith's eyes. A jolt of electric pain fires from his heart, making him jerk abruptly in bed and whip his eyes around.

"No.. no.. no... not him! Not..."

Hope raises his arms to swat at the air, but his movements appear clunky and uncoordinated. He abruptly slaps his own face, startling himself and making his adrenaline skyrocket even further.

Gently, Amelia presses on the Wordsmith's arms and holds them in place.

"Calm down, Hope. Nobody's going to hurt you. I'm here, now. Amelia's here. Remember me? I love you. I love you so much... listen to my voice..."

After several minutes, Amelia manages to somewhat calm Hope down. Sweat drips off his face as he tries to comprehend his circumstances.

"Arms... my arms... gone? Not gone? What- where did they go? Why they are back againnn...?"

He slurs his words, suffering from disorientation. But even so, Amelia quietly listens to him and explains what happens.

As more time passes, she manages to fully explain the situation to Hope. He calms down more and more as he realizes his current situation is not, in fact, a trick designed by Gressil to lower his defenses. Even so, the phantom memory of horrible pain wracks his mind, making him mentally flash-back to those horrible moments, such as when Gressil took a sharp knife, cut into his shoulder...

"Ahh! It hurts! It hurts!" Hope screams, grabbing his left shoulder. He touches it all over, checking to make sure his arm is still attached. "He cut it! He cut it off! Why did he do that? WHY?!"

"Jason grew it back." Amelia whispers. "He... he fixed you up, Hope. You're fine now. Everyone's here for you."

"I'mnotfine!" Hope mumbles loudly, slurring his words again. He blinks heavily as dizziness wracks his mind. "Itallhurts... ribs... throat... legs... ahh! AHH! My legs! My LEGS!"

"We grew those back, too..." Amelia says, fighting back tears. "You're fine now, Hope. You're okay. You're alright. Nobody's going to hurt you again."

For the next half-hour, Hope rants and raves, still not sure if he's experiencing another one of his delusions. Compared to the physical torture inflicted upon him by Gressil, the prison-warden's mental means proved far, far more long-lasting and sinister.

When he calms down again, Amelia returns to explaining what happened. She conveys not only how Hope was rescued, but all of the events he missed. Hope discovers, to his horror, that while Gressil was torturing him, humanity lost millions of people during the Stormbringer Attack. Jason's daughter died. Kar died. Neil was taken prisoner. Beelzebub self-detonated, killing a million people in one massive thermonuclear attack. 

Then he learns about what transpired after. Not only the attack on Hell Harbor, but the arrival of the Archangels, the transformation of the Cherubiim, and its battle against the Volgrim.

"The Volgrim... they're involved, now?" Hope asks.

"Yes. I heard from Jason yesterday. He got a visit from someone named 'Kordonis.' Also, the Demon Emperors are planning a second big attack, but Jason seems confident he can stop them."

Hope grits his teeth. "Stop them?! Jason is worthless! He couldn't save anybody! Not even Kar! Kar was my BEST FRIEND!"

Amelia winces.

"Jason... is different now. More like you. Losing Daisy... it changed something in him. You'll understand, soon. Oh, and you should probably know..."

She hesitates.

"Everyone knows my real identity now. They know I'm not 'Amelia.' They know I'm really Annette."

Hope blinks twice.

"Annette... wait, you remember, too? But... but I thought the memory-blockers..."

Annette smiles at Hope.

"It's not your fault. Gressil's anti-magic energy... It erased your mental blocks. But don't worry. I'm fine the way things are, now. I'm Amelia too, you know? And if you want me to be Amelia again, you can just block my memories once more."

The Wordsmith sags against his pillow.

"So that's why you've been calling me 'Hope.' We agreed that you would always call me Jason, but I overlooked... I'm sorry. I failed you, and everyone else. And... and Gressil... he..."

"Let's not talk about that monster." Amelia says, her smile tinged with pain. "He won't hurt you ever again. Look. Jason made you a... friend?"

Amelia points toward the far corner of the room, parallel to to Hope's headboard. When he turns his head to his right and follow her finger, he nearly jumps out of his skin! In the dimly lit room, a man in shining armor stands at attention, his brilliant blonde hair, light blue eyes, and fair skin giving him a heroic aura unlike any other.

Hope nearly chokes on his own spit. "K-King Arthur?!"

"A construct. Not a reanimated body." Amelia clarifies. "Jason made him from... well, I don't know, exactly. He's not a human. Think of him like a robot or something. He doesn't have a soul. He's just something Jason made to protect you."

The surprise of seeing a guy creepily standing in the corner gives way to a different set of indescribable emotions. Hope examines King Arthur closely, noticing that he doesn't wear any specific facial expression, but instead gazes at the opposite corner blankly.

"A construct. Right. That's... okay. And why is he here?"

"To protect you." Amelia explains. "I don't know how strong this fake Arthur is, but Jason claims he can fight Dukes and even Emperors."

She pauses.

"I fought Arthur, you know. Well, not me, but Amelia. Since I possess her memories, I remember the battle. She ultimately lost to him, but after he died, she dug up his body and enslaved it with her draconic magic. That's how she was able to bring him out to fight the Archdemon on Polaris."

Hope licks his dried lips. "More water, please."

After Annette passes Hope another bottle of water, he spends a minute chugging it down, finishing off every last drop. When he finishes, Hope casually drops the bottle off the side of his bed. He lifts his right hand to stare at his regrown fingers, while innumerable dark thoughts cloud his mind.

"I have to tell everyone you've awoken." Annette says. "They asked me to alert them the instant you woke up, but, well... I wanted to spend some time with you alone, first."

"Thank you." Hope says, his voice a whisper. He turns to Annette to smile tenderly at her. "I... I don't think I could have faced the others so soon. I'd rather spend time with you... not Jason."

"I know." She replies, reaching out to grasp his hand. "I'm just glad you're okay. You're still alive. We can work through what happened together."

He nods.

"Amel- Annette. I... I don't want us to have to lie to each other, anymore. So, after this, why don't we just change you back to your original self? I love you, not Amelia. I'm sorry you had to go along with my selfish desires, six years ago. It was thoughtless of me to force you into that position."

"You didn't force me." Annette answers. "I offered. I wanted you to be happy. Besides... I'm just an ordinary woman. It's expected that a plain Jane like me wouldn't catch your interest. The Black Queen is so much more... fun. She's so much cooler than I ever was. I don't want to go back to being an ordinary woman again."

"That's not true." Hope counters. "Amelia was never the person I thought she was. When it was just Jason, before I existed, she cut open his throat and left him an invalid. Compared to her, you're so much more compassionate. I love _you_, Annette. Not Amelia."

"But... but you're wrong!" Annette protests. "I may be Annette, but I'm Amelia too! I have all of her memories. I'm not purely one or the other. And I wouldn't want you to take her memories from me, either."

Listening to the force behind her words, Hope creases his brow, but ultimately relents.

"...Alright. But I won't lock away your memories again. And I'll call you Annette, instead. Is that fine?"

"Yes. I'd like that." Annette says, with a smile.

The two of them spend a few hours catching up, reminiscing on their lives spent together.

Eventually, Annette pulls away.

"It's time. I need to tell the others you've woken up."

Hope sighs.

"Okay. Just do it. Let's get this over with."

"Hey..." Annette whispers, gently touching his arm. "Give Jason a chance. I know you hate him more than life itself... but he _has_ changed, and for the better. He lost his little girl. He lost his best friend. I feel when I look at him that his eyes have become... sharper. Like yours."

The Wordsmith snorts. "I'll believe it when I see it."

Annette stands up, walks across the room, and touches a button on the wall, pre-installed for her convenience. She walks back over and sits down. Fewer than five minutes pass before the door to the hut opens and Jason walks in, flanked by Phoebe, Belial, and Blinker.

"Hope! Hope, you're awake!" Phoebe exclaims, beaming a sunshine-smile at him. Her smile deflates after she remembers his condition. "H-how are you feeling? Are you doing okay?"

It takes Hope a few moments to respond.

"Yes. I... I think I am. Annette explained everything to me. I know you all rescued me from... from the prison."

Annette smiles guiltily. "I told Hope about what happened to spare you the trouble. I also caught him up on all the current events. He knows about the Volgrim and stuff."

"How long has he been awake?" Jason asks, shooting a mildly annoyed look at Annette.

"Few hours." Hope answers. "Don't blame her. I asked her not to tell anyone until I got my bearings."

Jason waves off Hope's worries. "It's fine. I'm just glad you woke up... pal."

"I'm not your pal." Hope mutters. "Don't give me that nonsense."

As Jason's group steps into the room, another figure follows in their wake, one that draws Hope's attention due to her pale, translucent body. The Wordsmith flicks his gaze away from Jason, then weakly rubs his eyes.

"Huh? Another Phoebe? Why is she a ghost?"

"Oh, hi. I'm Fiona." Fiona says, waving at Hope. "Um. Well, I'm a clone of Phoebe, sort of. A spirit-clone. Jason made me after he lost Solomon's Crown. I help him out with stuff."

"Solomon's Crown..." Hope murmurs. He doesn't say anything else, but in his heart, another bolt of shock fires through his veins.

_Where did the Crown go? I'm not wearing it!_

Not wanting to alert Jason to his knowledge of the Crown, Hope keeps silent. Instead, he frowns at Jason again, this time, with a look of visible disgust.

"Hold on. You _cloned_ Phoebe?"

"I had to." Jason says. "Well, I guess it wasn't _absolutely_ necessary, but I needed a way to protect my mind from Ose and others like her. With Solomon gone-"

Hope sits up straighter in his bed. "That's beside the point! Six years ago, you cloned me but wouldn't give me a clone of Phoebe because you said it would 'feel weird,' yet when the time came, you made a clone for your own purposes! What kind of goddamned hypocrite are you?!"

Fiona and Phoebe exchange wordless glances.

"To be fair..." Phoebe says, directing her words to Jason. "You didn't even ask me before you cloned me. I'm still annoyed about that, if only a little."

"Cloning without consent." Hope snarls. "_Typical Jason._"

The original Wordsmith makes no attempt to defend himself. He bows his head guiltily and sighs.

"Yeah. I fucked up a lot of things. Countless friends died because of me. I regret many of my past choices now... but regret won't bring the dead back."

Seeing Jason's contrite expression, Hope's anger fades. He sags back against the bed and shakes his head.

"Whatever. At least you know you did wrong."

Blinker flies over to Hope and lands on his shoulder.

"Hey, big guy. I brought you some cake!"

"Hope glances at the tiny fairy. "Cake?"

"Mhm! It's right here in my spatial storage!"

Blinker motions with her hands to make two plates, each with a slice of carrot cake, plop down on Hope's outstretched legs.

"It's a... get-well-soon cake." She says.

Hope smiles weakly.

"Blinker. I'm sorry about Kar."

"Me too." She says. "But don't worry. Kar's still alive. Mephisto has his soul... but we might be able to get him back, someday."

"Really?" Hope asks.

"Yeah." She says with a nod. "And... even if we can't... well, I don't know if you heard, but I'm pregnant. I'll carry on the crocodile lineage myself."

"Congratulations." Hope says, his smile filling with warmth. "If you'd like, I can be your children's god-uncle."

"That would be swell!" Blinker chirps.

Hope passes Annette a slice of cake. The two of them quietly begin to eat, with Hope especially enjoying the sensation of food entering his starved stomach.

"So." Hope says to Jason in between bites. "What's all this about a Volgrim recording? Annette mentioned it in passing, but didn't give me too many details."

Jason gestures to Phoebe, standing beside Belial. "Show him."

Phoebe pulls out a small drone from her pocket, one pre-loaded with the recording. The drone flies into the center of the room, then projects an image of the Founders hovering on their platforms inside a huge arena. For the first time ever, Hope gets a good look at the aliens ruling the galaxy. He continues silently eating, while Jason, Phoebe, and Belial plop down in seats Jason Wordsmithed along the edges of the room.

As Hope watches, he pays careful attention not only to the statements and truths uttered by the speech participants, but the hidden bits they only allude to.

"These... Kolvaxians." Hope murmurs. "What are they?"

Unlike Jason, who picked up bits of information through Marie and others, Hope hasn't learned much about the Volgrim or their enemy. Jason periodically pauses the video to explain to Hope all the bits he's missed out on. Belial fills him in on others, such as information regarding the Changelings.

When Dosena opens her hand to reveal a certain Heroic Artifact, Hope nearly chokes on his last bite of cake.

"Puhak! What?! Solomon's Crown?!"

Jason nods seriously to Hope's confusion, not noticing the other minute details of his clone's expression.

"Yeah. I mentioned before that I lost Solomon's Crown, right? Well, it turns out the Volgrim are the ones who stole it from me. I don't know how, why, or when they pulled it off, but I'm just as shocked as you are. We cannot let it remain in their hands."

While Jason intently gazes at the holographic image of Dosena hovering the Crown a few inches above her palm, Hope quickly suppresses his panic.

_This... how did this happen? Wasn't I wearing the Crown when Gressil... but then, how did it end up in the Volgrim's grasp?! Jason thinks the Volgrim took the Crown from him, but he doesn't know Solomon used his own Wordsmithing to transfer the Crown to me!_

A cold chill descends on Hope's bones.

_Gressil... he must have somehow taken the Crown from me and given it to the Volgrim. But why would he do that? Or was it a coincidence? My memory is so spotty... I don't remember anything before... before he..._

When Hope mentally flashes back to the time he spent trapped in Gressil's cell, a panic attack overcomes him. His joints lock up, and his teeth chatter, while he stares vacantly at his knees. The plate in his trembling hand shakes visibly, while he tries to fight off flashes of that hideous, bird-like face hovering over his own.

_He cut me. He cut me. He cut me. I don't like it. I don't want it. Stop, please! Stop, it hurts! It hurts!!_

"Hope!" Annette calls out, startling the Wordsmith and making him drop his plate. "Calm down. What's wrong? Do you want some water? Are you okay?"

Hope's eyes flicker around the room as he returns to reality. His panic subsides, but that cold fear settles back into his bones, making him feel as if someone evil is watching him.

"No... no... I don't want- I don't need anything. I just- the memories... I can't stop thinking... AHHH!"

The Wordsmith grabs his head and starts to rock back and forth as a second, even more visceral panic attack hits him. This time, the images of Gressil's face stab into his eyes like pointed barbs, making him blink furiously while tears drip from his sides.

"STOP HURTING ME! STOP IT! **I DON'T WANT IT!**" Hope screams, startling everyone in the room. Jason and Phoebe both jump up, but Belial is the first to reach his side.

Before Hope can fall any further into his deluded state, Belial gently grabs his forehead and injects magical energy into his mind.

A wave of exhaustion overtakes Jason's clone. Hope's body convulses a few times before he slumps backward and Belial gently guides him into a laid-down position.

When the room goes quiet again, Belial keeps her hand placed on Hope's head, a look of sadness on her face.

"His trauma... he was trying to keep it suppressed." She mutters. "But that isn't as easy to do as it might seem. He won't be the same as he was before. We'll have to be a lot gentler with him."

She removes her hand and lifts her gaze to look at a moderately panicked Annette.

"Hope... is he going to be okay?" Annette asks.

"I don't know." Belial whispers. "I'm not a mental health expert. This is all I can do. What he needs more than anything is a therapist."

Jason steps toward the foot of the bed.

"I could erase his memories." Jason says. "I could make him forget about Gressil."

"No!" Annette says, standing up. "You mustn't! That wouldn't work, and it would only mess him up worse!"

"How so?" Jason asks.

"Hope blocked out _my_ memory." Annette reminds Jason. "But Gressil undid those blocks! If you block out Hope's memory, you'll only leave him vulnerable if Gressil shows up again! You can't do that!"

Jason winces. "Right. I forgot about that. Well... I don't know what to do then, either. I feel as if I can't leave Hope like he is, now."

Phoebe loops her arm around Jason's elbow. "PTSD isn't something a person can get over easily, remember? We have to let him cope in his own way. Maybe we can find a decent therapist among the people here?"

"Right. What about... Makoto Ueda?" Jason asks. "He helped me out in the past. Has anyone seen him recently?"

The Wordsmith glances around the room, but not one person gives him an answer.

"Makoto... he was that Japanese fellow, right?" Belial asks. "Hung out with Neil a lot?"

"Yeah. Him." Jason says. "Have you seen him? I know he survived Stormbringer. I talked to him not long after."

Belial shrugs. "Haven't heard from him in at least a week. But then again, I'm pretty busy at the hospital."

"I haven't seen him, either." Phoebe mutters. "Jason, why don't you try locating him?"

The Wordsmith nods. "**Locate.**"

A few seconds later, he frowns.

"**Locate.**"

Twice, he tries to find Makoto Ueda, but both times, he fails.

"What the hell?" Jason mutters. "Is he... dead?! I didn't hear anything about this."

"Neither did I." Phoebe says. "I'll ask Hans. He always knows where Makoto is. The two of them are quite close."

Jason chews his lower lip. "Alright. In the meantime, Sam, why don't you stay here with Hope for a while? I need to finish the last bit of work on the Lazarus Tower. Let me know if he wakes up."

Belial nods. "I can do that. See you later, Jason, Phoebe, Fiona."

"I'll stay here too." Blinker says. "Bye, guys!"

"Bye."

Jason and his wives leave the other three women behind with Hope. After they depart, Blinker crinkles her nose.

"Oh. Hope spilled cake crumbs all over the place. Welp, waste not, want not!"

She uses her telekinesis to grab up all the crumbs and form them into a single super-crumb, then she brings that crumb to her mouth to munch on it.

"Mmm. Not bad."

Annette sighs. "Blinker... you're weird."

"Yup."
