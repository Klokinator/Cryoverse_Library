Part 192: Mad Madam Mildred

Sir Lorent pats Shana's head affectionately. Despite his gruesome, bloodstained chest, Shana doesn't look at him with any fear, given his relation to her surrogate mother, Phoebe.

[Yes,] Lorent says, answering one of Phoebe's most recent questions. [His Majesty died in battle. However, he did not perish to a demon's blade, but the discharge of his overwhelming mana. In a final attempt to eradicate Satan the Devil, King Arthur fired a blast of holy magic far stronger than anything he had unleashed in the past. The resulting energy tore his body apart, killing him instantly.]

Phoebe rubs her eyes as she tries to suppress her tears. "His Majesty died a most magnificent death. As expected of the Hero King, he would give his very soul if it meant he could protect his people."

Lorent nods sagely. He closes his eyes and exhales deeply. 

[Haah... if only I had known His Majesty, I might have lived a more fulfilling life. Instead, I worked as a butcher for his son, the corrupt King Mordred. Some Hero I was. He used my powers to cow his political opponents into submission. Had they known what my power entailed, or had the reputation of a Hero not preceded me... Mordred might not have made it past his eighteenth birthday.]

Phoebe dabs at her eyes. "What is your power, my nephew?"

Lorent chuckles. [Haha... it's quite underwhelming. I hardly dare name it a 'power' at all.]

Lorent motions with his hands. Suddenly, a paintbrush appears between his fingers. With another motion, an easel materializes before him.

[My power allows me to summon objects from artwork.]

He begins sweeping the brush across the easel. As he does, Shana leans forward and stares at it with wide eyes.

[What this?] Shana asks. [You touch paper. Sword appear?]

Lorent continues drawing, revealing a crude painting of a broadsword. After finishing its basic shape, he touches his finger to the painting. A copy of the sword leaps off the paper and transforms into a razor-sharp weapon. It clatters to the ground with a metallic _twang_, and Lorent immediately leans over to pick it up.

[That's right. Have you never seen an artist's work, young one?]

Shana shakes her head. [No...]

[I see. If you'd like, I can teach you how to draw.]

Shana's eyes light up with excitement. [Yes! Me want artist!]

Lorent turns his attention to Phoebe. [As you can imagine, this worthless power only lets me summon weapons for others to wield. I can craft bowls, spoons, and other such things, but Mordred used my ability to create weapons en-masse.]

Phoebe stares at Lorent in disbelief. "But... but your ability is incredible! Can't you see how amazing it is?"

Lorent frowns. [I do not understand. Summoning weapons to butcher others is hardly a power worth praising.]

This time, I send my thoughts into the Mind Realm.

_Phoebe is right. Your power might be second only to Wordsmithing. You can draw any object, and it will become a physical item. My power is technically more robust, but yours is only slightly inferior. The sword you drew lacked in detail, yet upon entering the physical realm, it instantly became an object of incredible craftsmanship._

Phoebe jumps in, adding to my point. "Jason's Wordsmithing allows him to do almost anything, provided he knows enough about the subject or his imagination can fill in the gaps. When you summoned that sword, weren't you picturing the weapon in your mind?"

Lorent nods. [I was. Unfortunately, my artistic merits are poor, and my creativity is lacking. A simple weapon proves no barrier to my mind, but whenever I try to create anything more complicated than a blade or a tool, I grow weary and fall asleep. It has always been this way.]

I nod. _Don't underestimate yourself. With some effort, you could be an incredible asset for humanity's future._

Lorent shakes his head. [I am but an errant soul, trapped within an artifact. My future is empty. All I wish is to spend time with my long-lost aunt. She is the only family I have left in the world.]

My expression darkens. 

Lorent's words send a pang of distress through the back of my mind. After all, I promised Marie that I wouldn't take any other artifacts from her. However, I can't leave Lorent behind. Doing so would break Phoebe's heart. 

I raise my eyes to peer across the distance. Marie chats with Momo quietly, but the moment I move my head, she pulls away and strides over to me.

Ignoring pleasantries and other basic social norms, Marie immediately jumps into questioning me. "Well? What have you found?"

I sigh. "The hero inside this axe is named Sir Lorent. He was an executioner who served under King Mordred, son of King Arthur."

"Mordred!" Marie gasps, a look of surprise on her face. "Incredible! Barring the discovery of King Arthur's divine artifact, this Sir Lorent fellow is the next best outcome! I'll be able to excavate all manner of historical knowledge from him. What else did you find? What are his powers?"

My heart begins beating erratically. Already, I can see things turning out horribly for me.

I didn't expect Marie to get so excited after hearing the barest information regarding Lorent's life. If I tell her the truth about Lorent's incredible power, she won't allow me to keep his artifact. I'll have to separate him from Phoebe, forcefully.

Fuck!

What can I do? I'll have to lie.

"His heroic ability is fairly underwhelming," I say, keeping my words measured. "He's... a painter. He can use his heroic aura to paint stuff."

The half-truth keeps my conscience from overworking itself. After all, my words aren't a lie, but I _am_ making sure not to give the cyborg too much information.

Marie frowns. "Painting, you say? Interesting. Based on the historical records I've obtained from that time, Sir Lorent strongly resembles the image of Mordred's Butcher. That man was known for slaughtering anyone toward whom he aimed his axe. He spared nobody; not the men, women, or even the children."

Marie smiles. "You've done well, Jason. Now, I want you to use your Wordsmithing. You need to create a projector, something capable of interfacing with Heroic artifacts. If I can't interrogate the heroes inside these artifacts, I won't be able to obtain the information I need."

I cock my head slightly. "Interrogate? These are human beings, Marie. They're people who often influenced human history for the better. We interrogate _criminals_, not _Heroes_, for Christ's sake."

Marie rolls her eyes. "I'm not going to use violent means to interrogate them, nor will I use the vast array of psychological torture methods at my disposal. However, I _must_ obtain answers. You spoke of obtaining humanity's freedom, but such a task will be impossible without my help. All you've managed to do over the last six years is paint a target on your planet. Sooner or later, the Volgrim will come for the Tarus star system. When they do, will you feel regret over arguing with me over such a banal matter?"

I scowl in annoyance. "Yeah? What information are you so desperate to find, Marie? Any intelligence these Heroes have is 100,000 years out of date! What could they possibly have to offer you that will help in the fight against the Volgrim?"

Marie stares at me blankly for several moments. "Their heroic powers, for one. All I need is to find one Hero with an appropriately versatile ability, and I'll be able to expand my operations dramatically."

"What sort of power? I'm a Wordsmith. I'm standing right here. Solomon once said I might be the most powerful Hero who ever lived. Why do you need these errant souls if you have me? There's something you're not telling me, Marie."

Marie straightens her posture slightly. "Do not continue pressing me. My reasons are classified."

"Classified? By who? You?"

Marie places her hands on her hips. "That's right."

"So there _is_ something you're hiding from me. What is it? Tell me."

...

Marie stares at me for several long, agonizing seconds. Our gazes meet one another's, and we size each other up. Half a minute of intense staring between a human and a machine ensues. Eventually, Marie relents.

"Fine. You leave me no choice. I'm looking for Excalibur, Jason. Not only Excalibur, but the Dominion Rod and several other legendary artifacts as well."

My heart skips a beat. "Excalibur? You mean King Arthur's heroic weapon?"

"That's right. I do not possess these legendary artifacts. That means they are, potentially, somewhere on Earth, buried where my drone sensors can't detect them."

I raise an eyebrow. "_Potentially_. What do you mean by that? Where else could they be?"

Marie raises her fist and spreads her index and middle fingers apart. "There are two possibilities. The first is that, during the Volgrim's surface-bombardment, they destroyed many heroic artifacts. The second is that those artifacts somehow ended up in space. Naturally, the latter is the possibility I find most worrisome."

Having gotten Marie to tell me her 'true' goal, I nod along and deliberate on her words.

"Do you think the Volgrim might have obtained Excalibur?"

"In my mind, the probability rests above sixty percent, yes."

"What other reason could there be for Excalibur to not be on Earth?"

Marie rubs her temple. "Excalibur is one of the mightiest artifacts Camael ever crafted; potentially the strongest of them all. Chances are if the Volgrim didn't seize it, and if the weapon endured the Volgrim's planet-glassing, it may have been launched into space and hurtled to the farthest reaches of the Void. I don't know. I have swept more than fifty parsecs over the past 100,000 years, yet never come close to locating the divine blade."

Marie narrows her eyes. "Do you understand now, Jason Hiro? Interro- ahem, _conversing_ with these errant souls is essential to locating Camael's mightiest artifacts. If I can triangulate their last-known positional data, I can uncover their resting places."

I lean back in my chair and smile. "What if you find Excalibur, Marie? What are you going to do with it?"

"Excalibur is only one of my targets. Each artifact possesses incredible potential. For example, Excalibur will allow its wielder to unleash planet-destroying blasts of holy magic. What might surprise you is that such destructive potential is quite useless for me, given-"

"Given the neutron bombs you've been cooking," I reply, cutting Marie off before she can finish. "Right. What else can you use it for, then?"

"Excalibur's power is functionally unlimited," Marie replies. "Right now, I am limited by the radiation I can siphon from Earth's core, as well as the energy generated by Sol. With another source of power, I'll be able to scale up my base of operations to new heights. The Volgrim possess a thousand worlds in as many sectors. Their research facilities outnumber mine a million to one. I can't challenge them without Camael's artifacts."

Marie waggles her finger. "Most importantly, _you_ can't help me find it. After all, your 'locate' ability doesn't work on inanimate objects. Isn't that right?"

I frown. "How the hell do you know that?"

"I've gathered plenty of intelligence on you over the last several years. Anything the demons know, I know. Kar is a good boy, too. He works with Hope, so I have obtained plenty of second-hand information from him as well. I hope you won't hate Kar for being mommy's little adviser."

"Ugh. Whatever. Let's get back to the original topic. Supposedly, you need Lorent's help in locating Excalibur. However, I don't think you do. After talking with Lorent, I, uh... well, I found out his full name is Lorent Berthold. He's a nephew to my wife, Phoebe Berthold. I don't mean to impose, but-"

Marie growls. "No! Absolutely not! Dammit, Jason, how many artifacts do you intend to steal from me, today? I need them! Locating Excalibur takes priority over some silly family values or your constant need to protect these errant souls. I let you keep the little girl, as she won't be of much use to me, but Sir Lorent can provide crucial clues in my quest to locate Excalibur!"

Phoebe's voice appears in my mind. "Here it comes. I knew Marie wouldn't give up without a fight. Be careful, Jason."

I continue to stare at Marie as she berates me for my impudence. "Marie, I understand that Lorent is an essential asset to your plans. However, he's a member of Phoebe's family. I won't let you turn her nephew into a guinea pig."

Marie metaphorically spews smoke from her ears. "Fucking hell, Wordsmith! You can't just stroll in here and seize all my artifacts! Do you have any idea the pains I went to, to dig them from the Earth's surface?! I need them!"

"I understand. However, I won't budge on this issue. I can return Sir Lorent's artifact to you at a later date. You've waited 100,000 years. What're another few years in the long run? I only want to give him some peace of mind. Perhaps he'll be more receptive to helping you once he experiences life outside of the crushing loneliness he's endured since his death, eh?"

Marie ceases her rambling. She glowers at me with hate-filled eyes for nearly a minute before whirling around and stomping toward Momo.

"Fine. Fine! Take all of my artifacts; why don't you? This one, that one! Take them all! What do I know about the galaxy compared to you, a man who has done little more beyond stirring up war and strife? You can't comprehend that which lurks at the edge of the Milky Way! You don't even know about the..."

Marie pauses mid-sentence.

I frown. "About the... _what_, Marie?"

Marie keeps her back to me. "Never mind. Let's move on. I have more artifacts for you to inspect. Continue telling me any details you find relevant."

"Oh, so now you're going to drop all of your demands? What happened? Did I ask the wrong question?"

Marie doesn't look at me. "Momo. Open alcove 4C and fetch its occupant."

"Marie-"

"This discussion is over," Marie says, shooting me a death glare. "I won't say anything else, so don't ask. You may keep Sir Lorent's artifact _for now_, but do not press your luck."

Without another word, Marie turns back to Momo and watches in silence as a glass alcove opens to reveal a beat-up white and silver music box. The tiny artifact, small enough to rest in my palm, hovers toward Marie under the stringy tendrils of a drone and plops into her grasp soundlessly.

Marie inspects the music box for a moment before turning around to walk over to me.

"Here. Take it."

The clearly annoyed cyborg plunks the music box into my palm and walks away, not bothering to say anything else.

Fine by me. I'm getting pretty tired of Marie's attitude. She's hiding something. I don't know what it is, nor why she thinks I shouldn't know... but I can hazard a guess. It must involve the Volgrim and her ultimate plan for overthrowing them. Marie must believe that I'm in her way, or else she would tell me this big, important secret.

With a sigh, I turn my attention toward the artifact in my hand. The music box is an old fashioned thing, dating back to well before the 1900's. Its design is somewhat bland, aside from an inked image of a lion head sitting atop its lid, half-rubbed-off.

I can only guess as to who its occupant will be. 

However, before I have a chance to aim my mind at it, Phoebe, Lorent, and Shana all gasp in surprise.

"Ah! So many people?!"

I turn my attention inward to gaze upon my Mind Realm. There, I spot five shadowy figures appearing from the void.

Without any dramatic fanfare, their faces and bodies swiftly materialize, revealing two women and three men.

The first woman, a rather portly-looking Victorian lady in her fifties, laughs gaily. "Ah, hah-hah-hah! Why, look what we have here! The dregs of society have come knocking at my door! Braaaavo!"

Her Portliness crows into her hand, each laugh grating on me more than the previous ones. Her attitude and posture give her the appearance of a smug know-it-all. Immediately, I hate everything about her.

"Who are you?" Phoebe asks, swiveling her gaze at the various people who've emerged inside my mind. "Why are there five people inside your artifact?"

One of the newcomers, a man wearing a turban, opens his mouth to speak. However, before he can say a word, the obese woman leans forward and laughs again, her irritating voice driving me to the edge of madness.

"Hah-hah-hah! Oh, dear, how small your brain must be! Heroic Artifacts can only hold one soul, don't you know? Might those be marbles I hear rolling about in your noggin, or are you too much of a simpleton to recognize _me_, the glorious, beautiful, Mad Madam Mildred? Hah-hah-hah!"

Phoebe winces. "Mad Madam Mildred? I don't believe I've heard-"

"Haaah! Oh, darling, of _cooourse_ you haven't! Those lips are always flapping to and fro, so what little can you say that I don't know?"

Madam Mildred begins strutting around like a peacock. Her gaudy blue dress, as well as the feathery boa wrapped around her neck, only adds to her nauseatingly annoying demeanor as her presence dominates the room.

"I am none other than the most brilliant, genius, intelligent person ever to roam the Earth! Oh, darling, look at how glazed your eyes become! You cannot begin to fathom the depths of my mind, the limits of my knowledge! I stand atop the precipice of Heroes, a legendary figure known only by those with the wit to seek out my name!"

I exhale deeply. "Oh, Jesus fucking Christ."

Madam Mildred presses her palm against her chest. "And who are _you_, but a little girl seeking the power of _my_ precious babies, _hmmmm_? I know what you want, you silly little defiler! You intend to use our power in some violent blood-stained quest for revenge! But daaarling, our talents are not yours to consume!"

[Madam Mildred...] An Arabian man behind Her Portliness says, keeping his voice low. [...We can speak for ourselves.]

The Arabian man wears a turban and monk-robes, along with a scimitar that hangs at his side. His demeanor exudes authority, as he gives Mildred no opportunity to belittle him. He gazes down at Madam Mildred; his eyes tinged with a mixture of pride and deference. I can tell just by looking at him that he treats Mildred with the same respect as two kings of equal status might accord one another.

Mildred swivels on her high-heels to gaze at the dark-skinned, blonde-haired, blue-eyed Arab. "Oh, Kasim! Do not tease me! These mongrels want nothing more than to treat us like slaves. Us! The mightiest, most brilliant Heroes to have ever walked the Earth!"

Mildred guffaws. "Not to mention the fairest and most beautiful! Hah-hah-hah!"

Kasim sighs. [Madam Mildred, that may be so, but unless they display hostile intentions, we have no reason to show them disrespect. After all, this Mind Realm's owner is a Hero as well.]

Mildred pauses. She cocks her head slightly, then raises her eyes skyward. Suddenly, a shudder goes through my body as her gaze somehow meets mine!

"Hah-hah-hah! How silly of me! The Magnificent, Magnanimous, Mad Madam Mildred must be losing her touch! You there, the boy named Jason Hiro! How could I have overlooked you? That stunning gaze! That chiseled body! What say you to fornicating with this blessed temptress for a night, _hmmmm_?"

I swallow a load of bile. _Pass._

Mildred turns away from my gaze and returns to strutting around like a peacock. "Oh, goodness, we've found a shy one! You'll come around eventually, my dear boy! All men do!"

Phoebe stares at Mildred like a hawk examining a field hare. Every bone in her body tenses up at the thought of Mildred 'taking' me for herself. "Jason is my husband. Watch your mouth, Mildred. I don't know who you are, but you've acted incredibly rude since your arrival. Why don't you tone the act down a few notches?"

Immediately, Mildred swivels on her heel to eyeball Phoebe, a look of surprise in her eyes. "You've quite the snake-tongue, you little sass-queen! I love a woman with conviction! Worry not, for there is always room for another in Madam Mildred's bedchambers. Now then, shall we get down to brass tacks? I know why you've brought me here, but you've no idea the demands I'll make in return for my services!"

Mildred yanks out a chair from the table and plops down in it unceremoniously. Immediately, her exaggerated expression deflates, and she eyes Phoebe with the look of a predator preparing to pounce on its prey.

Phoebe crosses her arms. "You claim to know a lot about me, but I don't know much about you. Why don't we start from the beginning? Tell me a little about yourself. Who are these other Heroes? How did you place them in your Heroic artifact?"

Mildred gently pets the feathery boa wrapped around her neck. "Hmph! Kasim, you tell the little lassie. I've no patience for explaining elementary knowledge to a preschooler!"

Kasim nods. He walks over to Mildred's right-hand side and bows politely. [My apologies, Lady Phoebe. Madam Mildred can act rather boisterous at times, but I, and the others behind me, owe her our lives.]

Phoebe raises an eyebrow. "How do you know my name?"

[We know many things, and our knowledge comes from Madam Mildred's power. You guessed earlier that all of us came from Madam Mildred's heroic artifact. However, that is not the case. All of us reside in Marie's artifact chamber, but within different artifacts.]

Kasim gestures behind himself to the other three people.

[This man here is Tucker. His heroic artifact is a weapon he calls a 'revolver.' The woman to his right is Karla. Karla's relic is a hairpin her father gave to her early in her life. And the last gentleman is Julius. His artifact is a dagger — the same one used to murder him.]

My heart skips a beat. _Julius Caesar? The legend himself who brought Rome to new heights?_

The Roman man presses his palm against his heart. [Correct. I am he.]

_It's... it's a pleasure to meet you,_ I say, trying not to sound overwhelmed with awe.

I flick my gaze between the three newly introduced Heroes.

Tucker immediately catches my attention. His wide-brimmed hat, boots with stirrups, and fancy silver belt give him the air of an old western-style cowboy. Coupled with the revolver on his hip, and his dashing good looks, the guy looks like he'd fit perfectly into a John Wayne movie.

Karla, the woman standing to Tucker's right, is a rather plain-looking Asian lady. I can't tell her racial origins apart immediately, but her red and gold dress appears Chinese in origin. A sash wraps around her waist, holding her garb in place. Her golden hair and bright blue eyes give her a look of innocence, but at the same time, I detect a hint of malevolence within her gaze.

Finally, Julius Caesar, the man, myth, and legend himself, looks only a little different from how I pictured. Just like all the other heroes, his blonde hair and blue eyes mix together with his white robes, sandals, and a purple sash spanning from his shoulder to his waist to give him a dominating aura of leadership. Unlike Kasim, who gazes upon Mildred with a tinge of deference, Julius barely regards her with any interest at all.

The cowboy, Tucker, smiles politely. "Now, c'mon, ya'll. I do hope we can git' along real nicely together. Ain't no reason for us to be jumpin' at each other's throats. Ain't that right, Miss Berthold?"

Tucker drawls in a vaguely southern accent as his eyes flick toward Phoebe. He walks past Mildred and the others to plunk down in a seat beside my wife.

"Name's Tucker. Kyle Tucker. I consider mahself fortunate to be in the presence of a pretty lady like you. I do hope we kin' make each other's acquaintance later, if you catch mah drift."

Phoebe barely glances at Tucker. "I'm married."

"Marriage ain't nothin' but a piece o' paper and swearing on God's name, and lord knows, we Heroes're proof that God's a simple, flesh-n-blood being."

[Tucker.]

Kasim glares at the cowboy.

[Control yourself. We're not here for you to proposition our hostess. Consider this a business trip.]

Tucker chuckles. "No worries, Kasim. I'm a man of many talents. I gots them political skills, and I gots them social skills. You could learn a thing or two from me if you opened yer eyes for a minute."

Julius Caesar takes a step forward. [Lady Berthold, please continue with your line of questioning. We all have the same enemy. We all have the same goal.]

Phoebe turns her chair to face Kasim, ignoring the cowboy seated behind her. "Right. Sorry, I got a little lost, what with all the introductions. You were saying something about the others here. What was the point you were making?"

Kasim nods. [As I said, all of our souls rest within different artifacts. Madam Mildred's heroic ability is the power to link minds together and share information. She helped create a Rennaisance during her lifetime and allowed people to evolve in leaps and bounds. Naturally, after her death, she also linked the souls of several artifacts together, allowing us all to come here at once when you touched one of our artifacts.]

Phoebe and I both reach the same conclusion simultaneously. However, my wife is the one to voice her opinion first. "Ah, so you must have linked to Jason's mind. Is that how you knew my name and the reason for our arrival?"

Madam Mildred chuckles into her palm. "Hahaha... indeed! You pick up faster than I expected, for an addle-brained plebian."

Phoebe's eye twitches. "P... plebian? If you're going to resort to insults..."

Madam Mildred waves her hand dismissively. "No, no, my dear! Don't take my cute little nickname to heart! All unenlightened lumps of coal are plebians in my eyes! Even among the diamonds in the rough, the brilliantly shining gems known as 'Heroes,' few can catch the eye of the Magnificent, Magnanimous, Mad Madam Mildred! Too few Heroes rise to the top of their era and reach the pinnacle of their power! Many squander their abilities and live lives of debauchery. Why, just take the young lassie behind me, little Karla!"

Mildred turns to smile at Karla, who continues to stand alongside Caesar behind Mildred's chair. Karla shifts uncomfortably as Mildred's gaze falls upon her. [Y-yes, lady Mildred?]

Mildred ignores Karla's question. "This beautiful peach might not have achieved much, but her power still allowed her to take revenge and throw her entire country into chaos! She executed her father, Emperor Yang Guang, thus ending the mad tyrant's reign! Her face might be plain, and her attitude demure, but Karla here achieved far more than the average so-called Hero!"

Karla lowers her eyes. She suddenly appears a lot smaller as she tries not to meet Mildred's gaze. However, Mildred turns away from her, ignoring the pain in the woman's expression. 

"In any case, all non-Heroes are mere plebians in my eyes! Even amongst those gifted the power of the gods, few can ever reach the heights necessary to deserve my flattery. That includes you, Miss Berthold!"

Phoebe balls her hands into fists. "My last name is _Hiro,_ dammit. All of you, stop calling me Berthold. I left that name behind when I married Jason."

Madam Mildred fans herself with her hand. "Oh, pish. You silly goose. You're not married at all! What would ever make you think such a thing?"

Phoebe's glare darkens. "I beg your pardon? Who are you to tell me whether I'm married or not?"

"Isn't it obvious? The young man whose Mind Realm we currently inhabit, Jason Hiro, married a flesh and blood woman named Phoebe Berthold. That woman... is not you! You are simply a duplicate of her soul. Do you dare deny this fact?"

Phoebe's anger falters. "I... I mean... no? But what does that have to do with anything?"

A look of pity enters Mildred's eyes. "Oh, you poor dear. You've barely lived for a day, so it stands to reason that you haven't yet figured out your place in the universe. When Jason returns home, whose embrace will he fall into? Yours, or the original version of yourself? The one who dwells within, or outside the Mind Realm? I think you know the answer."

Mildred leans back in her seat. She smooths her shirt down and smiles condescendingly at Phoebe, causing my wife to fall into a daze. "I... I don't..."

When Phoebe hesitates, I jump into the conversation. _Mildred, what is your problem? I love my mind-wife and my flesh and blood wife equally. I'm married to both of them, and that's that. Besides, it has nothing to do with anything! Why even bring this crap up, beyond stirring the pot?_

"My dear, all of this is perfectly relevant."

Mildred's expression turns haughty.

"Don't you know, boy? You're a soul manipulator, now, yet you haven't the faintest idea what forces you've begun to agitate. Clones, copies, and tigers, oh my! You cloned yourself, only for the newly created Jason to go rogue. You copied your wife to protect your mind, but you failed to consider the long-term repercussions of that action. What folly will you perform next? Shall you attempt to bring back the dead for some selfish pursuit?"

_No, never,_ I say, scowling at Mildred. _Why even ask such a thing?_

"Because, my cute little pom-pom, your power is extraordinary; certainly mightier than all the Heroes assembled here combined. When a man possesses might equivalent to yours, corruption inevitably follows."

I nod. _You must be referring to that old saying about power corrupting people._

"Precisely. Power corrupts, and absolute power corrupts absolutely. Of course, some people believe that power merely attracts the corruptible, but while that might be true, it is also a much rarer situation."

Mildred's gaze falls upon Phoebe's trembling form. Tears well up in my wife's eyes as her memories keep replaying what Mildred said moments ago. Only now do I realize that Mildred's words hit her harder than I expected.

"Come, now, my pretty little lily. You need not cry. Madam Mildred understands your pain. You and I are not so different, as we have both left our bodies to become errant souls."

Phoebe sniffles. "But... but you're right. I can't... I can't be with Jason. I hadn't yet thought about it... but I'm not _me_ anymore. I'm just a soul. I'm only half a person."

_Phoebe..._ I say, lowering my mental voice. _Don't talk like that. You might be an errant soul or whatever, but you matter to me. You're still my wife, and I love you immensely._

Mildred nods. "Ah, young love. It truly is wonderful to behold. I remember the first five men who embraced my bosom-"

[Madam Mildred,] Kasim says, a hint of annoyance in his voice. [...Must you?]

"Ah, goodness! I cannot help but express myself honestly! Ah hah-hah-hah! Forgive this beautiful enchantress, for I am but a humble, homely old woman in body and soul. Where was I? Oh, yes!"

Mildred's laughter comes to an abrupt halt. Her smile vanishes, and a solemn gaze spreads across her face.

"The galaxy is a dangerous place, Phoebe, Jason. Unlike many of the souls trapped inside their artifacts, I have not fallen helpless within my artifact. My power allowed me to touch the minds of any who wandered within range of my aura. While I am unable to connect with the cybernetic body of Marie, I am more than capable of touching her assistant Satyr's mind. As such, I know well the dangers which threaten humanity. I might seem arrogant and indifferent to your undiscerning eyes, but rest assured! I am no enemy of humanity."

I nod along to Madam Mildred's words. _Alright. Can I assume that you wish to help us, then?_

Mildred nods. "Mmm. Something like that, my dear."

_What do you want in return for your 'help?'_

Mildred smiles. "Oh, not much. A promise is all I need. Right now, the thing I want would prove detrimental to everyone gathered here, today. However, in the future, that is likely to change. If you promise to help me after you've saved humanity, I'll offer you my assistance, as well as the aid from these Heroes present, post-haste!"

_Tell me what it is that you want so that I can decide. If you don't, I'll have to decline._

Mildred pauses. She shoots a strange look at Kasim, before turning to look skyward, meeting my gaze.

"Freedom, dear child. I wish for myself and the other Heroes to depart our presently unbreakable bonds. Promise me that someday, when the time is right, you will grant us our freedom. If you do, then I will assist you to the fullest extent of my power."

I frown. _You want me to grant you freedom? Can I... do that? How?_

Mildred purses her lips. "Promise me."

_But-_

"Is that a 'no?'"

I sigh. _Fine. I don't know how you expect me to free you. However, if I do have that power, I'll do it. Alright? I promise._

"Excellent. Karla, my dear! Did you catch all that?"

The Chinese woman takes a step forward and bows deeply. [Yes, Madam Mildred. I have ensured the contract's signing.]

_Contract? What contract?_ I ask.

Mildred waves her hand flippantly. "Oh, it's a minor matter. A soul-binding agreement to ensure we maintain our ends of the deal. That's all!"

I stare at the corpulent woman in disbelief. _You made me sign a contract without my knowledge?! Who are you? The Devil?!_

"No, no, sweet-pea. If it makes you feel any better, know this: Your Wordsmithing is more than capable of nullifying Karla's contracts. I merely wished for you to sign as a formality. Surely, you wouldn't try to cheat this beautiful seductress out of her one wish?"

_Fucking hell, Mildred. Your every word tests my patience._

"Huzzah! It's settled, then. Since you agree, it's time for me to uphold my end of the bargain! Ah, hah-hah-hah!"

Mildred laughs uproariously. She pulls her self out of her chair and begins strutting around the room once again, smiling creepily at all who meet her gaze.

"Tell me, child! What information would you like to know? Marie seems to be withholding some interesting tidbits from the almighty Wordsmith. Perhaps you'd like to start there?"

I nod.

_Yes, please. Tell me about the Volgrim. I want to pick your brain for anything useful. I need to know what kind of threat they'll pose to humanity in the future._

Mildred slows her pacing. She eventually stops and stares blankly at a wall.

"The Volgrim? Goodness. You've set your sights on the Heavens, dear boy!"

Mildred closes her eyes.

"Very well! Since you asked so politely, mommy dearest will be happy to oblige! Now, focus your mind, and listen well."

I nod. _Alright. Lay it on me._
