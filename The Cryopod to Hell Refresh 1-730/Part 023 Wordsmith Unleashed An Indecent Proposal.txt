Part 023: Wordsmith Unleashed, An Indecent Proposal

"**Summon.**"

A suit of metal armor materializes and wraps around my body. All at once, over a hundred pounds of weight pops into existence, causing me to slump forward. I catch myself before falling on my face and speak another word of power. 

"**Strength.**"

The metal's weight seemingly vanishes as my body's strength increases to heft the added bulk. The only exposed parts of my body are two horizontal slits for my unseeing eyes to pierce through, as well as seven thin vertical openings in front of my mouth. I'm not only protected, but I can also breathe, too.

Metal encases my arms, legs, feet, and hands. My abs, heart, back, neck, and every other part of my body can rest easy knowing that only an incredibly precise attack targeting the tiniest jointed areas could hope to hurt me.

The only problem is how slowly I move. I guess that's the price to pay for impenetrable defense. Based on the tests I've run, even if a rhino slammed its horn into me, full-force, I might get the wind knocked out of me at worst.

In addition to my armor, my fusion backpack appears, automatically attaching to my lightsaber when I yank it into my hand with telekinesis. _It's go time._

My metal suit grinds together as I step through the portal. The sound is annoying, but at the same time, the noise vibrates the air all around me, giving me a continuous spherical view of the area.

The _whumph_ of air pressure change, as always, is disorienting. Immediately, the cries of pain throughout the room, the clanging of claw and blade, and the sound of magi chanting spells transform the room into a fully visible entity, enabling me to see every corner and every individual battle with perfect clarity.

Nobody pays my presence any heed. I can't complain. It gives me time to figure out what's happening. Winged creatures, possibly harpies, fly around in the air, drawing their bows and arrows and firing with deadly precision into the crowd below. At the far end of the Core, bright red spots indicate hot-blooded demons, each one forming hand signs and chanting to cast magical spells. A bolt of black energy sails from the one's hand halfway across the room and strikes a snake-shaped monster in the back, making it hiss in agony and fall over, dead. I don't know what the spell was, but it was moderately powerful. I wouldn't want to get hit by it.

Two monsters jerk their heads toward me, and I recognize the shape of their legs and tails, marking them as kobolds. I've only seen a few, and from what Kar has told me, most are fiercely loyal to demons above other monsters. That makes them likely enemies.

The two kobolds dash at me and raise their swords to strike. My lightsaber blade pops out with a high-pitched wail, causing them to flinch and gaze at it in surprise — the perfect opening.

While they stare in shock at the weapon, I yank the wolf-like monsters toward me with telekinesis and swing the saber up and down in two quick movements, lopping their sword-arms off, then shoving them back with a telekinetic push. _Just like a Jedi, heh._

I don't want to kill if I can help it, but anyone who attacks me will regret their life choices. The kobolds screech in pain and slam into several other monsters at the bottom, alerting them to my presence. While those monsters look up in alarm and their eyes jump to my glowing blade, I decide to take things to the next level.

Under my breath, I mutter, "**Amplify. Amplify. Amplify. Deepen. Shift.**"

My voice tastes menacing, reminding me of an enraged minotaur at the prime of its life. Without even speaking I can tell my vocal cords have shifted adequately. Now, it's time to make my grand entrance.

"**SILENCE! CEASE THE FIGHTING, OR I SHALL ANNIHILATE ALL OF YOU!**"

My voice is so loud and powerful that sound waves smash into the far wall and tear a few chunks off. Nearby monsters go flying, and even I stagger slightly under the recoil from my own voice. Luckily, I use my telekinesis to steady my body.

The fighting in the room careens to a stop as my voice echoes for miles in every direction. Everyone falls to their knees and grabs their ears, howling in pain. In fact, the room goes so silent despite all the screaming that it isn't until I feel blood running down my neck that I realize I blew out my goddamn eardrums. "**Heal.**"

I mutter the word as quietly as possible, but it's hard to tell how far my voice travels. Once my hearing is back, I notice everyone scanning the room, looking for the source of the Voice of God. 

After a few moments, several heads turn to face me. Then others. Finally, the entire room looks at me.

It's at this point that I notice the downside of spherical vision. I can mostly make out each species of monster and demon, but I can't tell who is on which side. Anyone could be an ally or an enemy.

"Hurgh! Get off of me!" Kar's voice rings out, and he leaps to his feet, throwing two blobs of smoke through the air. I can barely even make them out since they have no heat in their bodies, and when they hit the wall, they evaporate into mist and then reappear several feet away.

Faint splotches dot Kar's body. Despite being stabbed several times, and blood rushing from the wounds, he can still move and fight. What incredible stamina he must possess.

His roar of anger shakes several enemies from their stupor. One demon leaps into the air toward me, runs across the ceiling fifty feet above after inverting his gravity, and begins casting a spell. He looks to be the same one that shot the black magic bolt from before. I don't want to get hit by it.

Oh. A simple solution.

Without moving a finger, I reach up with my telekinesis and yank him from the ceiling, smashing him into the ground. The yelp of fright he makes on the way down is both amusing and also sickening. "Eeyak?!"

I raise him into the air and smash him against the ground two more times before letting him go. His body flops like a ragdoll and lands with a sickening _crunch_. 

Naptime.

A strange sensation washes over me. _I enjoyed that. I held the demon's life in my hands and nearly snuffed it out._

_I've never felt this powerful before. It's... a little intoxicating._

Before I can dwell on the morality of my attack, a group of kobolds rushes forward. One of them hurls three knives at me. I don't bother blocking them. The blades smack against my armor and _tink_ to the ground harmlessly. Not one to be deterred by my impenetrable defenses, that same kobold dashes in front of me and delivers a gut-busting punch straight into my stomach...

And breaks every bone in his hand.

For a single moment, a look of shock races across his face before every muscle in his arm pops at once. The poor thing howls in pain, and I lop his arm off with a quick lightsaber swipe. I can always grow it back later if he decides to join my side. If not, the limb was useless anyway.

Shoving him away with my telekinesis, I glance over at the Demon Lords. All of them chant in sync and pool their energy together. "**Analyze.**"

My voice's almost robotic timber booms out, and a series of information floods into my mind as my Wordsmithing breaks down the components of their spell. It's the equivalent of a micro-nuke, but made of dark energy, and if they unleash it, the bomb will probably wipe out every soul in the room. They've realized I'm a significant threat, so this must be their only way to win.

Either way, I can't let them cast that spell. At the moment they fire it, my voice booms again. "**Absorb.**" All the energy from the spell vanishes and floods my body. An odd sensation, like pins and needles, spreads through my limbs. It's more uncomfortable than painful, and it dissipates as quickly as it arrived. It felt as though I had dive-bombed into uncomfortably hot water, then climbed right back out.

Weird.

All of the Demon Lords take a step back in surprise as their ultimate magic vanishes into the breeze. I decide to use my dominant voice one more time, though I tone the volume down substantially. "ALL WHO DO NOT SURRENDER WILL FACE THE SAME FATE AS THE DEMONS WHO DARED TO ATTACK ME."

Saying this, I lift the dozen or so Demon Lords and smash them into the ground before they can react. A few slams later, and they're out cold. "**Normalize.**" I return my voice to normal afterward and smirk.

My proclamation and follow-up attack catch the attention of all the enemies in the room. Every remaining monster and demon peels away and races out the Great Doors, grabbing their Lords hurriedly on the way.

A minute later, a collective sigh of relief washes through the room. "Turtle!" Kar yells out, and when I turn to look at him, the already low temperature in his body has dropped another few degrees. The giant lizard-sphinx stumbles forward and falls flat on his face.

Using my telekinesis, I deactivate my lightsaber and lift my armored form through the air with telekinesis to land next to Kar. He flops onto his back and gasps painfully. "Huh... hurgh... this is... this is it, for me. The shadow-walkers h-hit too many vital areas... I'm a goner."

I fight the overwhelming urge to laugh. "**Heal.**"

Before Kar can continue giving me a deathbed speech, all of his injuries vanish. The lizard-sphinx blinks in surprise and suddenly sits up. "O-oh. You can do that. I forgot."

"Yeah... sorry, I interrupted your dramatic speech."

Kar clears his throat and jumps to his feet. "Ahem, ah, well, this is uncomfortable. I had prepared myself to die. My power is nearly on par with a Demon Duke, but there were too many enemies. You saved my life."

"Hey, no sweat. You saved mine too, remember? Just think of it as me repaying a favor."

Glancing around, I quickly heal every monster in the room that I can see, reducing my internal energy substantially. Some of our allies are already dead, and I consider resurrecting them. _I've never tried it before. Would it work? Now's as good a chance as any to find out._

Kar interrupts my thoughts. "Turtle. Thank you for showing up. You've saved our lives, but this was just the beginning. Those demons were not ordered by the Dukes and Emperors to attack us. They arrived of their own volition."

I turn to look at him questioningly. "What do you mean?"

Kar grimaces. "All demons must obey the orders given by the Demon Dukes. However, in the absence of an order, they are free to do as they please, even killing one another. There are only a few strict rules demons must follow, and none of those cover our situation. The Demon Dukes and Emperors didn't order those Lords to attack, but they didn't order them _not_ to attack. Do you understand?"

Slowly, I nod. "More will likely strike, knowing the first wave weakened us already. And those were just Lords and Monsters. If the Dukes and Emperors decide to attack..."

"We'll lose," Kar replies flatly. "Demon Dukes are not to be trifled with. The lowest-ranked Duke, Mephisto, commands the Labyrinth's undead, and even a few shadow-walkers. He can pass through walls and reap souls from bodies. Little is known of the other Dukes and Emperors since they prefer secrecy, but you won't sleep well knowing they all tower over Mephisto."

I flick my gaze to the Great Doors. "How did they get inside? We shut the doors more than a month ago."

"Hurgh. Several Demon Lords working together have more than enough power to blow the doors off their hinges, let alone merely push them open. Besides that, it appears Bahamut installed demonic codes into the doors. If one knows the password, they can speak a few words, and the doors will automatically open."

"Oh, fantastic. That's _great_ news," I mutter. 

Kar shakes his head. "I disagree, but you're free to your opinion."

From behind me, the sound of people exiting the Tarus II portal echoes inside the chamber. Phoebe, Neil, Hans, Saul, Makoto, and their militia come charging through, swords and bows at the ready, but confusion passes over their faces as they realize the battle is already over.

"Excuse me, Kar. I'll be right back." Instead of levitating to them with my telekinesis, I de-summon my armor and jog over.

"Phoebe! Are you alright?" 

As I slow to a stop, Phoebe looks away. "I'm fine, no thanks to you. Ass."

"What happened?" Neil asks. He continues scanning the room, as several of our monsters pin down enemy monsters I healed up. I'd be amused at the incredulous looks on their faces if it weren't for my focus on Phoebe. Right now, if all the enemy monsters and demons died, I wouldn't even care.

"I came in and put an end to the fighting. Nonlethally."

Makoto rests a hand on the katana at his belt. "Impressive, Jason-san. Did you heal everyone with your magic?"

"Yes. Even the enemies."

"Most honorable. You are a kind and compassionate leader."

Makoto heaps some praise on me, and I force a smile, but honestly, I couldn't care less. Stepping forward, I reach past everyone to touch Phoebe's arm. "Hey, listen. Can we talk?"

She looks like she wants to pull away, but reluctantly sighs. "Is the situation stable here?"

I've never seen her this grumpy before. It's a little unsettling. "For the most part."

Phoebe jerks her head back and forth to look around for a moment, before gesturing to a quiet spot in the distance behind one of the portals. "Over there."

Nodding, I follow behind her as she takes off. 

I'm going to apologize and hope she doesn't hate me. I can't just say what I did was a prank. It was mean-spirited and not like me at all.

I hope she's willing to listen.

---

Phoebe stares at me icily, keeping her arms crossed and body language closed. Her usual warm smile has turned into a blank facade of half-visible anger. The look on her face makes me feel like a backstabber, someone who intentionally hurt her for my amusement.

"Look, Phoebe, about what happened... I didn't mean... that is, I never intended to hurt you. I didn't think about how you'd feel being trapped in a vehicle you'd never seen before, and-"

She silently holds a hand up. "I've thought about what you did to me, Jason. You trapped me in that metal box, tied me down so I couldn't escape, and unleashed a pack of lions on me. Their roars were unmistakable. What were you hoping to accomplish?"

Phoebe's words make me flinch. 

She doesn't know what a 'car' is, let alone its function. All she knows is that I seemingly terrorized her for no reason. I treated her reactions like a joke... and ended up hurting her needlessly.

I'm awful.

"Phoebe, I'm sorry. I screwed up. That's all there is to it. If you hate me, I understand. I want to try and explain what I was thinking, okay? I thought it would be a hilarious prank until I realized how badly I hurt you. I'll never do anything like that ever again."

Phoebe purses her lips. "Never?"

"Never."

Her head turns slightly as she looks past me at the wall. It's only now that I notice her lip quivering. Is she going to cry again? Fuck. Goddamn, I really stepped in the shit this time. I may never live this-

"Hahaha!" Phoebe suddenly laughs, and then the laugh explodes into an uproar as she doubles over and holds her stomach with both hands. "Haahahaha! Oh, my goodness, Your Grace's expression! It's like a puppy caught pooping on the rug for the third time! How could I ever stay mad at you?! Hahaha!"

I'm beyond confused. "W-what? You're not angry?!"

"Of course not! Heavens, no! Oh lord, have mercy!" Phoebe nearly falls to the ground laughing, and this time her laughter is an upbeat new emotion I've never seen her display before. Relief overwhelms me.

Phoebe laughs for a solid twenty seconds, before choking to a stop and wiping her forehead. "Oh, my days! I was so scared at the time, but after you left, I realized what an exhilarating feeling that speed was! I couldn't walk straight until the militia showed up. Hans explained to me what an 'automobile' was but also how he'd never seen one as advanced as yours."

I nod, unsure of how to respond. "Hans did, huh? Well, that was nice of him. S-saves me the trouble, I suppose..."

Phoebe forces herself to look stern. "Nevertheless! If you ever do something like that to me again, I'll beat you senseless during our next training! Do you understand?"

I quickly stand rigid and salute. "Y-yes ma'am!"

A moment passes, and Phoebe relaxes visibly. "Praise God in the heavens above. I must say, Jason, that had you properly warned me about that machine, I might have enjoyed it. I would like to ride in it in the future, if you don't mind." She pauses before quickly adding, "Not now, of course. Later. We've... dead to bury."

The joking mood sobers up between us as I glance out at the cooling corpses. "You've got that right."

---

An hour later, I move the final body to the right side of the room, as per Neil's instructions. Having divided up the allies and enemies, I now know that over a thousand Central Core monsters died, as well as three thousand monsters and demons on the opposing side. Unfortunately, the fallen from our team were more powerful, higher ranked monsters. A quick estimate tells me we took a three to five loss. Well, the numbers are meaningless anyway, since the demons are an unending horde outside the Great Doors.

We are few, and they are many.

Thinking similar thoughts, Neil exhales. "Huuu... not good, my liege. We'll be lucky to survive the next skirmish, let alone last to the end of the month. I hope you have a plan."

Makoto Ueda gracefully walks over to us, hands cupped in front of himself. "Hiro-sama, before we were so rudely interrupted, we were discussing rebuilding human society. Today has shown that we must continue that conversation and get to work at once."

"I concur," Saul says, as he meanders to us with Hans in tow. "If Demon Lords are already our enemies, I don't imagine it will be long before a Baron or a Duke shows up. Had you not healed Kar, we'd have lost one of our best warriors, and the title of King of Monsters would pass to another."

Glancing at him warily, I ask, "If Kar dies, what happens to the power of the Sphinx?"

"I haven't the faintest clue."

Hans gestures toward the Great Doors. "Vee need veapons. Good ones. Using swords and sticks to battle the demons is entirely useless. Vhy can't vee make guns? Zey vould give us a ranged tactical advantage."

My hand brushes against the lightsaber at my belt. _Hans is right. Advanced weapons and armor would give us a fighting chance. It doesn't matter how much damage a demon's fists and claws can cause if I gun them down before they get close. DOOM taught me that much._

"Good idea, Hans. Maybe I should add some automated defenses too, like turrets and such. The fewer casualties we suffer, the better."

Phoebe walks up to the group with a spark in her eyes. "Demons are highly resilient. I fought alongside Arthur and slew many in ages past. If you want to kill a demon, you need to pierce their hides."

Hans grunts. "Nnn. If that is that case, perhaps bullets vould not vork very vell. Vee might need something more sophisticated than the technology I used during the Great War."

He looks at me pointedly, as if to say, _you're the genius inventor, here. Surely you can think of something._

My lightsaber and armor come to mind. _If we need raw force to withstand demonic attacks, and the Crown of Solomon allows me to envision complex machinery like my lightsaber, then I've barely begun to tap into its latent potential._

"Fine. I have a few ideas." I pause to gaze meaningfully at the five people surrounding me. "If my plans work as well as I hope, then I need a promise from everyone here today. My goal is not to conquer and subjugate, but to unify humans, monsters, and demons alike. We have to try not to kill when possible. You can slaughter demons all day long, but every demon you spare will catch their attention and defrost their hearts. I'd like to release the ones I healed today, too, after we interrogate them."

After I finish speaking, Neil glances at Makoto. "Have you been talking to the king recently? His words sound like something you would say."

"I met his majesty only today, when you introduced us," Makoto replies, smiling.

Neil adjusts his jacket. "Mmm. I wouldn't go so far as to call your goals naive, my liege, but demons are not the soft-hearted creatures you believe them to be. Perhaps after you combat them again and again, you will understand how they think. They will slaughter all of us in a second the moment they realize they can end our resistance. In fact, I assume that will be their next goal."

Nodding, I reply, "I know. However, I won't budge on this issue. We have to try not to kill demons where possible. I'll make sure to equip our troops with better weaponry and armor, but we have to lead by example. In the era I came from, only those who stood up against injustice and took risks with their lives changed the world for the better."

"That's admirable," Neil says with a nod. "Very well. We'll try your approach, but you'd better hurry. More demons could attack at any moment. The sooner we fortify our position, the better."

"Already three steps ahead of you," I murmur.

Makoto folds his hands together and bows his head. "It has been long time since I see a leader work hard to spare the lives of his enemies. His majesty, Hiro-sama, is most gracious and kind."

I force a smile. "Thanks, Makoto...san? I'm more American than Japanese, so I don't know honorifics very well."

He cocks his head questioningly. "Did your mother or father not teach you about your homeland?"

"My mother died when I was very young. My father abandoned me. I don't remember either of them."

Makoto's face pales. "Ah, very sorry to bring up sensitive topic. If you ever need guidance, feel free to seek my help, Hiro-sama."

My smile shines more genuinely. "Thanks. I'll keep that offer in mind."

---

It takes me a minute of concentration to picture the mass of gears, circuits, machinery, and metal in my mind. The Crown works overtime forming mental bridges from neurons to receptors, and finally, when I open my eyes, I speak a word. "**Turret!**"

Attached to the wall outside the Great Doors, a round half-orb appears. Reaching twice the size of my body, with a red iris that stares ahead blankly, the weapon sits at attention, waiting for an enemy to step in range. Bulletproof black glass envelops the iris, protecting it from attack.

A laser turret. It can fire lasers at the speed of light, cutting through most armor and flesh with little to no resistance. If the demons attack, they'll come from the side doors or the end of the hallway, exposing themselves to turret-fire.

"**Turret! Turret! Turret!**"

I create three more turrets, placing two on each side of the Great Doors. These weapons alone should eviscerate any attack force that approaches us. Good enough for now.

There's still a problem, though. The turrets will run out of energy after a minute of continuous fire. Just like my Lightsaber, they need a sustainable power source. I'll have to build a fusion reactor somewhere.

Neil steps through the still-opened Great Doors and stares at the black bubbles on the walls. "What are these spheres?"

"Automated defensive turrets. They have advanced Friend or Foe targeting systems, and can see a mile in absolute darkness. They're always scanning for threats, so if demons show up, they'll take him down."

Neil nods seriously. "I have no idea what you said, but it sounds good. I thought you were going for a non-lethal approach, though? These spheres seem antithetical to those values of yours."

"They have several settings. I set their attack priorities to 'incapacitate,' though they will switch to a lethal mode if they come under attack. I'll install a broadcast system to warn incoming demons of our defenses, too. If they ignore the warning, that's on them."

Phoebe slips out of the Core and approaches me. "We'd like to close the doors as soon as possible. Are you done out here, Your Grace?"

"Not yet. Soon, Phoebe."

She lingers for a moment and stares at the turrets before walking back inside, a briskness in her step. She seems slightly afraid of them.

I can't blame her, though. They remind me of the computer from an old movie I used to watch. _2001, a Space Odyssey._ They resemble HAL 9000, with his unmoving red eyeball.

Creepy. I can't say I'm a fan of evil AI.

I spend the next several minutes installing a speaker and camera system, along with lights in the ceiling to illuminate the corridor. I can see in the darkness, but the others can't. A lack of light might make some demons think they're sneaky, so it's best to remedy that, now.

Stepping back inside the Core, I create two new computer terminals and beckon for two nearby Knolls to come over. They shoot questioning gazes at me but shrug since they still don't know who I am. _Thanks, Kar._

They stop before me, and I beckon at the computer terminals. "I don't care who does it, but I need someone on each of these computers 24/7. This one is set to monitor the cameras outside the Great Doors, and this one is for keeping an eye on energy levels of the turrets, as well as a bunch of subsystems. You'll pick everything pertinent up as you operate the computers."

Both gnolls nod, their eyes wide. "Issh assh you sshay, hooman. You build weaponsh to protect ush, we thank you. Lose many brothersh, we did."

"I'm sorry to hear that. I hope that, in due time, no one else will have to die."

I leave them behind and walk over to an empty section of the room. It takes a few minutes for the Crown to help me envision a small fusion reactor, but when I finish, I embed the circular reactor halfway into the wall.

The rest of the work involves magically creating power lines inside the walls of the Labyrinth to power the turrets, but that takes only a few seconds.

Once I finish, I pause to sit down. My mind is woozy. Creating complex mechanical devices drains both my mana and my brainpower. It's been a while since I felt this tired.

"Are you unwell, Your Grace?" Phoebe appears beside me, and I flinch. I hadn't even noticed her there; that's how distracted I am.

"I... I'm fine — just a little tired. I can't rest yet, though. I need to start making some weapons too."

She nods and leans down beside me. "For what it's worth, I wanted you to know that your concern for my well-being was very touching."

It takes me a moment to form a coherent thought. God, I'm slow as shit right now. "Yeah? Well, it's you. I always think about you. I never want anything to happen to you, Phoebe. You mean more to me than anyone else. Perhaps even myself."

"Oh, don't say such an awful thing like that," Phoebe pleads. "You have to take care of yourself. Think of how it would make me feel if you were badly hurt. I couldn't bear to see you in such a state again."

A smile spreads across my face. "Yeah, but then you could take care of me again, like you did before. We'd have a lot more time together."

She blushes and looks away. "You don't have to get hurt for that to happen. We could do that now."

"Oh? Are you asking me on a date?"

She stands up quickly. "Gods, guide me. You're always ready with a funny quip, Your Grace."

My smile fades. "I wasn't joking."

For once, Phoebe is at a loss for words. She opens and closes her mouth several times before forming a sentence. "Haven't we gone far enough?"

"You've asked that before. I think you're making excuses." I reach over and gently take her hand in mine. "I don't know if someone hurt you in the past. I know you cared for King Arthur, but I'm not him. If... if you think I can't match up to him or something, say the word. I'll stop prying."

She doesn't pull away. "It isn't that. I... I've lost everyone I ever cared about to the winds of time. Maybe... secretly... I fear developing a deep bond with someone."

"Maybe? You're usually more certain than this. Unlike you, I don't have such fears. When I entered my Cryopod, I knew everyone I cared about would disappear into the past. I felt empty enough that I didn't care. You changed that, Phoebe. You changed me."

Rising from my chair, I try to hunch down a little to her level, but probably end up looking goofy. Just this once, I don't want to tower over her.

"Phoebe, I told you a few months ago after my paralysis that I cared about you. That hasn't changed. If anything, my feelings have only deepened. Every minute I spend with you gives me the energy to live life to the fullest. I'm not only protecting the other people and monsters, but I'm also protecting _you_. Everything I do, I do it for you."

I'm stunned by the words coming out of my mouth. I used to think I'd die of embarrassment if I opened up like this to someone else, but Phoebe means everything to me. I don't just care about her because she healed me... I... I love her.

Yeah. That's exactly it. "Phoebe, I love you. I want you to be mine, whatever that entails."

She stays quiet for several seconds, the look on her eyes like a deer in the headlights. The expression passes, and her gaze softens.

"I do too, J-Jason. Love you, that is. Maybe I was afraid to admit it to myself, but... if I don't say it now..."

"You'll regret it later?"

"Yes."

"I know the feeling."

Silently, I kneel down on one leg, and mutter under my breath, "**Ring.**"

A shiny silver ring with a tiny red gem inset appears in my palm. I suddenly feel horribly flustered.

"Uh, look, here's a ring. If you, uh, if you wear it, that means you're... well, you and I are, sort of... you know?"

She grins hugely. "Yes! I know exactly what you mean!"

"So... will you wear this?"

Phoebe purses her lips. "What does the ring mean? Say it."

"Uh. It means I'm proposing marriage."

"Oooh!" Phoebe bows down suddenly and wraps her arms around me, taking me by surprise. "You're so cute when you're flustered! I will! I'll marry you!"

With her face pressed against mine, she kisses me on the cheek, and I grin stupidly.

Today is the best day of my life. Nothing can take this moment from me.
