Part 33: Stab and Seek!

"How?! How do you keep finding me?!" My head jerks around wildly as I strain to listen for the slightest hint of Mephisto's position. Sometimes I can make out a scraping sound against the wall, but I think he's fucking with me at this point.

Mephisto continues to stand about thirty feet away. He's allowing me to put some distance between us, but any notion of escape is an illusion at this point. "Ksss... My minionsss include not only the dead, the damned, and the decrepit... but the bugsss asss well. While you flee, left and right, through thisss passsage and that... the wallsss part for me. I sssee you, no matter where you run. You will be mine..."

_He can communicate with insects?_

A quick stomp of my foot illuminates the room around me. Here and there, tiny imperfections on the walls display various bugs crawling around.

_Damn! This bastard never lets up! While I run up and down the hallways like a chicken with its head cut off, he can travel through the walls and show up right in front of me!_

_Okay... okay... if that's the case, then focus! I can escape him! It isn't impossible!_

Several ideas appear in my mind. _Slay the bugs. Distract him. Fake left, but go right._

However, there's an even better idea. I don't have to kill Mephisto's spies if I can turn them against him.

Under my breath, I whisper, "**Mirage. Shimmer. Illusion. Smoke.**"

All around me, copies of my body appear. They're hallucinations, fake Jasons to distract him. A cloud of smoke erupts, and my clones and I take off, racing away from the Duke. I shift left and right, dashing between each of my copies and shuffling them around.

To my surprise, I hear Mephisto hiss in anger. One of my copies registers a hit as he swings to hit them, his knife passing through their illusory body. I don't look back, but instead smile as I hear him yell, "What?! Foolisssh worm... you can't ssstop me with tricksss! I'll cut all of them to piecesss!"

Each of the mirages shares a mental link with me. When a path split appears up ahead, I run left with several hallucinations, while the others go right. _Every time you encounter a junction, break off from each other,_ I order them.

The mindless incorporeal copies don't respond, but a tingle tells me they understand.

_Good._ I continue running, and grumble as Mephisto lucks out and follows my crew and not the others. He continues to stab mirages one by one. When the next split appears, I whisper, "**Waypoint.**"

It's only a hunch, but if this works, I might be onto something. Sometimes it's almost funny how the threat of a demon chasing after me can get my almonds rolling.

Wordsmithing has infinite possible effects. Having played many video games in my life, I hypothesize that if I set a waypoint, I might be able to jump back to it. Teleportation is something I've strongly considered, but it also terrifies me. What if I teleport inside a wall and fuse into it? I could die!

But as everyone always says, there's no time like the present. This Demon Duke won't leave me alone!

A split appears a hundred feet ahead. I scowl as the Duke of Mist slashes at yet another of my illusions, dissipating it into nothingness. 

_I have only a dozen mirages left. He'll hit me soon. I don't want that knife touching me._ The sensation of a million volts of electricity slamming into my spine was more than a _little_ unpleasant.

When we split this time, I continue moving with only two clones. For a split-second, Mephisto pauses behind us and curses. "Devilsss... what if thisss isssn't even the right one?!"

He relents a moment later and chases after me, making me grimace. _I almost lost him._

One slash, my rear mirage falls. Another slash, the final copy falls.

Under my breath, I choke out, "**Waypoint. Return.**"

Boom.

I vanish while running and reappear at my first checkpoint, slamming against the wall. The change of scenery only disorients me for a moment, and I quickly spot the bugs crawling on the walls. _Mephisto will figure out what I did within seconds. There's no time to lose!_

"**Invisibility. Stealth.**"

Transforming my body in a way similar to Mephisto's, I take off down the opposite corridor that I first went, heading left toward what I hope is freedom. My shoes become noiseless, neither squeaking nor scuffing the floor as I run. Without my sonar, I have no choice but to rely on my infrared vision to see.

This time, I spend an hour fleeing for my life. Left, right, up, down. Corridors and hallways pass me by the dozens. 

I slow down once I enter an area with several puddles splashed across the floor. My mental map tells me this biome is most likely a marine area, one with vast reservoirs of water.

Fireflies buzz about, here and there. The ones in the Labyrinth are significantly brighter than the ones I knew about on Earth, and they illuminate rooms like twenty-watt bulbs. They don't do anything for me with my current vision, but seeing a lot of them means I must be nearing an area with a high monster density. Demons and monsters aren't allowed to eat the fireflies.

Creeping forth, I avoid puddles and squint at the ground, unable to make much out. My shoes produce no noise, and the cool temperature in this marine biome makes my infrared vision nearly worthless. My best bet is to follow the fireflies since they produce a small amount of heat as they buzz around and crawl on the Labyrinth's walls.

"**Normalize,**" I whisper, returning my body to its original loudness level. I keep the invisibility active, just in case. It's slowly draining my energy, which is terrible, but at the same time, I don't want to take any chances. Better to lay low and pray the Duke can't find me.

Using my Telekinesis, I hover over the ground, slowly moving forward without splashing in puddles. Every so often, I grab a rock and tap the walls to light the way, but otherwise, I remain entirely silent.

Splashing sounds in the distance, along with the voices of incredibly cute women, make my heart flutter. _Not this time,_ I think to myself. Sirens can go to hell.

"Oooh, hehehe..." One of the girls coos to someone out of sight. As I draw closer to one walled-off alcove, a large opening reveals a massive demon with hulking armor sitting silently before a pool. Located before him is a giant pool of water, one where dozens of creatures splash around playfully. After a moment of observing them, I realize they aren't sirens, but instead, mermaids.

"Barbatos, sweetie, are you feeling okay?" One of the mermaids pulls herself out of the water and splashes onto the land beside the iron giant. I slow my hovering for a moment to observe their interaction. "You were gone for over a month! Come on, take off all that armor, and we'll show you a good time!"

The demon shifts slightly, his heavy plates of steel grinding together. "No need."

"I heard that nasty Black Witch hurt you! I can't believe it, love! I thought you were invincible!" While the mermaid crows at him, two others swim over and stop at the edge of the pool.

"Defeated. Yes. Agares, dead. I was lucky."

The demon's voice is deep and ominous, yet tinged with sadness and melancholy. Only now do I remember his name. 

_Barbatos! He's one of the Dukes of Hell! Shit. I can't stay here. I barely escaped the last guy. What the fuck is wrong with me?_

Continuing past the entrance, I start to hover away, only to freeze as Barbatos speaks. "Intruder. Reveal yourself."

My heart slams. _Fuck that! This guy is the size of a mountain!_

I land on my feet and start running, only to gasp in alarm. Without so much as disturbing the air, the metal monster appears in front of me.

_Red flag! Danger!_ My mind screams at me to run, but I hesitate, unsure what my best move is.

Barbatos stands, arms crossed. "Show yourself."

Guilty, I mutter under my breath, "**Reveal.**"

As my body reforms, the Duke's helmet shifts slightly. "Human?"

"If it's a fight you want," I say, raising my tiny fists and striking a pose, "I'll give it to you."

Barbatos is several inches taller than me. His armor adds to his size, making him appear nearly eight feet tall. However, despite his intimidating appearance, the Duke makes no motion to fight. "Sacred place. Leave. Never return."

I stare dumbly. "W-what? You're letting me go?"

"Fighting. War. Unending. Not for me. My creed. Protect friends only."

"Ohhh. O-okay then, big guy. Well, uh, th-thanks."

Slowly, I take a few steps away, careful not to turn my back on him. The Duke doesn't move. Returning from the direction I came, I creep past the mermaid alcove. 

Two of the mermaids hiss at me. I ignore them.

Once my spherical vision can no longer make out Barbatos's form in the distance, I turn and quicken my pace, vigilantly ensuring I don't step in and puddles of water.

_Never return,_ echoes Barbatos's voice in my head.

My teeth grind together as I shiver in fear. _Hey man, no problem. I'm sorry I bugged ya, pal. Have fun with the mermaids!_

---

Sigh... today is getting worse minute by minute.

"Gwahaha! You lost him?! What an idiot!" Bael roars and slaps his knee while guffawing at Mephisto. I stare Bael down until he realizes his laughter is annoying me. He tapers off and rubs his nose. "Err, sorry, boss."

"Mephisto," I begin, turning away from Bael, "Where did you last see the human?"

The Duke of Mist twitches his boney fingers impatiently. "Ksss. Emperor Diablo, the flessshbag wasss only inchesss in front of me, and then he disssappeared. Hisss copiesss sssplit up and made it difficult for me to follow."

I nod slowly. "So when you say he vanished, you mean you slashed the copy, and then it dissolved like the others?"

Mephisto bows his head. "Nay. The flessshbag vanissshed before I could ssstrike hisss back."

"Teleportation, then, in addition to illusory magic and a blade of light that can cut through anything. This Jason fellow has a veritable grab bag of powers. Damn. I should have questioned Beelzebub more about the human's abilities. Defeating a Lord isn't a small task, even if he is a Hero."

Bael rolls his eyes. "I've seen some weak heroes in my life. Remember that guy with no legs?"

"How could I forget?"

"Dunno."

A moment of silence passes while I contemplate how to capture this newly awakened Hero.

Bael glowers at me. "Hey. Boss. Do... do you think that the sword Beelzebub saw was..."

"Excalibur? Not a chance. If the human possessed Excalibur, we'd know by now. Hell itself could be rent in half if that weapon fell into a Hero's hands."

Bael chuckles slightly. "Hehe. Okay, good. I was a little worried, ya know?"

"So am I," I mutter. "A Hero and the Black Queen. One alone under ordinary circumstances would be an emergency, but both at once..."

Mephisto's head twitches. "I ssshould contact Umi. Ssshe is our bessst Oracle. Ssshe will help us find that flessshbag, and then-"

"No. We have bigger problems to worry about." I hold up a palm to silence Mephisto. "So far, Jason hasn't caused us any problems. I'm much more worried about what he said regarding Amelia. If she's alive and devouring another planet, we may be too late to stop her. We've already lost Agares, and the Overlords won't take kindly to us requesting the destruction of a second planet. They'll think me incompetent." 

I pause for a moment to consider my options. "Mephisto, I want you to scour the Labyrinth and find out where that damn Belial is hiding. If she doesn't help us, we'll never defeat Amelia."

Bael's smile melts away, and a look of worry crosses his face. "Say, boss, what if when you fought the little witch, you used-"

"No. Don't even whisper what you're thinking out loud. I'll never unleash that power again." 

Mephisto cocks his head curiously, but I don't answer the question in his eyes.

_I only used my power once. I damn near killed every other demon in existence. If I lose control like that again, nobody here will be able to stop me._

An Emperor's job is to oversee all the other demons. I have to keep them alive, happy, and healthy... not necessarily in that order. Matters of war should almost always be for the Dukes. Besides, if I die, who will lead? Bael? Belial?

No. A recluse of a First Emperor, a whore of a Second, and an idiot for my First Duke. I'm the only remotely capable demon here.

I wish it didn't have to be that way. _Ruling is such a devil-damned pain in the ass._

"You heard me, Mephisto. Find Belial. No second-guessing my orders. Bael and I will hunt that human down. With luck, he'll lead us right to the Black Queen... and checkmate."

"Keh keh keh... asss you command, Emperor Diablo." Mephisto says with a bow. A moment later, he turns around and fades into the wall.

Bael and I start walking down the passageway opposite where the human vanished. He nudges me. "C'mon, boss. If you use your transformation when other demons ain't around, there shouldn't be a problem, right? The Black Bitch will be on a human planet! Nobody will get hurt but her!"

I nod. "I know, Bael. However, that sick taste in my heart was something I never wish to experience again. It's not something I can put into words."

Bael nods, and for once, he takes a moment to think before he speaks.

"Well, if that's the way you feel, ain't no way I'm convincin' ya. Anyway, I can't wait to wrap my hands around the little bitch's neck. Agares owed me three blood pies! Now I'll never get 'em!"

"I'm glad you have your priorities in order," I mutter.

"Thanks, boss."

Sighing, I avert my eyes. _If Bael ever ran the Labyrinth; our kind would go extinct within a hundred years._

_Count on it._
