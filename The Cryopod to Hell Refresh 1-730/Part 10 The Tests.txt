Part 10: The Tests

For some reason, standing inside a five-by-five green square makes me uncomfortable. I know my 'barrier' is only comprised of lines painted on the platform's surface, but being forced to stand in a box makes me feel like a lab rat. At this rate, someone is probably going to stick a needle in my ass to test my reaction to Botox.

Kar, Amelia, and I watch Bahamut raise her hourglass in the air. She does it slowly, allowing our eyes to track the movement of her hand.

"The grains in this hourglass represent one hour. The first two challenges are not timed. However, the final challenge is. If you fail any of my tests, you will become my slaves. If you choose to forfeit, I will kill you without hesitation."

The sound of an electrical pop at all four corners of the stadium makes the hair on my head start to stand up. A second later, beams of light shoot out from the green squares. They rise fifteen feet, then force fields pop out between them faster than I can react, sealing the three of us inside our squares.

"What the hell?! What are you doing?" I start to reach forward, only for a painful _zap_ of electricity to arc off the wall and jolt my heart. My arm flops uselessly, causing me to nearly pass out from the pain. Luckily, before I faint, I gasp under my breath, "**Heal!**"

My breathing quickens. I look to the side to see that Kar and Amelia don't appear especially concerned.

Kar reaches a finger forward, and when the electricity zaps him, he merely shakes his wrist in annoyance. "What is the meaning of this?"

Bahamut chirps. "Hee! Many mighty warriors used to flee my test when they realized their doom was upon them. I devised this measure to keep them safely locked in place until the test is over. By the way, when I asked if you would take the test, I forgot to mention that the tiles you're standing on have magical infusions. You're honor-bound to complete it now."

Kar crosses his massive arms. "Get on with it."

"Mmm. The first test is rather simple. You must face your inner demons, wrestle with them, and come out victorious. This test has a 10% failure rate. Those who cannot pass will typically end up as drooling hounds, lying on the ground and whimpering until I put them out of their misery. I doubt this will happen, but The Test has a way of bringing out the worst in a monster."

After she finishes talking, Bahamut waves her free hand through the air and mutters several inaudible words, all of which the Crown tells me are Latin in origin. Why Latin? What's so great about that dead language?

Nausea boils up in my stomach. I want to vomit, but I don't know why. The world twists around me. Kar, Amelia, and Bahamut all vanish into blackness.

Bahamut must be using magic to play with my mind. Am I being tested too? I thought she only cared about Kar!

The blackness shifts around me, and a familiar scene materializes. A person I once called 'mother,' my foster parent, beckons to me. 

Amanda Tate, age 35, a woman living with her husband Robert and us six foster kids under one roof. We were always a handful.

My body moves by itself. I start walking toward Amanda, and my old house forms around us. The beat-up wooden floor creaks under my feet, and the smell of macaroni and cheese mixed with bacon bits fills the kitchen. She must have just finished cooking dinner.

My gaze wanders to the right, and I spot a mirror in the bathroom. My face reflects off it, revealing me to be much younger than I am now. By the looks of it, I must be around... twelve? If this is a memory, it's the most life-like thing I've ever experienced in my life.

"Jason," Amanda whispers, and her throat catches. "I need to talk to you, honey."

"What is it, mom?"

I ask the question, but it isn't me asking. It's like someone else is controlling my body.

"R-Robert... I just got off the phone with the hospital..."

"Mom? Did something happen?"

"I don't want you to be scared, okay? Robert, he... he was... there was an accident." Her voice catches again, and she quickly wipes her eyes. Now I realize the macaroni is getting cold and hard. It must have been sitting there a while.

"An accident? Is he okay?"

She wrings her hands and looks away, unable to meet my gaze. "H-he's paralyzed, Jason. I haven't told the others yet. You're the oldest, so I thought you should know first."

"Para...lyzed... how? Why?!"

She takes a step toward me, then stops to sit in a chair next to the kitchen counter. She taps the one beside her, so I quickly slide in.

"Jason, honey, life isn't fair. Sometimes bad things happen to good people, understand?"

"Is he going to be okay?"

I've heard this conversation before. I already know what Amanda is going to say, yet my words are ignorant as if I haven't a clue what's coming next. Not only is Robert paralyzed, but the accident crushed part of his skull. His job at the factory is over. He can't support the family.

I already know how this conversation ends. 

From this day forward, I flitted from one foster family to the next. I was one of the lucky kids. Nobody ever abused me, but I had to deal with an unstable life, always worried about whether I would leave everything behind on any given day and start a new life.

Chaos. Disarray. My life never stabilized; not until I was old enough to fend for myself. By then, it was too late. I had lost my hope for a bright, sunny future. My mind was too weak to endure so many stresses from a young age. I put myself in a Cryopod and froze myself, arriving where I am today.

"The state is already looking into our residency, Jason. I've been on the phone for an hour talking to everyone, trying to figure out what to do. I'm... I'm at my wit's end."

Since I was eight, I've lived here. Four years of my life gone, flushed down the drain. I thought after I was tossed between the first three families, the Tates would be my final home. I got attached to Amanda, even going so far as to call her 'Mom,' but it didn't matter. In the end, the universe forced me to move on.

"I'm sorry, mom. Can we see dad in the hospital?"

Her teeth grind audibly. "Not just yet. He... he's comatose. They... oh god... they said he won't... won't wake up..." Amanda lowers her face into her hands. "Uwaa! It's not fair! It's not fair!"

"Don't cry, mom!" I jump out of my seat and hug her, but the words my mouth forms aren't the words I think.

_Yes. Cry. You have to let your feelings out. Don't do anything rash, Amanda._

The memory fades away. 

I'm back in the darkness now. I know how Amanda's story ends. After the children in our household, including me, were sent to other foster parents, Robert died. He only lasted three months before they turned off life support.

Amanda killed herself one year later.

I didn't find out until I was seventeen. That was a year before I entered the Cryopod.

The next four years, up to age 16, were a blur. I bounced from one family to the next. _He's too quiet,_ they'd say. _He reminds me of a serial killer._

I knew what other kids in the house would say. I didn't care. It was whenever the adults whispered about me behind closed doors, the times I accidentally overheard things no child should hear- those were what hurt me the most.

At age 16, I got a job delivering newspapers. Once I had enough money, I moved out, rented a flat, and put myself through school.

_What would my life have become if I had stayed with Amanda and Robert?_

Would I be here now, in Hell?

The darkness pulls away, and I blunder back to reality. The hum of electricity fills the air. I pull myself up and wipe the glumness from my face.

Bahamut cocks her head. "Oh? A fleshbag is the first to wake up. How little trauma you must have experienced."

"My name is _Jason_," I say, quickly correcting her. "That memory was unpleasant. I never wanted to remember that day."

"Chit-chit! Hee! The more distressing the memory, the deadlier the effect it has on the body. You're already on your feet, so you must have had an easier life than the other two."

I glance at Kar. The crocodile is bent down, one knee on the ground, while he bows his head and rests his arms on the other knee. His brow furrows, and a look of distress gradually spreads across his face, worsening every second. He looks like he suffering an unimaginable nightmare.

Then I look past him at Amelia, and my heart skips a beat. She's rolling around on the ground; her body curled up in the fetal position as tears stream down her face. Her eyes squeeze shut with enough force to crush concrete. Every few seconds, she moans under her breath, "No... no... I don't want to... I don't want to..."

"Amelia? Amelia! Are you okay?" I turn to Bahamut. "What's going on? She's having a panic attack!"

Bahamut holds up her claws to examine them. "It seems the female has experienced some awful trauma in her life. She appears younger than you, yet some unknown horror has corrupted her mind. Hopefully, for your sake, she'll pass the test. I think not, however."

_Fuck passing. I don't care about the stupid test. Amelia's my friend, and she's in pain! I have to help her!_

My head spins as I try to think of what I can do to escape the trial. Right! Wordsmithing! I can probably use it to get out of here and save Amelia! I just need to figure out-

"Hurgh." 

Kar's voice startles me, nearly making me stumble backward. The crocodile slowly rises to his feet, but his knees tremble, and he has difficulty standing. "That was... the worst experience of my life. Experiencing the loss of my mate. How _dare_ you make me relive that pain."

Bahamut nudges a few feathers around on her head. "Hee! The magic does all the work. I had nothing to do with it. It forces you to relive your most painful memories. Take heart, Kar, second mightiest of your people. At least you aren't experiencing the same pain as that poor child. She'll be an empty vessel once her mind rots."

Kar looks at me in confusion, then at Amelia. He gasps. "Frilled one? What's wrong?!"

Blaarjiim materializes in my mind, but his image warps unnaturally, giving him a distorted appearance. It's like I'm watching him on a busted TV, one that barely works and has those awful lines scrolling down as it adjusts the picture.

"Jason. Listen to me. This is important."

"What's happening with Amelia?" I ask.

Despite Blaarjiim's image being even more shadowy and hard to make out than usual, there's a sense of anticipation in his posture that I haven't seen before. "My queen is in agony. I can only guess what event she's reliving, but based on how tight-lipped she's been about her past before we met, I'm sure it will reawaken her primal nature. This is not a game. Bahamut is playing with fire, and it will burn us all."

My eyes flick to Bahamut. She ignores me again and opts to banter with Kar. Neither of them can hear Blaarjiim, so he must be projecting himself into my mind only.

"Is Amelia going to be okay?"

Blaarjiim turns to face Amelia in my mind's eye, stares for a few moments, then looks back at me. "You need to know, Jason. My queen isn't the same girl she was when we met. She used to be a monster, unlike anything you faced in the Labyrinth. There was a rage burning in her heart, one she neither tried to extinguish nor wanted to. I once preferred that persona. However, ever since she emerged from the statue, I've also enjoyed the simple talks we've had. Thousands of years of silence as I rested in her palm changed me as well."

"What are you saying? Out with it."

Blaarjiim's image flickers. "I feel it again. My queen's burning rage. She told me yesterday that while in the statue, she was at peace. She dreamed and allowed her mind to wander. After you released her, she had mostly forgotten the pain Joan inflicted upon her."

I finally understand what he's saying. "You're saying... she's reliving her encounter with Joan?"

"Possibly. Probably. I can't read minds, not even hers. What I do know is-"

Before he can finish, Amelia wails on the ground, releasing a high-pitched scream from deep in her chest. "Eeeeeeeeeh!!"

Bahamut and Kar both jerk back in surprise. Big Bird squeakily chirps in astonishment. "Hee?! What the Devil?"

Amelia leaps into the air from her fetal position on the ground. The movement is so quick that I can barely register it before she smashes into the electric ceiling above, which repels her body back to the floor. She pounds into the tiles and snarls viciously like a wild animal. 

Amelia's heads jerks back and forth wildly as she searches for a way out. Seeing nothing, the little girl leaps at the barrier and batters it with her fist, sending what must be thousands of volts of electricity coursing through her arm. She doesn't flinch, and whales on it again, this time with her other hand, the one clutching Blaarjiim's orb. She turns his orb into a battering ram, bashing it against the field over and over again.

"Stop! Stop it this instant! The test isn't over yet!" Bahamut screeches and grabs for something in the folds of her robes. She pulls out a small device that resembles a remote control, and after tapping a few buttons, a blast of lightning erupts inside Amelia's cage, making her scream in pain.

"Eeeyah!!"

Bahamut's breathing falters. "There! Get ahold of yourself, foolish child! If you don't, I'll send another pulse your way! You won't like-"

Bahamut doesn't get to finish her sentence. Amelia grinds her teeth together and rams her entire body into the front of the cage, then kicks off of it and slams against the other barrier. Our platform shakes as she bounces back and forth. Only now does she begin screaming obscenities. "You fucking bitch! I'll tear you apart! You took him from me! YOU TOOK HIM FROM ME!!"

Bahamut's confidence vanishes. She quickly presses more buttons, and another wave of energy surges inside Amelia's chamber. This time, Amelia doesn't even feel it.

Amelia's bloodthirsty shrieks increase as she hurls Blaarjiim's orb at the wall with all her strength. He bounces off, but she catches and flings him again. By now the arena has begun shaking like a leaf in the wind. I can barely keep my footing.

"Jason!" Hoarhiim roars into my mind. "Don't just stand there! Do something! Use your Wordsmithing! Calm her down! If she breaks out, she'll tear Bahamut apart, then the rest of us! She isn't sane right now!"

Blaarjiim forms, but this time his appearance is much sharper than before, and his figure seems twice as big as usual. "Hahaha! Yes! That's right! My queen remembers her hatred! I feel fury welling up inside of us!"

Jesus Christ. Blaarjiim was calm only moments ago, but now that Amelia's awake, he's acting like a lunatic. I have to stop her. I have to calm her down.

Wait... yes! That's it!

I quickly reach my hand out, aiming it at Amelia. "**Calm.**"

Once again, Amelia swings with all her might at the wall, but suddenly her momentum stops. She pulls her punch right at the last second, only giving the electrified barrier a tepid nudge.

Silence.

Amelia stares forward blankly. Bahamut, apparently not hearing me Wordsmith, hovers her finger over the remote. Her breaths come in shallow gasps. "Wha... what was that? Is she done?"

I, for one, can only stare at Amelia in astonishment. _I changed her mental state. I calmed her down with a single word. Is this the true power of Wordsmithing?_

Finally, Amelia blinks her eyes. Color returns to her skin. "Huh? What happened?"

"D-don't give me that!" Bahamut steps off her pedestal and cautiously approaches Amelia. "What are you? A shapeshifter?! No human has strength like yours! Only an ancient Hero could have the sort of power you just displayed!"

"Hero?" Amelia cocks her head. "I'm not a hero. I hate them. All heroes are my enemies." 

Her voice is monotone. The entire way she holds herself is unlike anything I've seen before.

"A likely story." Bahamut's feathers ruffle slightly as a shiver goes down her back. "After this test is over, I'll investigate you more carefully." Her voice holds a note of fear.

Bahamut turns and walks back to the podium. Blaarjiim reforms in my mind, but something about him is a little off. I can't quite put my finger on it. "Haha... Intoxicating. Did you feel it? My queen's power is formidable. Even this false god, Bahamut, knows better than to underestimate her."

I eyeball Blaarjiim's smoky form for a few moments. "You went berserk for a minute. I'm beginning to think you really _are_ the embodiment of evil."

He sneers at me and vanishes without replying. 

_Typical._

Kar kneels to Amelia's level. "Small one, are you of sound mind? Whatever you experienced during the first test, it left mental scars that run deep as a canyon. I am here if you wish to discuss anything."

Amelia stares at him blankly. "Yes. Thank you. I am fine. I feel no emotion at all right now."

Now that she mentions it, she is eerily calm compared to her usual self. My spell must be the reason. How long will it last? Can I disable it? _Should I?_ Maybe it's best to let her clear her head for a few minutes.

"Ahem." Bahamut, looking worse for wear, stands on her little platform again. This time, she keeps her remote held in hand. "Well, ordinarily there would be two more tests, but after that display, I think I'll skip the second test. I'd rather not have another mental breakdown. Besides, it's too easy. Nobody ever fails."

Kar glares at her. "Good to hear."

I notice with a small amount of amusement that as Bahamut reaches into her robes again and pulls out the tiny hourglass from earlier that her claws are shaking ever so slightly. _Yeah, tremble, you fucking bitch._

"Hee. I will remind you of the rules regarding the third test now. It has a time limit of one hour. You must find the answer to an elaborate riddle, one that nobody has been able to solve for all of my life. The completion rate for this final test is zero percent. To be blunt, it's impossible. However, you have two options for proceeding."

Kar flares his nostrils. "And what are those?"

"Firstly, you may surrender. If you give up now, I will flood your chambers with lethal gas. If you fail, your chambers will fill up with lethal gas. Your death will be gruesome and unpleasant. I assure you, surviving would be worse than not." 

"Secondly, you may choose to undergo the test. If you fail, you will become my pawns for all eternity! After analyzing that strange female, I will send the fleshbags to the mines and press you into my service, Kar! Of course, if you succeed, I will elevate you to the position of my first-in-command! I will give you one of my rings, and you will become one of the mightiest existences in the Labyrinth!"

Bahamut holds up her free hand, and only now do I notice three average-looking brass bands stuck on her index, middle, and ring fingers. They look drab and dreary, but a hint of intrigue enters her eyes.

"These are not just any rings, mind you. Long ago, when angels ruled the Earth, I fought alongside the demons and partook in the final battle against their mortal enemies. Each of these rings belonged to one of three archangels. Michael, Gabriel, and Raphael. They are partially responsible for my awe-inspiring power."

Hoarhiim snorts. He whispers in my mind, "An unlikely fantasy. _Rings belonging to the Archangels._ As if such a thing could be true."

"You don't believe her?"

"Nay. If those rings are, in fact, such holy items, their power would likely kill her where she stands. Monsters and demons cannot use the holy power of an angel, let alone an Archangel."

"If you say so."

Hoarhiim knows better than me about these sorts of things. I should defer to his wisdom, but if the lie is so blatant, why bother? It seems like one of those 'so ridiculous that it turns out to be true' stories.

"Make your choice, Kar."

Her taunting words berate us, giving us little time to think. Kar glances at Amelia, then at me. "Turtle. What should I do? Bahamut's test is impossible. We'll die if we don't try it, but if we do, we'll lose!"

Kar's fearful words ignite rage within my heart. 

_I want to wipe that smug look off Bahamut's face. Zero percent? Get out of here. She's forcing us into a corner. Bahamut crafted this entire bullshit test to break our spirits. I won't go down without a fight._

I turn to Bahamut. "A question. Is only Kar allowed to answer? Can we speak?"

She coyly chuckles into her hand. "Hee hee! Of course, you may. Whether you have three heads or one, it makes no difference. The riddle is impossible to answer. Besides, two backwater savages like yourselves will be of little help. However, only Kar can make the final response. He is the candidate, so he must make the final decision."

I nod. _It'll do._

"Kar. There's a saying on Earth, where I was born. _You miss a hundred percent of the shots you don't take._ Accept the test. I'm good with riddles and wordplay. Maybe we'll lose, but I can't stand the thought of willingly submitting to this stuck-up arrogant bitch."

Kar growls in his throat. "Hurgh. Wise words for a little turtle." He raises his head up to face Bahamut. "Very well. We will accept the test."

She clicks her tongue. "A shame. I had hoped to make you my pawn. Perhaps in death, your bodies may yield some use." She holds the hourglass up. "After I give you the question, I will upturn this device, and your hour will begin. If there is even one grain of sand still falling once you give your answer, it will count. If you run out of time, you lose. If you answer wrong, you lose."

I groan inwardly. _Bahamut just looooves playing up the drama._

Gently, Bahamut lowers the hourglass. "Here it is, then. The final test. Riddle me this, riddle me that: **What walks on four legs in the morning, two legs in the afternoon, three legs in the evening, and no legs at night?**"
