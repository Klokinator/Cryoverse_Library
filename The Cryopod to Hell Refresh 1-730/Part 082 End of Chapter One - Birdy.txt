Part 082: End of Chapter One - Birdy

**[Recommended listening.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=014z7lFnWCc)**

_Click. Clack. Click. Clack._

Ose's high heels slap against the floor as she strides into the deepest, darkest region of the Labyrinth. While she walks, monsters and demons ogle her with looks of lust and curiosity. _I wonder what the newest Emperor is doing down here,_ their eyes say.

Ose ignores the covetous gazes of grunts. The sounds of prisoners screaming in pain as demons torture, rape, and devour them fill the air, making her quicken her pace.

_I can't wait to leave,_ Ose thinks to herself. One man's cry for pity suddenly cuts off, making her pause in front of a cell before continuing onward. It isn't fear or terror that tugs at Ose's mind, but disgust. 

Faith's End, the Labyrinth's sole prison complex, is a mass of tangled rooms and corridors, extending for miles in any given direction. Rats scurry about here and there, while every so often, a goblin will race across the ceiling and duck into a hole. Their kind lives in the bedrock of the Labyrinth.

A pungent odor strikes Ose's nose. She doesn't react visibly, since that would be undignified, but her throat catches nonetheless. _Rotting flesh. You'd think these worthless goblins would at least clean up the messes in the cells after a demon has his fun._

Ose's heartbeat slowly increases in tempo as she continues rounding corners. Her destination draws nearer. _No rats. No goblins. No signs of life. This way is the correct path. Unfortunately._

The sounds of screams fade away behind Ose. The further she walks down this particular corridor, the quieter the Labyrinth becomes. Where lit torches once lined the walls, maintained by goblins, darkness looms instead. She enters the Forbidden Area, the place where the master of Faith's End lurks.

Silence swallows the world around her. Soon, the clicking of her high heels pound like drums, echoing against the walls while making her hands twitch.

She pauses for a moment to collect herself. _There's no reason to be afraid. You're an Emperor now. Calm down. Breathe. Inhale._

_I'm not the scared little girl I once was._

_Those days were a long, long time ago._

Ose begins walking again. This time, it's her pounding heart, not her heels, that boom in her ears.

A man shrieks in agony up ahead, piercing the silence. Ose flinches instinctively. His cry of pain comes from a door further up the corridor. The door is one of many lining the walls, nondescript in appearance, and seemingly unimportant. However, Ose spots a faint stream of light emanating from a crack at the door's bottom, revealing its _occupied_ status.

Slowly, tentatively, Ose takes one step, then another, carefully pressing her heels down so that she doesn't make a noise.

The man bellows again, his voice hoarse. "Oh, god! Please! NO! NOT THAT! ANYTHING BUT- GAAAAAHH!!!"

Ose winces. Even outside the door, the sound of burning, blistering flesh reaches her ears. The sizzling noise makes her stomach roil.

Ose places her hand on the door handle, inhales, and yanks it open.

"Gressil!"

She speaks a single word while intending to add more, but after saying the warden's name, she loses her nerve.

Inside the room, a fireplace stands lit, causing smoke to fill the air. Given the Labyrinth's lack of chimneys, the gag-inducing black smoke washes across her face, tearing the breath from her chest.

Two round pillars stand in the center of the room, each one as wide as a tree. They rise from the ceiling to the floor, nearly fifteen feet tall in total. A human male and female, each in their 30's, stands chained with their backs to each pillar: the man on the left, and the woman on the right. With their naked bodies covered in blood, cuts, and bruises, the pitiful nature of their torture sends nausea crawling up Ose's stomach.

The man and woman hang limply, their broken arms twisted behind themselves and suspended with chains and shackles to the pillars. The man wheezes and gasps, a fresh burn still festering on his body, while the woman rests motionless against her pillar, dead.

Ose's eyes don't linger on the humans, or the table of torture tools standing between the pillars and the door, but instead, travel toward the prison warden. 

The master of the dungeon.

The cruelest, vilest, most despicable demon in the Labyrinth.

_Gressil._

The 9-foot tall demon, a two-headed monstrosity with a birdlike-beak on his right head, and a horrifying melted face on his left, turns to face the Emperor.

"Ose. How rude of you not to knock."

Glowing red eyes dot Gressil's four arms, his chest, and legs. Each glowing eyeball swivels and spins in seemingly random directions. Underneath Gressil's flesh, lumps form and disappear, as if something were trying to escape the warden's body. His left head slumps forward, its eyes closed. The eye on its forehead stares ahead blankly and doesn't glow like the others scattered around Gressil's body. The entire head appears to be sleeping, but Ose knows better. 

The Emperor of Infiltration swallows slowly and smoothes her uniform. "I... apologize. I didn't mean to... to interrupt your _playtime._"

Gressil cocks his long, pterodactyl-like head. "Aren't we formal, today. Have you come to join in on the torture?"

The warden's monotone voice never fails to send a spasm of nerves racing down Ose's spine. "N-no. I know you've been busy... _brother_. You probably haven't heard the news."

Gressil raises his clawed hand to scratch his beak. "You'd be shocked how few people want to talk to me these days."

Ose somehow manages a weak smile. "They're missing out."

A moment passes. Gressil's eyes lower to the table between him and Ose. He slowly brushes a claw across the assortment of bloodied knives and forks sitting atop it as he considers which toy to use next. "Don't keep me in suspense. It's not often I get a visit from my little sister."

"I'd never dream of making you wait."

A trickle of sweat drips down Ose's face.

"I-I'm an Emperor now."

Gressil doesn't meet her gaze. Instead, he plucks a knife from his table, then reaches over and caresses the man's chin. "Is that all?"

"I came to order you- that is, to ask for something. I need a favor."

The human trembles as Gressil's claw digs into the underside of his chin. "No... please... not the knife again... mercy-"

"Quiet. I'm talking to my sister. Don't be _rude,_ or I won't let you die when I'm done."

Gressil's words bring the human's complaints to an instant halt. His desire to pass away overrides all the logic centers of his brain.

Gressil continues to play with the man's chin. "A favor. How strange. Now, what, pray tell, could an almighty Emperor of the demons need from little old me? I'm nothing more than a humble flesh-melder."

"I wouldn't normally, Gressil. I hate to impose. You don't have to. In fact, never mind. This- this was a mistake." 

Ose's nerves get the better of her. She starts to step out of the doorway, but Gressil's voice stops her dead in her tracks.

"Why are you leaving?"

Ose stops, one foot primed and ready to drag her out the door. Every fiber of her being screams at her to leave, but her body won't move.

"I... I... I'm not- the demons- there was an accident. Barbatos. Uriel. He fell asleep. There-"

Ose babbles incoherently. Her mind begins to race as a sickening, primal fear seeps into her mind. Nothing scares her more than being in the presence of her disgusting, torturous, barbaric older brother.

Gressil holds up a claw. "Oh. I thought you were going to pester me about something important."

Ose gathers her courage and turns to face Gressil. "It _is_ important. I defeated Uriel... but the Archangel killed half of the Lords, and most of the Barons. Our ranks have been decimated. And on top of that... we still have the humans to deal with."

Gressil's casual demeanor fizzles away, and his expression turns icy. He pulls away from his prisoner and rises to his full height. "Oh? Humans, you say?"

"You _know_ what's going on," Ose growls. "It's _your_ fault we're in this predicament."

Gressil feigns ignorance. "Is it? My memory is quite poor these days."

Ose takes a step toward her brother. "You were supposed to guard the last cryogenic container. How did a human manage to slip past you? That shouldn't be possible!"

Gressil smiles. "Heh... heh heh... amusing. Is that what you think? A little rat skittered by when I turned my back?"

"What?" Ose's breath catches. 

The Emperor of Infiltration stares at her brother for a moment. A sinking realization appears in the back of her mind.

"You... you let the human go? Even for you... that's..."

Gressil laughs.

"Haha. Come, now, Ose. Ask yourself: why would I let an insignificant fleshbag slip past me? Isn't the answer obvious?"

The prison warden slowly waggles a finger at Ose.

"...Because I thought it would be fun."

Ose stares, dumbfounded. "Fun? You let a Hero roam around for _fun_?! He's destroying everything!"

Gressil smiles. "I know. That's the point, sister. All your silly machinations, your little tricks and schemes... they always did annoy me."

Ose lowers her eyes. Rage boils in her mind, but she forces herself to suppress it. Even as a freshly minted Emperor, she hasn't the power to punish her brother for the wrench he tossed into her plans.

"_You_ annoy _me_, Gressil."

Gressil laughs. "Haha. Is that all you have to say? I find your courage lacking. Stop wasting my time. If you've no more business here, then leave."

"Fine. Far be it for me to keep you from your _hobby_," Ose says, rolling her eyes. "With the demons decimated, we need new forces. The humans have already begun making advanced technology, and I believe they will soon improve even faster. I would like for you to make me a new demon warrior. Something that can stand up to technologically advanced humans."

Gressil smiles. "Haha. No."

"What? No?! What do you mean?"

He turns to the human male and bends down to wipe the knife on the man's shoulder. "Your request doesn't interest me. I despise your orderliness and the way you treat humans like livestock. They deserve torture and death. Nothing more, nothing less."

Ose calms her nerves. "I agree. I always have. However, I have to be practical. Demons need humans for soul energy. If we kill them all off-"

Gressil suddenly swings his hand around. He backhands the deceased human woman with enough force to rip her head off and send it flying. It smashes against the wall with a tremendous _bang_ and explodes into a mass of bone and gore.

The prison warden clenches his fist and breathes through his nostrils like a mad dog. "There is no _if_. Demons do not require soul energy to live. Your need for control disgusts me. You have to put _everything_ into a box, don't you, little sister?" 

Gressil rises to his full height again, but this time a menacing look appears in his eyes. "You think you're better than the other demons. Better than _me_. Isn't that right?"

Ose slides back half an inch. "N-no. I didn't say that..."

"You don't have to." Gressil begins walking around the table. He approaches his sister, and any trace of strength leaves her body. The massive monstrosity towers over her. His shadow stretches out like an invisible hand and crushes her spirit with its oppressive weight. "I know your thoughts. I know you better than you do yourself. You require validation — approval from the other demons. You wish to prove that the other Emperors were nothing compared to you. Isn't that right? Answer me. ANSWER ME!"

Any semblance of calmness leaves Gressil's voice. He lunges a hand out with frightening speed and grabs his sister by the throat, then slams her against the wall. Immediately, Ose claws and grabs at him, but no matter how she wriggles, she can't pull his hand away.

"B-brother... you're... hurting me..."

"Good. You deserve to suffer. While you forget your roots, I live them. Every breath I take is a reminder of what the humans did to me. They can never suffer enough." Gressil's tongue flicks out and licks his beak in a circular motion. "I'm a freak — a sick, twisted reminder of what humans do to our people. And you... you want to... to reward them! You want to give them cushy lives so they will spawn more of their brood!"

"No...! That isn't right... kah..." Ose wriggles and tries to kick her brother away, but she can't tap into the electricity inside of her body. Her strength reserves diminish every second, leaving her defenseless before Gressil. "Th-think of the demons... we have to-"

"Forget the demons! They can burn for all I care! I'll throw them into the Lake of Fire myself if that's what it takes!" The eyes on Gressil's body begin swiveling and spinning in a frenzy. Their colors brighten dramatically.

Finally, Ose has enough. She grinds her teeth together. "Gressil... what would... would mother say... if she saw you hurting me like this?"

A moment passes. Gressil's eyes flick to his second head. A moment later, it rises and blinks its eyes. A soothing female voice speaks from its lips. "Gressil... what are you doing to your sister?"

"Mother... mother..." Gressil releases his sister and takes a step back. "No! You filthy bitch! How... how _dare_ you wake mother up!"

Gressil's lower left arm swings up and punches his beak-head with staggering force. He stumbles back and gasps. "Augh!"

"You worm! I take one little nap, and you start strangling your sister?! Some demon you are! You're a failure! A disgrace!!"

Gressil reels around as his second head berates him. "No... no... I didn't mean anything by it, mother. Please... please don't yell..." He presses himself in a corner and covers his head with his hands.

"Don't you call me 'mother,' you pathetic insect! You call me by my name!" The second head screeches like a banshee, berating him mercilessly. "You can't do anything right! Freak! Monster!!"

Ose winces at the cruelty of the second head. "Mother, please don't hurt Gressil. He didn't mean anything by it... you know how he is."

The second head goes quiet. Gressil continues to shrink into a ball and whimper, but the second head takes control of his body and forces him to stand and walk over to Ose. "Oh, my beloved daughter. Why haven't you stopped by recently?"

Gressil peeks through his fingers at Ose, as if to beg her for help. However, Ose locks eyes with his second head. "Sorry, but I've been busy. Um... I'm an Emperor now! Just like you used to be!"

Her mother smiles. "Oh, that's wonderful, darling. It's good to see you devoting yourself to the path of power." Her expression hardens. "Perhaps someday, your incompetent brother will amount to something!"

"Moth- I mean... Lucifer... please..." Gressil whimpers like a beaten dog.

Lucifer ignores his pleas and continues fawning over Ose. "My precious little girl, it is wonderful to see you again. If you ever need anything, be sure and let me know."

A spark appears in Ose's eyes. "Ah. Actually, mother, there is one matter I need assistance with. Gressil said no, though. He doesn't want to help."

"I see. Well, whatever it is, I'll straighten him right up. Don't you worry! Anything for my little genius daughter!"

Ose smiles uneasily. "Mother... don't go overboard, please."

"Gressil will be fine," Lucifer replies. "More importantly, how are you an Emperor, now? Did you finally eliminate that empty-headed dullard, Diablo? Or perhaps Satan's little skank, Belial?"

"No. There was an insurgency among the humans that forced Diablo to use his Omega form. He went into a coma afterward."

"Mmm. So it's only temporary. You'd best finish him off while you can, dear. The last thing I want is my little pumpkin getting demoted! Don't taint my legacy with simple mistakes."

"I'd never dream of it," Ose replies with a smile.

Before she leaves, Ose steals a glance at her brother's cowering face. He continues to mutter words under his breath, but Ose can't make them out. "Gressil? Are you alright?"

The formerly intimidating prison warden doesn't reply. He continues to cower behind his hands, terrified of his second head.

Ose shrugs. She leaves the room and slowly paces down the hallway. The sound of Lucifer screeching at her brother sends pins and needles through her body, but she forces herself to ignore it.

_Gressil chose to escalate the situation. I only did what I needed to calm him down. Now that I have what I need, I won't have to talk to him again._

Ose's expression turns to steel.

_Besides. Gressil killed our mother. He deserves to suffer._

"Aaaooo..." Gressil whimpers and squirms for several long minutes, even when his mother yawns, closes her eyes, and goes to sleep. The memory of her abuse lingers in Gressil's mind.

"Damn you... Ose... waking mother like that. How could you be so cruel?"

Tears stream down the deformed demon's beak and plop onto his shriveled body. He shakes and moans for several seconds before finally pulling himself to his feet. 

He stares emptily at the human man, who is now incoherent and muttering quietly to himself. Gressil presses his toothy beak together and glares at his second head. _Lucifer's_ head.

"Callous... vile... despicable..."

Gressil rises to a standing position. "Birdy. I need Birdy. Where is Birdy?"

He walks around the table, grabs the man's head, and tears it off without the slightest effort. Shakily, having done his good deed for the day, Gressil walks over to the wall opposite the door and pulls a rack of keys off a handle.

"Birdy... Birdy... I need Birdy..."

That's right. Birdy isn't like his other prisoners. Birdy is special. Birdy has been his favorite for a very long time.

Birdy always makes Gressil feel better, especially when Mother emerges.

Gressil throws the door open and stumbles out into the darkness. He sniffs the air, feeling apprehensive about the direction Ose went.

Satisfied his sister headed toward the exit, Gressil's eyes flick left and right. He walks in the opposite direction. His two-toed feet, shaped more like a dinosaur's than those of a demon, _clomp_ like horse-hooves against the floor. He leans against the wall as he walks.

"Birdy... Birdy... need Birdy. She'll make me... make me feel good... not like mother... mother only hurts me..."

Gressil glances around to make sure nobody is following him. He eventually stops before one door, among many. It appears unimportant, given it has the same markings as all the others. However, a faint series of scratches above the wooden doorframe reveal a name if one looks closely enough.

_Birdy._

Three dozen locks litter the side of the door, as well as the top and bottom, making entry incredibly tricky if one lacks all the keys.

Gressil's anxiousness melts away. He flashes a sinister grin and quickly unbolts the door, then starts releasing one lock after another.

His heart skips several beats. "Birdy is waiting. She hears me unlocking the door. She can't wait to see me!"

A childlike excitement grips the hideous demon as he unhitches the final lock. After suffering all the humiliation from his mother, and after Ose laughed and mocked him... he'll have the final laugh.

Gressil slowly opens the door. It creaks loudly as its rusty hinges grind together.

A pitch-black room awaits. Even Gressil's demonic eyes can't pierce the veil of shadow.

"Birrrrdyyyy..." Gressil steps inside the room. The sound of something scratching the ground catches his attention.

Birdy is awake.

A muted whimper catches his ear. Gressil sighs. "Oh, Birdy. It's been a while, my pretty. I've had the worst day. You wouldn't believe it."

Gressil pushes the door shut. The pitch-blackness of the room swallows up his form. Only his glowing eyes remain, like smoldering embers to light up the night.

The sound of chains clinking excites him. Birdy presses herself against the wall with what little strength she has left.

Keeping his voice low, Gressil leans down and caresses her broken, lacerated leg. She quivers and squeaks in fright.

"We're going to have fun, Birdy. I'll hurt you in ways I've never tried before. You won't forget tonight... not for another ten thousand years."

"Nnn..."
