"You think I wanted to kill millions of angels and demons? My intention was to kill the Great Demon. Had I waited any longer, he'd have killed everyone anyway."

Several veins bulge along Raphael's head. "Lying snake! My people were on their way out of Heaven! Were it not for thy cowardly attack; they might have survived!"

Satan narrows his eyes. "The Great Demon destroyed Heaven's Gate. _My_ people were going to die while yours would have escaped safely. I didn't see you offer to help them."

"I can only open one portal at a time!" Raphael bellows. "Blackheart! Don't even think of slandering me! 'Tis none other than thy suicidal attack that killed three million angels and demons! Thou art a stain upon the mortal realm!"

Camael walks over and places her hand on Raphael's shoulder. After taking a moment to calm Raphael down, she gestures toward Diablo, still held in Satan's grasp.

"That fellow there. He's the one who transformed into the Great Demon. Give him to us. He must pay for his deeds with blood."

Satan narrows his eyes. He glares at Camael with a look of fire and fury.

"Hm? Are you stupid, grandma?"

"Excuse me?"

"You saw me unleash enough power to kill the Great Demon. If you want to continue pissing me off, maybe I'll do it again."

Camael falters. "...No. D-do not jest, Devil."

"I ain't in the mood to joke around," Satan mutters. "You wanna know the truth? I'll tell you. I planned for this. My goal, from the beginning, was to take your kind down a few pegs. I succeeded. I won the war."

Satan smirks.

"What can you do? Your best fighters are down for the count. You're exhausted. You're running on fumes. Me? I just blew up all of Heaven and killed the Great Demon without batting an eye, and I've got tons more kick left in my step. Face it, grandma, grandpa, your time's up."

Satan turns around to face his portal. His smirk widens.

"The Age of the Angels is over. The Era of the Demons is here. Maybe next time you guys see me, I'll finish you off. That depends on my mood."

Raphael stares at Satan's back. Every second, a new understanding of Satan's planning enters his mind.

"Blackheart. All of this death and destruction... and thou expect me to let thee walk away? Not a chance."

Raphael's eyes flash with anger.

"I shan't let thee take another step. Today is the day thou die, damned soul-manipulator."

Raphael's wings twitch. He coils strength into his legs and prepares to jump at the Devil, but before he can make his move, Satan whirls to face him.

"I don't think so, pops. I already beat you once. Your little illusion tricks won't work on me. Why don't you just thank me for sparing your life, eh?"

Raphael pauses. "My... illusion tricks?"

Several seconds pass. Satan says nothing, but instead continues to grin like a maniac. As he does, Raphael's frown deepens.

_That's right... I awoke to find myself resting only a short distance from the Devil while he battled the Reaper. But... how did I end up on the ground? AH! Don't tell me... I lost?! The Devil defeated me in a clash of souls?!_

Satan nods. "That's right. I see the gears turning, old man. Seems you've pieced it all together. I beat you. I left you to rot, and yet you still haven't thanked me for sparing your life. Oh, well. It seems good manners are out of style these days."

Satan rolls his eyes. He turns back to his portal and takes a step toward it.

"How many angels are left, I wonder? How will your kind hold out, while we demons replenish our numbers? Better start planning for the future. The clock's ticking. The hunt is on."

Satan steps inside his portal. It seals shut behind him, sending an ominous silence through the air.

Raphael stares, unmoving, at the spot where Satan stood only a moment before.

Camael echoes him. Her eyes turn misty as a flood of emotions surges through her mind.

"Satan played us for fools," Camael mutters. "All along, we assumed the Devil was after the Archangel's heads... but he wasn't. He only wished to reduce angelkind's numbers. In a war of attrition, he always held the advantage."

Raphael nods.

"We've lost the war. There's... there's no hope left."

Uzziel stands behind her elder brother and sister. A complicated series of emotions pass over her face — guilt, denial, self-loathing.

_I created the demons. It's because of me that... that all my brothers and sisters died. It's my fault. If only I hadn't been so damned foolish..._

The stench of blood hangs in the air, warning the gathered angels of their inevitable future.

Whether one year or one thousand, eventually... they will fall to the might of demonkind.

Elsewhere, on Earth, a single human male sits atop a rock several miles from Mount Sinai. His hawk-like eyes scrutinize the rippling tears in space as they fade away, and a mixture of emotions washes over his face.

"Master Hercules. With you gone, what must I do? Was any of this within your expectations?"

His eyes lower to a field of angels. Nearly ten thousand of them mill about in the grass. Some sit, some stand, while others pace back and forth while they wait for their brother to return with news of Heaven's fate.

"With the angels all but extinct, my people will have to fend for themselves. How will we survive when a single demon outmatches a hundred of our finest warriors?"

The human lowers his eyes. He gazes at a nearby flower, as if waiting for it to give him a reply.

"Is that why, in your final moments, you gave me the rest of your power? Did you choose me as their leader?"

The human climbs off the rock. He stretches for a moment and pops his back.

"I am but one man. To defeat the demons, I will need an army."

He raises his eyes to the perfectly blue sky above. Already, all traces of the deadly demonic energy from Heaven have dissipated, leaving Earth in its former pristine state.

"So be it. I will raise an army the likes of which the universe has never seen.  I will stay true to my heart, and I will take my revenge."

"I will kill them all. Every. Last. Demon."
