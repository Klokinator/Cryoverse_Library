Part 407: Jason Goes Home

After departing Unarin's Sanctum, Jason's expression becomes somewhat dull and muted. He falls deep into thought while autopiloting his way toward a turbolift which will lead him back to Sangin Kordonis. At the same time, he converses with his mind-wife at the blistering speed of thought.

_We've learned so much. But I feel like we barely even scratched the surface. Unarin held back all sorts of key information._ Jason thinks.

"Like who he thought _really_ attacked Valhalla." Fiona points out. "That Unarin talks a slick game, but he is quite the slimebag."

_I must have counted a dozen instances where it seemed he was holding something back or outright lying to me._ Jason thinks.

The Wordsmith flicks his eyes around as he walks. The inner corridors leading from Unarin's Sanctum to the turbolift pass quickly, and before long, he steps inside and begins to drop down toward the makeshift guest floor where his soldiers stayed behind.

_Nobody in sight._ Jason thinks. _But I can feel someone watching me. Probably one or more of the Psions. Creeps me the fuck out._

He turns his attention away from Fiona.

_Well, Raphael? You've been holding shit back from me. As usual. Can't say I trust Unarin in the slightest, but he made at least one good point. I definitely can't trust you either._

The Archangel of Wisdom sits on a tree stump inside Jason's Mind Realm. His expression appears forlorn, as if he has taken too many mental hits of late.

"Verily." Raphael mutters. "That Unarin-lad, he knoweth my thoughts. I am so... alone. All my brothers and sisters, gone. I cope with my grief in my own way. I wish every day that thou mayest unlock some method to bring back the angels. All the same, I know thou shalt not do such a thing. Thou detesteth my people, and rightfully so."

_Rightfully so._ Jason agrees, faintly nodding. _Monsters, the lot of you. The oldest Archangel himself gave the order to kill two of his Archangel sisters. And then you pinned Samael down and forcibly erased his memories. Couple that with your creation and torture of the dragons, or your creation and torture of the Titans, or your creation and- say... doesn't this story sound somewhat cyclical?_

Fiona turns to Raphael. "How did you know about Valhalla? More specifically, how did you instantly recognize it?"

"Camael spake to me of its existence." Raphael replies half-heartedly. "She showed me a vision of its splendor. Many Titan Kings also lived in Heaven, following the War in Heaven's conclusion. Zeus weaved tales of Valhalla's glory, following the loss of his arm to bring forth Queen Ivaldi."

_Sure. That follows._ Jason grunts, barely suppressing his rage. _Why the fuck didn't you tell me about humanity's flaw, Raphael? I should have known about that six years ago. That could have changed EVERYTHING for me!_

Raphael shakes his head. "Thy people's flaw is not easily solvable. Even with thy Wordsmithing, I fear thou mayest not be capable of resolving it so easily."

Jason's jaw makes a clicking noise as he angrily grinds his teeth.

_Fuck you. FUCK you! Our cooperation is over. You don't even have the decency to come up with a believable excuse._

Without hesitation, Jason plucks the ring off his finger, causing Raphael to disappear from his Mind Realm. He holds it in his palm for an instant before whispering a Word of Power. "**Return.**"

Without fanfare, the ring vanishes, sent off thousands of light-years to Tarus II.

"Did you give Raphael's ring back to Uriel?" Fiona asks.

Jason rubs his glabella and sighs. _Yeah. I can't stand these people anymore. I wanted to get Solomon's Crown back at first, but now I'm not so sure. I'm tired of ancient old men constantly trying to manipulate me. Between Solomon, Raphael, and Unarin, I've had my fill for the next three lifetimes._

After a moment of silence, Jason opens his eyes. He gazes out of the glass-covered turbolift toward the world outside, where the only greenery on all of Volgarius's surface stretches out for a mile or two before instantly transforming into the endless walls of silver and grey stratoscrapers the world is known for. They rise endlessly into the sky, towering far and above the Founder's Fingers.

_I talked too much._ Jason thinks. _I even thoughtlessly let slip that Hope was my only clone when I last chatted with Unarin. The problem is, I can't make any further clones without Solomon's Crown. I need to come up with... a counter-solution..._

Fiona gestures across Jason's Mind Realm to the only other denizen currently present. "Maybe the Phoenix can help?"

In the distance, the little red birdie-girl chirps when she hears her name. "Friends want help??"

Jason chuckles. _I don't think she can, but it's a nice thought._

The Wordsmith cocks his head.

_Though... I do wonder what the spirit of an artifact from the Primordial Realm CAN do? I'll have to look into it at some point._

The turbolift reaches its destination and swings open, allowing Jason to step out, where he finds Sangin Kordonis standing right in front of the exit, waiting for him.

"You've returned sooner than I expected, Wordsmith." Kordonis says. "I thought you would revel in the First Founder's wisdom for many more cycles!"

"We exchanged a lot of insights." Jason says diplomatically, while the two of them fall into step beside each other and head back to the dormitories. "Unarin is a... broad-minded thinker. I certainly fall short."

"As do all." Kordonis replies, a faint look of admiration and envy on his face. "I won't pry if you don't wish to say, but... what sorts of things did the two of you converse about?"

"A lot of top-secret stuff." Jason says. "He actually took me on a ride in his spaceship. I had the grisly privilege of watching a world get devoured by the Plague."

Kordonis winces. "What world was it?"

"One of the planets in the Gallax System." Jason replies.

"Ah. I have heard of Gallax's fall. Another senseless tragedy..."

The two men banter idly for a while until they reach the place where Jason's so-called bodyguards were left. He enters their dormitory's shared outer area to find half of them laying on luxurious quilted couches, idly reading holo-books or engaging in other menial pursuits. Emperor Serena sits at a table quietly, her eyes half-shut. Across from her, Lieutenant Samuel reclines with one of his legs crossed over the other, fiddling with his Power Glove.

"Commander Hiro!" Officer Ashley exclaims, standing up. "You're back so soon?"

"What, were you having too much fun without me here?" Jason asks, sweeping his eyes over the troopers as they hurriedly stand up and walk over to meet him. "I hope you slackers didn't just faff about while I was off watching a world collapse to the Plague."

"No, sir. We engaged in a few training drills with the facilities the Volgrim built for us." Ashley says. "I found out becoming an eight-foot-tall minotaur is a lot of fun!"

Yamir, standing behind her, flashes a thumbs-up. "Ashley makes for an excellent Minotaur, if I don't say so myself!"

Jason directs a pointed glance over at Samuel. "And you?"

Expecting Samuel to get all pissy like before, Jason finds himself surprised when the Lieutenant simply nods. "I... experimented with all of my transformations, commander. It turns out an S-Class compatibility isn't something to sneeze at. I won't make fun of fairy magic anymore."

"Oh! Well, haha, that's great." Jason laughs. "We're heading home immediately. I still have a lot of work to do on Tarus II, and the First Founder gave me a tremendous amount of things to think about."

"We're ready to go." Samuel says.

"Like the Lieutenant says." Ashley replies.

Jason directs his attention to Emperor Serena, the only person who remained seated upon his entrance. "And you?"

Despite her blindness, the demoness seems to 'sense' Jason's gaze. She stands up and quietly walks over to the group of humans. "I have obtained what I sought. I am also ready to leave."

The Wordsmith faintly frowns. _What she sought? Did she even do anything but sit around and twiddle her thumbs? I'll have to ask Samuel later, in private._

Kordonis nods. "Alright, then. I will take you all back to my ship and set a course back to Tarus II. With any luck, the return journey-"

"That won't be necessary." Jason interjects. "I'm not about to waste two weeks when time is pressing. Thank you for escorting us here, but me and my people will make our own way back."

"Hm?" Kordonis says, blinking in surprise. "You... will make your own way back? But how?"

"That's easy." Jason answers. "**Return.**"

After uttering a single Word of Power, Jason flashes a shit-eating grin at Kordonis while magical energy wraps around himself and the rest of the other humans, along with Yamir and Serena.

_Foop!_

All twenty-ish visitors vanish from the spot, leaving Kordonis in a slight daze.

"Oh. So that's how. Well, okay then..."

The Technopath captain stares blankly at the spot where they stood for a moment or two before shaking his head and blinking his eyes.

"_Mud-dwellers._ They're all so impolite."

...

On Tarus II, Jason and his people materialize a short distance outside of the city without any fanfare. Not wanting to cause a ruckus, Jason and his people quietly separate, allowing them to return to their usual duties.

However, he does glance at Serena's back as she walks away.

"Did you do anything... suspicious... on Volgarius?"

The demoness pauses to turn her head toward the Wordsmith's general direction. "That would depend on what you define as suspicious."

Without waiting for Jason's reply, she turns and returns on her way. "Until we meet again, Wordsmith."

After she leaves, Jason deeply frowns. "The fuck is that Emperor scheming? There's no way someone named the Emperor of Soul Whispers sat quietly and minded her own business."

His mind-wife pipes up. "I'm guessing Gorn sent her on a secret mission. There no good way to know."

The Wordsmith's frown slowly turns into a scowl.

"No, Fiona. You're wrong. There _is_ a way."

Rather than making his way back into the city, the Wordsmith conjures a wooden chair and plunks it down in the grass, absentmindedly swallowing the view of his people working diligently at whatever their respective duties might be, some half-a-mile in the distance. He gazes from atop Tarus II's plateau at the flatlands below, a sickening feeling boiling in his stomach.

"I can't get Unarin's words out of my head." Jason murmurs. "He's a snake. He's duplicitous. He's almost certainly hiding important information. But when he expressed his disappointment in my usage of Wordsmithing... I felt like he slapped me across my face."

"Because you agree with him?" Fiona prompts.

"Yeah. Because I agree with him." Jason confirms. "I did plenty of experiments with Solomon, but he never mentioned looking into the future, or the past. And it's not like the idea never crossed my mind. I just... I felt _afraid_. I hesitated. I don't know what will happen if I try, and that makes it all the scarier."

Jason gestures in the direction Serena left. "And what secrets is Serena hiding? I could find out easily. All I'd have to do is read her mind. It's that easy! Nobody could hide the truth from me."

"Then why don't you?" Fiona asks, leaning against the wall of some random log-cabin in his Mind Realm.

"Because it... it doesn't feel right." Jason says. "It's an invasion of privacy. It's also too easy, even for me. But also, there are sure to be risks. I doubt I'll just be able to pluck the information out of someone's head... or more specifically, a Demon Emperor or Psion's head, without any trouble. Those types are sure to possess defenses capable of fighting me. And if I alert them to my intentions, even if I get the answers I want, I could cripple relations between us."

Fiona shrugs. "Pitting your Mind Realm against one belonging to a Psion or a Demon Emperor does have many risks. But as for the diplomacy thing, these people are our enemies."

"Nominally. Yes." Jason affirms. "But... it's like I told Unarin. I'm trying to seek COOPERATION. I don't want to go around filching the information I want out of people's heads. How an I honestly look someone in the eyes when I've sneakily raided their most important secrets?"

For a brief moment, Jason's mind-wife taps her fingers on her waist.

"...You're a good guy, Jason. Caring about stuff like that. If you were even a little more bad of a person, you wouldn't feel any shame in getting what you want if you thought it wouldn't hurt anyone. And if you were a sociopath, you'd take it without regard for their feelings. You might be a bit of a lazy fellow, but you have a good heart."

"Um. Well, thanks, honey." Jason chuckles, slightly embarrassed.

"If you don't want to read people's minds, then don't. I agree there might be hidden risks. But... what about looking into the past or future?"

"I don't know..." Jason mutters.

The Wordsmith starts to voice a half-hearted concern, but his mind-wife interrupts him. "Jason, look over there. See that man next to that apartment building? The one with the green awning?"

Jason follows Fiona's guidance, spotting a random 40-year-old man standing outside his residence and drinking his morning coffee.

"Yeah? What about him?"

"What do you think that man has been up to, today?" Fiona asks.

"I don't know." Jason answers hesitantly. "He looks like he just woke up."

"Right. Why not try using a Word of Power on him? Say... '**Retrace**'? See if you can visualize his last thousand steps, but inside your Mind Realm."

The Wordsmith ponders his wife's words.

"Well, okay. That sounds reasonable. **Retrace.**"

Involuntarily, the Wordsmith closes his eyes. His Mind Realm violently shifts, recreating the local surface of Tarus II as observed by that random citizen. In his mind's eye, Jason replays the man's entire day in reverse, including what he ate and drank, how he showered, and how long he slept.

After a brief but intense visual experience, Jason reels in his chair and snaps his eyes open, blinking quickly to shake himself out of his daze.

"Holy...!"

Fiona, having experienced those sensations along with him, nods seriously as his Mind Realm reverts itself to its original configuration.

"That's what I thought. You really haven't been creative enough, Jason. How about this? Try changing the colors of smells in the wind."

Jason, still slightly shaken by the experience, doesn't quite understand Fiona's meaning. "Pardon?"

"The color of smells. Like that flower near your feet. Why don't you think of a way to color its aroma blue, so that you can see other similar flowers emitting the same fragrance?"

Jason nods. He plucks the cute white little daffodil-looking flower, then speaks a Word of Power. "**Colorize.**"

After a few moments, a barely distinguishable trail of blue emits from the flower's bud and puffs into the air.

"Huh. It worked." Jason says. "I guess that's neat."

Fiona smiles cutely. "Look around you."

The Wordsmith jerks in surprise as he looks away from the flower, finally noticing the much more distinguishable cloud of blue swirling in the local area, all emitted by the other flowers around himself.

"Oh shit. Did I just... rewrite a law of reality? I'd better not frighten people by leaving things like they are. **Normalize.**"

He reverts the 'blue smoke' to its original transparent color, allowing the smoke to seemingly fade into the wind before anyone else can notice.

Jason sits in his wooden chair, momentarily worried by these tests.

"Are you leading up to something?" Jason asks his mind-wife. "These can't be random things you're asking me to do. You always have a reason for your actions."

"Naturally." Fiona says, puffing out her chest in a somewhat haughty matter. "Even Solomon praised my wisdom! Hah-hah-hah!"

"Stop that." Jason chuckles, with an exaggerated eye-roll.

"Okay, fine. Anyway, think about what these two Words of Power did. They gave you the ability to visualize things you've never seen yourself, and they also rewrote reality itself. That's a ridiculous ability, Jason. All this time, you've been seeking out those old monsters to try and get your questions answered. But don't you have the ability to seek out the answers yourself?"

Jason nods, but his expression becomes somewhat darker.

"Sure. But there's a difference between looking at the footsteps of a man's movements from the past several hours versus trying to peer at events from ten zillion years ago. You saw what happened when I tried to visualize the events that rocked Valhalla. I couldn't make anything happen, probably because I didn't have any sort of a solid date in mind. I didn't have a... a..."

He frowns, trying to think of a good word to describe his conundrum.

"I didn't have... an _anchor_. Yeah. I didn't have something that could point me in the right direction. I was just randomly flailing my past-vision about like a blind guy."

"You could trial-and-error it." Fiona says. "You start by aiming for 100,000 years ago. Then 80,000. Then 90,000. Look for big changes. Try to determine when the biggest anomalies happened."

"That sounds time consuming." Jason says, smiling weakly. "And you know me..."

"Lazy to a fault!" Fiona exclaims. "Okay, that's fine. Let's try something else. Why not... well... why not try looking into the future? Just a few years. Maybe three?"

"I don't know..." Jason says, visibly hesitant. "It's not like I don't want to see the future. Who wouldn't? But you saw what happened during the Ancient Era. Camael could look into the future. She also seemed to lose her mind whenever she did. After all, she foresaw the end of the angel species."

Fiona fidgets awkwardly. "Jason. I never saw the Ancient Era. That was you and Solomon, remember?"

"OH. Right. Sorry." Jason laughs, embarrassed. "Just take my word on it. I saw what happened when Camael looked into the future. And a problem arises, too... what if looking into the future makes Fate become set in stone?"

His mind-wife crosses her arms. "I don't follow."

"Look, have you heard of the Grandfather Paradox? Where you try to go back in time and kill your grandpa, but you can't do that because then you wouldn't exist to go back in time? There are other similar paradoxes, but for looking into the future. What if the reason angelkind died out was because Camael made overt choices or accidental missteps that led her directly toward the future where they died? Maybe if I look at the future, I'll create a self-fulfilling prophecy."

"That might happen." Fiona acknowledges. "But you assume we'll see a bad future. What if we see a good one and it invigorates you with the willpower to make it happen? Also, don't you believe in free will? I think even if you see a bad future it will make you want to fight harder not to make it happen."

Jason's expression becomes gloomy. "That's exactly the problem, babe. What if it _does_ make me fight harder? What if I decide to abandon my ideals just to ensure the bad future doesn't come to pass? Cooperation can never happen if people are at each other's throats or full of suspicion, and that includes me."

For half a minute, neither the man nor his wife say anything. Instead, they fall deep into thought.

Fiona reflects on the pros and cons of looking into the future, weighing her curiosity against Jason's words of warning. At the same time, Jason remember all the horror movies he saw as a kid.

"I can't let Terminator come to pass."

"What was that?" Fiona asks.

"Just some old movie." Jason mutters. "About killer robots from the future."

"Oh. Well. After thinking about it..." Fiona says, "I still think you should try it. Just one peek. What's the worst that could possibly happen? You see a terrible future you at least know to prepare for? You see the Plague ravaging the galaxy, like you already know it will?"

Jason violently cringes. "_Babe._ Never EVER ask if anything bad could happen. That guarantees something HORRIBLE will happen. Now I really don't want to do it."

Fiona sighs. "Well. Alright. If you don't want to, then I can't force you. But... you know... I just feel like..."

She pauses for a moment, making Jason frown.

"You feel like what?" Jason prompts.

"I feel like... maybe we could have saved Daisy if we knew Stormbringer was coming." Fiona mutters. "If only I had just an extra month of prep time, maybe I could have made the planet unassailable."

The Wordsmith becomes quiet. He gazes out at the peaceful unexploited plains of Tarus II, momentarily imagining a future where humanity prospers and spreads across the entire world, making it their future base of operations.

"Yeah." Jason says quietly. "Maybe we could have saved our little girl."

Several minutes pass.

The birds of Tarus II, giant winged creatures soaring in the skies above, cry out in hunger, allowing their screeches to fall upon the ears of the people below them.

The distant humming of activity mixes with the sounds of inaudible conversations.

The summer breeze gently rustles the slowly-regrowing wild grass near the Wordsmith's feet.

In Jason's mind, he recalls those fond moments he shared with his little girl. He thinks about all the time they'll never get to experience together, ever again.

All because of his failure to protect her.

The man lifts his eyes to the moon above.

"**Foresight.**"
