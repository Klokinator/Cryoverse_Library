Part 071: A New Hope

"WARNING! THERMONUCLEAR DETONATION DETECTED! SEEK COVER!"

Centurion roars in my ear like a tiger howling for blood. My eyes bolt open, and I swivel around to see Beelzebub's entire body convulsing.

"Holy shit! Samantha, look out!"

"FIVE SECONDS TO DETONATION. FOUR. THREE..."

Instinctively, I aim my mind at Samantha. "**SHIELD! SHIELD! SHIELD! SHI-**"

My mind melts away. A supernova explodes ten feet from my face, and every cell in my body screams in agony.

I open my eyes. Everything is still.

The taste of metal permeates every corner of my mouth. A hundred bells clang in my ears. My vision is little more than a wild splash of colors and images. Cat. Dog. Car. Plane. Cloud. Tree.

I can't move. There's no pain, but invisible anvils crush me against the floor. 

Somewhere between one second and one eternity passes. I open my mouth to speak, but whatever comes out, I can't hear it.

Finally, the colors melt away. Black and white flashes of heat replace them, followed by a sensation of burning across my skin.

"Ahhunn..."

I let out a pitiful whimper. At least I can speak.

"-ope? Hope?"

Someone's voice speaks to me.

"...God?"

"No. It's me,  Samantha. Are you okay? I healed you as best I could."

I can't see her at all. I blink my eyes. "I'm blind."

"You'll have to fix that. Anything involving a brain is beyond me. I did what I could with the burns and broken bones."

"**Heal.**"

I croak out a word of power, causing my vision to return. The darkness of the Labyrinth replaces the pure black and bright white from before. It takes me several seconds to pick out Samantha's face from the gloom surrounding us.

"Hey, are you okay?"

I reach up to rub my face. My hands scratch my skin, reminding me of pressing sandpaper together. I wince and pull myself up.

"What happened?"

Only now can I fully understand the scene around me. I sit not in a small room like before, but a massive cavern. There are no walls anywhere in sight. The floor around me is pure black and covered in a cross between ash and soot. One look at my body reveals my tuxedo is in tatters, with only a few strips clinging to my skin through static electricity. Similarly, Samantha's white bridesmaid dress is gone, burned to a crisp. She doesn't notice.

Samantha shakes her head. "I think... I think Beelzebub blew himself up. You saved me. I would have died if it weren't for you." She smacks her lips. "Eugh. I'm nauseous. My stomach won't stop turning."

I speak again. "**Light.**"

A bright light appears above me, illuminating the floor around us. A shiver goes down my spine.

I still can't see the walls or the ceiling; only the ground stretching out endlessly into the darkness. Beelzebub is also nowhere to be seen. I think he died.

"Incredible," A deep voice says from behind me. I leap to my feet and spin around to see Barbatos- no, I mean Uriel, the Archangel, standing and staring at the cavern around us. She looks up, down, left, and right. "Such devastation. Thou shoulds't consider thyself lucky I was here to protect thee, young Hero."

"Protect me? I shielded myself."

The Archangel chuckles. "Heh... amusing. I created several Light Walls at the moment of Beelzebub's detonation and enveloped us in their protection. I didn't particularly want to protect Satan's whore, but I knew ye would think better of me if I did."

I glance at Samantha and shrug. "I mean, the Archangel called you a whore, but she did save us, too. Do those cancel out?"

"Uriel didn't save my life out of the kindness of her heart. She isn't good enough to do that." Samantha crosses her arms and glares at the Archangel. "Still, thank you. I would be dead if it weren't for you."

Uriel ignores the succubus. "Tell me, Hero, now that thou hath seen the devastation bloodskins can unleash, will ye stand apart from the demon whore at thy side and join me? If ye striketh down Belial, the temptress of man, thou shalt earn thy place among the legends of times past."

The color drains from my face. "What the hell? After saving Samantha, now you want me to kill her?! Are you insane?"

The Archangel shakes her head. "Hardly. 'Tis a reasonable proposal. Since time immemorial, angels and humans have always walked arm in arm as brothers and sisters. Perhaps after a hundred thousand years, humans may have forgotten, but I have not. I want nothing more than to save thy people from slavery at the hands of the bloodskins."

Samantha rolls her eyes. "Oh, that's _rich_. You're the ones who enslaved imps in the first place. Slavery wouldn't exist if it weren't for you."

"Quiet, wench. I have little patience for thy silver-tongue."

"What?! You're the liar! All you utter are falsehoods!"

I watch silently as the Archangel and Demon Emperor bicker, each trying to compel me to join their side. Thinking back on what I've witnessed, not once have I seen Samantha kill or injure someone else. On the other hand, Uriel's aura drips with bloodlust and murder. If I had to compare the two of them, I can't see myself sympathizing with the Archangel.

However, Uriel's hatred isn't likely to have sprouted from nowhere. The fact that a Demon Duke, Barbatos, trapped her within his body implies she met some horrible end at his hands. Who can say if her quest for vengeance is unwarranted? I've already realized, unlike Jason, that killing is sometimes necessary.

After all, not even one day ago, I killed hundreds of orcs to save a human village. If Uriel is a monster, then I share a similar stain on my conscience.

One lingering issue, however, is that the Archangel pursues a grievance from a hundred thousand years in the past. None of the demons alive today had anything to do with the murder of the angels. It's not right to judge children for the sins of their ancestors!

...

No. I'm overlooking something. Samantha is an Emperor. She's old enough to remember the angels and the war, along with their destruction. Surely the other Dukes and Emperors also remember. In that case, Uriel's grievance is legitimate. The same corrupt leaders of demonkind who exterminated the Angels and Gods continue to live today.

I pause for a moment to itch my arms. They tingle every so often, causing me to notice other weird sensations in my body.

While Samantha and Uriel take menacing steps toward each other and yell, I glance down at my arms. With the light illuminating them, I see...

What the hell?

My skin is mottled and gross. Tiny worm-like objects crawl all over my limbs and chest. Bugs? Spiders? No, something else.

...

Son of a bitch. That's right. I was wearing the T-REX when Beelzebub exploded. What the hell would a nuclear blast do to nanites? 

I slap the button on my chest. Nothing happens.

My armor is destroyed. What if the nanites embedded inside of me?

"**Observe.**"

I speak aloud, making the other two turn to look at me.

My body's status pops up in front of me, detailing my current state via a series of images and diagrams.

_Physical Condition: 45%._

_Stage 3 Radiation Poisoning detected_

_Stage 3 Cancer of the kidneys, lungs, heart, and brain detected._

_Lymphomatic cells detected within 14% of the bloodstream._

_1,603,978,801 defective nanites detected throughout body._

"Holy fuck," I mutter aloud. I turn to Samantha. "**Observe.**"

Her condition comes up.

_Physical Condition: 90%._

_Stage 1 Radiation Poisoning Detected._

_Stage 1 Cancer of the heart detected._

_Lymphomatic cells detected throughout 3% of the body._

Samantha raises an eyebrow. "Is something wrong?"

"You're in better condition than me. Beelzebub's attack infected my entire body with cancer. It also did something to my suit, since it embedded over a billion nanites into my bloodstream."

"Are you okay?"

I shake my head. "I don't know how to target something the size of a nanite. Maybe if I had the Crown, things would be different."

Uriel sighs. "I apologize, young Hero. I wish I could have protected thee. Now, the horrors of the bloodskins will affect thee for the rest of thy life, as they have mine."

I rub my head. "Actually, I'm pretty sure once I return to the Core, Jason can heal me."

Uriel's somber expression freezes. "Oh."

The Archangel's eyes shift. I can't see her face, but her expression seems to be something akin to... disappointment? Why that emotion, of all the possibilities? I'd expect her to be glad that I haven't suffered any permanent damage.

Something doesn't add up. I can't bring myself to trust this Archangel. Ever since I first laid my eyes on her, she's acted in her self-interest. She doesn't look at me like a person. She thinks of me as a tool.

Just like Jason does.

But I'm not a tool. I'm a flesh and blood human. I'm sick and tired of people treating me like a nobody, or a servant who must do this and that. _Protect the Core, Hope. It's your job, Hope. Do this, Hope. Do that, Hope._

Shut the fuck up, all of you. I barely survived a nuclear blast, and Uriel only looks relieved that she can still use me. She's happy I'm not... _defective._

If I'm going to kill any demons, it'll be for my sake, not hers.

I shake my head. "Come on, Samantha. Let's return to the Core. I'm not helping this Archangel. I've heard enough."

Uriel narrows her eyes. "Thou art leaving?"

"Yeah. I am."

Samantha meets my eyes. "Hope, we came here to fight Uriel. We can't leave."

I shake my head. "I'm not interested in dueling an Archangel. She saved my life, after all. Both of you have history, and I'm not going to piece together who started what. However, I'm not going to help her kill anyone either. Her quarrel with demons isn't mine."

The Archangel takes two steps toward me. "Disrespectful child! How dare thou turn down my alliance! Thou art a Hero. Angels created the Heroes. Thy life would be insignificant and worthless, were it not for our sacrifices!"

"I beg your pardon?"

"I speak the truth, boy. Thou art little more than a child who has grown attached to the likes of bloodskins. I know not why or how, but I won't allow thee to walk away. Thy lineage is mine to control! Thy _life_ is mine to use as I see fit."

Samantha pales. "W-what? That's terrible! Even for you-"

"SILENCE!" 

Uriel flicks her eyes toward the succubus. The ground shifts under Samantha, and a dozen knives burst out of the Labyrinth's floor to rush at her. 

With her body weakened by radiation sickness, Samantha can't react in time. The knives impale her limbs, spin her around in midair, and slam her into the dirt. Samantha coughs up blood. She wriggles to try and free herself, but she can't move.

"Samantha!" I turn to run to her, but an invisible wall blocks my path. "Uriel! Let her go! What's wrong with you?!"

"Hero, thou art a misguided fool. If I cannot change thy mind, then I will at least make certain the bloodskins cannot seize thy power for themselves!"

A warning tingles in the back of my mind. I duck reflexively as a knife sails over the back of my head and lands in Uriel's palm. She frowns. "Impossible. How could a mere human dodge an attack like that?"

A buzzing feeling hisses throughout my body. I jump to the side, duck, and hop two feet into the air to avoid several shards of metal flying at me from my left and right.

As my body moves on its own, a realization strikes me. Something's different. I've never moved like this before, not once in my life. I'm not athletic, even after Wordsmithing my body to be more physically fit.

What's going on?

I don't have time to think. I evade several more shards of metal and aim my mind at my body. "**Strength! Speed! Enhance!**"

This time, when I sense danger approach my body, I snatch the handle of a knife before it can impale my back. 

Uriel's tongue flicks out. "What happened? How have thy movements quickened? How art thou keeping up with my attacks?!"

I shrug. "Dunno."

Before she can say anything else, I aim my palm at her. "**FIRE!**"

A blast of Holy Energy sprays out and flies at the patchy-skinned demon-angel hybrid. She watches the beam approach and catches it in her palm, absorbing its energy without taking damage.

"Silly boy. I am an angel. Manipulating holy magic is child's play."

I flick my eyes to Samantha. Uriel hasn't paid any attention to the succubus since she engaged in battle with me, and Samantha's been slowly molding and shifting her body to free herself from the knives.

I'll give her more time, then.

"Hey, Uriel. Doesn't it piss you off that you're trapped in that hideous demon body? I bet it's still better looking than your original form."

The Archangel gnashes her teeth. "Impudent shit! When I walked the cosmos, all knew of my name and bowed at my feet! I stood atop a precipice of beauty, surrounded by the likes of the Valkyries and the Earthmother herself! A shriveled little boy like thyself would have counted himself lucky to court someone of my class!"

More danger appears in my mind, but this time I can almost make out a voice, but I don't have time to listen intently to what it says. I duck and dodge several knife attacks, then turn to Uriel.

"You only think of me as a weapon against demonkind. Maybe I'm naive, but I always thought angels sat on clouds and played harps. You're more of a demon than the demons I've met so far."

"Speak not of what ye don't know!" Uriel bellows. She stomps her foot, causing the ground to shake violently, knocking me off my feet. "The damned bloodskins took everything from me! EVERYTHING! If ye choose not to be my ally, then thou art my enemy! Burn in the lake of fire!"

She forms several signs with her hands, and flames surge from her chest, screaming across the short gap toward me. No time to dodge!

I'm dead. I know I'm dead.

I tuck myself into a ball and flinch. Warmth washes over my body. 

A moment passes. Two. Three.

Uriel speaks again, her voice softer now. "That fire was over eight hundred degrees. How is he still...?"

I blink and pat myself all over.

_She's right. What the hell? How did I survive that?_

A voice speaks in my head again. 

_PILOT AGHSK NNBSSPP._

I recognize the voice as Centurion. Why can I hear him?

He clears up.

_PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR, HOPE HIRO. THE TRANSFORMING REPLICANT EXOSUIT WAS DAMAGED DURING THE NUCLEAR ATTACK. ALL SYSTEMS HAVE SWITCHED TO BACKUP MODE. I HAVE ADJUSTED MY OPERATING PARAMETERS ACCORDINGLY._

It takes me a moment to understand Centurion's words. "You... you merged with my body?"

_AFFIRMATIVE. CENTURION'S SYSTEMS ARE UNABLE TO CONNECT TO THE EXTERNAL FUSION POWER SOURCE. CENTURION HAS ADJUSTED ITS SYSTEMS TO DERIVE POWER FROM YOUR BIOLOGICAL PROCESSES. EXCESS BODILY WASTE WILL BE EJECTED AS A HARMLESS AEROSOL BYPRODUCT._

I jump to my feet and feel my arms again. Within seconds, the bumpiness on my skin from before vanishes, replaced instead with a clean, metallic texture resembling armor.

A warning signal appears in my mind again. I'm so distracted by my thoughts that I don't move quickly enough. Uriel's knife stabs into my spine, only to bounce off my skin and hit the ground with a _clang_.

I get it now. Centurion's nanites have merged with my body to form invisible armor.

My vision shifts. Something moves around in front of me. I swat at the air, but quickly realize my eyes themselves are changing.

_ADJUSTING OLFACTORY, VISUAL, AND AUDITORY RECEPTORS TO OPTIMUM LEVELS. PROCESS COMPLETE. ENABLING NEURAL CONTROL OF CENTURION'S PRIMARY AND SECONDARY SYSTEMS. ONLINE._

I blink in surprise. _Holy shit, does that mean I can control Centurion with my thoughts?_

Centurion replies to my mental query. _AFFIRMATIVE, PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR. CENTURION'S SYSTEMS AND SUBSYSTEMS ARE NOW CAPABLE OF ACCEPTING MENTAL STIMULI AS COMMANDS._

Uriel scowls. "Thou have yet to activate thy Wordsmithing, yet somehow, thou hath vastly improved thy fighting skills since awakening! How is this possible?!"

What happens next surprises me. Samantha, having shifted her body enough to melt around the knives, leaps from the ground and transforms her arms into two swords. She chops down and hacks off Uriel's right arm. The sudden assault causes the Archangel to scream in pain and retreat several steps while forming an invisible wall to block Samantha's ambush.

Samantha transforms her hands from blades into hammers. She smashes against Uriel's invisible wall, battering it so hard that shockwaves ripple outward, making my insides quiver.

Centurion evaluates the situation. _ARCHANGEL-HYBRID DESIGNATED URIEL HAS FORMED A WALL OF SOLID LIGHT. ERROR. THIS EXCEEDS CENTURION'S TECHNICAL PARAMETERS. UNABLE TO EXPLAIN THE PHENOMENA._

"Uriel's using magic," I say. "I don't know how her magic works, but it seems she can control metal, fire, and light."

Uriel staggers back, and Samantha changes her method of attack. She dives into the ground and burrows inside the scorched floor of the Labyrinth. Uriel flicks her gaze to where Samantha disappeared.

"Tch, foolish female." Uriel motions with her hands, and the ground trembles. A moment later, Samantha explodes from the dirt, almost as if something had spit her with incredible force. She sails into the darkness and lands somewhere unseen.

My vision suddenly changes, startling me — the world shifts to hues of red and black.

_THERMAL VISION ACTIVATED._

"Uh. Thanks, Centurion."

A blip of red in the darkness catches my eye. Samantha slinks around, careful to stay in the shadows where Uriel can't see her.

The succubus makes a motion with her hands and races at Uriel. Her speed multiplies by a factor of ten. She covers the quarter-mile in under a second and slams against the Archangel's invisible wall, breaking through it as if it's made of glass. She crashes into the Archangel with thunderous strength, causing both women to tumble and roll into one another. Before Uriel can react, Samantha stretches, bends, and twists, wrapping herself around her opponent like Silly String.

Uriel gasps and struggles, trying desperately to unravel the demoness from her body. "Get... off... me!"

Samantha's fist transforms into a hammer again. She smashes it against Uriel's face over and over, pounding and pummeling the Archangel senseless.

Uriel motions with her hands and quickly forms a single magic sign, despite Samantha's intense barrage. Two winged figures of light appear; the same ones that stood beside her before the blast went off.

"Samantha! Look out!"

The blank face of one opens up. A hole appears in its head, and it fires several shots of holy energy at its master.

The bolts slam against Samantha's body. She screams. "Aiieee!!"

The succubus turns to swing her hammer at the winged pseudo-angel, but the other jumps forward and summons a sword of light. It chops off her hand, causing her to scream again.

"Paha, foolish bloodskin! BURN!" Uriel forms another sign to summon fire from her pores. She releases it outward, scorching every inch of the succubus wrapped around her. This time, Samantha's anguished cry churns my stomach. Her pitiful wailing makes me realize she can't hold out much longer.

I've seen enough. 

"**Remove.**"

With a single word, I teleport Samantha off Uriel's body and over to me. Her stretched form is covered in blood and burns, making her look like a mass of burned Jello. I can barely tell where her head begins and her legs end.

Samantha slowly twists herself back into a humanoid shape, but her badly burned body makes my stomach lurch. The succubus clutches her severed hand and winces. "Ahh, it burns! Shit... shit..."

"**Regenerate. Clothes.**" I quickly heal her arm and cover her naked body with a simple jumpsuit. Then, I step between her and Uriel. "That's enough. You've both gotten the anger out of your systems. Uriel, if you hurt my friend again, I'll have to get involved."

The Archangel's eyes explode with rage. "No! That's it! I've had it with thy cowardice! I'm done playing nice! Thou hath made thy bed, now sleep in it, _forever_!"

Uriel forms signs once again. I grimace.

There's no backing down for me, now. I have to fight the Archangel.

I look at Samantha. "Sorry. I can't protect you and fight at the same time. **Return.**"

"Wai-!" Samantha starts to say something, but disappears and returns to the Core.

Centurion booms in my mind. _PILOT ZERO ZERO FOUR! CENTURION DETECTS A BUILDUP OF ENERGY INSIDE THE ARCHANGEL-HYBRID ENEMY!_

"Yeah, I bet you fuckin' do. Let's take this cunt down a peg or two."
