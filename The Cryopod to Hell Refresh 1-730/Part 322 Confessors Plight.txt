Part 322: Confessor's Plight

The Cherubiim rockets from Hell Harbor's atmosphere into space, flanked by Jörmungandr and hundreds of its other summons. It leaves behind a few dozen primordial lifeforms to harass and ultimately kill the Demon Emperors, but compared to the massive Volgrim armada, the demons no longer pose the Archangels much threat.

Despite thousands of kilometers separating the Volgrim fleet from Hell Harbor's atmosphere, the Cherubiim advances upon them at an alarming speed. Within seconds, its hundreds of summons crash into the fleet, ripping their mighty ships to fragments. Of the eight thousand vessels present, more than four hundred come under immediate attack, unable to back off compared to the Cherubiim's summons' superior acceleration and maneuverability.

Primordial Angels, Titans, and Dragons treat the Volgrim ships like Christmas presents, tearing them open to expose their insides to the vacuum of the Void. The Psions on board the nearest vessels fight back, ignoring the dangers of space thanks to their durable bodies deliberately designed and modified to unleash hell in even the deadliest environments. However, their fellow Technopath and Changeling Volgrim don't possess the same physical attributes. With almost none of them adapted to the harshness of space, they perish to suffocation at alarming speeds, assuming the primordial beings assaulting their ships don't kill them first.

The Cherubiim does not engage directly in the slaughter, but instead continuously conjures shields of divine energy to protect its summons and itself from the Volgrim's counter-fire. The Technopaths in charge of the fleet unload their ordinance as quickly as possible, shelling the phantasmal monsters battering their forward-vessels with reckless abandon.

"Gunners! Aim for the Titan-Class lifeform attacking our portside nacelle! Missiles, lock onto the Winged-Monster raking our engines! Shields, rotate frequencies!"

"Watch your crossfire! Send out the Psions!"

"We may die here, but we will still honor the Founders!"

Twenty ships explode into flames within the first thirty seconds of combat. So violent is the Cherubiim's initial attack that many Changeling Observers have to scramble to rectify the data.

"Confusion! Our scans only indicated the presence of a single Archangel lifeform! Where did these hundreds of others come from?"

"Unclear. Transmit additional data back to Volgarius. Request additional assistance from the Founders."

"Response negative. The Founders have already given this fleet seven percent of the Volgrim Empire's elites. We cannot afford to divert additional combat vessels away from Plaguetouched territories! We must defeat the Archangel and its supporting lifeforms on our own."

As the battle quickly heats up, the Volgrim attempt to encircle and hem in the Cherubiim's allies, but Jörmungandr alone proves to be a terrifying foe far beyond their initial estimates. The Mother of all Dragons opens her mouth to scream soundlessly into the void, firing off an attack that strikes the souls of every Volgrim present.

The Psions, possessing no souls, barely notice a tingle in the air around themselves. However, the Technopaths and Changelings are not so fortunate. Countless low-ranking crew perish instantly as Jörmungandr's attack obliterates their souls and leaves them laying on the floor as lifeless husks.

While the weakest Volgrim bodies begin to cool, the slightly stronger-willed Volgrim barely survive, but experience agony as their souls suffer terrible tearing and leaking of their soul energy. Thousands of crew fall comatose, never to wake again. Even the crew who remain standing still experience nausea, disorientation, and imbalance in their movements. The Technopath gunners, elites though they be, miss many shots they ordinarily wouldn't, simply because their vision wavers too much for their augments to compensate.

Aboard the Volgrim flagship, the VLL Planet-Crusher, a ship spanning five kilometers in length, one and a half kilometers tall, and three kilometers wide, Confessor Vulpanix immediately notices the drop in her fellow Volgrim's fighting capabilities. Several deckhands collapse to the floor around her, while even the strongest-willed Technopaths and Changelings slump forward in their chairs and breathe raggedly, having experienced a soul-attack the likes of which no Volgrim has endured since the Energy Wars.

[This is the Confessor speaking!] Vulpanix mentally transmits in a wide-area telepathic message to her fellow Psions. [The Archangel has attacked the souls of our brethren! Do not allow these inferior mud-dwellers to sully the name of our great Founders! Exit your ships, enter the void, and slaughter them all! Kill every last Archangel and any of their allies you see!]

[Yes, Confessor!]

[For the glory of the Founders!]

Rather than exiting through their ships' airlocks, the thousands of Psions present teleport directly into the void, some using the power of Spatial Psionics, while other use Temporal Psionics. Any Psion lacking these critical abilities hitch rides with their more capable comrades, eventually forming a wall of deceptively tiny specks compared to the massive primordial lifeforms ravaging the fleet.

Confessor Vulpanix leads the charge. She rips a gash in the Void and steps through it, arriving mere meters to the left of the roaring Jörmungandr.

The dragon senses her presence. It turns toward her, only for an invisible weapon weighing hundreds of tons to slam into its head, knocking it aside. Jörmungandr reels from the hit for only a moment before shaking its head and snapping at the newfound threat.

[You are nothing before the might of this Confessor.] Vulpanix sneers.

Her six insect-like eyes lock onto the dragon as it spews a gout of solar flame at her, the heat of which would easily melt a starship to slag.

However, Vulpanix shimmers out of reality, vanishing before the terrifying attack can strike her. She reappears above Jörmungandr's head with her palm aimed down, conjures a micro-singularity by condensing the space below herself into a black hole, and fires it downwards.

_THUMP!_

The singularity tears through Jörmungandr's head, exploding the dragon's spectral form into particles of light. Satisfied at her conquest, Vulpanix turns to look at the Cherubiim, only to flinch when the headless dragon pounces at her from below and rakes its claws toward her back.

[An undying monster?] Vulpanix wonders.

Jörmungandr's claws impact a barrier surrounding Vulpanix's body. Were they on a world with an atmosphere, the shockwave from that hit would have leveled skyscrapers and flattened mountains, but in space, no noticeable aftereffects occur, keeping the collateral damage to a minimum.

Vulpanix flickers in and out of reality once more, tearing apart the void with the same ease one might rip a wet piece of paper in half. Her body flickers several times as she appears to copy herself, appearing in five separate positions around Jörmungandr at once.

As a master of not only Temporal Manipulation, but Spatial Manipulation as well, the Confessor has evolved her abilities over tens of millennia while battling Volgrimkind's enemies. She breaks down reality itself, temporarily breaking once-thought-immutable laws of physics to create momentary paradoxes.

Each of Vulpanix's copies summons different attacks, all empowered through her innate psionic energy. One hurls an explosive spear of lightning at the headless dragon, while another freezes the space around Jörmungandr, preventing her from moving. Another redirects nearby ordinance fired by her capital ship, shells and missiles that missed their initial targets, to fly at the dragon.

All together, the dragon becomes frozen for five long seconds, unable to protect itself. These deadly planet-busting psionic attacks crash against the Mother of all Dragons, blowing apart even more of her body and turning her into a floating blob of phantasmal energy.

Seconds later, the paradoxes Vulpanix summoned subvert themselves, returning her to reality as just one version of herself.

The shattered and broken body of Jörmungandr wriggles weirdly, making Vulpanix pause for a few seconds to evaluate its threat level. Eventually, it fully breaks apart, leaving the hovering face of a dragon mask in the air, one which rapidly zips away and flies back to the Cherubiim several kilometers in the distance.

The Confessor evaluates the battle she just fought, sizing up the abilities of her enemies, paying careful attention to how badly they are destroying her fleet.

_This Archangel is not like the ones we faced before. Its appearance is wildly different. Might it be a previously hidden one, or perhaps a mutated variant? Its power surely eclipses the strength of the four known Archangels, Raphael, Gabriel, Camael, and Uzziel. As for the two historical Archangels, Uriel and Michael, we possess little information on their battle capabilities; only hearsay given to us by the demons._

Despite Vulpanix's supreme authority among the attacking forces, she has never fought the Archangels, as she was not alive during the Energy Wars. Her experiences come only from the post-collapse of humanity, when she stepped out of her mutation chamber a thousand years after the wars ended. Every bit of experience she possesses comes from historical archives and detailed documents, some of which she only had time to skim before arriving at Hell Harbor.

This lack of insight makes her pause as she carefully considers whether or not she should call for backup from Volgarius.

_No! I alone am enough! I must prove myself if I hope to obtain a worthy new title! How will anyone regard my role as Confessor with respect if I immediately demand backup from the High Psions? They have Plaguetouched worlds to contain! I will destroy the Archangel lifeform myself and earn the title of Divinator!_

She roars back into action, noting the mask's trajectory as it attaches to the Archangel and begins to slowly glow with power.

[Archangel! Your opponent is me! I will crush you and this insurrection, here and now!]

Vulpanix sends out a taunting mental barb at her opponent, transmitting a series of images of her past glory. Scenes of carnage, including her single-handed suppression of a plaguetouched world, give her a sense of pride while hopefully demoralizing the Archangel she intends to slay.

However, unknown to Vulpanix, when Raphael, Michael, and Uriel all receive her mental transmission, their reaction differs greatly from her expectations. The images momentarily startle Raphael as he quickly examines the meaning behind their contents.

_A Plague? What is this lifeform the Volgrim art so hellbent on annihilating? It appears to be something of a ravaging attacker from a distant galaxy. Verily, I hath never seen anything like it in all my years._

Unwittingly, Vulpanix gives her enemies a crucial piece of intel they didn't have before. Rather than demoralizing them, the information only serves to increase their curiosity, particularly Raphael's.

The Cherubiim and Confessor meet each other in battle. Despite towering over Vulpanix and standing more than fifty times her height, the Cherubiim does not act with outright impunity, as Raphael knows well the power even a single high-ranking Psion can unleash.

_Beware, brother and sister. We do not know what branches of psionics this Psion specializes in, but the fact she dares to challenge us directly, even after ripping apart our mightiest summon, indicates she will not be a simple foe. Continue spreading out our summons to attack her fleet; we shall kill her ourselves, god-willing._

The Archangel fires a dozen deadly blasts of holy energy at Vulpanix, but her body flickers from side to side, evading the attacks as she steps in and out of reality. By adjusting the flow of causality, she affects her dimensional position with extreme accuracy, making her movements faster than teleportation or even the concept of 'lightspeed' itself.

Uriel summons more than one hundred swords made from holy energy. She launches them at Vulpanix, slashing her from every direction. However, Vulpanix activates a temporal barrier around herself, causing the weapons to slow down to just 5% of their original speed when they draw within a few meters of the Confessor's body. 

With the Archangel's attacks momentarily slowed to a snail's pace, Vulpanix easily dodges them, levitating through the Void with the ease of a fish in water. She slips through small gaps in the weapons' flight trajectories, steps through tears in space, and draws within striking distance of the Cherubiim!

All at once, the oh-so-close image of the Cherubiim distorts, startling Vulpanix. She finds herself submerged in an illusory realm, one that mirrors her earlier act of duplication by causing the Cherubiim to distort and separate into a dozen separate beings.

[**I know not thy identity,**] Raphael says, transmitting his voice telepathically to his opponent. [**But coming to this battleground was a foolish decision, and the last mistake thou shalt ever make.**]

From out of nowhere, something unfathomably heavy crashes into Vulpanix's back, making her shudder in pain and flinging her off-course. She spins and falls through space, only for the Cherubiim's fist to magically materialize directly in her path.

_THUMP!!_

The Cherubiim socks the Confessor, _hard_. The amalgamated Archangels blast her backward, instantly reversing her momentum and sending her rocketing back toward her fleet. Just as the Cherubiim prepares for a third strike, this time consisting of two spears wielded by Uriel and two swords wielded by Michael, Vulpanix spreads out her arms and halts her movement. She vanishes from sight, stepping through a fold in space to arrive right beside the Cherubiim's core.

_BOOM!!_

A pulse of raw telekinetic energy smashes into the Cherubiim, causing all of its constructs to visibly flicker and weaken for a moment before they regain their spirits and continue attacking.

This slight deviation does not escape Vulpanix's notice.

_The Archangel appears to have its life directly linked with the other creatures attacking my fleet. If I destroy its main body, the others will collapse._

Her cold, predatory gaze fixates on the Cherubiim's core. She rears back to attack again, but the Cherubiim vanishes, melting back into its illusions once more.

At the same time, Jörmungandr's face detaches from the Cherubiim's main body, fully recharged. She regrows her main body and roars, sending another soul-crushing shockwave outward to assault the Volgim fleet. Several alarms beep in Vulpanix's integrated transmitters, but she ignores them, knowing her fleet is taking a beating.

Minutes pass.

Vulpanix battles with Jörmungandr and the Cherubiim both. At one point, a squadron of other Psions show up, delivering terrible news.

[Confessor! Half the fleet has fallen! Our analysts have determined that only by killing the Archangel's main body will we be able to slay its sub-units!]

[I have comprehended the same facts.] Vulpanix cooly replies. [Assist me! Perform an Energy Transference Formation! I will finish the Archangel myself!]

[Yes, Confessor!]

The five Psion disciples, Vulpanix's personal entourage, work together to summon a massive empowered barrier around themselves. While their master battles Jörmungandr, they manipulate their energy into a psionic 'totem' of sorts, one shaped in Vulpanix's image.

[Confessor! The formation is complete!]

Vulpanix flicks her gaze toward the shimmering psionic image of herself, hidden within her fellow Psions' impenetrable shield.

[Excellent work! Now, I shall-]

Before Vulpanix can finish her statement, a cold dread crawls up her back. She only gets an instant to sense a shift in the space around her before the Cherubiim rushes toward her five disciples.

[No!]

Confessor Vulpanix steps through space in an attempt to intercept the Cherubiim, thinking it must intend to attack her disciples. Instead, the Cherubiim changes its body's composition, allowing it to summon a massive ethereal arm made of pure soul energy. It grabs at the barrier enveloping her students, but instead of attacking the barrier, the Cherubiim's limb phases right through!

Raphael, ever the clever Archangel, detects the massive amount of psionic energy Vulpanix's disciples have created and greedily grins.

_The youth are truly too foolish!_

Vulpanix's disciples launch frenzied attacks on the soul-arm reaching into their cage, but their psionic power passes through it harmlessly. Without a moment's hesitation, Raphael snatches the concentrated energy and absorbs it directly into his soul, causing a massive amount of energy to well up within himself.

Comparable to devouring Wolfram's soul, the amassed psionic energy offers the Cherubiim a huge burst of power right when the battle has hit its most critical stage. Before Vulpanix can stop him, Raphael and his siblings devour the energy and ascend to a new level of power, outputting even greater soul pressure than before!

[Thieving mud-dwellers!] Vulpanix roars. [That energy was MINE!]

Enraged like never before, she launches a brutal series of psionic bombardments on the Cherubiim, but Uriel's barriers easily block and defect the attacks, becoming even more resilient than ever. 

Michael's sword dances around like lightning, knocking aside massive fireballs, bolts of psionic lightning, and even another micro-singularity.

All her attacks fail to cause the Cherubiim any discomfort. Despite being a high-ranking Psion, she is not an Elder, she is not the Executor, and neither is she the Second Founder.

Compared to the legendary Psion warriors of yore, those who shattered the heavens and split the galaxy, Vulpanix's power pales when placed alongside the Cherubiim's.

She fights relentlessly, joining forces with her students. Together, the six of them output tremendous psionic firepower against the Cherubiim and its summons, but as the Volgrim fleet falls to pieces, so too do Confessor Vulpanix's chances of defeating her adversary.

Weariness and exhaustion beat her back. The more psionic energy she outputs, the lower her reserves fall, until she can no longer fight at a level that threatens the Cherubiim.

[Gah!! Pull back! Retreat!] Vulpanix roars. [Save what we can of the fleet! We never should have come here!]

The Cherubiim prepares to land a fatal blow, but Vulpanix warps herself and her students away just in the nick of time. She arrives at the rear of the fleet, where the horde of primordial lifeforms have nearly overrun her capitol ship. Despite lacking the power to kill Jörmungandr a second time, or the Cherubiim, she still manages to tear apart the weaker Angels, Titans, and Dragons threatening her flagship.

[Retreat!] Vulpanix repeats to the nearby ships. [Escape while we still have ships worth saving! Maximum warp!]

Minutes later, less than five hundred functioning ships manage to turn around and warp out of the system, while the rest can only cry out and wail at their awful luck. The Cherubiim's forces continue ravaging the remaining ships until they've been completely dismantled, left as nothing more than giant scraps of metal to rain down upon Hell Harbor's surface.

Raphael sweeps his gaze around the battlefield.

_Excellent work. We have crushed the Volgrim armada. Now, let us return to finish off the last demon stragglers._

He and his forces drop back down to Hell Harbor's surface, sending a fresh burst of fear through the struggling Demon Emperors' souls.

Without Wolfram, the demons find themselves completely suppressed by fewer than two dozen of the Cherubiim's primordial constructs. Dukes from the Hidden Hells show up to try and reinforce their Emperors, but even with thirty fresh fighters, they can only manage to hide inside a force-field made of human blood.

Yumagi, Kristoff, and Crow stand together with twenty Dukes from each of their Hells. Several Angels, Titans, and Dragons whale on the blood-barrier protecting them, while Kristoff and several vampires from his Hell holds their arms overhead, pouring as much power into it as they can.

When they sense the return of the Cherubiim and the departure of the Volgrim, a sense of total despair swallows the demons. They finally realize that their day of reckoning has come.

Yumagi grits his teeth. "I hate to say it, but we don't have a chance! I can try to evoke my last two remaining Platinum Aspects, but if a High Psion cannot defeat the Cherubiim, I doubt my cards will succeed either!"

Crow suffers from multiple debilitating wounds. No longer does she possess a left arm, having had it ripped off by a dragon before Kristoff managed to rescue her.

"I cannot believe the angels possessed such power, yet never used it. How can we be so unfortunate? I assumed they were little more than paper tigers, but they were actually dragons in sheep's clothing!"

As the Cherubiim descends, a twisted grin spreads across Michael's face.

"**Ah... now those are the sorts of expressions I enjoy seeing on bloodskin faces. Thy despair finally gives me a faint bit of comfort in my undead form. After we smite the last few remaining Demon Emperors, the rest of thy people shalt fall like wheat to the chaff. None can stop the Cherubiim.**"

At that moment, the ground explodes like a bomb! Thousands of tons of dirt and rocks shoot into the upper atmosphere, some of the debris even bouncing off the Cherubiim's reinforced body.

The three Archangels turn their gaze toward the newcomers, only to see Bael, Fae, and a few other demons crawling out of a hole in the ground. All of them freeze in place and gaze at the Cherubiim like deer caught in the headlights.

Bael's jaw drops visibly. "What the heck?! That's the biggest chicken I've ever seen!"

"Is that the Archangels?!" Fae asks. "Spawn of a Broodmother! What the fuck happened while we were out? Did they eat a bunch of steroids?!"

Despite the unexpected appearance of two Demon Emperors, the Archangels appear less than impressed. Raphael gazes condescendingly at Fae, but pauses for a moment to examine Bael warily.

"**Emperor Ose. It seems thou hath survived thy death and become an undead puppet...**" Raphael says. "**Of all the demons here, thou art the one that might be able to cause us some slight harm. Sadly, thou art outnumbered in every sense of the word. Thou art truly... inferior.**"

Bael stares vacantly at the Cherubiim for a moment before blinking his eyes.

"...Huh? Wait, me? No, no, no, pal. You've got it all wrong. I'm not-"

"**Thou art not going to live a moment longer,**" Raphael interrupts, before aiming his palm at Bael and charging a massive blast of holy energy. "**Tis best if we smite thee here before thou can becometh a thorn in our side.**"

"No, no, no smiting, please! My heart can't take it!" Bael yelps. "C-can't we just talk this out, bub? AW SHIT!"

His words don't make Raphael pause for even a moment. The Archangel of Wisdom releases his charged blast, firing a deadly stream of holy energy toward Bael that will surely vaporize him and the other Demons around him.

"Watch out!" Emperor Fae yells. She summons bone armor onto her body, then coils strength into her legs in order to jump into the path of the oncoming beam, hopefully saving the lives of the other demons.

Before Fae can intercept the attack, Hell Harbor's continent rumbles.

A massive blackened hand bursts out of the dirt directly in the path of the Archangel's attack!

Raphael's holy energy crashes against the hand and explodes, yet the monstrous limb barely takes any damage at all. 

The Cherubiim stares at the unexpected defense in surprise.

_What new manner of devilry is this?_ The Archangels wonder.

Moments later, a second hand bursts out of the ground, followed by a head. A gigantic undead Brute, more than three hundred meters tall, emerges from the planet's depths and roars at the top of its lungs, drawing the attention of everyone present.

At the same time, several undead Demon Emperors bubble up from the ground, including Emperor Fenrir, Emperor Gorn, and Emperor Zamiel.

Emperor Fenrir's eyes glow with a dull light. After her original soul was obliterated by Raphael's soul-crushing attack, her body remained without a host. Now, Emperor Lupus, lacking a body of her own, slips inside to try and clumsily take control of Fenrir's power. While somewhat incompatible with Fenrir's original abilities, Lupus still possesses the soul and body foundation of a true Emperor, making her the ideal 'fit.'

Finally, a low, cackling laughter spreads throughout the air.

"HEH... HEH... HEH... HEH!"

Mephisto emerges from the planet's underbelly. He levitates into the sky, shocking the other Emperors with his newly reinforced mana signature.

"That bastard..." Fae hisses. "While we were fighting for our lives, Mephisto stole souls from the River Styx! He became an Emperor without our permission!"

Bael breathes a sigh of relief. "Eh, whatever! At least we've got a chance, now."

Mephisto, now the Emperor of Legions, cackles cruelly while facing the Cherubiim.

"What luck I have for you to deliver me sssuch fine sssoulsss and bodiesss to empower myssself! I thought my luck had run out, but it ssseemsss I have reaped quite the bounty today! Kekeke!!"

Kristoff's eyes fixate on his wife's body. He immediately realizes the soul possessing it is not hers, making his heart ache.

"Mephisto! You body-stealing thief!"

The Emperor of Legions turns to look at Kristoff, laughing all the while. "Kekeke! I have come to sssave you! Don't everyone thank me at onccce! Now, behold the power of a necromancccer Emperor! Not sssince Valac'sss era hasss a mighty demon like myssself roamed the cosssmosss!"

Unlike the Brutes Mephisto made when he was a Duke, his newest champion possesses truly extraordinary defenses, high resistance to holy energy, and a body capable of unleashing death upon those unlucky enough to face it. The Brute roars at the Cherubiim, taunting them and daring them to attack.

"**Hmph. A few more Emperors, thou sayeth? Verily, we art quaking in our boots. Come, then, Mephisto. Deliver thyself to us on a platter. We shalt offer thee one chance to save thyself. But know this; thou cannot defeat the Cherubiim. Thou art _far_ from worthy.**"

Mephisto's cackling ceases as a smoldering fury erupts in his eyes.

"Oh? You claim that we lack the qualificationsss?! We ssshall sssee about that, Archangelsss! Brute, ATTACK!"
