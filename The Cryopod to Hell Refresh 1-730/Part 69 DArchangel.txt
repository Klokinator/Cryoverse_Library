Part 69: D'Archangel

The Labyrinth junction reveals a path ahead, as well as to the left and right, forming a plus shape. I sense the bloodskins. I smell their fear, their terror. They lurk at the edges of both hallways, ready to strike. Two Barons stand alongside dozens of grunts, one on each side. I know not their identities.

Nor do I care.

My armored feet, those belonging to Barbatos, shake the ground as I walk. I'm mostly naked, now. Only boots, gauntlets, and a helmet cover my body. Shame? I feel none. This disgusting form is merely a vessel I borrow to exact my revenge — my punishment on those who slew my brothers and sisters.

All bloodskins must die. I will force them to grovel and weep in the moments before I snuff their lives out forever.

I approach the junction. Fifty feet. Forty. Thirty.

One hundred and sixty-four demons burst from their hiding places and charge at me; bloodlust etched onto their faces.

"KILL THE ARCHANGEL! SLAY URIEL!" One of the Barons, a male, bellows triumphantly. His moment of glory is soon to come. He knows he cannot fail.

The other Baron, a female, is not so sure. "Uriel is powerful! Dodge anything she throws at you!"

"Fools," I whisper. "My advance cannot be halted. I am inevitable."

Raising a hand, I summon a wall of Holy Light, sealing off the path before me, just as I did in the Ancient Era when the demons assaulted Heaven's Gate. Both Barons slam against the barrier, followed immediately by several dozen scythers and shadow-walkers. The demons stagger backward and stare at my divine shield in disbelief.

The confusion on the bloodskin's faces would make me laugh if I weren't already furious. My rage is not a simple matter of emotion, nor is it about the loss my people and I have suffered.

I hate demonkind, but I hate myself more. Weak. Useless. I made a mistake, one which ended my life. I can never redeem myself, nor can I bring back my brothers and sisters.

All I can do is bring holy vengeance upon those who wronged us. I have no other reason to live.

That is why I have assumed the title: _Archangel of Retribution._

The Barons quickly form hand signs, likely in an attempt to nullify my barrier. Perhaps if they focused their minds for a thousand years, they might manage such a feat. Sadly, only seconds remain.

I raise my other hand. A second wall of light forms behind the contingent of bloodskins, sealing off their escape. Their confusion turns to panic. They swivel on their heels and press against my barriers, but such weak strength will not save them.

"Uriel! You bastard! I haven't forgotten what you did!" The female Baron turns to glower at me. Her eyes burn like embers of flame, scorching the innermost parts of her soul.

"Have we met?"

"No. But I remember you. I was only an imp when you last struck. You killed over ten thousand-"

"I don't care," I reply, cutting her off. "Thy words serve only to slow me down. Perish with the rest of thy kind."

Slowly, I pull my left hand back. The second wall of light behind the attackers begins to press against the first, crushing the demons within. The Baron's eyes widen as she realizes my goal. Immediately, she forms hand signs, teleports through my barrier, and jumps at me, raising two knives to strike.

"Filthy Angel! One thousand years of suffering!!"

A smile caresses my face. _One thousand? That's all? If only thou understood._

I tilt my head. Her knives yank to the side. The bloodskin yelps in surprise and slams against the wall. I nod, causing the knives to jump out of her hands and follow my eyes. They float above her for only a moment before plunging down, impaling her into the floor.

"Aaaaiiiee!!!" She squirms and tries to pull away, but her efforts only cause more blood to spill from her wounds.

To amuse myself while I crush her allies, I wiggle the knives back and forth, widening the holes in her heart and stomach. Her movements grow more sluggish. She claws at the ground, trying desperately to free herself. Eventually, the fire in her eyes cools.

The Baron slumps over, dead. Her allies scream in agony as my walls compress them together. They implode from the inside out and melt together into a mass of blood and bone.

Satisfied, I release my barriers. The river of gore splatters across the floor and rushes around my boots.

The smell of bloodskins somehow soothes me. They stink of unwashed dogs, yet knowing that another hundred and sixty-four have died gives me comfort.

A tingling in the back of my mind makes me groan.

**You damned monster — servant of the enemy. You could have spared them. I know angels are no better than demons, but I wish you would prove me wrong. Cease the cycle of violence. Barbatos has shirked a life of endless murder, so why can't-**

"Silence, Artorias. Thy feeble attempts to dissuade me shall prove useless, as always." A throaty chuckle escapes my mouth. "Hehehe... Thy brother made several mistakes, but going without sleep for forty thousand years was by far the biggest. He weakened himself more and more as time went on. He won't wake up in a few hours, as he did before. This time, I am fully in control."

**You underestimate Barbatos's resolve.**

_Splash. Splash._ I stomp through the lake of blood and continue walking down the hallway. "Resolve won't be enough. Not this time."

I pass the junction and continue walking. One mile. Two miles. Three.

Being able to draw upon Barbatos and Artorias's energy reserves in addition to mine is a stroke of luck, but at my current speed, it will take me a thousand years to slay all the bloodskins.

I raise my hand again to form magical signs. _Summon. Summon._

Two angels birthed of Holy Energy appear at my side. The faceless creatures stare ahead blankly and await my orders.

"Unit One. Unit Two. We'll enter a population center, soon. One, protect my flank. Two, if any Dukes or Emperors show up, slow them down. I'll finish them myself."

The three of us continue walking. Artorias bubbles up again. **What are these creations? I've never seen anything like them before. Certainly not from you.**

"Light Constructs. Aren't they beautiful? Compared to the disgusting, malformed look of thy species, these lovelies exude righteous auras befitting my people. They will serve as my agents of justice. I shall take the demonic energy from those I kill and use it to create more allies of light. We will transform this hellhole into a place where humans and angels can live free of bloodskin oppression."

**Living weapons. Why am I not surprised? Your idea of freedom is little more than genocide.**

"Yes. Bloodskins slew my people. I will return the favor tenfold."

**An eye for an eye only makes the world blind.**

"Human proverbs mean little to me. They failed to protect those who gave their existence meaning. In my eyes, they share the blame for what happened to the angels and gods."

I raise my head and peer forward. The sound of a multitude of demons catches my attention.

"Ho ho, listen to that. The fools haven't a clue what's coming."

A bloodskin stiffens. He looks around, scanning the dimly lit room. "Did you guys hear somethin'?"

I stifle a laugh. Barbatos's unique power, Dark Form, makes me invisible. It extinguishes my Holy Energy entirely and merges my body into the world of darkness, allowing me to bypass anyone standing in my way.

Silently, I walk past the demons at the entrance to the center of the chamber. It appears to be a slave factory. Barbatos's memory tells me it is the Smeltery, where laborers forge weapons and armors for low-rank demons and monsters.

However, this area also has a Keymaster. If I can catch him, I'll be able to force him to open a portal to Hell Harbor.

And if I make it there...

I stop in the center of the room and look around. My constructs wait for me in the corridor, flattening themselves against the wall so that they won't draw attention.

My eyes lock onto a figure dressed in black robes. It's my lucky day.

Grigori, Diablo's warper, sits at a table reading a book. What is it about? I couldn't care less. I'm lucky this region of the Labyrinth hasn't heard about my approach yet. I can grab him and-

"She's here!"

A squeaky voice shrieks out a warning from the ceiling. I raise my eyes to look in its direction. A goblin with a strange device on his head watches me intently. "The Archangel!"

The walls come alive. Camouflaged shadow-walkers leap into action, jumping at me from every angle. Grigori leaps to his feet to reveal a partially casted magic seal. He creates a portal, allowing hundreds of reinforcements to pour through and rush at me.

Hehe. An ambush. I fell for it.

Fortunately, the bloodskins here are no match for me.

I flick a finger, and a portal of my own appears, allowing my Constructs to leap into action beside me. The appearance of my Constructs causes some of the shadow-walkers to stiffen in surprise as they rush toward me. 

Unit One summons a sword and shield of light. He pounces at the shadow-walkers and cuts five of them in half before they can react.

Unit Two creates a hole in her head. She fires several tiny blasts of Holy Energy and blows out the brains of a dozen other shadow-walkers.

The rest of the shadow-walkers fall toward me. I can't block all of them.

Why bother?

I inhale. My Dark Form converts into its polar opposite, Light Form. Holy Energy radiates from my body, blinding the shadow-walkers and burning their skin. They scream in pain and cover their eyes, only to splash against me like a pile of wet rags. I don't flinch as the eighty demons slap my flesh. They fall to the ground and try to scurry away, shrieking like banshees the entire time. My Constructs make short work of the blinded, fleeing bastards.

Holy Energy is the bane of demonkind. The kill switch. Angels created the demons. We gave them life. We formed them from the dust of the Earth to be our servants, and as such, we could end them whenever we pleased.

If only we had done so sooner.

"Thy people art lucky I lost Gungnir and the Gae Bolg, all those years ago. However, I will make do."

Raising both hands, I summon two spears of light into my grasp. The army of demons that poured through Grigori's portal only moments before slows to a crawl. The bloodskins recoil in anguish as my brilliant aura beats upon their faces.

I jump into action. Using my spears, I stab and cut apart shadow-walkers, scythers, grunts, and a variety of monsters as well. Their screams of agony mix with those my Constructs slay, forming a beautiful symphony of death.

A Demon Lord steps out of the crowd and aims his palm at me. He shoots a ball of corrosive acid, but I counter with a Light Wall, making it explode and backfire against him. He's impervious to the acid, but it catches him off-guard enough for me to raise the barrier and slam it down on top of him, flattening him into a puddle of blood.

Horrified looks spread across the faces of the bloodskins.

"W-who in the hell are we dealing with?!"

"Hold your ground! There's only one enemy!"

"We have to slow him down until the Dukes arrive!"

I roll my eyes. _The foolish bloodskins always assume I'm a man. If they knew a female Archangel was responsible for their annihilation, they'd probably kill themselves out of embarrassment._

With a flick of my wrist, I gently lob my spear of light into the crowd of bloodskins. It explodes, sending shards of Holy Energy shrapnel flying in all directions. Each of the shards tears through multiple enemies and pivots in midair to fly back and target others. They buzz around like a cloud of wasps, ripping demons and monsters to shreds indiscriminately.

My Constructs walk over to me and nod. _Done._

The three of us watch with satisfaction as my shards of light tear apart everyone in their path. I flick my eyes over toward Grigori, who stands alone and trembles as he watches the others fall. When he meets my gaze, he panics and quickly forms signs to make a portal.

He thinks he can escape. That's adorable.

Grigori's belt has an iron engraving of a phoenix on it. I use it to yank him toward me. He screams in fright and tries to undo the belt, but his neck lands neatly in my palm, and I slam him into the ground.

"Hello, Grigori. Remember me?"

The bloodskin grits his teeth. "Uriel. You... you foul..."

"Now now, no time for pet names. Open the portal to Hell Harbor, or I'll rip thee limb from limb."

"You'll do it anyway! Piss off, you damned witch!"

"Correct. Still, I can drag out thy suffering for quite a while before painting the Labyrinth with thy innards. There are other Keymasters out there. Would not thou prefer I attack Hell Harbor, where I'll have to face the fearsome, monstrous Dukes?"

"You can't trick me. I remember what you did last time! Torture me all you please, but I'll never open the gate!!"

The Warper reaches into his robe and yanks out a knife, then stabs it into my arm. "Get off me!!"

I barely feel the blade's edge. Warpers have pitiful combat abilities.

Silently, I squeeze Grigori's neck hard enough to snap a bone. He gasps and squeezes my arm, trying to pull me off. "A-angel... scum... let me..."

I take my remaining spear of Holy Energy and slowly press it against his chest. The energy funnels into his blood, causing him to spasm violently.

"AHHH!! AHHHH!!!!! IT BURNS!! TUH-TAKE IT OUT!!"

"This won't end thy life, Grigori, but it will hurt worse than anything thou hath ever experienced. I'll keep the spear pressed to thy heart until thou summon the portal."

He screams even louder and kicks like a wild minotaur. "OUT!! TAKE IT OOOUT!!!"

A sensation of air rushes past my body. I glance up at where my Holy Shards eviscerated the bloodskins, and cock my head at the sight of a second portal. My eyes widen greedily. _That one leads to Hell Harbor. Perfect._

Then, something surprising happens. Someone walks in front of it and steps through. 

_Beelzebub._

The newly crowned Duke gestures with his hand, and the portal closes.

"Uriel. I've heard rumors about your existence. Release Grigori. I'll be your foe."

I slowly stand up to Barbatos's full height. I'm a few inches taller than the newcomer, and Barbatos's body is far stockier and more muscular than the Duke of Inferno.

Grigori slumps in my grasp. I toss him to Unit One, and he catches the bloodskin with ease.

"Dost thee plan to fight me by thyself?"

Beelzebub undoes his suit collar and peels off his top. He tosses it to the side. "Oh, yes. I will. I'd have come sooner if I could, but I had a date with the warm side of a bench. I hope you don't mind."

_Haha. I can't believe my luck. I thought I'd have to fight the Dukes and Emperors all at the same time, but this buffoon wants to fight me one-on-one._

I plant the butt of my spear on the ground. "Oh, I'm going to enjoy this."
