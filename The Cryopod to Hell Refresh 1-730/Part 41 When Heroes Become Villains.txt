Part 41: When Heroes Become Villains

**[Recommended Listening](https://youtu.be/90o0Aq4VfRg?t=96)**

Jason stares blankly at Amelia as she takes a step back. "You brought this upon yourself."

In his hand, a sword of pure blackness forms. It's a weapon familiar to Amelia, one she saw long ago when Joan tore her to shreds.

_Catherine. The Sword From Heaven._

"No! No!!" Amelia shrieks as she backs away even further. "How can you- where did you get that?!"

The Wordsmith rises, and his wounds heal. Dark energy rushes into his skin, and his eyes shift from white to grey to black. "**Achilles. Orion. Hermes.**"

Several pieces of armor appear in midair, wrapping around and encasing his body in darkness. "The Armor of Achilles, the Belt of Orion, and the Boots of Hermes. All legendary treasures from on high, all of them ruined by this godforsaken hell. If I must wear them to defeat you, I will."

The Black Queen raises Dellfingler defensively. Her nervousness and fear threatens to paralyze her. "W-what are these artifacts, Blaarjiim? I've... I've never seen anything like them."

Blaarjiim shakes his head. "Neither have I, my queen. If they're even close to the level of the Sword of Heaven, then this battle won't last-"

Jason leaps at Amelia, screaming with a pitch similar to a minotaur. "**GROOAAR!**" He swings his sword down, and Amelia barely raises Dellfingler in time to block. The impact of their weapons flattens the grass around them.

"Ow! Oh god, that hurt!" Dellfingler cries out in pain, but barely gets a second's reprieve before Jason's sword turns into a blur of motion. _TING-TING-TWANG THUD!_ 

Amelia deflects three of Jason's attacks before dodging the fourth and jumping back. The Wordsmith smashes his sword into the ground, spraying dirt in a wide arc. After a moment, his tongue snakes out. "**Where do you think you're going?**"

Blaarjiim assesses the situation. "My queen, something about those artifacts is corrupted. They're poisoning the mortal's mind. He's not fighting like he normally does."

Jason cackles evilly. "**Hahaha! You don't say! Oh, Amelia! I haven't forgotten what you DID to me!**" He stands up straight and aims the sword away from his body, as if lowering his defenses. "**For thirty days, I lay in that fucking bed! Paralyzed, unable to speak, barely able to think!**"

Jason vanishes, startling Amelia and Blaarjiim with his speed. A moment later, he reappears behind the Black Queen and licks his lips. "**I'll make you SUFFER!**"

Jason grabs Amelia by the hair. He ignores her scream of fright as he flips her over his head and slams her into the ground. Thanks to Amelia's enhanced body, the attack barely hurts her, but fear still appears in her eyes. She swings Dellfingler and slices the back of Jason's legs, but he doesn't even wince in pain. The Wordsmith releases Amelia for a moment, falls forward, and heals the wound with his Wordsmithing. "**Heal.**"

Dellfingler yells, "W-watch out, honey! He's strong as the Devil himself! That dark energy's messing with his head!"

Amelia dodges another grab as Jason swipes at her. He grows enraged, and his hands turn into the claws of a werewolf. He grips the corrupted Sword of Heaven more tightly and spins to face her. "**I released you from that statue, and look at how you repaid me! You split my throat, broke my back, and left me to die!**"

Amelia's hand trembles as she grips Dellfingler. "That's not true! I healed you! I even made sure everyone knew you were lying outside the Core!"

"**LIAR!**" Jason stomps toward her, and his dark energy shifts around him, transforming him from a man into something resembling a Shade. His body becomes slightly fuzzy and indistinct, and then morphs into a shadow of a woman.

Amelia's eyes widen in terror. "N-no! Joan! Stay away!" She takes a step back, fails to notice a rock, and trips. At that moment, Jason lunges forward, clearing the short gap instantaneously.

"My queen!"

The Sword Of Heaven swings up, lops off Amelia's right arm with one movement, then reverses direction and cuts off her other arm. She flops onto her back and screams in pain. "Aaiie! No, Joan, please!! It hurts!!"

Jason grins devilishly, and yanks Dellfingler out of her dismembered hand. 

"You'd better stop this right now, you little punk!" Dellfingler roars.

Jason ignores the sword's protests and plunges Dellfingler into Amelia's stomach, then stabs the Sword of Heaven into her heart, pinning Amelia to the ground.

"Aaaah! Uwaaa! It hurts! Hnnngh!"

It takes several seconds before she can do anything other than cry and choke for breath. Jason leans over her body and smirks. "**Not so tough now, are you? Fucking bitch. Do you think this ends here?! I've barely begun!**"

The Black Queen wiggles and tries to kick Jason, but he wraps his legs around hers, pinning her completely. She whimpers in return. "Joan... please... I'm sorry. I'm sorry. I didn't mean-"

"**I'm not Joan! Stop calling me that!**" Jason gnashes his teeth. "**You hated me because I was weak, but now I'm unstoppable! It takes a monster to kill a monster! Beg for mercy!**"

Jason leans imposingly over Amelia's helpless body. His expression changes slightly as his eyes travel from her legs all the way up to her face. "**Maybe I've been doing this all wrong. There's a way to get out of your little predicament, Amelia. If you stay by my side and promise to be my whore, I'll make sure nobody hurts you. Not the demons, not Joan... nobody but me. You get to live; we get to be together...**"

His face approaches hers. Amelia squirms and glances down at her arm stumps. They still haven't grown back yet. Catherine's holy energy, driven straight into Amelia's heart, prevents her body from regenerating.

Jason caresses Amelia's thighs. Slowly, his werewolf-like claws roam up her body to her breasts, only to come to a stop on her bloody arm stumps. The mortal presses her against the ground, digging into her wounds. 

Amelia cries out in pain. "Eeeyahh!!"

Jason softens his voice. "**We don't have to hurt each other anymore. Just say you want me, Amelia.**" He leans down to kiss her, but Amelia wrenches her head away.

"Stay away! You're not Jason. I hate you." Tears form in her eyes as she tries to avoid his touch. "I knew it. You're no better than Joan."

Her voice shakes, despite her attempt to dampen her terror. "I cared about you. For a day or two, maybe it would have worked. But I knew, once I found out you were a Hero, it was over between us. I finally understand what that armor is. It's not making you evil, Jason... it's drawing out your deepest desires."

Jason falters, pulling back a few inches. He scowls. "**Lies. The armor is evil, not I.**"

"No, you are." Amelia turns to face him defiantly, their faces separated by inches. "If I'm wrong, then kill me. Don't turn me into a plaything. Take the sword and cut my head off, then destroy the rest of my body with your Wordsmithing."

The Wordsmith stares at Amelia for several long moments, taking shallow breaths. "**I... I don't want to.**"

A moment passes. 

Amelia nods knowingly.

"You said the armor would give you the strength to kill me, but that's not true. The last thing you want is for me to die. You want my body; nothing else. Admit it. The corrupted armor brings out your selfishness, your repressed thoughts. That's all it does. It isn't _making_ you evil, Jason. Those thoughts were there all along."

He jumps off her and takes a few steps back. "**Y-you're wrong! All wrong!**"

"I'm not!" Amelia doesn't kick or try to stand. Instead, she glances away, across the field and into the distance. "You could have been honest, Jason. If there was even a drop of love in your heart or a pale shadow of empathy for others, you could have told me you loved me."

She inhales painfully, the swords still impaling her feminine form to the ground. "But now you can't. You've done the unforgivable. Look at yourself. You wanted to be a righteous Hero who fought for truth and goodness, but you're as much of a failure as all the previous ones were! It's true, Jason. I wanted to enslave you, make you into a weapon against the Emperors. The difference is that I have no path to redemption. I'm evil. Stained."

"**Amelia...**" Jason whispers, his eyes downcast. "**I... I didn't mean to hurt you.**"

Amelia stares up at the sky, the sun waning in the distance. "I deserve what you've done to me. But... you were supposed to be better." She chokes and tears up. "You were supposed to be better! You were supposed to be a good man! Why did you have to change?! I hate you! I hate everything about you!!" 

Amelia kicks and squirms violently for several seconds, futilely trying to break free of her impalement. She fails.

The Wordsmith says nothing for several moments, as Amelia sobs and gasps. Despite his demonic, monstrous appearance, his aura shrinks until it's no longer recognizable. The intimidating monster fades away, revealing a broken husk of a man.

Jason raises his head and walks to Amelia, then slowly kneels beside her. His voice normalizes as his negative energy fades. "You're... you're right. I can't apologize enough. I don't know what to say."

He reaches over and yanks the swords out of Amelia's heart and stomach, sending blood flying. Then he tosses the weapons to the side. "**Heal.**"

Amelia stares at him silently as he sits beside her. Her arms rapidly regenerate. Despite their battle only a minute before, she lies still and doesn't move to attack him.

"Jason?"

He avoids her gaze. "Don't look at me."

"I'm not mad."

"You should be. I am."

Jason leans over and starts yanking on the Boots of Hermes. He struggles for a solid minute before sighing. "I can't... can't get these fucking things off." His voice cracks as he fights back tears.

Amelia pulls herself into a sitting position. She silently takes Dellfingler and sheathes him inside his scabbard.  "Jason. I've never seen you in as much pain as you are right now. Look at me."

He turns away, putting his back to her instead. "I don't want to. Everything you said was right. All your fears... all your suspicions. You were right to hate me."

"That's not what I said," Amelia replies, chewing her lip. "I never hated you, Jason. Not until today. Not until you attacked me. Not until you brought out the evil within yourself. Still... I would be hypocritical if I acted like you were the only one at fault."

His voice is numb. "I suppose."

Amelia pulls herself a little closer and wraps her arms around Jason. "You love me... don't you?"

He doesn't answer.

"You can say it, Jason. Now is the time for honesty."

While she wraps herself around Jason's back, he rubs tears from his eyes and stifles a sob. "I.... I did. I do. Maybe. I don't know. What I did wasn't loving at all. If this armor brings out my inner thoughts, then I'm just a sick, evil man. I'm worse than the demons."

"I wouldn't go that far."

The Black Queen pulls away from Jason. She sits and stares at his back for an entire minute. Already, the waning sun casts long shadows across the plains. She looks up at the darkening sky and sighs.

"Jason. I want you to kill me."

The Wordsmith sniffles, then turns to look at her. "What? I can't. I won't."

"You need to. I'm a mass-murderer. I won't stop until I've devoured the universe."

Another minute passes. Jason strokes the dead, brown grass. "Don't ask me to hurt you."

Amelia narrows her eyes. She climbs to her feet and walks over to Catherine. The sword lies on the ground a dozen feet away. 

With a light kick, Amelia sends the sword clattering over to Jason. "Pick it up. This time, finish what you started."

"I won't." Jason turns away again and stares into the distance.

"Pick the fucking sword up."

"No."

Amelia shakes her head. "You almost had me. Goddamnit Jason. I told you to stop hurting me, not to spare my life! There's a difference!"

He doesn't reply for a few moments. "To me, those are the same thing."

"They aren't."

Blaarjiim watches silently, trying to understand his queen's thoughts. Never before have they been so chaotic and disorganized. Confusion and contradictions swirl around in her mind.

"My queen, please, stop and think. Take the Wordsmith's life. Enslave him like we planned. Then, you can-"

Amelia mutters under her breath, "No, Blaarjiim. I won't. I've made up my mind."

"You wish to die? Why?! Don't throw your life away!"

She ignores him. "Goddamn you, Jason. Finish me off. You're a Hero. I'm a monster. It's your destiny. Your job."

He crosses his arms. "Fuck destiny."

Amelia inhales. "Fine. I'll beat you to death. How about that?"

The Wordsmith shrugs and looks away. "Go ahead. I don't care anymore."

His apathy pushes her over the edge. "Argh!! One little failure, and you fall to pieces. Pathetic! If you won't fight me willingly, then I'll make you!"

Amelia unsheathes Dellfingler, then rushes at Jason. She strikes the front of his chest armor, punches his stomach, and sends him careening head over heels several feet away. He lands in a patch of dirt and lays limp.

Jason rolls over and shakes his head, unharmed by her attack. "I won't fight you."

"Bastard! Stop mocking me!" Amelia forces anger into her voice, but her heart isn't in it. She wants to fight him, but only because she doesn't know how to deal with her feelings either.

Jason pulls himself up. "Your attack lacked conviction. Hurry up and kill me, Amelia. I know you want to."

"Ooooh!" She howls in frustration, then stabs Dellfingler into the dirt. "I can't! I can't do it if you won't fight back! Come on! Summon Joan again!"

Jason doesn't reply. His emotions fade away, leaving nothing behind. He becomes little more than a sailor floating atop an ocean of despair.

Amelia sinks to the ground. She buries her head in her hands. "I can't do it, Blaarjiim. I can't kill him. What changed? Why... why?"

Begrudgingly, the orb of darkness offers his opinion. "You wanted honesty from the mortal. Despite the unhealthy way it manifested, you got what you wished for. Haah... perhaps Dellfingler was right. Kiss and make up, or go your separate ways."

Time passes. The young woman and man sit across from each other, a fifty-foot gap separating them. Neither looks the other in the eye.

Amelia blinks and raises her head. "I can't kill Jason. He won't kill me. There's only one thing I can do."

She forms signs with her hand and whispers. Beside her, dark energy coalesces into the form of a familiar red-skinned demon. His short, stocky body, and strangely ill-fitting suit combine to give him something of a comical appearance.

The newcomer yawns. "Aaah, geez. What's the big idea? Why'd you wake me up?"

Jason, hearing the unfamiliar voice, looks up. "A demon...? Who?"

A twinkle of recognition appears in the newcomer's eyes. "Oh? Is this that guy you were telling me about?"

Amelia stares sullenly at the ground. "Yes."

"Great! Might as well introduce myself, then. 'Sup, kid. I'm Satan, the former First Emperor of the demons. You must be the Wordsmith. The Black Queen kept _blubbering_ about you."

Jason jumps to his feet and looks at Amelia in confusion. "The First Emperor? You're allies with _him_?! But... but he's supposed to be dead! You killed him!"

Satan rubs his muscular bicep. "The reports of my demise have been greatly exaggerated."

The Wordsmith eyes the First Emperor cautiously. While Jason towers over Satan, standing seven-feet tall to the Devil's below-six-foot stature, the demon's stocky body and nonchalant attitude gives him a far more intimidating presence.

Amelia touches Satan's leg. "I can't fight Jason anymore. Please... finish the job. Leave him almost dead so I can-"

Satan rolls his eyes. "Steal his soul. Duh. I know the drill."

The former First Emperor raises his wrist to stare at a strange-looking analog wristwatch, one without any moving parts. Then, he crosses the field, closing the gap between himself and the Wordsmith. "I don't get out often. Give me some fun, chum. If you don't, I'll hurt ya ten times worse than you could ever imagine."

Amelia turns away and closes her eyes.

"I can't watch."
