Part 282: Ose's Checkmate

In the skies of Tarus II, an unimaginably terrifying thunderstorm swallows the horizon. Covering the entirety of Hero City and all of the battlefronts, it swells to more than forty miles wide. Inside, Ose draws energy from the planet's magnetic field, capturing small amounts of its potential to energize her ultimate weapon, Limitless Demonic Thunder.

As Ose conjures energy onto herself, Belial flies through the storm, searching in vain for Ose's true body.

"Show yourself, Ose! Where are you? Come and face me!"

Ose, however, remains silent. With her body incorporeal and fused into the storm's latent electrical energy, her presence becomes impossible to detect. Her vision penetrates every inch of the cloud's domain, allowing her to easily track Belial's location.

_As if I would fall for such a childish provocation..._ Ose sneers. _Don't worry, Belial. I'll certainly deal with you in a bit. But first, how about these humans?_

She turns away from Belial and extends her perception downward. Ose scrutinizes the tiny, insignificant humans battling her equally tiny and insignificant armies. Compared to the gargantuan storm in the sky, they aren't more than specks of sand when viewed from such a height.

Ose's senses sweep across the land. She picks out numerous key areas of conflict, places she can attack to deal heavy damage.

_I've held back, until now. I wanted to slowly make my way into humanity's fortress and capture their leaders. However, I can't keep going on like this._

She turns her attention to the Eastern Front. There, she witnesses Blinker slaughtering her army with reckless abandon, driving them into a terror-filled flight. None escape her wrath as she conjures enormous claws to fall upon them from above, crushing them into meat paste.

_It seems Mephisto slew the Monster King. But at what cost? He lost two of his best Barons. Given how the Fairy's strength has increased, she must have absorbed the Sphinx's power for herself. Even I might not be a match for her as she is now._

She glances toward the south, where Yamir and several officers fight alongside a platoon of veterans to hold the armies back.

_Bael's an idiot, Artorias has gone AWOL, I'm the only Emperor, and even Beelzebub has fucked off to the planet's crust. Who knows when he'll return? Right now, I can only rely on myself._

Ose continues looking. _Damn! Where has Mephisto gone? Has he hidden himself beneath some rock like he always does? Where is Mara? Vepar? Laharl?_

Ose turns her attention toward the city, where she takes a moment to observe the battles between five Titans and the lone Hydra.

Sure enough, Cronus, Zeus, Ivaldi, and two other Titans beat the Hydra black and blue. As always, the Hydra merely shrugs off their attacks while screeching incoherently.

Ose observes the situation intently. She narrows her eyes while looking at the other two Titans, trying to identify them.

_Both use water-based magic. Hm? Poseidon and Neptune? Father and Son? What a strange coincidence. These five Titans have never walked the Earth at the same time. Ivaldi... I believe she only reappeared during the War in Heaven, then she vanished back to wherever she came from. As for Cronus, he died to his son's insidious deception. Zeus survived all the way to the final battle of the Energy Wars, along with Neptune, while Poseidon..._

Several seconds pass. Ose frowns as she tries to recall long-ancient events.

_I don't believe I ever heard how Poseidon perished. Tsk. In any case, for these five to randomly show up here, there can be no mistake that someone brought them back from the dead. But who?_

The Emperor's thoughts dwell mostly upon the Wordsmith, Jason Hiro, as well as his clone, Hope Hiro.

_I've battled Jason once already, but not once has Hope made an appearance. This is strange, far too strange. For the Wordsmith to ignore his home's destruction, something must have happened to him._

Ose's eyes flash with realization.

_Spawn of a broodmother! Did the Hidden Emperors make a move while I fought tooth and nail during these past three days? Perhaps one of them captured Hope without my knowledge! If the Hidden Emperors make a pawn out of a Wordsmith, it won't be long before the First Hell loses its position! I'm already much weaker than the other Emperors, having lost my power base. I can't afford for one of them to obtain such a valuable strategic resource! My future reign depends on capturing both Wordsmiths for myself!_

The Emperor's gaze turns menacing. She transmits an electrical pulse toward a location underground.

"How long until the Atlas Cannon reaches firing range?"

Her voice transmits electrically to a radio transmitter. Seconds later, a female voice replies.

"Not much longer, Emperor Ose. We've cleared out Humanity's leaders. We've done as you ordered."

"Specifics, imbecile!" Ose snaps. "How much longer _exactly_?!"

"One minute, thirteen seconds."

"Excellent. Transmit these coordinates to the Atlas platform. Set the weapon to maximum power, maximum radius. I want to cause as much widespread damage as possible."

Ose transmits another electrical signal, one listing off a series of coordinates. Seconds later, her human spy responds.

"The Western Front?"

"Yes! Hurry up!"

"Of course."

Ose turns her attention to the Western Front. As humanity's most heavily fortified position, it possesses the most troops and the heaviest defensive lines. Many more demons pour through the Warpgate Encampment than through any ten Warper portals combined, forcing humanity to defend the west more fervently then the other fronts. The warpgate's size and stability allows demonkind to travel across space much more quickly, thus making it a valuable strategic position.

"Good, good, yes. Not much longer, now."

Ose waits impatiently, chuckling to herself as she glances at Belial's uselessly flailing form. Belial fires off random mana pulses through the cloud, trying to attack Ose's true body, but fails every time.

"Pathetic woman," Ose mutters condescendingly. "You're like an infant who has yet to learn- hm?"

By sheer coincidence, Ose's eyes happen to pick up an unexpected anomaly. Atop the Hydra's back, a shiny barrier of light barely three meters in diameter engulfs a pair of human bodies, both of whom she instantly recognizes.

"Th-the Hero's wife? His daughter, too?!"

A horrific gleam of greed explodes in Ose's vision.

"Hahaha! There you are, you despicable little rabbits! Bouncing to and fro, yet never once stepping into my trap! I don't know how you escaped my grasp for so long, but... but..."

Ose trails off. Her excited expression dims visibly, replaced instead by confusion.

"Why are the two of them asleep? Why are they inside that light barrier? Why would the Wordsmith hide them atop the back of a raging Hydra?? Does he care nothing for his beloved wife and daughter? Impossible! I've seen the sickening puppy-love in his eyes many times from afar. I know he dotes on them well."

Puzzle pieces slowly click into place within Ose's mind. As she thinks, her enlightened perception and electrified brain allows her to comprehend many things at a thousand times the speed of any human.

"Titans... Wordsmith... Mephisto... not Mephisto... daughter... daughter is a Hero? Heroic abilities. Light barrier? Maybe. What else? Guh!!"

Ose's eyes bolt open. A look of horror passes over her face as the most terrifying possibility emerges in her mind.

"No! No, that cannot be! I refuse to believe it! How could a toddler summon five Titan Kings and a devil-damned Hydra?! That's utter nonsense! If she could do that, then she would be... the mightiest being to ever set foot in the universe!"

Ose's breathing turns ragged. She reflexively recoils in fear, gazing at the Hero's sleeping daughter with trembling eyes.

"I've never heard of such a terrifying power! Not in all my life! Is that why Yama's shades failed their task? Could they simply not contend with a mere child?! Heroes almost never awaken their full potential by their sixteenth birthday, let alone their sixth! If she's this strong now, then what sort of demon-slaying deity will she become in the future?!"

For a split second, a new thought emerges in Ose's mind.

_I must kill her. She's too dangerous to be left alive! If that little girl grows up, she'll wipe out the entirety of demonkind with a single thought!_

Several more seconds pass. Eventually, Ose calms down. 

"No. Daisy Hiro is a rare gem. She's young. Malleable. If I can take her for myself, I will have a pawn at my disposal far more formidable than even the Wordsmith. Capturing Jason is still my top priority, but his daughter is no less important."

Ose glances around at the other humans shouting and screaming. She snorts.

"As for the rest of them... they can fall to ash."

A voice speaks to Ose, communicating through her electrical senses.

"Five seconds to launch, Emperor Ose. Four. Three. Two..."

Ose turns to look at the Western Front. Uriel stands half a mile away, gazing into the sky while sweeping her divine sense through Ose's thunderstorm. However, not even she can pierce the smog to find Ose's position.

At that moment, a bright light shines above the clouds, one that no human can see, thanks to the curtain of darkness shrouding their vision.

The Atlas Cannon, a weapon forged by the Wordsmith for combating demonkind, activates its power cells. It condenses a tremendous amount of power into its planet-facing railgun, opting not to shoot a single small, precise bullet for assassinating Demon Emperors, but a high-impact payload with a huge area of coverage.

Silently, the light condenses to a point on the satellite's tip. A moment later, it flashes for a split-second with a light brighter than the sun.

_Fwip._

A quiet rush of air is the only thing anyone hears before a low-yield tactical warhead instantly travels fifty miles from orbit to the planet's surface.

**BOOOOOOOM!!**

A cosmic blast detonates smack in the middle of the Western Front's battle-lines. Fifty-seven thousand humans vaporize into ash, enveloped by a light that destroys anything it touches.

One second later, the explosive shockwave surges outward as well, obliterating the eardrums of anyone and anything in range.

Humanity's Planet-Walkers, gone.

Their strongest combatants, vaporized.

Their military leaders, reduced to ash.

Never getting even a chance to realize what happened, an additional thirty thousand humans perish within seconds, their nanite-based armor peeling away under the scorching heat, leaving their skin and innards to liquefy into mush.

Along with the humans, many thousands of demons perish, too. Ose pays them no mind.

The Third Emperor watches from above as the blast-wave travels outward, rapidly weakening until it fades into nothingness. In its wake, countless horrifically mutilated corpses lay unmoving, their figures coated by the ashen remains of those even closer to the blast zone. Only tiny pockets of scattered survivors manage to hold on to their feeble lives, but it won't be long before the demonic horde falls upon them.

Ose nods. "Not bad."

Belial, however, falls through the clouds to gaze at the humans in absolute horror. Her eyes become as wide as saucers, while her jaw visibly drops.

"N-n-no... no... nooo... Ose, you... you couldn't have..."

Tears well up in her eyes. Countless friends and acquaintances she's made over the past six years, people she's healed and spent time with, even at only the most surface of levels, still mean something to her.

Belial chokes back anguished sobs. "No, devils, no... Ose, you vile... despicable..."

Uriel, too, lays on her back, having been flung away by the Cannon's impact. A few injuries line her body, but she heals them quickly, then rises to her feet.

"...Monstrous. To think Ose possessed such a level of power. Verily, I have underestimated my foe."

Many demons pull back, unsure of what to do. However, not long after, their eyes turn greedy as a telepathic communication reaches into all of their minds.

_KILL ALL OF THE HUMANS. SLAY EVERY LAST ONE. SHOW NO MERCY._

These words, projected by Ose through her thundercloud, cause every demon to roar with excitement.

"Ose! Bahaha! She did this! Not the humans, our Emperor! Now we can kill the fleshbags once and for all boys! Let loose and kill some gooks!"

The demons rush forward, this time not holding back at all. Unlike before, when they had to move slowly so that Ose could locate the Wordsmith's family, she no longer needs to worry about such things.

Ose turns her greedy gaze toward Daisy and Phoebe.

"Hehehe. First, why don't I soften your people up? Then, I'll come for you."

The Emperor of Infiltration focuses her mind. She closes her eyes, calling upon the thunder she's been building up for the past ten minutes.

_Hong-long!_

The maelstrom hums energetically. It builds up within seconds, narrowing to several focal points above humanity's encampments.

Ose's eyes bolt open. "DESTROY!"

All at once, innumerable lightning bolts fall from the sky. They rush toward thousands of human and monster soldiers, lancing into their suits and vehicles with far more energy than the random strikes from earlier. Countless soldiers scream in agony as Ose's magic tears through them, obliterating their innards and killing them on the spot. Many survive with heavy injuries, while only a few of the hardiest monsters, like Yamir, manage to shrug off the damage.

Ose releases every last drop of her pent-up power, collapsing humanity's other three battle-lines, before exhaling heavily and shuddering to a stop.

With her thunderstorm exhausted, Ose retracts her power. A jelly-like sensation spreads through her legs, making her tremble weakly, but she quickly reaches her palm out to suck up a bit of nearby electrical energy from some nearby vehicles and structures.

Ose sighs. With her energy much lower than before, she realizes she doesn't have much left to spare.

"Tch. I planned to take my time, but Uriel's appearance has changed things. I'll have to move swiftly if I want to achieve my ultimate objectives."

As Ose's thunderstorm dissipates, Uriel and Belial both lock onto her, with Belial's anguish quickly turning to wrath.

"Oseeee!!"

Belial flaps her wings. She rushes toward Ose, but the white-haired Emperor pays her no mind.

"Fuck off, Belial."

Ose snaps her attention to the Hydra. She turns into a bolt of lightning and instantly travels to the holy energy barrier atop its back.

"Break for me!" Ose roars, as she fires an electric-bomb at the barrier protecting Daisy and Phoebe's sleeping bodies.

_Boom!_

The barrier trembles under Ose's might, but to her shock, it doesn't shatter.

"What? So powerful?!"

Ose gapes for a moment, then starts to condense more power into her hands.

Meanwhile, Belial follows Ose's path. She, too, spots Phoebe and Daisy atop the Hydra's back. Her vision turns red as murderous rage surges within her heart.

"OSEEE!! GET AWAY FROM THEM!"

She increases her speed to the max, swooping down on Ose like a bird of prey. Her leathery wings creak under the G-forces as she rears her fist back to punch Ose with all her strength.

Suddenly, the unthinkable happens.

One of the nearby Titans, Zeus, swivels his head toward Belial's falling figure. He bolts toward her, moving at an equally ridiculous speed, rears his arm back, and slaps down.

_WHUMP!!_

Belial doesn't even get an instant to react. One moment, she's speeding toward Ose. The next, a mountain falls atop her, slapping her against Hero City's confines.

**Boom!**

Belial crashes into the dirt, breaks countless bones, shatters most of her teeth, and falls into a muddled brain-state. Several seconds of traumatic shock pass through her body before she manages to open her eyes and blink dazedly.

"Uhhh..."

Belial's self-preservation instincts kick in. She blearily motions with one hand, then presses it against her side, healing herself. She still needs more than thirty seconds to revive herself to peak condition, and even then, her eyesight blurs every few seconds; the side-effects of a concussion.

"...hit me. Something hit me. Bird? A bird? Was it a bird?"

Belial mumbles to herself. She struggles to think, while a distant whine rings in her ears.

...

Atop the Hydra, Ose blasts the energy barrier ten times. She pours her strength into her attacks, yet still, it doggedly holds on.

"Seven devils!" Ose howls. "How fucking durable _is_ this force-field?! It's like I'm trying to cut a diamond with wood-chips! My attacks seem only to be strengthening it!"

Indeed, the more Ose attacks the barrier, the sturdier it becomes. Eventually, the Emperor of Infiltration takes a step back, gasping weakly for breath.

"She's right there... hah... the little brat is ten feet away but I can't get to her. What is this infernal barrier made of?!"

The Hydra ignores Ose and the women on its back. It continues attacking the Titans, while they, too, battle it. The more they fight, the more Ose realizes something is amiss.

The Emperor glances toward the action. As she watches, Zeus beats the tar out of a pair of Hydra heads while wielding his two infamous lightning rods. Similarly, Poseidon wields a trident, a golden weapon made for expertly channeling his aquatic abilities.

However, what stuns Ose the most is when she looks more closely at Neptune. There, she sees him, too, wielding a trident. And not just any random one, but the same weapon as his father! Somehow, the two of them wield the exact same trident, as if someone had found a way to copy unique artifacts and deliberately bequeathed one to each Titan King.

Ose's expression turns to disbelief. She watches as, without any fanfare or particular tactics, the group of Titans merely attacks the Hydra like a group of stupid idiots, repeating the same moves over and over. The Hydra, likewise, dumbly attacks them back, ignoring the minor threats on its back.

_Something isn't right about these Titans..._ Ose thinks. _Why are they acting so... robotic? It's as if they aren't the actual beings who once roamed the Earth, but mere constructs... mere illusions..._

As always, her rapid thinking and quick analytical abilities allow her to dig right to the core of the issue.

_These aren't revived or reborn Titans. They're... projections? Capable of causing great damage, yet ultimately still only illusions given flesh. Could the Monster Queen have made them? No. Even if she devoured the Sphinx's magic, I find that possibility to be unlikely._

_Instead, it seems... this child... did she perhaps summon them from her dreams? But why would a little girl imagine such horrifying monsters? And since when did she learn of their existences?_

Many suspicions boil within Ose's mind. She dwells on them for ten seconds, thinking of several possible conclusions. In the end, she shakes her head, not sure what to think.

_I've heard of demons and Heroes who could create dream-worlds and other such things in their sleep, but this is the first I've heard or seen anyone summon such high-level existences into the real world. They're so realistic that I mistook them for the real Titan Kings. Given their frenzied rampaging, it seems the child is too young and inexperienced to control them. That works out fine for me. Once I capture her, I'll be able to slowly twist her into my little puppet._

_Nothing has changed. I still need to capture her and her mother, but now, the girl has far more direct value to me than I previously expected._

Ose smirks. She turns away from the Titans to look at Daisy once again.

"...eh?"

Ose's smirk vanishes. To her horror, she turns to see Daisy slowly rising to her feet within the energy barrier. The little girl rubs her forehead, as if having awoken from a terrible dream.

When Daisy opens her eyes, the frenzied Titans pause-mid-movement. The Hydra, too, slows its rampage.

Daisy raises her head to look at the Demon Emperor outside her protective bubble. Her face morphs into a frown.

"Hey! I heard what you said! You are a bad person! Very bad!"

All of the Titans swivel their heads in unison. They turn to look at Ose, making her blood turn to ice.

Ose freezes in place while a crushing mental pressure falls upon her.

"You... you... you're not a normal Hero. What the hell are you?!"

Daisy doesn't answer for a moment. Instead, she raises her eyes, as if searching the sky for something.

"Hmm. Daddy's gonna come back soon. I'll have to beat your butt real quick, then."

Daisy turns to look at the Titans.

"Hey! What are you doing? Give this bad lady a spanking! Hurry up!"

Ose's heart jumps into her throat as all of the Titans start moving toward her in unison.

"Oh, SHIT."
