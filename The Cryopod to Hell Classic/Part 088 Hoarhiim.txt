Part 088 - Hoarhiim

I walked over to the small, broken human on the ground. Pulling out my sword, I raised it over the feeble body of the youth.

" **...Perhaps, if you were stronger, she might have even... fallen for you.** "

And then I stabbed the sword down.

It pierced his heart, instantly killing him. I pulled the sword out, my gaze darkening as I looked at the corpse in front of me.

Now that Hero was dead, my queen could return. But... in my haste... I had destroyed the door to leave this place. Perhaps the reason I did so, was that I knew I would win. If we cannot escape, then my queen would be alone, with only me by her side.

Just me... the thought filled me with joy. I would have the most powerful queen ever all to myself. Even if it took an eternity, I would find a way to create a real body for myself, once again... to undo what had previously been done.

I love my queen. I may be the Lord of Darkness, but it is merely a title. It's a transient thing. Titles mean little, they are passed around like candy at a party. I'm fortunate that my title reflects my current abilities, as I am truly a lord of darkness, but even if I were the Lord of Fire or the Lord of Flies, it would mean nothing to me.

I turned away from the unsightly, broken corpse behind me. I walked away from it slowly, pacing myself. My lady probably had no feelings for the youth but... I would not reawaken her and have her control the body right in front of his shell. If she did possess feelings for the insect, it would be unnerving for her to reawaken right in front of his body.

I walked away, and before I'd taken ten steps, a familiar voice spoke. One I had not heard speaking aloud in...

" _Where do you think you're going?_ "

Hoarhiim.

I turned back around and gasped. The body had silently healed and he was standing up, perfectly straight. His wounds were all gone, but more impressive were the other physical changes. His muscles hulked. His entire facial structure was altered. He definitely resembled my old rival in body, and in voice.

" **Hoarhiim. One last trick, huh?** "

" _This is no trick, Blaarjiim. This has been fated since the beginning of our lives, since we first met, as youths, all those years ago._ "

I nodded. I could no longer remember my distant past, and he likely couldn't either. But he was right. We always fought, we always bickered, we always argued.

" **You are the light, I am the dark. Neither of us can win, you know?** "

" _No. You are wrong. This one is unique. I will finally achieve my ultimate goal through him._ "

I gnashed my teeth. " **You think it will be so simple? We've fought tens of thousands of times. It's always a stalemate. We are equal in power. The light needs the darkness, just as much as the darkness needs the light.** "

Hoarhiim shook his head, as a spear of light appeared in his hand. _Gungnir._ His personal weapon. " _Many things have changed, one of which is that I now have the ability to control fate itself._ "

A bluff if ever there were one. I summoned my own weapon, sticking Dellfingler back in its place at my side. " **Sharur.** " A mighty club of darkness appeared in my hand. Ah, so good to feel its power once again. The only time I would ever take this out was when I fought my rival. Not even my queen knew of its existence. It was an unspoken rule between him and I. Personal weapons only.

I stared up into the sun, as its light beamed down on the battlefield. The ground was torn up and loose from the fighting. Patches of grass were missing, here and there.

_Master, are we to fight Hoarhiim once more?_ My club spoke to me. Sharur was a sentient weapon, one of the few in existence. It sometimes had a mind of its own, deflecting attacks that I might otherwise miss, and striking weak spots I might otherwise not see.

" **Yes, my brother. We will finally, on this day, strike him down in combat. And then we will be alone with the mistress until the end of time.** "

The club trembled with anticipation. _You focus on defense. I'll worry about the attacking._

Good. This would all be over soon.
