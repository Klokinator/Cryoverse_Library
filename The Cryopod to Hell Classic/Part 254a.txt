Part 254a:

Satan lurched towards me, his body moving erratically, as his ten extra limbs gave him the ability to quickly change direction on the turn of a dime.

Satan grabbed hold of a tree, ripping it out of the ground effortlessly. With just one of his invisible limbs, he wrapped it around the trunk and swung at me, sweeping the makeshift mace across the ground. I leaped into the air, jumping over it as it swished below me. Unfortunately, that was exactly what he seemed to want me to do.

Five of his limbs reached up and grabbed me in midair, one by my throat, the rest grabbed my arms and legs and started pulling them apart at full force. If my suit weren't resistant to every form of attack, he'd have instantly torn my limbs apart. As it were at that moment, he pulled with such force that it felt like my suit, and thusly my body, was going to come apart at the seams.

"Well well, how will the 'hero' escape this _unfortunate_ circumstance? Are you going to start praying to your gods for aid?" Satan cackled as he held me up in the air. No matter how I struggled, I lacked the strength to escape his grasp.

"You're a strong demon, I'll give you that. But crushing you will feel so satisfying! And... if I manage to beat you today, this will be the final proof I need..." I didn't finish my sentence, but that was a good thing. I didn't want him to know that I was doubtful of my ability to change the future. If I could defeat Satan on this day, that would mean that Amelia didn't defeat him, and that would mean I was even now, changing the future.

But if I couldn't beat him, that meant we were one step closer to the original timeline.

Satan smiled as he crossed his arms and stared at my wriggling body. "I told you, 'Hero'. I hold the ultimate offense and the ultimate defense. For all your talk, your little toy suit is at best a good defense. No matter how powerful it makes your punches, you will never come close to defeating me."

I grinned my own wicked grin. "Well, Satan, that's because you don't know a thing about my suit. I've got you all figured out. You were lying earlier, weren't you? You've never actually fought someone who had wordsmithing. You made that up."

Satan's smile disappeared. "You're calling me a liar? Alright, I'll bite. What makes you think that?"

"It's simple. The mere fact you called it "Wordsmithing" is all the proof I need. I invented the term myself. Another hero would be unlikely to use the exact same term. They might call it 'word magic' or something else. And this curse that your inner demon put on my face... Hades, was it? It probably has some ability to read my thoughts, or you spy on my words at the least. You somewhat know how my powers work, but not fully."

Satan grimaced. "Alright, fine. You're right. Not that it matters, I've already protected myself against every word you could possibly use in any language that could kill me. It took me months to prepare those spells, you know. What's your next move? You still can't escape my clutches."

I nodded as he squeezed on my throat, which was luckily protected by my suit. "That's what I'm getting at, Satan. You only know a little about me. I created this super-suit long before you put that damn curse mark all over my face. Therefore, you don't know about the hidden abilities it has."

Satan narrowed his eyes. "You're bluffing."

I didn't respond. At that instant, my visor closed. "Harold, activate Lightning Mode, and the M.A.D.S. as well."

"You got it, boss!" Harold cheerfully yelled as he listened to our conversation. "By the way, thought you'd like ta know, them clones are cleanin' up jes' fine!"

"Good." I grinned as I felt my suit rapidly powering up. "Let's end this quickly."

Satan's face faltered as he felt the strength in my suit beginning to surge. In the blink of an eye, I became transparent as my suit's position began fluctuating, vibrating thousands of times per second. The intense vibrations rattled his grip so much that he slipped off, leaving only his one limb on my neck. Before he could reach back to grab me again, my boots lit up as rocket propulsion systems fired, sending me flying around rapidly.

Satan scowled as I suddenly flew out of his grip and went sailing into a clearing. Before I could crash into the ground, the M.A.D.S. (Mechanical Automated Defense System) activated a small bit of thrust on my chest, abruptly jerking me straight up into a standing position.

Satan was clearly able to track me with his eyes, so the invisibility didn't help much, but I had other ideas. " **Mirage!** "

As I dashed at him, I summoned multiple fake versions of myself, and they all zipped around, their movements making it hard for him to tell which one was the real me. "Excellent! Now you're using your head!" Satan praised me, as if I were a student of his, and not an enemy fighting to kill him. I gritted my teeth but ignored the subtle insult.

He swung wildly at each mirage, one of the arms swinging at me as well. Luckily the M.A.D.S. system ejected a blast of superheated gas to deflect the attack. Before he could realize I was the real one, I used my innate teleportation abilities to pop behind him, slapping my hand directly against his back.

" **RUPTURE!!** "

A blast exploded inside his body, as several hundred blood vessels blew forwards, out of the front of his chest. Satan screamed in pain as he stumbled forwards. I couldn't help but notice that his ten extra arms stopped moving and went totally limp, falling to the ground lifeless for a few moments as he recovered from the pain. So, it seems that intense pain makes them lose their movement? He must actually have to concentrate to move them around. Good to know.

Satan whipped around, looking directly at me as he spat green blood all over the ground. "Kah... excellent... you're not like the first hero I ever fought, he was just a stupid... just a stupid lunkhead who only thought with his fists..." Satan coughed as he quickly made more hand motions, rapidly healing himself. Just like with Amelia, he had too many pesky abilities to deal with.

Satan grinned as he flexed his arms, his ten other limbs once again rising up. At least now I knew he had one weakness; his concentration was key to defeating those damned arms. And they were only a major threat if you didn't know how they worked.

Satan stared at me levelly for a few seconds, neither of us moving. "Well, I hope you had fun. It's my turn, now. And I do warn you, child, I'm not in the greatest mood right now."

"Blow it out your ass."
