Part 395b: Repression

" **Bury.** "

With a single word, the ground in the training room heaves up and engulfs the bodies of the two girls, pulling them down into a permanent resting place. Lora wipes away a tear, but Silvia stares stonily. Silvia, I've noticed in the short time since I met her, never emotes in the slightest. She helped me move their bodies down, since I felt using telekinesis would have a less somber feeling to it, and she never once appeared sad or cried.

I haven't either, of course. I don't care about the dead girls. I never knew them, and their only meaning to me was that of how they made Lora feel. She was close friends with both of them, and even as we watch the bodies get swallowed into the blackened dirt, she rests an arm on the younger girl's shoulder. She cares about Silvia, that I can tell, but Silvia doesn't seem to return those feelings.

I don't know what to make of the situation. Perhaps we should leave Silvia to the labyrinth and let her continue living her life, but on the other hand, she would be useful to me, possibly in some small way. She might help motivate Lora with a motherly instinct, guiding Lora to improve herself to protect the young girl.

Then again, the cold, emotionless eyes I've spotted in Silvia have their uses. In time... she might grow to outperform even her senior.

I glower at the newly erected graves as the ground finishes shifting around. It occurred to me afterward that instead of just sending Danis off to return to the human realm, I could have taken some of his memories. Those might have been quite valuable and given me real insight into the state of the galaxy. I am still coming up short on too many details for my liking, and this revelation about memory theft came to me from nowhere, like a faint whisper from the past.

Sometimes I feel as if I'm on the cusp of remembering a past life, something involving reincarnation. It's stupid, of course, but it still itches at the back of my mind. I find it hard to explain with words, but it is pervasive, never leaving me.

Sometimes the subconscious self can be a buffoon.

"Silvia. I'm going to pull you from Yama, and you will be my slave, along with Lora. As such, I am giving you back that which was taken from you." Silvia looks up at me questioningly, and Lora turns to me in surprise, even while I speak again. " **Heal. Regenerate.** "

For the next minute, Silvia finally opens her eyes in shock and staggers backward, grasping at her mouth and throat as her vocal cords and tongue return from the nether.

Several minutes pass, and she sits on the ground breathing quietly. Then she surprises me by speaking. "You heal me. Why?"

It takes me a moment to realize she can talk, but I gather my wits about me. "I will do this for all slaves I take from Yama. He has his way, and I have mine."

"Oh." She doesn't thank me, though I ponder to myself that might be due to her upbringing. "What you want me do?"

I stare at the young girl, with her short-cropped black hair and eyes of a similar shade, and ponder the question to myself. What do I want?

Well, she has to learn to protect herself, just as Lora did. That is non-negotiable. But her small size means I should probably train her differently from Lora. Besides, if we were in a video game, it would be stupid to have a party of only warriors.

Silvia and I stare at each other silently as I think and she waits patiently. Finally, I have my answer. "I will train you to be an assassin."

"Okay." She doesn't question me, but instead just nods. Good. I like that. At least subservient slaves don't give me lip, which works out better for both of us.

" **Knife. Knife. Knife.** " I create three knives, all of them with double sided blades that taper into elegant triangle points, and small handles suited for her hands. Then I give them to her. "I know your friends died, but a demon will not wait for your sadness to end. You have to start training immediately."

"I not sad. Them not important." She stands up and takes the knives away from me, staring at them questioningly. "What do with these?"

I point her at a training dummy Lora broke yesterday. "I want you to practice throwing these knives. It will take a lot of work, but eventually, you should be able to stab that dummy from this distance right on that red dot in the center of its chest. Do you see it?"

She barely even nods. "Yes. Will try."

_Do or do not, there is no try._ I feel the urge to say those words but shake my head instead. I'm not that wise, so it's best I don't pretend otherwise.

"Uh, get a bit closer though. While you're first starting, you're going to miss a lot, and your arms are probably too weak to throw them a hundred feet." I quickly aim my mind at a spot twenty feet from the dummy and use my telekinesis to etch an _X_ into the ground. "Stand and throw from there."

Silvia obediently walks away as soon as I'm done talking, and I hastily speak up to remind her of one thing. "Also, if you get hurt, stab yourself, or cut off a finger or something, tell me at once. I can heal any wound, but I can't bring back the dead."

She doesn't nod or say anything, but a quick mental impression I get from her tells me she heard.

Hmm, come to think of it... how do I know I can't bring back the dead? I've never even tried. Maybe my power is more mysterious than I thought.

Oh well. I turn back to Lora and sigh silently to myself. She's still staring emptily at the place where her friends are buried. I take a few steps towards her and rub her arms. "I'm sorry about what happened. Nobody deserves to go out like that. However, you avenged them. You did the right thing."

She bobs her head and another tear comes trickling down. Strange, I always thought of Lora as not caring much about the other slaves, but perhaps I don't understand her as well as I thought I did.

"All right. Come on, let's get back to training. No matter how long you stare, you can't bring them back. The best way to honor their memory is to become powerful so you can punish filth like Danis whenever you encounter them."

When I speak his name, a shudder runs down her spine and she pulls away from me, pushing past to head over to the training dummy I made yesterday. I ignore the slight, as it would be discourteous of me to call out someone grieving. Still, she has to get over them eventually. One cannot dwell on the past forever, or it will damage their future.

I grimace as I spot large chunks of flesh and blood covering the backside of her armor. Her plate mail is filthy now and coated in that man's blood. " **Clean.** " It vanishes and I breathe a sigh of relief, wondering to myself why I suddenly felt so possessive. Well, there's nothing wrong with that. I saved her, so she's mine. It's that simple.

Danis didn't just kill the two girls I buried; he had apparently hurt Lora and Silvia in the past too. That explains the marks on Lora's back. I had thought the demons did that to her, but thinking of the human and how he treated members of his own species, it occurs to me that perhaps there are monsters on both sides of the conflict.

I need to know more. I need to find out the full situation.

"Oohh!" Silvia lets out a yelp, and I quickly turn to look at her, wondering what happened. I'm mildly amused to see that she's already planted a knife square in the center of the dummy's chest.

"Excellent. Now you just need to get to a point where you can do that fifty times in a row without missing, and we'll move onto bigger things."

Silvia's expression of awe at her skills dissolves instantly as I suck the wind out of her sails. She slumps down a little and goes back to throwing her knives. She'll be fine. A little disappointment is good for the soul.

Lora picks up her sword and shield and starts hacking at her dummy, carefully chopping each limb off, one by one. I watch with a slight disinterest and notice she's more precise with her strikes than before. There's an intent to kill that I didn't feel yesterday. Killing Danis must have changed something about her.

I grab my sword out of the ground and walk up behind her. "Lora, enough with the dummy. This time I want you to go on the offense against me."

She stops and turns to look at me, but once again I see that same hesitation from before. She looks nervous at the thought of swinging a sword at me.

"Go on. Do it. I'm waiting." I raise my sword and assume a defensive stance, so she hesitantly assumes an offensive one, but even as she shifts around, I can already tell she isn't going to attack.

"Come on, Lora. You threw me out of the way to get at Danis. Channel that anger once again. Attack me mercilessly. I'm plenty protected, so you won't be able to do serious harm."

_I think._ I swallow at the thought that maybe my invisible armor isn't as sturdy as I expect.

Still, she doesn't move to attack. Her face is resolute, and it's starting to piss me off. "Lora, this is childish. I don't care what your feelings are towards me, you have to treat me like you did-" I pause as an idea crosses my mind. It only takes a second, and I quickly put it into action. " **Disguise.** "

I feel my body shift as my features change. I don't have a mirror to see myself, but the sudden change in Lora's expression as her face melts into an expression of horror is enough to make me giddy. Finally, she knows I'm serious. I start to speak, and already my voice sounds just like Danis's did. "Come on, bitch. If you don't take a swing at me, I'll kill that little girl. Yeah, that's right! I'll kill Silvia! What are you going to do now, eh? I'll fucking cut her into little pieces and feed her to the dogs! Bet you'd like that, wouldn't you?"

Silvia hears my altered voice and turns around to gawk in amazement, or perhaps disgust. I can't be sure, but based on the look they're giving me, I must look just like the filthy animal.

"Come on! DO IT! If you don't, I'll kill the little girl! I'm not bluffing either, you worthless whore!"

I perceive the exact moment Lora snaps. She can't take the taunting anymore, or my face resembling Danis's. Lora leaps at me with a ferocity far overwhelming what I expected, and I rear back in surprise. Her speed increases as she slams her sword down at my head and she shoves her shield into my gut at the same time. I deflect the sword but get knocked backward by the shield.

A stab, a parry, we trade blows and I keep getting forced back, all while she howls something inhuman, the sounds that escape her mouth sounding even more primal than what she let out against Danis just a while earlier. Truth be told, I can't hold off against her, and have to keep increasing my strength just to parry. Her berserker rage just becomes even worse when she feels me resisting more.

"All right, that's enough! You did a good job-" I'm cut off as she bashes me in the face with an elbow and knocks me on my ass. It doesn't hurt, but I roll out of the way just as her sword smashes into the ground where I was sitting, blasting dirt up into the air from under the black tiles that cover the floor.

She isn't listening to me and instead is releasing the anger she's been holding in ever since she saw me let Danis walk away. Maybe she doesn't know why I let him go, but she still resents me for it. Even so, this is getting dangerous. I have to get her to stop.

I lift my head up just in time to see a boot swing at my face. A moment later my world goes spinning end over end as she kicks me thirty feet back, smashing me against a wall. My mind is swirling, and I shake the feeling away, before aiming a word at myself. " **Normalize.** "

My body shifts once again, and I breathe quickly as I wonder if I look like myself again. Lora, however, doesn't seem to care that I look like myself, and she jumps at me and swings again, though I dodge the attack and slip around behind her with surprising agility. Using little effort, I send a telekinetic wave into her backside, smashing her against the wall where she just pummeled me.

Finally, this seems to knock some sense into her, and she steps away from the wall, turning to look at me. Her nose is broken, and tears are pouring down her face.

"Christ's sake! I told you to calm down!" I jam my sword in the ground and walk over to her, cupping her chin in my hand. "Let me have a look. That's a nasty break."

She nods slightly, and her anger fades away in an instant. I heal her nose, and both of us stare at each other for several long seconds.

"Are you feeling better now? Did you get that rage out of your system?"

Lora shifts her shoulders and averts her eyes, and then she does something completely shocking. She jumps at me and wraps her arms around me, pressing us close together.

I return the embrace, and she slowly looks up into my eyes. A question is visible, deep inside her mind. I softly probe with my telepathy before smiling at her. "Yes, you may."

We kiss, and I press her face against mine. My hand glides along the back of her head, subtly making her move how I want her to.

Seconds pass, and finally, we pull our lips apart. Lora's breathing is shallow, and she says nothing, but I can tell by the few days we've been together what she's thinking at a glance.

A hint of motion to my left makes me glance over at Silvia. She's watching us with a silent stare. There's no curiosity, or interest, or envy, or anything else. She's just... watching.

I pull myself away from Lora and both of us suddenly feel the awkwardness in the air. Silvia isn't making things any easier.

Lora rubs her knees together and smiles shyly, so I turn to Silvia. "Let's call it a day. That's enough training for now. We'll go back to my room and I'll carve you out an alcove across the hallway."

She just nods. "Okay."

I beam down from my ship into the main labyrinth entry facility. Instantly, all the personnel on deck stop and salute me, probably by the order of a yet unseen person. "As you were." I nod at them and continue walking forwards towards the gate that will warp me into the Labyrinth. It's a massive circular thing, big enough to fit an enormous tank from my youth. That is, one of those primitive vehicles that moved on treads. They fired rather weak metal shells with an explosive compound, as I recall.

The hangar is decent sized, though nothing like Earth's main military port. A handful of starships of varying sizes are lined up beside mine, with space for at least ten more on this level. Dozens of men and women roam about, here and there, dressed in different colored uniforms, such as orange, green, and black. My eyes look past them all and stare silently at the gate that Marie Becker helped design. Unlike what the angels and demons use to get from their dimensions to ours, our warp gate is powered by normal energy, and a colossal amount of it. This star system alone needed to build a Dyson Sphere just to give the gate the power that it needed.

As I head towards the warp gate, a plain looking woman walks up to me. "Supreme Commander, I'm Sergeant Florence. I'm in charge of this facility. We weren't expecting you." Her tan hair is chopped back in a masculine style, a butch cut that doesn't make her look as gentle as she could otherwise. I do prefer long hair on a woman.

"Yes, I remember you. I believe you helped during the Era of Suppression. You led the all-female Verus Battalion, as a matter of fact. How have you been?"

She blushes. "I'm surprised you remember that, Supreme Commander. That was such a long time ago. I didn't even do all that much. It took a concerted effort during those dark times."

"Of course. However, some people exerted more effort than others. You lead the defense force to protect this gate, so that makes you rather important in case the demons get uppity. A fitting promotion, I'd say."

"Yeah. It's a shame immortals don't get to retire permanently."

"You're telling me." I rub my forehead and grunt a little bit. "I've got a splitting headache. Anyway, I'm heading into the labyrinth."

Her eyes open wide in surprise. "You are? I beg your pardon, Commander, but surely whatever the reason is, you could send someone else in. Humanity can't afford to lose the highest ranking leader we've got. At the least, I'll pull together an elite guard for you."

I shake my head and smile. "That won't be necessary. This is a... diplomatic mission. Yes, I need to go in by myself. You know how weak the demons have become."

She bobs her head slowly. "I... see. Extending an olive branch then, are you? A noble gesture, but if the demons capture you, it's all over. I can't let you go in alone, even if you are the Supreme Commander. Humanity's fate rests in your hands."

I lick my lips slowly. "I'm just one man, Florence. I can be replaced. That being said, I think you are underestimating me... and overstepping your authority. I outrank you in every meaning of the word. Activate the gate, and step aside."

"I... I can't just do that, Supreme Commander. You know what you're asking of me, right?"

"I do." I reach up and scratch my grizzled beard thoughtfully. "Truth be told, I don't have to use this gate. I could just use another route, but it would waste my... _energy_, and leave me weakened. Is that what you would prefer?"

She looks back and forth at a few passing crewman, but they offer her no easy answers. "Well, no..."

"So step aside." I lean down close and murmur so only she can hear. "You have distinguished yourself over the years, but there's no reason I can't strip you of your rank and put in someone more loyal. You know what happens to traitors, don't you?"

Her glee at seeing me from earlier is completely gone. She takes a hesitant step backward. "Not the mines... you wouldn't..."

"This mission is imperative to the survival of humanity. Activate the gate, _Sergeant._ I'm not asking again."

Finally, defeat is visible in her eyes. "Alright... fine. I'll do it. Just... be careful, Commander. There are still unpredictable and savage demons in the labyrinth."

"I'm counting on it." My hypersuit seals up and my faceplate closes as she inputs a long series of digits into the password matrix. When the gate begins to activate, a hundred soldiers line up, weapons at the ready, just in case anything should exit through the gate.

"Thank you, Sergeant. I remember those who are friends."

Walking forward, I step through the warp gate as it opens up, stepping into a place I haven't been in millions of years.

The soldiers on the other side are much tenser and more alert than the ones on humanity's side. They barely nod at me, waiting until the instant I walk through to close the gate down.

A man speaks up. "Just one? You're joking."

I shake my head and walk past him, smiling to myself. He can't see my face, and this suit is a nondescript model, so he has no way of knowing who I am. "Special orders. I'll be out in a day or two."

"It's your grave, buddy." The soldier's voice sounds off after me as I trudge down a long, dark hallway, leading deep into the labyrinth of Hell.

There's another Wordsmith in here, skulking about in the shadows. I need to find and capture them. I just pray I'm not too late... the potential for casualties is too high.
