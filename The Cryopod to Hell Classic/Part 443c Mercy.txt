Part 443c: Mercy

A familiar face greets me once again. My own.

My imposter and I stand facing each other, him at the edge of an unthinkably tall precipice, and me, not ten feet away. His grip on Samantha's throat tightens as he holds her over the cliffside.

" **You thought you could protect them. It took a robot to save their lives, a machine of death, ironically enough. You were useless then, just as you are now.** "

Samantha struggles and claws at my doppelganger's hand, but she can't fight his grip off at all. I take a step toward them, and he holds her a little further away. "Leave her alone. Your fight is with me. She didn't do anything to deserve this."

" **She loves you. Killing her will hurt you far more than any wound of the flesh. I'll give you a moment to say your goodbyes. Don't waste it.** "

"Don't you dare hurt her!" I jump at him, but he merely smiles at me, a cold, emotionless grin, as he loosens his grip. Samantha, surprised by the sudden movement, let's out a tiny squeak, before plunging off the edge of the cliff. Her scream muffles a second later, and by the time I stumble to the side of the cliff, she's gone.

"S-Samantha! No!"

The copycat paces around behind me and gazes down into the chasm. " **Oh dear, heh heh. She fell. And now, you will too.** "

Before I can react, he kicks me in the ass, sending me stumbling forward off the cliff. "Guh!"

My eyes squeeze shut as I brace for the feeling of rushing wind, but instead, a soft, beautiful voice whispers into my ear. "You don't have to do this alone. You don't have to be our bulwark. We can protect ourselves, Hero."

I blink my eyes open, and Cassiel's smiling face greets me. Her massive wings flap steadily, as she braces my body against hers, with my toes just barely touching the edge of the cliff. Samantha clutches onto Cassiel's back, one arm wrapped around Cass's shoulder, the other rubbing her neck.

"Cassiel is right, Jason. Just because you're a Hero, doesn't mean you have to save everyone. No-one can go at it alone."

" **What?!** " My copy takes a step back as Cassiel flaps again, shoving me to my feet at the edge of the cliff, as she and Samantha land a few feet away.

"You think we don't know who you are? You are the darkness. You wish to kill the purity inside Hero, but that's where you went wrong. Heroes never die."

The other me takes a step forward as he grabs a second wind. " **I am not darkness, I am strength. When the time comes, I will be the only way for you to save them. You will have a choice to make, and I will be your salvation. I will be your... hope.** "

Copycat Me whips his hand forward and aims his palm at us. " **Begone!** "

A wave of energy races forward, pushes us off the cliff, and our screams of terror rebound off the canyon walls as we plunge to our inevitable...

.............................................

"...Demise!" I yell out awkwardly, suddenly realizing I'm staring at the ceiling of my bedroom again. Despite how erratic my breathing is, it calms down in seconds. "Just another nightmare..." I mutter sheepishly.

To my right and left, neither of the girls are here, so they left me alone for once. I lean up in bed and scratch my back as I stare vacantly across the room. _What I said to them earlier was messed up. An apology is top of my priority list._

After a few minutes, my clothes are on, my face is shaved, and I'm ready to take on the day. A glance at the clock tells me I was out for around seven hours, so I slept better than expected. "Computer, where is the ship located?"

The AI's monotone voice answers immediately. "The One-Hundred-Fortieth Galactic Exploration Vessel has been landed on the surface of the planet of Volgarius Prime."

_God, that's a terrible name for a ship_, I think to myself. I suppose it doesn't matter since we'll be leaving it behind. Still...

"Computer, please rename the ship 'U.S.S. Pickle.'"

The computer takes a few moments to pause. _'Are you kidding me right now?'_ I almost expect it to say. It doesn't.

"Command confirmed. This vessel has been renamed the U.S.S. Pickle on star-date 1.451.009. Are there any further commands?"

"No."

Saying that, I stride out of the room and down the hall to the Bridge. _It's high time I stopped lazing about and got to work,_ I think to myself. The Labyrinth isn't going to fix itself.

My boots thud against the harsh metal floors and echo slightly around me as I walk. _How am I going to fix the Labyrinth anyway? Should I cage humans back in bubble worlds? That's stupid. Nobody should be forced to live like that, with demons feeding on them periodically. What a horrible way to go out. Should I force the demons to surrender? If I do that, the humans will probably kill them. Too much bad blood between them. I can't just expect both sides to back down and sing Kumbaya._

_What if I forced them with Wordsmithing? I could make each side surrender and order them to never attack each other again. That would work, maybe._

_But is it ethical? Should I just change their free will, as if I were a dictatorial god sent from on high to mold them to my beliefs? I barely even understand myself, let alone the nature of war and conflict. The humans have every right to lash out at the demons after the abuse they've suffered._

My thoughts slowly revolve around my brain, like a leaf spinning atop a whirlpool. Where does the vortex of philosophy lead? What is right? What is wrong?

The door to the bridge slides open, and I raise my head up from the ground. "I'm awake."

Only then do I realize the Bridge is empty. Nobody else is- wait, there's one 'person' here. OMEN.

The robot turns to look at me. "Initial greeting: Hello, Hero. I see that you have woken up. Condescension: Your dreams were as terrible as I expected, judging by how you tossed and turned. If only you had taken me up on my offer."

"Shut up. I can Wordsmith and make myself sleep like a baby anytime I want. I don't want a band-aid, I want to work things out properly. Anyway, where are the girls?"

The robot taps a button to show a lush forest outside the ship, with trees that wouldn't look out of place on Earth, and birds sitting atop the branches of the local flora. "Smooth transition while ignoring the brushing off of my offer: The two female non-humans are outside of the ship agitating local wildlife. Amused observation: Here you can see the winged one hurling sticks toward a group of vicious wolf-like animals."

Indeed, the screen changes to show Cassiel... playing fetch with a dozen or so random Border Collies, all while cooing and saying things like 'Get the stick! Good boy!'

"OMEN... you know she's just... playing with the dogs, right?"

The robot's face contorts into an eerie expression of mock horror. "Astounded response: I had no idea! Your keen eyes and insightful wisdom have changed my outlook on the meaning of life!"

"Wow, I didn't know you had a fucking sarcasm module installed." I roll my eyes and groan inwardly. "Where's Ionis?"

"Serious response: The only intelligent member of this crew, barring myself, is located inside the cargo bay. Observation: He has accomplished more in one week with this crew against the Volgrim than the human known as 'Hero' has since he arrived a month ago."

My tongue snakes out and wets my lips. "I swear, I'll teleport you into a star one of these days."

.............................................

Slowly, the ramp extends down until it hits the forest's grass. The clearing around us is quite large, and trees surround us, almost as if this exact spot was made for the U.S.S. Pickle to land at. The girls are in the distance, playing with the group of dogs, but I stop and lean down to look at a flower on the ground. It looks like a tiny sunflower, as if someone hit it with a shrinking laser. A bug crawls around on the center of it, looking like a ladybug... but blue.

"I guess Wordsmithing and the Crown of Solomon didn't quite get all the details right," I mutter under my breath.

The girl's voices grow more audible as I head over to them. Cassiel nudges Samantha and points at one of the Collies. "-one I named Fluffy, because his tail is poofy! And this one over here is Dipsy, because he's kind of a doofus! And this pretty girl over here is Lora, because it's a pretty name for a cutie! If I had a daughter, that's what I'd call her!"

Samantha nods, but the look on her face is serious. "Dipsy is a condescending name though, don't you think? You don't want that boy to grow up to be a rebel outcast, do you? The other dogs will pick on him because he has such a silly name."

"No, no! It'll toughen him up! If everyone picks on him, then he'll become really strong and eventually he'll be like the alpha wolf or something!"

"I don't know if it works that way," Samantha says, clearly unconvinced. Before I can say anything, she turns just enough to spot me out of the corner of her eye. "Oh, you're awake!"

"Yup." I keep my tone neutral. "You two look like you're having fun."

Cassiel, apparently having already forgotten the way our conversation ended earlier, beams a radiant smile at me. "Are you here to play with the dogs too? This one is Hero Junior; I named him after you!"

One of the dogs turns and pants at me and immediately a look of revulsion passes over my face as I take a step back. " _Huagh_! What the fuck is wrong with his face?!"

The ugly hound has eight spider-like pitch-black eyes, and the two in the center are much bigger than the others. His innocent floppy tongue belies the horror of the upper half of his face. At my outburst he closes his mouth and bows his head, whimpering.

"Don't say that! Look, you made him sad." Cassiel leans down and hugs the wretched animal, kisses his forehead, and shoots a glare at me. "Apologize!"

I suddenly feel awkward, since I was initially going to apologize to her and Samantha for the way I treated them earlier, but now it feels awkward since she wants me to ask forgiveness to the dog first.

_Might as well get this over with._ I lean over and eye the ugly son-of-a-bitch up and down, swallowing my pride. "I'm sorry, Hero Junior. I didn't mean what I said. You're a _-hurgh-_, a good boy." The fearsome animal stares back at me and gives me a lick on the chin, just to spite me.

I turn back to Cassiel, but she speaks before I can. "And now us."

"Huh?" My look of dopiness makes her and Samantha snort to each other.

Sam pokes me in the shoulder. "You've apologized to the dog. Now, what about us? You were awfully rude, earlier."

Guilt washes over me. "Um. I was actually going to do that before you, uh..." _I feel so stupid_. I should have just said it quickly. I'm awkward in situations like this. "I'm sorry. I was in a bad mood after what Beatrix told-" I catch myself as the girls' smiles vanish. "-Well, that is, it's not important. I was wrong, and I apologize."

Cass curls up her lip and looks at Sam. "I guess it'll do."

"Yeah. Could have been more heartfelt, but he's just a guy after all."

"Hey... I'm standing right here, you know."

.............................................

The portal back to the Labyrinth yawns before us. Cass glances back at the forest. "I kinda like it here. Do we have to go back? It's so dark and dreary inside."

"We have to. Humans and demons are fighting, and I need to find out what the situation is."

Samantha leans against me and yawns. "So what are you going to do? Kill more demons? Kill humans? Put them in a corner and time them out?"

"I don't know, Sam. I'm still wondering how I should proceed. I'll think about it."

Cassiel offers her input. "Timing them out seems pretty reasonable to me. Just make sure they don't peek. Nose to the wall!"

_We'll call that Option B_, I think to myself.

The portal swallows us up as we step through, and the all-consuming darkness of the labyrinth is barely illuminated by the sunlight from Volgarius Prime. The corridor stretches ahead, and its dampness makes our footsteps sound _sloshy_ compared to the dry ground from outside. _Was it always this mossy?_

Cassiel's soft glow radiating from her body doesn't light up much, so I make my own source of brightness. " **Illuminate!** "

A ball of bright energy, not unlike Hoarhiim's orb, appears and hovers above us, like a 300w lightbulb. It's bright enough that it easily shows us the caverns around and before us, but by focusing my mind, I can force the light to radiate in any desired direction. Knowing this, I make it brighten up the path far into the distance.

"Wish I'd had this when I first got out of the Cryopod," I mutter to myself.

Apparently hearing me, Cassiel responds, "A most impressive display."

Minutes turn into an hour, as the Crown of Solomon's mental map leads us down this path and that one, left and right, up and down, toward the place I called Home for a short while; Hero City. Sometimes I see walls that look familiar, and remember the ten years I spent exploring this Labyrinth.

The shifting walls and ways the Labyrinth reconfigures itself is a mystery. How did a place like this come to be? Why is it so massive, like the size of ten Suns from the Terran system? How does it even make sense for the Labyrinth to work in the way it does?

Samantha sighs. "Do you know where you're going? I feel like we've looped around five times already."

"My mental map is three-dimensional and comprehensive. It's a little incredible how well it works." We stop in front of a path split in three directions to look around. From the end of one hallway, an odd _clink_, like the sound of an empty plastic bottle being bopped against the floor. When I shine my light down the hall, I'm surprised to see undead skeletons slowly shambling toward us.

Samantha curls up her lips. "Ugh, necromancy. I am not a fan. So uncute."

Neither she nor Cassiel looks particularly concerned by the creatures holding swords and shields. Cassiel swipes a strand of hair from her face. "How do the skeletons move, anyway? Who makes them?"

"Dunno. Well, I think there are some demons whose magical abilities relate to raising the dead, but it's a stereotype that they're always ugly-looking impish skugs, so I never got to know any of them." Samantha takes a step back, "Sadly, these bony fellows are probably better looking than their masters."

With that, she lunges forward, at a speed that takes me by surprise. Moving like water through a series of pipes, she ducks, dodges, and bats the weapons from each skeletons' hand, crushing each of their skulls in her grip by clapping with a tremendous force. Each skeleton falls to pieces on the ground and doesn't get back up.

After destroying the two dozen or so, Samantha skips back over to us, whistling daintily the whole way, before thrusting her hands in front of me. "Could you be a dear and clean my hands off? They're filthy now."

" **Clean.** " With a single word, the powdered bones are cleaned from her hands, and Cassiel smiles giddily.

"That was so cool, Sammy! You kicked their- well, I was going to say 'butts,' but you crushed their heads, so I guess that doesn't make sense. Hmm." Her excitement vanishes as the strange philosophical questions of _butts versus heads_ appears in her mind. "Well, given those were, you know, demonic skeletons, don't you feel bad about killing them?"

The question sends a wave of visible disgust washing across Samantha's face. "Oh, seven hells, no! Skeletons are disgusting, creepy creatures. I always like to put them out of their misery. I have no particular affinity toward generic monsters either, but I try not to harm other demons. They're kin, after all."

We take off down the opposite path as more questions occur to me. "Where do monsters come from, if they're not demons? Do demons create monsters, like orcs and goblins?"

Samantha adjusts the strips of fabric barely covering her body. "Well, humans like to pretend that monsters are from myths and legends, or rather, they preferred to at one point, but monsters have always been around. They were violent and didn't quite catch on with the human populace, since they aren't cute and cuddly, you see. The monsters in the Labyrinth were once on Earth, but they moved inside the Labyrinth at some point before Earth's destruction."

I raise an eyebrow. "So... who created them?"

"No idea." Samantha's curt reply makes me drop the subject.

A few minutes later, Samantha stops suddenly as we round a corner. Cass and I pause as the succubus squints and looks up ahead. "Now, that's odd."

In the distance, the sound of bare feet pattering catches up to us as dozens of goblins and orcs round the corner. Each of them is armed to the teeth, and the moment they spot us, a bloodthirsty look enters their eyes.

This time, I step up to bat. "I'll take care of these, Sam. Even if they aren't demons, it's not right that you should kill them."

Samantha chews on her lower lip. "No, Hero, this is odd. I sense a larger amount of energy in their bodies compared to their expected amount, and they seem much more vicious than they should be."

Cassiel's usually cheerful expression is gone, replaced with a darker one. "Need a second opinion? The energy in their bodies is twisted and malformed. It's as if they're consumed with rage."

"I agree," Samantha says, as she glances at me. "I would normally prefer we don't kill them, but... a fight might be unavoidable."

The goblins and orcs begin charging down the corridor, and Cassiel's expression brightens. "I have an idea!"

Saying that, she aims her hand at the horde charging forth. I feel an odd sensation in the air, and a moment later, confusion spreads across their faces, then they stop moving and flop forward to the ground. Cassiel smiles, "Piece of cake. I gave them a nice snooze."

"Maybe when they wake up they'll be less cranky," Samantha mumbles.

I teleport the three of us past them, wondering privately to myself why I didn't just teleport us to a different floor before, and after a few more minutes of walking, Samantha sighs. "Oh, brother. I hate to be the bearer of bad news, but we're being followed, Hero."

"We are? By who?" I look behind and in front of us, but Samantha shakes her head.

"I don't know by who, exactly, but they're a demon of tremendous power. She or he is following us and keeping a distance, but probably doesn't know I can feel the aura of anger from here. Seriously, the weird emotion in the Labyrinth is so out-of-place compared to normal. I wonder what's going on?"

A few ideas come to mind, but since I'm unsure, I keep them to myself. "Let's find a bigger area where we can face this demon and spread out. If they're as powerful as you say, I'd rather not take any chances."

Using my mental map, I trace a path to one particularly sizeable nearby room, and as we run, Samantha looks increasingly more and more agitated. "Still following us, and picking up the pace too. I think they're getting closer..."

We hurry around one last corner and step into a large chamber, one that immediately makes me cock an eyebrow. The room is very familiar. A pedestal sits in the center, and the room is filled with gold and jewels, but it takes me a moment to recognize the place.

It's the room where I first found Hoarhiim.

Indeed, the pedestal sits by itself, but the three statues I remember from before are missing. The gold and jewels are still everywhere, but the room looks _different_ from how I remember. _Odd_.

Samantha, Cassiel and I turn around and face the hallway from whence we came. It takes a whole minute, but Samantha tenses up. "They're here."

From around the corner, a long beak appears, filled with razor-sharp teeth. A scaled monster I've seen before steps from around the corner, standing nearly as tall as me. His clubbed tail thumps against the wall, as if to announce his entrance, and his clawed talons clench as he spots us.

"Hoho, it seems I've found my prey. The human and the angel who escaped me once, as well as..." He pauses to squint at Samantha. "The Second Emperor in the flesh? It's my lucky day."

Cassiel's body trembles slightly, but she keeps up a brave face. "Gressil. I've been thinking about you a lot lately."

I glance at her, trying to keep a neutral expression. _You have?_ I want to ask, but don't.

A long forked tongue slips out of Gressil's mouth as he takes a menacing step forward. "Interesting, my pretty angel. I've thought about you as well. All of my angelic prisoners are dead, and you were the last. I've been _dying_ to put you back where you belong."

Nobody says a word for a few seconds, but Samantha gently pulls Cassiel behind her. "I don't care how strong you think you are, Gressil. If you want to hurt my friend, you'll have to go through me first. You're overestimating your chances of winning here."

"I don't need to _win_." Gressil's reply comes immediately. "I just want my prisoner back. Is that too much to ask, _Second Emperor_? Look at you, snuggling up to a human and an angel. You're a disgrace to the demons. It's no wonder the First had to step up to fix the mess this 'Hero' has caused."

The blood drains from Samantha's face. "Th-the First Emperor? He's- he's working now?"

Gressil chuckles. "Kukuku... you don't know? Ah, that's right, you've been away from home for a while, haven't you? The pests have risen to fight the demons, and until recently, they were cleaning us right up! But thanks to the power of the First, we're taking back the Labyrinth from the worthless meatbags."

Samantha's eyes widen as she looks at me. "This explains a lot..." Her voice is nearly a whisper. "The First Emperor is aiding the demons now. Unless you help them, the humans are finished."

The monstrous demon chuckles again. "Now you see the error of your ways. If you'll be so kind as to give me my prisoner back, I'll overlook this transgression. The First doesn't need to know about your betrayal." He snickers to himself and turns to Cassiel. "I look forward to hearing your whimpering and begging once more. It has been quite boring without you to entertain me. You were always my favorite, you know."

"I've had just about enough of this." I take a step forward and reach toward him, but Cassiel bats my hand away.

"No, Hero. Violence isn't the answer. I don't hate Gressil. He may be a monster, but he's not a monster just because he's a demon. Samantha has shown me that much. As bad as Gressil is, I won't take revenge, and I won't let him control my life. I'm free, now."

Gressil blinks twice. "You're not afraid of me? As if you think I can't see your soft, pale flesh trembling. You're quaking in terror. You remember the shape of my claws, the ripping of my talons against your skin. I doubt the scars have healed, especially the ones in your heart."

Cassiel shakes her head. "I'm not scared of you. In a way, I... I pity you. When I was alone in that cell, I hated knowing you would return- but at the same time, I wanted you there, rather than the silent emptiness around me. In a way, you were my only friend. If you hadn't been there, I would have withered away and died."

Gressil's usual confidence cracks slightly. "A friend? Impossible. Who could be friends with their torturer? You sound delusional! Perhaps your mind cracked under an eternity of torture. Breaking you was always one of my deepest desires... and it would seem I achieved that goal."

"You're sick. A vile evil creature." Samantha hisses the words out and clenches her fist, but Cassiel stops her again.

"He can't help the way he is, Sammy. If there's anyone I should hate, it's humans. Many humans experimented on myself and the other angels, through awful periods of pain and suffering that would make you weep. Gressil, however, was just one demon. He doesn't represent the rest of them. From what I know of demons, they follow a hierarchy, so he was assigned to be a prison warden, and that's what he became. He adapted to his job, and he enjoyed it. He made the best of a bad situation."

Samantha and I both are in shock. She's the first to verbalize. " _The best of a bad situation_? Cass, he's a psychopathic monster. You're being way too forgiving here."

"Yes, but that's my choice." Cassiel takes a tentative step toward the menacing demon. "Gressil, sometimes you scare me, but I still forgive you. I would consider you an ally, even, if you were to change your ways."

For once, the monstrous demon is at a loss for words. "You...! I can't just- what?! Foolish little-!"

He stutters and stammers, and I stare at Cassiel in shock. "You take pacifism to a new level, Cass. This is out of the ordinary, even for you."

She shakes her head. "It's not like that, Hero. I have to lead by example. You're always itching for a fight, and you always want to solve problems with your fists, but that's not the right way to do things. If I can't forgive Gressil, I can't teach you how to put an end to war."

Gressil finally seems to have cleared his head. He sighs. "I suppose in the end, I didn't break you. I'll just have to torture you more and more, until I finally do."

A glint appears in his eye. Gressil lunges forward with his tail, aiming to wrap around Cassiel's throat just as he did to me. Before I can react, Samantha dashes forward, batters his tail out of the way, and delivers a colossal blow to his abdomen. A violent explosion of blood blasts out behind him and a wave of energy rocks the room around us. Gressil's feet talons dig ino the ground, somehow holding him in place for a second before his upper body rips from his legs and smashes into the wall on the other side of the room.

Not a sound escapes his lips at any point during the brutal slaughter.

Cassiel stares at his corpse numbly. "Sam... Sam... why? You shouldn't have interfered. I- I was going to change him!"

Samantha shakes her head, and when she turns back to look at Cassiel and me, I notice the amount of brown blood covering the front of her body. "You're wrong. Gressil was set in his ways. He would have hurt you, and I can't allow that. Fellow demon or not, you matter more to me than he does. If someone tries to cause you harm, I'll end them."

Cassiel's lip quivers as she fights back tears. "I can- I can protect myself! Hero has given me all this energy, this power! I could have destroyed Gressil with a thought... it wasn't about whether I was in danger, it was about my conviction! Why can't you see that?!" The sadness in her voice turns to anger as the tears finally roll down. "I wanted to show you that love conquers hatred! All you've done is show that anger leads to more violence!"

As Cassiel yells, images from a memory I can't place of her destroying Gressil with an energy beam enter my mind. "She's right, Samantha. This was her battle to fight. You've hurt her by undoing her act of forgiveness. I'm glad you stood up for Cass, but that was the wrong way to do it."

Samantha pivots and glowers at me. "You're taking her side in this? Gressil was going to attack her, and you would have let him do it! Where's your masculine pride and chivalry?"

Cassiel quickly calms down, though she can't stop staring at Gressil's warm, quivering corpse. "Sammy, he was no danger to me. You jumped the gun, and now he's dead. You didn't have to do that, and shouldn't have. Is that what you're going to do when we see the humans? Are you going to kill every demon that has a bone to pick with the last remaining angel?"

"Well... no..." Samantha's indignation softens and is replaced with embarrassment. "I wouldn't go that far."

"Then think before you punch." Having delivered her verbal backhand, Cassiel sighs deeply. "Well, what's done is done. He's dead." She squints at the body. "I didn't even see you move. Are you really that fast?"

Samantha smiles slightly. "Strong, too. You know that the Emperors are the strongest of all demons, right? Even powered up by the First, Gressil never stood a chance."

"Good to know," I mutter quietly. "Let's get a move on. Our little detour has cost us more time, but luckily this room is close to the entrance to Hero City. We'll be there sooner than you expect."

Both girls nod, and as we head out the opposite exit, Cassiel turns back to Gressil's corpse one last time. "Sometimes I'm not sure why I even bother."
