Part 253a

"How do you know about Wordsmithing?"

The direct approach, it always works for me. Not this time. Satan grinned at my question. "That's for me to know, and for you to wonder about."

"You said you've fought someone in the past who was a Wordsmith... what was their name?"

"I'm not going to tell you anything, idiot." Satan laughed quietly. "Especially since their power was the only one I couldn't... well, never mind that. Actually, I'll tell you what, mortal... if you can defeat me in combat, I'll tell you anything you want to know. How does that sound?"

Combat? Sounds like he's bloodthirsty, as expected of the highest ranking devil. "Is that why all of this is happening... just so you can battle another 'hero'? Is this all just a game to you?"

"No. It's not just a game. This is serious work." Satan's smile faded. "It's been millennia, you know... thousands of years have my people fought for the right to live on this world, breathe the same air as you mortals, roam in the same spaces... instead we're forced to live in our own space, separated and segregated, simply because humans are too bigoted to accept the likes of us."

"You mean the labyrinth?"

Satan's eyes narrowed slightly. "You know of it, hmm? You're more dangerous than I thought. Time travel is no mean feat. I can only assume you used your wordsmithing to somehow travel backwards. You mentioned when you first arrived that Earth was destroyed by the Volgrim a few centuries from now, so if you know of the labyrinth... that would mean that you were likely born inside of it. Am I correct?"

"I'll return the slight, devil. I'm not telling you anything."

Satan sighed as he shrugged his shoulders. "Ah, well, if that's how it's going to be, I understand. It appears both of us will have to beat the living hell out of one another to get the answers we seek. The only issue for you is that in this battle, I have _all_ the advantages."

"Ah, is that so? We will see."

Beneath my calm exterior, I was actually quite worried. If Satan knows about wordsmithing, even to the point of defusing my instant-death words, then that would mean he's fought a wordsmith in the past. How was he able to evade their death words though? Perhaps he was a strategic genius, able to figure out their abilities beyond what even they knew. Perhaps they simply didn't think to use the words on him at first. Anything could have happened, and I don't even know who they were or when they fought.

Satan stood stiffly and closed his eyes. "As a gentleman of unprecedented charisma, I shall let you have the first attack. Make it count, for you will not get another."

"How civilized." Without waiting for a response, I jumped at him as my visor closed shut. " **BIND!** "

An invisible force suddenly jerked around Satan, planting him firmly in place. He momentarily appeared shocked, but then seemed to ignore it as he braced himself for my fist. A split second before my fist connected, I yelled again. " **PIERCE!** "

My fist rammed through Satan's tough hide, the impact jarring through my suit right into the deepest core of my body. Satan flopped backwards a few feet and landed on his ass. As I shook my hand slightly, I realized it felt like all the bones in my fingers had been shattered. " _Heal_."

Satan stood up a second later, rubbing his chest. "Not bad! That actually hurt, mortal!" He quickly cast a spell and then stopped rubbing his chest. "Oh look, the pain is gone. Now the question you must ask yourself is thus: do you forfeit now, or try for another one? I won't go easy on you."

He already escaped the Bind spell? Damn, he's making fun of me. I flipped my visor open to respond. "Even if I were to forfeit, you won't let me walk away. Besides, I have other tricks up my sleeve."

"Excellent. That's the spirit." Satan stared at me blandly. Despite his words, he appeared to be wholly bored by the interaction. Was I really not a challenge for him? "However, next time, don't just go running around, swinging your fists. It's so inelegant and thoughtless. The mark of a buffoon."

"I'll show you 'buffoo-'" I started to try my hand at a witty retort, but suddenly I felt something invisible grab my body by my right leg and yank me up into the air. "What the hell-?!"

Before I had a chance to react, I felt my body lurch towards the ground at insane speeds, my suit's inertia dampeners screaming to compensate. I smashed into the ground at an ungodly speed, the impact surely being strong enough to grind me into mulch, were it not for the defenses of my suit.

As my mind quickly tried to process what had happened, I was suddenly lifted up again, and smashed into the ground again, this repeated over and over a dozen times or so before I was tossed over to the side, my brain spinning as I struggled to orient myself.

"Oh wow. You're not dead yet." Satan spoke emptily as he eyed me struggling to my feet. "Truly incredible."

I finally stood up tall again, mentally ordering my suit to begin scanning all types of energy on the visible and invisible spectrum. In a flash it rotated through hundreds of known frequencies before settling on a variant of infrared, showing me... invisible arms?!

Ten invisible limbs stretched outside of Satan, their movements erratic, jerking to and fro, as if they had a mind of their own. I could barely perceive them even if I knew they existed. However, that was only due to the limits of technology. I possessed magic and technology both...

" _Detect._ " My visor closed shut for a split second as I used the spell, and suddenly, just like that, I could see the limbs moving around as if they were made of flesh and blood. Satan couldn't see my eyes, but by now he knew that when my visor closed, I was casting a word spell.

"What did you try this time, little mortal?" Satan almost seemed to look into my eyes for a split second. "Oh, I understand. You can see _them_, can't you? My most treasured ability. However, even if you can see them, they will prove to be quite impossible to avoid. The only reason you landed a blow on me earlier was because I allowed you to do so. These 10 limbs form the perfect offense, and the perfect defense."

"Do they, I wonder? That's the second time I've heard that phrase as of late. We should test that theory out."

What happens when an unstoppable force meets an immovable object? I'll find out soon, and I don't know if I'll like the answer.
