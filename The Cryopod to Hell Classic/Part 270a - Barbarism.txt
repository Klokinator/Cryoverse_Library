Part 270a - Barbarism

I am old. Very old.

Not as old as the archangels, or even the gods, of course, but still exceedingly old. I lived through the wars of the second era, and the third era, and now hundreds of eras later as well. I have seen many things, and I have killed many with my own hands.

And yet, for a demon as hardened in combat and as world weary who has seen as many things as I have, this is still so... stomach churning. I must say that this is not how I imagined this battle would work.

I recently read about the atrocities humans committed against their own in their second world war. No demon, angel, god, or dare I permit myself to guess, not even a Volgrim would treat their own or the enemy as the humans treated their own kind during those awful wars. I suppose the saying 'anything goes in war' was coined by somebody who lived through that era of barbarism, but I still suspect even they would find this to be unsettling.

Thanks to that strange device the human woman showed us, we have the ability to completely nullify an angel's ability to fight. I'm not into the mechanical stuff like those young demons are, but I can understand the general way in which it works. Something about their unique spiritual energy backfires inside them, rendering their entire body immobile, as they fall to the ground, unable to lift even a finger.

I hate the angels and the gods. I really do. They butchered my brethren when they caught even a whiff of free-thinking. But when you can render an entire army totally helpless, and the only thing you have left to worry about is how quickly you can turn their bodies into mulch... it doesn't sit right with me.

It's not that I sympathize with the angels. By no means! I just prefer a foe who can at least offer a modicum of resistance, no matter how pitiful. Worse, we're not even killing real angels right now. These are pseudo-angels, humans born long after the end of the Great War in Heaven. They really have no skin in the game, and were brought here by the despicable gods and angels to do their dirty work for them. And, unlike even the imps of my people, these pseudo-angels have no ability to leave Heaven. They are imprisoned here, until they are sent out to die.

I cannot simply allow this to happen.

"Satan." I walk up to our dear leader, who is once again exercising the abilities of Hercules, the first Hero he ever killed in combat. The ability to command his armies all at once, granting them unparalleled unstoppable power.

Satan's eyes twitch to and fro, as he looks from one end of the carnage to the other. "Yes, Agares? I'm busy right now. Can it wait?"

A head is ripped from a pseudo-angel nearby and hurled to the rear of the field, as it smashes into the ground, exploding in a bloody mess just a few dozen feet away from the both of us. I glance at it, but Satan doesn't pay it the slightest bit of attention.

"My lord... this is unbecoming of us. Slaughtering angels and gods that are unable to fight back... we're no better than they were when they killed our forefathers. Surely we can fight at least somewhat fairly?"

Satan's face scrunched up as he flicked his eyes over to me. I could tell he didn't want to have this discussion. "Elder Agares, I have always respected your input, especially in matters of war. However, the time to object was before we entered the battlefield, not afterwards. Further, we are not the villains here. An eye for an eye, that is the demon way. They did in fact slaughter our forefathers, so we will enact full revenge on them as way of repayment. Do I make myself clear?"

Satan's last words were strangled, as if to threaten me with his mere tone of voice. I leaned heavily on my gnarled wooden cane as I nodded quietly. "Yes... my lord."

Satan went back to ignoring me as his eyes roamed forwards to the clouds soaked red with the blood of his most hated enemies. Of course he wouldn't listen to my counsel. I should have mentioned my objections earlier, as he said. He was right.

Still, I looked around the battlefield with saddened old eyes as I watched tens of thousands of angels falling like wheat to the scythe. I started to turn around to walk away, but Satan spoke up. "Hold. A moment, if you will. The Hero is coming, and I am busy guiding the army. Would you be so kind as to slow him down for a while? Distract him. Take his focus off me, if you would. I will deal with him when the time is ready."

I slowly turned back around to Satan, as my knees creaked. "Ah, but I am just an old demon, worn out and tired. I don't think I would be able to do much against such a young and powerful Hero. Perhaps you should ask Bael or Diablo? Surely they would be better equipped to handle one with such immeasurable power?"

Satan sneered as he stifled a chuckle. "A good jest, but let's end the charade. You will do just fine against him, Lord of Reptiles. It was not a request, it was a direct order."

I felt a sparkle gleam in my eye as he spoke. "You'd order a poor old man to his death? For shame. However, as my lord, I will obey your command. I'll try to slow him down, if only just for a few moments, even if it should kill this old man."

As I turned and hobbled away on my cane, Satan chuckled quietly. "Yes, yes. Just don't kill him, please. I need to have some fun too, you know."

...................................................................

As I flew forwards using my Hypersuit's flight system to conserve my own energy, I felt panic rising at how easily the pseudo-angels were falling. My suit quickly brought up estimated stats... two million dead. To be fair, out of tens of billions, it was a small number, but not a single demon or Volgrim had died yet. This was a veritable holocaust with how easily they were cleaning shop.

Zeus and Gabriel flew alongside me, as we raced towards the enemy. While only a few miles away, I constantly kept flying down to smash the devices that were being shot randomly all over the place, each device completely incapacitating any angels or gods within a certain area of its presence. The good news was that they were quite vulnerable to being smashed, but the bad news was that I was the only one who could smash them, and the enemy was shooting them around far faster than I could break them!

"Fuck! I need help! Ah, wait, I've got an idea!" I quickly landed in a small clearing where the angels weren't bunched too close together. Aiming my hand up in the air, I quickly cast a spell.

" **SUMMON!** "

As I cast it, over and over, my clones began to appear, one by one. The energy cost of bringing them to me was quite small luckily, so soon I had over three hundred hypersuit-outfitted clones to help in the battle.

Quickly I yelled out. "All clones! Immediately clone yourselves now, then have those clones clone themselves!"

They nodded as they quickly took note of what was going on around them. Seconds later, their numbers swelled from 300 to 600, and then to 900. It would have to do.

"Spread out, destroy all the energy nullifier devices you can find! We can't let the demons and Volgrim kill our friends!"

Within seconds, all of them were speeding off to seek these devices. I could have summoned more clones, but the lowest level clones would be down to pitiful amounts of energy, and in one fell swoop, I had taken us from 7th level cloning to 9th level. I wasn't sure what the immediate repercussions might be, nor did I wish to find out what the long-term effects would be either.

I quickly located the leader. Satan. As I thought, he was over with his other generals, a terrifying fighting force, one I was not keen on attacking so soon after he had crushed me swiftly last time. Still, to kill a snake, you have to cut off its head.

I dashed forwards, leaping into the air as I ignited my jets to blast over towards him.

A few moments later, something odd happened. My forward momentum turned into downwards momentum, and I suddenly careened at the ground, as if a massive anvil were attached to my body.

**BOOM!**

I hit the ground with a tremendous impact, dust flying up in the air in all directions as my body felt the pressure that had yanked me downwards release. Zeus and Gabriel had continued flying ahead to attack the enemy head on, but as I raised my head up, I found myself lying a few feet away from... an old man.

"Greetings, young one. My name is Agares. Forgive me for pulling you in, but I was hoping we might have a nice, splendid chat."

An old demon stood before me, as I pulled myself up to a standing position. With a long white beard and very bushy eyebrows, combined with a receding hairline, and an unimposing hunched posture accentuated by a beaten-up wooden cane, he cut quite the miniscule figure in front of my Herculean body.

"Sorry, but while the demons may be my enemy, I'm not in the habit of attacking the elderly. If you move out of my way, I won't hurt you."

I coiled my legs to spring up, the impact from a few seconds before having caused me no damage whatsoever. In the blink of an eye, I shot upwards, leaping over the strange old man the final quarter-mile towards Satan's lookout, where he appeared to be facing down Zeus and Gabriel both.

Before I could clear much distance, I felt an invisible arm grab hold of my foot, yanking _hard_ as I was suddenly pulled in reverse back to my original jumping location, once again smashing onto the ground with a loud _crash_.

Now I lay on my back, once again facing that strange old man as he stroked his beard. Suddenly, I perceived a slight dark aura engulfing his body as he eyed me with cold, unforgiving eyes.

"It's not polite to disrespect your elders like that, mortal."
