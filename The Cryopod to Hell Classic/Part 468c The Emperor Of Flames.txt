Part 468c: The Emperor Of Flames

"Look what the Hellhound dragged in," Beelzebub quips as I teleport to Ben's location. Cassiel and Samantha pop in beside me and shake the sensation of disorientation off in an instant. "I'm guessing the Orcs, Skeletons, and Vampires couldn't delay the _mighty_ Wordsmith for as long as I'd hoped."

I shoot a glance at Samantha, then back to Beelzebub. "I only saw Skeletons and Orcs. But you're right; they didn't last long."

"Accursed vampires. Sloth is their greatest weakness. Can't even show up for a quick distraction."

My eyes flick to the side to look past Beelzebub, and I spot several species of monsters I haven't seen before, all of them lined up behind him, ready for action. Two massive golems, their bodies made of brick-shaped rocks, stand fifty feet tall, towering over all the smaller monster swarming around their feet. Aside from them, dozens of harpies, goblins, scythers, and other assorted monsters cluster together, waiting for Beelzebub to give the command to attack.

"You returned to the demon side, huh? I guess Diablo needs a new field commander after Samantha took him out of commission. What does turning coat pay nowadays?"

Beelzebub _tsks_ at me, and Samantha nudges my arm. "Jason, Beelzebub is different. You probably can't sense it, but he has way more energy than he did before. A stupid increase, if I'm reading it correctly."

"Quite right, Belial. You may be a filthy human-lover, but you can sense the power of royalty when it presents itself." Beelzebub fans out his fingers to examine his nails, and puts on an amused smile. "I suppose you could call my new power a _gift_ from Diablo. He couldn't give me his energy quickly enough."

"Nice one, boss!" A goblin cackles in the background, but Beelzebub doesn't think it's amusing.

"You'll speak when spoken to, worm." With a flick of his finger, a tremendous flame balls up in Beelzebub's hand, sucking the oxygen from the air in a single second, and he compresses the flame into a tiny water-drop sized ball, then fires it at the goblin.

The poor sap doesn't get a chance to contemplate his existence. The instant the fire strikes his chest, his body swells up like a balloon and explodes, showering the horde behind Beelzebub in a gory fountain of blood. Whatever Beelzebub hit him with, it was condensed like a tiny atom-bomb.

"Gross," remarks a Harpy in the front row. She shakes the blood from her wings and scowls at Beelzebub, but a menacing glare from him shuts her up instantly.

"What was I saying before the peasant so _rudely_ interrupted me? Ah, that's right, I remember." Beelzebub quickly pivots back to us and forces another fake smile. "I'm here to kill all of you, by order of the First Emperor. I should be polite and firstly inform you of the recent changes in demonic hierarchy, since you clearly weren't aware of my surge in power. I am the new Second Emperor of the demons, ousting the failed leader, Diablo, and now I even surpass the power of Belial, thanks to a most generous energy increase from our dear First Emperor."

Samantha is unamused. "You mean, Satan, right?"

Her simple reply makes a vein bulge on Beelzebub's forehead, though he tries not to let his sudden frustration shine through. "O-oh, you remember? He told me that he wiped your memory. Well, at least that makes things simpler, though he won't be pleased that the sphinx is out of the bag." Another look of irritation appears and he relents to scowl at me. "I assume you restored the whore's memories?"

"Yes, but don't call her a whore. I have a new no-killing policy, but even for a _good friend_ like you, nothing's stopping me from reverting to my old ways."

"Always the white knight, I see. A hero trying to flee from his bloody, dictatorial past. Tell me, though, if you won't kill anyone, how will you defeat me in a battle?"

His question might usually inspire a long-winded answer, but this time I opt to show instead of tell. " **Sleep.** "

All at once, a series of sounds resembling boulders bouncing down a hill create music for my ears, as the horde behind Beelzebub suddenly trips over themselves and flop to the ground, fast asleep. Curiously, the two massive golems are unaffected. Probably a good thing, considering if they fell over, they'd crush the smaller ones beneath them.

Beelzebub yawns dramatically. "Oh yes, use your Wordsmithing to put them to sleep. Nobody saw that coming."

What happens next takes me aback. Beelzebub snaps his fingers, and the moment he clicks them, my spell is broken. All the monsters suddenly wake up and clamber back into a standing position, confusion on their faces.

It looks like Beelzebub learned some new abilities. "How did you do that?"

Cassiel, silent up to now, interjects before he can answer. "He couldn't possibly have used magic. Snapping a finger doesn't activate magic, at least not for demons."

"Are you sure about that?" Beelzebub chortles to himself. "I'm not the same demon I once was. Go on, try something new. You have two more chances, but if you won't kill or defeat us, I'll-"

He doesn't get to finish his sentence. From out of nowhere, Benjamin Brown leaps into action, blasting up to Beelzebub at nearly the speed of sound, and delivering a punch right to his face with an explosive level of power that knocks his head clean off. Ben follows up with a terrific blast of energy from the palm of his hand, obliterating Beelzebub's body instantly, and creating a massive impact crater on the ground.

Every monster leaps back in fright, except for the golems who stare forward blankly. Ben coughs into his fist. "Stupid fucker. I couldn't stand to hear him blather on for a moment longer. Why do you idiots always let the bad guy just sit there and talk? Take a weapon and blow his fucking head off! It's not rocket science!"

"You wound me."

From the horde of monsters, Beelzebub emerges out of nowhere and strides over to us. He nods at Ben. "That was a good effort, black-skinned, white-armored man with the unkempt beard. Fortunately, such profane and impolite violence will not work against the Emperor of Flames."

"What the fuck?!" Ben practically spits the words out. "I took your head clean off! No demon magic I've ever seen can prevent death!"

A moment passes, and Beelzebub stares at Ben blankly. Then, he chuckles. "Perhaps. Of course, I've died once before, so I probably know more on the subject than you."

Ben grits his teeth. "The fuck you do." His eyes swing over to me. "He's a clone. That ain't the real Beelzebub. Neither was the first one I killed."

Beelzebub's smile vanishes. " _What_? How could you have figured that out so quickly?"

"Oh, simple. I'm a clone of the original Benjamin Brown. It's something that keeps me awake at night; I'm sure you understand. Jason, look for the original. I need to break his back and shove an apple in his mouth."

I don't know how Ben pieced together this information so quickly, but my warning bells are going off. "How can you clone yourself? You've never had magic like that. And waking up the monsters- that's not something you can do."

Casually, Beelzebub begins to pace back and forth. "Fire brings death and destruction, but it also brings life. After you killed me, those few years ago, my revival taught me that death could be defied. I've plotted, brainstormed, and calculated all the ways your Wordsmithing worked, and finally ended up with ways to counter it."

Despite his short speech, neither the girls nor I believe him. Cassiel smiles uneasily. "If you keep lying like that, your _pants_ will be the new Emperor of Flames. Don't tell me you killed the people in Hero City just to experiment with cloning."

"I've done what I needed to increase my powers," Beelzebub says, as his tone becomes defensive. "I didn't kill any humans, though. Not that I wouldn't want to, of course. In fact..."

The words leave Beelzebub's mouth, and a flame suddenly sprouts in his hand.

"Look out!" Ben's helmet closes up, and he races forward, just as Beelzebub hurls the micro-bomb of fire at our soldiers in the rear flanks.

The pinpoint of flame explodes against Ben's armor, sending him flying backward, where he smashes into half a dozen men and women, and the blast surges outward, nearly engulfing the girls and I in it. Before that can happen, Cassiel raises her hand and a barrier of light shields us from the worst of it.

We don't get a second to catch our breath. Five more balls of fire appear above each of Beelzebub's fingers, but just as he's about to throw them, I yell, " **Extinguish!** "

The fireballs vanish, but reappear an instant later, and he sends the bombs toward our flanks, fanning them out in several directions at once.

_I can't say the words in time! He's going to-!_

The flames suddenly vanish in mid-air, and I realize with a start that Ionis has appeared in front of the soldiers. He cradles Howard in both arms, and the duck stares at me, almost judgmentally.

ORGANIC UNIT DESIGNATED JASON HIRO; THE COMMANDER UNIT HAS ORDERED ME TO PROTECT THE ANGEL-TYPE ORGANIC AT ALL COSTS.

Beelzebub jerks back in surprise. "The robot? Where- how?! My flames!"

I breathe a sigh of relief, though that was way too close of a call. "N-nice work, Ionis. I don't think Cassiel needs any saving, but those men did."

A few moments pass, and my heart pounds in my chest. _I've had enough of this shit._

I'm still wearing the Crown of Solomon. Time to put it to use.

" **Teleport! Bomb!** "

With two words, I forcibly send Samantha and Cassiel to the rear flanks, several dozen feet away, and a strange, perfectly round sphere of metal appears in my hand.

"Beelzebub, I didn't want to kill anyone today, including you, but I've just changed my mind. The next time you make the wrong move, I'll detonate this."

He doesn't flinch. "Oh no, a bomb. Yes, kill us all, including your comrades, why don't you? Even you wouldn't be that foolish."

"This isn't an ordinary bomb. It's a magical device. I've designed it to teleport all nearby angels and humans back to Hero City the instant it detonates. However, all demons in the immediate radius will be engulfed in a deadly radiation that will melt their body, killing them instantly. This includes Samantha, but I'll teleport her to safety before that can happen."

Beelzebub's smirk vanishes, and I eye him carefully. "Go ahead, Beelzy. Try me. Make my day. It won't be my fault if you die."

He takes two steps back and evaluates the device in my hand carefully. "You're bluffing. Either that, or the human has finally remembered his savage roots."

"Yeah, I'm a savage, all right. Let me guess; the real Beelzebub is hidden away somewhere safe, and if you die, he'll just create another one. The thing is, you care a lot about your image. Even if you dying won't bother him, losing all those monsters will make you look like a shitty leader to the rest of the demons. It'll be a loss, and you _hate_ losing. Sound about right?"

He slowly licks his lips. "When did you have the time to give my personality so much thought? You can't be the Jason I know."

I shrug. "I'm trying to improve myself. What can I say?"

Beelzebub doesn't find my reply amusing. "There's something you didn't think of, though. What if there aren't any monsters to kill?"

Again, Beelzebub snaps his fingers, and instantly the golems, harpies, goblins, and every other creature vanish. They simply pop out of existence.

_Just as if I had teleported them with my Wordsmithing._

Beelzebub ignites a flame above his finger and points it at my bomb. "There's nothing more fun than calling a bluff."

He releases the fire.
