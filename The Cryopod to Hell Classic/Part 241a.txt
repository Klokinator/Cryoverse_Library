Naturally, Helen was quite surprised to hear that Harold was still alive, even sound in mind, though I warned her that like herself, he had undergone a change of his own.

"So, Helen can come back with me to the Mothership?" I turned and spoke to the one who had apparently found her, who happened to be Horus. Apparently, being a god of wisdom, he was able to comb though like a hundred billion people or whatever to find one single person, and unlike the stories on earth, the Pearly Gates don't actually keep a record of who is revived. Heck, it might be kind of crazy if Hitler, a catholic, was hanging around up here somewhere... actually now I'm kind of creeped out, I'd better not think about it.

Horus nodded sagely. "We are only allowing it, since, as we understand things, the Mothership is fully staffed by robots and there are no humans or demons on board, aside from yourself. The existence of angels, at least for now, _must_ be kept a secret. The demons know we have grown in power, just as we know they have, but neither of us knows by exactly how much. It's a dangerous game we're playing. We stand on the edge of a knife, and if we stray just a little, our forces will surely fail, to the ruin of all." Horus spoke in that usual strange 'old woman' voice while possessing a male body and the head of a bird, and the entire visual and audible effect just weirded me out.

"That's fair enough. I'm glad the archangels and gods came to this conclusion, Harold will be very pleased. To be honest, you all act as if you're separated from humanity, like you don't care at all, but I see glimpses of compassion for humans every now and then. I think you're all just big softies."

Raphael chuckled softly. "Is that so? Perhaps you are correct. Our fates are bound to humanity's, so perhaps we have become somewhat high-strung over maintaining it on the correct path. I don't see it as compassion, so much as obsession."

Oh, Raphael, always putting things in a box. He likes to treat everything as if it's a puzzle that needs solving, but I can't say I dislike him for it. He's certainly earned the title of 'wisdom king'.

I glance down at the sword now hanging by my side. Excalibur, the legendary holy sword... and I definitely felt King Arthur's presence enter me, though I can't figure out how to speak to him. It's probably just too soon. What I do know is that this sword has vastly increased my power level... I'd say I'm at 1/4th my strength compared to when I was God-King. That might not seem like much, but that makes me a quarter as powerful as an even stronger combination of Raphael/Michael/Gabriel than what might exist in this current time.

I don't honestly know how I compare to others in this time, especially entities such as Satan. Without Amelia's protection against wordsmithing, would I be able to kill him with just a single word? Even if I couldn't, would I be able to defeat him, one on one? I'm not the same as I was when we first fought. By now, I'm a combat veteran with almost twenty years of experience using my abilities. I'm not a child anymore playing god. I think the battle would be hard fought, but I would prevail, if only because of the strategies I could use against him.

Curiously, as much as she loved talking about him, Samantha never told me anything about Satan's combat abilities. I only remember the feeling of a thousand fists pounding my body about like a rag-doll. Was that his special ability? Was it his only one? To be the First Emperor of all demonkind, he must be unfathomably powerful, yet I know nothing about what his abilities are. If I must face him in combat, I should seek out anything I can with regards to his abilities.

In fact, an idea just popped into my head, but I'll have to test it when I'm alone.

I rested my hand on the hilt of Excalibur, it's glowing power filling me with a feeling of ease. "Zeus, the Volgrim will return sooner or later. We must decide now how we will proceed. Offense, or defense? We have faster-than-light technology in our grasp now, the effects of which will reverberate throughout humanity for a long time to come. We must think of a counter strategy for every possible scenario. If we can survive past the 24th century, that means that I will have changed the future, hopefully for the better."

Zeus nodded thoughtfully. "You might be right. In fact, I have an idea. Are you willing to hear me out?"

I nodded intently as I leaned in. "Yes, please. Anything would be helpful."

Later that day, I surprised Harold, who had been paying no attention to my boring talks in Heaven. I teleported back to the Mothership using "Recall", as I have a permanent waypoint set on board the ship, allowing me to return to it with super minimal energy cost any time I like. Naturally, I brought Helen along.

Harold immediately sensed me return. "Ah, kid, yer back! So good ta' see ya again. I've been workin' on them samples ya gave me and- hmm? Ye brought a lady friend?"

He started to speak again, but his voice caught in his throat, as his voice went from an internal one in my head, to one projected by the ship's PA system. "Ah can't... believe it... H-Helen? Is that you?!" He projected an image of himself mentally dropping his jaw. "Sweet heavens, yer alive! Yer really an angel! And damned gorgeous too, hoo-leey sheeit!"

Helen teared up as she heard her husband's voice. "Harold... I spent... fifty years in heaven, waiting for you, trying to find you. I never gave up hope, not even once!"

In the blink of an eye, she vanished, as Harold teleported her to his special holding room. "Ah, uh, 'scuse me, Jason. We need some private time ta... ta catch up. Hope ye understand."

I nodded soberly as I pictured how emotional this must be for them. "No problem, take your time."

He went totally silent, as he likely began directing his thoughts to his wife. _Till death do us part_, indeed. It seems marriages were made of different stuff a hundred years ago. A shame how everyone has let it degrade over the decades. This... this is true love, love that skips over time and space itself, preventing even the Reaper from interfering. Will I experience love like this? I personally doubt it. I'm too jaded. I was born in a different era, molded by a different system. I can appreciate their love for what it is... but I will never experience it.

Cassiel and Samantha... do I... did I love them? Truly love them? If I do, will I wait a hundred million years to be reunited with them? Will I bring them to me? Will I go to them? Will I... meet others and forget them?

I don't know. I only know that if it happens, I will have lost something precious to me.

In the meantime, there is something I must do. The idea I had back when I was at Heaven... I need to try it out. Satan may be powerful, but I have something unique. I have Wordsmithing. I can make the fabric of the universe change at my whim. And now, thanks to Excalibur, I have gained a tremendous amount of spiritual energy, the kind that could shatter planets. If I can't do this, what can I do?

I walk over to the Captain's Chair, the only chair on board the bridge of the Mothership. It's a large chair, the body made of stainless metal, and the cushions of the chair made of incredible comfort, since I repeatedly used 'soft' on it to make it easy to sink into.

Sitting down, every fold of the chair sucks me in, almost making me want to go to sleep. But I cannot rest, I must try this as soon as possible.

I close my eyes and spread my spatial vision to its absolute limits, but aim it in a conical direction at the Earth my ship is orbiting. I can't see the entire Earth, but this will do.

" **Locate!** "

I cast a spell, using a lot of energy to search the planet. My target is Satan. My mind spreads out, like a thousand spaceships swarming all over the planet at the speed of light, looking into every nook and cranny. For ten minutes, I sit uncomfortably, my heart pounding as I feel my colossal reserves of energy draining slowly. Sweat drips down my face as my body heats itself to deal with the incredible strain I'm putting on it. Luckily, my suit starts cooling me down to compensate. The A/C in this is crazy!

After almost fifteen straight minutes, I'm gasping audibly. The strain is unthinkable. I feel like I've run twenty marathons in a row without a minute of rest. But my patience pays off. I finally detect Satan, only to realize why I didn't detect him quickly before, as I had with Samantha. He's not on Earth, he's in... an entirely different dimension? Now that the strain of searching has worn off, my blood runs cold as I realize where he is. He's in the _labyrinth_. It already exists in this time? Then... it must have existed long before I was born, though I can only wonder how long that was in the first place.

But now that I've found him, I do a quick mental check of my energy reserves. 80% left?! Wow, I thought it drained a lot more than it actually did! This is great!

I need to understand Satan's powers, his abilities. I need to find out his weaknesses in case we should battle. So naturally, this is where my wordsmithing comes in.

Heh, this is just too easy.

" **Inspect.** "

I inspect Satan himself, trying to glean information about him. With my abilities, I may be able to see a lot of information, possibly even his full range of abilities, his energy level compared to mine, his background, maybe a description of his life!

However, my excitement turns to confusion. I get nothing back. Nothing happens at all. So strange, I feel like I should be getting even a negative reaction, but nothing happens at all.

My confusion rapidly escalates to **horror** as I suddenly feel something inside of me, there's a foul presence lurking, as if it's watching me from beyond a gate I shouldn't have crossed. I try to open my eyes but can't! From the darkness of my soul, I suddenly feel two white-hot burning fiery eyes up high in the sky, staring down on me with scorn and hatred. _He is watching_. I invaded his mind, but he had defenses set up. He knew someone might try this!

A voice blasts into my mind. " **THOU ART TRULY A FOOL! THOU WHO HATH ENTERED THE MIND OF MY MASTER, PREPARE THYSELF, FOR WE ART COMING! WE ART COMING TO RID THEE OF THY FLESHLY BODY, AND SEND THOU TO THE DEEPEST PITS OF SHEOL, WHERE THOU WILL BASK IN DEATH AND DEFEAT FOREVER! TEN THOUSAND CURSES BE UPON THEE, AND LET OUR MARK BE BURNED INTO THY FLESH!!! FEEL THE WRATH OF HADES, THE DARK GOD WITHIN THE FIRST EMPEROR! TREMBLE IN TERROR, AND MAY YOUR DEATH BE SLOW AND UNENDING!!!** "

A searing pain burned into my eyes and face as I screamed wordless agonizing screams of anguish. In a second, it was all gone and I staggered forwards, falling to the floor in front of me. I quickly jerked my eyes open. I could see, but something was horribly wrong. " _Mirror!_ "

I created a mirror in front of myself and gasped in horror. A blood red pentagram had been carved into my face, as if with a serial killers carving knife, its scar pulsing red like a demon lived inside of it. It pounded during each pulse, like it was re-burning itself into my flesh every time. " _Numb!_ " I tried numbing the pain, but it didn't work. This scar defied my abilities, feeling as if it had been especially created for me.

The pain was agonizing, but luckily the human body quickly grows used to such things. For almost ten minutes, I lay on the ground, gasping in pain, but slowly, I began to not notice it as intensely as I had a few minutes prior.

This... this... what the hell is this?!
