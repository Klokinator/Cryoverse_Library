Part 080: Battle Bowl 2

Simple words won't work. Amelia may say she's intelligent, but she probably won't know words from my world.

Looking at the swarm of flies that were about to hit me, I yelled again.

" **Exterminate!** "

Instantly, the swarm died, and Amelia looked pissed. "Those were my pets, you asshole!"

They could not have truly harmed me, but maybe they could have distracted my spatial vision or slowed my movement and hearing down... it could have made my situation much worse.

Amelia appeared to be mulling my powers over. "You really do possess many abilities, and you can make anything you want appear out of thin air... but..." She pulled out her dark sword again. It looked familiar to me. "When it comes to battle, I am much more seasoned than you, or any other demon. I'm going to make sure I take my time killing you, until you understand what true despair is!"

Suddenly, I recognized it. That sword, it was the one from my dream! But not only that, it was actually Dellfingler!

Amelia lurched at me again, this time quickly moving her hands to cast a spell. The sword _merged into her arm_.

Slashing and stabbing at me, I blocked her attacks with my bare hands. Now that I could track her position everywhere, she didn't appear fast to me at all. I punched hard at the spot I estimated her to appear, and right in time to blast her away from me, smashing through five layers of trees. When I tracked her again, I was shocked to see that her neck was snapped and her body covered in bruises. I... killed her?

No. The damage healed, and I noted a curious movement of energy from Blaarjiim to her body, as her neck straightened.

She got up, and stepped forward again. Suddenly jamming her hand into a tree, she lifted it effortlessly, using it as a massive crushing weapon. She charged at me and swung down.

I jumped away and it missed by a mile. It slowed down her movements noticeably.

"Grr... STOP MOVING! I'm going to squash you like a beetle!" She was visibly irritated that I was dodging so easily. This was easy. Really easy. I had always thought she was powerful, but apparently I've gotten even more powerful than her.

I can't be cocky. I refuse to let my guard down. I still know next to nothing about her power. All the demon lords I've turned into puppets had zero information on her abilities.

I decided to experiment a bit. " **Clone.** ", " **Clone.** ", " **Clone.** "

I summoned up three clones. "Kill her." I ordered them. If she had any new tricks, I'd know immediately. Each clone was half as powerful as me, but three clones were roughly 110% as powerful as I was, given the only somewhat coordinated teamwork.

It was vicious. Amelia didn't expect something like this. Over the course of three minutes, the clones attacked her relentlessly. Since I could stand back and watch, but also receive data from them, I noticed her attacks would grow weak, her body would be battered, and then she would heal up again. Each time this happened, that strange energy transfer from Blaarjiim would happen.

So that was it. She didn't eat the souls, she must have given the souls to Blaarjiim to hold. It made sense. He was a being of purely condensed spiritual energy. More spiritual energy would only increase his power, he probably had a limit but it was likely vast.

"Beelzebub, what are your thoughts so far?" I turned and spoke to the puppet. The puppet stared ahead blankly.

"I have no opinions due to your destruction of my original personality." Again with the jokes. I swear, this guy would make for a great deadpan comedian. Maybe if I learned to harness his wit, I could make a fortune in the stand-up districts. Worth a thought anyway-

Suddenly there was a massive explosion as Amelia erupted a dark wave out of her body. My clones stumbled back, visibly hurt and staggering in pain, but enduring wordlessly.

"I've had just about enough of this!" Amelia was throwing a tantrum again. Great. "If you want to pick on a poor little innocent girl, why not pick on someone your own size?"

I shrugged. "Tell that to the thousands of people you killed."

"Millions." She corrected me with a cold glare and a single tart word.

"Right..." Every time she spoke, I became more and more confident. I had to put an end to her. She has no compassion for anyone other than herself.

Amelia stood up straight. "You want a real fight then? Let's do this."

She held up Blaarjiim and grasped onto him with both hands. "Blaarjiim, _do the thing_."

" **My pleasure.** " A wicked demonic smile sprouted itself in my mind.
