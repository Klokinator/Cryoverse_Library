Part 079 - Unexpected

Amelia staggered to her feet. "Ah... that REALLY hurt!"

I quickly aimed my hand at her again.

**"DIE!"**

I bellowed this with all my power. Nothing happened.

She stared at me, and started slowly laughing. "Heh heh heh..." Her laughing increased in both volume and power as she started bellowing in a deep, demonic voice. "HAAAAAA HAH HAH HAH!"

She slowly calmed down and returned to 'normal', whatever that might be. "You truly are an absolute fool. How stupid do you think I am?"

I took a step back. It didn't work? Was it ineffective against her because she lacked a soul? Was there something involving her and Blaarjiim VS myself and Hoarhiim? Perhaps the command only worked on others who didn't possess the Lord Orbs?

I didn't know, but what I did know is that, for some reason, _'die' doesn't work on her_. That alone is a scary thought.

"How did that not hurt you?" I decided to try the simple approach.

"It's easy. I'm not an idiot, I'm a dark queen that even the other demons fear. I am a strategic genius. And when I found that your abilities were due to the power of wordsmithing, I simply put several hundred layers of protection against word spells that you might try and use. 'Die' was _such_ a predictable word. It's laughable that you'd even try it."

I nodded slowly. It made sense. She got me there. I don't know what words she blocked, but simple words would probably be useless against her. I'd have to do this trial-and-error.

"By the way, let's see how strong you've really gotten, oh mighty _Hero_." She spit the last word out sarcastically. I was going to retort but then she charged at me. Such frightening speed. To be honest, my speed may match or exceed hers, but without a way to focus on her as she moved, my spatial vision was mostly ineffective.

An attack from the back! And the left side! And the right!

I struggled to defend against it, as she pulled out a dark sword and cut me with it several times. She must have been testing me because the wounds weren't very deep.

Amelia jumped back, eyeing the damage. She had literally stabbed inside the chinks of my beaten armor. "That's it, huh? Worse than I expected. The power is good and the speed is okay, but fighting and being unable to track my movements? You've much to learn."

As she spoke, a thought crept in my mind. Jerking my hand out, I started to cast a spell. She appeared some feet behind me, avoiding my feet so she didn't get kicked. She's learning.

My spatial vision focused on her vision.

" **Tracking!** "

Suddenly, there she was. A bright red dot in my vision. I smiled. She didn't plan for that word.

"Huh? Nice try idiot, you missed me by a mile." She laughed as she saw my hand raised.

_Good_. An evil grin crossed my face. She doesn't know that I don't actually need to use my hands to aim. She hasn't been secretly following my development.

"Are you sure about that?" I asked, as I kept my back turned to her. "Why not try your luck... just _one more time_?"

She frowned. "Okay, but I won't go easy on you this time!"

"Don't worry about me." I said, holding a laugh inside. She has no clue.

She fell for it. Charging forwards, she stabbed at the chink in my armor, and my palm was waiting for her. SMACK! I sent her flying, but before she could hit the ground, I appeared underneath her, punching her into the back, up into the air, and then I delivered three more kicks directly into her spine. She gasped in pain and I let her smash into the ground.

"Does it hurt?" I asked, a wide grin on my face. "You keep thinking that I'm the pupil and you're the master, but I've already surpassed you. And now I will repay the debt you owe to those you consumed."

She grimaced. "You're sneaky, aren't you? You don't have to use your hands for spells, eh? I won't let you do that again." Her eyes burned with fire, as she once again unhinged her mouth. But this time, a massive swarm of flies erupted out of her from within her gut.

All right, that is officially the most disgusting thing I've ever seen.
