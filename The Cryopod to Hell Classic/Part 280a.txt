Part 280a

I knew this was going to happen. Damn my luck!

As I walked, I felt drops of water dripping downwards, as the sky bared its fangs at me, dumping a light torrential rain over my head. Fantastic. This is what happens when you live like mongrels in the middle of the 21st century and don't have weather control towers!

Standing under an open doorway as a massive blast of rain falls from the sky, I sigh in defeat. I've been at this for hours, and I haven't got the slightest whiff of her trail.

Glancing behind me to the doorway I had rushed under, I notice it's actually an antique shop of some sort. 'Will's Weird, Wacky, and Worn Heirlooms', huh? What's up with all these stupidly convoluted names? All the people around here act like they don't want to meet strangers, but then they name their buildings such weird names that you want to have a peek, just to see if the carpet matches the drapes.

Pausing hesitantly, I finally gather the courage to step inside. The place is dimly lit, apparently by old incandescent bulbs from the early 2000's. Man, talk about living like a caveman in a modern city. They must really hate technology.

"H-hello?" I almost cough as I speak, the dust is so thick in the air here. Everything is covered in it, clearly not a place that people visit very often.

Walking around, I glance here and there at all the... _crap_ that's littered about. I dunno, 'antique' just isn't a word you throw around. Valuable china pots are antique, things created in limited quantities are antique... but random pots and pans are decidedly _not_ antique. I spot a Mickey Mouse mug with a chipped bottom piece, with a hole you could stick a straw through. Too bad, that might actually be valuable were it not for the functional and cosmetic damage.

Walking past an entire wall of grandfather clocks (I don't know why, but every antique shop always has clocks for some reason), my nose begins to detect a whiff of... something. It smells like iron, maybe? Someone certainly has not been cleaning up around here in a long time. It's probably rust or maybe rotted food.

As I sneak my way past all manner of other assorted junk, including a bunch of fake jewelry, I wrinkle my nose as the smell becomes stronger. There's a door closed to the back room. "Employees Only", it says. Employees? Plural? More than one person ever worked here?

I knock hesitantly at the door, but nobody answers. Waiting for several long seconds, my stomach is twisting in knots. I'm undoubtedly breaking several laws by being here right now, and opening this door adds several more. Oh well, are they really going to prosecute the savior of humanity for a little crime like trespassing, or breaking and entering? Get real.

I reach for the door handle and rotate it, but it just spins forever in my hand. Seems to be broken. Huh. Pushing on it lightly, the door loudly creaks open, which makes the otherwise silent room aside from the pitter-pattering of rain feel even creepier than it did before.

As the door opens, a wave of nauseating smells slams into me, blasting from out of the room as I detect a quiet but definitely audible buzzing noise. My eyes squeeze shut as the smell smashes into me, but I quickly use my wordsmithing, not giving a damn if anyone hears. " **Purify!** "

Instantly the smell vanishes, but the memory of it lingers in my mind... and my mouth.

Finally I open my eyes and almost yelp in horror. There's a body in the room, and an old man. I shouldn't say 'body', as it's more like... large chunks of him... thrown all over the place. It's like if a massive crocodile grabbed hold of someone and shook them around so violently that pieces ripped off and went flying. Actually, that's _exactly_ what it looks like. What the hell is this?!

" **Inspect.** "

An array of data enters my mind. Well, this is Will, the guy who ran this place. Analysis shows that he died at the ripe old age of 85 years old. But his body has been decomposing in this room for... five entire months?! Disgusting.

His body is melted into the ground in several places, and the entire room is covered in rust-colored blood. Whatever killed him, it certainly had his way.

Then, I glance more carefully at the data. It seems there are... traces of magic? Whatever killed him, it used demonic magic of a sort. So... it was probably a demon... or worse. " **Killer?** "

Speaking a question into the wind, I decide to ask the wordsmithing gods, assuming they exist. If it doesn't work, no harm in trying.

It works.

An image of the killer's face appears in my mind. That is, without a doubt, Amelia. She was here. She did this.

It's hard to believe that I found this murder on my first day of being here in Paris, but this place was out of the way. Were it not for the rain, I probably wouldn't have even stopped in here to look around. Seems nobody else did, either. I wonder if there are any other bodies in these slums like this? Are there others the police haven't found?

Walking back outside, I use wordsmithing to clean up evidence of myself being here. After phoning in the crime to the local police, I quickly leave the premises so they can come in and clean up. I wonder if the old guy had any family or friends? If he did, they'd be horrified to see what happened to him.

It's just... unforgivable. If Amelia really did do this, then she's every bit the monster I knew in the future. Just how long has she lived? How strong is she? How _evil_ is she?

I need to find her and speak to her.

...................................................................

Harold pops into my mind later that night, as I'm staying in a nice 3-star hotel on the west side of Paris. I'd prefer a five star, but I don't want to attract any attention, and three stars are plenty comfortable for one night.

"Yo, kid! How are ya holdin' up?"

I shrug slightly, as I ponder the question. "Not sure. I found a body today... some poor old codger was offed by the girl I'm looking for."

Harold nods slowly. "Mmm. Mmm. Yeap, you gotta watch out fer them gold diggers! I tell ya what, you find yourself a nice wife like mah Helen, you'll be one lucky man! Anyways, I was jes' callin' ya up to let ya know that Beatrix called and wanted to talk to ya about help with the moon-base expansion project. If ya can give her a call, I'm sure she'd appreciate it."

I smile at him. "Sure thing, Harold. How are you and Helen doing? Enjoying the time together?"

"Yeap, as always, we're doing what we do best; spendin' lots o' time with each other to talk about life and the universe. You know how it is. And if ya don't know, well, maybe someday ya will!"

I nod quietly as we say our goodbyes. As Harold signs off, I'm left with the thought that he brought up... I miss Cassiel, and seeing Samantha ripped my heart in half. To think that she's with an enemy of mine doesn't make me feel very good. I just want to say 'screw the timeline' and whisk her away, but she's far too attached to him. Then, if I kill Satan, she'll surely blame and hate me forever for it. Then again, maybe she'd get turned on or something... women are complicated, especially when they're Succubi.

Oh well. It's sleep time. I'll just have to think about it in the morning.

As I climb into bed and immediately feel the effects of sleep grabbing hold of me, I once again hear a familiar voice speaking to me... one that sounds like...

...................................................................

"Look at that. You're back for round two." A familiar man stands in the distance, a sword held firmly in his hand, his body garbed in chainmail, as he wears a crown I've seen before, in a previous dream.

As he takes a swing at the tree, I note that the blade chops cleanly a few inches into the trunk and swipes through as if it were made of paper. "Your name is Arthur, right? King of the Britons?"

He rolls his eyes as he stabs at the tree, pulling the sword out a split second later with little effort. "You're a smart cookie. You know, I entered your mind years ago when you first took Excalibur from Lady Brittania, yet you never once thought to talk to me. Yet here you are again."

I shrug as I walk closer to him. I can't help but notice the sword he's swinging around is none other than Excalibur itself. "I dunno. I didn't exactly _want_ to speak with you, it just kind of happened the first time. And now this time too, I guess."

He returned the shrug as he sheathed his sword and turned to face me. "Well, you're here now. That's what matters. Have you come to seek my counsel on how to properly wield this mighty, legendary blade?"

I stare at him for a moment with my mouth going slightly slack before I finally respond. "Uhh, no... not exactly. I didn't have any questions for you at all, to be honest."

"Oh. I... see." Arthur returns the dumbfounded stare as he quickly turns away and walks over to the tree, laying his back against it. "W-well, I mean, since you're here, I suppose if you err, wanted to... you know, if that was a thing you wanted to do... I could maybe show you how to use it...?"

His cheeks flared up a little and I realized that he was feeling embarrassed. Of course, he was a legendary hero whose exploits were told in children's tales, and if he was a literal Hero, then it's possible his legends were actually _weakened_ over time, since most probably thought they were just silly children's stories blown up to sound exciting.

"Actually, rather than that, how about we discuss your life? I'd like to know more about you and your exploits as a Hero." Deciding not to let the moment go to waste, I felt the need to pry into his personal life. If he told me 'no' then no big deal, sword training would be nice too.

"Well... I mean, if you've heard the stories, you've heard all you need to know." Arthur held his palms out with the flats facing up to show there wasn't much to talk about. "I slayed the Saxons, built a kingdom, killed a hundred million demons, and married a queen from a Roman country. No big deal I guess."

"You killed a hundred million demons?" There was a small grin plastered on his face, but it disappeared when I brought that up.

"Well... yes. Probably more, actually. It was hard to count accurately, considering their bodies were totally obliterated in an instant. However, to say that it was a total rout would be... inaccurate."

He shuffled uneasily as he spoke. Something about this seemed like it was less of an epic conquest and more of a major mistake he deeply regretted. "I mean, I don't know much about you, Jason, only what you've seen when you grasp Excalibur firmly in your hands, but even you know a little about Excalibur's signature power."

I nod slowly. "The beam of light? The one that I used to destroy a Volgrim Battlecruiser with?"

He nodded quietly. "Yes... that's the one. But the thing is, that beam of light is a double edged sword, literally. I should preface this by informing you once more that I was, by far, the most powerful Hero who ever lived, at least in terms of raw spiritual energy."

I cock an eyebrow. "Really? The most powerful by sheer quantity then? Just how much exactly did you have?"

He chuckles a little bit. "That's amusing. I still _have_ that energy. I'm not actually dead, Jason. I never died."

"What? But you told me you died an old man on your bed. How could I have the Hero's power if you didn't die and pass it on to the next in line?" I was dumbfounded. It would certainly make sense that he wasn't actually dead, given that I was speaking to him now. But... how was it possible?

"That's the thing, Jason. I still am the most powerful Hero. Far stronger than even you, as you are now. My Heroic gift given to me was monstrous levels of energy, the ability to detect energy, and the ability to predict energy. Combined with Excalibur's ability to fire off blasts of its wielder's energy, as well as healing them if they died, I was beyond unstoppable when it came to combat. Beyond that, I was forced into a circumstance later on in life that prevented me from continuing to live normally. Since I was going to die, I simply gave up the actual presence of the Heroic Aura, and it passed on to the next one when I sealed my soul inside of Excalibur, leaving it to Lady Britannia to keep hold of. In the wrong hands, Excalibur could destroy the entire planet. It is no child's toy."

His long speech left me with many questions, but already I was filled with wonder. "So, then, if you're here in this sword, I bet I could just manifest you physically outside of it! I can already think of the words required! You would surely be helpful to have as an ally against the Volg-"

Arthur cut me off with a vicious glare, causing me to stop mid-sentence. "I think not, Jason. Don't ever presume to take my life into your own hands. I do not deserve to be out there, in the physical world. After... after what I did... I..."

Arthur paused for a moment as tears welled up in his eyes. Turning his head away so I couldn't see, he wiped them away quickly. His voice was choked when he finally regained his composure a moment later. "...All that power... and in the end, I was no Hero. I killed them all, you know. Not just the demons, but the humans too. Excalibur's blast leveled an entire mountain, causing a landslide that killed 100,000 humans. And in the end, they still called me a hero. Wrote stories, and plays about me... just... rubbish. Garbage. I don't deserve the title."

He bit his lip bitterly as he gazed off into the distance. I stayed silent for a few moments to allow him to clear his mind, before I replied. "I understand. I... I didn't realize something like that had happened."

"Yeah... nobody saw it coming. The power of the Hero had been growing exponentially, and after what happened with me, the Gods decided to severely limit it for future generations, for fear it would grow out of control. The Heroic Aura manifests itself the most in times of need, you know. If I've learned anything from my life... it's that there are no heroes and villains, only greedy people on one side or another looking to save their own skin, serve their own interests."

I paused thoughtfully as he spoke. "So, if that's the case, what about my powers? I don't know what powers many past heroes had, but mine seem especially strong and useful, almost overpowered to a certain extent."

He shrugged. "I'm not sure what your abilities are. I only know what I've seen when you held Excalibur in your hands. You'll have to explain your abilities to me first."

I hesitated, given that I didn't know if I could trust him with the information or not. Finally, I decided to simply explain it.

A few minutes later, he nodded thoughtfully. "Wordsmithing, hm? Yes, that does sound especially useful. Certainly more robust than previous heroes, including myself. The ability to create and heal, in my opinion, is far more important than the ability to kill and maim. With this power, you could do real good to help the world, even the universe."

I snicker slightly. "So, what, I'm like a god, right? This power is pretty ridiculous, right?"

He sighed slowly. "Well... perhaps it is. Frankly, I see too many openings to even consider it good. For one thing, if your mouth is ever maimed, your powers are useless. If someone silences you, they're useless. If someone sucks the air out of your body, they're useless. If you get dunked underwater, they're useless. It's certainly a useful ability, but it's not impregnable. Not to mention your body is quite frail. Against a demon, like Diablo, or Bael, or Satan, they would crush you into pulp with one punch. And, as you said, they have ways of shielding themselves from your kill-words. Don't get a big head when you're easily destroyed by such powerful adversaries. Even Satan was... too much for me."

"Satan is strong, but he's not the one I'm worried about. It's Amelia who I'm having trouble with."

I toss her name out nonchalantly, but Arthur's face changes in an instant, as a look of total horror spreads over his face. "T-that name... what was that name again?"

I raise an eyebrow at him, noting his posture has changed and he now stands defensively, as if to prepare for an attack from an unseen foe. "Uh... Amelia. She's really tough. I only managed to kill her in the future thanks to the help from a dear friend of mine."

He swallowed carefully and collected himself with a single, long deep breath. "You can only be referring to Amelia Greyheart. I will not forget that name for as long as I live. I discovered her identity in some old Roman records when I defended a village on the Egyptian coast to kill some raiding Saxons. After that, she _came_ to me in Camelot, and announced her presence. She somehow evaded all of my best security like it was nothing, something even the demons couldn't do."

He paused quietly as he wiped his forehead with his hand. "...Suffice to say, I did not kill her. She was monstrous. She broke my body down in ways that would horrify you, and she left me to die in my own land. I only survived thanks to Excalibur's healing powers, something she luckily did not take notice of. I would advise staying far, far away from her."

The terror on his face was palpable. I licked my dried lips carefully as I felt my own throat was cracking. "I believe it. However, this is non-negotiable. She is brutally killing innocent people, in horrifying ways. Devouring their bodies. I can't let her continue to terrorize the world from the shadows. Even the demons don't seem to know about her."

Arthur nodded quietly. "Yes, and it needs to stay that way. Trust me, Jason, if you chase after her, only agony and torture await. She will kill you in the most gruesome manner possible. She will maim you so badly that even in the afterlife the nightmares will haunt you. Don't make the same mistake I did. If you see her... _flee_."

I nodded quietly, as I noticed that the world around me seemed to be fading.

"Well, seems our time is up. Perhaps we'll talk again in the near future. Make sure you show up, Jason, or you won't get to learn about Excalibur's secret abilities." Arthur smiled a friendly grin as he wandered away from me, the world dissolving into a veil of light...
