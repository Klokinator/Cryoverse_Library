Part 388b:

It's not that what I'm reading is too horrifying to comprehend, I simply just don't know how to react to the words on the page. Accounts of humans capturing demons, butchering them in brutal, vile ways, and even performing barbaric chimeric surgeries before releasing demons back into the demon's realm to traumatize them... the journal of history is filled with dozens, if not hundreds of gruesome reports about humans treatment of the demons.

Yama watches me carefully as I flip through the pages of the dossier, finally speaking up once I have quickly scanned many of the grainy pictures. "You're not the only one who can use Wordsmithing."

I wait for him to continue, but he doesn't. I slowly set the faded folder down and slide it slightly away from me. I can't be bothered to eat any of the food anymore... I've simply lost my appetite. "Alright. So if I'm not the only Wordsmith out there, then that means...?"

Beelzebub harumphs and coughs audibly into his clenched fist. "The humans have an army of enhanced super soldiers. They're unstoppable in combat. We don't know who the first wordsmith is exactly, but we suspect he or she has given the power to many others. Wordsmithing has many practical applications, and is a special means of going beyond the laws of physics, as you might know them."

My hands somehow end up folded in my lap, though I don't know when I took my hands off the folder. Frankly, I'm just glad not to be looking at it anymore. "So why do I have this power, then? What makes me so important to you? Why not just take the power for yourself?"

Blaarjiim enters my mind. "It doesn't work like that. We're not sure how the original human did it, but he created an army of wordsmiths using his ability, and he's been using it to hunt us down and exterminate us. If demons could simply take the ability, we'd have done so by now." Blaarjiim gestures at Bael. "The first emperor, for example, is powerful enough that he might be able to capture a wordsmith by himself. As for you... well..."

Yama cuts him off. "You were part of a reserve unit. Apparently, the monster that terrorized our kind placed several humans he imbued with the power inside of stasis pods as a secret auxiliary force. We managed to take you, but we had no idea how to activate the unlock feature for the pod."

"No idea? That is hard to believe. It's a matter of pressing buttons."

"Mmm. Demons are terrible when it comes to understanding human technology. Assume that we simply 'pressed buttons,' what might have happened? We could have drained the fluid improperly and killed you. We might have shut down the power wrong. If we had a human who was willing to help us, we probably could have taken you out but... such a human was never in play."

I lean back in my chair and scratch my head. This whole thing sounds very fishy. It's as if they planned their story all out in a way that would make some logical sense to me, but... there are holes in the plot. "I wasn't part of an auxiliary unit. I entered the cryopod willingly. I remember doing it because I was... well... I was depressed. I uh..." My voice falters for a moment as I feel a sudden rush of emotion. Yeah, that's right... I put myself in the cryopod because I didn't want to live anymore. I thought by donating my body to science I could perhaps improve the world around me.

Huh? What a joke. That's not me at all. I wouldn't do something so pathetic. Why did I really put myself in the cryopod?

The demons at the table evaluate me carefully, and it's at this moment that I realize... they're lying to me. They're lying about something big. I definitely wasn't part of some secret fighting force. Maybe my memories were altered or something, but that's unlikely as hell.

Why are these demons lying? What do they have to gain? Everything, now that I think about it. If I possess some crazy power that allows me to manipulate the universe with a single word, then I'm a living nuclear bomb. The demons aren't lying about being crushed by humanity; I can tell that much already. But as things are going now, the demons are probably going to ask me to give them my power, or copy it over or something.

As if. I don't trust anyone sitting at this table. They're using me.

However, I can just play along. I'll learn how my abilities work, and give them small favors here and there. Maybe I'll end up running the place in due time.

"A question, Yama. Why didn't you just capture a human scientist or two? If getting me out of the cryopod was your best hope, I'd expect you to have put a little more effort into the task. I mean, you have human slaves running around all over the place here."

Yama shakes his head slowly. "Your kind is crafty and aided by many brilliant minds. Their technology far outstrips our magical powers. Besides, these slaves are several generations past. They hardly bear any resemblance to the humans that exist outside of the labyrinth. All human scientists have small bombs planted in their brains just in case a demon were to capture them."

For once, Yama says something that surprises me. "Bombs in their heads? That's ridiculous. Why would they go along with such a thing?"

Blaarjiim interjects. "The reason, Jason Hiro, is that they must. Humans are led by a few undying never-aging immortals... plus the leaders of our worst enemies... the angels, as well as the Volgrim."

I involuntarily take a deep breath and exhale through my nose. "Angels. Volgrim. What the fuck is going on, even? I can guess what angels are, but Volgrim?"

"They are... ah... aliens." Beelzebub looks slightly flustered as he says this. "You likely come from a time before their arrival, but indeed aliens exist. They are extremely dangerous as well. Their technology might just exceed that of humanity's."

This is a lot to digest. I finally reach forward and poke at a strawberry with my finger, picking it up and ripping out the leafy stem before tossing the red fruit back in my mouth. Its softness and juicy texture are quite delicious, possibly the most normal thing I've tasted so far.

"Humans... Demons... Angels... Aliens... what's next? Necromancers, goblins, witches, giant fighting robots, and Godzilla?"

The demons at the table look back and forth at each other seriously for a moment or two before looking back to me. Yama speaks up. "Actually..."

"I don't even want to know." I cut him off quickly. "So let's get to it. You want me to use my powers to fight this war for you?"

" _With_ us. You don't need to do everything by yourself." Yama beckons toward Beelzebub. "The third emperor will assist you in learning how your powers work."

Beelzebub's faint smile immediately crumples as he shoots an angry glare at Yama. "I beg your pardon? I don't recall signing on for such a task. I will not be a babysitter for a... _fleshbag_."

I first want to agree with him, but the insult makes me angry. If he hates humans so much, then I'll get a good chuckle out of ruining his day. "Beelzy, I think Yama has a good point. We could make a good team, you and me."

His crimson eyes light up with a most amusing fiery rage, and he rises slightly in his chair as if he wants to jump across the table and throttle me. "By the seven devils! I won't allow you to refer to me in such a disrespectful way!"

Yama, for once, shows a hint of a smile. "Now now, Third, you insulted him first. You deserved that."

Bael and Blaarjiim both nod along with Yama, and I quickly realize they don't particularly like the prissy, snooty jackwagon sitting across from me.

Beelzy slowly closes his eyes and breathes deeply before sitting back down. "Fine." He hisses the word out between his teeth.

I'm going to enjoy torturing this poor soul.

Yama stands up in his chair abruptly, his comically tiny self now towering over everyone at the table. "Well then, that should be the end of it. Third Emperor Beelzebub will assist you in using your powers, you will train to help us against the humans, and if you need anything, we are here to assist you."

_Yes you are._ I smile inwardly to myself and stifle an urge to lick my lips. I've hardly known demons for a day, and already I see how simple-minded they are. It's no wonder the genius of humanity has been able to pacify them to this extent. They couldn't even figure out how to unlock a cryopod.

I stand up too, popping my back and cricking my neck. "That will do then. Will I be able to get some sleep tonight?"

"Hmm. Right." Yama murmurs to himself, showing that he had once again forgotten about my human foibles. "Demons do not require sleep, so I forgot. My apologies. I will have the chattel clear out one of the- ah, no, those rooms are rather... grimy. I will have my assistant furnish something for you at once."

He nods at me and then shifts from the chair to the ground in the blink of an eye, moving as one with the shadows in the room. The room is hardly even illuminated by the candles all around the place, so the effect is rather... creepy.

"Just show me to an empty space. I'd like to try out this wordsmithing for myself."

Yama quickly turns from me to Beelzebub, shrugs, then looks back. "Do as you please, human."

He makes a quick motion with his hands, and a moment later Hellga limps into the room, looking disgusting as ever. "What be ye needin', Lord Yama?"

....................................................

Several minutes of walking pass, as Hellga leads me back down those halls I had traversed earlier for my bath. This time, the path takes several rights and lefts, and it doesn't take long for me to give up trying to remember the way back.

"Just tell the serfs to lead ya back, it's all they're good for." Hellga grumbles under her breath when I inquire about the path, but she shoots an evil smile at me afterward. "Hehe, well, that ain't _all_ they're good for. Human male like you will understand, I'm sure."

Naturally I grasp what she's talking about. The idea isn't repulsive to me, but it's simply not something I'm interested in. Taking someone who doesn't resist is not nearly as much fun as making a game of it or winning their affection.

Odd. I don't remember thinking like that before I entered the cryopod. I wonder why my personality feels so different now? Maybe the ice messed with my brain. Yeah, that's probably it.

Finally, we arrive at a rather large, plain looking doorway. It's a room not unlike that of the women's rooms all around me, but it's substantially bigger and less cramped, plus it's empty.

"I'll make sure Sheena gets you bedding, boy. This is last minute notice, after all."

"No, that's fine. I won't be needing their assistance." I smile politely at Hellga, even though the action repulses me. She is simply the most hideous looking thing I've ever seen, human or demon. In fact, every demon I've encountered so far has been quite good looking. Demons seem to pride themselves on their looks and status. I wonder why she is the exception?

I turn my back to her and think carefully about how I killed Gressil earlier today. Was it this morning? A few hours ago? Without the sun or a clock to be seen, time is hard to keep track of. The darkness seems to be stunting my biological clock too.

Let's see... it was a single word... something that conveys the meaning of what I want...

" **Bed.** "

I speak the word and feel a strange tingling in my body. An instant later, something vaguely resembling a bed appears in front of me instantly, startling me and making me take a step back. Even Hellga is surprised.

"Ye made it pop out of nowhere. Strange magic, that is."

I steal a glance at her face behind me. She's surprised by my magic trick, but what interests me is that despite what Yama spoke about a few minutes ago, she doesn't know what Wordsmithing is. If she's a well known and respected demoness, why doesn't she know about Wordsmithing? Wouldn't such devastating magic be well known to all demons?

Something doesn't add up here. Something is rotten in this labyrinth.

Hellga finally shakes her head and walks away, mumbling incoherently under her breath. Suits me fine, she's rather annoying to be around.

I glance back at the bed I've made. It's rather awful, misshapen, and nothing like what a bed should resemble. It slopes down at the angle closest to me, meaning the legs closest to me are shorter than the other two, and it's hardly more than a wooden frame. Mattress not included.

I try again. " **Bed.** " Instantly, another just like the one in front of me appears in midair and falls down abruptly with a loud _bang_ that nearly makes me jump out of my skin.

Right, I wanted just to edit the existing one, not create another. This is a bit confusing. " **Delete.** "

I somehow feel as if I'm aiming my mind at the bed on top, and sure enough, it pops out of existence, disappearing into the nether. A thought crosses my mind. _Could this be a way I might kill someone? Do different words lead to different killing methods?_

I file that thought under _maybe_ and resume work. This time, I don't summon another bed. Instead, I imagine the bed's legs raising up a few inches. " **Alter.** " Sure enough, the bed adjusts itself and now sits perfectly level.

So I can create things, remove them, and alter them. Those alone have... an astounding number of uses.

I quickly create a soft mattress, one that defies the normal limits of what comfort entails, and sit down gently on the bed. It's unbelievably soft, as if the moment I lay my head on my pillow, I'll be sleeping in an angel's arms.

I stare out the doorway. Now that things have quieted down a lot, I can hear the distant sounds of collective breathing, all around me. I passed many rooms on my way here, and it seemed to me that about half the girls sleep while the other half work, and they seem to rotate out at regular intervals.

" **Barrier.** " I create a soundproof invisible wall at my doorway, and instantly the sounds in the distance are cut off, and my room is weirdly silent. Still, I prefer hearing just my thoughts. Other people can be extremely annoying.

Time to play around a bit. " **Fire.** " I imagine a small flame appearing in midair before me, and sure enough, it appears. " **Sword. Shirt. Gun.** "

To my surprise, the gun appears in my hand, but it's even more misshapen than the bed initially was. Every piece of it is stuck together, and I quickly realize that I don't know what the inside of a gun looks like, only the exterior. It seems my abilities are limited by my imagination, but thus they only have that _singular_ limit.

Well, I can't assume that much. There might be other walls I'll run into. Yama and the other demons were cryptic, to say the least. I have the feeling they're going to try and limit my growth.

I sit on the bed for a few minutes, just staring at the gun, before I finally shrug and lay down. I'll worry about it in the morning... or whenever I wake up. I guess 'morning' doesn't really exist in this place.

....................................................

I need to piss.

My eyes jerk open, and I sit up in bed. I feel like I slept for a week, and my body is so rested that I don't even want to think about sleep anymore. In fact, sleep repulses me.

But more importantly, I need to piss. Really bad.

I quickly get off my bed and walk through my soundproof barrier, where the faint sounds all around me become audible once again. Where do they hide the bathrooms in these catacombs? Why are the hallways so meandering? Where is the dining room where I met the other demons?

Should I just wordsmith the piss out of myself? Tempting. I don't want to kill myself or something though accidentally, and I don't want to piss in a corner either. People live here, after all.

As I have these thoughts, I suddenly feel a presence beside me. Glancing to my left, I'm surprised to see a familiar figure standing a couple of feet away, and she isn't saying a single word.

Lora, as always, has her head downcast toward the ground, and it bothers me, as I was always taught by the orphanage adults to look people in the eye when I spoke. I guess the same isn't true for human cattle, as the demons seem to treat these girls.

"Lora. Is there a... um... bathroom here?"

I feel slightly embarrassed to ask, but she simply pushes past me and begins walking quickly away, so I shrug and follow along. She seems to understand speech perfectly well, so she isn't stupid... she simply can't talk.

Frankly, the thought disgusts me. I remember a quote I read once. _If you cut out a man's tongue, you have not proven him a liar, but only that you fear what he has to say._ It's not really the same thing since it seems the demons just don't like to hear humans talking, as if our very voices are an irritant. Still, it bothers me a lot.

Lora leads me down just a few short corridors before she finally stops in front of a very primitive looking toilet, really more of a latrine than what I'm used to. Unsurprisingly, it's designed for women, but it will have to do.

She continues to stand by the open doorway as I enter, and I realize she doesn't quite understand the concept of privacy. "Would you mind waiting around the corner?"

She walks away without acknowledging my words, and I relieve myself, sighing contentedly.

I wordsmith _water_ to create a bubble of liquid in the air, wash my hands, and then make it disappear a moment later. Thinking back on it, if I'd used my head, I could have just created a toilet in my room. I'll have to remember that for the future.

I step out of the bathroom and spot Lora standing just around the corner a few dozen feet away, and wonder to myself several things all at once. Perhaps she can give me answers that others cannot, even if she can't speak.

I walk up to her and she stiffens noticeably as I draw closer. It unnerves me, as if she's scared of me. Probably a wise idea, if I'm being honest. I'm a killer, after all.

"Lora, do you understand me?"

I ask the question, and it takes several seconds for her to shift her feet. She doesn't give any indication she heard me, and instead keeps her head bowed to the ground.

"Just nod if you can, or shake your head if you cannot."

I hardly give her the instruction and she immediately nods her head. It's as if she won't do anything unless expressly allowed to do so.

"Would you be okay with looking me in the eye when I speak to you? I'm not like the demons."

Her hands quickly clasp together and she fidgets slightly. Just as I'm about to ask again, she slowly raises her head up to look at me.

I can't help but flinch. Her face is scarred, badly. It looks as if she was burned with several red-hot torture tools several times. Scar tissue is built up over other scar tissue. It looks inhumanly painful. The scars are years old by now, but whatever caused her the injuries to her face, it must have been horrible.

She must see the look of disgust in my eyes and quickly lowers her head again, and is now noticeably more uncomfortable, as she slightly pulls away from me.

I feel like an asshole.

"Sorry, I didn't realize... I mean, that is..." I quickly feel awkward, and then in the blink of an eye, an idea pops into my head. It's so obvious, but it's worth a try.

I reach over slowly, so as to not startle her, and carefully lift her chin up so she has to look at me again. The scars are old, and have been a part of her face for years now... but I can change that.

" **Heal. Repair.** " I speak two words, one after the other, and marvel to myself as the skin on her face softens and reforms, the valleys and lumps of mottled skin across her face smooth out and a transformation occurs. In seconds, she goes from a deformed young woman to something much more beautiful.

Then I do something else. " **Regenerate.** "

This time, a look of absolute shock passes over her face as she stumbles away from me and falls to the ground, grasping her throat and coughing weakly. I take a few steps forwards and kneel down to her level, worrying that I did something wrong. A few seconds later her weak coughs begin to sound more audible, as her vocal cords stitch back together, along with her tongue. She stares at me in confusion before quickly jamming a hand in her mouth to touch the weird organ that wasn't there before.

"Can you speak now?" I force a smile as I look at her, and once she pulls her hand out of her mouth, she gives a valiant effort.

"Wrghh nsh"

My forced smile turns a little genuine as I try not to laugh. It's quite a serious thing and probably fills her with joy to have her body fixed up, but the way she speaks is just funny. Though, I don't know how long she hasn't had a tongue. If it was since infancy or young childhood, she never had any ability to speak and would take years of practice to learn.

At the least, it might make her eat food more comfortably.

I smile and stand up, trying not to break my arm patting myself on the back. Only then does a thought occur to me.

_What if giving her back a face and a tongue is a bad thing?_

If Yama doesn't like that I did this, then he might cut out her tongue and scar her face all over again. She would suffer for the second time in her life, and it would be all because of me.

Well, honestly, I don't care, I suppose, but yet I also do. This feeling is confusing.

I should probably take responsibility for what I've done.

She stands up and stares at me with a strange look in her eyes, almost something like that of awe.

My smile vanishes as I look at her. "You're mine now. I gave you back your face and tongue. I will tell Yama not to lay a hand on you. From now on, you do as I say."

She nods along, and her expression of awe fades as she too begins to realize the true repercussions of my actions. Soon, her expression is that of unease and wariness.

The look on her face is perfect. In this place, no-one is your ally. Everyone is your enemy. You have to look out for yourself. Already, I've come to realize that.

"Take me to Yama. I need to speak with him."
