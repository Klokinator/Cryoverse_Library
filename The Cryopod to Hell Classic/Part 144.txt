Part 144

Ahh... this is awesome. I never got to live in the future, but now I am bringing the future to _me_!

While everyone else gasped and ooh'd and ahh'd, Samantha, the sneaky little _devil_, popped up alongside me and slipped her arm around mine again, even going so far as to clasp her hand inside mine.

"Oooh honey, you're so good with _words_, you're making me shiver with anticipation!"

I sighed.

Then Cassiel grabbed my other arm _because of course she did_. This is... well it's actually kind of great and yet awful. I've got things to _do_, ladies.

"Get your hands off him! He's- He's mine!" Cassiel said something that made me instantly blush. Shit, when did she get so daring?

Samantha giggled. "Aww, that's so cute. Angel's got her first c-r-u-s-h~!" Cassiel fumed at those words. "But you know, I'm not necessarily a one-guy-one-girl kind of gal, you know? I don't mind sharing~!"

Jesus Christ, the heavens need to protect me. I'm trying to do work and two girls are fighting over me. I seriously don't know whether to be delighted or annoyed.

While the two pressed their bodies against me, and begin to say all manner of embarrassing things, Cody cleared his throat. " _Ahem_, Samantha, that is not conduct becoming of a soldier."

Samantha turned around and glowered at him, sticking her tongue out. "Nyaah. I do what I want, I am my own woman you know."

I decided I'd had about enough of this. "Samantha, you're very lovely, but could we do this another time? This is important."

Samantha frowned as she slowly and very hesitantly pulled herself away. "Aww... well, I know you're just _shy_ so I'll come visit you sometime, mkay?"

This girl... Cassiel, seeing the succubus had let go, she walked over to her and pulled her away. When they were out of earshot, they started speaking to each other, but I couldn't tell about what exactly. I could read their lips if I wanted to, but I decided against it. I don't have a death wish.

I raised my hands again and aimed them at the Replicator. " **Clone!** "

Unlike before, when I had tested with Adams, I was pretty sure that cloning machinery would be easy, and unsurprisingly, it was. I cloned the replicator three more times, for a total of 5 of them.

"Listen up, Cody. These replicators are insanely valuable. They can create any form of non-biological matter, so long as you have the blueprints for it, and enough energy for it. Unfortunately, energy is the biggest problem you will have. Fortunately, I'm here for that.

I aimed my mind at my own body again. " _Suit!_ "

Around me, a spacesuit appeared. I wore this because... I had to go to the surface. I waved at Samantha and Cassiel. "I'll be right back."

Cassiel saw me in the strange suit, and I saw a look of concern pass over her face. But just as it came, it disappeared. And then I cast another spell.

" _Teleport!_ "

Instantly, I was on the surface. The sun seemed close enough to give energy, but since the people of this planet are way deep inside it, I decided to... ahem... _increase the output_.

I aimed my hands up at the sun and concentrated deeply.

" **Enlarge!** "

Slowly, after a few minutes, the sun became 50% bigger, its size now massive compared to the sun on Earth. On top of that, the heat became quite intense. It felt like it was easily 200 degrees on the surface, extremely dangerous even for my powerful body.

I aimed my mental image a few hundred feet underground. " _Hollow!_ "

And empty space appeared, about 20 feet in diameter and roughly the size of a small rounded apartment. " _Teleport!_ "

I appeared inside the hollow spot, the space still quite cool as the heat hadn't yet gotten down this deep. Using my mental imaging, I started casting more spells.

"Panel!" A solar panel appeared, as I had imagined in my mind. " **Resist! Resist! Resist!** "

I made it heat resistant, to the point where heat wouldn't negatively affect it at all.

And then, for the next three hours, I cloned those panels. Over and over and over again. More than 25,000 times.

Energy for the replicator would soon be an issue of the past.
