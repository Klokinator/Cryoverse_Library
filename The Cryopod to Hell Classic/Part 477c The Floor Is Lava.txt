Part 477c: The Floor Is Lava

With a single word, I transport the five of us a dozen miles across the open plains, and we teleport a few hundred yards above the area Tvoorik pointed out from before. The instant we appear, everyone takes a defensive stance, and we put our backs to each other, whipping our heads around to search for threats. Now that we're much closer to the city, the smell of burning flesh and crumbling infrastructure force our instincts to sharpen. Something about the danger in the air forces the human, and apparently the angel and demon body to tense up.

The land below us, once covered by blood-red brambles, is now torn apart and ripped up. Fresh dirt covers everything, leading to a gross brown surface in every direction. If the Kolvaxians were walking around, they'd stick out like a sore thumb.

Tvoorik points again, this time directly below us. "There's a door. It leads to a hangar big enough to fit a small spacecraft. The scientists barricaded the entrance and are holed up inside, weapons at the ready. Their chances of survival are zero."

"Without us, at least," I reply. " **Structure.** " Using my Wordsmithing, I quickly create a mental picture of the internal structure, sending the information to the Crown to hold, and allowing it to create a new mental map for me. "There's a long corridor just inside- looks like the scientists have set up some auto-turrets. They use anti-energy in addition to plasma, so Cassiel's shield probably won't hold up. I'll teleport us where the majority of them have hunkered down. Cass, create your shield before we jump, just in case they freak out and start blasting."

Cassiel smiles tightly. "Right." She holds her hand up, and a balloon of white energy surrounds us, making us stand out in the sky, but no Kolvaxians appear. I'm worried their invisible arms or whatever will grab Cass again, though at the same time I keep wondering why they're hiding. Are they burrowing underground? Why the secrecy?

A passage from a book I once read comes to mind. _Secret operations are essential in war._ One significant advantage the Kolvaxians have is their secrecy. Even Helen, who has watched Jason Alpha fight them for a trillion years (or however long it's been) doesn't know how they operate. Nobody knows where they come from, or what their goal is. Total dominance? Peace? If we don't understand them, we can't fight them.

Maybe that's their action plan.

Cassiel shivers as a gust of chilly upper atmospheric air hits us, bringing the smell of death with it from below. "Shield's ready whenever you are, Jason!"

I aim my mind inside the compound. " **Teleport!** "

All five of us vanish and reappear, and it takes a few seconds to reorient ourselves once again. This time, I absorb the faces of hundreds of Volgrim pivoting to stare at us in shock, and their reflexes are put to the test once they realize intruders are in their midst. Before I can say "We come in peace," a hail of plasma smashes into Cassiel's force field, though I breathe a sigh of relief as none of the weapons they're using are anti-energy. I suppose they wouldn't be much use against the Kolvaxians, given their bodies have anti-energy in them. Why would that be the case, anyway?

" **Malfunction.** " I speak again, and every gun in the room jams at once and stops firing, courtesy of the Crown expanding my knowledge of their workings.

Helen quickly holds up her hands and speaks in fluent Volgarian. "We are your allies! Please stop shooting!" Her wording is slightly comical, given I just jammed their guns. Oh well.

Images of males and females shaken up over the losses of vast swaths of the population greet me as several of the scientists lower their trembling hands. Compared to the commanding presence of that Kryios Volgrim from before, these people are barely holding on to sanity. The room is filled with piles of guns, spears, metal boxes and benches overturned to form barriers in case of a firefight, and two of the four corridors leading into the room are completely barricaded. The path ahead leads to the hangar where the Kolvaxians will likely enter, while the way behind probably leads deeper underground where the scientists will be unable to flee any further. _A deathtrap,_ my mind whispers.

"You! Who are you?" One male tentatively steps toward us, a gun in each hand. The Crown identifies the weapon in his right hand as a plasma pistol, while the one in his left fires anti-energy. Both are disabled already though, but I don't think he's realized it yet.

"We're friends. We've come to save you from the Kolvaxians," Samantha says, as she takes a cautious step toward him.

"Friends? You're not even Volgrim!" The male inserts a small amount of pride in his voice, though it sounds weak and ineffectual, given the circumstances. Unlike the scientists, this one has a slight air of authority to him, not unlike someone stuck at a dead-end job in middle-management. Essentially, he thinks he has power and authority, but really doesn't.

I sigh and heave my shoulders in the air. "Ah, I guess you're right. We aren't Volgrim. Well, since you're being picky, we'll just leave. I'm sure you can stop the planet-busting enemy force outside all by yourselves."

That calms him down. He glances at the others before looking back at me. "Fine, you've made your point. What can you do to help? The best weapons we've got won't last long, and now that they've converted the Elders, it won't be long before they simply shut down our defenses."

"I can't fight the Kolvaxians," I say, though in my head I think, _at least, Jason Alpha can't. If he's more powerful than me on a scale of millions, I probably shouldn't chance it._ "What I can do is get you out of here. Where are the other Volgrim on the planet? I want to teleport as many as possible to safety."

A female against the wall to my left gazes at me questioningly. "Why? You say you're an ally, but what do you have to gain from saving our lives?"

Nice. Questions. Just what I want to hear at a time like this. "I'm not doing this out of obligation; I'm doing it because it's the right thing to do." I turn to Helen. "I'm going to teleport them to Axis Mundi. That work for you?"

Immediately, her expression sours. "Please, no. Nobody is supposed to know of its existence. Keeping the existence of the angels from the rest of the galaxy is why Alpha created the place. Just put them in Hero City or something."

I pause to think about it. Hero City has a population of perhaps a hundred thousand. Most humans live on outer-worlds that Hero City's central portal hub links to. A few hundred Volgrim could comfortably fit in there, but there might be millions of others that I have to save. Hero City is a short-term plan, though when I put things in term of population, Axis Mundi is the far worse choice.

Wait, I just remembered a second option!

"Hey, you." I turn back to the Volgrim male. "You said the Elders were turned... were they the highest authority among Volgrim? Who's in charge now?"

The man stares at me blankly. "I don't know, human. There is no formal command structure right now. We've lost contact with the other bases. I suspect a signal scrambler is being aimed at the planet."

"Fantastic. That makes things easy." I speak two more words, aiming my mind throughout the cosmos to find the one I seek. " **Locate. Teleport.** "

A moment later, a familiar Volgrim female appears in the middle of the room, her mouth freezing open mid-sentence as the sudden change in surroundings disorients her. Her shock barely lasts a moment before she starts spinning around, looking at every individual in the room, taking all their images in at once as she tries to understand what's going on.

Her eyes spot me, and she continues to look around but does a double-take once she remembers my face. "Ah! You- what did- where-?!"

I cut her stammering off. "Dosena, long time no see, except not really."

The former Second Founder of the Volgrim blinks multiple times before taking a deep breath and staring at me, forcing a neutral expression. "Jason Hiro, what are you doing? I was right in the middle of something."

"It's an emergency. The Kolvaxians are about to wipe the Volgrim off the face of the galaxy, and your people need a leader to take command and gather-"

She holds a hand up to interrupt me. "Kolvaxians? Invasion? What are you talking about? I can barely understand what's going on."

_Oh god. This is going to take a few minutes._ As that thought enters my mind, the sound of automated turrets activating and firing hundreds of shots at once makes everyone in the room jerk their heads around to look back down the hallway that leads to the outside world.

Shit.

"Uhh..." I freeze, unsure how to speed things up. After a second, a solution comes to mind. I aim my mind at Dosena's. " **Share.** "

A stream of thoughts and memories from the past few weeks copy from my mind to hers, including everything I know about the Kolvaxians, the battle with Satan, and even Jason Alpha.

A stunned expression passes over Dosena's face as she violently recoils from the sudden flood of mental information. She starts to fall backward, but once again Helen demonstrates surprising agility and dashes forward, catching the former Second Founder before she hits the ground.

"Jason! Give a little warning next time!"

Dosena's speech slurs for a moment, but she quickly shakes the handicap off. Pulling herself to her feet, she glares at me for a moment. "It's beyond rude to invade the personal space of someone else's mind. Something you would know if you were born Volgrim." After a pause, her anger fades. "That being said, I somewhat grasp the situation. Why do you need me?"

The sound of metal scraping against metal makes my back seize up as several turrets stop firing. A few others continue, but it's clear the Kolvaxians are ripping the static defenses to pieces. Many of the scientists smack their guns, trying to get them working again, but given I disabled them, that won't solve the problem. I quietly mutter, " **Repair.** "

Satisfied their guns are working, the male from before starts setting up a defensive perimeter while I try to explain to Dosena what I want. She listens to me intently, but I can see she doesn't like it.

"I left that life behind, Jason. I'm not fit to lead a people I have nothing in common with. I don't know millions of years of their history, who my subordinates would be, or anything else. You're free to put the refugees on Voldarius for a short while, but we haven't the food nor the living space for them to stay long. Your kindness is always going to be limited by external factors."

She gives me a fairly definitive _no_, but at the same time, food and living space aren't an issue, given I can just increase the planet's size with Wordsmithing. I don't have time to argue with her, and I got a 'yes' for the important question, so it'll have to do.

"Thanks, Dosena. I'll talk with you later. You need to reconsider for the good of your species. **Teleport.** " I don't give her a chance to deny me again, instead opting to send her back to Voldarius before she can respond. Then I quickly aim my mind at clumps of the scientists standing nearby. " **Return. Return. Return.** "

Each cluster of Volgrim takes a few seconds to focus on, send, then continue, and after a rapid session of mental math, I realize it'll take at least a couple of minutes before I can send them all.

Luckily, Cassiel notices. "Keep teleporting them, Jason. The four of us will attempt to slow the Kolvaxians down."

I nod and continue speaking. " **Return. Return. Return.** "

Cassiel and Samantha run to the beginning of the hallway that leads to the hangar. By now, the turrets have all been blasted to smithereens or destroyed. The silence from that direction is deafening, but despite tearing the defenses to shreds, no enemies have started moving down the corridor toward our position.

Tvoorik glances back and forth before grabbing Helen's arm and hurling her to the middle of the room. "Below!" She yelps awkwardly, right before spotting a Kolvaxian appearing from under the metal-plated floor. As if made of pixie sparkles and unicorn farts, the fucker jumps from the ground to swipe at where her leg would have been, before hissing and grabbing for Tvoorik instead. I pause my wordsmithing for just a moment to deliver a violent kick to the thing's head, but it sucks back underground in the blink of an eye, and I nearly shatter my ankle pounding the ground.

Tvoorik keeps his cool, and his eyes quickly begin moving around, roving the ground below as he mentally tracks several _things_ below us. "Twenty-four. They can phase through solid objects."

"Great!" Helen shouts. "Everyone, watch your feet! They're trying to nab us one-by one and-"

She doesn't get to finish. One of the female scientists screams in fright as a Kolvaxian leaps from the ground, stabs its hand forward, and impales her through her stomach, then yanks her down, pulling her under the floor in the span of a single second. It's at this moment I realize the awful truth. _Going underground was a fatal mistake. This is their most advantageous position._

Indeed, as long as we can't take to the skies, the green bastards can grab us anytime they want, and we're at their complete mercy. Since they have none, I have to move quickly, or else it won't be the scientists who fall today. For several seconds, as I continue returning them to Voldarius, I ponder sending my friends and I instead and leaving the Volgrim to their fate.

_You can't save everyone, Jason. You'll never be a good enough Hero. You're nothing compared to the legends of the past. You're a failure._

A voice inside my head whispers to give up. It's that 'other me,' the one who haunts my dreams and turns them into nightmares. I can't listen to him. If he's right, then what am I fighting for? What's the point of anything? Why should I even-

"Jason! JASON!!" Cassiel screams in fright as three Kolvaxians leap from the ground, one of them hissing and biting onto her shoulder as she cries out in pain, and the other two grabbing her by the legs and pulling her down. She tries to fight them off, but in less than a second, she'll be pulled under!

Samantha darts forward and pounds the first Kolvaxian that bit her right in the face, delivering a clean blow while grabbing Cassiel's hands at the same time. "Cass! Don't let go! Don't let go!"

Cass is already half-buried, and she screams in terror. "Get it off! Get it off!!"

I leap forward and pound the ground with my fist, then begin yelling out a word repeatedly. " **Earthquake! Earthquake! EARTHQUAKE!!** "

Violent rumbles start hurling people around the room as the planet transforms from a solid object into a tsunami of movement. I wrap my arms around Cassiel and pull up with as much strength as I can muster, but it's as if the lower half of her body is trapped inside a fucking black hole! No matter how the ground shakes and crumbles under my feet, it's like I'm trying to lift a skyscraper with my bare hands.

Cassiel slips down another inch or two, and several other Volgrim scientists shriek in terror as they're hurled around the room and grabbed in midair by more Kolvaxians, but I don't give a shit about them anymore. _I won't let them take Cassiel! I won't!_

Suddenly I feel pressure on my back, and a horrible pain as I realize several Kolvaxians have jumped on me and started biting. The anguish from an unknown poison rushing into my bloodstream almost doubles me over and makes me let Cassiel go, but I won't do it! I can't fail her! I can't allow the voice in my head to be right!

Samantha screeches in fear as a Kolvaxian leaps onto her back as well, then starts throttling her and twisting with all its might, cutting her voice off mid-yelp before biting a substantial chunk out of her shoulder.

_Hah hah hah... I warned you, idiot. You'll be turned and transformed into monsters. You tried to fight a plague that even a thoughtsmith with tens of millions of years of experience couldn't overcome. You tried to play hero with the lives of a few hundred worthless Volgrim, just so you could feel good about yourself, all while ignoring the tens of millions the Kolvaxians already turned or killed. Why bother? Give up. Accept your fate with dignity._

A face appears before me as numbness floods my body. It's my face, again. _What is this, the second time now? The third? How many times will you let them die? If only you were stronger! If only you were smarter! But you aren't, because you're worthless! You'll never put your mind to use, because you can't even-_

Shut up!! Shut up!

My eyes jerk open as an idea forms. The crown! Use it, idiot!

Wordsmithing and magic won't work on these fuckers, and if that's the case-

" **COPY! COPY!** "

My mind aims outside the bunker, into the sky and the space above the planet. Several hundred Kolvaxian vessels lie in wait, but I only need one shot.

In the void, a tiny speck appears, insignificant compared to the size of a capital ship. Then, another does. Two tiny, trivial satellites, copied from the one orbiting Hero City.

_Target! Aim! FIRE!!!_

"Cassiel! Shield! NOW!"

I don't get a chance to find out whether she heard me or not.

A light, like staring directly into the sun, engulfs everything. A titanic beam of energy, caused by two fusion reactors blasting our position with every ounce of power they contain, destroys every speck of land for a hundred miles around.

The light transforms into darkness.

I can't see.

I can't hear.

I can't feel.

Maybe I'm dead. Nobody could have survived that. It was a blast powerful enough to rip apart two small planetoids. Yeah. I'm definitely, one hundred percent gonezo.

After a moment, my vision clears. I'm... falling?

Falling? Why?

Wind whistles past my ears. Cassiel is unconscious. Her sleeping face is cute... so is Samantha's. Oh look, there's Tvoorik and Helen, just above us.

Why are we falling?

Oh goodness, that's hot on my back. Where's the heat coming from?

Casually, I turn my head behind me. _Oh wow, an absolutely massive pit of magma, and we're falling toward it. That is going to burn once we hit it. Yep._

I should definitely do something about that.

Aaaaaany time now.

Any second.

HOLY SHIT!

" **Teleport!** "

All five of us jump away seconds before splashing into a fiery hot death, and I send us way over to the east, where we land safely on a soft patch of ground. Our bodies smash into the dirt with sickening _crunches_, but I wheeze for a moment before tilting my head to look at the others. Cassiel landed on top of me, and Samantha just off to the side, but Tvoorik and Helen are a couple dozen feet away.

Cassiel's skin is discoloring, and fast. For the second time in a day, her soft complexion is mottling at a visibly alarming rate. Even Samantha's skin is doing so, though Tvoorik and Helen don't seem to have been bitten by the Kolvaxians.

" **Hea- kah! Heal! Revert! Heal! Revert!** "

I quickly repair the damage Cassiel and Samantha took, wheezing in satisfaction as their skin slowly reverts to its normal coloration. Reaching up to wipe my head, I suddenly notice my skin is greening as well. Shit! Idiot! Always give yourself medical attention first!

" **Heal! Revert!** "

My phlegm-filled throat thanks me silently as a horrible buildup of mucus vanishes.

As I wait for the others to wake up, I finally notice my surroundings. To my right lies the crater that my dual cannons in the sky formed. It takes me a second to comprehend the scope of the devastation. The entire city that the Volgrim once lived in is gone.

All of the scientists I didn't teleport out in time... they're dead. Cassiel didn't have a spare second to protect everyone. What if I hadn't rushed? What if I had realized the Kolvaxians could attack from underground? What if I'd just teleported them to Voldarius without wasting time explaining things to Dosena? I could have done that afterward. They're dead because of me.

I can only take solace in the fact that I killed a few of the Kolvaxians as well.

" **Destroy.** " I speak the kill command to each of my weaponized lasers in orbit, blowing them apart in spectacular fashion. Even here, miles below, I can see faint blips of light as the remaining energy in their reactors detonate, reducing two machines that may have given the Kolvaxians an extra advantage in the war to fragments of their former selves.

The clouds, while mostly uniform in the sky, split in half above the impact site where the lasers of ultimate death pierced them from before.

Despite the sunlit sky, I can see a few stars above. They're beautiful.

After a moment, I sigh. _Those aren't stars, idiot. Those are Kolvaxian warships. In the next minute or so, they'll probably launch more pods to the surface._

Helen stirs. "Ahhh..." She slowly pulls herself together, a notable feat given her body was broken in several places just a minute ago. She rubs her back and cautiously pulls herself to a sitting position. "J-Jason...? Where- no, what happened?"

"I saved the day. We triumphed against the Kolvaxians," I mutter dryly. "Everything went according to my brilliant plan. No regrets at all."

She doesn't pick up on the sarcasm. "That's good to hear. What happened to the scientists- oh my goodness!" She suddenly notices the giant crater filled with magma bubbling in the distance. We're much too far for the heat to reach us, but a worry appears in my mind that it could erupt and shower us with agony, not unlike the figurative fires of Hell.

"Yep. That's all me. Best Hero in the universe." I toss my arm toward the devastation flippantly. "That used to be where the scientists worked. Nothing left of it now."

She stares at the crater for several long moments, as Cassiel stirs in my arms. "Are they all dead?"

"Mhm. The Volgrim, as well as the Kolvaxians. I doubt-" A thought occurs to me, and I pause. _I may have leveled the area, but hundreds of pods fell from the sky. There might be other Kolvaxians underground nearby! Only Tvoorik can sense them._

I can't keep overlooking details. " **Wake.** "

Tvoorik suddenly jerks up, as alert as if he'd never been knocked unconscious at all. Still, he takes a moment to look around before noticing me. "What happened?"

I quickly fill him in, and he nods. "Ain't nobody around. They'll probably send more, but I dunno. We should get out of here, while we still can."

A voice speaks up from my left, startling me. "Yooo! What's going on, guys!" I turn to spot myself waving at me. Once I notice the glowing skin, I realize who it is. "Jason Alpha? What are you doing here?"

He smiles cheerfully before pausing to notice all the people lying around on the ground, dazed or unconscious. "Oh you know, I was doing a bit of this and that. I was gone a little longer than I expected, but otherwise- whoa! What's that giant crater? Where even are we?" He quickly looks around, and I stare at him in dumbfounded awe.

"Did you just get here?"

Harold appears at his side. "Yeap! I told him this was the wrong time coordinate, but this dummy never listens to some old geezer like me! Oh what do I know, I'm just a dumb, stupid old man!" He rolls his eyes and strolls over to Helen. "Heya sweetie! What've you been up to while we were gone?"

She flashes him a genuine smile before winking at me. "Oh, you know. The usual. Existential horror and terror coupled with crippling loss and the realization nothing we do will save every life."

Harold merely nods as he leans over and kisses his wife on the cheek. "'Bout par for the course, I'd wager- HOLY SMOKES! What the hay are you kids doin' takin' a vacation on the crater of a live volcano?!"

I sigh and shake my head. _Wait until he finds out what they missed._
