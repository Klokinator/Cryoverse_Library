Part 414b - Fifteen

Ben and I arrive on a spaceship he owns, and after twenty minutes, we soon start cruising down to Earth's lower surface. The sun begins to darken as we pass through a thick haze that hovers over the ground.

Ben flicks a dial and the screen changes from a camera-based display to a wire-frame mode outlining buildings and other ships as we fly past them. "The smog down here is awful. I try not to go to the surface unless I absolutely have to."

"That bad, huh?" I slump back in my seat and relax while he expertly pilots us through dense hover-traffic that spans the vertical and horizontal axis as far as my eyes can see. "Sure are a lot of people flying around."

Ben shakes his head. "Maybe one in ten of these ships has a human inside. Most of them are automated. Drones to deliver goods to and from the surface to the stratoscrapers above. Only a quarter of Earth's population lives in the Shit Zone."

"By 'Shit Zone', you mean the smog?"

He pulls a hard left to turn us around a particularly tall skyscraper. "Yeah. It thins out the higher you go, but it's thick enough to make you gag if you go anywhere near the ground. Only the truly desperate live there."

A few seconds pass as I let him concentrate on bobbing and weaving through a particularly bad cluster of traffic. "Why are we going to the surface?"

His brow furrows in thought, and he glances at me for a second before looking back to the screen. "I love Marie, you know. I always stand by her decisions, no matter what. However, even I think Marie has flaws. She doesn't care much, if at all, about the lower-class citizens of the Human Empire. If you're serious about changing things for the better, the best shot you've got is seeing what it's like down here."

I smile smugly at him. "I guess even Marie has her faults, eh? I didn't take you to be the caring type."

"You don't know me very well. As I said before, Marie hired me because I'm a 'people person'. My job is to communicate her will to others who can't understand her genius."

The ship slows, and a docking bay opens up, swinging outward to let us land. "Uh huh. So, what, you want to feed the poor, save the children? Marie wouldn't do it, so now you're going to show me the worst side of society and hope I do?"

Our small ship autopilots its way into the hangar, and Ben sighs and bows his head. "Marie was always going out of her way to keep the other Jason limited. She never did anything that would allow him more freedom than necessary. Since it's clear that you won't be controlled like him, your Wordsmithing could be put to use fixing the elements of society that he couldn't, or wouldn't. It's up to you if you do."

"I'll think about it."

The display switches back to camera mode and a small crew of men and women appear on-screen. "Flight 4815, you are cleared for up to sixteen hours of docking time. Please deposit two hundred energy credits, and you may get on with your business."

Their polite but constrained tone makes me wonder to myself questioningly if they have anything against Ben, or perhaps just visitors from the upper districts in general.

After he follows the prompts on his screen and deposits the credits, he smiles jokingly at me. "It's on me this time, but you'll owe me two hundred credits if we come down here again."

"Sounds fair." We walk to the exit hatch in the back of the ship, squeezing through a narrow passageway in the process, and I ask him questioningly, "Can't I just Wordsmith energy credits?"

He groans. "Cheapskate."

It didn't answer my question, but the answer will have to do.

Molegrass pops out of a small hatchway behind us, grumbling to herself the whole time. "I can't believe you stuffed me in that tiny little box."

"Shut up, dog. Say one word in front of anyone down here, and I'll neuter you myself."

She whimpers and nods in response. Just in time, too, as a group of people walks past us. She'd better not think I'm joking, or this time I'll throw her off a ledge, and I won't mess up.

Ben looks at me for a moment before turning away. "You stick out like a sore thumb. Everyone knows what the Supreme Commander looks like, down in the slums. Even if they don't, you're dressed in high-ranking military garb. You won't find anyone willing to talk to you if you look like that."

"Easily fixed." The three of us walk behind a console out of sight from everyone else. " **Disguise. Clothes.** " My professional suit changes into something much plainer looking, as does Ben's, and I even alter my face to look like my normal self, removing the beard along with it. "How about that?"

He snickers. "You have a real baby face under all that fur. Nobody's gonna recognize you like that."

"Yup, that's me, Baby Face Nelson. Let's hurry up and get this over with."

...

Half an hour later, Ben and I walk across a catwalk surrounded by dingy, brown glass. If I squint and look closely, I can see the air outside slowly swirling around us, which reminds me of milk spilled into a vat of black food coloring. Every so often, the three of us pass by poor downtrodden souls. They never look up at me, never beg for money as people would in the world I came from, and simply mosey on about their business.

The smell in the air is thick, almost acrid. It's a smell that causes me to remember gas stations from the past, and it makes me not want to light a match for fear of blowing everything up. The air inside the buildings is clean enough, and the temperature is about perfect, all things considered, but the odor almost gags me. Nobody around us seems to mind; they're probably long since used to it.

"What is that smell? How does all of this smog exist in such an advanced society?"

Ben scratches his throat and frowns. "Well, the facilities that recondition food in masses burn fuel of different sorts. Nowadays everything is powered by fusion, fission, or solar energy, but that wasn't always the case. Most of the smog is leftover from the ancient times, millions of years ago. Marie didn't want to waste energy cleaning up a dead planet, and it was already too late by the time she'd thought of technology that could reverse it."

"The Earth is dead? How so?"

He waves his hand at the world past the glass. "You think animals can live in this? If any mammals or amphibians or anything else is alive aside from bugs, they're horribly mutated creatures, disgusting to look at. Vegetation still exists, but much of it is moss and fungus. Trees have long since choked and died." He sighs and pauses to stare at the brown air swirling around. "Yep... the Earth is toast. Humans killed it."

I stop to take a breath. "I can't believe it. All the animals are dead, all the greenery... Marie just let this happen?"

Ben quickly shakes his head. "Oh no, I didn't explain everything yet. Animals and plants are still alive, on Martian nature preserves, and other planets throughout the galaxy. Rygar, for example, is a world where we migrated 60% of Earth's remaining species around thirty million years ago. Humans aren't allowed to set foot there, aside from the 4,000 scientists who spend their days preserving the lives of those animals." He waves a finger around. "Not only that, but they preserve genetic differences and keep the animals the same over time, allowing new species to form only after applying for permits. It's a whole process, very fascinating. Earth is completely dead though."

"Oh. That's better than I expected." Indeed, I thought all the elephants and polar bears were gone. At least there's some comfort that even if Earth is dead, it lives on, its inhabitants spread all over the galaxy. Humanity is in no danger of an imminent extinction event.

We continue walking, and Muttwiggle nudges my hand with her nose. Nobody is around, so she whispers. "Pardon me, but I'm hungry."

I glance at Ben, and he shrugs. "I'm good. I had coffee."

I ate too before I left. I shoot a glare at the dog. "Well, you'll have to deal with it. Should have eaten before we headed out."

She mumbles something under her breath that sounds vaguely like a curse word, but I ignore it.

Ben nudges me. "We're here. This is the bar where most low-level workers hang out after their shifts are over."

He points at a metal doorway nestled in a wall. The door has indentations shaped like letters pounded into it. _Thompson's Bar and Grill_.

I look at Ben inquisitively, and he shrugs. "This place is old, really old. It's been around forever. Heck, I even used to eat here, before they stopped serving normal food. Nowadays it's one of the few places in the lower levels where you can get nutrition bars purer than 15% reconditioned quality."

He presses a button, and the door slides up into the ceiling, making a quick gear-grinding noise as it does.

The room inside is dimly lit. Smoke fills the air, and I cough reflexively, but Ben doesn't mind at all. "You'll get used to it."

I hope so. We take a seat at a table and Ben waves at a waitress. Rather than coming over and taking our order, she just nods and walks into the back. A moment later she walks out, holding two nutrition bars wrapped in foil and two ceramic cups full of coffee. After setting them down at our table, she scurries away without a word.

"Polite lady, isn't she?" I pull the coffee toward me and sniff it. It smells like shit. I hate this stuff. The nutrition bar isn't doing me any favors either.

"There isn't much to say. All the nutrition bars here are free, provided by the government. This place is considered a cultural hub, so they give it some exclusive benefits. She isn't paid anything, just does this to pass the time." Ben sips the coffee and smacks his lips. "Man, that's the good stuff."

I don't see what's so good about bitter brown swill, but to each their own. My eyes pass over the crowd to a few guys playing a game I recognize off in the back, away from the other diners. Billiards. A short guy with a scraggly beard and a limp in his step lines up the cue ball and rockets it off the 9-ball, smacking it into a side pocket. The cue ball rolls back to almost the same position from where he started, and he shoots again, sinking the 13-ball after, only for the cue-ball to move right back to where it started.

The other two men shake their heads in defeat as he picks off the balls, one by one. Once he's done, he drops the 8-ball in and smiles as the cue-ball rolls back to where he started. "That's ten points for me. Quite the deficit, wanna double or nothing?"

As he stuffs his hands in his pockets, the other two guys shake their head. "Nah, you win this 'un, Edgar." Both of them press a button on their datapads and walk back to their table and sit down.

Edgar seems satisfied with his winnings, but disappointed the other guys walked away so quickly. A moment later, he spots me looking at him and winks. With a hop in his step, he waltzes over to my table. "Evenin', sirs! How'd you like to play a friendly game of Fifteen?"

Ben grunts and shakes his head. "No thanks, son. We're just having a friendly chat, that's all."

Edgar seems miffed that Ben won't even look at him, but a moment later there's a spark of recognition in his eyes. "Hey, wait a second, you look familiar! Yeah, yeah! I recognize you!" He shakes his finger as he struggles to find the name on the tip of his tongue. "That's right; you're Benjamin Brown! I'd recognize you anywhere!"

Ben shoots a look of annoyance at me. _Great, so much for going incognito._ His expression betrays his thoughts, though I can also read them thanks to my telepathy.

"Um, yes. Do I know you though?" Ben glances up at the guy and quickly shakes his head. "I remember most faces, but not yours."

"Nah, but that's okay!" Edgar grabs a chair and yanks it out, then plops down at the edge of the table, between Ben and me. With a practiced movement, he reaches down and pets our dog without actually looking at it. "You're a famous guy 'round here! You were just like us, a bottom dweller who busted your ass and made it out of this shithole! And you killed a whole lot of demons while you were at it, too!"

Ben grunts and mutters something under his breath, but I don't quite catch it. He opens his mouth to say something, but suddenly his eyes shift to meet mine, and I can feel a thought pass through his mind. _Hey, this guy could be useful._

He smiles suddenly, far more than I expected, and gestures at me. "Ah, I forgot to mention, I'm here with my grand-nephew, Jasper. He was born and raised in the upper levels, so I wanted to show him around the lower levels. He's a bit of a privileged kid, see."

Edgar swivels to me. "Great! Good to meet you, kid! Your grand-uncle's a stand-up guy!" He thrusts a hand forward, and I quickly shake just to get him away from me. Already the stench of alcohol is hissing from the edge of his mouth and making our table stink. He immediately forgets I'm even here and turns back to face Ben. "Aren't you working at the upper levels now for bigshots? I heard you were in private security!"

Ben smiles, but I sense a lot of discomfort from him. "Yes, uh, something like that. You really keep up to date on my status, mister...?"

"Aw, shucks. I got so excited that I forgot to introduce myself! I'm Edgar Boris, gamblin' extraordinaire and flamboyant ladies-man, yessir! Though I make my money now by winnin' _honest_ games of Fifteen these days." The way he enunciates 'honest' makes me suspect he's a little less forthcoming than he pretends to be.

I decide to poke my way into the conversation. "Say, what if we play against you in your favorite game for a friendly bet? I have some questions about life down here in the lower levels, and you look like a pretty smart guy."

Edgar bows his head and blushes. "Aw, shucks. I ain't that smart. But I will whip you good in a game o' Fifteen! Come on, let's do this!" He hops up from the table and quickly walks over to set up the table.

Ben and I stand up to follow, and Ben nods at me. "You've got the same idea I do. He's a talker, might give you useful information."

"That's the idea." We walk over, and Edgar beckons to the balls he's just racked up. "Alright, I'll let you boys decide the order of play."

I speak before Ben can. "My grand-uncle will play first; you go next, then I'll finish."

"Mhm. What are we betting, Mister Boris?" Ben looks suspicious, and Boris makes a big show of hemming and hawing it over. "I dunno, what say maybe, ahhh, hmm... how about fifty energy credits, eh?"

I'm not sure how many credits 50 energy credits are worth, but Ben doesn't look like he'll lose too much sleep. "Yeah, that'll do. We'll just bet from my stash since Jasper doesn't have any credits in his... _allowance_." He stifles a snicker, and I resist the urge to punch him. He's enjoying treating me like a kid way too much. Though, to be fair, I am only 18, realistically. My time spent in the cryopod did nothing for me, so my mental age is approximately my real age.

Ben lines up a shot and slams the cue-ball into the 1-ball, smashing all the others apart and spreading them over the table. Luckily, the 13-ball goes in.

"That's a point for the both of you." Edgar looks perfectly happy that we've gained a point. He acts as if he has this game in the bag. We'll see about that.

Ben aims and manages just barely to knock the 6-ball into the far corner pocket. "I played quite a bit of Fifteen when I was younger." For a moment, I spot a look of smugness on his face, but he brushes it away. After lining up another shot, he fires it off and smacks the 1-ball, but it glances off the side hole and rolls away, the cue-ball sinking instead.

"You did, huh? Well, I'm glad you're takin' it easy on me. Two against one just ain't fair for a poor cripple like me." He slaps his ball on the table, lines up a shot, and misses catastrophically, then pretends to play it off. "Man, I must be tired from all those games I played earlier."

I nod at him and line up my shot. "So, Edgar, what are you planning to do with the credits you earn?" I sink the 2-ball in effortlessly, but miss my next shot, and it's back to Ben.

Edgar shrugs. "Well, I gotta get my leg fixed first. It's in real bad shape. Medical care is decent down here, but the premium stuff is for middle-level workers and up, so I'll probably have Four-Finger Freddy do somethin' for me. I've been earning favors from him for a while now."

Ben whistles aimlessly and fires his ball, hitting his target but missing the shot. It's three to zero, in our favor.

"Who's Four Fingered Freddy?" I ask the question casually as Edgar steps up to the table and lines up his shot. He sinks it casually and scrunches up his face when he looks at me.

"You ain't heard of him? Shit, he runs the market this side of the city. Nice guy, but real stern. You don't disrespect him, or you get a visit from his friends." Edgar leans back and pops another shot off, dropping another ball in. Score 3-2, our favor still.

"The market? You mean the black market?" I ask the question innocently, but I can feel a few people turning to listen to our conversation.

"Yeah, _sure_." Edgar suddenly looks annoyed as people glance over at him. "You can call it that, if you want. Ain't really accurate though. Everything they've got, they earn it legit. They offer medical services for people like me. They're heroes down here; I'll have you know."

Ben coughs as Edgar sinks yet another shot. Score 3-3, tied. "We understand. We're not prying or anything, just curious. It's been a long time since I lived down here."

"Yeah... a long time." The man hobbling around the table echoes Ben's words hollowly. Even though he suddenly seems more distant than before, he lands another shot without trouble. Score 4-3, his favor. "You know, sometimes I wonder if it's even worth the trouble."

"What do you mean?" I've scarcely asked the question, and he slams another shot in, using a bit more power than the previous one. Score, 5-3, his favor.

"Oh, you know. The rat-race. I wasn't always poor. Had an adequate job in the middle sector for a while, but it felt like they had me pushin' buttons all day rather'n doin' anything useful." He takes aim and smashes the cue-ball again, but this time it glances off his intended target and rolls to the side, so he hands the stick to me. "Ended up losin' my position when I hit on a coworker. He didn't like me much, I reckon, so I had to sit down with upper management, and they fired me. Two hundred thousand years down the tube."

I sink a shot in, then miss the next one, bringing our score to 5-4, his favor. "Sounds rough. What's the alternate, then, if not working your way back up the ladder."

He sniffles and waits for Ben to take a shot next. "Oh, I just hate the smell down here, but I have lots of friends now, and more fun than I ever used to up top. I've been thinkin', ya know... what if I killed myself?"

He says it so casually, but Ben doesn't even flinch. I nod slowly. "Uh... you want to _kill yourself_? Seems a little extreme."

"Oh, sure. I get to be an angel, live somewhere clean. Of course, there are all the problems that come with that."

"Like what?"

Ben smacks two balls in with a single shot, then misses the next. 6-5, our favor.

"Well, you know how it is. Once you're an angel, you can't ever leave Heaven, 'cept if you get a permit or pay their ridiculous fee, which nobody can afford. Only people who die as high-level citizens ever get to come back, that's the way it is. Still, I'd maybe get to see my ex-wives again. If any of the gang dies after me, we can all hang out together. I'm just... torn."

He takes the stick and instantly lands one shot, then pivots around and knocks two more balls in at the same time. Score, 8-6, his favor. The last ball standing is the 8-ball, which he sinks without hesitation.

Ben chimes in with his thoughts. "If you're unsure of what to do, why not keep working your way up the ladder again, so when you get to mid-level, you can earn the energy credits to go visit your friends and wives who have passed? It'll take a long time, but you've got forever. It's not like you're going to die of old age."

Edgar chuckles. "Now that's a fine idea. You're a smart guy, Benjamin. No wonder you ended up at the top. Lot smarter'n me."

Ben shakes his head and looks at me, though his eyes seem to look past me, at the wall behind. "I'm not that smart... just a lucky guy in the right place at the right time."

Edgar nudges Ben in the arm with his fist. "I hear ya. You gents wanna have another go?"

"No, but that was fun. I know when my ass is chapped." Ben snickers and brings up his datapad, sending the energy credits over to Edgar. "Good talk though, you were a real help."

"Thank ya kindly for the credits, good sir. I'll be seeing you two around...?" Edgar trails his sentence off, but Ben shakes his head.

"No, I've got a lot of work to attend to." He nudges Edgar. "You know how it is, button pushing, busywork. I'm a little jealous of you."

"Pff, jealous of me. Funny guy." Edgar waves at us as we turn and walk away. I beckon to Moldrush, and she stands up and follows after us.

As we step outside, the door closes behind us, and I pause for a moment. "That Edgar guy has lived a rough life."

"Mhm." Ben shrugs. "Nobody's life is easy."

"Yeah, but, he's lost a lot of people, yet he's still trying to be optimistic."

Ben continues nodding. "Some people are just that way."

We start to walk again, but I pause and speak a single word to the air. " **Pill.** "

A small orange pill with liquid inside appears in front of me and lands in my hand. "One sec, Ben."

I turn back and walk inside, then find Edgar, now sitting at a table and staring off into space. When I tap his shoulder, he turns back to look at me and is a little surprised. "Oh, you're back! What's up? Want another whooping?"

I smile and shake my head. "No, not really. Here." I hold my hand in the air and drop the pill on the table in front of him.

He glances at it suspiciously. "What's this? Cyanide?"

"No. You're a good guy, Edgar. Take this pill, and I bet that foot will feel a lot better tonight."

He turns to look at me in surprise, but I'm already halfway out the door. Ben stares at me with a look of amusement. "What was that about?"

"Nothing in particular. Let's find that Four Fingered Freddy guy. He sounds like an interesting character."
