Part 259a

"These are the candidates, then?" The man known as Judicator glanced back and forth at my comrades and I from inside his pure white suit. I never really understood why he wore that suit, if all he wanted to do was protect his identity. Something less flashy would have done the trick, if you had asked me.

But now I understood. Nineteen suits identical to his, but painted grey, were set neatly on a long table behind him. Our platoon commander had been in a bit of a rush, but he got the gist of it across. These were super-suits, designed to make a man damn near unkillable. They had all sorts of special abilities we didn't have time to test out, but we'd get time after we killed these invaders. For now, they were made to allow us to wade into the field of battle and not get hurt, leading our forces to victory.

"Yes sir, Judicator. Let's skip the formalities, these boys and girls are my best, I trust all of them with my own life." Commander Hudson nodded briskly at the man who was blessing us with such a divine gift, as she showed no expression on her face. She was a feared and respected woman, one who demanded no special treatment in the military and beat the boys at their own games. We in return would readily lay down our own lives for her, just as she would for us. It was a bond of trust, not ever to be broken, even if it meant death.

"Great. We don't have time to waste." Judicator's mouth-covering thing closed and I felt slightly apprehensive. Then something odd happened... the suits started changing sizes, as if custom altering their own sizes. I ain't never seen such a thing in my life, and I thought it was quite the marvel if I don't say so myself.

Then, all of a sudden, one of the suits vanished and Thompson yelped, all of us jerking our heads over to look at him. He was wearing the suit! It had up and jumped off the desk before we knew it! I didn't even see it move!

Then Sally jerked back, as she was suddenly wearing a suit too! Then Ryan, and after that was Tom! And since I was next- oh _DAMN_! In the blink of an eye, I was suddenly wearing the suit too. I almost wouldn't have noticed it, but my vision suddenly went weird, as if someone had put a pair of weird 3D virtual reality glasses on my face. I appeared to be looking through some video game lens, as I could see all sorts of numbers and readouts. One of them displayed my name, Private Ben L. Brown. A quick readout of my military history scrolled down rapidly. It even knew how many Volgrim I'd killed! Wow!

After a minute, everyone was inside their suits and looking around in awe just like I was, as their suits were scanning the environment and displaying tactical data about all sorts of things, including the terrain.

"Alright, no time to lollygag. I had to leave the frontlines to come here and get you guys suited up. As we speak, your friends are _dying_ out there. Let's go over the basics, and quickly. Your suit will make you unbelievably strong, very fast, and will compensate automatically for wasted movements, allowing you to fight far more efficiently. It auto-tracks and aims at enemies, it's essentially impervious to all forms of damage, and once you learn how to use it, you can even use it to fly."

"You can fly in these?!" Sally seemed to fill with glee at the thought. I couldn't blame her, the thought excited me too.

"Yes. Worry about that later though. You can play around with them later, they will be permanent tools for each of you. You need to get out there and fight, right now." Commander Hudson sternly stared to us, as our suit's picked up a large explosion a few miles away.

"Sir, yes sir!" We all quickly switched back to full military mode as we stood at attention and saluted.

"Now get out there and fight, for the people of Britannia! They're counting on you!"

...................................................................

Each of us went to our own 'sector'. We were strong enough that we didn't need backup, so our orders were to go into enemy territory solo and cut a path for the rest of the troops to swarm in. I don't know how he did it, but Judicator somehow instantly moved me from my spot at base over 13 miles into a different spot in Beijing. The metropolis itself was swarming with Volgrim parasitic scum, and I held nothing but hatred for them. I lost a lot of friends to their murderous ways, and I will never forgive them.

As I appear, my suit starts talking in a strange voice, kinda reminds me of a robotic Jean Claude Van Damme, a favorite classic movie star of mine.

"OPERATOR BROWN, I HAVE DETECTED THREE THOUSAND TWO HUNDRED AND FOURTEEN HOSTILES IN THE IMMEDIATE HALF-MILE VICINITY. WOULD YOU LIKE FOR ME TO SET PRIORITIES FOR TARGETS BASED ON RELATIVE COMBAT ABILITIES AND SPIRITUAL ENERGY THEY ARE LIKELY TO POSSESS, OR BASED ON DANGER TO CIVILIAN LIFE?"

What the hell was he talking about? Spiritual energy? "Uhh, let's focus on the civilians."

"AFFIRMATIVE. PRIORITIZING DANGERS TO CIVILIAN LIFE. FIRST PRIORITY FOUND. CIVILIANS IN DANGER AT THE SUBWAY SYSTEM ENTRANCE LOCATED ON YOUR MAP. THERE ARE SEVEN HUMANS IN DANGER, AND ONE OF THEM IS A HIGH LEVEL DIPLOMAT FOR THE CHINESE EMBASSY. THE OTHERS ARE ALSO MID-LEVEL POLITICIANS AND BUREAUCRATS IN DANGER OF IMMEDIATE DEATH."

I nodded quickly as the location showed up on my HUD, with an arrow telling me which direction to go to. "Alright, let's do it." As I started moving, a thought crossed my mind. In a city of over twenty-five million, I was about to try saving just seven people? Was it because they were 'important' people? Did the computer prioritize something like an individual's value to society over raw numbers of people? In either case, I'm a soldier, and I follow orders. I'll do as I'm told.

I grimaced as I ran. It was tough to see, even with the suit's obviously enhanced visual capabilities, due to the horrendous smog, as well as the smoke that was in the air from fires caused by the Volgrim. Beijing had only gotten worse over the decades, and now you could hardly see twenty feet in front of yourself, maybe a hundred with a super-suit like mine, but that was pushing it.

"Fuck, I can't see a thing." I felt nervous as I ran forwards and muttered to myself. Apparently, the AI decided to respond.

"VISION DEEMED INSUFFICIENT. WOULD YOU LIKE TO SWITCH TO INFRARED MODE?"

"Yes! Yes, that sounds fantastic, err... what was your name again?"

"CONFIRMED. SWITCHING TO INFRARED MODE. OPERATOR BROWN, MY DESIGNATION IS CENTURION."

"Thank you, Centurion." Immediately as I spoke, my vision altered and the world turned into varying shades of red, yellow, orange, grey, and black, as the hotspots lit up all around me. Suddenly my nerves were on fire as I saw absolute chaos unfolding. In a similar manner to how I might have imaged totalitarian extermination of lives in the middle of the 20th century, civilians were being gunned down all over the place. I was watching a vicious slaughter take place, as Volgrim in all directions were tearing into innocent humans and ripping their bodies to pieces, whether with blaster fire, or with terrifying melee weapons.

I stared in horror. I was prioritizing seven humans over all these other people? How could the computer coldly calculate those specific people, even as I watched a dozen people cowering behind some furniture get blown to pieces by an explosive device?

"Centurion, is there some way I can attract the attention of as many Volgrim around me as possible? I need to save the lives of as many people as possible."

"QUERY RECEIVED. THE HIGH PROFILE ASSETS ARE TOP PRIORITY. HEAD TO THE SUBWAY IMMEDIATELY. A DISPLAY THAT DRAWS ATTENTION WILL NOT SAVE THEIR LIVES, AND IS MATHEMATICALLY INSIGNIFICANT."

"What? There are so many other people to save! Is there a way to save all of them?"

"QUERY RECEIVED. AFFIRMATIVE. THERE IS A WAY TO DRAW THE ATTENTION OF A LARGE NUMBER OF HOSTILES. HOWEVER, THE LIVES OF THE ASSETS IN THE SUBWAY ARE DEEMED MORE IMPORTANT TO THE SURVIVAL OF THE HUMAN RACE THAN THE OTHER ONES. PROCEED TOWARDS THE SUBWAY AT ONCE. THEY ARE IN IMMEDIATE DANGER."

I gritted my teeth. "Centurion, I want you to prioritize the lives of as many assets numerically as possible, not based on their perceived value towards human life or whatever else. Do I make myself clear?"

I stared carefully into the sensor array as the AI paused for several painful seconds before responding.

"...CONFIRMED. SWITCHING PRIORITIES TO SAVING HUMAN LIVES BY RAW NUMERICAL VALUE RATHER THAN BY ORDER OF IMPORTANCE. I HAVE SENT A FULL AND DETAILED REPORT TO YOUR SUPERVISOR. WHAT ARE YOUR NEXT ORDERS?"

I openly gaped at the display in front of me, but swallowed my anger and let it steep. So be it, I'd rather face a court martial than let people die. "Create a distraction, let's kill some Volgrim."
