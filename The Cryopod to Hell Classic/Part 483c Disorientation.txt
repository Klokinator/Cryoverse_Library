Part 483c: Disorientation

"How'd it go?"

I stare ahead blankly at the person asking the question. I'm looking at myself? No, wait, Alpha. Jason Alpha. He's asking how it went.

How _what_ went? Did I do something?

I start to take a step forward, then suddenly realize I'm standing. Why am I standing? Wasn't I asleep? He went off to talk to Alpha Marie; then I dozed off. How did I get here?

Marie walks between us, then I feel the brush of another Marie's shoulder against mine, and another, and I realize I'm in the waiting area from before- that is, from when I was almost asleep, which feels like an instant ago. _What?_ Evidently, the look on my face betrays my thoughts.

Alpha waves his hand in front of my eyes. "Hello? You're still Sparky, right? I didn't grab the wrong black-skinned glowing-tattooed Jason, right?"

"Me? What? Yeah. Thing? What thing did I- I went where?" My words are wrong. The order is incorrect. I don't even know what I'm saying.

Alpha nods. "Hmm. I guess they wiped your memory after the test. That makes sense. They probably leaked time travel details to you."

"They did? Who? Marie?"

Alpha nods and smiles, but shoots a glance back at Harold. "Yeah. Standard fare. They wanted to evaluate you and create a psych profile to make sure you aren't a threat to the multiverse. Can't have Satan posing as a Jason now, can we?"

"Uhh. Sure?"

An awkward silence follows. Alpha shuffles his feet slightly, before sighing. "Oookay, well, the fact they haven't thrown you in a containment unit means you're not the epitome of evil, so we should be good to go."

I slowly bob my head. "Oh, great." After arriving in this weird place and arriving in the middle of a crowd of Maries, unsure how I got here, I can't wait to go back to my home universe. This whole trip was a waste of time.

Alpha beckons for Harold and Helen to come along with us, but before they can make it the few feet from their benches over to us, one of the many Maries in the hall approaches us, specifically Alpha. "Before you go, please check Sparky's profile and sign off on his departure forms." She hands him a datapad, and he nods, tilting it away so I can't see what's on the screen.

"Hmm. Hmm. Interesting. I see." He nods mechanically as he skims whatever she gave him to read, and I start feeling a little antsy.

"You told them my name was Sparky? Seriously? And why can't I read that? It's my profile!"

Alpha raises his eyes up from the document, meets mine, then lowers them again. "All Jasons need a designation. Most are commonly referred to by their universe identifier for short. However, in your case, you come from my Source universe, so that would be kinda clunky. As for your profile..."

Alpha's expression darkens as he reads. "Very disturbing."

"Disturbing? What the hell does it say?!"

He shakes his head slowly. "Based upon the memory files they've recovered, it seems that you..." He pauses, as if for dramatic effect. "...hate puppies. That's disgusting. You're a monster."

I stare at him for several long moments as he continues to read, all while the Marie waits patiently, not even tapping her foot. "I don't hate puppies."

"I know." He smiles. "But I had you going, didn't I?"

"You're an asshole."

"You're me, so back at'cha." With unparalleled bantering skills, he deflects my attack and immediately returns to the datapad, scribbles something on the screen with his finger, then hands it to Marie. "Thanks."

"No problem." Her dry voice grates on my ears as it has every time up to this point, and she walks away, leaving us alone.

Alpha stares at me for a few moments, a thoughtful expression on his face. "You're me."

"I know. You just said that."

He shakes his head. "No, the memories are identical. I knew your body was familiar, but it's been so long since I saw it. You have the power of the three archangels. Gabriel, Raphael, and Michael."

"Um. Yes?" His sudden pressing about my past makes me uncomfortable, especially given how lost I feel right now. "Is that a problem?"

"I wonder." He chuckles wryly. "I ended up giving those rings to someone important. I guess you got your hands on them, somehow. They're probably the same rings I once owned."

Whatever he's saying makes no sense to me at all. I remember how I obtained my rings, and that was via a battle with Bahamut. Then again, Alpha's been alive a long time. Did he give the rings to Bahamut? _Why?_

Before I can ask, Alpha continues. "Everything about you is identical to me. I don't know how you arrived in my Source universe, but seeing you reminds me of my past. I don't like that."

From what I've seen of Alpha, he smiles and jokes around here and there, but sometimes his eyes hollow out, as though he's just putting on an act. For what purpose, though? For whose benefit?

"What happened in your past?" I ask, but quickly follow up with, "Besides destroying an entire universe."

"Many things. I've done a lot I'm not proud of."

"Like what?"

"Things." Alpha turns away and gestures to Harold and Helen, who are standing beside him now. I didn't even notice them there. "They know. Being a hero doesn't always mean being a good guy. Sometimes you have to make choices, and both options are the _worst_."

"Like killing a whole universe of people?"

"Yeah. However, the choice is sometimes whether to save your best friend or the woman you love. Somehow, those events burn more than the former."

I think I get the picture. Alpha somehow was unable to save someone important to him, even with the power of Wordsmithing. It's caused him to feel resentment and anger, but he covers up those emotions with jokes and sarcasm because he still must perform his duty, even if he doesn't always want the job.

"I'm sorry things turned out like that for you."

Alpha nods. "It's in the past. Boring time travel stuff. Can't change it, can't fix it. Anyway, enough chit-chat. Let's get out of here. We've got a mission." Immediately, he starts walking toward the exit.

"A mission?" I let Helen take my arm, and we fall into line behind Alpha. "What do you mean?"

"Didn't I say? We're on a mission to investigate the second Source universe. A lot is riding on this. There is a suspicious new Jason who has appeared, and we think he might be-"

"Hold on; I didn't sign up for that!" I interrupt him before he can finish. "I thought you were bringing me here for an evaluation, then returning me to my universe. Why do I have to go with you?"

"I want to keep an eye on you," Alpha says, without looking back. Several Maries, all clustered together, pass us in the tight corridor. "Also, you might prove to be relevant. The Jason we're investigating is probably Hope."

_Hope?_ I repeat the name in my head. "You think he's shown up now?"

"Mhm."

"So you need me to come along... because... why?"

"Like I said, to keep an eye on you."

I scowl. "I don't need you to do that. I don't _want_ you doing that. You're kinda creepy sometimes."

"It's not up for debate," Alpha says, as he suddenly stops and turns to face me. Fire burns in his eyes. "I don't know who you are, or where you came from, but there are an awful lot of coincidences surrounding you. I know it sucks being yanked away from your cute little girlfriends, but the entire goddamned multiverse is at stake."

"So you expect me to leave them behind?! What about the Kolvaxians?! Those monsters nearly tore them apart the last time we fought!" An urge to punch Alpha's stupid smug face boils up in my gut, but I resist, given he can splatter me all over the floor. It's smarter to use my words, not my fists. "You're telling me to go dawdle off for god knows how long, leaving them to fight in a warzone!"

Alpha groans. "God, how many times have I told you? That's not how time travel works! We can jump back to five minutes after we left and they'll barely even notice you were gone!"

Cautiously, I stare him down. "We can return to a point right after we left?"

"Exactly!"

A thought clicks in my head. "Doesn't that mean there's no rush to do this mission?"

"What?" Alpha's jaw slackens. "What's that supposed to mean?"

"I mean that if we can travel back to that time, then surely it works the same way for the mission. Whatever universe and time you plan to go to, we could spend a year in our source universe, then jump to the mission location and it would be the same as if we went now."

Alpha's face contorts in disbelief. "Well- I mean, that is..."

Helen squeezes my arm a little. "It isn't the same, not when we're dealing with other travelers, sweetheart. Satan is still out and about, and the Jason we're going to investigate, who might be Hope, is a big problem we can't leave unattended."

"How is it different?"

"Can't say." Helen cuts me off with two quick words, but then she turns to smile at Alpha. "You know, dear, it wouldn't hurt for us to go back to his Source universe for a day or two. Let's not be sticklers for the rules just because Marie barks an order."

I glance around and check, but no Maries are in the hallway. Somehow, given all the implied surveillance, it feels like a bad idea to insult her, but since turrets don't pop out of the walls and litter our bodies with anti-energy, we're probably okay.

Alpha rubs the top of his head and closes his eyes. "God, why do things always have to be so complicated? A single alarm about an activated Sentinel has turned into the biggest, dumbest mess of my life, and I've lived a long time!" He turns away from us to face down the corridor and quickly starts walking, so I pull Helen along and follow after him.

A minute passes, and Alpha tromps along but says nothing. Finally, he relents. "Cripes. Fine, whatever. We'll go back to your Source, you can say hi to your girlfriends, and then we're leaving. Okay?"

"Thanks, Alpha," I reply, gratitude and sincerity prime in my thoughts. He's an asshole, but he isn't impervious.

I mouth _thank you_ to Helen, and she winks at me. _Alpha's not that bad, you just have to use the right words,_ her expression says.

....................................................................

Many minutes later, we pass through the exit to the Marie section of the Central Hub, and into the Agent area, before arriving back in the room where we first started. A blank, empty void where the only visible color is white.

The door closes behind us, and Alpha taps some buttons on what looks like a smartwatch with holographic functionality. "Harold, do you have the stream synchronized?"

"Yeap, pretty much."

"Good." Something about Alpha's curt reply irks me, like he doesn't believe in Harold's ability to synchronize or something. "Once Marie drops the barrier, we'll jump."

"Hey, question." I shoot a suspicious glance at Alpha. "Last time you said you were going to come back after three days, but you returned in less than one. How reliable is your method for dating when to return?"

A drop of sweat visibly slides down Alpha's neck. "P-pretty reliable. We usually get close to the mark."

"Usually?"

Alpha quickly looks away, right as Marie's voice plays over the loudspeaker. "Jason Alpha, your exit slot is one-four-nine, six-three-three, one-four-three. We're lowering the anti-energy field now."

He exhales audibly, but I cut him off before we can jump. "Are you ignoring me?"

" **Travel!** " Ignoring my interruption, Alpha barks the word out, startling me, and a second later the room vanishes around us. A jarring transition occurs as white walls disappear and reappear as dark stone ones.

Blinking, I look around and try to reorient myself. This place is... the outpost? Yeah, the one right outside of Hero City.

It takes me another moment, but I quickly notice there's nobody else around. Not even a guard.

"What the hell? Why did you bring us here? I thought we were going to the girls in Axis Mundi!" Pausing, I wave my hand to indicate the empty room. "And where is everyone?!"

Alpha grunts. "You interrupted me. I was trying to focus my mind, and you kept asking dumb questions. I couldn't focus on Cassiel, so I brought us here. Jeez, just gimme a second."

A second passes. Then two. Three.

Harold frowns and taps his smartwatch. "Son... this date..."

Alpha glowers at the old man. "Shush. I found her. Cassiel and Samantha are about two hundred miles away, as the crow flies. Cassiel seems distressed, like she's fighting or something."

"Fighting?! Two hundred miles?! Where did you take us?"

Harold holds up his watch to show me its display, but the string of numbers means nothing to me. "We missed the return doohickey. It's been 'bout two months 'n sixteen days since we left."

_Two and a half months?!_ My mind blares several alarms at once. " **Locate!** " Instantly, my mind finds Cassiel, partially thanks to Alpha giving me a ballpark estimate of her location. " **Teleport!** "

Leaving the other three behind, I catch Alpha's mouth open as he starts to yell _'stop!'_ However, it's too late. I'm already gone.

A moment later the deafening sound of an explosion makes my ears ring. I'm knocked backward as a grenade erupts, sending shrapnel all over and hitting every chunk of my body.

"Ah!" I let out a weak yelp and go flying back, hitting something unthinkably hard, and probably metal.

After a moment, my ears stop ringing. I blink and gather my wits, start pulling out chunks of metal from my body, then yell, " **Heal!** "

War. A battle of epic proportions is taking place in front of me. I quickly scan the horizon. I'm inside the labyrinth, but it's barely recognizable. Massive chunks have been blown out of the walls, and enormous sections of labyrinth walls litter the landscape, raising into the air like tiny office buildings. The ground is uneven, descending into dips and valleys, and the room looks like it's been deliberately carved out to form as much open space as possible.

My blood cools as I realize the awful hissing noises are from Kolvaxians. " **Storage.** " Within three seconds, I place the Crown of Solomon on my head and pull up my mental map. This place is very close to the portal that leads to Volgarius Alpha, the world where I found Ionis. _Did the Kolvaxians already find it? Are the humans okay?_

God, I can't believe I'm thinking of them at a time like this. They've been turned by now. No way they escaped the Kolvaxians. More importantly, where is Cassiel? Samantha?

"Undulating Joy: Oh look, it is the biological that left this unit without a companion." A robotic voice behind me makes me jump to my feet, though I wince from the pain.

"OMEN?"

"Response: Correct. That is my designation. I see that your mental faculties have degraded by less than one-point-three percent."

A horrible scream of a man being tackled by monsters makes my stomach flinch. "I don't have time for this. Where is Cassiel? Where's Samantha?"

Turning to look in the direction of the arena-like area, I flinch as three men rush past me, guns raised, oblivious to my presence. They jump toward a Kolvaxian that appears from nowhere and blast it in the chest and brain, and the human-like monster hisses in agony before disappearing back into the ground.

One of the men holds his hand up to stop their charge, then taps something on his ear before rushing forward. Once again, a different Kolvaxian, female this time, jumps up and attacks them, but they manage to get the drop on her before she can hit them. She dips back into the ground, and I stare in awe all the while.

"They're fighting those monsters... and winning? How?!"

The ground vibrates underneath me suddenly, as if a micro-quake were occurring, and a moment later, it stops again.

"Thoughtful Response: Given your first question, the answer is that the biological designated Cassiel is currently on the frontlines inside the arena. This OMEN unit does not know her precise position. As for your second query, the biologicals are losing ground slowly to the Kolvaxians."

"They are?" I shoot him a questioning glance, but the robot stares blankly at me.

"Amusement: The biologicals are unable to combat the Kolvaxians fully. Soon all will be transformed into Class C biologicals, except for this OMEN unit. It will likely be disassembled and thrown in a landfill. Poetic."

I ignore his weird quirkiness. "But they were fighting those Kolvaxians just now! And winning!"

A hand grabs my shoulder and a deep voice barks in my ear. "What the fuck are you doing standing around, dipshit?! Grab a gun and help us fight, you little pansy-ass piece of-" The voice cuts off suddenly. "Jason?"

I turn to see who's talking. It's Benjamin Brown. He stares at me in shock. "It's World War goddamn Seven out here, and you've been off smelling roses and scratching your ass! Where have you been?!"

"I was only gone for a day. Jason Alpha screwed up and returned us much later in the timestream. Are Cassiel and Samantha okay?"

He notices the urgency in my eyes. "Yeah, kid. They're fine, for now. The problem is, these goddamn scum-suckers are already inside the Labyrinth! We can barely keep them in this area by blasting sonic pulses in the ground, but they'll escape soon enough, and everything'll go to shit!"

_Sonic pulses. That must have been the vibration I felt._ "How long have you guys been fighting the Kolvaxians?"

"Two weeks." An explosion a hundred feet away makes Ben flinch, so he grabs my jacket and hauls me over behind a wall. The sound of plasma-fire in the distance is like a jackhammer pounding into a vat of jellybeans. "We fight nonstop! My boys barely get a few hours of sleep, and have to constantly reinforce each other! Don't even think about poor Tvoorik! That son of a bitch hasn't slept at all!"

"Tvoorik?"

Ben nods. "Yeah. Tvoorik's the only one who can sense the little bastards. He's using those earpieces of yours to coordinate our troops wherever the monsters show up."

More questions answered. Great. "Where's Cassiel? I'm going to go help her."

"The fuck you are." A voice just to my side makes me jerk up in surprise. Alpha's standing there, staring at me. "I'll take care of this. _This time_. Don't go rushing off just because you have a death wish."

I nod at him. "I won't. Sorry."

Alpha growls as he looks toward the battlefield. "Fucking hell. They've already made it here. I'm going to have to take drastic measures."

_Drastic measures. I don't know if I like the sound of that._
