Part 282a - Sparring

Amelia punches me so hard I can almost feel it through my suit's dampeners. Damned impressive, when you consider this suit should be powerful enough to protect the occupant from a supernova at point blank range.

"Amelia, stop! I don't want to fight you, this isn't how friends treat each other!" I implore for her to stop but she just punches me again, this one knocking me back just a little bit further.

"PILOT JASON HIRO, SUIT INTEGRITY IS AT 95%. CHEST INTEGRITY IS DOWN TO 76% AND-"

Centurion is cut off as another punch knocks me flat on my back. "CORRECTION, CHEST INTEGRITY IS AT 72% AND DROPPING!"

Great, the suit has a sense of humor. Amelia grins widely as she pauses to laugh at me sitting on the ground. "Wow, you're the first Hero to ever take my punches so easily! Most of the other ones died or got splooshed by the third one! But you're a terrible fighter, one of the worst."

She starts to say something else, but then a look of irritation passes over her face. "Yes, I know, alright? Calm down, Levvy, I can just heal my hands up later. Stop being so overprotective."

Once again, she seems to be speaking to someone I can't see. Was it the _thing_ that spoke to me when I first accidentally entered her mind? What the heck is a Levvy, anyway?

Jumping up to my feet, I pop my visor open as I scowl at her. "If you won't listen to reason, I'm going to get serious now. I nearly killed Satan, and I can do the same to you, too."

This wasn't a lie. I had gotten a good grasp on her strength. She was maybe the same level or a bit stronger, physically, than Samantha. Her energy levels were also much higher than mine, and seemed to come from a terrible dark source I couldn't quite identify, but she fought fairly straight-forwardly, and her worst quality was her arrogance. She thought she was invincible and that I was a simpleton. She didn't even know what my abilities were yet, nor what my suit could do. It would be close, but I had a feeling that I could incapacitate her fairly easily, even without using my wordsmithing. It just wasn't my goal, and I wanted to avoid it if possible.

She glared at me as I spoke. "You're so full of it! I'll tell you what, give it your best shot! I'm not being serious, either! I'm just having fun!"

Saying that, she quickly made some hand gestures and I felt a disturbing amount of energy leave her body, as... she... what the heck?!

A shadow appeared to her side, a humanoid form, totally encased in blackness. It almost looked like something I had seen before, but it was hard to tell.

"Let's see how you deal with two on one!" Amelia didn't even give me a moment to respond as she rushed at me, reaching down and grabbing my ankle in the blink of an eye to yank me off my feet. The shadow behind her began to make various hand motions as I realized that most of her former spiritual energy was condensed inside of it. It was almost as if she had created a second form of herself that cast magic, while she relied on brute strength.

A mage and fighter combo?! Unfair!

A bluish form of energy began to crackle in front of the shadow as I reached my hand up towards it, my body still held up by Amelia. My visor shut as I used a word spell. " **Forcefield!** "

As I spoke and a blue field appeared in front of me, the shadow figure fired off a bolt of lightning, the energy rating registering on my suit's HUD as more energy than the output of all of Earth's industrialized nations combined. Smashing through my shield, I was blasted away from Amelia's hand with the force of the meteor that killed the dinosaurs, as I was temporarily deafened by the sound of the shockwave.

Flying away from Amelia at a frightening speed, I plowed through several buildings in the heart of Paris, careening into the Seine River, which flowed all the way through the city.

Landing with a sizzling splash, my suit's internal systems faltered for a few moments as they rebooted. The energy that had surged into the suit was temporarily too much for it to handle. When it came back online, I noted with alarm that the front of my suit had heated to almost a thousand degrees centigrade. Landing in the river had caused a massive steam explosion, the fog that kicked up obscuring my vision for a half-mile in every direction.

Activating my suit's thrusters, I glanced at my internal specs. Chest integrity was down to a terrifying 12%! One more hit like that would completely destroy my body.

Luckily, I came prepared. Aiming my mind at one of the many hypersuits I kept in my dimensional storage, I spoke a word. " **Swap.** "

Instantly, my vision shifted as the new suit appeared on myself, the sensors and power activating a moment later as they booted. Curiously, I noticed that my HUD looked different from the one on the previous suit, and Harold _had_ mentioned some tweaks for the new suits... I'd have to test them later.

This suit came equipped with new and highly advanced anti-gravity systems, which I activated a moment later.

Flying up and out of the lake, I flew up until I was out of the steam cover. Looking below, I used my innate magical energy sensing abilities to search for Amelia. Sure enough, there she was, standing on the edge of the riverbank, looking down into the fog. She... couldn't see me?

Perfect. She wants to play rough? I can do rough.

"Centurion, activate Lightning Mode."

A moment passed before Centurion spoke. "ERROR. THAT SYSTEM IS NOT INSTALLED IN THIS HYPERSUIT. HOWEVER, PHASING MODE IS A PART OF THIS SUIT'S CAPABILITIES. WOULD YOU LIKE TO ACTIVATE PHASING MODE?"

Phase Mode? Guess that's what I've got to use. "Yes. Activate it."

"AFFIRMATIVE, PILOT JASON HIRO. PHASE MODE ACTIVATING... NOW. HYPER DEFENSES ARE ONLINE."

Hyper defenses? Damn, that sounds cool. Wonder what they do? I should probably read the manuals on these suits more carefully.

I'm not invisible, but the suit's abilities aren't the only way for me to accomplish that.

" **Invisibility. Reptile. Undetectable. Silent.** "

I felt my skin change composition as my body changed itself into a cold-blooded form. Slapping my hands together, I noted that they in fact did not make a single noise. Perfect.

As Amelia gaped down at the fog, it began to dissipate as she realized I was no longer standing there. Lightly landing behind her I reared back to aim a punch at her head.

"I'm right here."

She quickly turned back to see where my voice had come from, and I punched her right in the face at that very moment. The impact made a satisfying CRUNCH as she smashed backwards, plowing into the lake and causing a large tidal wave to blast outwards in every direction.

I cringed at myself for hitting her so hard. Making her an enemy was not what I wanted, as I might need her for insurance against Satan. Still, she told me to deliver a punch, and deliver I did. Can't fault a guy for listening.

As the water settled, she blasted upwards, sailing into the air as she hovered on high, scanning everywhere for my presence. Wiping her face off, she grimaced as thin rivulets of blood came from her nose. Did I break her nose?

The answer seemed to be yes, as she healed herself and the bleeding stopped a moment later. "Where are you?! Too afraid to take me head on? I bet you're so scared you can only fight from the shadows! Loser! Wimp!! Meanie!!!"

She started tossing insults my way, and to be honest, I felt a bit miffed. Leave it to a woman to tell you exactly what she wants you to do, and when you do it, she gets pissed off. Then again, I suppose anybody would be mad at getting sucker punched.

Sighing deeply, I once again wordsmithed. " **Human. Visible. Audible. Detectable.** "

As my body came into focus, her eyes snapped over to my position. "There you are! What kind of coward hits a girl when she isn't even looking?!" She seemed genuinely pissed off, not at the pain, but more than I had managed to pull a fast one on her. She's... such a child.

"It's over. I punched you in the face. I won, so honor your side of the deal."

She stonily glared at me for a few moments, and I could almost imagine puffs of smoke leaking out of her ears. "F-fine! But you better watch your back, Hero! I hate it when guys hit on me!"

I stifled a grimace. _She makes puns as terrible as that? What the hell?_

As she delicately floated down, I felt her energy return to her body, as the shadow from earlier transferred itself back into her body. Landing gently on the ground, she glared at me with a pouting look on her face. "How did you do that? I can't get a handle on what your abilities are! You have so many of them!"

I shrugged. "Once you acknowledge that we are truly friends, I would be happy to tell you all about them."

Her expression changed into one of boredom after a few moments. "You're a weird one, Hero. Ehh, 'Hero', that's kind of a bland name. What should I call you?"

I smile at her from under my mask. "My name is Jason. Perhaps we can take hold of that temper of yours and put those abilities to some good use."

She shook her head quickly. "No thanks! I am not interested, not one bit! And I won't be your friend, period! Besides, you could just tell me what your abilities are right now without me being your friend, right?"

I shook my head as I turned my back to her and sighed deeply, pretending to look at the ruined buildings she has smashed me through. As my mouth visor closed, I quickly spoke a single word. " **Contract.** "

A rolled up set of papers appeared in my right hand as I opened my visor again and turned back to her. "Well, alright. You win. I'll tell you about my abilities."

Her eyes lit up immediately. "Really? For reals?!"

"Yes. All you have to do is sign this magical contract saying that you will never ever attack me, harm me, or try to harm me ever for the rest of our lives. What do you say, is it a deal?"

Pulling out the papers, I let the first one unfurl. A long list of terms and conditions rolled down as Amelia gaped at all the words. "No way! Eww, gross! I'm not signing some stupid contract just to learn about your abilities!...In fact, forget it. Hmph. I'll just learn about them myself."

She turned up her nose at me and grinned evilly, but in truth I was lying. This was the same contract I had Gregory sign in the past, it even had his signature on the final page. I hadn't written a new one for Amelia, and even if I did, I really wouldn't want her knowing about my powers at all. Simply too dangerous.

"Ah, well, what a shame. I don't know if you're smart enough to figure my abilities out, so I guess you'll just never know."

"What?! I'm not a dummy! Don't insult me like that!"

As she fumed and raged, I felt a tight smile spread across my mouth. It used to be like this all the time... before I found out about the real Amelia. If only it could go back to the way it was when we first met... that would be nice.

That would be... ideal.

But it never will.
