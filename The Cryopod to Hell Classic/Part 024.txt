Part 024

We exited the El-Dorado room, leaving behind mountains of gold and jewels, and headed up the long hallway. It no longer curved, now it was straight. Exiting it, we found ourselves outside that same familiar colossal door I had first seen what felt like an eternity ago. The door was covered in moonspeak now, which had changed since the last time I came through here. However, thanks to Solomon's Crown, I was able to read them.

_None shall enter._

Strange. Did this crown give me knowledge of languages as well? That would be fortunate if it did. This crown was like a cheat code, it gave me too much mental prowess.

We continued walking away from the doors. _None shall enter_, huh? I'll have to come back to that room in the future for sure.

Walking down the hallway, Amelia was happily chatting with Blaarjiim as if I didn't even exist. "It was so boring being locked up inside that statue! I'm glad you stayed with me though, and I was never bored since we could talk all day long!"

"True, but being able to get out and stretch must have felt a great deal better." Blaarjiim seemed cheerful. Odd, when I first met him, he was always mad, always going on about being the Lord of Darkness, but now that Amelia was here, he was pretty chill. He seemed like a nice enough deity, or whatever he was.

At the end of the hall we reached the door that looked like the one I came from. It was hard to tell with the shifting halls, but this was probably it. However, the door was shut, and it opened inwards, towards me. It also had no handles.

"Should I just break it down again?" I asked nobody in particular. I instantly decided that was a horrible idea. Then I had a new idea.

Putting my palm outwards, I aimed it at the door.

" **Pull.** "

Instead of blasting out of me, energy was sucked in from all around me, making the door rattle and then pull outwards. It also pulled in many other things around me, including Amelia, who was lifted up off the ground and flew at me.

"Ahhh!" she let out a little yelp and smacked me square in the body, knocking both of us on the ground in a heap. "A little WARNING next time!"

I grimaced. "Sorry. I'm not used to this yet."

"Yeah! No kidding." Getting up, she brushed herself off, wiping her skirt down and fanning it to look more modest.

Then we turned to look inside the room I'd just opened.

It was empty.

I felt disappointed. Perhaps the scary statue monster from before wasn't here. We entered the room, looking around. Amelia pointed at the ceiling. "Look. A hole." I jerked my head up. It was the same square hole I remembered seeing when I came here. This was the same room.

The floor was no longer covered in moonspeak though. Neither were the walls. Why were they gone? What made them come and go? Was it my presence? We walked further into the room and examined the door leading to -what I presumed was- my original Cryopod room.

I put my hand up to cast the 'pull' magic again.

At that moment, the door behind us slammed shut with incredible force.

" **So. You've returned.** "
