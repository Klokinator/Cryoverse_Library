Part 474c: Axis Mundi

Samantha and I follow Helen and Cassiel as the older woman walks around Axis Mundi, a cheekily named dimension that the hundred or so remaining angels live in. She points out each angel we come across and has Cassiel shake their hands. "This man is Roger, he used to be an architect before he died, and this pretty lady over here is Petra, she was a diplomat, and over here is..."

Cassiel, bless her heart, smiles and says hello to each new angel, though it's apparent to everyone besides Helen that she hasn't a chance of remembering all their names. While she introduces herself, Samantha and I gaze around at the architecture. It's a self-contained bubble dimension, completely flat, and stretches out into the distance for miles around. Every angel has a mansion they live in, and each one has a distinct style. Some of them are Japanese inspired, others remind me of Roman architecture, and there are tons of space-age buildings I don't recognize at all. As Helen explained earlier, the angels moved here so the demons and Volgrim wouldn't find them, and they're mostly cut off from the outside universe.

Given that angels can only survive so long off their energy, I wasn't sure how they survived all this time, but it turns out Jason Alpha gave them a single infusion of all of his energy at once several million years ago, and that alone has given them more than enough. They haven't used even half of it over that period, and he regenerated it after one night of sleep anyway. Wordsmiths are overpowered.

Samantha slows to a stop, allowing Cassiel and Helen to keep walking, but she lightly pinches my long sleeves. "Hey, Jason. All the angels are staring at me."

I glance at her ridiculously exposed outfit, then to the pure white robes most of them are wearing, except for one angel who's wearing Roman legionnaire armor. Apparently, he's the odd one of the bunch, since he was an avid Renaissance Faire attendee and loves to dress up, even after his death. At least the angel wings kinda make him look like a Valkyrie.

"I can't imagine why, Samantha," I snort sarcastically, "it's probably been a long time since they saw someone openly baring that much skin."

"That's not it," she says, and her tail jerks around like a worried dog's. Occasionally it pauses and twitches. "It's because I'm a demon. Everyone smiles at me, but I think they're disguising their hatred."

"Possibly. As Helen said, Satan and millions of demons attacked and killed the angels during their final war, only sparing these remaining stragglers after killing off the purebloods and forcing Gabriel to sacrifice himself. Considering you're Satan's ex-girlfriend..."

Samantha bows her head. "I know. I just let him do it. I didn't know he planned such an awful act, but by the time I found out, it was far too late."

"It's not your fault. Satan seems like..." I pause, realizing that badmouthing someone she once loved will probably twist up her insides. "...he was... an _interesting_ fellow."

"He was a piece of shit. I don't know why I stuck with him as long as I did, nor what I saw in him." Samantha states her opinion bluntly, rendering me speechless. "Maybe I was just young and naive. I lost that excuse after four thousand years of his plotting and scheming, though. Maybe I assumed that as long as he won, it was proof that his ideals were correct."

I reach around her back and grip her shoulder, pulling her close to me. "Everyone makes mistakes. You just have to accept them and try to move on."

Sam leans her head back against my arm. "I don't think I deserve that though, Jason. These angels became refugees because I never stood up to Satan. I was the second most powerful of the demons, and for years I was the only one who had the power to stand up to him, but I didn't. Directly or indirectly, it's my fault they're in this situation."

"Yeah, well, Jason Alpha failed to defeat the demons, so he's at fault too." I stop talking for a moment to notice the irony of criticizing another version of myself. What if I had lived his life? Wouldn't I have made the same mistakes? "Hell, time travel can't solve all the issues of the multiverse, so I don't think you should feel responsible for Satan's shitty decisions."

"I guess," Samantha mutters noncommittally.

Before I can question her about why she keeps beating herself up, Cassiel and Helen appear before us, having wandered back after noticing we disappeared. "Ooh, what are you two up to?" Cassiel winks and smiles, but it deflates when she sees the look on Samantha's face. "Oh."

"It's not a big deal," I say, "Sam just feels a little uncomfortable because of how all the angels are staring at her."

Helen nods. "Well, your choice of clothing certainly stands out. If you think it's because you're a demon and they hate you, I'd say that's quite inaccurate." Apparently, the old woman is more perceptive than she lets on.

"Huh?" Samantha pulls her head back and gazes at Helen quizzically. "Why would that be inaccurate?"

"It's war, sweetheart. Bad things happen. People die. All of the angels here were humans, turned back into angels. Heaven, as great as it might seem, was bland, boring, and inescapable. Human converts were expected to fight on the frontlines and die first, should war break out. Purebloods treated half-bloods as their inferiors, even if they did smile and act pleasantly to our faces. Only one or two purebloods were considered good and honest, but compared to the comfortable existences we lead now, Heaven was a torturous place." Helen taps her lip. "I lived in Heaven for over fifty years before the man you know as Jason Alpha set me free. I was able to speak with my husband again, and I even got to live aboard a spaceship. It was great fun! However, other converts were not as lucky as me."

Samantha eyes the older woman carefully. "Are you saying many of the angels here are _glad_ that Satan nearly wiped out their brethren?"

"Not glad, but also not angry. Death is a regretful thing. Freedom from bondage is a joyous thing. I'd say most are happy for the change in scenery, sad for the price we paid. Several of the converts here lived in Heaven for two thousand years, having been reformed during the generation after Michael's sacrifice. They made many, many friends over those years, only to have the overwhelming majority wiped away in an instant. Tens of millions of years can dull the heartbreak, however. I would say most are content with how things have ended up, and that's that."

"I see." For the first time today, Samantha smiles faintly. "I'm glad some good came of the war, however little it may be."

"In times of darkness, a glimpse of light can shine like the midday sun. The fact everyone gets to meet an angel the demons captured all this time, yet who also survived, has bolstered their morale dramatically. Even Jeremiah is smiling, and he's always a grumpy old grouch!" She points over at an angel whose boyish figure makes him look twelve years old. He dribbles a basketball in a nearby court and rolls his eyes when he sees us staring. Nobody else is playing, but he's having fun shooting some hoops.

"Grumpy... old man?" Samantha's face twists in disbelief. "He looks like a kid."

"He was just a boy living in the Roman Empire when a bunch of barbarians tore him to pieces. Took him two days to die. Angels don't usually change appearance, no matter how much time passes. He's much older than me!" Helen laughs. "Of course, so are you, but age is arbitrary after a certain point. We call him 'grumpy old man' just to rile him up. If you play along with the joke, it's much funnier."

Samantha chuckles. "Heh, I see your point." She and I both ignore the bit about the kid being torn to pieces since it's too awful to imagine. "Maybe I should say hi to some of the angels here. I'm probably just a little shy."

Cassiel's wings twitch, and she quickly steps forward and yanks Samantha away from me, giving her a big hug. "Oh, you! You're the least shy girl I know!"

"Jeez, Cass! You're embarrassing me!" Samantha protests weakly, but she doesn't actually mind.

Minutes pass. Soon we arrive at a building Helen dubs the 'Administrative Facility'. If there is a Creator of this universe, he's _terrible_ at naming things. "I have a few friends among the demons; ones who know of the Angels' existence but keep silent, never telling anyone about us. They feed me gossip and tales of what's going on, but they aren't very high up. I heard rumors that the humans were staging a comeback and it was causing the Emperors many problems, but I never imagined the cause would be another Jason."

We make our way up the chiseled marble steps, and I marvel at how it looks like a courthouse from the time period I originated. Ten giant pillars lead up to an overhang above the entrance, and a plaque affixed to the protrusion reads ' _In Jason We Trust_ '.

Cute.

Helen notices where my gaze is directed. "Just to be clear, we put that up, not Jason Alpha. He's fairly humble, despite his power. Alpha thought it was a little tacky."

_Well, that's another thing we share in common._

I decide to ask the question on my mind. "Why the name 'Jason Alpha' anyway? If he's so humble, the name kinda says the opposite."

"The clone naming system was initially convoluted and changed several times. We needed a direct way to identify the primary Seed for each Wordsmith, so he came up with the Term 'alpha' since it's the first letter in the Ancient Greek alphabet. Likewise, Satan Prime is considered the progenitor of his clones."

"If he's the original Jason, then what happens if he dies? Do the clones die as well?"

Helen shrugs noncommittally. "Nobody knows. We won't know unless it happens, and the goal is to obviously make sure it doesn't. I'm 99% sure they won't, just based on events involving other clones, but it's tough to say. Have you ever cloned yourself before?"

Several memories bubble up. "Yes."

A moment of awkward silence follows, and Cassiel clears her throat. "So, um, Helen! Why are we coming here anyway?"

"To find out if anyone has intercepted news about the Volgrim. If their fleet can't hold off the Kolvaxians, we'll need to know."

"Why not just pop over to the Volgrim headquarters and say hello?" I ask. "You think they'll attack us on site or something?"

"One does not simply walk into the Council of Elders," Helen replies. "Especially not you. While they likely know of your Wordsmithing thanks to communications with the demons, no Volgrim is aware of the past or its history with relation to Jason Alpha."

"I don't see why that's a problem."

She sighs. "The Volgrim are a proud race. The reason Jason Alpha allowed them to eventually rule the galaxy outside of the Labyrinth is because he saw a certain quality in them; one of fairness. They treat subordinates equally, including the demons and humans. The Energy Wars were caused entirely by misunderstandings, not because they hated humans. Well, not at first. To be fair, destroying their homeworld may have annoyed them, just a teensy bit!" Helen smirks. "Anyway, while they are fair, we believe this is only due to them forgetting the cause of their homeworld's destruction."

I try not to look too bored. "Uh huh. And what's that?"

"Jason Alpha, of course. He destroyed Volgarius. Eradicated the entire star system, and killed trillions of Volgrim in an instant. They only managed to escape thanks to a space station several lightyears away that we didn't know about."

My jaw drops. "What? He did that? That's... dark. Genocidal. Evil, even." My hands shake slightly as another thought enters my mind. _He and I are very similar. Would I commit genocide as well? Is that something I'm capable of?_

Samantha butts in. "Hold on, how did he destroy the planet? I'm... I'm having memories come back to me, all of a sudden."

Helen shakes her head slowly. "He created a meteor made entirely of iron, then reinforced it with Wordsmithing beyond any natural limits, and flung it at warp speed toward their planet. The resulting catastrophe obliterated the planet, the star, and every other speck of matter in the system."

A minute passes as Samantha and I gaze at each other, then Cassiel, then back to Helen, trying to wrap our mind around the idea. Samantha is the one to break the silence. "Th-the... the Kolvaxians... Jason, don't you remember? Beatrix and Ben Brown... they told us that the Kolvaxians came pouring through a wormhole- a tear in space."

Cassiel's eyes widen. "The one at Volgarius Prime?! Then, doesn't that mean Jason Alpha created the tear?"

Helen opens her mouth to speak, thinks better of it, closes it, then opens it again. "Ah- that's... are you certain? I suppose that would explain how the Kolvaxians arrived. In fact, that's the most disturbing news I've heard in years. Alpha will be furious when he learns this news... mostly at himself."

"Yeah. He should be," Samantha says, tossing her hair back. "He not only committed genocide, he tore a goddamn hole in space with his stunt. Monsters are invading because of his actions."

I know it isn't logical, but Samantha's words stab right into my heart. _That could be me she's talking about,_ my brain whispers. _You can't say for sure you'd never do the same thing were you in his shoes._

"The pieces are falling into place," Helen grumbles. "And now I think I see how the Kolvaxians arrived here. Saint Peter bless my soul, I think they're pouring in from the K universe."

"Can I seal the wormhole?" I offer, but Helen shrugs.

"Maybe. The problem is that you might be a Wordsmith, but the Kolvaxians are unlike anything you've ever faced. There are Jasons with hundreds of thousands of battles against Kolvaxians who come up short sometimes. Tracking them is difficult, their bodies emit trace amounts of anti-energy, and they adapt to new tactics remarkably fast. To close the hole, you'd have to go there in person. If this hole is the gateway from which they originate, they will guard it fiercely. Plus, I don't know if Wordsmithing or Thoughtsmithing can seal a wormhole. It might be impossible, in which case you'd be..."

Helen trails off, and her skin pales. "You know, there's something about the Kolvaxians that should give you sleepless nights, young Wordsmith."

I'm already a little bit scared, but I keep my expression neutral. "Yeah?"

"They convert humans, demons, Volgrim, anything they come into contact with into other Kolvaxians. We've fought them for ages, and still barely know anything about them, but what we do know is that they're a hivemind. They absorb the knowledge of each individual and add it to their collective. We've gone to great lengths to ensure they never ever get their hands on a Wordsmith. It might spell the end of the Multiverse if they did."

"What, like, they could use Wordsmithing if they took over Jason?" Cassiel's expression contorts into horror. "That's awful! You can't take a risk like that!"

"Yeah," Samantha chimes in. "Even if you knew for sure you could seal the tear, the Kolvaxians might transform you into one of them, and they'd become unstoppable."

Before I can allay their concerns, a buzzing sound rumbles in my ear, transmitting to my mind. The telepathic earrings, apparently. Good thing I built a connector node inside this place when I arrived.

Samantha and Cassiel raise their hands to their ears simultaneously, almost like they're answering a phone. Helen gives us a confused look, but I ignore her and answer the 'call.'

_This is Jason. Is someone trying to use this channel?_

Ben's voice appears in my mind. _You still hanging out with the angels?_

_Yes. Why?_

_Two demons just appeared at our doorstep. They declared they were friendly and demanded they speak to you. Said it was a message from Diablo._

Samantha raises an eyebrow and glances at me. "The heck would Diablo want?"

"Dunno, Sam."

_Ben, detain and restrain them. I'm a little busy right now. I'll talk with them later._

For a moment, I think he's shut off the feed without answering, but his voice appears in my mind, a hint of worry in it. _They say it's about the Kolvaxians. I pressed them for an answer, and they said something about an S.O.S. signal from the Volgrim._

"Oh," I say out loud. "In that case, tell them I'll be right there." I pause, realizing Ben can't hear me talk out loud. _Tell them I'll be right there._

"Helen, two demons just appeared at our doorstep. They have information about the Kolvaxians."

Helen's eyes bulge. "Really? Well, get down there, then- no, actually, take me with you. I want to hear what they have to say."

"I assumed you would." I pause, glancing inside the Administration building. "Maybe we should finish this first."

"Most demons don't know about Kolvaxians," Samantha says. "If two random ones appear saying Diablo sent them, I'm certain they aren't lying. Their information is probably, no offense to Helen, more up to date than anything here."

Helen rolls her eyes. "Sad, but true. My spies among the demons are fairly unreliable. If those demons don't give us anything useful, we can come back."

"I'll make it quick," I remark, as I aim my mind at the ground. " **Waypoint.** Now we can return here once we're done. **Return.** "

The four of us vanish and reappear inside the heart of Hero City, and I activate my earpiece. _Ben, where are you holding them?_

...

A minute later, I teleport again, this time taking us to the border of the city, just inside the portal to the Labyrinth. Ben is just putting anti-energy cuffs onto the demons, both of whom look a little sullen. Immediately, I identify a Shadow-walker, the scummiest of demons, and an odd one dressed in grey robes, with a gold brooch clasped onto his clothes just under his throat. He looks somewhat important and carries an air of authority about him.

Perhaps he's the leader of these two. The information might be reliable!

The robed one spots me and nudges his companion. "Ylvusk! Looky! It's the Hero guy! I told you he exists!!!"

His friend, the shadow-walker sighs, his voice a deep growl. "I thought you said he _didn't_ exist, nutcase."

"Same thing! You're always getting hung up on the icky-sticky details!"

It's at this moment I realize something significant.

These idiots are going to be no help at all.
