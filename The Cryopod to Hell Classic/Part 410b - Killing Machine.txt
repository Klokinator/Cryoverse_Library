Part 410b - Killing Machine

The feeling of the ship's sonic shower washes over me, and I rub my skin slowly to get the blood off. For over an hour I've pummeled my body with waves of sound to wash the smell of death away, but even after scrubbing my skin raw, I barely feel any cleaner than before.

I finally get tired of trying and switch the shower off, wiping myself down with a towel before stepping into a fresh set of clothes Amy left for me on a ledge nearby. The pristine walls of the bathroom, where the metal has been painted white, blend in with my pale skin. I feel as if I haven't seen natural light in years, but Amy told me I had only been in prison for a few days.

A mirror greets me just before I walk out of the bathroom. The scars all over my face and body are already mending up quickly since my natural regeneration allows me to heal any non-fatal injury.

The people I killed could only wish they were so lucky.

I step out of the bathroom and walk several feet down the cramped hallway until I arrive at the ship's cockpit door. When I press the button to open it, as Amy showed me, she jerks her head up and stiffens as I walk in. A nervous smile spreads across her face as she sets a book down in her lap. "Oh! You're done! I... I hope the shower went... well."

Her nervousness makes me feel weird, so I just quickly sit down in the copilot's chair and stare blankly at the dizzying array of dials and buttons before me. I have no idea how anything on this ship works, and don't think I ever will.

"Yes, thank you for the shower." I mumble under my breath and start picking at the blood on my knuckles. No matter how I scrubbed, I couldn't get it off. Even now, it stubbornly sticks to me, a reminder of the pain I just inflicted on other humans.

Amy's eyes wander to my hands and back up to my face, as she seems unable to look at the blood in the same way I am. "Are you doing okay? You really let loose out there."

A beeping sound emits from the control panel, and she leans forward and taps a button to shut it off, all without taking her eyes off me. She's scared of me now, I can tell. "I'm all right."

It took me a while to figure out who she was. She doesn't appear to recognize me at all, but I knew after spending some time with her... she was the woman in the white armor that Master fought in the Labyrinth. He crushed her, and all of the others under her command, so it's probably not right to think she works for Master.

I was disguised as a demon, and so was Master when he fought her. Even so, after the way I killed those guards on Tarus, I think she would be less frightened of me if I looked like a demon.

"You say you're fine, but I didn't ask you to kill all those people." Amy swallows and looks away for a moment to regain her composure. "You... you tore them to pieces. I've never seen such anger and rage."

"They hurt me, and they killed my friend."

Amy nods. "Yes, they did. It was wrong of them. Even though they outnumbered you a hundred to one, they were still outmatched. I understand why you killed them all." She looks up and stares me in the eyes. "Danis was not a man I admired. He and I both believed in justice, but his ideas of justice were a far cry from mine, and the man whom I represent. Even so, I would never wish death upon him, for I believe my ideals are purer than his. Death belongs only to the demons, who are irrefutably evil."

I scratch at the blood on my fist a little more, but it still won't come off. "What do you think makes someone evil?"

Amy is taken aback by my question. "Oh, well, I suppose that would be... committing heinous acts just for the fun of it? Someone who hurts others for personal gain is also evil."

I nod quickly. "The warden was evil, then."

She cringes slightly and leans back in her chair to look out the viewport, to the stars rushing past us. "You've got me there. I still don't think it's ever okay to kill a fellow human, though." She turns to me and scowls. "By the way, could you please quit picking at your hands? You're going to tear your skin to shreds if you keep it up."

Her abrupt change of topic makes me flinch. "I can't get the blood off. It stays no matter how much I scrub."

There's a long silence as I look away from Amy guiltily, but her next words surprise me. "What blood?"

I turn to look at her, and she stares at me quizzically. I hold my hand up to show her my knuckles. "This blood. It's everywhere. It's hard now, and I can't wash the blood from my hands."

She slowly inhales, and her expression softens. "Lora... there's no blood on your knuckles. Are you seeing things?"

I frown and look again. "I... might be."

She shakes her head and pulls herself to a standing position in the small cabin, ducking down, so she doesn't bump the ceiling. "You need to sleep. Come with me."

........................................

_A few hours before._

My body is weakened from being chained up like an animal, but the rage buried in my belly makes the exhaustion fade away. I pull a strength up from inside my body that I didn't know I even possessed, and stand up to face Warden Scullis.

She smiles as her guards aim their weapons at me. "You're going to hold us off, huh? I know you're strong, but a fully outfitted regiment of trained marksmen is a very different situation from catching a few guards unaware. These men and women aren't like the ones you tore apart before. Give up, Lora, and I'll let you live."

My breathing evens out as I stare at her. "You just want chain me up. I... I not let you."

"You won't _let_ me? Did I just hear you correctly?" Scullis slaps her stomach and laughs. "Haha, stupid bitch! You don't have a choice! Take her down!"

The sound of the starship behind me activating its thrusters makes my heart warm. Amy is a good woman, an amazing person. Even if I die here, as long as she escapes, I will be happy.

But I _won't_ die.

Scullis takes a step back, and all of the masked guards fire their weapons in unison. I'll be dead the moment the blasts of energy hit me.

Instead of standing and letting the attacks hit, I dive to the side and roll, using my momentum to jump high up into the air. The sound of guns firing after me and energy shots whizzing past makes my heart pound like drums. I fall and land behind a big rock I spotted before, allowing it to take the attacks for me. Using all my strength, I slam my bare foot into the ground underneath the boulder, then I flip it up into the air before me, and deliver a punch to smash it at the guards.

Horrible screams sound off as the giant rock blows through a dozen of them, killing the bastards instantly.

_Remember to sow discord and fear among your enemies during a battle. This will make them panic and lose control._

Master's words come to me, and I dive once again toward the remaining enemies, keeping a crouched profile as I leap, and using my arms and legs to skitter across the ground like one of Demon Emperor Beelzebub's hellhounds. Even I am surprised by how quickly I close the gap, leaving them no time to group back up.

I jerk my hand down and grab a small rock barely visible under the soft green sticks that coat the ground around me. Using all my strength, I hurl it at the closest guard's head. Their skull, though protected by a helmet made of something fairly durable, explodes into a thousand pieces as the stone passes through, as if their head weren't guarded at all.

"She's a demon!"

"Get away!"

Cries of alarm go out as I smash my fist through one man's chest, then use his gun as a blunt weapon to beat two others to death. I don't have my sword and armor, but I might not even need them!

Suddenly a burst of energy smashes into my back, and I scream in pain and fall to the ground. Someone landed a shot on me, and it hurts like sin!

"Quick, shoot her while she's down! Shoot to kill!" Scullis barks an order, but her voice is further away than it was before. She must have pulled back from the fight, like a coward.

Only then do I realize something. Scullis's original orders must have been to take me alive. The fact she wants me dead now must mean that she sees me as a threat. And... that's because I am!

I slam my hands on the ground and force my body to jump into the air several feet, giving me a quick grasp on my surroundings. Dozens of them are still alive! Master told me never to let people surround me, so I quickly clap my hands in midair, using the wind shockwave to knock me away from them.

The spaceship sounds louder in the distance. It must be getting close to the time where Amy can pick me up!

I drop down and smash into one poor unlucky soul, then back-flip around, using her as a human shield to protect from the energy blasts coming my way. Once she's dead, thanks to her friends, I use her as a blunt weapon and beat several people nearby unconscious, then fling their corpses at others to knock them down.

Several blaster shots keep hitting me, but the more injured I get, the stronger I become. The blasts hurt horribly, but I keep getting angrier each time. I just really want to tear these people to pieces! I hate them! I hate all of them!!

Things blur in my mind as one person being ripped apart melts together with the next. I lose count of how many I've killed. It's only once I hear a voice yelling from the skies that I blink my eyes and look around. Only a few of the guards are left, but they've given up entirely, and are now trying to run away.

"Lora! LORA! Stop, you've won! Get aboard and let's go!"

I look up into the sky and spot Amy's ship much bigger than I first pictured it, flying several feet above. I nod slowly and shake off the feeling of coldness that is chilling me to the bone. I have to escape. Of course, I almost forgot.

A horrible electric pain shoots through me from out of nowhere, and I collapse to the ground, unable to move. My eyes scan around me frantically, but it takes a few seconds before I spot Scullis, now wearing a suit of white armor, her helmet opened up so I can see her face. There's a whip in her hand, something I saw the demons use several times, but this one has sparks popping off it as she spins it around.

"I underestimated you! You're a monster, a real demon, Lora! But if you think you're just going to kill the men and women that work for me, and that you'll escape, you're dead wrong! Not on my watch!"

The whip lashes out, and I try to steel myself, but the crippling pain it inflicts is worse than anything I've felt yet. I can't move a muscle!

"I was saving this punishment for later I'll have you know! We were going to have such fun times together, but you had to ruin it! Now I'll put an end to you myself, and make sure your murderous ways end here and now!"

I try to move my arms just so I can crawl away, but the whip strikes again, blasting across my back and inflicting a horrible pain. It's just like what the demons and Danis used to do to me... horrible! It hurts so much!

Before Scullis can strike me again, I suddenly feel the wind shift as Amy's ship pulls back a hundred feet. A massive gun lowers from its underbelly and aims at Scullis. She pivots around and aims her back at the ship just as it fires a barrage of bullets. The bullets reflect off her armor and fly in hundreds of directions, but none of them hurt her in the least.

Amy keeps the gun firing for several seconds, but eventually it stops firing, and Scullis turns around to look at the ship warily. "I'd stay out of this if I were you! You should be flying out of here! Interceptors are on their way!"

While she yells at Amy, I clench my fist and bite my lip. The paralysis is already wearing off, but if she's inside that armor, then I haven't a chance at killing her. It's just too tough. Master warned me about facing people wearing it, since even he could barely hurt them.

Master...

Wait. There is a way!

I spot a small rock nearby and grab it quickly before pulling myself to my feet. My body feels sluggish, but my ability to move has almost entirely returned. Scullis spins back around to look at me but doesn't see the rock in my hand. "Oh, you managed to stand up? You're a sturdy one."

"With sufficient velocity, even a grain of sand can be deadlier than a bomb."

Scullis stares at me, mouth agape. "W-what did you just say?"

"It is just something my Master told me once. Goodbye, Scullis."

Master enhanced my mental speed, my agility, my strength, and even my accuracy. He forced me to train with throwing weapons for days on end, because, as he put it, _if you get in close, you will tear someone apart. But if they can keep you at range, you will need a backup plan._

He is truly wise.

I flick the stone with pinpoint accuracy. It smashes into the Warden's temple and blows out a chunk of her brains instantly. She remains standing for a second or two before she falls over, flat on her back.

The Warden is dead.

Amy wastes no time letting me relish the kill. "Come on, Lora! We've got less than a minute before we have company!" Her words echo from somewhere inside the ship, but I can't see her at all. I coil my legs and leap up, landing on the outstretched landing platform as she lifts away from the surface.

I finally found my voice... but at what cost?

........................................

Oh man, this is bad. I can't move a muscle, and I feel like my blood is turning into lead. Ben is already over at the console, slapping away at buttons I can't see. Another minute or less, and he'll activate this helmet on my head... once he does that, I'm going to have my memories erased.

My eyes flick around trying to think of anything that will save me. Hazmat suits are hanging on a wall, over fifty feet away. Those won't do me any good. If I could move my body just slightly, then I could fall out of this chair and ditch the helmet. It might slow him down at least.

Think... think! I need to get out of this, but I can't think of a solution fast enough!

_Calm down, Jason. I won't let him turn you into his drone._

Blaarjiim speaks into my mind again, and I twitch in surprise. I whisper so only he can hear me. "Can you help me escape?"

His face appears, and now he looks like his usual self, and not like a black dragon. _Listen carefully. My energy is not the same as yours, the demons, the angels, or ordinary humans. I have my own energy type. It is not being nullified right now by the anti-energy in the room. Do you understand?_

I blink in surprise. There's a humming sound above me as the machine prepares to mold my memories. "No, I don't. What can we do?"

Blaarjiim babbles rapidly at me. _If I merge with your body temporarily, you can kill this human and free yourself. Marie is the one who created anti-energy, and she does not know of my existence._

It clicks in my mind, and I stiffen as I feel my hair standing on end. "Do it!"

Seconds pass. Nothing happens. Blaarjiim's face is a mess of concentration as he puts forth a ton of effort to merge our bodies. For a single second, I feel something tugging at my heart, but it fades away quickly. Blaarjiim stops, and his face is somehow covered in sweat. _I... I cannot merge with you. We are not linked at all. We must share a deeper connection to join with each other._

"So what do we do, then-"

I feel a sudden mind-numbing pain as the Memory Molder activates. Instead of sending me into the familiar white room as it did before, this time there's an audible _pop_ to my right side, and smoke shoots out, making me wince.

Ben looks up from the console at me. "What? It didn't work?"

"It didn't work?" I repeat his words, and Blaarjiim nods in my mind.

_I sent energy into your mind right as I felt it turn on, but our connection is too weak for me to keep doing that._

"Well, do something else then! I can't move!"

Ben notices me whispering and his eyes immediately narrow. "Who are you talking to?"

"I'm talking to myself." I lie, but it's a poor one. He sees right through it.

"Unlikely! Is there someone else in here?" He quickly walks around the console to look at the opposite side of my chair, but when he doesn't spot anyone sitting there, the frustration rages across his face. "Come on! I know you were talking to someone!"

I ignore him and mumble under my breath. "Any time now..."

Blaarjiim's pitch black eyes suddenly shoot open. _I've got it! I will give you a significant portion of my energy all at once. Use your telekinesis to kill this man and flip the kill switch for the anti-energy. It's our best shot!_

"Got it!"

Ben is confused now, so he grabs me by the throat and shoves me back against the chair. "Who are you talking to?! Tell me, goddammit!"

As he does, I feel Blaarjiim's insidious energy enter my body. It's faint, but it makes me feel as if I'm swallowing gasoline, or swimming in a tar pit. It's a sickening feeling, foreign, alien, and altogether unsettling.

Ben's fingers crush my windpipe, but I'm not worried. Something about Blaarjiim's energy makes me think everything is going to be alright.

The pouch at my belt pops open, and a second later, several smooth stones fly out at high speeds, blasting through Ben's gut and chest. Immediately his grip falters, he looks down, and his jaw drops. "Y-you..."

I use my telekinesis to hurl him backward, smashing him onto the floor with brute force. "Get off me!"

I can't move my body at all, but using my telekinesis, I toss the helmet away and hover over to the console. I don't know what buttons to press or what settings to change at first, but a few seconds later, the previous Jason's memories appear in my mind. Wordlessly, I move my arm forward and press the command to disable the anti-energy field.

Just in the nick of time, too. Blaarjiim's energy cuts off, and I fall to the ground painfully. It takes a few more seconds before my body regains some feeling and I can stand up, but when I do, I dust myself off and smile at the man on the floor. He's seconds away from dying. "Heh. You were right, Ben, I did have a friend. Too bad though, you will die alone, here and now."

The life fades from his eyes, but as it does, a smile crosses over his lips. His final expression sends a shiver down my spine.

"He just died... what the hell does he think is so funny?"

A few minutes pass and I manage to relax a little bit and gather my wits about me.

" **Clone.** "

Instantly, a copy of Benjamin appears in front of me. This time, I won't make the same mistakes as I did before. The clone stares ahead blankly, and his soulless gaze creeps me out.

"You have free will. You may do as you please, just as the regular Benjamin would. However, from now on, you will report anything Marie does directly to me."

He blinks his eyes and nods at me. "I can do that."

A thought occurs to me. "Will you answer any question I ask you?"

He nods. "Yes."

"If that's the case, then who are the other Maries? I heard about them when Gyro let something slip that he probably shouldn't have."

"I don't know."

Huh? He's her closest confidante. He has to know. "You're lying."

"I am incapable of telling you any lies. I do not know what other Maries you speak of."

I lick my lips slowly and puzzle away. "Okay, fine. Then what about the other Wordsmiths? Marie mentioned something about me killing a 'fellow Jason'. The way she said it wasn't like she was talking about the one before me, but other Jasons... _in general_. Are there secret clones of me that she has hidden somewhere?"

"I don't know." Ben's voice sounds bored, as if I'm wasting his time and he has a million other things he'd rather be doing.

"Oh, come on! You know something! Tell me, damn it!"

Blaarjiim speaks up. _Jason, your questions are futile. Whatever it is that Marie is hiding from you, she wouldn't even tell her lover. The woman trusts absolutely no-one. More importantly, haven't you forgotten something important?_

"Huh? Like what?"

_Benjamin Brown didn't die. He's going to be reincarnated as an angel, most likely._

Oh shit. I... didn't realize that. In the heat of the moment, I killed him without thinking. If he contacts Marie, he can warn her about what happened. That is not good, not one bit.

" **Locate.** "

Nothing happens as my mind wanders the cosmos. Wherever Ben is, I can't find him at all. Though, come to think of it, how did I know to use a word like Locate? I feel as if I've done it before.

"Blaarjiim, I can't find him. What do I do?"

He sighs. _You're going to need to go to Heaven and kill him before he can talk to Marie. I'm not intimately familiar with how Heaven works, but I know that when humans are reincarnated, they appear at a station where they are registered, and the details of their deaths are logged down. The queue is somewhat long, so it might take a while before he can contact her._

My lips purse of their own volition as I scan the room around me blankly. "Fine. I guess I have to do that, then. But... why couldn't I locate him? Are people in Heaven immune to wordsmithing?"

I decide to answer the question myself, this time by targeting Zeus. " **Locate.** "

A moment later, his face appears in my mind, as well as his exact position. Unfortunately, he's too far away for me to teleport to, though that would expose me as a Wordsmith anyway.

This is all such a pain in the ass! Every person I kill has to be killed twice! What a pain in my goddamn ass!

The clone of Ben turns to me. "Are you fine with me leaving, now?"

"Yes. However, you are not to tell Marie of what transpired today. Oh... there is one thing you could tell me before you go. Why can't I locate the other Benjamin with my Wordsmithing?"

He shrugs. "It could be because now that he's an angel, you've never seen him before and thus cannot locate him. However, the more likely answer is that he is using the anti-energy wrist-communicator he and Marie stashed in Heaven. It is a failsafe in case you were to go rogue and kill either of them. By attaching it to themselves, in much the same way as his anti-energy necklace prevented you from killing him with a single word, this communicator will also allow him to hide from you. Oh, and if he's wearing it, he's probably already contacted Marie."

That last bit stuns me, shaking me to my core. "The other Ben has already contacted her?! You mean I'm too late?!"

"Probably." He scratches his chin. "It doesn't matter, anyway. They got what they wanted from you."

Now I'm confused. "What did they want from me? Wasn't their goal to revert my mind into that of the previous Supreme Commander?"

"No. My predecessor's main goal was to download your memories so he could try and locate the cryopod you came from. Additionally, he was hoping to find out the exact manner in which you killed the previous Supreme Commander. Recovery of the body might allow a clone of him to be created. Reconditioning your mind would have just been a bonus."

The clone stop talking, and my blood stops pumping. I feel dead inside. They're going to find out the previous Supreme Commander is still alive, and being held by Yama. While he probably doesn't have a chance at beating me, I can't be overconfident. I nearly had my mind melted down only a few minutes ago... I'm going to run out of luck eventually.

Marie is beyond dangerous. She needs to be stopped, at any cost.

"Can I go now?" The clone stares at me blankly.

"Well, if the other Benjamin is still alive... you don't serve a point anymore."

He bows his head. "Ah. I thought it might come down to this. I guess you're going to kill me, now."

"I'm weighing my options. Can you help me with information I'll need? If yes, I'll let you live, and you will have to help me out as we go along."

"I don't have much of a choice, now, do I?" Ben's clone stomps his foot and groans. "My life is over."

"Your life? I'm the one in for a world of hurt soon. Come on; we need to get out of here and find Marie."

He nods and falls in line beside me as we head for the hover-shaft. "You're not going to kill her, are you?"

"I am. Don't worry, though. I'll make a subservient clone of her, just for you."

"Nice. You're not such a bad guy after all, Jason."

"I try."
