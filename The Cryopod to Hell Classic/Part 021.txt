Part 021:

Oh god, please don't suck my soul, crazy little demon girl. That kept replaying in my head as the suddenly very evil looking little girl slowly walked towards me, a look of a serial killer on her face with a glint of bloodlust mixed in.

"N-now now! I think there has maybe been a small mix-up?" My legs shifted as I tried to pull myself up to get away from her but my body was still in bad condition.

"Amelia, calm down." Said Blaarjiim. "I was just joking, my dear. The Child of Gaia is not our enemy."

Instantly, the look of Satan left her face and a cheerful smile spread across it. "Okay! If you say so Blaarjiim! Teehee!" She smiled happily and danced over towards me, coming to a sitting position right in front of my splayed out body. "By the way, you look like you're in a lot of pain. Does that, uh, hurt?"

"Yes." I groaned, eager to change the subject. "I can't move anything. I think I broke every bone in my body. I'm probably going to die here."

There was yet another uncomfortable silence as both orbs and the little girl narrowed their eyes and stared at me. I didn't like the feeling. "W-what? Did I say something stupid?"

"Yes..." Hoarhiim replied. "You have the infinite power of a supreme being inside your body and the wisdom of an ancient king pouring through your mind, but you're still surprisingly stupid sometimes."

I blushed, feeling slightly embarrassed, and then realized what he meant.

"Oh, uh, right." Carefully raising my right wrist, I aimed my palm slowly towards my body.

"**Heal**".

I instantly felt 30% better. My broken bones weren't fixed much, but the pain eased and I was able to take a much deeper breath. Steeling myself again, I tried again, this time with more force.

"**HEAL!**"

The shock of the healing was actually more painful than the initial impact into the pillars. My bones violently mended themselves, jamming together at a maniacally fast pace, my cuts and bruises instantly disappearing. I jumped to my feet and suddenly felt very weak, falling forward and missing Amelia by inches.

"Ahh... what the hell... why am I so... weak? I can't move at all even though I'm healed." Looking up at Amelia I noticed she was rolling her eyes.

"Duh, stupid. If you use all your spiritual energy to heal yourself, you'll be lucky if you don't die immediately afterwards. You're very brash."

A thought crossed my mind. "Wait, spiritual energy? That's really what that is?"

Hoarhiim nodded. "That is correct. You have a most interesting way of using my power. You're the first being to have gained my abilities that uses words to formulate magical concepts. Most others reach inside the Thought Energy to pull out their desired attack. You even somehow make the force of your words somehow amplify the effect. Whatever it is that you're doing though, it seems to have some serious side effects."

Blaarjiim looked my exhausted body up and down. "It's interesting, this one doesn't tap inside the limitless power of thought energy, but instead pulls from within themselves. It allows for a more explosive amount of power, but the amount of energy available is much smaller and more finite, and it seems to drain from the Child of Gaia's own body."

"I concur." said Hoarhiim in the affirmative. "A very unique ability, with more power able to be used at once, but also much more risk to their life. Such an interesting child."

Their words didn't escape me. Mentally, I was perfectly fine. Despite having practically no energy or ability to move inside my body, my mind was fully awake and seemingly boundless in energy. It would seem that I was in some fantastical place where the laws of physics were either nonexistant, or significantly different. Nevertheless, the concept of energy conservation seemed to exist. To give to one thing, you must take from another.

"Say, this thought energy you guys keep mentioning, what is it? How do I tap into it?"

Amelia smiled. "You can't!"

Helpful. Thanks a lot for that truly detailed and concise answer. "I can't? Why not?"

"We're not sure." said Blaarjiim. "You appear to be an aberration. Most beings pull from the Thought Energy almost instinctually, you completely bypass it. You would appear to be cut off from it."

I grimaced. Unlimited power was not within my grasp. Still though... "But you said my power was more explosive? So that means I have a much higher possible amount of power, but also a much lower minimum?"

"Yes, your power is more wild. It's hard to get a good grasp on. You are an interesting creature, to be sure." Blaarjiim projected a mental image of stroking his chin.

We chatted for a few minutes more and after Amelia propped my numb body up against the wall, an obvious question crossed my mind. "Hey, Amelia, I just realized that I have no idea where you came from. Were you mixed in with those monsters from earlier?"

Amelia shook her head and cracked a big smile. "Nope! I was trapped inside the statue, but you freed me when you destroyed my container!"

I looked past her and out the gaping door. The statue was in pieces on the ground, shattered into a thousand fragments by the impact of the massive golden doors.

What the hell? She was inside a statue? What the fuck is up with this place and statues? Why are they all so freaky? Next statue I see, I'm going to talk to it like it's a real person.

Looking my own body up and down, I turned my head towards Hoarhiim. "Well... what do we do now then?"

Hoarhiim raised his head to the ceiling thoughtfully. "I have an idea."
