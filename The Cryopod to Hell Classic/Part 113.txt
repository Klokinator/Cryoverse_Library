Part 113:

Cody staggered back as he saw the Grez running around the curve ahead of him. "Shit! Grez! Clone B21, attack at will!"

Clone B21, staring ahead lifelessly as always, suddenly jerked into motion, silently running up to the front of the group.

"Slow!"

The clone cast a spell, and then a split second later Cody thought he heard the clone say something else that sounded like "Speed" but he couldn't be sure.

Ahead of him, something astonishing happened. The clone started moving around at close to the speed of sound, making thunderous noise as he blasted around at speeds Cody couldn't even keep track of.

In an instant, 5 Grez died before Cody could register their bodies taking hits. Icicles formed out of midair, flying into their squishy neckline (A key weak spot noted by autopsies) and impaling them into the ground.

There were 5 explosions within two seconds, the members of his marines group were feeling dazed watching it. They had heard rumors of the power of Slayer, but this was his autonomous _clone_. How much more powerful would the main body be?

And as they took a moment to think for themselves _this is too easy_, the clone suddenly came to a stop and smashed into the ground in front of them, his body crumpling up into a terrifyingly broken resting position.

The Grez had halted their advance as they... waited? They stared ahead blankly at the group of humans. Only ten Grez left, the other 15 had been killed by the clone. But that was still a lot of Grez.

What happened to the clone?! Cody spared a glance from the Grez to the body smashed into a broken mess just twenty feet away. Had something hit it? He didn't know much about Slayer's actual abilities other than that he was a 'wordsmith' so it might be anything at all. Maybe he moved too fast and tripped on a rock?!

Cody ignored those thoughts. The Grez were still here. Just as he was about to issue an order to his men, the _Slayer himself_ appeared right next to the corpse of his clone. The Slayer completely ignored the broken body next to him, raised a hand up and said a single word.

" **Dissolve!** "

Instantly the Grez in front of him wordlessly turned into puddles of blood and bone, quickly covering the floor around them in their disgusting cocktail of internal body parts. A smell erupted from their liquified corpses and blasted out all around them, resembling the smell of every kind of rotten food known to mankind mixed with horrible smells involving rotting body parts. Three men behind Cody instantly fell down to their knees, heaving on the floor.

Cody and most of the others barely managed to hold the contents of their stomachs, and even the Slayer was visibly sickened by it. The Slayer quickly jerked his hand up and yelled another word. " **Deodorize!** "

The smell vanished just as quickly as it came, to everyone's highest relief. After a few minutes of soul searching and stuffing his inner contents back down where they came with his mind, Cody spoke to the Slayer.

"Slayer, you came at the perfect time. It was about to get hairy here. How did you know to come?"

Slayer stared ahead blankly, not looking at me or anything else, which made sense given his blindness. "My clone died. When they die, I can sense their death. It feels like a bee stinging my brain... hard to put into words. I just transfer over to their corpse and see what happened."

He bent down and placed his hand on various areas of his clone's body. "I used to be able to follow my clone's actions in real time, but there's too many of them now. I've lost count. I only know when they die anymore because of the mental feedback I receive. All the constant thoughts streaming into my head made me have to silence them."

He stood up again. "This isn't good. This clone died because of overexertion. He used 'slow' which puts a tremendous stress on the body, and then used 'speed' which increases the stress a hundredfold. By moving around at high speed and draining his energy even faster with spells, he destroyed his own internal body with G-forces. I never use Slow and Speed together unless I'm wearing the crown so at least I can perceive damage to my body."

Slayer rubbed his head while he spoke. After some time, he rose to his feet and aimed his hand at the body. " _Reintegrate._ "

The clone instantly disappeared, it vanished so fast the men and I almost thought it had never even been there. Slayer rubbed his closed eyes with his fingers, and then summoned another clone.

"Slayer, are you doing okay?" Saul stepped forward and spoke to the Slayer, a look of concern on his face.

Slayer shrugged. "Yeah, I'm fine I guess. Just... tired these days. Very tired. Try not to let this one die, alright? I'm very busy in the capital these days."

Cody nodded. "Sorry that you had to come here personally, Slayer. But we're thankful for your abilities. You've given us a new hope for the future. For humanity."

Slayer nodded slowly as he stared ahead blankly. "Yeah... thanks, Lieutenant Cody. You keep doing what you need to. We have a species to save."

And with that, Slayer recalled himself to the castle, disappearing in the blink of an eye. I looked over at the new clone. They resemble him perfectly in appearance, wearing the exact clothes he wore when he summoned them, but the empty and robotic personality breaks the uncanny valley illusion.

"Alright, clone, let's go." I paused. I don't have a name for this clone. Slayer forgot to name it before he left. Right, ah, your name will be Clone B-" I paused. It just seemed wrong to give the clone a designation ID. This was a human in front of me, even if it didn't act like one. "...Your new designation will be Hope. You and your kind are the hope of our species, so I think it's only fitting that you have a name befitting that station."

The clone stared ahead vacantly. But... for the most fleeting moment of time, I could swear that I saw a hint of a smile pass the corner of his lips.

"Alright, boys, and Samantha. Let's head out."
