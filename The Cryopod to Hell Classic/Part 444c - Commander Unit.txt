Part 444c - Commander Unit

It's the shiver down my spine that gives his sudden appearance away, long before I turn around and spot the killer robot. I rub my arms just as my eyes meet Ionis's single, beady red diode. Samantha and Cassiel turn around only a second later and spot him too.

"Uh... Ionis? What are you doing here? Weren't you in the cargo hold when I left with the girls?"

I'm surprised he managed to find us. The Labyrinth is a maze of twisting corridors, shifting walls and hallways that move around dynamically. Only an evil genius could have made a tunnel network that outdoes a million stacked together Rubik's Cubes at boggling the mind.

Ionis swivels his eye from each of the girls to me, and only now do I realize he's cradling that damn duck in his arms.

_THIS SENTINEL UNIT'S HIGHEST PRIORITY IS PROTECTING THE FEMALE ANGEL-TYPE ORGANIC. ONCE YOUR ABSENCE WAS DISCOVERED, THIS SENTINEL IMMEDIATELY MOVED TO LOCATE YOU._

Samantha nudges me. "What's Stinky saying?"

_She still calls him that?_ "He followed us to protect Cassiel. I guess he brought her duck, too."

Cassiel _squees_ and hops giddily as she takes Howard away from Ionis. "I was worried that OMEN was going to forget to feed him, but now it won't be a problem! Thanks, Ionis, you're so sweet!"

My hands slowly rub together as I eye the giant death-machine down. "Yeah, Ionis, _thanks_ for bringing the duck. How'd you find us, anyway?"

_I LOCATED YOUR UNIQUE ENERGY SIGNATURES AND RAPIDLY TRAVELED TO THIS LOCATION VIA USAGE OF MY INCINERATOR BEAM._

"Incinerator beam?" It takes me a few seconds, but I suddenly realize what he means. Pulling up my mental map, I'm stunned to see a giant hole straight from the portal to Voldarius Prime straight to our current location. When I look past the robot, I spot a gaping hole sliced into the wall at the end of the tunnel. "Holy hell."

Samantha sees what I'm looking at and gets the picture before I can even explain. "Did he really cut a path directly here? Satan would be pissed if he found out. The Labyrinth is ruined." A pause as she taps her lip. "Well, the Labyrinth will repair the hole given time. It'll probably take a day or two at most. I'm overexaggerating, as usual."

Howard _warks_ and Cassiel looks more thoughtful than usual. "The Labyrinth repairs itself? How does that work?"

"Magic." Samantha chuckles. "It feeds off trace amounts of the energy of its inhabitants and slowly expands over time, while also repairing any damage it suffers. It's much more versatile than his first Labyrinth. Easier to hide from invaders, too."

"Because of the shifting hallways?" I give her a look that says _yes, I know about those._

"Mhm."

She doesn't say anything else, but I get the picture. Satan built this Labyrinth. No wonder it's so annoying. Even dead, he's a nuisance.

After a few moments, I turn back to Ionis. "Well, I guess if you're here, you're here. I kinda didn't want to bring you in front of the humans, but if they have anti-energy, you might be useful in a fight."

"Not that we'll be fighting," Cassiel says with a suspicious look on her face. "I think there's already been enough of that."

"Yeah, haha." Samantha giggles nervously.

As we turn back to the great door that leads inside Hero City, I glance at the duck in Cassiel's arms. "Ionis, why'd you bring the duck, anyway? Seems kinda pointless to me."

_THOSE WERE THE COMMANDER UNIT'S ORDERS._

"Uh, no?" I cock my head back and shoot a quizzical look at him. "I never said anything of the sort."

_THE HUMAN-TYPE ORGANIC KNOWN AS JASON HIRO IS NOT THE COMMANDER UNIT._

Now I'm really confused. "Okay, who is, then?"

_THE COMMANDER UNIT IS DESIGNATED HYPER-INTELLIGENT UNKNOWN-CLASS UNIT DESIGNATED 'HOWARD'._

Several seconds. Perhaps ten. That's how long I silently stare at Ionis. Taking a step to the side, I wrap my arm around Samantha's shoulder and whisper quietly to her.

"Hey. Sam."

She gives me a weird look but keeps her voice low. "What? Did he say something?"

"The death robot that can exterminate entire species thinks the duck is the commander unit."

"Oh." Samantha looks unconvinced. "He said that?"

"Mhm."

Sam's eyes flick to Cassiel, who is politely keeping out of our whispered conversation, but seems annoyed she isn't being included.

"That's the dumbest thing I've ever heard."

I nod. "My thoughts exactly. Should we tell Cass?"

"She might actually believe him. It'll go to her head. Better make up something else."

"I don't know if I can think of anything right now. I'm still in shock."

"You're a smart guy. Use your other head if you have to."

"Clever girl."

Carefully shambling past Samantha, I slide over to Cass. "Ionis has declared his eternal, undying love for you. I didn't want to tell you because it seemed so out of left field, but now you know why he never says anything to you and Samantha. He's shy around girls."

"Ohhh." Cassiel's eyes light up. "That _does_ make sense, now that I think about it."

_Oh my god. She took the bait._

Nodding slowly, I turn back to stare at Ionis, ignoring Cassiel's _oohs_ and _ahhs_ as she meditates on the importance of being a good wife to a genocidal murder machine.

"Your secret is safe with me, Ionis."

Ionis stares me down. _WHAT IS LOVE?_

I guess he heard me whispering to Samantha.

"It's what you feel toward Cassiel. Let's get a move on. My head is going to explode if I hear any more about your latest... opinions." A snicker from Samantha makes me whirl to face her. "I mean the head on my shoulders. Your mind is filthy."

Blankly, I turn and stare at the door. It still hasn't _hit me_ that the Sentinel follows the orders of a fucking duck, but it certainly explains why he's not half as intelligent as OMEN. OMEN would never say something so dumb even if he were ordered to.

Coiling up the muscles in my arm, I give the colossal, towering doors a shove, and they quickly swing open.

The light spills out into the hallway, and a group of men in uniform spin and spot us. The door guardsmen I set to guard the entrance, it seems, and they noticed us immediately. Looks like discipline hasn't slackened since I left.

"You! Who goes there?" An Arabian looking man steps forward, wearing a grey and blue uniform, with the symbol of a winged tiger on his chest. His flat cap is colored a dark maroon, and judging by his muscles, he stays fit.

He quickly looks at Cassiel, then me, but when his gaze turns to Samantha, his eyes size her up and down, but they don't freeze on her chest as most men do, but instead on her tail. "Wench, are you a demon? No demons allowed. You're lucky I'm a tolerant fellow, or I'd have a platoon here in an instant. As for the man..." He turns to glare at me. "No horns, no tail, but if you think that dark skin and the glowing tattoos don't out you as an Animus, you'd be quite wrong. Two demons, an angel, and a robot. If you leave at once, I won't do anything hasty."

Everything he's just said makes no sense at all. It takes me a couple seconds to come up with an answer. "What do you mean, 'no demons allowed'? Who came up with a stupid rule like that?"

His eyes widen, and he puts his hands on his hips. The other men behind him slowly reach for their small-arms, and one spits a cigar on the ground. "Well well, seems someone's a little behind on the times. Do you take me for a fool, animus? We haven't taken a single one of your kind for weeks. If you don't leave in five seconds, you'll think death was a mercy."

His arrogance is starting to piss me off. "I'm not an animus, nor am I a demon. I'm Hero, you moron. The one who founded this city, Hero City. The one who freed humanity. Don't tell me that after one month everyone's gone and forgotten my face so quickly."

The look of contempt on his face loses a tiny bit of its bite as he squints and studies me for a few moments. "You don't look anything like him. I worked with one of his clones for a while, before the Clone Wars. In fact, you look like his polar opposite. Anyway, even if you _are_ Hero, we certainly don't want you here. That's why the king banished you, fool."

"Banished me?" Now I'm bewildered. "Since when?!"

"You call yourself 'Hero,' and you don't even know? Obviously for tearing the city apart with your damned clones. How short do you think our memories are? You even planted demon traitors in our midst, assuming you're actually him."

One of the other guys, a morbidly obese man wearing what can only be described as several layers of blankets, coughs into his hand. "You tell him, Renji. Ain't got no room for magical mumbo-jumbo or scum-suckin' demon trash in our fair city."

Cassiel gnashes her teeth. "How rude! Despicable! And you call yourselves human?! Humans are supposed to be compassionate and empathetic, not bigoted and filled with irrational hatred! Just because you're warring with the demons doesn't mean all demons are bad! Hero built this city with his magic, and he freed you from the clutches of that snake lady person! You owe him your lives, and your respect!"

"I don't owe you three a goddamn thing!" Renji lashes out and steps toward us as he reaches for an anti-energy pistol at his side. "Demons tried to kill the king after we gave them safe harbor! Hero and his clones trashed this city! Demons killed many of my friends! Don't come asking us for safe harbor and act like you can lord over us with a false identity!"

He draws his pistol, and I've had enough. " **Sleep.** "

"What?" The last thing he says before his eyes slam shut is somewhat anticlimactic. He flops to the ground, as do all the other men at the entrance.

"The hell is going on?" I glance back at Ionis. "From this point forward, you are not to attack or harm anyone at all, unless they incapacitate all three of us. I'll handle whatever happens myself. Understand?"

Ionis looks from me to Howard and back again. _THE ORDER CONTRADICTS THE COMMANDER UNIT'S ORDERS. THIS SENTINEL UNIT WILL NOT COMPLY._

It takes all of my control not to facepalm so hard my palm crashes through my skull and reduces my brain to jelly. "If you attack the humans or anger them, they will turn their full might against us, including Cassiel, and she might die. Do you want-" I pause and remember what I told her earlier. "Do you want Cassiel, your lover, to die? That's what will happen if you piss them off. Isn't that right, Howard?"

I turn to the duck and whisper under my breath " **Agree.** " Immediately, the duck nods.

This satisfies Ionis. _UNDERSTOOD. THIS SENTINEL UNIT WILL COMPLY WITH THE ORDER NOT TO HARM ANY OF THE HUMAN-TYPES IN THIS AREA._

He really does take orders from the duck. My head hurts.

Quickly, we step over the bodies and an idea occurs to me. "This is just a temporary measure. **Disguise. Disguise. Disguise. Disguise.** "

Aiming my mind at each of the girls and myself, I remove my odd tattoos, make Samantha's tail invisible and cover her up a little bit so she doesn't stand out, and hide Cassiel's wings and glow. Somehow, she looks less regal and queenly without them.

"Ew, clothes." Samantha writhes uncomfortably, as if the few extra scraps of fabric were made up entirely of maggots and flesh-eating bacteria. "Do I have to?"

Cassiel reaches behind herself and bumps something invisible. "My wings are still there?"

"It's just a disguise. Samantha has her tail, too. I could morph them away if I need to, but I think both of you wouldn't feel right. As for-" I pause, realizing Ionis hasn't changed at all. I intended for him to turn into a giant Russian man, but nothing happened. "Ionis, why don't you look different?"

His eye swivels down and examines his chassis. _THIS SENTINEL UNIT IS COATED IN A MICROSCOPIC LAYER OF THE SUBSTANCE DESIGNATED 'ANTI-ENERGY.' ENERGY OTHER THAN TYPE ONE WILL BE NULLIFIED AS A RESULT._

"Everyone and their mother has anti-energy these days," I mutter under my breath. "If I bring you with us, you'll stick out like a sore thumb."

Samantha suddenly looks excited. "I have an idea! Make a bunch of fake skin, then put it on him and cover him up with a bunch of robes. He'll still stand out, but people will just think he's a blind man following after us or something."

I nod. "Good idea. Not perfect, but anything will do."

Several minutes later, I stare in disgust at the final product. "If anyone asks, he's a mute acid-attack victim."

Cassiel slowly licks her lips. "He's maybe the most disgusting thing I've seen in my life."

"I think it's an improvement," Samantha jokes with a wry smile. "At least he looks less robot-y."

"He looks like a Terminator that got half-melted during time travel." A pang of sickness ripples in my stomach as I eye the freakish lumps and folds of skin covering the metal frame. "At this point, the robes are for my protection. I don't want to have to look at you."

After wordsmithing some giant shirts, pants, and a bunch of cloaks, I can barely see under his hood. His eye is covered, but when I ask if he can still see, he responds with: _THIS SENTINEL UNIT IS CAPABLE OF SENSING THE ENVIRONMENT IN ANY DIRECTION FOR UP TO 1000KM IF THE SITUATION CALLS FOR IT._

I guess that's a yes.

The door closes behind us as I use my wordsmithing to seal it up and hide the sleeping men under tarps, and we begin to make our way downtown.

Immediately, I realize this isn't the Hero City I left a month ago. Before, it was a bustling metropolis, with buildings multiple stories tall and a Victorian feel to it, combined with an aesthetic similar to Arthurian and Viking legends. Chapels to unknown gods, marketplaces filled with food grown offworld, many such things were part of this capital I once knew.

But now...

A child sits on a stool, a glum expression on his face, as he slowly lowers and raises his foot in and out of a shallow puddle of mud. His clothes are ragged, and his demeanor is that of defeat. Sadly, he is not the only one. Aside from a few women quietly chatting with each other in a group, the street full of people has a despondent depression hanging in the air. It's only when I spot a man in a military uniform leaning on crutches, with a bandaged leg, missing one of his hands, and trying awkwardly to make the crutches work for him, do I realize just how bad things have become.

Samantha inhales slowly. "I wasn't here long, but the place has certainly changed. The war must be worsening for the humans."

At least she doesn't sound happy about it. Cassiel pats Howard's head. "I'm going to go talk with that little boy over there." Saying this, she takes off to the boy splashing in the mud puddle. Samantha and I watch curiously as she bends down and flashes her radiant smile at him. Despite whatever was making him sad, he's delighted to see such a beautiful woman talking to him. Not even twelve years old, and the poor lad looked like he was on the verge of suicide until she showed up.

A minute later, her smile is gone. The boy's is, too.

She finally trudges back over to us. "Both of his parents were in the military. Demons killed them. He's been an orphan for only a week."

Samantha's gaze lowers. "I'm sorry to hear that. War is hard on both sides."

"Sounds like the demons are putting up quite a fight." I scratch my nose and stare at the boy whose eyes are fixated entirely on Cassiel. "Did he say anything else?"

"He doesn't like demons. Nobody does. They tried to kill King Cody, but he survived." Cass pauses and glances at Sam before continuing. "All the demons have been taken away."

"What does that mean?" Samantha tries desperately to hide a look of revulsion but fails miserably.

"He didn't know. He's just a child, after all. Wherever the demons are being taken, they never return."

As if a blizzard had just decided to sweep through the alley, I rub my arms as a strange coldness falls over me. "Is this really Cody, the man who led the Gold Battalion? Didn't you work with him, Samantha? I didn't think he was like this."

She lightly chews on her thumb. "I don't know. He loved torching Grez. He saw the power I could unleash. If he was attacked by demons, anything is possible."

Cassiel sets Howard on the ground, and he hops up and down, _warking_ in front of her. "We should pay him a visit, don't you think? Let's get to the bottom of this and-"

"MAKE WAY!" A man shouts, his voice filled with alarm. "Clear the streets! We've got wounded incoming!"

As if by magic, every man, woman, and child leaps into doorways and alleys, leaving just the four of us dumbfounded as the sound of marching feet, like hail on a stone roof, come stomping toward us from further down the street. Dozens, if not hundreds of soldiers move toward us, hauling hundreds of other men and women on stretchers or other medical mobility devices. The three of us quickly start to move to the side, but Ionis continues standing perfectly still like an idiot.

"Ionis! Get over here! Stop standing in the street!" Cassiel hisses at him, and he quickly complies, stepping into the doorway along with us.

The soldiers march past, looks of pain and mental wounds apparent on their faces. One man weeps and clutches an ice pack to his face, and a gaping burn wound just barely sticks out from under his hand.

It takes many minutes as the horde of women and men washes past us, but soon, they're gone.

An old lady steps out onto the street and sighs. "How many did we lose today?"

Another responds in kind. "Too many. Did you see the boy? How can they send such young children out to fight?"

"He's sixteen. That's old enough for the king."

A bum sitting across the street chuckles. "At least it ain't me." He slurps down a mystery liquid in a metal canister hanging from his side. "I'm done sticking my neck out for that no-good tyrant."

"You never did in the first place! And that 'tyrant' is protecting us!" Both old women glower at the bum, but he just shrugs.

"Yeah, sure looks like he's doin' a mighty fine job. Demons'll tear us to pieces in a month or two. At least the rum will dull the pain."

"Ignore him, Agatha. Lee's always like this." The second woman pulls the first away, and both of them quickly scurry over to a market stall where some suspect-looking tomatoes are being sold, all the while shooting dirty looks at the old bum.

Samantha and Cassiel both look at each other, then at me. "The people seem divided about the King," Cassiel says, "and the war isn't going well either by the sound of it."

The succubus shrugs and leans against the wall. "Once the First Emperor became involved, the humans lost immediately. It's just a matter of time."

"Not if I can help it." I pull both of them along with me, and Ionis follows close behind us. "It's time we paid the king a little visit. I think he'll be unhappy about what I have to say."

As we pass a building that smells like a fermented outhouse, one thought comes to my mind: _What tore apart Hero City in the single month I've been gone?_
