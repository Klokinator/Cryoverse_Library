Part 090 - Battle Bowl 3

Our attacks were thunderous. Every impact made the sound of a small bomb going off. Our fists and weapons collided faster than the speed of sound. If I took a hit, Blaarjiim took a hit.

We really are still evenly matched.

I paused for a second to catch my breath, and Blaarjiim did at the same time. " _Tell me, old friend, why and when did we start fighting?_ "

" **We aren't friends. We never were.** " Blaarjiim took long, slow breaths, at the same tempo I did.

" _Were we originally human, or were we demon? I can't even remember my original form._ " My gaze dropped to my spear, the _Gungnir_. How long have I possessed this weapon? Was I born with it? Did I forge it in the clouds of heaven, perhaps the pits of Hell? Did I find it amongst some old ruins? Millions of years I have lived, always fighting Blaarjiim. My life is a blur. My goal in life seems distant and hazy.

Why do I wish to rule the labyrinth? Why do any of the demons there wish to? Am... I even a demon? Am I an angel? Most demons are weak to the power of light, but it makes me stronger.

I gazed up at the sun. This would be it. My final stand. I am strong in light, and Blaarjiim is strong in the dark, but against each other, neither of us ever holds an advantage.

I turned back to gaze upon the black, hideous aura that rippled off him. He had ingested thousands, even millions of souls from this world. He had consumed all life that could produce any form of thought energy. Even so, his power was still not greater than mine. Even if he consumed all life in the universe, I would still equal his power.

It was a curse that had haunted us for the shorter part of eternity, but it would end today. All thanks to the powers of this human.

" _You still think humans are beneath you, don't you? You still, after all this time, underestimate their spirits. They have suffered much because of the Great War between angels and demons, but they still fight on. Across an infinite number of universes and an infinite number of worlds, they always fight on. They possess something neither you nor I possess._ "

Blaarjiim gazed at me with interest. " **Oh? And what might that be?** "

" _The survival instinct. Humans fear death more than anything else. They may willingly bludgeon one another to death, but in that same respect, they would do anything to make their short lives worth something. I have seen it time and again. With this hero, with Joan, and with many before her. They simply want to make the universe a wonderful place for their kind to live._ "

Blaarjiim sneered. " **And they will do anything, including genocide of their own kind. Truly enlightened.** "

" _No. Not enlightened, willful and proud. But above all else, they are an evolving species. Our kind is stale. They make new advances constantly, in their grasp for power. Someday, the average human will exceed this one's current abilities._ "

" **Exactly. This is why I must rule the labyrinth, so I can crush them before that happens.** "

" _No. I cannot allow you to achieve your goals._ "

He charged at me suddenly, using his club to smash Gungnir from my hand. I quickly assumed a defensive stance and tried to repel him, but he grabbed me by the throat and slammed me to the ground.

" **Even this probably won't kill you! You are an infuriating opponent! Why can't you just DIE so I can complete my quest?!** "

I choked as his grip tightened. Wrapping my hands around the one he choked me with, I struggled mightily to pull him off me, but could not do so.

" _Ha... ha ha... I said I would win, and I will not lie, I had hoped to finally defeat you here once and... for all, but..._ " I choked the words out as he held his grip on my throat. " _...you will not let me win the fair way... therefore I must use this child's power... just one more time._ "

My eyes raised up to the sun again, as I gazed at its incredible glory. This will be the last time I see its light. Farewell, world of the humans.

I jerked my hand up and aimed it at the sun.

" _SUPERNOVA!_ "
